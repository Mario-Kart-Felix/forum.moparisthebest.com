Mopman July 31, 2016, 6:38pm #5

yes, you probably do want direct rather than belt drive

direct drive decks have the motor coupled directly to the platter, belt drives have a belt connecting the two. belts slip if you put too much pressure on them, they get worn out, they generally dont have much torque, etc. youll end up having to compensate for this by doing shit like giving the record a little nudge when you drop it to get it up to speed… theres a school of thought that if you can get good on belt drives you can spin on anything, but uh, tbh just save yourself the hassle and get direct straight off

also if you wanna scratch ever you wont be doing that on belt drives or youll go crazy

if you can afford it you cant really go wrong with technics sl-1210s obivously, even second hand would be fine if you can make sure theyre not fucked, since they’ll probably outlast you, they’re solid as and basically the gold standard deck, theyll also resell fairly well if you look after them

if you need somethign a bit lower budget then the usual suspects are fine, gemini numark etc.
ask a dj friend if you can though for specific models, i havent bought decks in ages

edit: it makes me sad that ollie assumed you were going cd, there was a time when everyone would assume wax unless stated otherwise

edit2: you should probably specify cd or vinyl since im not sure anymore

edit3: if your budget is really really REALLY tight and you absolutely must get belt drives, then its not like you wont be able to spin on them, but you will be selling them on fairly quickly (at a big loss) if you decide you wanna stick with it, be warned
