chillwitm888 August 2, 2016, 3:08pm #2

You can format your hard drive. That will remove all data; but, otherwise your hardware will remain the same, and your i.p. address depends upon your Internet provider not your computer.

and secondly
If you have your Recovery CD/DVDs you must drop the partition and let setup from the CD/DVDs repartition and format. If your system does not come with Recovery CD/DVDs then make some, or if your system has been installed OEM then you can just use your OS CD and perform all of this. It might not have the file for dropping the partition, if that is the case then you’d need to find a DOS 5 (I think that is the lowest version that can be used? it’s been awhile) or better to perform these actions. The IP is from your Internet provider, you would have to change ISP to accomplish that. If you have a (wireless) router you can set the IP range to be different then the default.

Repu August 2, 2016, 3:08pm #5

You wont be able to format your drive fully if it’s in use, I’d recommend using your BIOS to handle such a task; you might have to ‘flash’ (upgrade) your BIOS if the computer is really old.
Then you can just install a fresh copy of Linux or Windows and use good anti-virus software to keep your machine running efficiently.

Yes, I recommend install Linux, but if you choose to stick with Windows, just acquire a virtual copy. (Heh Heh) Burn it onto a CD, boot from the CD and it will give you a pretty user friendly menu, just select the option to install Windows, and from there you can format your partition and have a fresh install.
