PKERZ July 31, 2016, 7:46am #8

Well, Della, as I said, what you are doing wrong is, you are declaring a method with two (2) return-types. The first being “String” and the second being “statname[]”. First of all, you cannot have two (2) return-types, and second of all, I don’t think you made a class with the name “statname”, which in turn makes it impossible to use that, even if you have only one (1) return-type.

To make it easier for you, let me show an example of a correct way to do it. Obviously this one will have its method-body omitted for convenience’ sake:

public String readLines(String filename) throws IOException {...}

And to introduce you to the words I used earlier, here they are side-by-side:

public - This is an access-modifier.
String - This is the return-type for the method.
readLines - This is the name of the method.
(String filename) - This is the parameter-list of the method.

And also, your “statname[]” was also referring to a return-type, making it duplicate.
