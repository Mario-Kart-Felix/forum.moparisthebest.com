omgh4x0rz July 30, 2016, 7:33am #2

They really don’t care what you write, as long as it is well organized and well written. Organization is the biggest factor because that is what SAT and AP Lit readers are looking for. Content…meh

Make sure you have at least 5 paragraphs with the hour-glass format. Intro starts really broad and then explains what your 3 body paragraphs are about, then has a specific thesis. Move into your discussion, then the conclusion is the opposite of the intro. In the conclusions restate your thesis, reword the summary of your argument, and then give a last-sentence that ties everything together. Good last sentences usually tie into something happening today, or you can search brainyquotes.com to find a nice quote relating to your topic.

Also, make sure you avoid the passive voice (The essay was written=passive/bad) (He wrote the essay=active/good)

Hope that helped
