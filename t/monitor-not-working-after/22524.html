Rictoo July 29, 2016, 11:48pm #6

If you have an IGP (onboard gfx), then you might have RAM sharing enabled (sharing RAM with video RAM).
Maybe the BIOS got confused or something from the new RAM. Maybe the second one you put in is really old? Try switching the RAMs (slot 1 to 2, and 2 to 1). If that doesn’t work try to disable RAM sharing.

(This is all based on the assumption that you have sharing enabled :P)
