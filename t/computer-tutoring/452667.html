I’ve done some of this, although mostly for free - be ready for frustration, learn to handle it and not take it out on the tutee. One of the most important things I found was that sticking to strict lesson plan is often a bit hard… keep it detailed but open ended, as some people may need more help understanding certain concepts than others, while others may zip through your material and leave you blank faced. It’s often a good idea to find ways to relate and interconnect things, e.g. relate the concept of something like sending data to a web page to talking and exchanging proof of identification/forms (shitty example). Also, from experience working in customer service and sales, you need to be ready to roll with multiple scenarios. It doesn’t sound like your audience would be the kind of person to eat you alive, but be prepared for setbacks/insults/frustrations/shocks/etc.
