I’ve already outlined my point of view in an IRC conversation with someone - too much talking, not enough doing.

I don’t care about cheating at all really. But I’ll give my opinion anyway - the opinion of someone peeping in through a window from outside.

What I have noticed lately are some people talking themselves up quite a bit, as if they ‘rule the roost’.
Here’s a very good example:

wL:

…believe me, sema is exporing areas in the client that are beyond your comprehension…

Let’s break this remark down: the obvious implication is that someone is inferior to him. What kind of attitude is that towards another member of the community? I don’t know wL personally, so I’ll presume he has two years of Java programming experience. Just two years. That’s nothing when compared to people who have been in the industry for many more years, who have their degrees, and are actually paid.
That would make wL an ‘elitist’ right? I’ll use ‘elitist’ loosely, because chances are he’s nothing more then a sun spot on someone else’s arse.
I don’t like elitists - especially in communities like this, where a majority of users are really just kids. I’d hate to see what would happen to wL if he joined an adult community where the members had been programming for 10 years or so, and were professionals - the first thing that would happen is one would take a pin, and help the person in question deflate their oversized head.

Of course, there’s an important reason for my comments above: When you guys talk like that, you’re blatantly discouraging people. People that read such remarks could be smart, but maybe they’re new. Congratulations, you’ve just made someone feel inferior enough to chuck it in and say ‘bah, forget it - i’ll never be good enough for this lot’.
Just because someone doesn’t know something NOW doesn’t mean they won’t know it later. Who wants to try and learn those things though if you guys are basically kicking them in the stomach before they get the chance to even start?

That example above isn’t attacking wL as a person, it’s simply a great example that really proves my point.

EDIT: After all of the above, I left out an important point. There’s no way in the world a person is going to make something and release it if they only predict insults and put-downs - I don’t blame them. Maybe that’s why you see a lack of ‘smart’ people, and a lack of development.
