The video is about how a lot of big and popular websites are filtering out information that you wouldn’t necessarily read otherwise to create a personalised web experience somewhat unique to you and how this technology is flawed.

Have you ever gone on Amazon to check the price of an item, and notice that item and similar results are now displayed in your recommended items? That’s a lot like how Google works, you see, if you go search for the 2012 election candidates and you click a link with a blog that praises Ron Paul, Google will alter your search results to show more links that praise Ron Paul, and if you click on a link that is against Obama, Google will show more results that are against Obama. A system like this is somewhat functional for websites like Amazon, if you search for beach towels it’s likely that you may be interested in beach balls as well, but it has the problem of ONLY showing you beach related equipment for long after you’ve bought your beach towels and balls. On a website like Google it becomes especially problematic and it’s not hard to see why.
