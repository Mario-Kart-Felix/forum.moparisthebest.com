Before we start you WILL need a way to hook your Xbox up to your computer. I use a crossover cable, it cost me about $9 for a 7ft cable at my local computer store, that is the only investment you will have to make. 9$…for a cable. That or figure some other free alternative to connect your Xbox to your computer. Good luck with that.

Step 1 Connecting Pc and Xbox
Alright start off by hooking your Xbox up to your computer, if you went and bought a crossover cable, plug one end into the back of your Xbox and the other into your Ethernet port in the back of your computer.

Step 2 Setting up Network on Pc
Now we’re going to setup your network on your computer.
Goto start, then settings, network connection.
Right click on Local area Connection, and click Properties

lan.jpg785×361

Now scroll down in the little box and select Internet Protocol (TCP/IP)

network.jpg615×570

Select properties, set yours up like mine…click ok…then ok again.
At this point there should be a delay as the changes are applied. Some computers may have to restart instead.

Step3 Setting up Network on Xbox
Fire up the Xbox, go to Settings, Network Settings.

*Note
Due to the fact that I have soft-modded my Xbox, I can not give you exact directions on this part, I can tell you what to set, but you’ll have to find it in the Network settings yourself…honestly I think everyone here is capable of that much. This will work on non-modded Xboxs don’t worry. You do not have to soft-mod your Xbox to do this.

Alright. Now I’m going to list what you need to set.

Mode: Static
IP: 192.168.0.3
Subnet Mask: 255.255.255.0
Gateway: 192.168.0.100
DNS is blank

Save this and reboot Xbox.

Step 4a Setting up Xlink Kai
Yes I know that means this isn’t actual Xbox Live, Xlink Kai is exactly like it, and this also works for Ps2, Gamecube, PSP, Xbox360, etc

Go to their website: http://www.teamxlink.co.uk/

Register an “X-Tag” http://www.teamxlink.co.uk/?go=register

Then download Xlink Kai to your computer. Install, open, and login yourself in. So now we’re looking at this:

xlink.jpg272×896

Step 4b Signing on Halo2 via Xlink Kai
Click on the globe this is bringing you into “Arena Mode”
Now wait for it to load…then click Xbox
Again after it loads click First Person shooter…wait for all the game pictures to load, scroll down and click Halo2

xlink2.jpg272×898

Now click your country.

Step 4c Making Sure Everything Works
Now if your Xbox isn’t on, turn it on and boot up Halo2, now go back to the computer. Click on the magnifying glass in the top left corner.

Make sure that says “Yes” now click on the file icon below the blue help icon.

*Note
If it does not say “Yes” and you use a Router, you may have to Port Forward.

From Xlink Kai official site:

You'd be happy to know that nearly all routers will no longer require any port-forwarding for use with XLink Kai. Upon connection, each user is probed for a randomly open port and assigned that port should a connection to the Orbital be successful. However, there will always be a select few people who will have no alternative but to force a port-forward.

If Kai reports your network as unreachable, port-forward port 30000 on UDP to the local IP of the PC running Kai. Remember to enter the port number in ‘Kai Port’ in the Kai Configuration Tool. Leave Kai Deep Port as 0!. If you are behind a network that will not allow you to forward ports, but are allocated a few ports (generally on College campuses for gaming), use whatever port number you have available, as long as it is a UDP port-forward

I can not help anyone here Port Forward as I do not use a router…the best I can do is refer you to this site: http://portforward.com/
If that doesn’t work, ask around here or at the Xlink Kai site.

Now if your not in System Link mode in Halo2 already, go there, and games will start appearing in the menu, select and join one.

Congratulations. Your playing Xlink Kai.
