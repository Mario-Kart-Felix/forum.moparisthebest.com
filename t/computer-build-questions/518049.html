doom_j August 4, 2016, 7:09am #9

SATA is an interface for the drives and the mother board.

As for SSD, not only are they much faster then a hard drive, they’re also more efficient with power and also can withstand a higher stock rating. So if you drop your laptop or desktop say from your desk you shouldn’t need to worry as much about the hard drive smashing to a pile of scrap. Some other massive improvements are PCI SSD’s which can get even higher ratings than a SATA drive.
