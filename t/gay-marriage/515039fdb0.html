To further my point, marriage is first and foremost a legal term that offers legal rights, Christian churches do not define what legal rights marriage offers nor do they control who gets to marry, those are defined in the legal definition of marriage. The whole idea that we should respect a churches idea of who should be marriage is also bunk, I completely agree with Bowser, if a church wants to obtain a licence to grant the legal contract of being married then they should damn well grant the contract of marriage to anyone that legally qualifies, it is NOT the churches decision to decide who can and who cannot get married. HOWEVER, if a church wants to refuse the religious ceremony part of a marriage in their church, then that’s their prerogative, however distasteful and discriminatory it may be.

To further expand on why I think allowing churches to decide who cannot get married to be bunk: the analogy of a radical lesbian Feminism church was brought up, how about an Islamic church in America refusing to marry people who aren’t Muslims? They aren’t breaking any rules, on what basis will you refuse issuing the licence?
