zyle1992 August 4, 2016, 1:13am #74

Let’s say that Texas, or any other state successfully secedes. At that point, they may no longer have the backing of the United States, or its allies. They would now be their own nation. They would need to create their own infrastructure. Most companies would take advantage of this, by serving the new nation, with increased cost. Companies could monopolize. They would still need a form of government, which may or may not be easy. One person could take over, and turn it into a democratic nation, or communist, or anything else. After that, laws would need to be made, or remade, because the federal government no longer has any leeway. Borders would be put up to protect the United States, so now there’s the whole international issue to deal with. Assuming the drug cartels don’t decimate the ex-state Texas, and they are not allies with anyone (cut ALL ties to US and Mexico) anyone can come in, take down this new weakened government, and claim the territory for themselves.

Assuming they do survive long enough independently, they would need to build diplomatic relations. It might be easier/harder depending on the actions they took to last this long. And if, at some point in the future, the decide they want to join back up with the US, many conditions would need to be met. It wouldn’t be a “Hey, long time no see, welcome back!” type thing. They would need to prove that they are capable of having a peaceful relationship, and compromises would have to be made in terms of laws and regulations.

Over all, at this point in time, it would be an absolute horrible idea to secede from the US, and is not a decision that should be taken lightly.
