Hi there.

I have something on my mind. Now this next statement may seem a bit biased/prejudice so please have some sensitivity whilst reading it: When has anyone in the private server community ever been successful. I mean really successful. Please read on to fully understand what I mean.

Now there are some very clever people here. I don’t know what you all do for jobs, maybe there are some official IT experts here? I don’t know. Maybe there is some people here that can or have created something completely new, original, from scratch with all ideas of their own (game wise; not small programs). Maybe.

Now this is more of a personal quote, as it probably doesn’t apply to you, but I thought to myself just a while ago “I play games - not make them”.

I play games. And I enjoy doing so, as do you I’m sure, because you’re here reading this. Now then, I enjoy to download a private server source, take a look at it and have a play around with it. It’s fun to create your own custom RuneScape.

But who am I kidding? I can’t impact the community by doing something amazing (coding wise), which I believe deep down we all strive to do. We all contribute in our own way, some more than others, to produce some something we can call our own, our release, our server. But is that what you really call success? Even the more approved servers out there, the more complexed ones, can you REALLY call them a success?

It sounds like I’m talking down to them right? I’m not. I genuinely appreciate the people that made private servers possible. I appreciate the people that do post some really nice content for us to use and abuse.

What I mean is, sit back for a second and think about the word “success”. Can any of us become a success here in the private server community? Can any of us become anything more than a “server-tinkerer” (I know we’re all different individuals; this does not apply to everyone).

You may think I’m just saying this becasue I can’t do it myself or I don’t have the right to say this. It’s something I was wondering. I don’t know jack-shit; that’s why I’m asking you.

I guess my real question is: if any of us were that great, would we be here?

But I will speak for myself.

Also, maybe there are successful people among the community, but have not yet come forward. I know if I really had done something special, I’d be reluctant to just broadcast it and let it be ruined.
