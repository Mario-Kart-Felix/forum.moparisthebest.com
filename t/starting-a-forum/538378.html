lothy August 4, 2016, 11:27am #5

Google rankings have very little to do with things like meta-tags these days, precisely because every idiot and his dog manipulated them in order to achieve higher ranking.
Instead it’s content driven and network driven. If your website has good unique content then it will receive a higher ranking. If it is linked to from lots of sites with good unique content then it will receive a higher ranking.

Using tricks, such as large blocks of unrelated content that are hidden on the page through the use of CSS, typically incurs a punishment in terms of ranking. However various reasonable activities can be performed to bolster ranking, such as using human-readable URLs (instead of something like this: http://web-talk.co.uk/index.php/topic,5.msg5.html?PHPSESSID=7d764799d14d7f51a44e04bc5b34958a#new)
