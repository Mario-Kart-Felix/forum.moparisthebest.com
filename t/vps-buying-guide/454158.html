Guide to the world of a Virtual Private Server (Vps).
Welcome to my guide on how to find a Windows or linux VPS.

Part 1: There are a lot of choices to make before deciding on your VPS. Such as what operating system do you want, how much ram/diskspace, bandwidth and Cpu you will need, Also what how much you are wanting to pay.
First off we are going to talk about the operating system. There are major differences in the two operating systems you have to choose from, now keep in mind that each of these major OS’s come with different versions for you to pick through to suite your needs best. My personal choice is the Windows server 2008 as It is fast, has the feel of a regular computer and is usually more secure. The same is to be said with Windows server 2003. These are the only Windows OP’s I would recommend for a VPS for RsPs. Now on to Linux, Linux comes in many variations and is the #1 free OS in the world. As you have so many choices in this area it can be very confusing as to where to start. For a Linux OS I would recommend centOS.
If you read below and look at my charts. I will compare the Prices, and the specs of my favorite VPS companies.
