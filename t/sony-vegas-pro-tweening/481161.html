Inbetweening

Inbetweening or tweening is a key process in all types of animation, including computer animation. It is the process of generating intermediate frames between two images, called key frames, to give the appearance that the first image evolves smoothly into the second image. Inbetweens are the drawings which create the illusion of motion. Traditional inbetweening involves the use of light tables to draw a set of pencil-on-paper pictures.
