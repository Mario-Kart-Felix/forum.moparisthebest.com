lothy August 1, 2016, 1:16am #2

Which subjects? In my opinion, I think the best learning is done by using at least two methods (ie, reading and hearing/listening), with content repeated over x days.

So say I was learning a foreign language, and working on vocabulary building.
Firstly, I’d pick 20 - 30 words per day. Secondly, I’d write them out, reading as I go. Thirdly, I’d consider recording myself saying the word followed by the meaning. And then I’d listen to that recording.
Basically, memory retention is enhanced by activating more areas of the brain AND repeating over consecutive days. You’re basically creating as many pathways as you can to the memory within your brain, using as many methods as possible.

If it’s a maths based subject (or even chemistry, among other sciences) then use http://www.khanacademy.org/.
