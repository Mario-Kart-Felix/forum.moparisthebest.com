veer:

Out of curiosity, Moparisthebest… being a minarcho-capitalist, how do you reconcile your beliefs with the FOSS ideals? They seem very Proudhon mutualist to me.

Capitalists like SETO are right to be afraid of open source. Inherent in the open source philosophy is work not based on profit, and there being no private property of software.

Let’s look at some definitions for a second.
Capitalism - private ownership of the means of production
Socialist - collective ownership of the means of production
Communism - the abolishment of private property

Free open source is about software not having a single owner, but being free to use/modify/share by everybody. If you look at the above definitions of different political-economic systems, you’ll notice open source effectively comes under the last two systems, not the first one.

FOSS people constantly fight against patents and DRM, after all what are those thing if not companies trying to make private the communal software that we collectively worked on?

every program is [b]threatened constantly by software patents[/b]. States should not allow [b]patents to restrict[/b] development and use of software on general-purpose computers, but in those that do, we wish to avoid the [b]special danger that patents applied to a free program could make it effectively proprietary[/b]. To prevent this, the GPL assures that patents cannot be used to render the program non-free.

If you look at the things SETO says against open source, they are the same stuff said for anarchism, socialism and communism.
“It won’t work because there’s no incentive”

But Linux, Apache, Firefox, Wikipedia and thousands of other open source projects are providing examples where people work for reasons other than personal profit. They are proving people like SETO wrong.

There’s a reason the GPL is described as a copyleft license, because the ideas that underpin it are straight from the political left wing.
