[center][size=20pt]Grammar Lesson: How to Not Sound Like an Idiot[/size]
[size=16pt]By: Java Is My Bitch & Davidi2[/size][/center]

[size=11pt]Two, To, and Too[/size]
Two is the number. EX: I had two bottles of Pepsi.
Too means “also”, or “more”. EX: I love you too. I had too much to drink.
To is every thing else. EX: I have to go to the bathroom.

[size=11pt]Your an You’re[/size]
Your is possessive, it means it belongs to someone. EX: That is your pee in the toilet.
You’re means you can exchange that word for “you are”. EX: You’re You are getting pee all over.

[size=11pt]There, Their, and They’re[/size]
They’re means you can exchange that word for “they are”. EX: They’re They are looking at you.
There is usually about location, but it can also be used in other ways. EX: The bathroom is way over there. There are a many people in it.
Their is possessive, it means it belongs to someone. EX: That is their pee in the toilet.

[size=11pt]Slang[/size]
Lol: Laugh out loud - Funny EX: That post made me lol.
Rofl: Rolling on [size=7pt]the[/size] floor laughing - Very funny EX: ROFL!
Lmao/Lmfao: Laugh my (fucking) ass off - Very funny EX: LMAO!
Iirc: If I remember correctly - Not 100% sure they are right EX: IIRC South America is below North America
Cba: Can’t be asked - They do not want to do it EX: I CBA to reply to that topic

[size=11pt]Capitalization[/size]
Capitalization is the act of emphasizing special characters in your written works. For example, you capitalize the first word of every sentence to show that it has started. Here are the main forms of capitalization:

Sentence Case: This is the most used method of capitalization. It should be used all of the time your write, and shows some maturity. EX: Hello, how are you today?

Word/Title Case: This is the second-most used method of capitalization. It is used when you write titles. You capitalize the first letter of every word except a few special ones. Please do not use this for emphasis, as it is very annoying. BAD: Hey How Are You Doing? GOOD EX: Romeo and Juliet
Notice that the “and” is not capitalized, and this is because it is a special kind of word. Here is an incomplete list of words you usually do not capitalize in titles (unless they are the first word):

[ul][li]and[/li]
[li]of[/li]
[li]the[/li]
[li]or[/li]
[li]to[/li]
[li]an[/li][/ul]

A more in-depth guide on title capitalization can be found here: Title Capitalization
