The second #4 I can help with for now…The initial mechanical energy of the system is:

E[sub]o[/sub] = K + U (Kinetic energy + Potential energy)
E[sub]o[/sub] = 0 + mgh
E[sub]o[/sub] = 7.84 J

As it approaches the ground, the potential energy decreases as the kinetic energy increases. Work is done in the downward direction by the earth through gravitational attraction, but 1.44 J is also done through air resistance in the upward direction. So by the time the ball reaches the ground, it has a kinetic energy of 7.904 - 1.44 = 6.4 J

Thus v = √(2W/m) = √(2 * 6.4 / 0.2) = 8 m/s

The resistance force of the sand is 260 N upwards. To figure out how far the block falls into the sand, we can solve for work done, since we know how much energy the block possesses.

W = Fd
6.4 J = (260 N) * d
d = 0.0245 m
