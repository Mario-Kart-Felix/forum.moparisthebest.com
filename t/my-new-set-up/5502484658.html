wildskiller August 4, 2016, 2:56pm #22

Overclocking seems to be simple to a lot of people. Really, it’s just changing the clock multiplier and voltage to give it more power. Intel Extreme Tuning Utility will help with a lot of that if you’re not sure you can handle the bios. The newer i3s, i5s, and i7s are already unlocked for you, so it should be easy with that tool. Also, I would go with water cooling for your CPU if you ever want to overclock. It’s not ENTIRELY needed, but it can do a lot better than most air-fanned CPUs and keeps the heat lower.

Overclocking really isn’t necessary if you have a new intel i3-5-7 because every core on them bad boys pack power and graphics. If your graphics card ever fails, then their onboard graphics can take over until you can get a new one. It’s not the best but you sure in hell can play Skyrim on medium-high with it.
