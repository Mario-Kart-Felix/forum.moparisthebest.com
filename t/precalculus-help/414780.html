Alright, so my teacher gave us this problem the other day, and I thought I had it figured out. Then he decided to tell us that there are 2 possible answers for it, and we needed both.

“The pattern pictured below is a piece of cardboard that will be folded into a rectangular box with two square sides. The surface area of the cut cardboard is 1400 in². The volume of the box is 3000 in³. What are the dimensions of the box?”

UNqEo.png707×374

The dimensions of the box that I got were length = 30, width & height = 10. My process:
Knowing w = h, volume = lw², so 3000 = lw², 3000/w² = l.
1400 = 2(2lw + w²)
700 = 2lw + w²
w² + 2lw - 700 = 0
w² + 2w(3000/w²) - 700 = 0
Graph that, then find out what y is when x = 0, where x = w.
when y = 0, x = 10, so w = 10, h = 10.
3000/10² = l, 30 = l.
