I’ve just fit my Ford Fiesta Zetec with a new sound system, and occasionally I will hear a squealing, which intensifies with the amount of revs. After searching the web I found it was likely to be my grounding, which I changed and this didn’t make a difference.

I am running 2 front door speakers of a headunit, with a 12" 1000W Sub and 2 100W RMS 6x9s running off a 1200W amp. I’m not sure if the problem is a power issue, and was looking for some advice before I go out and get a capacitor.

The squealing will not get louder when I alter the volume, but will increase when the engines revving.

Any ideas?

moparisthebest July 31, 2016, 5:10pm #5

It’s because you are using a common ground for all of your components. The only way I fixed this in my truck with a 1200W amp is to run all grounds back to the battery instead of grounding them to the body like you would anything else, it’s a pain in the ass, but it worked.

Also what makes a big difference, for me at least, is if you use the low (rca plugs) or high (speaker wires) inputs in the amp, the high inputs made a lot of noise for me and the low inputs made a lot less.

Good luck.
