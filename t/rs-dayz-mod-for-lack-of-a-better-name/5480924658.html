Ok thanks, Ruby.

I think there are limits with my knowledge and the way the game is set up atm that certain things will need to be improvised. As a deterrent I plan to expand running energy to be your character’s overall energy. You need energy to build, energy to destroy (think of the amount of energy it would take to break down 1 wall in real life), energy to run, energy to walk.

Hunger and water gives you energy. But you need to sleep. No sleep makes you fatigued which drastically impacts your energy, which impacts your ability to complete tasks. That way players won’t be able to just destroy walls 1 by 1.

Instead of 100 running energy you may have 1000 energy for your entire body. Breaking down a wall may take several minutes in game and use up 500 energy. Including hunger, thirst and fatigue this would only mean you could destroy 1 wall and then 20 minutes later you could maybe destroy another one. This would make breaking down walls impractical after replenishing your energy. Hopefully this makes players ration their energy and creates the illusion that walls are hard to break down.
