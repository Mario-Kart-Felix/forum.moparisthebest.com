Ruby:

So you’re creating a social networking site?

Nope. I for one hate social networking sites. It’s a website for people to join the forums, to talk normally and discuss features of their profiles and what to do better/change, and to host a 100% custom profile. Take MeetMe, Facebook, Google+, and even Myspace for example. All of them force you to have specific things on your profile. MeetMe, being unique, allows you to edit custom CSS (sarcastic “woo”) which allows you to move or hid elements and change some colors, backgrounds, few other slim things. But you can’t really ADD anything extra, like a banner, or new box, or something like that. The other sites mentioned allow nothing of the sort. Then there is IMVU (I do play it :3). The profile on there allows essentially the most freedom of all, with custom html and css being available. But you get banned for the simplest of things, like not having a couple buttons on there or removing the “block” feature. And you can’t get far on making a profile on there unless you know how to enable the sandbox. This is where my site comes in. You would sign up and upload your index, images, or what ever (limits would be in effect). Then you choose features like “public or private” and the blur image thing and other stuff. Public or private would just be there to ask if you will allow me to show your page link, and a snapshot, on the “Public Profiles” page. If set to private you may link it where ever, and we will not invade your privacy except to check up on photos (we being the staff of the site.).
Does this clear things up?
