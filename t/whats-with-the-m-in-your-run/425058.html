It’s good practice to put it as low as you know wont crash your program. If it’s a dedicated server, you can allocate as much memory as you have, but otherwise just allocate as much as you don’t need.

mod_taharok August 2, 2016, 2:26pm #5

It depends on whether it is Xmx or Xms. Xmx is the maximal amount the application can use, whereas Xms is the minimal. And adding to what David said about good practice, it’s better practice to understand why the system uses too much memory before just bumping up the amount the application can use. Ideally, these servers have way too little features to require more than 256mb of memory. They use as much as they do because they are poorly engineered and developed.
