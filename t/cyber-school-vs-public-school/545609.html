ExtremeX-Scape August 4, 2016, 1:35pm #3
  1. No, most of them are the same as any public school regarding how your transcript works and recommendations, etc
  2. Not for the school unless they have local curricular activities which you can enroll in.
  3. State tests such as SAT, ACT, etc are performed at some local location, for example I took my OGT tests a few years ago and the location was in a hotel located nearby my public school district.

sources: online high school and college student

davidi2 August 4, 2016, 1:35pm #4

First two for AtomicInt are mostly right, #3 not really correct. First of all SAT and ACT are not state tests

  1. Pretty much what Atomic said. However it depends how your district handles it. For example, I was just in an online school section of the district I was already enrolled in, so they just gave me the same highschool name on my diploma that I would have gotten if I was actually on-campus, and I could have walked at the graduation, etc.
  2. Again, depends on how your district handles it. I was in the same district and still enrolled in the on-campus highschool, just taking online classes. I could have signed up for any clubs/activities that the campus offered. Important to note however is that if you’re not taking classes at the school so nobody on the teams recognize you, it might be hard for them to accept you.
  3. Well, SAT and ACT are not state tests first of all, but again it depends on your state and district. They might have you go to a proctor for any important class tests (midterms/finals), or just for state tests (in my state that’s the WASL/HSPE). For SAT/ACT you will definitely have to go to a proctor as well, your district/state wouldn’t even have a say in it.
Elrick August 4, 2016, 1:35pm #5

I’d have to say David summed it up pretty well. Your best bet is to contact someone in your school district and ask about policies regarding course and state testing when taking online courses. The only way I think you can enroll in sports is if you are enrolled in the school, presumably for at least 1 course. If that is the case then see if it is possible to enroll for an elective each year. However, this means you either have to take the bus to school and then walk home, or you have to get a ride home/drive if you’re of legal age. I had a friend in her junior year who was taking college courses at the local community college and she was only at school for the last 2 periods because she was getting credits from online and college.

Like David said, a lot of your questions are going to have answers that depend on what your district allows. I would suggest sitting your guardian down and discussing what you want to do, and then find out what your options are with them.
