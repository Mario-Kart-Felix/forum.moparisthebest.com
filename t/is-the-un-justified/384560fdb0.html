lothy August 1, 2016, 8:27am #73

Big countries should always have the right to veto something. Whether or not they’re prepared to in the face of potential repercussions depends.
The reality is that not many countries can successfully place sanctions on the US without doing more damage to themselves. On the other hand, when the US places a sanction on someone it is crippling.
That’s just how the world works for big countries.

Also, why shouldn’t the US/UK/etc sometimes say no? Being a member of the UN does not override state sovereignty. They’re still at liberty, as individual countries, to do whatever they please at the end of the day.

A lot of scope creep has occurred with the UN since its inception. Specifically, the original goal of the UN was to stop a third world war… After doing it twice, most countries realised that there were probably alternatives.
