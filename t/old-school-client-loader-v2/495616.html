SwiftKit - RuneScape Toolkit

A RuneScape Toolkit providing RuneScape players with Runescape tools and utilities, Runescape quest guides, Runescape maps, and loads more!

Jagex launched the old (new?) RuneScape 2007 servers not many minutes ago (at the time of this posting), and we'd like to assure you, our users, that SwiftKit will have support for Old School RuneScape as well!

We will need a few days to get things sorted, but rest assured that in a couple of days an update will be out for you all!

So in light of this, a quick F.A.Q. with what most people will be wondering, we’ll keep this updated throughout until Old School is officially released.

How do I play Old School on SwiftKit?
Old School is available trough the RuneScape main website, access it using the “Play RS” button like you normally would, and under their banner a new button labeled “Play Old School” will take you to the Old School page (We may make this easier for you to access once Old School is officially released, or earlier if you behave ).

Will the calculators still work?
They most certainly will, most of them at least. Some calculators use new data (like the slayer calculator, and combat calculator), but for skills you’ll just get some extra skill buttons available as it stands, but you should be able to use them just fine.

Can I look up my character on the Hiscores?
It’s currently not possible to look up your user with SwiftKit for Old School. Support for this will be coming shortly.
A temporary solution is available, but requires some manual input on the users end.

Start by going to your SwiftKit install folder (This should be “C:\Program Files (x86)\SwiftKit” by default) and open the Data folder.
There should be a file named Links.ini that you need to open, the 4th line starts with the text “Link1=”, replace this line with the following: Link1=http://sk.eliteazza.com/index_lite.ws?player=

Save the file and close it, your stats lookup will now look up Old School stats.

Again, this is a temporary solution until we release a proper update, and it may not be as responsive while under heavy load as it should ideally be.

Can’t you just bring back SwiftSwitch!
Unfortunately, SwiftSwitch has not aged that well, there are a few key points that keep us from releasing it at this time, we may look into it, but we make no promise. SwiftKit is our primary focus, and it should be just as useful to you on Old School servers as new servers.
