No Cost Method

Flax is the buzz word here. Note that this is member only. First, go to Camelot. Directly below Cammy, there is a flax field. Stock up, and head to the bank. Repeat until you have at least 1K. Then post on the forums that you are selling flax. Do not make it into bowstrings! This eliminates your crafting buyers. Flax generally sells for 50-100 gp each. Sell on the forums. The profit odds for this are 0:50-100 (0 being it costs you nothing and 50-100 being the unit price). Profit odds will be used a lot in this guide. These profit odds are good, but time consuming for little cash (only 50K-100K for 1K flax).

Low/High Cost Method

The second method is to buy water runes. This works for both free players and members. To do this, you need around 40K-100K. Go to a lowly populated free world. Next, go to the Port Sarim rune shop. Talk to Betty, and, if there are 5,000 waters in stock, buy 1K. If not, switch worlds and try again. You buy 1K waters at around 6 each, on average. After buying 1K, switch worlds and repeat. At an average of 6 each, you can buy anywhere from 6.5K-16.5K waters. Sell these waters on the forums for 20-30 each. You will make around 130K-495K. Now that you have the low cost money-making methods down, let’s see higher cost strategies.

RuneScape is Like the Stock Market?

Yes friends, it is! At first glance, you might think I am crazy for stating this. However, when you think about it, that’s exactly what RuneScape is. If you are familiar with the stock market, you know that you invest in a stock you think is going to go up. Now, replace the word “stock” with the word “item”, and you get “You invest in an item you think is going to go up”. For example, a few months ago, the value of claws skyrocketed. At this time, the claws were overpriced, and were twice as much as the price now. There has been no way to detect tomorrow’s popular item, until now! I have devised a system to detect hot items, using basic business principles.

Supply vs. Demand

This principle is used most commonly with holiday drops. As time passes, and the amount of these items decreases, price goes up. This has happened with all tradable holiday drops, and is the reason that Jagex stopped giving out tradable holiday drops.

New Items

RuneScape has come out with many new items. Usually, these items start out really inflated in price, and then the price plummets as the item becomes more common. Examples of this include claws, Infinity Robes, planks, and Dragon 2h. So, the rule of thumb here is that if a new item comes out, wait a few months before buying it.
