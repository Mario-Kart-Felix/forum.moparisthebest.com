Basic synopsis is roughly the same as the myth of Pyramus and Thisbe, which it was based upon. Alternatley, use West Side Story as a modern-day retelling (although I don't advise it).

People fall in love, their romance is doomed because their families are in a feud. There is a fight, and Romeo kills someone. As a result he must leave the city, and so he and Juliet plan to run away together and elope. Juliet talks to the helpful friar, who gives her a potion that will make her appear as one dead. She takes the potion, and her family believes that she has died. They bury her, and two messengers, one from the friar, one from Romeo's friends, are sent to him, both with different stories of what happened. Guess who reaches him first? (Yes, the messenger from his friends/Juliet's family, who says that Juliet died) Romeo, devastated, goes to Juliet's tomb, lies down beside her, and drinks a vial of poison. Juliet awakes shortly thereafter, sees Romeo dead beside her, and kills herself despairing with his sword/dagger.
