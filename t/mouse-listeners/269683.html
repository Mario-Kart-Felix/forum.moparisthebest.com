mod_taharok July 31, 2016, 6:30am #9

I’m far from any client hacking expert or anything, but usually the canvas isn’t added to the Applet container until a little while into the initialization. Try sleeping for like 500 milliseconds to 1 second before grabbing the component.

You can also do [tt]rs.getComponent(0);[/tt] instead of [tt]rs.getComponentAt(1, 1);[/tt]

And another thing, I don’t recommend you do this at all. Once the login screen is displayed, the Canvas will be reinstantiated and the new instance will replace the old one in the Applet container. Along with the new instance, the old listeners will still be there. This most effective way to avoid some very icky system that checks if the right listeners are in place is by intercepting all AWT events sent to the Canvas class at all. This way, you can effectively prevent user input (if desired) and not have to worry about any listener changes.
