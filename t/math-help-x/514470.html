The elimination method means that you are removing one factor from the two equations in order to find the solution. This being either your x or your y. For example, the two equations:

x+3y=-7 (1)
6x-y=15 (2)
^ ^ ^

The equation is already lined up, so you have to figure out which factor would be easier to remove. In this case it is the second factor (y), using Lin‚Äôs example. To remove this, the y factor in 1 and 2 need to match. This is when you times the bottom equation to match to top:

x + 3y = -7 (1)
6x [sup]x3[/sup]- y [sup]x3[/sup] = 15 [sup]x3 /sup

so the equation becomes:

x + 3y = -7 (1)
18x - 3y = 45 (2)

Then as Lin said ‚ÄòIf I add this to the top equation, y cancels out and you‚Äôre left with: 19x = 52‚Äô.

  x + [s]3y[/s] = -7 (1)
  • 18x - 3y = 45 (2)
     19x = 52
