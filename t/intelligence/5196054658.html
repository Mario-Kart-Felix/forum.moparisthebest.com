The people who got A* at my school were those who put the work in, this didn’t make them intelligent, it didn’t mean that their mental ability was superior to those with Cs it’s a complete failure of the educational system to teach people to work on their own. John Stuart Mill who lived just over a century ago could read greek and latin at age 8 and began reading plato at age 10, was this natural ability? I don’t think so his father had access to many books and was a very intelligent man who taught John to read and extract information for himself.

What I think is this:

Natural ability doesn’t exist - the reason some people are better then others at things they’ve never studied is due to cognition and retaining of similar situations, e.g a person who plays one instrument might be able to play another instrument well, why can’t someone be unconsciously aware of this? This is different in the case of sporting exercises when genetics play a part, although there’s some debate about how much they play, muscle is the product of nurture and to some extent so is your height (if you don’t get nutrition you won’t grow as tall).

Nurture - Is the primary indicator of one’s intelligence, if they’ve grown up around intelligent people then they’ve pushed their influences onto their children, it’s the same setting in an educational background if you’ve been taught mathematics by a complete and utter moron without self study you will be a complete and utter moron.

Cognition - Intelligence is a measure of cognition, memory, mental processing, abstract thought (e.g one who is able to make theories that fit the facts, and not facts that fit the theories, and who is able to extract the meaning of text in a deep interpretation rather then just reading word for word and agreeing without any logical construction and arguments, e.g intelligent people are often philosophers who delve into extracting large bodies of text and finding deeper meaning)

In other words what separates an intelligent person from a knowledge one is cognition and the ability to think for themselves without reliance on others; they’re also not afraid to come up with outrageous theories. Someone who goes to school and gets good grades is someone who knows what the educational institutions want them to know, someone who self studies is someone who knows what they want to know.
