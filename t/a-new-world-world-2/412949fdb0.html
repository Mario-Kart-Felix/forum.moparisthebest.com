reyx7 August 1, 2016, 2:38pm #80

Alright, let’s begin.
There is a number of you that dislike Pvp. You want a server where you can freely build in with little to no threat, and want teamwork to be important. Now, on the current server, who actually has any problems with mobs? Sure, clearing a new area can take a bit of time, but who is in constant fear of mobs attacking them? I know any skilled minecraft player wouldn’t have a problem, as the area they plan to use would be all torched up. The point David brought up was this server was to bring everyone together, just as the first server did. And yet I found people rather spread out, as a portion of the moparscape community was new to minecraft and had little/no experience. After some issues, the second server was made.

I would say teamwork was the biggest part of the second server. People worked together in two ways. The first was finding friends they could make a city with. Build a nice little town to defend against the mobs, and slowly expand their village. But they also found people for a second reason, and that was because of the fear of an attack. In the early days of the server, pvp was not active, but within 2-3 weeks, it was on. This gave people time to prep up and have something nice ready. I would say it honestly only takes 1 RL day, from scratch, to lose all my fear of mobs above the surface. Get some quick resources, build a wall, torch it up, and I’m ready.

The mods want teamwork and togetherness, right? So how about we join together in different factions. Use eachother for survival against an actual threat, other players. Mobs lack to be a threat after a bit of time. How about the new server be pvp for 7-14 days. This gives people time to get materials and move where they choose. MITB has a practically impenitrable fortress, with lava walls reaching from sky to bedrock (or something along those lines). Anyone who is cheap and wants to kill for resources will go for the quick kill, so if you’re worried about people like that, even a medium level of defense would defend against that.

When I decide I want to kill people, plans are set up, ideas are created. I don’t say I’m just going to go on a slaughter spree. I pick a target and I hunt them down. I go for that one person, and I’ll really only kill someone else if they hit first. There would be plenty of ways to combat an attack. If you get 3 iron and find a lava lake at layer 12, then bam, you can put some sort of lava floor below your castle to prevent people coming up into you. Obsidian farms aren’t to hard, but the first three diamonds and the redstone source in the early days may prove to be tricky. Most people who raid wouldn’t bring diamond tools, a diamond sword at the most. Anyone defending has an advantage. Even if you don’t know someone is attacking, pistons provide a great way to set up an escape route. Agl (noobs are ftw) has one of the most advanced, unnecessary and ridiculous emergency escape area that I’ve ever seen. But the thing is, if anyone was attacking, he would get out safely with no problem. I’m sure just a simple piston door could lead you to safety, or a painting on your wall with a door behind it.

As for your items… infinite lwc was the thing most people liked about the last server. This gave us the peace of mind that, even if we did get killed and maybe lost a stack of wood or an iron pickaxe, that we still have resources that can’t be touched inside our chests. Infinite Lwc should definitely be brought back, as this removes most of the complications of death.

Have I convinced anyone who disliked pvp for it be brought back? And would anything else like to add some supporting arguements?
