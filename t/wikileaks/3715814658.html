To me this whole affair is a classic case of a government against the people. Remember that every single right you have was won by groups of people fighting hard to extract concessions from the powerful.

Before any action started, the common person was nothing more than property that a Lord could buy or sell along with the land. The peasants suffered bad conditions while their rulers lived comfortably. There were plenty of priests on hand to remind people that a better place awaited after they died. Then something amazing happened.

The Spanish Reconquista took back southern Spain and had access to the Arab libraries. They found new ideas in the books there. Powerful new ideas about debate and democracy, evidence and rationalism, science, mathematics and proof. This entirely new way of thinking lifted Europe out of the dark ages and into the Age of Enlightenment.
Newton found he could explain the heavens without resorting to the supernatural. Paine published his thoughts on human rights, the ideas that all men should be treated equally. Mill wrote about freedom and liberty, the view that people should be able to decide their fate for themselves.
So what happened? A new country was founded in the new world, with an Enlightened constitution. In France the oppressed people rose up and guillotined those who claimed to have the supernatural powers on their side. Although the British never saw a revolution of the likes of the US or France, there were still many popular movements to extract concessions from the powerful. The Chartists, for instance, campaigned for the right to vote and to stand for election or all men, also for a secret ballot and for equal district sizes.

The Americans and the French pioneers both knew that power corrupts. Those in power must constantly be kept in check or they will certainly use and abuse their privileges. For this reason they enshrined the freedom to speak out against your rulers. For if the government was embarrassed by people reading about the bad things it does, maybe it would stop doing so many bad things.

And in Britain? Well we still have a monarch, who claims to be appointed by God. She has little power in government but when the prince gets married it’s still on the front page and will cost the economy £5 billion.

Since Reagan and Thatcher, the rights won by labour movements have been slowly rolled back. Since 9/11 civil rights have been rolled back.
The support for Wikileaks is a reaction to every authoritarian government rubbing its hands with glee when they realised how much of our rights they could reign in.

Paine, Hobbes, Mill, Jefferson, Franklin… should not Assange be added to this list?

Radeon:

What Yakman said… Arabs are the founders of the modern day world.

Jumping to conclusions.

The ideas of debate, freedom and reason were first discovered (or maybe invented) by the ancient Greeks. They had the more fundamental idea that you could find truth about the universe by observing reality and trusting your reasoning. Contrast that with the Hebrews who would derive ‘truth’ by tripping, writing it down and saying the book was holy.

If you think about it, humanity has existed for about 300000 years. Yet it was only in the ancient Greek period about 2500 years ago that progress was achieved. For the very first time as far as we know, a man could be more knowledgable about the world than his grandfather and could be sure that his grandson would have even more knowledge.
Yes the Egyptians had a crude Pythagoras’ theorem and the Babylonians had the quadratic equation. But they were only treated as recipes for practical use. Why in Egypt, Pythagoras’s theorem was discovered and used unchanged for five centuries! No progress at all.

Where did the Arabs get their ideas from? They got them from the ancient Greeks. When the Spanish opened Arab libraries they found the works of Euclid, Pythagoras, Eratosthenes and Aristotle, amongst others. True, Arabs had contributions of their own. Algebra, Alchemy, Alcohol and distillation were Arab inventions. Kitāb al-Manāẓir‎ (كتاب المناظر) who did work on optics, derived the laws of refraction, reflection and image forming. He made the first camera and deduced that outer space was empty. But the Arabs did not invent the flame of objective reasoning, they only kept it alive.

You might ask why didn’t the Enlightenment happen in Arabia instead of Europe? It’s clear to me that one enemy of reason is totalitarianism. If people in society are not allowed to think freely, the flame of objective reasoning will go out and progress will stop. The problem with freedom and objective reasoning is that it causes people to question everything. This is simply unacceptable to totalitarian regimes as criticism would make them fail.

We can see that in Nazi Germany, how many scientists fled to freer places. I’m convinced the main reason the Germans didn’t build nuclear weapons first was that their scientists were not allowed to think freely without ideology blinding them. For instance most German scientists of the time rejected Einstein’s theory of relativity as ‘jewish science’. Now think of Germany before totalitarianism. Terms from quantum mechanics like eigenfunction, eigenstate and bremsstrahlung testify to their German origin.
Another example is Soviet Russia. They rejected genetic evolution, which it condemned as a “bourgeois perversion”, in favour of Lysenkoism. This dogma lead to their agriculture collapsing and people simply running out of food.
The story of Catholic totalitarianism persecuting Galileo and Bruno is too well known to repeat here.
So what happened in Arabia was that Islam, which was a young religion then, gained power and became totalitarian, as most religions do. It proceeded to stamp out free thinking and Arabia sank into a dark period from which it has not emerged yet. To put this into perspective, today Harvard produces more scientific papers then the entire middle-east!

The Enlightenment could have been stamped out in Europe too by the Catholic church, had it not been for the protestant reformation which created a decentralised society with no one faction strong enough to have total power. If you look at European scientists and philosophers after 1642. Newton, Bacon, Leibniz, Descartes and Boyle were all protestant.
This is not to say that protestantism is good, just that the historical circumstances were like that. My former religion was Catholic anyway, I have no particular bias towards protestants.

If you want to read a book about all the Greek rationalism, I recommend Uncommon Sense by Alan Cromer.

TD;DR
Religion is crap. Rationalism is fantastic. Past religious cultures achieved progress in spite of their religion. The Greeks, not the Arabs, were the founders of the modern world
