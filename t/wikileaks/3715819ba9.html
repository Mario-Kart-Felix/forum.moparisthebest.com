SomethingToHide August 1, 2016, 1:29am #51

I agree that he (Assange) should not (and possible can not) be tried for Treason or Espionage.

Straight from “The Law of Public Communication: 7th Edition”:

"Although journalists may be liable for trespass, at least one court has ruled that they are not liable if they receive the fruits of someone else's trespass."

It then talks about the Drew Pearson case where a journalist published private information about a Senator that was stolen by the Senator’s staff.

Note that although this is about trespassing, it can be seen equally as Treason or Espionage. Assange received information from Manning. Manning did the Treason and Espionage (if you could call it that). Assange is therefore NOT liable for Treason and Espionage.

Also note that this does NOT mean he is also not liable for knowingly publishing secret and stolen information, just that he did not commit Treason or Espionage.

I don’t know that much about laws spread across continents and countries, but if Assange did come here to the US, he could be sued for publishing that and he wouldn’t be protected under the First Amendment. But we can’t exactly go kidnap him and bring him here to try him.
