yakman August 1, 2016, 12:45am #24

I admit I was wrong with my predictions about FOOS and governments in my last post.

However I still think FOOS goes against free market principles because they rely on having incentives to do stuff.

If your company makes bad software, you’ll go out of business and end up starving to death, thus everybody will try to make good software.
FOOS people volunteer their spare time to contribute to a FOOS project with no promise of monetary return. If they make bad software that nobody uses… nothing happens. They have no incentive to make their software better.

So free market principles predict that paid-for software will always be better than FOOS.

I reckoned neo-liberal governments wouldn’t want to use FOOS because that would mean admitting the market isn’t always perfect. But since they have, I think that means these governments are not as right-wing as I thought they would be.
