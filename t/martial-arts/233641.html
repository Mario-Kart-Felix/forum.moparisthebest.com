lothy July 31, 2016, 2:01am #12

There’s no such thing as a martial art that sucks dick. Each and every one of them have both strengths and weaknesses.
I suppose you need to look at it from a particular viewpoint: do you want to do a martial art that is just that; an art? Or do you want to do a martial art that has fighting as its focus.

I’m sure that sounds funny, and someone will ask “what martial art doesn’t have fighting as the focus?”. For the record, tae kwon do (which I used to do) was focused on perfection of technique and patterns. A large emphasis was placed on patterns, which make up the artistic side of the sport.
I’m sure it isn’t like this at all tae kwon do dojos, but it was at mine.

You’ll find that muay thai, the one yz85racer mentioned, is quite good if you want an art with a focus on fighting. Unlike tae kwon do (and many others) they don’t limit themselves to just kicking/striking (tae kwon do), or predominately throwing (judo). Muay Thai makes use of 8 body parts for strikes (hands, feet, elbows, knees), something that can be very valuable, and something not found in quite a few arts (where elbow and knee strikes are often illegal sporting-wise; again, I’m speaking from a tae kwon do background when I say that).

Muay Thai is also one of several martial arts taught to police, security guards, military combatants and so on, simply due to the nature of the art (ie, it can easily be used offensively to quickly incapacitate someone).
