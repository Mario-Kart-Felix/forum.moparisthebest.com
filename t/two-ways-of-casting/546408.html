So there are two ways to cast an object to another in Java.

You can use:

Object1 object1 = (Object1)object2;

Or you can do:

Object1 object1 = Object1.class.cast(object2);

(i think that’s correct)

I’m here to ask you which is better to use for casting objects that aren’t primitive data types (int, double, float, etc).

For all of you who will tell me to google it, I did, I read, I just couldn’t understand where this second form of casting would be required.
