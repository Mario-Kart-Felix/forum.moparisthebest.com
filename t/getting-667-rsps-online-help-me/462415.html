winslow August 4, 2016, 12:24am #12

what router are you using? Have you opened your ports on windows firewall? if not let me explain…
1.Go to your start button and search “windows firewall” click windows firewall advanced security…
2.Go to inbound rules and add a new rule…
3.Click “Port” and next…
4.Make sure its on tcp, type your port, and hit next.
5.Click allow connection click next twice.
6.type your servername as name and type a description (optional)

OK now were gunna open up this port on the router
1.go to your cmd.exe and type “ipconfig” hit enter
2.look for your router ip mine would be “192.168.2.1” yours may be different
3.Go too your browser and type you ip
4.On your router setting there should be a option called “virtual servers”
5.Now add your port! make sure u put your ip as the description you can see your ip here: http://www.ipchicken.com
