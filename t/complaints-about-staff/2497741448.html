The Staff team is designed to be on almost every point of the day. There is usually at least one moparscape moderator on. The globals pop in now and then, as do the administrators. There are very few points in the day that actually don’t have staff members online, and those are obviously the points at which someone would wish to attack the site. But I highly doubt there is any period in the day that there are no staff members on for longer than 30 minutes, which is not very long at all. Any events that could possibly happen can easily be cleaned up by a staff member who will be on shortly after the event has taken place.

As for suggestion future moderators, leave that to the staff team. If we feel we cannot cope with the amount of work Moparscape takes to manage it, then we will suggest future mods to our fellow staff members. If the globals and admins agree, then the person will be tried as a moderator. Your suggestions do nothing to influence the choosing of future moderators. We have our own ways of selecting moderators out of the several thousand members here and then monitoring them to decide whether they can handle the position.

And about the age thing, I personally disagree with letting someone younger than thirteen be a moderator here. In fact, I don’t like the idea of even a thirteen year old holding a position, but I started off at that age myself, so it would be hypocritical of me to disagree. There have been past experiences with younger moderators who we thought were mature enough for the position, yet ended up making a fool of themselves and of this community. The generally popular people of this community also happen to be younger, which makes it a very bad combination. The community will thrust suggestions of these popular people to become staff members, but they generally do not qualify due to their age (which is not a requirement, but moreover a convenience) or their impressions they leave upon us.

The one thing that will certainly break my own vote for a future staff member is whether they show they want the position. If a person wants a staff position and enjoys the attention the community is giving them for whatever they are being recognized for, then I believe they will become power hungry and end up abusing their position, which I do not want to see happen. However, on the opposite side of the spectrum, neither would I vote for someone who absolutely doesn’t want the position. The type of person who is ideal for moderator in my mind is the one who accepts the position, perhaps with minute want, but with no wanting prior to the offer and no lack of wanting after.

And finally, a devotion and positive attitude toward the community means everything. Also the adaption to other staff members and an open mind to suggestions and changes.
