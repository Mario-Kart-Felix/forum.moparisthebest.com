Yeah, it doesn’t really make sense that the same file would always corrupt. Maybe the actual corruption can be anywhere, but the problem just appears to be in filesystem.squashfs?

Downloading over wireless rather than ethernet can cause corruption because of interference and packet loss etc. I remember when I was trying to direct-download a disc image for something a while ago, and it corrupted every time. Torrents are much more reliable because the protocol is pretty much built around hash checks. So I’d suggest either torrenting over wireless, or direct-downloading over ethernet.

Anyway, it’s probably a good idea to just check the hash on your download. Just to make sure corruption is the problem here…
