Up to a certain point in your school life, your classes may not be of any worth to you, however, when you turn 14/15 you can choose the subjects you want to take, which is part of pursuing what path you want to follow in life. On top of this, in the UK most jobs nowadays require 5+ GCSEs A*-C (Which must include English & Mathematics), so this part of life is compulsory if you’re wanting to get a half decent job if you don’t wish to pursue any further education beyond this point.

For example, when I was 14/15 I chose the following four GCSEs:

  • Triple Science (Biology, Chemistry, Physics).
  • Business Studies.
  • I.C.T.
  • Geography.

On top of those I had to continue with the compulsory classes such as Mathematics, Physical Education (P.E), Religious Education etc. The only GCSE choice I regretted taking is Geography; the teacher I had hated me and the feeling was mutual, therefore making that class hell for me. After the two year course I passed all four of the above subjects as well as the compulsory subjects (Excluding Religious Education; I failed that miserably :P).

To be honest, you don’t really need any qualifications if you’re not going to be working for anyone else (i.e. creating your own business/company), but it’s highly unlikely that you would get very far without basic qualifications such as the ones stated above (5 GCSEs A*-C). I mean if you were to create the next best invention or food product, then you’d be rich for life and wouldn’t need to pursue any further education (unless you felt the need to).

Though, some people may not think that success is all about their wealth and education; some people believe that success is having a happy family or something along those lines.
