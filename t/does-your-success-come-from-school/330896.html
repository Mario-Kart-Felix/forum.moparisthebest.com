cooper July 31, 2016, 3:47pm #19

Does your success come from school?
It depends on what you consider ‘success’. In my sense, partially - I view success as understanding and knowing as much as possible, however not just in a scholastic sense, also in a person-to-person sense. My class in epistemology as well as ones that build analytical and critical thinking skills certainly have helped me, but social experiences with others also plays a major role. Although, I would not have had many of these experiences if not for school, however that is the social aspect and I wouldn’t consider that under school’s education.
Do you believe your grade in school defines how intelligent you are?
I do not believe this to be true. Grades are affected by multitudes of internal and external influences - the difficulty of your classes, your mental condition, your physical condition to an extent, your desire/effort, and the teacher and source material you are given. This is partially why I abhor the American educational system, however I understand that ‘success’ is most likely different to a capitalist looking for employees compared to my definition of it.
Does it truly affect your future and opportunity toward success, or is it only a stepping stone in the right path?
Again, it depends on what you consider ‘success’. It may affect your future, but like grades, there are always internal and external influences that can take prevalence.
