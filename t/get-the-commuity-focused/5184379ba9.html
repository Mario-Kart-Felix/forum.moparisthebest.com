For the simple reason of getting the community focused. If it can be thought of, then it can be planned. With a plan comes opportunity, and with opportunity comes those who are willing to take the chance.
From what I’ve seen, the reason there has never been a successful community project was because it was never managed - at least not efficiently. Sure, you would need to provide a sort of motivation, and although I am very much against the idea of monetary reward, if that is what it takes, then so be it. If even one of these community projects could at least get to the point where work is significantly noticeable, then I’m sure it could be done time and time again.
