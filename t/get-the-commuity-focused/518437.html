My opinion is completely biased, and I will most likely receive bad feedback from stating it, but I really think the problem is the forum software itself and the current community. I love SMF and I’ve grown quite attached to this theme, but having more features, whether it be with this forum software or another, is something that would most certainly attract newer members while hopefully keeping the current ones.

As I stated above, another problem is the community. There is way too much drama, and that’s because everyone knows each other, except maybe the select few that are newly registered. The hatred towards other in this community is rather vague, but still very much noticeable. I don’t mean to bring notice to pure hatred, but rather judgmental slander and things of the nature. Of course, I’m sure there are people that completely despise someone else, but that is only human nature; however, this hatred can be stopped by just ignoring or attempting to adapt to the current social situation you are in.

I’ve been a part of this community for nearly 3 years, and even though this is a website which features only virtual communication, there have been several times I’ve been scared to death just to comment on something, for the fact that people are extremely judgmental. There are many things that could be done to fix this social corruption, and a great start would be to enforce the rules and be more strict, and even monitor new users to ensure that they aren’t breaking the rules either.

Edit: It seems that there are classes of society on this site, so that some users are more important or less valued than others, which is absurd. Starting a community project is bad, because there are a lot of people who don’t fit in certain groups, but there should seriously be alternatives to motivating people to participate in the community, whether it be private server competitions, game competitions, or even general programming competitions.
