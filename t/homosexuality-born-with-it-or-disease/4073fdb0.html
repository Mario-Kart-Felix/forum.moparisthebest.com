Erm, please elucidate where I’ve failed to comprehend biology. I would very much appreciate it.

Firstly, I’ll mention how humourous it is that you slip in “all rights but marriage and children” when absolutely nothing else you’ve said had anything to do with it. This already betrays that you’ve got your agenda from the outset, and are more than likely going to come to the necessary conclusion to somehow back that up. Not that, of course, homosexuality being a choice would back that up in any way, as it could just as much be a responsible, rational decision in order to promote a particular human’s well-being and mental stability, thereby creating a home which would arguably be better than a great number of households provided by “normal” families.

But, to the meat of your… erm, objection? Why, for example, did you not bother to recognize the fact that “genes” are not all that you’re “born with”? There’s that roughly 9 month period where all kinds of crazy things are happening to the zygote/embryo/baby, and by all accounts, these have at least some sort of effect on the child. If homosexuality were somehow brought about at this stage, it would certainly still qualify as being born with it. But that complicates the issue, which is what those who would rapidly dismiss it either can not allow or are incapable of. Weigh the options more carefully, in the future.

For example, you seem to be under the impression that genes can only be transmitted or effected by means of reproduction, which is patently not the case. Lateral gene transfer is extremely well documented, if only in the past couple of decades. This is why many geneticists are gradually moving away from a “genetic tree” model for evolution; everyone is increasingly aware that it’s a far more complex issue than that. (We’re also seeing theories of inter-species breeding cropping up, which only complicates things further) Were LGT to occur early on in utero, say, by some random viral infection which is semi- but not particularly common, neither parent would have to be a carrier of a “gay gene” (nor, for that matter, would the two parents, in conjunction, have to be capable of producing the proper alleles or gene pairings or whathaveyou, if you aren’t prejudiced enough to limit yourself to this concept of “one gene = you’re a homo”).

As for “only X chromosomes,” this is silly at best. As I just alluded to, and stated in a previous post, this presumes that there is a single gene pair – ONE – which determines sexual preferences of a person, or else none at all? Hair color is more complex than that, which involves, at the very least, two gene pairs, and most likely a number more in order to account for the varying shades of colors. Let’s repeat that, just to be clear what we’re talking about: HAIR COLOR. Surely something as nuanced as sexuality would also require more than a single gene. You are completely ignoring the possible influence of blended phenotypes and co-dominance of alleles (which would, I suppose, be a possible explanation of bisexuality). And these are just what I, an very uninformed laymen, can think of off the top of my head.

I don’t know whether to attribute these repeated oversimplifications by just about everyone to general ignorance, laziness, maliciousness, a rabid inclination to disregard all facts and simply jump to the conclusions you want, … The list goes on.

Whatever it is, many of you are making a pitifully superficial analysis of what is outright STUMPING professional geneticists. You don’t find it a bit presumptuous that you happen to know the answers? Were the matter as simple as, “An XY chromosome can’t mate with an XY chromosome,” don’t you think the issue would have been put to rest by now? I really can’t comprehend this: why are a bunch of people, most of whom aren’t out of bloody high school yet, deciding that they understand an issue, where people who have been working in the field for god-knows-how-long don’t yet fully know what’s going on?

Think this through. Of course, it’s quite possible that I’ve completely failed to understand anything about biology. I don’t study it regularly and have at best a minor awareness of the subtleties involved. I’m sure I’ve already made some errors in what I said above, as I didn’t bother to take the time to refresh my memory on the topic.
