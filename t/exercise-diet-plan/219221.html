I would say add in a morning weight check, morning heart rate check (possibly during the workout as well), and overall feeling that day (quality of workout and level of difficulty [1-10], as well as if you felt like you had a good day or not). Then maybe a quick comment box on how your day went and what you did.

This way you can look back at some day when you improved alot and see exactly what was different from the previous days, or the same if you had a bad day and you can see what to avoid. And heart rate is a very big plus, as it never lies. It tells you straight forward what kind of condition you are in.

All professional athletes involved in endurance sports keep a log like this, to make sure they stay in top shape. Even with just simple dieting, it will be worth it in the long run if you want to be dedicated.
