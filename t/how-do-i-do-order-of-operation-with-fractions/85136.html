I think that is arguable. You can, of course, state that the limit as x approaches 0 of a/x is infinity, but that implies that 1/0 → ∞, not 1/0 = ∞. Arithmetic is defined on the premise that all values used are real. Infinity cannot be defined as a real number, but an upper limit to all real numbers. Thus, 1/0 does not exist in terms of real numbers.
