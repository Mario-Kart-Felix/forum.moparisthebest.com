Liquid Magic:

The PS3 also has more RAM, a better hard drive, better networking, and better audio .

Both the PS3 and Xbox 360 have a total of 512MB of memory.

The PS3’s memory is split into VRAM and System RAM.

PS3 System RAM: 256MB XDR memory @ 3.2Ghz
PS3 VRAM: 256MB GDDR3 memory @ 700Mhz

The Xbox 360’s memory is the full 512MB shared between both the GPU and CPU.

512MB GDDR3 memory @ 700Mhz.

As for networking. Yeah, the PS3 has the Gigabit Ethernet port, but the Xbox 360’s 100Mbit is more than it will ever be required to do. So, that part is irrelevant.

Wireless, the 99% of the PS3s have 802.11b/g built in. The Xbox 360 has only just started building wireless in, but has had the option for 802.11n for a while, and the new models have 802.11n built in.

So, not really better networking. Not in any practical sense, anyway.

I also own both the consoles (two of each, an old model of each, a new slim model of each) but I do prefer the PlayStation 3 for its exclusives. Sony own around 20 of their own studios, whereas Microsoft own around 5. Sony can make more exclusives for its system, whereas Microsoft cannot - and have to rely on third part developers, who often don’t always stay exclusive or never make them properly exclusive in the first place, and release them on PC too - where they are better.

The Xbox 360 has xbox live, which is brilliant, I don’t think it is worth the money, but it is better than PlayStation network for features and uniformity. But, actual online play - I see little to no difference between the two. Except Sony seemed to be a little more willing to be a bit more open - with stuff like MAG (256 player online games), and LittleBigPlanet - user generated content shared across networks - which Microsoft said themselves they would be hesitant to do for security reasons.
