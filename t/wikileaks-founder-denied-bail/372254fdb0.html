yakman:

Where in the first amendment does it make the distinction between stolen speech and unstolen speech?

First Amendment doesn’t protect all speech. Mainly obscenity and false advertising aren’t protected. It’s not said in the First Amendment itself, they didn’t think of it at the time of the First Amendment. It’s been decided in court cases by the Supreme Court.

Read some decisions by the Supremes on cases about Free Speech. The press doesn’t have protection under the First Amendment for information either they have obtained illegally, or have received information they knew was obtained illegally. Like journalists don’t have protection under the First Amendment to NOT speak about their sources (i.e. promising source confidentiality to their sources). States have the ability to make their own (39 states in 2009 had shield laws protecting sources), however Federal > State and if a case is appealed there’s a strong chance Federal law will overturn the State law.

Read the case I mentioned in the last post or any other related case. Also know the law.
