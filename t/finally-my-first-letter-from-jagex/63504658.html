Krichevskoy July 29, 2016, 8:53pm #26

Well, aren’t we glad all our servers are open source now that they have to go underground?

Anyway, they can get you with the client thing, but here’s the route they would have to take:

Distributing pirate copies of the client encourages the development of private servers, which directly threatens our profits through loss of membership.

That’s essentially the reverse of Blizzard’s arguments against the WoW emulators, that creating servers, even ones for “educational purposes” that don’t actually use Blizzard IP (the WoW protocol wasn’t/isn’t copyrighted like the RuneScape one, iirc) encourage the distribution of pirated WoW clients. This is the argument that took down GotWoW:

While we appreciate the devotion and enthusiasm the GotWoW? community has shown for World of Warcraft, [b]the development of emulator servers directly contributes to the illegal copying of the beta game client[/b] and creates the opportunity for people to continue to play on unauthorized servers after the completion of the World of Warcraft beta test.
You can probably infer that it was [i]not[/i] a voluntary takedown, since apparently Blizzard still has control of the domain (more than a year later? I don't remember how long WoW has been retail).

Obviously Jagex is no Blizzard, but they still have considerably more legal resources than mopar. My point here is to warn against the “they can’t REALLY do that” trap, because tenuous arguments become very convincing in the presence of large sums of money.

Here’s a simple process that has worked sometimes in other places.

  1. Remove all tutorials, links, files, and forums related to private servers.
  2. Float the rumour that Jagex was involved. Clueless leechers give up in despair, purging the community.
  3. Any dedicated developers move to IRC, where they are effectively untouchable (as long as you don’t also own the IRC network…the best are obscure foreign ones. What was that .lv network that Kaitnieks used for a brief time?)
