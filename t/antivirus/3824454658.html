jme August 1, 2016, 7:35am #28

Keep your windows updated. This is important.

Do not use internet explorer. Use Firefox or Google Chrome and make sure you’re always using the latest version. There are many exploits on webpages that can infect you just by visiting the page.
Just by using thepiratebay.org website (without downloading anything) I got a nasty virus from an exploit in one of their advertisements by another company, and it installed some rogue antivirus fake shit that was difficult to remove. I’ve gotten similar virus/trojans just by visiting web pages. I think the first time it happened to me, it was the WMF image exploit, which installed a smitfraud variant. It wouldn’t have happened if I didn’t use internet explorer.

If you are using programs such as limewire, learn to identify viruses just by the filenames, file type, and size. It’s pretty obvious how to tell. Also, use limewire for music only. If you download an mp3 or any audio file that wants you to download a codec or go to some website, cancel and delete the file.

You can download infected files without getting infected. It is not until you run the file, will it do anything. Should you be suspicious of an executable (.exe) file, just scan it with virusscan.jotti.org

Running an antivirus in the background uses up resources and may slow down your computer. In my experience, programs like norton, avg, or nod32 are all garbage. It’s pretty rare for me to get a virus because I try to be smart about them. But when I have gotten them any time in the past 5 years or so, those programs have been no help at all other then identifying names of the virus/trojans, so that I can search for manual removal.

Your best friend is malwarebytes antimalware, should you ever have a problem. If it doesn’t work, do it in safemode or without internet connection. It will save your ass when nothing else has worked.

Follow what I say and you will probably never need an antivirus program.
