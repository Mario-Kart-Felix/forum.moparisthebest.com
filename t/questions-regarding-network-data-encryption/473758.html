Cres:

Why do some games (like RS) encrypt data before sending packets?

well rs has a few ways it does it:

isaac random number generator: for stopping packet injection and figuring out what the id of any given packet hard (it’s only useful for stopping injection now, since we already know everything about the 317 protocol)
randomized data types/mutations: makes reverse engineering and reading packets hard (useless now since we understand the protocol)
rsa encrypted login block: so people can’t steal your information. or, if they have your login information, steal your session (because of your “uid”) by forcing a reconnect to their client

idk what you mean by charlist. strings are immutable, meaning they can’t be modified once they are created. you can use stringbuilder or use an array of characters.
