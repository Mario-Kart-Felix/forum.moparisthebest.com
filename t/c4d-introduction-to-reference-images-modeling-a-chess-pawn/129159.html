C4D - Introduction to Reference Images - Modeling a Chess Pawn

Graphics
maru July 30, 2016, 1:09pm #1

When I first started C4D, I followed a tutorial that helped me model a chess pawn. I have long lost that tutorial, so I thought I’d write an easier one

  1. Get in to ‘Front’ view mode by pressing F4. This is where we need to set up our reference image. Press Shift+V and you should see a menu like this:

    (Ignore that F Lock: Off. That’s just my keyboard being stupid).

  2. Go to the ‘Back’ tab and select the reference image. Find a decent frontal pic of a pawn on the internet and use it.

  3. Your reference image should now be in the background. This is where we grab our spline tool and go round the pawn in splines. Use a bezier spline and then you can play about with curves and such using the arrows by pulling your mouse on the spline. When you’re done, you should have something that looks, better, than this:

THE BASE OF THE PAWN MUST BE FLAT
That’s the hard part out of the way. Remember that you can mess about with the splines as much as you want. Spend a minimum of 10 minutes perfecting it to how you want.

  1. Now grab a Lathe NURB and put your spline under it:

  2. Now, go back to your perspective view, F1, and you should see something pawn shaped

You can put it under a Hyper NURB if you want. Now we just texture, and get a nice result:
