I can vouch for the mention of a DDoS attack knocking a home computer offline.

I sit behind two routers, one is a Belkin Wireless G router which is connected to the cable modem, and the other is an Apple Airport Express 802.11N router. The G offers ethernet and 802.11B/G compatible wifi to my legacy hardware and the Airport Express gives me Wireless N at 270 mbps for my newer computers.

DARScape has been under a constant UDP/TCP SYN flood for weeks. Our dedi host, Sharktech.net does a great job at filtering the flooding and the intrusion detection firewall does the rest.

I briefly moved the dynamic IP domain from the IP belonging to my dedi to my residential IP address. (Use of a No-IP.org) My Belkin router has a built in hardware firewall which had been set to enabled. I had set port forwarding and was running a replica website/game server as a test environment. The Airport router froze, then the Belkin froze. Ability to connect to the internet stopped. I rebooted the routers and my cable modem only to find it happen again. Roadrunner ISP didn’t seem to be filtering anything, the whole flood was coming through and exceeding my 512mbps upstream bandwidth allowance.

I was lucky to have a Smartphone with an internet connection so that I could go to DynDNS.org and disable the IP forwarding and redirect it back to the dedi.
