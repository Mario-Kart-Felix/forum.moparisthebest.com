Uncalled August 4, 2016, 8:45am #16

It could be better. Try making the canvas size on a larger scale, and the text not take up so much room. I don’t like the borders on the first couple that you have up there. There’s no depth with them, and they do not flow together really at all. You could try to handle the lighting better, get more precision in your depth, get a focal point (as you don’t even have one). Also, the font is killer, and doesn’t look good. If you want to use crazy font like that, you have to have a scene where it fits in. This bland-style theme you’ve got going does not look good with your font. You should also remove the glow effect from your font completely, unless you reconstruct your design. Overall, they kind of look immature in a way. And for the last one, you should make the green more visible and not use the same picture three times in a row. Get a soft edged brush and have the images flow together, then fill it up better with some effects. It’s a good start though, and could get you going in Photoshop.

If you really want to get going fast, I suggest that you take a look at videos and figure out how to do some cool stuff in Photoshop. After you’ve done this and that and gotten the basics of it down, you’ll start to improvise and catch on fairly quickly. Read and watch tutorials, Google how to do certain things, and eventually you’ll just start whipping up your own stuff very soon.

A suggestion to an overall design: You should make the height bigger, and get a render of some sort. Give us something to look at and not just some text that’s floating around. Add effects to the render, get your focal point, get your depth, do this and that. Make it look neat and well designed. If you are to use a render, I suggest that you do not center it, and have it slightly off to one side or the other. Well, that pretty much sums it all up. I hope to see you progress in GFX and can’t wait to see future graphics of yours.
