Then linux is not for you. The beauty of Arch is the fact that it comes with only minimal utilities and just enough to get you by. It also has a large userbase, not quite as large as Ubuntu but just as helpful, and a wonderful wiki ( http://wiki.archlinux.org ). Soemtimes I find that the smaller userbase is better. There are less redundant and old solutions for the same problems that will no longer fix the issue you’re having.

You mentioned having a problem with Ubuntu + wireless. There is a wiki for ubuntu full of information as well. Arch’s solution: http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Wireless Another nice thing about Arch is that it doesn’t come with a desktop environment. With Ubuntu, you’ll find many solutions when you look for help that require you to be using gnome. With Arch, you’ll find that most solutions do not care whether you’re using a desktop environment at all.
