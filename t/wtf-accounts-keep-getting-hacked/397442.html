Tom August 1, 2016, 12:09pm #4

If they threatened to crash your PC, they’ve probably installed a RAT on your system. I suggest you run a scan immediately (Malwarebytes etc). Also, try and remember what might of infected you. If the RAT is FUD (fully undetectable) you’ll need to take more measures to remove it.

It’s most likely that the virus launches on startup also, check your startup folder for any suspicious files (e.g winlog.exe, system32.exe, svchost.exe are common names). Check common directories like the below.

C:\Windows
C:\Windows\system32
C:\Windows\system
C:\

Those are all common virus installation directories. If you’ve had no luck download a good scanner e.g malwarebytes, norton etc.

Download.com

Malwarebytes

Malwarebytes 3.0 was a major update when it arrived in December 2016 -- and like many major updates, some aspects were a little rough, particularly stability

eset.com

ESET NOD32 Antivirus

Record-breaking antivirus and antispyware protection. Enjoy advanced technology that does not slow your PC. Download the latest ESET NOD32 ANTIVIRUS now! 30 Day Free Trial

Are some good, free examples. Ensure they’ve been updated then boot into safe mode and scan with them, safe mode will disable the virus making it impossible for him to stop you while you’re scanning. If all fails and it is FUD, you can monitor connections from specific processes in order to gain an insight into where it is, and how to remove it.

You can also take a look at your system processes (ctrl alt del), if you notice anything unusual, go to properties, get the location then end the process and remove it (Only if you’re sure though)

Once you’ve removed it, install a good firewall and do regular system scans, and be careful what you click on! Your virus scanner doesn’t sound very good. One vital thing you have to look out for is the disabling of UAC, this allows the person to perform admin tasks without your consent, if this has been disabled re-enable it now! I’d also suggest you wipe your private data from all browsers so the person can’t grab the data from the browser file. In most cases it’s just

Menu > settings > delete private data

and that’s it. If you’re really struggling either find a white hat forum, or PM me. Or restore your system to a saved restore point (Which is basing on the fact he hasn’t spread the file)
