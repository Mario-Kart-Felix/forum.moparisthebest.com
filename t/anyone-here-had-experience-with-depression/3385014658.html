Original_Daniel July 31, 2016, 4:12pm #22

Basically different parts of your brain will ‘light up’ when in use. When 2 or more parts light up at the same time, they start to associate with each other and light up automatically.

This is how ‘practice makes perfect’ and muscle memory works.

If you’re in a sad mood and then turn on the tv, these parts of your brain will light up. Do it enough times and whenever you turn on the tv you get depressed. Obviously it can work with every action you do, which leads into a downward spiral.

The obvious way to break this is to think positive, which is cliche but it works. Put out good things and make a note of them, no matter how ridiculous. Then the brain starts associating the actions with happy thoughts and overwrites the old ones.

That’s how I got over depression without meds after having it for years.
