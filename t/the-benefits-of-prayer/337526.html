While helping to reduce the mortality from childhood leukemias, Hodgkin’s disease and other rare cancers, chemotherapy has done virtually nothing to cure or prolong survival from big killers like cancers of the lung, breast, colon, rectum and prostate. Well over 50 percent of all cancer patients receive chemotherapy, yet these relatively toxic drugs help only five percent of these cases.
Proponents of chemotherapy counter criticisms by quoting statistics such as 75 percent response or "success" rates with chemotherapy in Stages III-IV of breast cancer, ovarian cancer, prostate cancer and multiple myeloma.

What they do not tell you is that subsequent disease-free survival rates are low to non-existent and that doing nothing may be no worse with respect to actual years of survival.
