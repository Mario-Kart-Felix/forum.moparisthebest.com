Tom August 3, 2016, 5:18pm #7

I never really liked the idea of a server where the staff team is breathing down peoples necks, a lot of the time a player runs up and goes “Tom! Tom! Person A said this to me!” or something that usually involves a player is pointing the finger at another player, so naturally, you go to the supposed offender and then they tell you a completely different story. It just isn’t an effective way of going about report handling.

I believe that for a start the community should be given some ability to deal with their own issues, for example (as mentioned above as well) a fully functioning ignore list. If somebody is being a massive asshole then they’re just going to get ignored by everyone until they get bored and give up. Though I will add that the ignore list should be easy to use and players should be made aware of its functionality (Perhaps an option under the players name or something?).

Secondly, my rule of thumb is this: Don’t enforce what you can fix with code. PVP servers are naturally competitive. I remember a friend of mine played a server where he would kill people and they would come back with just a weapon and cheap shot him, this caused him to get pretty pissy about it. You can avoid problems here by checking what items people have on before they enter the wilderness.

[ol][li]You avoid an annoyed player when they loose their stuff to somebody who just has a weapon. This player will go to the staff, the guy will most probably have no proof, nothing will be done, one more upset member of the community who is now mad at the management (That’s just how their brains work, I don’t know why).[/li]
[li]You avoid revenege by the player using the same dirty tactics, simply because they can’t do it. Period.[/li]
[li]The relationship between the two players is kept positive, the last thing you want is a community where everyone is fighting over something.[/li][/ol]

I also think that it is important you maintain the relationship between management -> community. I don’t believe that moderators should log ingame, teleport to the player who’s been reported, and personally ban him. I think there should be a formal report form and a formal appeal form where the moderators can handle/read reports/ and punish anonymously. The idea of players collecting their own report information is one I don’t like either, most of the time they conveniently miss the part where they provoke the guy they’re reporting.

The issue with a lot of servers (especially PVP) is that when a moderator specifically punishes a member they assume its because they’ve killed them in PVP, or something like that. Just handle it like Jagex does.If a player has an issue, they can appeal and include the issue, it would be up to the administrators to chose the correct staff and ensure the correct process is being followed when it comes to handling them.

Forgive anything I’ve typed wrong here, it’s currently 10AM and I’ve not slept yet
