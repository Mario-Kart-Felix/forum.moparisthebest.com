davidi2 August 4, 2016, 5:43am #33

Like Jc was implying, the more variables in the question, the harder it is to answer…

The mathematical backbone of the question is
“5 die or 1 dies, which do you choose?”

I think everyone can agree you’d pick the 1 in this scenario. But then you start to add more variables…
“5 die or you kill 1, which do you choose?”

It’s suddenly a much more complex and morality based question. A lot of people would just flat out refuse to kill anyone, no matter how many others would die. Some would ask if the 1 was elderly (and/or willing to die), or if the 5 were children or females.

So let’s take it a step further.

2 children, one pregnant mother, a baby, and Osama bin laden die in a train crash, or you have to kill a 6 year old boy (saving the other 5). Or what if both were going to die, and you got to pick one group to save (the boy or the other 5)?
