A group of lives is better than one

General Discussion
TabbyKiller August 4, 2016, 5:37am #1

A group of peoples lives are in danger, a train is speeding towards them not slowing down. You have a choice, push the person in front of you onto the train track making the train come to a screeching halt saving the group of people down the track or don’t push the person in front of you and let the group of people die.

My question is, is it ethically right to take a life to save more. I mean if you think about it, murder is murder and you’re killing someone who has their own life, who has their own family and friends. If you think about it a group of lives to save is better than one but when it boils down to murder, is it right or wrong? I mean if it’s within the law how would you explain it to the victims family? Who knows, maybe the person you just killed was planning on proposing that night, or maybe taking their kid to their sons first baseball game. All these things should rush through your head when deciding and I believe you just wont have the will power to do that, to kill someone using your hands.

As a summary, will you kill one person to save the lives of more people and is it right?
