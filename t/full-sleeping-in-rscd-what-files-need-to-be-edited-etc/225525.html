system July 31, 2016, 12:52am #8

It can all be summarized somewhat as the following:

  1. When you click on an item on the client-side, a packet for this command will be sent to the server. If this item is represented by an InventoryItem that is used for sleeping (sleeping-bag then I assume) on the server-side, the sleeping-proceedure will take place.

  2. You have activated the sleeping-proceedure, and your Player-class on the server-side could hold a reference to a class called for instance ‘Sleeping’. This class generates a random word, or String of text (either if it is something you can calculate, or just simply compare with another String of text). The generated text will be stored in a String-format in the Sleeping-class for your Player-class. Note: If it is for calculation, you might want to store the result as the word in your Sleeping-class instead, for easier management.

  3. The String of text held in your Sleeping-class will be translated into a byte-array, and sent to the client.

  4. When you write a word in the client, and clicks Enter (or whatever it uses), your client sends back another byte-array, that later is translated back, and compared to the word in your Sleeping-class. If both the words are correct, your ‘sleeping-session’ will be done with… If not, then just re-do the whole proceedure (also re-do it if the client requests a new word).

This was just my understanding on how it works by reading some of the above text, aswell as analyzing the matter in my head (logical thinking). But as I havn’t really looked into it yet (the reading and writing of bytes to it), I might be wrong… This was also to give you ideas on how to solve it (the ones of you who already havn’t, that is).

Also, what is written in the box client-side, is just a simple String of text… The client doesn’t know anything, except for how it should show it to you. The client only does what it is told by either you, or the server. The server, however, does not need you to tell what it should do.

Yes, I just looked into this a little bit. It does use images, but I don’t think the server actually sends the images to the client (as I thought you said KO9).
