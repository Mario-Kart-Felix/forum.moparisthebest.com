Most rsps owners will depend on payments (or “donations”) from players keep themselves afloat. However, the typical server will have one of the following:

  1. You send money to the owners paypal, then the owner gives you the item.
  2. You’re presented with a few items and can pick from the list, and then get the item from the owner.
  3. Same scenario as 2, but there’s an auto donation system in play.

However, there is also the system like ikov has. Where you purchase a fixed amount of tokens and then purchase the items with said tokens. Runescape uses the same idea for solomons.

What do you guys think? Do you think that token-based shops are better than paying for the item(s) alone?

It depends on what you’re going for. If you want a lot of money, microtransactions for another type of currency are the way to go. People are more likely to spend money if the things they are buying do not have an exact dollar amount linked to them. That said, my personal opinion has been that anything you can buy with real money shouldn’t have a significant impact on the game. Unfortunately this isnt the best way to make money so people dont do it. But if I was going to spend money it would be purely cosmetic.

Donations are given without return consideration.

If you get something in return for a donation, legally (in the United States at least) you can only call the difference in value a donation. It’s called a quid pro quo donation. If you donate 100 dollars and get a 30 dollar gift, you can only legally say you donated 70 dollars. In the case of these ‘donation shops’, they are valuing their “products” at the listed price, making the difference between the donation and the cost of the item zero. You’re buying an item from a store, not making a donation.
