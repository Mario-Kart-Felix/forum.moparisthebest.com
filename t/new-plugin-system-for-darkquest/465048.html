imthenull August 4, 2016, 12:48am #1

I think it’s safe to say the current plugin system for darkquest is horrid… for those of you who don’t know (which im fairly sure everyone with access to this section does), DarkQuest uses a thread-per-action approach in it’s plugin handler, within the plugins you can find a lot of nasty stuff including, but not limited to, Thread.sleep(), yuck! I truly find the infinite amount of threads (even though they’re reused) disgusting.

With that being said, I thought of ways to redesign the plugin system…

The thing I hate most about developing plugins (or any game content for that matter) in all RSCD sources is Java’s verbosity. Namely the classes DelayedEvent, and MenuHandler. I find that this issue can be resolved with implementing a scripting language with lambda capabilities as the primary content handler such as Jython, or JRuby

The next issue (of which I started the topic with), is the infinite amount of threads, and sleeping they do! After browsing some topics here on MoparScape, I found a post by super_, which introduced me to continuations. This, this, this, will be a life saver… implementing continuations in DarkQuest would improve it’s efficiency by 200% (a little over reacting, but you get my point).

What are y’alls opinions on these ideas?
