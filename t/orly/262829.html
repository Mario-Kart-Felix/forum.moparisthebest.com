Yes shadow is cut off.
Either he is bending backwards to look at the camera or you fucked up with perspective.
Halfway agree about the light. The bright spot is a reflection of the light, but still where it seems the light would be the shadow is not.
Also, camera flash was used (I’m assuming from the other shadows in the scene) yet there’s no shadow from that.
Compositing is also, not, just using grain to make both match. There’s a LOT more to it that I think you would really benefit from. Research it. It’ll help.

You need references. And not just a picture you took. Get the size of the table and model it exactly. Get the measurements from the camera lens to the table. Write down the aperture, focal length, shutter speed, everything. It all has to match.

The measurements of the table and the distance from camera will almost completely solve the perspective problems.

I rate believability in three levels. 0, 5, or 10.
0 - Instant recognizability as fake.
5 - Looks real. But any inspection and it doesn’t.
10 - Mind fuck.

You get a 2.5, so I kinda rate in four levels now
