davidi2 August 4, 2016, 10:28am #34

I think the part Vain isn’t grasping when we think it’s a bad idea is that there’s a difference between abusing your power and using it incorrectly. Someone given locking power with an award doesn’t necessarily have to abuse their power to use it incorrectly, locking topics that maybe don’t need to be locked because they have bad judgement on that front. Does that mean they need to lose the award they earned because of it? No, it shouldn’t really. The problem with the suggestion is that there really is just no correlation at all between getting an award and being trusted/able to know what topics to lock in a board. The only arguments I see are:

  1. It helps reduce the workload on moderators.

    • Does it? If you give someone the ability to lock just because they are good at drawing or java, can you trust them to lock the correct topics all the time? No not really, and you end up having to have actual moderators double check the locks the person makes.

  2. It gives badges another purpose.

  • Do they need another purpose? Right now they do exactly what they are designed to do, and I don’t think there’s any reason to change the definition of a badge here.
