ThAt0n36Uy July 30, 2016, 6:38pm #22 
      Because I like to get involved in criticizing 'signture's, I'm going to give my two cents. And I'm sure no one wants to hear me go on and on about how horrible it looks, and why it shouldn't exist, and someone needs to give Unid H4x0r professional PhotoShop lessons. But I think I'll keep it short. 

      It's absolutely hideous, all of them, and each variation is worse. There is too much going on as exps said. You have your background, which, if it were to be revamped a little, and used in another signature, it might look decent. But with this, the colors are way too close together on the color scale, it's mostly warm colors. With the second and third variation you have these Abstract renders or something, I can't tell, but a green and red, it's ugly. The abstract renders cover up the actual main render. The suposed focal point of your pieces. For the abstract renders to be green and red on an already horrid background, it looks like, and I'm not kidding, vomit.

Back to the background, you have it brushed completely wrong, the background is used to support the render, it’s flow is the most important part of a background, you must (but not always) have the background in unison with the render, to make it all look as one. When someone says blend, you don’t place a glow or a drop shadow. You lightly brush on the render, you could smudge, or even use some abstract render. But the render needs to stay visible, and have some degree of contrast, now that may conflict with what I said earlier about the signature needing to be in unison, but you want the render to stand out somewhat. It’s what the signature is about, not your name, not the website, but the image, the image represents your view. You chose that image for the signature.

You should use some tutorials, I suggest a text effect, blending, abstract-grunge signature, and a signature flow tutorial, if you can’t find what I’m talking about, google them.

3/10
