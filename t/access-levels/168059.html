Well, this helped me a lot, and I haven’t found another one as “short and sweet” online, so I decided to share it.

This is from a book I have, it describes the different visibilities (or whatever you want to call it) of methods, variables, etc. in Java.

[table]
[tr]
[td]Visibility[/td][td]public[/td][td]protected[/td][td]default*[/td][td]private[/td]
[/tr]
[tr]
[td]From the same class[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]Yes[/td]
[/tr]
[tr]
[td]From any class in the same package[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]No[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]No[/td]
[/tr]
[tr]
[td]From any class outside the package[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]No[/td][td]No[/td][td]No[/td]
[/tr]
[tr]
[td]From a subclass in the same package[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]No[/td]
[/tr]
[tr]
[td]From a subclass outside the same package[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]Yes[/td][td]No[/td][td]No[/td]
[/tr]
[/table]

*Default being where you do not declare public, protected or private infront of a method or variable name.

[size=8pt]From Sams Teach Yourself Java 6 in 21 Days[/size]

Again, I just thought it might help some of you, I know most of you probably already know all of this stuff, but I know I had a hard time grasping this concept early on.
