The_Un-Named August 4, 2016, 12:52am #2

If you’re not going to get a second video card within a year, don’t bother aiming for sli compatibility. Mid to High end gfx cards are usually supported 3-4~ years if you want to stay in the loop, but assuming you don’t then you can get away with 6 years for a high end card. My old PC had a 9800gtx and I’d still be using that today if it didn’t start dying on me, the 9800GTX being a 4~ year old card which was essentially a re-branded 8800GTS, a 5 year old card this November.

What am I getting at here? Unless you opt to go for sli within a year, better graphics cards will come out for cheaper and if you go the sli route you’ll be paying about the same for similar performance but will be paying out the ass in electric to run the damn thing.
