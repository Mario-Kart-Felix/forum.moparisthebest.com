quux July 30, 2016, 7:28am #13

quux here - freenode staff. Couple of things:

  1. The DCC exploit some folks have been using is a problem with some home routers, not IRC clients. We’re hearing that some (not all) Netgear and LinkSys vx-based routers are the main problems. See http://www.milw0rm.com/exploits/1551 for a better explanation. Basically those routers will briefly drop the irc connection when they see the DCC SEND anylongrandomstringhere in a plaintext port 6667 context. The cure: update router firmware, or connect to IRC via any port other than 6667 (8001 is a good one).

  2. We at Freenode do understand that a community can’t be responsible for every action of every member. But we also know that communities form around common interests, behaviours, attitudes. We know that good communities reinforce the behaviours they are proud of, and shun the the behaviours they dislike. Every community member’s actions are a reflection on the community as a whole, so think: are your actions helping your community get a good rep? Or a bad one?

  3. It’s way too early to think about shutting down channels. But guys, let’s be real. If we (Freenode staff) keep seeing multiple people from #mopar community joining other channels and starting antisocial behaviour on cue … then seeing folks in the mopar channels laughing about how funny or cool that was … we’re gonna start making connections. And you’ll be seeing more of us.

That’s not meant as a threat - it’s just the way things are. Freenode is a community of over 20k people who believe in the principles you can find at thise links:

http://freenode.net/catalysts.shtml
http://freenode.net/philosophy.shtml

…so help us keep Freenode great, eh?
