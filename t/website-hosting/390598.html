If you don’t use the default password while using XAMPP you should be fine, but if you’re creating a website that uses SQL then I suggest using

mysql_real_escape_string($username); mysql_real_escape_string($password);

md5(serialize(base64_encode(mysql_real_escape_string($username))); md5(serialize(base64_encode(mysql_real_escape_string($password)));

Tom August 1, 2016, 11:09am #13

Well it depends what you have available, I have a PC dedicated to hosting in another room, I run Linux only on it. Then I have a laptop for actually browsing etc (Which also uses Linux). I’ve successfully hosted a site in the past over a long period of time. It also depends on what skills you have available, I ran into a ton of problems over a stretched period of time.

If you can’t get used to fixing your own things and have experience and patience then I doubt you’d get far. I prefer using my PC because it is a lot more comfortable knowing I have complete control, it’s also easier for me and appears to run a lot faster for other people. I simply don’t have the money to dish out on premium hosting. If you intend to host over a long period of time and truly wanted to go far don’t go with Windows.

You would only need to look into premium hosting (Which would cost) if your site became very big. As for me, I’m more than happy with my home hosting. You could just look for upgrades to your current computer but I think under that circumstance you may be better of just going premium for the sake of time and money.
