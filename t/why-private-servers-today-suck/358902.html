theUnforgiven July 31, 2016, 7:08pm #1

Disclaimer: This isn’t a complaint about the moparscape community, so please read on before locking this thread.

Right now if you check the moparscape status page, the majority of “popular” servers are plain delta-scape sources with no originality except for the name. You may refer to these as “Leeches” or “Delta-Leeches”.

The general consensus among these servers, besides the poorly-written lines of code, are to extract money from the consumer(you). Many of these staff members are personal friends of the person who started the server and are often immature and often don’t understand that “Great Power comes with Great Responsibility”- Spider Man.

This is why I disagree with the idea of paying to advertise on the moparscape status page, the only way that these servers can get their initial investment back is to constantly update their game with only one thing in mind: profit.

Any “actually” good servers get pushed to the side because the owner often doesn’t have the resources to host/promote their server. And then the server just becomes another lost-cause.

I understand that the earnings from the status page go to support good servers that are written off bases like Hyperion and RSDaemon and 317 serv, but how many of these servers serve a practical use beside the constant awe they receive for their well-written lines of code.

It’s like how part of the money from every pack of cigarettes you buy goes to health-care for kids. It really doesn’t make sense.

So the question is: How do we give more incentive to the working class seasoned-java guru and less incentive to the leecher-skid that uses his parent’s credit card to host/promote their pathetic excuse of a servercough delta-leech

I personally believe in starting a network between the real programmers and the skids that can host/promote so we get the best of both worlds (ideally). Of course moparscape could get a cut from this to help keep the site running, and to “supposedly” pay people to make good servers.

It just might not be lucrative, but then again private-servers were made in first place for fun not for profit.
