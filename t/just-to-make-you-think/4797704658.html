mike4ever:

So we as humans live, learn and “die”. Well how can we be sure that anything we know it true?

We can’t be “sure” (although I feel that’s a poor word to use - perhaps a better use of semantics would be “certainty”) that something is true or not. Many claim to be sure about certain aspects of the world, but it’s simply an irrational take. Science has mapped out all of its knowledge into a concept known as a “best fit model” of reality. For example, if you walk in a room with a table in it, then you leave, how do you know the table hasn’t flown out of the room. Can you be sure it hasn’t? Maybe it took a trip to the moon, then returns to its exact spot right before you enter the room. You see, these things are clearly irrational. BUT, they are not impossible to be true. They are what we call an non-falsifiable hypothesis; it’s simply not possible to falsify whether or not it’s true - but rational people don’t believe those things without evidence. So since we can’t be certain or “sure” of these things, we say it isn’t true because there’s no evidence - but really, this is just our best fit model of reality. Maybe future evidence will appear that this table theory is true, and our best fit model of reality will shift.
