Alright, this is something I have wanted for a long time, and today Brent and I sat down and thought it out. A base ten time system. We have base ten measurements of distance, mass, pressure and all the values related: area, volume, velocity. So why does time remain base twelve? It is silly.

Okay, the month/year situation still needs a bit of work, but when dealing with 364.25 days it is a bit difficult to get things perfect.
Any suggestions for improvement are very welcome.

My plan from here is to make an actual clock that works in base ten. It will have to be digital to make the mechanism click over every 0.864 of a second instead of every ‘normal’ second, and reconfigured to click around 3.6degrees instead of 6degrees. I would love to make an analog version to hang on my wall, but it would still need a digital component to vary the ‘normal’ function to my new one.
One day my dears, one day.

NB:
d=days, h=hours, m=minutes, s=seconds (very simple really).
A ‘1’ following a d, h, m or s indicates a value used at present. A ‘current’ value.
A ‘2’ following a d, h, m or s indicated a proposed value. A ‘future’ value.

Okay
d1=24h1=1440m1=86400s1
d2=10h2=1000m2=100000s2

BUT d1 must=d2 so therefore
86400s1 must =100000s2
864s1=1000s2
s2=0.864s1
A new second is 0.864 old seconds.

m2=100s2
h2=100m2
d2=10h2

Now, given m2=100s2 and s2=0.864s1
m2=100x0.864s1
m2=86.4s1
(divide by 60 to put into m1, 60s1=m1)
m2=1.44m1
A new minute is 1.44 old minutes.

Now, given h2=100m2 and m2=1.44m1
h2=100x1.44m1
h2=144m1
(divide by 60 to put into h1, h1=60m1)
h2=2.4h1
A new hour is 2.4 old hours.

Now, given d2=10h2 and h2=2.4h1
d2=10x2.4h1
d2=24h1
(divide by 24 to put into d1, d1=24h1)
d2=d1
A new day had the same value as an old day.

Q.E.D.

Further more, on the matter of years.

If we make one week 5d2 (which is the same as 5d1 because d1=d2) instead of 7d1 then we can tidy up the months.

Instead of 3 years of 364 days (7x31, 4x30, 1x28) and one year of 365 days (7x31, 4x30, 1x29) I propose 3 years of 364 days (8x40, 1x44) and one year of 365 days (8x40, 1x45). A total of 9 instead of 12 months, and I suggest we name them with direction from carbon compounds, Ethember, Methember, Propember, Butember, Pentember, Hexember, Heptember, Octember, Nonember which gets rid of all that silliness whereby SEPtember is the 9th month instead of the 7th etc.

This means that instead of 7x4.429weeks, 4x4.286weeks and 1x4weeks for 3 years of 4 and 7x4.429weeks, 4x4.286weeks and 1x4.143weeks for the final year we will have 8x8weeks and 1x8.8weeks for three of four years and 8x8weeks and 1x9weeks for the fourth. Much neater.
