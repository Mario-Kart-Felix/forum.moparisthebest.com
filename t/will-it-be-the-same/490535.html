davidi2 August 4, 2016, 3:33am #1

Will it?

We will never have the excitements of the old school good updates, wondering what will come next. The community is not the same. We have experienced it all already. Will it feel the same experiencing it again?

I’m not talking about those hours or days of wonder when you first start the game, I mean the feeling of accomplishment as you strive to better your account. Completing quests. Leveling up. Making money.

What are your goals? Why will you be playing? I’m sure some of you know, but I don’t. I played back in the day for the community, paying for two memberships so that I could use my brothers old account to advertise my level 70 construction house parties, where people had fun interacting and exploring the funny bugs and glitches. Will this still exist? Or have the minds of those that continued playing already been broken? I’m sure some of you know that house parties are all but extinct in the modern day RS… some would blame distractions with other content. I blame the shrinking player base and the new highly competitive nature of the community (aside from PKing, of course). How popular will this version be? I think as some people realize this is not what they imagined (not saying a majority or anything though), it will start to slow down as well.

I can assure you it will not be the same. I’m depressed. Knowing that what I want is impossible. But then I realize… we can make the most of it. I realize… I know some parts of the game will still be fun! I can train my Magic, it’s my favorite skill besides Construction, and then use it in Castle wars! I can relive my quests, knowing it wont be the same as doing them the first time, but I can laugh at how incompetent I was when doing underground pass with less than 30 agility.

It will not be the same, but we can make the most of it. And through it all, I think the most important part is not what Jagex decides to do, it’s what we do to make it fun.
