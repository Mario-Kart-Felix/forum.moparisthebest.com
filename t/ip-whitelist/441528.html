Kryptix August 3, 2016, 2:33am #1

After running RSCE for years I’ve seen so many people get hacked, lose their items and then quit private servers completely because they cannot be bothered to earn their stuff back or whatever. They probably got hacked because they used an easy password, used the same password on another private server, used a bot or something else that could be prevented.

On RSCE we have a recovery system in place so they can recover their account via recovery questions but that doesn’t mean much when they’ve lost their entire bank.

I was thinking of a IP Whitelist system where basically it won’t let them login in-game unless their IP is on the whitelist. To add your current IP you simply login to the website and enter your recovery questions which will add the IP you’re behind to your accounts whitelist. You can remove IP’s from the list by simply clicking remove.

It’s an optional feature that they will have to enable. Without enabling it you wouldn’t know it’s there.

Can anyone see any flaws in this? Is there any way to spoof an IP or anything through Java?

It’s kind of like a bank pin system I guess, but I’d hate having to enter a bank pin every single time I login.

I suppose you could enter a bank pin which then remembers the IP, and it only asks for a bank pin if it’s a new IP? Still though I wanted to leave the game untouched so wanted to keep everything done via the website.

You wouldn’t understand unless you have seen what I have seen over the years.

You might think I’m exaggerating but I’m not; a player signs up using the same login credentials they used on a previous PK server and spends a few weeks/months setting up their character with items, PK supplies and then all of a sudden someone has changed access to their character, stolen all their items and distributed them to multiple people whilst the original account holder is left deflated thinking it’s everyone’s fault except his own and in turn protests but quitting the game completely. This is a negative for the game in more way than one as not only have we lost a player but all his items are now spread around the economy devaluing everything else. If you do this thousands and thousands of times you end up with a crowded economy.

I’ve seen it literally thousands of times. It’s near on impossible to track items whilst the game is online and live so when you inform them there’s nothing that can be done they simply stop playing. The most common ways a user gains access to another players account are:

[ul][li]Player uses a basic/obvious password when signing up[/li]
[li]Player uses the same password that they used on a previous private server (which had their database leaked or whatever)[/li]
[li]Player uses a bot with a basic password sender when logging in[/li]
[li]Player plays another private server which turns out to be a RAT[/li][/ul]
Now I’m not familiar with RAT’s and viruses because I haven’t ever really played with them but I can’t imagine any of the things I mentioned being able to get around the system I’m planning without being purpose built for it.

Yes it’s quite annoying having to login to the website and enter your recovery questions every time you’re behind a new IP but ultimately it takes under 30 seconds to do and most people have static IP now. If they can’t be bothered to do so and they get hacked it’s their own fault, it’s an optional feature but you can’t help people that won’t help themselves.
