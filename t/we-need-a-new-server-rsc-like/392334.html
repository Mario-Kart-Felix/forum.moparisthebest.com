Motaro August 1, 2016, 11:25am #20

I’m honestly surprised that nobody has taken an approach similar to the way Jagex did it since Reines and RSCD.

I mean think about how RSC was when you played compared to how the servers you play now are…

-You weren’t harassed by mods every half an hour forcing you to talk.

-You didn’t move around with portals to get where you wanted; you walked places if you couldn’t teleport.

-Weapons/Armors had quest requirements, not just stat requirements.

-There was no (tremendously annoying) global chat.

-Armor went to rune, and there were only two dragon armor pieces. Although on this one, I think servers with custom armors or weapons have had a 100% fail rate in the past, so this one is a giveaway. Same with NPCs, etc.

-You couldn’t change your own stats.

This was the game people played years ago. This was the game people remember when they see ads advertising RSC servers.

This is not what servers have to offer.

I know it’s not a huge list, but what’s on it (in my opinion) are some pretty big game-altering updates that don’t give players that sense that they are playing RSC again, but rather some RSC spin-off.

For example, you log onto RSCE and do what? You go straight to a portal to be put somewhere on the map without any work whatsoever, and then talk to an NPC to fill your inventory full of food. It’s not RSC.

RSC was challenging; [current] RSC servers are not.

You had to work long and hard for stuff in the old day, but now you don’t.
