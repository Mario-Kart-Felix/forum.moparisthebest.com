What’s better, an addict stealing to support a habit, or an addict that has a government-sponsored habit who is leading a proper life?

Actually, you are saying that, because you are saying “drugs” are addictive. Not all drugs are addictive. Weed is not addictive. There is no differentiation between “mental” and “physical” addiction like Freevibe would have you believe - the brain is physical after all. When people say “mental addiction” they are referring to habituation, which is not limited to drugs at all. In this case, I am mentally addicted to the internet, the internet is therefore dangerous and should be banned. Addiction is a recordable, chemical occurance that only a certain amount of drugs cause, certainly not all of them.

As Pault has already said, “people need money to get them” is really an argument for legalisation. I forgot the numbers, but it was figured out for a BBC report that, if heroin was legalised and controlled by the government, the NHS would be able to produce an unbelievable amount very cheaply, simply because they have much more resources available to them than illegal drug dealers. There would be no need to turn to crime.

If you take nothing else from what I say, at least understand this: I do not want drugs legalised because I believe they are safe, I want them legalised because I recognise that they are dangerous. In the current state, a dangerous market is in the hands of criminals. It dosen’t matter what anybody says, I believe and will always believe, that a dangerous market is better off in the hands of regulated authority than criminals.
