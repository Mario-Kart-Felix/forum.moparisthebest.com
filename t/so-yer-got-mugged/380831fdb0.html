Only read the first page of this thread at the time of writing. I have some mugging stories.

I’ve lived in this area for about 10 years, it’s quite a bad area in the inner city with much deprivation. When I was around 14 and walking home from school wearing school uniform is when muggings happened. Here are some of my memories.

The first time it happened I was in a nice-looking street which was next to a rough estate. A was walking along and a guy says ‘hey’. I turn around and see a black guy in a hood wearing a balaclava. He tells me to keep walking, to ‘be cool’ (which I believe is a command to keep calm) and to ‘stop making it hot’. He taps my pockets and demands my mobile phone. I didn’t have one then. He notices my watch (a crappy plastic one) and requests it. I stall a little, pretend I didn’t hear, have slow movements. At that point he notices a woman pushing a child in a buggy and walks away quickly.

Once I got mugged the same day twice.
The first time was at the entrance of a park I was about to walk through. Two guys approach and one of them asks if I have money. When I say no he complains that kids these days don’t have any money and leaves.
Further along that journey, a short guy approaches and orders my pockets turned out. Upon learning I have nothing of value (I never carried anything then partly due to muggings) he threatens to stab me. We were standing close to some houses and at that point a tall built man opens his front door and demands to know what’s going on. The mugger leaves.

A few days later, the guy who mugged me at the entrance of the park approached me again. I recognised him but he didn’t remember me.

I’ve never had any property lost to muggers. It was mostly due to them that I didn’t buy a mobile phone until I was 16 (and didn’t wear school uniform anymore). I reckon muggers see people in school uniform as good targets.
So here’s some thoughts I’ve had due to my experiences.

Its irrational to be afraid of dark places if you think they’ll make muggings more likely. Thieves will go for people in broad daylight. A policeman once told me most reports they get happen right after schools finish, not night time. I reckon muggers are afraid more of witnesses than light.

If you think someone is following you, don’t speed up your walk. It seems people are more likely to go for you if they think you’re scared. Instead slow down. Especially if you’re in a situation where you’ve just turned off a main road into a side street. If you speed up by the time they catch you you’ll be far away from the main road thus witnesses. Also if you walk slowly and maybe even stop for a few seconds you might appear confident and awesome.

If walking late at night and worried. Walk right in the center of the road. You should be right in the glare of streetlights and have empty space all around you. If someone approaches you can have at least a few seconds warning. Cars are rare at night and you can just move out their way.

I haven’t been mugged ever since I stopped wearing school uniform. In fact once or twice I’ve walked down the street with over a thousand pounds in my (inside) coat pocket. Okay the first time I ever did that I stuffed the envelope under my belt and tucked my shirt over it.
