I’m sorry but the argument that scamming should exist for the prevention of scamming is a terrible one. That’s like saying it’s okay to rob people at gunpoint because it teaches them to stay in their homes, or many other examples. To put it simply, scamming is nothing short of stealing. RS gold can be exchanged for real currency, which means it has real monetary value. If you steal 1 billion gold from a friend, it’s the mathematical equivalent of going and stealing around $500 from them. If you can justify stealing, then you have your own issues to deal with.

Adding some pathos in, some people spend significant time earning their way on a game. Some people may be able to earn 100m in a week, whereas other may be able to only earn 500k. That 500k is priceless to them. There’s a lot of idiots that will say that “hey, it’s just pixels,” yet they fail to realize that behind the graphics is the hours and efforts of a person. The same applies to money. It’s just paper, but it has value relative to the amount of effort you spent getting it. Scammers do not spend time earning money but, rather, spend time taking others’. They are robbing people of their dedication to a game for their own gain.
