I guess I should input something on this considering everyone else is.

   I [i]partly[/i] agree with David on the fact that the entire front page looks like a blog, but realistically, if that is going to be used as a mainstream way to display announcements there aren't a huge amount of ways to go about doing it. If there was simply a link to a blog, very few people would click it unless it had a noticeable way of saying there is a new blog post. I feel like having it like R-L's homepage, with a module for it, wouldn't be very effective either. My reasoning for that is the fact that pictures, no matter how minute, will draw attention away from text. The more pictures, the more distractions there will be.

   I'm not saying that the way it is laid out now is bad, but it could be improved on. At the moment, it looks very generic and boring (no offense, but it really does). I understand it may be updated soon, but for the time being it isn't very impressive. Also, I can't think of a blog being very practical for this site. There is already a section dedicated to announcements, and thats quite effective on its own (as you can tell by this thread). The only other practical use I can see for it is using it for less important or even section-specific news, such as programming contests or other things of the like. 

   It is definitely a step forward, but it does need to be worked on. Perhaps add a side bar for navigation instead of one at the top if at all possible. Also, use a different font for the text. I know it sounds really stupid, but using a font such as Calibri is more attractive to the eye and looks nicer as a whole. The text should also be centered instead of positioned more to the left as it is. A larger margin would look much nicer, along with a smaller font size as well.

I don’t mean to sound nit-picky, but those are a few of my opinions on the matter that I feel may help a bit.
