lothy August 4, 2016, 6:04am #5

Absolutely stupid business move. As a company, they’re in the sunset stage. Worse still, they’re a one-game-wonder.

What they should be doing is coming up with games (or content for existing games) that’s actually fun and exciting to play. I’ve read company reviews by their employees which essentially criticise management for not knowing when to drop a piece of content. They’ll test their content, the tester will say it’s not fun to play… but they’ll steamroll ahead anyway.

The other thing their employees are critical of is that developers of non-runescape projects are coerced to be ‘more like Runescape’. With this being the case, it’s little wonder that their projects keep failing – runescape itself is failing. More runescape, as per senior management’s requests, is the last thing they should be doing.

EDIT: To clarify my position: There are no accessibility issues with the current game that could be considered a dam holding would-be players back from the game. In short, there’s little to gain by moving away from Java at this stage, especially for such a mature product. If they want to survive into 2015 and beyond then they should be looking to introduce a new and innovative game.
The Evolution of Combat was shit, plain and simple. They’ve taken the notion of keybindings and so on from WoW (and most other MMORPGs that followed) and produced what is essentially a really slow-paced equivalent.
Additionally, it takes too much time to reach the end-game. And even when you do, you’re looking at hundreds of hours of shit work to obtain the top armour… If you don’t have the top armour, you basically can’t function at a high level anymore.
At the end of the day, this means that it’s a kids game as far as competitive play goes… Oh, and adults like myself who started playing a decade ago. Even for kids though, there probably aren’t enough hours in the day to get the best equipment and maxed skills.

tl;dr: Waste of time to move to HTML 5. Technological accessibility is not an issue, but playability is. The ‘buy-in’ is too high (hundreds of hours) in terms of leveling, and the same can be said of obtaining items.
