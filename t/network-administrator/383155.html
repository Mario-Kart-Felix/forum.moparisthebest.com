tL August 1, 2016, 7:41am #2

Look for Cisco qualifications. You’ll be starting on CCNA and the later exploration courses are pretty good, the early CCNA 1 - 2 discovery courses are a bit of a waste of time for most people who are interested since all they teach are networking fundamentals which you’ll likely already know (Ethernet Encapsulation, etc).

In my experience from knowing people in the industry, looking at jobs in the industry and doing a bit of contract work, Cisco qualifications have been a decent thing to have and quite valuable when you get into the CCNP. They do expire every 4 years (or maybe a bit less), though.

It seems so, Newty. It would be better to wait towards the end of your college course, just so that you know what you do and don’t know. Because there are many Cisco qualifications that start from very basic shit like computer systems and you can safely skip them and not be judged negatively by an employer for doing so, if they know their cisco stuff.

The ones you linked to seem to be again, most of the fundamentals, OSI, TCP/IP, etc that you’ll likely be learning on your course so maybe you could try and skip ahead and look for the more specialised qualifications. I wouldn’t personally know where to look for them as I had the course organised for me and was taught it.
