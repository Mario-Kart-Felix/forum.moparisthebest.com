I REALLY need to test myself and learn some new things, so I’ve started a new adventure for me.

Ok so here’s what’s going on. I’m modeling my bathroom. The entire thing hopefully. And I need to stay focused on this. I’ve measured out most of the things in my bathroom and I’m modeling them in real scale to keep it easy for reference. I plan on modeling every detail and treating each object as it’s own separate project so I put my highest quality work into it. Who knows how long this will take. But in the end I want a beauty render, and if I feel like it a realistic dirt and grime style render.

All modeling in C4D and I’ll do the rendering in Maxwell MOST likely. If that’s too heavy of a strain on my computer I might just do it in VRay. Since I realized when modeling I have a VERY small and tight bathroom the angle will be kinda odd and wide. I’m using the equivalent of a 35mm camera for rendering, and since I have my own DSLR with an equivalent lens I should be able to take a picture and have it very similar.

So far I’ve got the correct dimensions of the room laid out. There is another room for the tub and toilet which you cannot see right now because of the door. The door is what I’m doing first. I’ve got the molding around it and the door itself done, and maybe by the end of the day I’ll have the knob and hinges on it. In the final render the door will be open leading to the other room so the hinges should be done accurately. If they come out really good I might even rig the door and have it in a library of objects so I can use it later on.

Here is what has to be done. (Loose order)

[ul][li]Resources (about five minutes)[/li][li]Door/Frame (about ten minutes)[/li][li]Hinges/Knob (about an hour)[/li][li]Floorboard Molding (less than ten minutes)[/li][li]Counter and Cabinets (about forty minutes)[/li][li]Counter top (about ten minutes)[/li][li]Sink (about forty minutes)[/li][li]Toilet (about two hours with breaks)[/li][li]Bath tub (about thirty minutes)[/li][li]Curtain (at least forty minutes)[/li][li]Toiletries (about forty minutes total)[/li][li]Lights (about twenty minutes total)[/li][li]Epic material run (In process)[/li][/ul]

Hardest and most time consuming will probably be the toilet. Everything else is pretty straightforward for me.

Anyways enough babble. Here is where I’m at right now.

Most amount of time I’ve ever spent on a door.

C&C Welcome. Or some insight into this entire thing, or other ideas.
I expect this to be something I’ll continue to work on for more than a day, maybe weeks.
This should be my best piece ever considering the time I’ll put into it.

The door was VERY easy.
Cube with two segments up and down.
Select the four segments on the front from that, and then inner extrude without preserve groups on. This will give you the four inset things. Then it’s just a matter of bevel after bevel to get to that part.
