3 miles underneath the ocean, fibre-optic cables run along the sea bed, providing the world with around 97% of its network.

Mean while, on a boat in the Pacific, men wait on 24/7 standby for a phone to ring. If it rings, it means a network provider has lost communications and they need the men to repair the under-water cables.

The cables get severed because the seabed floor is constantly collapsing due to the tetonic plates moving, and underwater volcanoes erupting etc. (this happens all the time).

Every time this happens, it costs the men half a million dollars to repair the cables. Ever had your ISP go down for a few hours? It’s more than likely that the cables that provide you with communications across the world have been consumed by the seabed floor.

Richard Hammond told me that.
