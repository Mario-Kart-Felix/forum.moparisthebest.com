jme August 1, 2016, 1:37pm #38

I am a phone/dsl technician. They will train me on fibre optics eventually. And if I wanted I could probably go into marine cables but that doesnt interest me.

I wouldn’t blame marine troubles when your internet goes out though. In a small region with about 30k people in the area, we get at least 150 calls per day which require repairs on the city lines.

Many of our orders are called eagle orders, which are troubles spotted by the ISP and not reported by the customer. Phone is not so fussy with the condition of your line. But for internet the conditions need to be perfect if you’re on copper lines. There are measurements we take and they have to meet standards for dsl to work. voltage, loop current, loop loss, power influence, noise metallic, real balance, stress, longitudinal balance, resistance, capacitance, etc
