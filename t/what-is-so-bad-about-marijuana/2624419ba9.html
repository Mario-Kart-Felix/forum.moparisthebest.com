bowserjr2:

it will take more than a month with no use before tetrahydrocannabinol will go out your body. You can get psychosis and other psychic damage.

You’re a little confused. delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol does NOT stay in your body for months. It is metabolised fairly quickly to 11-hydroxy-thc (amongst other active metabolites) and then those are further broken down into inactive metabolites which are stored in fat cells. They no longer have any psychoactive effect on the human body, they do however provide a way for people to tell if you’ve smoked recently (piss tests/ blood tests etc).

Pretty much every chemical you consume is broken down this way and has some form of inactive metabolite that stays in your body for some amount of time (usually a lot shorter, but it makes no difference)

EDIT: Anotehr example of the wonders that is in vivo metabolism: when one smokes tobacco (or ingests nicotine in any other way) a primary metabolite of that process is cotinine, which is detectable in the body for days after the cigarette was smokes - dosen’t stop cravings though, does it? Metabolites are not the same as the drug that was consumed.

TLDR:
11-nor-9-carboxy-thc, one of the long half-life secondary metabolites has NO PSYCHOACTIVE EFFECT. Once you are not stoned anymore, you are not stoned anymore.
