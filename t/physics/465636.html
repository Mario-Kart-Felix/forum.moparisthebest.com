A yard engine shunts a freight car along a level siding. If the car stops in 50.0 seconds, 250m from the point where it was released by the engine, calculate the initial velocity of the car when it was released.

If someone could walk me through how to solve this problem, that would be really helpful.

justaguy August 4, 2016, 12:54am #2

Recognize it? It’s one of the 5 kinematic equations for uniform motion. This is what we are going to use to solve the problem.

Given:

∆t = 50.0s
∆d = 250m
v[sub]f[/sub] = 0 m/s

In order to fulfill the above equation, we need a final velocity (v[sub]f[/sub] or v[sub]2[/sub]), which is obviously 0 because the car stops.

RTF: v[sub]i[/sub] = ?

Sol[sup]n[/sup]: Now that some variables have been established, we need to rearrange the equation to solve for v[sub]i[/sub]:

∆d = ((v[sub]i[/sub]+ v[sub]f[/sub]) / 2) * ∆t

Since v[sub]f[/sub] is 0, simply ignore it and keep rearranging.

∆d = (v[sub]i[/sub] / 2) * ∆t
2∆d = v[sub]i[/sub] * ∆t
(2∆d) / ∆t = v[sub]i[/sub]

Therefore,

v[sub]i[/sub] = (2*∆d) / ∆t
= (2 * 250m) / 50.0s
= 10 m/s

The car’s initial velocity is 10 m/s.
