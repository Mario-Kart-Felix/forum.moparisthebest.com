Method 3: Brushing

Description: The brushing method is used to highlight key areas, and gives it a radient lumminecence. It works great for water, or anything elce that you need to add brighter highlights to. Note: This tutorial is more advanced than the others. This adds a nice affect but is a bit harder.

Dificulty: Intermediate

Step one: Open up your stock. I am using the water and rocks pic from Method 2.

Step two: Create a new layer overtop of the stock. (Ctrl+Shift+N)

Step three: Take out your brush tool. (It can sometimes be hidden under the pencil tool.) Make sure you have the “Basic Brushes” Pack selected, (Default setting), and chose the soft brush, at 30 pixels. (You can make it smaller or larger deppending on what size your canvas is.)

Step four: Brush over the layers to be highlighted, and don’t worry about how it looks now, it will end up looking better.

Step five: Set the top layers blending mode to soft light , and you can see that instantaniously there is a luminecent glow over the brushed areas. If you see some areas that the brush goes off course, use the SOFT (0% hardness) eraser to take some of it out.

Step six: If the water seems to luminecent, or you don’t want as much of an effect, tone it down a bit by lowering the opacity. Also, for best results, use a gaussian blur in the background. (Find out how from my first tutorial.)
