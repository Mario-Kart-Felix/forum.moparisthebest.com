Not really.
Try reading up on the order of operations, it comes in handy in places just like this.

4 * (567) * 89 * (78 + 890 ) * 6 / 100

First, you look at the parenthesis and solves those. So you’ll end up with:
4 * 567 * 89 * 968 * 6 / 100

Then, it’s simply a matter of going across from left to right as the rest is multiplication and division.
2268 * 89 * 968 * 6 / 100
Then:
201852 * 968 * 6 / 100
Then:
195392736 * 6 / 100
Then:
1172356416 / 100

Your answer:
11723564.16

Actually, the order of operations is entirely correct. This isn’t to say that it is the correct way of solving an equation in order, as there is no correct way of doing that, hence why parenthesis were made. If you were to say PEMDAS always applies, you’re wrong. If you say to solve a problem using the order of operations, PEMDAS is, and always will be, correct. However, if it specifies a different hierarchy of operators, you’d be using that one (obviously). To say PEMDAS is wrong shows a complete lack of understanding of why it was made in the first place.
