Immortal July 30, 2016, 4:26am #6

Ok I’ll be nice and won’t be too harsh. Just several improvement points:

  1. There is no focal point in your sig, what are we focusing most on in your sig? Your render or your background… think about it

  2. You need some kind of light source or some lighting effects to lighten up the main part of your sig (the render).

  3. Blending - your render needs to be ‘fused’, if you will, into the background slightly better than a simple fade effect on the hair. Try using a simple brush with scattering to overlap your render and the background and erase parts you think overlap the render too much.

  4. Your text needs a bit of work to increase its perfection and flow into the signature. Try using a font that flows with the sig and try to blend it in with a slight soft light or overlay effect to help blend it into the background. Or you could add a shadow and lower the fill of the text layer to about 25% opacity.

  5. Colour. This needs to be stressed, as it is important. You don’t want to have too many different colours in one sig. Try using colours that go well ‘against’ each other and try to imply colour into both your render and the background. One of the best ways to do this is to use gradient maps to overlay your entire sig.

This is probably the best advice I can give you. Also, try to give your sig some flow, for example the background and lighting and effects all go in one direction. Sorry if I’ve been a little harsh, but when people ask me to CC I really do what they asked

Good luck on future sig making
