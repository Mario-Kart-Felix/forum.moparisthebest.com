Ace1 July 31, 2016, 3:46pm #2

Alright, I’m no expert on this situation, but I’ll try to help you in the best way I possibly can.

Step One:
Go in-game, and click on the ladder (purposely do this, so it teleports you to the blank area).

Step Two:
Type the ::mypos command, or whatever command you have built in to tell you the position of where you are in-game.

Step Three:
Read the co-ords. It should say something like the following:

CoOrd [X]: 3016 CoOrd [Y]: 3064

Step Four:
Open your ‘Client.Java’ file, with Notepad or whatever editing program you use to edit your Client.Java, and search for the co-ords.
Search by pressing Control+F, which opens a ‘Find’ box. Type in the [X] Co-Ordinate you got from when in game, after clicking on the stairs and teleporting.

Step Five:
Keep searching, until you find the exact co-ordinates.

Step Six:
It should say something like the following (I think, please remember I’m not expert on this situation):

if (objectX == 3049 && objectY == 4813) { toX = 3029; toY = 4821; }
Basically what it says, is when you click the object that is at [X] 3049, and [Y] 4813, it teleports you to [X] 3029, and [Y] 4821.

What you do, is replace the numbers at toX = 3029, and toY = 4821, to where you want to be teleported once clicked on the stairs.
PLEASE NOTE (IMPORTANT): This object command was from DeltaScape. If you are not using DeltaScape, instead of toY and toX is would be something like teleportToX, or teleportToY.

Step Seven:
Press Control+S, which is the shortcut to save your file, and then close. Once you have both saved and closed the file, open the ‘Compile’ program.

Best of luck!
