public class student{ //i will use this class to make an object of students

student student1 = new student("Yakman",{0,1,2,2,2,0,1,2});

Sean July 30, 2016, 3:46am #30

sams is not a good publisher, all of their books I have read have disappointed me greatly. The x days series is particualry bad.

If you want to start a fresh with a “for newbies book” theres only one I would ever recommend. http://www.prenhall.com/deitel/ java - how to program. They have been in the game for a long time and introduce uml and design, with exercises for you to do if you wish. It is commonly used in university courses (as is their c++ book) around the world, I know several people that have been taught using their books. And no it isnt too hard for the younger members of the community to understand either, I read the book when I was 15.

But, as we all know, o’reilly is great, but dont expect it to hold your hand along the way (well the only ‘introductory’ java books ive ever glanced at of theirs read more like referance for experianced programmers, rather than introductory to programming and thus explaining concepts you wouldnt otherwise know.)

And a book you really should read? (or atleast one of the many books you really should read) http://java.sun.com/docs/books/effective/ – good book.

List of other (may be updated, the only ones I can bother to think of atm):
hardcore java (very similar to effective java)
Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software - the legendary gof book.

From there it really depends on your interests, I love books myself, ebooks or otherwise. I would disagree with mopman whole heartedly about “dont look at example code”, when you are at the learning stage example code can greatly increase your understanding of something, especially when you play with the code. Is this copy paste coding? No, this is learning. If anything I suspect he says this as he has seen noobs copy paste and claim its their own code, which is a far leap from what im proposing here.

To your original question, how I learnt java. that deitel and deitel book, and many more books after that. Setting myself small projects and large, talking to other developers and generally having a passion for programming in many ways.
