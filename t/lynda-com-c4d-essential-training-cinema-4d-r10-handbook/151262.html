It seems more and more people are getting in to the 3D scene, so I thought that I’d give you all two recommendations.

Firstly, the Cinema 4D R10 Handbook. Great…for very beginners. It isn’t for people have gone past basic modeling, and are starting to work with more complex and advanced tools. However, this book is good for lighting, basic animation, materials/textures, cloth, hair, dynamics ect. I’ve gone through most of it, and wont say it was the best investment, but it’s good in the last 4-5 chapters.

Secondly, I very big recommendation to the Lynda.com Cinema 4D Essential Training by Larry Mitchell. May not exactly be worth the $100 it is, but it comes close to that value. I learnt do much for these series of in-depth, level minded tutorials. Absolutely fabulous for really starting to work well with C4D…

I know I haven’t been around much lately, but I very much plan to post some stuff in the coming weeks, once I’ve got this college crap sorted.

Goodluck to everyone who I have seen working and starting with Cinema 4D.
