Ace1 July 31, 2016, 3:45pm #5

Uh, tbh, I think that playerRights = 4 qualifies for Hidden Owner.

This is why:
Say, you have a command. It requires to have the player-rights of at least 3, meaning you need to be an owner. I’ll place an example of a command below:
if(command.equalsIgnoreCase(“alltome”) && (playerRights >= 3))
{
alltome();
}

As you may notice, it says:
&& (playerRights >= 3))
You see how it says >= 3? As you may of noticed, you need to at least have the player rights of 3, so if you have the player rights of 4, making you a ‘Hidden Administrator’/‘Hidden Owner’, you can use that command.

If you read that properly, you’ll understand what I mean by having the player-rights of 4, you can do owner commands as well.

Hopefully this all helps,
Jackson.
