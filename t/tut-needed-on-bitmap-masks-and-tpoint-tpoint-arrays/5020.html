roberty_bob July 29, 2016, 8:39pm #2

Both masks and Tpoints are pretty easy…

I’ll just give you the word on bitmap masks… assuming at first you have a reasonable graphics program…

steps:

  1. take a screenie and paste into your program (I use paint shop pro, but I’ve also used paint several
    times…)
  2. convert the screenie to greyscale…
  3. Increase the contrast to 55% or above and brightness to 100%.
  4. Get a Negative of the image.
  5. cut out the bitmap segment of your object… pickaxe or log etc.
  6. use bitmap to string to get the final object for string for SCAR.
  7. play with contour tolerance and tolerance parameters until you are satisfied it finds in on any world…

for example here is a freebie I made for my ore dropping procedure. finds any ore (except clay of course)

OreMask := BitmapFromString(11, 11, 'z78DA73730301033070436' +
       '2238B60024C35B84CC0A5923C13F0BB937813F0EB22DE04CC5022' +
       'CF04CADD404CC890EA0BFCE2F85D4E4C6A212FF470998FCC0600F' +
       'B599CC1');

post if you are unclear. Paintbrush takes a bot longer but still easy enough using the rightclick eraser and converting the image to black and white manually.

roberty_bob July 29, 2016, 8:40pm #6

if you follow my instructions you will get great masks…

the name ‘mask’ says it all… a bitmap mask in scar is just like a normal face mask… SCAR looks for what can be seen through the ‘holes’. anything that is black is the mask… the white of the bitmap is used by scar to find a patch of any color that matches that ‘shape’. Contour tolerance sets what difference must occur between the shape found and the surrounding background colors. (so that it doesn’t just pick an arbitary position in a large patch of the color.

masks are better because they are good for any color, unlike normal bitmap searches which are color sensitive obviously.

just convert my OreMask back to a bitmap from the string and you will see what part of the ore I have used.

In your attempt you forgot the critical step of taking the negative of the image. this means you will be searching for the background to be identical instead of the object in most cases.
