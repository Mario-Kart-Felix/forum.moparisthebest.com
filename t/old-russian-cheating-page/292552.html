Despite the obfuscation, the first method uses a modulus 38 technique on the password. However, the modulus 37 algorithm already maxes out a long integer, so modulus 38 would totally overflow the long and deform the resultant String. To fix this problem, Jagex writes the resulting values from the modulus 38 algorithm as 2 sets of 7 bytes, or 14 bytes in total.

The first 7 bytes represents the first 10 characters of the password. The second 7 represents the second 10 characters of the password. So obviously a password cannot exceed 20 characters, according to that method.
