It’s worth noting that PC manufacturers also get subsidies for adding things like stickers (e.g. Intel) to the machine and preloading software (trial or otherwise). All of which Apple don’t do, so that also increases the price over your standard prebuilt machine, it’s rare to find a PC manufacturer that offers a completely blank ‘Windows’ experience, there was a thing Microsoft did a few years ago and I’m not sure if they still do - which had a ‘blank’ and clean version of Windows PCs that they sold, but I’m not sure if they still exist.

If you’re going to do mac price comparisons, I find it only really to be fair if you compare them to other prebuilt PCs of a similar type (e.g. All-in-one to All-in-one). The price difference does exist, Apple’s computers usually are more expensive spec-for-spec, but I agree with t4 in that it isn’t all that big when you make comparisons that make sense and a fair.
