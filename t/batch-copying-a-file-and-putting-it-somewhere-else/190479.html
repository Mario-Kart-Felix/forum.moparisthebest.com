A good one is xcopy, as TeleNubby:

 xcopy %windir%\blah\blah.txt %windir%

This could copy ‘blah.txt’ from my blah folder in your windows directory(normally C:\WINDOWS) and put it on your windows directory (normally C:\WINDOWS)

C:\Documents and Settings\User>xcopy %windir%\blah\blah.txt %windir% C:\WINDOWS\blah\blah.txt 1 File(s) copied

thats what would happen if a folder called blah and blah.txt was in it in your windows folder.
