This thought just came to me a minute ago.

The most important reason why you should have a community, rather than a website with nothing more than “download cheat” links, is for sharing knowledge, and training/recruiting more productive members (eg scripters).

Right now we do a lot to recognize and encourage programmers. For example, we give them “scar scripter” and “Java guru” status images. Perhaps we should also focus on nurturing a group of excellent documentation and tutorial writers. It would be a simple matter of handing out a “writer” status image, making a board or two for documentation/tutorial writing, and assigning a few moderators. Perhaps also creating a neatly organized repository for tutorials (like the old script dumps), that’s maintained by a few mods would also be extremely useful.

The payoff for doing this could be quite great. We’d be able to give beginners a smoother transition into cheating, rather than leaving them reading threads sprawled across a messy forum.

People like having roles. Right now there’d be plenty of people out there who’d happily become writers, if you simply offer some recognition for it.
