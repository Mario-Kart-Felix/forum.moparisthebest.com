i’m guessing he wants to know how to work it out from the data given, not just a value.

you probably have to assume that the entire universe is only made up of hydrogen atoms, which is mostly true anyway. i also THINK that i’m assuming a flat universe, rather than open or closed.

first of all, you need to calculate the mass of a single hydrogen atom:

 moles = mass / molar mass = atoms / N[sub]A[/sub]
 mass / 1 = 1 / N[sub]A[/sub] (because the molar mass of H is 1, and we have 1 atom)
 mass = 1 / N[sub]A[/sub] where N[sub]A[/sub] = avagadro's constant
 mass = 1 / 6.0221415x10[sup]23[/sup]
 mass = 1.6605x10[sup]-24[/sup]g
 mass = 1.6605x10[sup]-27[/sup]kg

now that we know the spacing and mass of a hydrogen atom, we can calculate the average density of hydrogen atoms in the universe:

 ρ = mass of H atom / atomic spacing
 ρ = 1.6605x10[sup]-27[/sup] / 0.17
 ρ = 9.7679x10[sup]-27[/sup] kg m[sup]-3[/sup]

this next bit may be slightly different depending on whether or not you’re supposed to assume that we’re in a flat universe:

 ρ = ρ[sub]c[/sub]
 where ρ[sub]c[/sub] = 3H[sub]0[/sub][sup]2[/sup] / 8πG
 (i'm not showing the proof for this because i think i need to integrate and i don't know calculus)

 we can work out the hubble constant using this equation because we know G and just worked out ρ[sub]c[/sub]:
   H[sub]0[/sub][sup]2[/sup] = 8ρ[sub]c[/sub]πG / 3
   H[sub]0[/sub][sup]2[/sup] = 8 x 9.76x10[sup]-27[/sup] x π x 6.673x10[sup]-11[/sup] / 3
   H[sub]0[/sub][sup]2[/sup] = 5.4606x10[sup]-36[/sup]
   H[sub]0[/sub] = 2.3368x10[sup]-18[/sup] - you could have just looked this up, but you need to show that you used the data given to dertmine the hubble constant yourself

okay so we can work out the approximate age of the universe using the hubble constant:

 the hubble constant is velocity per unit distance, si units: m s[sup]-1[/sup]m, which simplifies to s[sup]-1[/sup]
 time in seconds is therefore the reciprocal of the hubble constant:

 age of universe = 1 / H[sub]0[/sub]
 age of universe = 1 / 2.34x10[sup]-18[/sup]
 age of universe = 4.279x10[sup]17[/sup] seconds
 age of universe = 4.279x10[sup]17[/sup] / 31556926
 age of universe = [u]13.56x10[sup]9[/sup] years[/u]
