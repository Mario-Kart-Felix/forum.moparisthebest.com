The biggest mistake that I see people make is that they fail to properly plan their project. Figure out a budget and place that money into a separate bank account. You want to treat the project as a separate entity and generally not add any of your own income into that account after the initial funding. It’ll make the stress a little more bearable and you’re not going to dig too deep into your own pocket.

Secondly, figure out a way to monetize the idea before you release the game. If the project fails or runs short on funds, then it’s generally a good idea to abandon the project and focus on getting a solid paycheck again. When you feel as though you have enough saved up to finish your project, continue onwards or start a new project with better budgeting.

I can’t think of anything worse than getting yourself into debt or stressing yourself out over a project. Assume the idea will fail and the savings that you’re throwing into the idea is simply buying you the time to develop something.
