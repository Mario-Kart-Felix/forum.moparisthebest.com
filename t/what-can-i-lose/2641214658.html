[me=Taharok]wonders how many people here actually took a Health Education class[/me]

Tbh, weight doesn’t matter. You can be dehydrated one day and be several pounds less than yesterday-- it means nothing! What matters is your body fat percentage. It’s a common misconception that working out to gain muscle will lower your weight. This is untrue. Muscle weighs more than fat. Your body weight is also made up of percentages that are either distributed to fat or to muscle. So if you burn fat, you gain muscle. You can loose 15 lbs. of fat and gain 17 lbs. of muscle.

If you really want to loose body fat, then watch what you eat. It’s healthy for a male around this forum’s age group to have around 15-20% body fat. It’s also regular for males around this forum’s demographic to consume around 2500-3000 calories per day. Just keep track how many calories you are eating, as well as how many you are burning. If you are burning more than you are consuming, you loose weight. Make sure you take saturated fats and processed sugars to a low, as they are worse for the body and take longer to digest. Unsaturated fats and natural sugars are quite healthy for you, so start eating a lot of fruits and vegetables. One more thing that might help you is 3500 calories is 1 pound of weight. Bear that in mind.

God damnit there are so many things wrong with this thread I dunno where to start.
[size=15pt]1. There are more than two muscles in your body.[/size]
Stop doing god damn bench presses and curls, OP. Biceps are a tiny muscle group and don’t really need any direct attention when you’re a beginner. Add in exercises for back, shoulders, and LEGS*. Do your squats, you’ll gain muscle faster than with ANY OTHER EXERCISE, and as we all know, muscle builds fat.

[size=15pt]2. Don’t you have anything better to do than spend 3-4 hours in a gym?[/size]
After about 90 minutes (results may vary), your body’s hormone responses change from anabolic (building muscle) to catabolic (breaking down muscle). Stressing the muscles is a good thing, but too much stress and the body starts to worry and begins breaking the muscle down in order to conserve energy. So, really, keep your workouts to around an hour.

[size=15pt]3. Less is more.[/size]
Don’t do weight training more than four times a week. Ideally you need 48 hours of rest between working the same muscle group. Hypertrophy (the act of building muscle fibres) works by tearing muscle fibres when you exercise (this is why you feel sore afterwards) and then building them back up bigger while you’re resting (and eating, see point #4). If you don’t give them ample time to grow bigger, then you’ll be undoing all the work you did.

[size=15pt]4. Eat more to lose weight.[/size]
OP, conventionally there are two ways to lose weight:

[ul][li]a) Eat hardly anything and do a fuckload of exercise,[/li]
[li]and b) Eat the same as you were before and do a decent amount of exercise.[/li][/ul]
Method a will work up to a point. Sure, you’ll lose weight fast as hell but it’ll be unhealthy. You’ll lose mostly muscle (since your body in times of prolonged calorie deficit is actually more likely to store fat), and since muscle burns calories your BMR (basal metabolic rate, the number of calories you burn by doing nothing) will go down, consequently you’ll have to eat even less and you’ll find it incredibly difficult to shift that last bit of flab. As a result, you’ll end up looking like a flabby muscle-less twig. I’ve been there.
Method b on the other hand is a lot more palatable. Not only will you gain some muscle, the muscle you gained will help you burn more fat. You can lose fat at a much more healthy pace, and gain some strength at the same time, and you won’t end up looking like a polished turd.
What I want you to take away from this, is that weight doesn’t matter, only fat matters.
