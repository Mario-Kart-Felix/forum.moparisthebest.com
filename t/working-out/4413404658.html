I don’t train to bodybuild, or too have the pumped up gym junkie look. But since you seem to be heading the right track, allow me to shed some light.

Jc22493:

I’m looking for tips on working out and what supplements to take, etc. I’m going to stick with my plan of P90X for 3 months, while doing free weights, etc. I am going to take some protein, the Muscle Milk brand (only after workouts, because it’s expensive).

I suggest against taking Muscle Milk. Want my reasons? I was sponsored by a supplement distribution company (see Pro Athlete Fitness) for a year, but my nutritionist would never let me drink their protein shakes because of the heavy metals. Muscle Milk contains Arsenic, Lead, Cadmium and others that I don’t remember. Although there’s no “indepth” study into if Muscle Milk actually harms your body, I’d prefer NOT to ingest heavy metals into my body in the synthetic form. Once they’re ingested, they’re there for a LONG time. For the aim of a protein supplement, Muscle Milk is horrible. It’s low quality, and filled with taste-fixers.

If you’re after high quality protein I’d look into Optimum Nutrition/Musashi/International Protein/Balance. Whilst I’m not trying to become the next Zyzz in terms of having the pumped up gym-junkie look, I train for muscular endurace/power. Regardless, protein supplements aid in RECOVERY. They don’t “gain” you muscles, you do not drink protein and acquire muscles, as every body builder/bro-scientist attempts to spew out of their mouth. Protein supplements (whether or not they even DO anything is still yet to be proven, one thing that is proven is that the best source of protein is FOOD. The effectiveness of synthetic extracts, i.e protein, L-Arginine [which by the way HAS been proven to do NOTHING] is still yet to be confirmed) are supposed to speed up “protein synthesis” which is the healing process of hypertrophy, caused by muscle fibers broken during exercise.

By the way, I’m not saying protein supplements shouldn’t be used - I use them too, I’m just saying relying solely on protein supplements is stupid and will not gain you muscle. You want muscle? Lift lift lift until you cannot lift anymore.

Now, in terms of your training. If you’re aiming to build LBM, you want to train for 8-13 reps of 70% of 1RPM. Since you don’t have access to the gym, chin ups will be your best friend. Followed by push ups, sit ups, crunches, squats, etc. Remember, if you are serious about muscling yourself up a bit, include a mix of Isotonic, Isometric, and Isokinetic exercises. An example of a varying program using this is: 20 pushups -> 2 minute hover -> Atomic push up.

EDIT: Also, Frell, 20 minutes of cardio will gain nothing other than burn a few calories. In order to make improvements in cardiovascular respiratory fitness, you need to train in the Aerobic threshold (65% of 220-your age TO 85% of 220-your age) 4 days a week. You can minimize this to 3, even 2 days, if you utilize the HIIT training principal to train the aerobic energy system on the border of the Anaerobic energy system (anything above 85% of your max heart rate). By the way, HIIT = High-Intensity Interval Training. This could be, 100m sprints, 50m jog, 100m sprint, 50m jog. Anything that gets your heart rate near the anaerobic threshold. THe closer you are to that threshold, the quicker your body, or aerobic/cardiovascular fitness increases.
