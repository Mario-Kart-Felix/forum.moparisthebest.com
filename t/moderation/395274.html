IlikeArrays August 1, 2016, 11:51am #9

I ripped out all the unnecessary commands before initial import as I felt they are highly unneeded so moderators have little to no commands. In “Last Stand” we all played legit accounts and from the experience it went a lot more smoothly. The only way the staff was vulnerable to drama skiddies was via the forum which already you can point them out in the forum right now.

As for moderation, I believe accounts should go through stages. The first stage an account should be “triggered”. Example being login time, if an account is non stop hitting 8 hours a day it is worth checking out. I know a few of you are talking about logging the mouse movements and such so that could be a trigger as well. Not all triggers will come out valid and that leads us to stage two… Investigation.

As soon as an account is triggered investigation takes place. It can take a few minutes if its really obvious or it can be an on going operation. With investigation moderators will go through a series of “tasks”. Examples being…
-Background check (are they known to be a botter?)
-View character (maybe be invisible and watch them?)
-task 3
-task 4.

from the investigation stage it you can either a) take action or b) log investigation. Taking action will require a report stating what was done (mute, temp ban, ban) why it was done and MOST IMPORTANTLY… another moderator will need to approve of the action. So if I investigated and filed the report another moderator will need to look over it and vice versa. The reports will not need to be like a full report, just a brief explanation and the evidence to back it up.

If no action is taken place we log the investigation of that account for future reference.
