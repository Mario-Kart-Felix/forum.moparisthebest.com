ravekitty is right.

In order to make (and what I may attempt to do) a “really good economy” I would try and make like an economy handler, that changes as items are acquired. I think RuneScape has something like this, so does other sources. But then again having it at 1 price could be worth it in some ways, because people would sell to the general store before other players and most likely it wouldn’t be bought therefore basically being wiped out from the game (if your general store deletes things after a certain period of time). That may stall your ptp trading for awhile though…

No massive bonuses for donors is another great way to go. Have their little perks, but don’t make it like most servers out there today, $50 for Dragon (or) Set, $100 for Party Hats, etc, etc. In the server I’m programming at the moment, we’re going to have a few special features for donors, and those won’t necessarily be items. So that’s not something that would most likely attract people. As long as you can get it not to lag with maybe like, 300 people online at a time, they’ll be happier than they would be on the one that holds 40 players and dc’s every 5 - 10 minutes.
