Why people play RSPS?
People play RSPS because they either want to stimulate the RuneScape experience at an easier rate or to relive past experiences such as oldschool servers.
Why people pay for items on a RSPS?
Because they either want to have an advantage on other players or want to support the server. Both is possible as well.

To start a great server, you need great content that appeals to people.

Why people play RSPS?
A lot of people play because the current style of runescape isn’t to their liking. Another good portion play for nostalgia. And some play because they like the small community that RSPS’s produce. And others, idk. I hardly play RSPS so can’t say much.
Why people pay for items on a RSPS?
Many of reasons. I think one being that it’s easier to pay for the item than to actually get it and it’s also a way to help the server.
How to start a great server?
Write a content-full base with potential to have a thriving economy, while making sure that PvP’ers have something to do. Don’t forget bosses, minigames, and time killers. After a player maxes (only stats not EXP) I’ve noticed that they don’t really go out to kill things, so you need content for them to stay.
How much to invest to a server’s first month?
Depends on your dedication. I’d say no more than $150. That should cover basic advertising (depending where you advertise), VPS cost (1/2 months actually, again depending) and your webhost.
Where to advertise your server?
Everywhere that it’d be relevant. I’ve seen RSPS’s advertised on non-RSPS sites a plenty of times (powerbot being one of them)
What is the best client data revision?
In my opinion? probably 525 - 667.
Any unique features/ideas?
Not for you
How to block against Jagex?
The only realistic way I see you stopping jagex would be offshore hosting, and that would only stop them for so long. You could also try hosting on The Deep Web
How can we improve RSPS’s?
Release a stable, easy to use, well documented client and server. PI became popular because it was super easy to just download and host, and it still is that way (well, relatively). A lot of people that release content (things to download, mostly) don’t have that idea in mind. There is a huge portion that will add anti-leeches and remove bits of code to make it so you must actually have some sense of Java to use it (While I don’t think that’s bad, I think it’s turning a small population of people off of RSPS’s).

Why people play RSPS?
The community, mostly. At least, for me, that’s true. I spend a lot more time on the forums of a server than I ever do in game, just because the community is the most appealing aspect of it in my eyes. Anymore though, most people just want to continue playing their favorite game.

Why people pay for items on a RSPS?
Most people that did play Runescape as a kid are grown up now, so donations and things are only a benefit. They more than likely have jobs with income, and investing $20 to help pay for servers costs with a small reward is only a benefit to both parties.

How to start a great server?
Get lucky enough to have a community that doesn’t suck and no rollbacks while everyone is getting started and you’re pretty much good to go. There’s loads of other stuff, but these two are the main reasons so many fail.

How much to invest to a server’s first month?
Enough to keep it up, really. A cheap VPS from a reliable host and a cheap webhost is plenty to get things actually started considering it’s not like you’re hosting a Minecraft server with 100+ players.

Where to advertise your server?
Anywhere you can.

What is the best client data revision?
Subjective. I grew up with 459 Runescape, others with 562. They all have their communities.

Any unique features/ideas?
Actually making your own items is good. In a mature way, though, having 20 flavors of whip is less than appealing.

How to block against Jagex?
You can’t. I haven’t seen them go after anyone in a long time, though. If you want to avoid them just make your own game.

How can we improve RSPS’s?
What I just said, you can’t. Even if we fully emulate Runescape of any era, it still takes too much time, too many people, and too much dedication for anyone in these communities to handle.
