What does 317 and 508 mean?

Games
Runescape
Noobs44 August 1, 2016, 1:16am #1

I’m confused what are 317 and 508 servers are. Can someone explain?

Mendorphins August 1, 2016, 1:16am #2

It’s the login protocol or revision, you can usually indentify them by looks, items or NPC’s.

A 317 typically ar the old graphics, items up to about 7,000 - 9,000 (377’s go up to 15,000 ie. Godswords).

A typical 508 has the newer looking graphics, around the time Jagex added Bounty Hunter.

Go with 317/377’s.

PS: If you look through the download section, you’ll see servers that are labelled 317 or 508, and you will be able to see by the pictures on what I mean.

Which client is general or universal to these servers?

Mendorphins August 1, 2016, 1:16am #4

As long as the client and server are the same protocol (317 and 377 doesn’t really matter), you can pretty much use any client available.

I suggest going for a plain deob though, as to avoid customs. (ie. just search for 317 Deob).

Good luck.
