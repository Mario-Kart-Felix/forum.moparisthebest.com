moparisthebest August 1, 2016, 12:56am #9

I think I’ve got 2.

First was we were working on my brothers computer, putting in some PCI or AGP card or something. The only screw we could find was one slightly longer than a standard size one you use for such thing, so we put it in. The computer wouldn’t boot any more, and it took me a few minutes to find out what was wrong, the screw was just long enough, and the motherboard was just high enough, so that the screw pushed right up against the mobo, cutting 3 minuscule traces going someplace. We had to get a new motherboard…

Second was rather interesting. One of my uncle’s friends had a computer that wouldn’t run, so he sent it to me to work on. It had virtually the same hardware as my brothers computer (CPU and RAM). It wouldn’t boot so it had to be the CPU, mobo, RAM, or power supply. I used the power supply on my brothers computer, it was fine. I used the RAM in my brothers computer, it was fine, I swapped the CPU to my brothers computer (AMD Athlon XP 2200+ iirc) and the computer wouldn’t boot. So it had to be the CPU! Problem was, when I swapped my brothers CPU back to his computer, it didn’t work either. I never knew a bad CPU could fry a motherboard, but now I do.

Also, this didn’t happen to me, but to a friend at a LAN party, he was walking down the stairs with his 19" CRT monitor, and slipped and fell down about half a flight of stairs, landing on it and destroying the monitor. We laughed hard.
