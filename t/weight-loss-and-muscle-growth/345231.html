lothy July 31, 2016, 5:15pm #4

Most people don’t actually do HIIT properly; they think they are, but at the end of the day they aren’t really putting in.
Jogging isn’t that bad. Neither is something like cycling at a steady pace, for that matter.

Weight training is definitely the most important part though, and should come first. You absolutely need to be doing squats.
I started out doing the Stronglifts 5x5 program for beginners, but found that the rest time that was being used was too drawn out for my liking.
I changed over to the 20 squats program (ie, one set with 20 squats in it); it fucks you up. You get to the 5th rep and you’re thinking “yeah, pretty easy – no dramas”. 5 reps later you’re starting to breathe heavy because of elevated heartrate. Once you hit rep 15, the final 5 reps can be a nightmare. Even though it’s only one set, you will definitely experience strength gains.
After I do the squats, I then move to dips, chinsups/pullups, and pushups.

As for eating, you said you want to slim down. What I would do is convert your weight to pounds, multiply that by 16kCal, and then eat that amount minus 100 - 200 kCal to begin with. You want to reduce intake steadily so that your body isn’t shocked into a starvation response accidentally. Make sure you eat starchy carbs pre and post workout only.
The downside with this is that muscular growth won’t happen (strength gains will though), and you might even feel a bit fatigued during workouts.
You want to be eating at least 1 gram of protein per pound of bodyweight, too. There’s 4 kCal per gram of protein, 4kCal per gram of carbohydrates, and 9kCal per gram of fat.

Personally, I think the macronutrient ratio (Protein:Carbohydrates:Fat) 30:50:20 is a good starting point. Since you’re cutting, you might want to experience with carb cycling. Do a high carb day (30:50:20) and then a low carb day or two (45:30:25). On the law carb day, you might also want to lower the number of calories that you take in.
The reality is that counting calories is completely unnecessary for fat loss, but if you want more bang for your buck then the clinical approach will deliver.
