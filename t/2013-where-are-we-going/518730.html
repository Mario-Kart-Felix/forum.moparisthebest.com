The_Un-Named August 4, 2016, 7:16am #17

There are several factors as to why the years 2006 and 2007 are misleading from a purely statistical standpoint. First and foremost: mitb already had a huge and existing user base from ex-kaitnieks members. Secondly: the site saw a huge amount of traffic from people seeking the aryan bot, again, a lot of this traffic was from ex-kaitnieks patrons as well. Thirdly: 2007 saw a huge private server boom, after many, many people were banned from using aryan they had quit official runescape and many of them had taken up playing on private servers. The year 2007 is especially misleading because forum policy changed heavily, before the server status page people would post advertisements for servers on the forums which greatly inflated the number of posts, threads and users.

Also try to keep in mind that many of the people active in 2006-2007 that were carried over from kaitnieks simply would have lost interest, kaitnieks was a good 8 years ago now, that’s an awful long time to stick around for a largely non-existent cheating scene.

Davidi2:

I wouldn’t be surprised if we passed 2012 (or at least got close). I’d say we get ‘dead’ (like MITB.com) within 5 years

Part of the reason that mitb is dead is because mscp was only ever intended for private servers and that isn’t the case any more. The more recent decline of mitb can be attributed to some active members being banned along with mscp getting more general discussion related sections, not to mention the fact that Avail blatantly stole the mitb games section and didn’t make a new one. The games section of mitb was probably the most active section.

It’s not a case of mitb died, the active userbase was forced to switch to mscp.
