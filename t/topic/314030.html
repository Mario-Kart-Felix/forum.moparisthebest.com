mod_taharok July 31, 2016, 12:30pm #1

Just went to see it. It was the best-effect movie I’ve ever seen. The movie is downright superb, from acting to storyline, it’s unique in every way. In nearly every scene you see people being thrown about like marbles, joining the chaotic death and ultimate end to a long-withstanding mankind. Anyone who likes Independence Day or The War of the Worlds will like this movie, as the same producer did all three movies.

Okay anyway, I’ll quickly go over the story line for those of you who care. The movie runs around a few people who are trying to evade the end of the world, one family in particular. This family, out of dumb luck, are able to know about the end of the world’s presence mere days before it occurs. Their source is some hippie-like character who lives in Yellowstone National Park (“ground zero” for the end of the world). He ended up figuring everything out and broadcast it over the radio, though hardly anyone took heed of it. Anyways, the family is able to escape death numerous times, again out of sheer dumb luck. It’s another movie where, once you look back on it, one step wrong and you die.

This producer amazes me on every end of the world movie he comes out with. In Independence Day, escaping death wasn’t particular difficult. If one would simply live in the middle of “nowhere”, they would never have to deal with the chaos evolving around them. However, I can’t say the same about the War of the Worlds. That was one story/movie where there was no chance of survival. You couldn’t fly, you couldn’t run, and you couldn’t swim. No matter where you went, you died. 2012 is the same in so many ways. The movie bases the entire storyline around a giant cover-up between all the major countries of the world (go figure).

To go into more detail, only a few people in the US government in this movie even knew about this major crisis. They actually knew about it since 2009, in the movie. They, and other countries, immediately began commencing on a major safety project to ensure the survival of the human race. In the movie, China was building 7 arks to house several hundred thousand people. If I recall correctly, only three or four of the arks were actually finished (since the “end of the world” occurred sooner than the scientists in the movie expected ;)). Leave it to China to build huge escape routes for the safety of mankind, and leave it to the US government to lead the conspiracy against mankind.

Like my other topic, I have a question for discussion here. If the end of the world would be known several years, or even centuries in advanced, how would the governments of the world react? Would they solely care on preserving rare artwork/artifacts, species of animals, rich people, politicians, scientists, and a few random people who are considered genetically “worthy”? In most end-of-the-world movies, the government always covers it up. Couldn’t it work the other way around too? If everyone knew several years in advanced, necessary precautions could be taken for every single family in America. Everyone could come together to work toward their own common survival, instead of relying on a few to save a few.
