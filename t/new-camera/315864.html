Sony. You can probably get a basic a700 with that.

a100 is a great camera for a lot cheaper too. I wouldn’t bother with the a250 or so.

They bought Minolta but didn’t change the lens mount, so all the Minolta lenses work on it as well, so you can get a lot of the really nice ones used.

Plus, In Body Stabilization, so you don’t have to fork out a lot more money per lens for IS, and every lens you put on there will be IS.

Although, your next GOOD purchase should be the lens. Almost all of the quality comes from the lens. The kit lenses are meh at best. But a great lens will work on future cameras and still have the same quality.

SomethingToHide July 31, 2016, 12:44pm #9

Most people go with Canon or Nikon just because they know the name, but the truth is you can’t tell the difference if I showed you the same picture from Canon, Nikon, or Sony.

I don’t know what chrisn is talking about, but I don’t have to use a memory stick and something standard like SD wouldn’t be the best option on a DSLR. Mine uses a Compact Flash, which are pretty fucking fast and big. Plenty of DSLRs use them.

If you want to waste more money, spend it on a Canon or Nikon.

If you want to save more money for the same product with better features, go for the Sony. I’ve never had a problem with my a100 or a700, and they handle nicer than the Canon’s I’ve used.
