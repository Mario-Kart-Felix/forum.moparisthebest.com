The consistency of Mopar

Games
Tom August 4, 2016, 12:37am #1

It’s an interesting subject - Moparisthebest, MoparScape, GameDev, and finally MoparCraft all follow the same theme in that we supposedly support game hacking, and/or what would be viewed as cheating and experimenting with the game and its mechanics, but to what extent should Mopar support a rule-breakers view of the game at hand. We offer a cracked client, and go to extreme lengths to do so and ensure server upkeep while doing so but at what cost? The idea of this topic is to balance the books in terms of what each part of MoparCraft costs, and how much we have to give it.

Because right now, the server setup is largely correct and this is by no means a map reset vote, or anything like it. The first topic is cracked clients, the most taxing of all things on MoparCraft we go to extreme lengths to manage the upkeep and stability of the server while allowing these clients, largely, people agree that the players coming in from cracked clients aren’t worth having anyhow (obviously, not all) and that if we disallowing cracked clients complex systems (to most of the traffic) could be removed like /login thus increasing the player activity (in theory).

On the flipside there are issues, for example, it’s traditional for the players of MoparCraft to be able to play cracked so we may loose players, and even staff, which doesn’t make the community look very good and caring if we’re simply forcing loyal players out of the server community it also means that we potentially close off to a large portion of the MC community, those who haven’t paid which could in turn lower the server activity (or more, incoming traffic) but I’d put this down to the argument of quality vs quantity. Do we want an active community mainly composed of ban evaders, or rather an active community which will take time to grow composed of easily manageable people. Personally, for me disabling cracked clients is building on a bedrock foundation, whereas continually allowing them is building on an unstable and sandy foundation which as already proven cannot be sustained, and comes with terrible upkeep.

The second subject is that of the forums, we’ve spent unbelievable amounts of time arguing over the server, implementing changes and what not but the forum seems to get ignored which I disagree with, just like Moparscape people get bored of what the forum is supposed to be about so stick around for the community and not so much the topic of the forum, this happens everywhere but more so with Minecraft, keeping an interest in the game for long periods of time won’t happen for a lot of people even now with low activity such things are evident with people only coming back for updates this is why the focus needs to be not only on the server but rather on every aspect of MoparCraft including the website.

I don’t really have as much time as I’d like to post long long topics, so I’ve compressed the detail, but if you’d like to discuss it properly I can always be found on the IRC. Now I have no intention of forcing this on anyone, or arguing beyond imagination for my points simply because I have too much other stuff to be doing so don’t feel threatened by this proposition, I’ve posted it in the staff section for a reason.
