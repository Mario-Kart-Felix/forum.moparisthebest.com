mod_taharok August 1, 2016, 3:12pm #20

Well combat really isn’t that fast when training it via slayer, but it seems to go by fast (at least it did for me). I was getting 60k-80k cbat exp/hour, and going from 72-85 slayer gets you quite a bit of levels, even though it took nolifing for about a month. With slayer, just think of it like this. You get roughly 10% of the creature’s health as slayer experience. And as you may know, you divide the amount of health by 2.5 (7.5 for constitution) to figure out combat experience. So if you want to get 1,000,000 (roughly level 1-73) slayer experience, you have to deal roughly 10,000,000 hitpoints, which will give you about 4,000,000 combat experience (which is roughly level 1-87), and 1,333,333.33 constitution experience (roughly level 1-76, give or take).

That’s how I look at it, anyway. I have level 90 attack, 91 strength, 91 or 92 constitution (can’t remember atm), 90 defense, and like I said before, 85 slayer. I think to myself, what can I get by getting 93 slayer? Well 85-93 requires roughly 3,937,035 experience. Converting this to combat experience gives me about 5,249,380 constitution experience, and 15,748,140 combat experience. First of all, I would have between 97 and 98 constitution. If I do melee only for combat, I’m receiving enough experience for 99 attack, 99 strength, and between 91 and 92 defense.

Worst part of slayer is how long it takes, but there are many more benefits than just the experience. For example, I have 79 summoning, and from my experience, 85-93 slayer would provide enough charms for about 79-90 summoning (give or take). The monetary benefits of slayer also would give me enough money for those summoning levels, and probably enough for getting my level 80 prayer up to close to 90. My favorite part of slayer, though, is the constant change of venue I get (no need to stick in one place training forever!)

That’s just 85-93, of course. Going for 99 slayer pretty much guarantees maxing your character out.
