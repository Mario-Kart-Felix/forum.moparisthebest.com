ok this is how i did it.

now, first… you must have jdk. So yeah you should have that. yep.

ok next

go into “my computer”

then in the top right hand corner there should be something that says, “view system information” Click it.

You will then have a box pop up that has many tabs at the top, you need to click the one that says, “advanced”

Down at the bottom, you will se something that says, “environmental variables” Click it.

now this is where it gets a little more confusing. !Read carfully!

ok so you will c, in blue,“system variables” and right below that will be a box with a bunch of stuff it in. Find the one that says, “CLASSPATH”.

Double click it. or highlight it in blue and click “edit”.

alright now what you need to type in is:

C:\Program Files\Java\jdk1.6.0_##\Bin;%CLASSPATH%;

what i mean by ## is that you have to type in what ever verson of jdk you have. For example, mine is 12, so mine would look like this: C:\Program Files\Java\jdk1.6.0_12\Bin;%CLASSPATH%;

ok now that’s only half of it.

now the other half, you find the variable called “Path” open it.

type this in C:\Program Files\Java\jdk1.6.0_##\bin;
then click OK. and then your done.

@NOTE@ If you do not have these variables, make them. and I think it will still work. remember the names:
Path, and CLASSPATH.
