tL August 1, 2016, 1:23pm #7

The gestures coupled with Mission Control is what makes it worthwhile for me, plus the full screen apps. Though, there seems to be a bit of misunderstanding when it comes to them. They’re not simply maximised applications (Apple still don’t have a button for that, for their reasons) - they’re entirely full screen and they move to their own space.

I think their purpose behind them, is that coupled with the gestures and mission control, it makes swapping between them extremely easy, much easier than fiddling around with various windows on a single desktop. Airdrop is there to transfer between friends, quickly, SMB shares aren’t as quick and easy, there is no setting up required for Airdrop, it just finds Macs near you and you’re ready to go.

I’m not that interested in launchpad, I have my applications folder set up so that it is almost as easy as using launchpad, although most of the time, I just use spotlight. Maybe i’ll get used to using it, but I didn’t when I was using the GM build, and I still can’t now. The Mail upgrade is fantastic, and something they’re doing in the background has made applications start up faster - even after a clean reboot - I’ve turned off App-resume.

I think what I’ve got most out of it is increased productivity on my laptop. Of course, spaces and expose was there before on snow leopard, but merging them together and making them function with the gestures does make it all that much better.
