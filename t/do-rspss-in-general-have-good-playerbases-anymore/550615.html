CSharp95 August 4, 2016, 3:03pm #11

I’m not entirely sure

On the one hand, the vast vast majority of people who play servers are generally people who care more about stroking their ego than do actually playing the game. It always makes me cringe when I see people call getting lucky with a random number generator ‘skill’ of any kind.
A lot of the people who host/own the servers know little about programming and just want the money or sense of power over something involving other people.
And a lot of people who develop could care less about the efficiency of their code or about fixing the broken features of the server and making it actually worth presenting.

But at the same time, it’s a community I don’t ever really see dying. I’ve seen forums for games that are well over 10 years old since people stopped talking about them and they still get more new people.
It’s also been like this since I started liking private servers 5 or 6 years ago now. It’s always going to be kids developing, running, and playing these things. If you like developing them, then go ahead and make something of decent quality and it’ll further the community that much more. But if you want to move on to making games and applications that will be properly appreciated, no one will blame you.
