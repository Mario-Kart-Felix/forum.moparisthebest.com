lothy July 30, 2016, 10:23pm #47

There are various reasons for Moparscape moderators having/lacking various powers.

Firstly there’s an age related issue - I do not believe that some of the younger moderators have the ability to make impartial decisions when handing out such punishments (which have the ability to completely incapacitate a user’s account). I’m aware of the difficulty some younger mods have with disciplining people that they consider friends.

Secondly there’s the obvious security issue. I don’t think any more needs to be said.

Thirdly, I personally disagree with giving people higher permissions on this forum when they have their own large communities to run.
It has the potential to create a conflict in interests; a moderator here (who shall remain anonymous) has used things restricted on this website (such as copy and paste tutorials, which are unwelcome) to encourage users to join his own website (which conveniently allows such tutorials).
That is a conflict in interest, and in this case scenario he has obviously chosen what is in his site’s best interest, instead of this site’s best interest (questionable, I know - I’m sure some of us welcome the removal of certain users).

This is quite all right, and naturally it makes sense to do what’s in the best interest of your own site. But in saying that, I don’t think it’s right to admit such people to higher echelons of the moderator team.

Please note that these opinions are mine and mine alone. They couldn’t possibly represent the views of the entire forum staff, and other members of staff will have their own opinions on the matter.
