Greeny August 3, 2016, 5:22pm #2

That seems like a very poor choice of first car. Cool perhaps, and sure it will get you all the womenz but for a first car? The one you’re probably most likely to ruin?.. not advisable! I don’t know what insurance is like down under but over here (UK) young drivers can expect to pay £1000+ for their first years insurance. With a car like that you could be expecting to pay >£3000 probably. Then there is the fuel, fuels costs stupid amounts atm and it is certainly not getting any better. To get a car like that you need to be able to afford to run it, not just buy it.

Gaming computers can be bought at a budget, you can pay a large amount and build something bleeding edge or pay half that and still get most of the performance it’s all down to what you want to spend and whats available to you. Budgeting really is key. Checkout our computer hardware section you’ll find you can get all the advice/ideas you need there.

Hints/tips to get money? There are no get rich quick methods, do not do online surveys, don’t leave your computer on over night running something that makes $0.02p/h instead do useful stuff for neighbours like gardening, or if you have pc repair or webdev skills try to promote those. A part time job is of course the most stable income you’ll be able to get at your age (if you can even get one, i don’t know aussie employment laws) if you can find one, then that is your best shot at up saving money. Otherwise odd jobs all the way, at least you only work when you want to and whilst its not stable income it is income and real relevant work that you can get references from.
