moparisthebest July 31, 2016, 7:12am #32
Cain_:

aoeuidhtns- I’ve been using dvorak for about a year :P. Sensored told me to start using it.

I believe Sensored is the one who got me to try it as well, I suggest everyone should read this:

http://www.dvzine.org/zine/01-toc.html

It is very entertaining and informative.

I’ve been using the dvorak layout for over a year, and switching from qwerty it took me about 2 weeks to get back up to the speed I could type at using a qwerty layout, since then I am now quite a bit faster than I was with qwerty. However, for me speed was not the biggest advantage. I found that after a day at the computer, either being on the forums, IRC, or programming (usually a combination of all three) that my fingers and sometimes wrists hurt and ached afterwards. Going into a career as a computer scientist like that seemed like a bad idea, and I’m happy to say that ever since I changed to the dvorak layout my fingers or wrists have never hurt again, no matter how much I type. That alone should be reason enough for anyone to switch.

As to having a different keyboard, or switching layouts on public computers, it isn’t a problem at all. In the beginning I switched the layout on my laptop and an extra keyboard I had laying around to make it easier to learn, but now I never look anyway and so I type on qwerty keyboards all the time without problem. As sppt said you can easily use this to switch the layout on windows, or you can switch it easily with the regional and language settings in the control panel (even with restrictions, the language bar is never restricted). On linux and mac it is easy to switch as well.

If you have any questions about it, feel free to ask me, I love talking about how great the dvorak layout is.
