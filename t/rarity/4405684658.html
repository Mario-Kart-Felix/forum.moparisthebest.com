Lin August 3, 2016, 2:26am #22

Thank you all very much for the support. Today I finally had a good chance to work with school out of the way for a while, and I got quite a lot done.

For starts, I fixed a left over region bug that messed with getting the near entities. I also fixed any form of player following to get rid of the walk backs, and also fixed a bug that would occur if the player was running but the generated path had a missing front walk point which wouldn’t let them reach their destination client-sided. I also fixed the initializing packet (249) sending the player index before the player index was even assigned, which fixed facing issues.

Now that the bug fixes are out of the way, interacting with an NPC works exactly like RuneScape. For example, with bankers, you walk up to the front of the bank booth and only then will the dialogue actually begin. If the NPC were, for example, a shop keeper, the player would walk next to the NPC (follow is more like it. If the NPC walks away before the player reaches the NPC, the player will follow. Packet 98 is not used) and end up at a non-diagonal point. The NPC faces the player and stops walking until the player stops leaving, and like RuneScape, the NPC will face the player until they randomly walk again. I also extended the dialogue system to include the 2-5 actions, supporting which top sword style is desired (Packet 171 to hide/show an interface part for anyone wondering). The NPC queuing action system is also more powerful. That’s pretty much it. I know it doesn’t seem like much, but I spent a long time finding out why things weren’t working.

That’s tonight’s update on the project. Next up is shopping, trading, banking, and all that other good stuff. Here’s a couple screenshots for your pleasure. On the second screenshot player 1 talked to Bob again after moving, and I haven’t sent packet 78 (clear destination flag) yet when moving to an NPC.
