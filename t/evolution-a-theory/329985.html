With the scientific method, theories are set up so they are very easy to destroy if only the evidence came up. The more people try and fail to disprove a theory, the more confidence we have in it. But if they manage the disprove it, the theory is dead.

To take evolution as an example of something that is very easy to disprove, all you need to do is find a single genuine fossil in the wrong period. A commonly cited example is a fossil rabbit in the precambrian. You could also try to find a single animal who’s DNA does not fit into the known tree of life.
All it would take is a single example to completely destroy evolution. And its never been found despite many people trying hard for over 100 years.

Evolution has such a massive amount of evidence supporting, such a wealth of facts and figures that back it up. It is as proven as can be in science. It often makes little sense to call it a theory in everyday language, people just say it’s a fact. Its just as much of a theory as the fact that the earth is round, that all matter is made from atoms and that gravity keeps us on the ground.

Religion, by contrast, sets its postulates up so they are very difficult or impossible to disprove. Many are unfalseifiable. In fact history shows time and time again how when a new piece of knowledge is uncovered, religions change their view saying it was only a metaphore or that they were actually right all along. This dishonest shifting the goalposts should warn anybody against using religion as a path to knowledge.

The scientific method of testing ideas against experiment is a real path to knowledge and understanding. Religious revelation is not.

Original_Daniel July 31, 2016, 2:51pm #8

Animals have mutations every now and then, occasionally a mutation will help that animal survive longer and have more babies to pass that mutation onto. Those babies survive longer than others and have more babies until every animal of the species (or at least in the area) has that mutation that helps them survive.

If an animal gets a mutation that hurts it, it doesn’t live as long and probably won’t find a mate. Mating in animals and attraction in humans both work in the same basic ways.

The survival and maternity rate of humans is so high nowadays evolution would probably be almost non existent for us.
