[size=16pt]What is DigiLife?[/size]
[center]

24xqux1.png1138×904
[/center]
[center][size=8pt]DigiLife launch screen with placeholder background, more will come on the right hand side[/size][/center]
DigiLife is a free, 2D top-down simulation game. It will feature an expandable world with customizable maps, NPCs, items and buildings and much much more. The game will take place in a pre-made city, the player is able to choose how to live his/her own life. Similar to that of a GTA style gameplay except with much wider opportunities such as building your own company, creating your own rockets etc.

The game will have 3 different ages, although players would be able to customize their ages in the future. Currently, there are Stone Age, Medieval Age and Modern Age planned. Development will focus on Stone Age first, then gradually move on to other ages.

The idea is that, in different ages, there are different styles of gameplay. From each age, people will have different skills they could master such as woodwork, metalwork, art, then gradually science, physics, astrophysics etc. The player is able to build whatever they want, start a clan, business or perhaps rule his/her people. Basically like a in-game virtual world.

[size=16pt]What is DigiEdit?[/size]

DigiEdit is the tile editor for DigiLife. DigiLife comes with the official tileset (or maps if you wish to call it) but will also be optimized for 3rd party maps. Server hosts will be able to host a server on an entirely different city of their own with DigiEdit maps.

DigiEdit first alpha version has been released, please download it here http://downloads.digilifegame.com under the DigKit section. Remember to report any bugs on the forums under the DigiKit section.

Major Features of DigiEdit v1.0 alpha:

MultiLayered Tile Editor
[center][/center]
[center][size=8pt]Multi-Layer options, they are, from left to right: Layer 1, Layer 2, Layer 3, Shadows and Layer Only View[/size][/center]

It is a multi-layered tile editor, which means that it supports editing tiles at different layers overlaying each other. This technique is used to get rid of extra hassle of base tiles. For example, there’s a table, but the table has to stand on something, lets say… a wooden floor. But lets say the table moved, an NPC pushed it outside, then, without multi-layering, we’ll need to paint the ground that the table stands on to ‘grass’. Therefore we’re using 4 layers, with the 1st layer being the base layer, where things like grass, wood, concrete and all other floor tiles are laid. On top of that we have furnitures and decorations, 3rd layer is further decorations and 4th layer is the shadow layer, where shadows are mapped out.

With Layered-Only view, the user can see exactly what tiles are on this layer. Therefore avoiding further confusion.

Detailed Information

[center][/center]

Lots of information about the map is right at the bottom left corner of the editor. From map properties, comments to view linked chunks (which is currently not implemented).

Tile templates
