mod_taharok August 2, 2016, 4:50pm #54

What on earth are you people rating yourselves on? Are you talking about mere ability to add some code to an application and make it work somehow? What about the ability to design an entire application, library, or infrastructure from scratch? What about understanding the different paradigms available to your language of choice (in Java’s case, OOP)? What about knowing the general API, popular extensions to the API, and other choice libraries? What about understanding the functioning of your language and its compatibility over different systems and platforms? In Java’s case, what about understanding its memory model, data types, and syntactical structures (both current and new ones coming in Java 7)? In Java’s case, what about understanding how bytecode works, how hotspot works, or the tendency of hotspot to compile to native code? What about the idioms of the language, you know, those strange quirks that only happen in Java 1.5u7 on Windows XP SP2 given certain hardware and drivers?

So, I will call bullshit for every single post in this topic. You cannot rationalize knowing a language in entirety (though, for simple purposes, I’ll leave out dynamic languages in that claim, as they are more of understanding an ideology than an actual language). Maybe if you develop your own language, you can get close. But let’s face it, you don’t need to know the complex low-level functionality of a language to build everyday applications. It’s when you get into challenging programming (and really just professional programming) when you should start considering knowing a lot of that stuff.

Point is, none of you is a “10/10,” nor do you know anyone as such. It’s simply an impossible place to be. Bleh, I don’t even know why I bothered posting.
