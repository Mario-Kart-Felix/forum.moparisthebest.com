I wasn’t trying to actually postulate a fail-proof argument here as there are NONE in existence. The ones pro-god fail to a degree and the ones anti-god fail to a certain degree.

I was just giving a humorous slant on the whole topic at first. My second “argument” was quickly drawn up and not meant to be a final proof of anything, just an outline of what the argument may or may not contain in order to give you, the reader, an idea as to why I think religion is selfish and childish.

Are we still so nearly removed from our ape-like ancestors, that we can ascend to the stars yet abandon fathoming their creation, ascribing this universe a supernatural cause and influence? Indeed, many of us are. The race is primitive on a large scale and religion impedes our advances at every turn throughout recorded history.

Were it not for the selfishness of priests and holy men, we could be living on the moon and terraforming Mars by now. Of course that is speculation but it in part remains true - religion has always had “the” truth and should you object or cause discord, you would be labeled heretic and possibly murdered. This is not the way to an advanced society, which religion never can and never will bring us to.

The human race is a blink of the cosmic eye, they say, and though we may judge our progress to be blindingly fast, we’ve yet to conquer the same demons of religion that haunt us with our capability for rational thought. This adds further backing to my OPINION that the human race is childish. An advanced culture need no religion except mutual kindness and rational thought. Those should be our religions if we are to take the next step towards a greater civilization.

These are my opinions I am discussing, but they are soundly founded upon an interpretation of facts and history. I am a rational, intelligent human being that chooses no religion over some religion. I’m not uncomfortable in this choice and neither should anyone else be. We were taught to fear from birth so we could be manipulated to do well. We are shackled to superstition and irrationality. It’s time we let these chains loose and practice kindness for it’s own satisfying qualities rather than practicing due to a desire to get into some imaginary place.
