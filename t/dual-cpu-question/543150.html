lothy August 4, 2016, 1:09pm #19

Honestly, modern CPUs are nothing to get worked up about. Improvements haven’t been drastic in terms of computing power per core since Sandy Bridge, maybe earlier. The main changes with the Haswell line were reductions in power consumption, and CPU product development will likely continue along this trajectory.

The result? The sandy bridge processor in my desktop computer (i7 2600K, IIRC) still feels relatively ‘new’ despite being from 2010 or 2011.
RAM hasn’t improved all that much either. In fact, the single biggest improvements, in my opinion, would be realised by upgrading to an SSD if you haven’t already done so and upgrading your graphics card.
