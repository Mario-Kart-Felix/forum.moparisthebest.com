[quote=“RuneSpace, post:1, topic:371601”]late 2005/early 2006: kaitnieks was an active and thriving cheating community for runescape
same era: wL made his first server in order to test bots and learn about the protocol. it was single player and horribly coded
early 2006: daiki takes wL’s source and makes it multiplayer, still a “proof of concept” with maybe a bit of support/interest for actually playing it, releases it under the name “blakescape d boosted”
same era: kaitnieks shuts down
soon after: mitb forms for the refugees of the death of kaitnieks, runescape cheating becomes centralized once more
soon after that: development continues by random individuals on the opensource blakescape server and interest increases in hosting/playing servers
soon after that (mid-2006): mitb separates itself from private servers by creating mscp
late 2006: google comes out
2007: mscp is booming with thousands of people
2007+: mscp/mitb are slowly dying (still) as cheating and emulation become decentralized, while content development is made by hundreds of amateur programmers

some history of significant server releases (in chronological order):
winterlove (first release)
blakescape (multiplayer)
mythscape (some early content)
cheezscape (combat, npcs, etc)
whitescape (improved content)
p16 (even more improved content)
rs2dv (entire new release)
hyperion (first commercial scale performance release)

wL = winterLove
