Consider the following game: we put a single king on the upper-left square of a standard chess board. On each turn a player may move the king one square down, to the right, or diagonally own-right (but no other direction). The players take turns moving the king (there is no passing), with the winner being the player who moves the king onto the bottom-right square. If the player who moves ﬁrst knows what he is doing, he will always win. Prove it.

But before you can prove it, you need a system to follow so that as the first player you always win. It is easier than it sounds, can you do it?
