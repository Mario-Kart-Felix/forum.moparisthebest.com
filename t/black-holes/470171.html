Ruby_Forums_Coder August 4, 2016, 1:26am #8

Of course black holes exist, there are contrasting theories on event horizons but black holes themselves exist. There’s two popular beliefs on black holes, that they infact own event horizons and that they don’t. Black holes with no event horizons come about if you look at them by the functional Schrodinger equation (the mathematical way) you’d find that the black hole as it’s forming it collapses in on itself. The scientists studying this way find that the gravity of the collapsing mass starts disturbing the quantum vacuum. This generates whats called pre-hawking radiation. This radiation loss causes the mass of the object to be greatly reduced and means it can never achieve the correct density to form an event horizon.

I believe that black holes exist, but do infact have event horizons and the theory above isn’t true. Of course, we won’t know for quite some time yet.

RandQm:

The pictures you can find are “possible black holes” as nasa says themselves. There is never an actual black hole found.

nasa has that disclaimer because in case you haven’t noticed, scientists are the last type of people who claim something with absolute certainty, you’ll also notice the disclaimer on nebulae and galaxies

we can observe black holes in the sense we observe something that matches what we think black holes to be, if you observe what you think is a galaxy 9 times out of 10, it probably isn’t a galaxy but if multiple studies also show similar results then what you are looking at would be “a possible galaxy”

we can also observe the effect black holes have, such as observing the effect of stars and gasses around the centre of our galaxy for 16 years and then finding out from the data how big it may be and how far it is, we obviously can’t observe the black hole but we can observe the orbits of the stars around it and compare it to what we know about how objects orbit
