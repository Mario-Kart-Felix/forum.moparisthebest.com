This was your method, first thing you needed an equals sign on this line,

cape.getId() + 1;

Should have been

cape.getId() += 1;

Because when you have cape.getId() + 1 you’re saying, “Get the data from cape.getId() and add 1”.
But this doesn’t assign it back to itself.

You would have to do this,

cape.getId() = cape.getId() + 1;

As now you’re assigning it back to itself.

But, when you have [i]cape.getId() += 1 you’re saying “Get the data from cape.getId() and assign 1”. It does the same as

cape.getId() = cape.getId() + 1;

but as a shortcut.

And your second problem you was missing two closing braces, it was closing the class before it was supposed to.

public static void trim(Player player) [b]{[/b] Item cape = player.getEquipment().get(Equipment.SLOT_CAPE); if(player.maxedSkills > 2) [b]{[/b] cape.getId() + 1; [b]}[/b] else [b]{[/b] cape.getId();
