Frell July 31, 2016, 5:21pm #18
Uriko:

ok, enough is enough, i’ve always browsed through here looking at your guys’ models but now i really want to get started… can anyone give me a list of programs to use/tutorials if possible?

You’re in for a wild ride. I got a pm from goatee about this a couple of weeks ago also :P.

For a program you can choose C4D, 3DS Max, Maya, and Blender. Blender is free but don’t let that make you look away. C4D is good for getting introduced into 3d, but if you want to get into anything complex like this ump you’ll need to move to max or maya (i couldnt imagine making this in c4d). I personally use Max because its more common in the games industry, but Maya is good to although its more common in the movie industry.

Max and Maya both have 30 day trials (http://autodesk.com/) and as i said before blender is free. If your serious about this than your going to have to get through some pretty intense learning curves. I already knew how to model when i switched to max so it was only a matter of finding everything again. But if you’re starting from nothing you’ll have to work your way up the ladder.

For tutorials you can use:

http://www.3dtotal.com/ (top toolbar)

http://cg.tutsplus.com/ - amazing. They only have quality tutorials and the authors get paid $150 if their tutorial gets accepted. I got an email from them about 2 days ago saying they were reviewing my tutorial for submission (:D). Also check out their Basix tutorials.

And for help you could either do something boring like read the manual or post here. Me and STH are both experienced in Max along with SilentCJ(<3 a little, he can get around), Archer left but he used Maya.

Itll probably be hard at first to make anything because you wont really ‘get’ what shapes make up an object. Eventually anything you look at that you want to model you will automatically see what shapes you could start with etc. The entire creation process kinda jolts through your head.
