SomethingToHide July 31, 2016, 8:56am #11

Don’t use gaussian blur, especially for DOF type blurring. Always use lens blur for that.

I don’t like the bubbles one. It’s like there’s three layers of bubbles, and each layer is perfectly flat in the Z direction. The blur makes it seem like you were going for depth, and if you were your combination of blurring, lighting, and size failed at it.

The bubbles wouldn’t in all the same location on the Z axis in each layer, so the ones farther away and closer would have varying levels of out-of-focus-ness.

Then the lighting makes it almost impossible to tell which layer is closer and blurred than which is farther and blurred. The lighting makes the front and back bubbles almost the same group of colors so there’s another faking-perspective rule broken.

And then the blurred ones are pretty much of equal size variations. That would mean the ones in front are really small compared to the ones in the middle, and the ones in the back are really big
