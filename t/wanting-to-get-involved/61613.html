Deobs are not the current method of creating bots - you just look at them to get a feel for how the client works. Without a lot of precautions, logging in with a modified deobfuscated client will almost guarantee detection. Most bots are made with bytecode editing (using BCEL), which is a bit more complex and requires a good understanding of Java and the client specifically.

Pretty much. My favorite way of doing it is to get an IDE with a good renaming refactor feature (like IDEA, my favorite, or Eclipse) and start renaming things in the client as you discover what they do. The more stuff you rename, the clearer other things will become until you finally have a good idea of how most features work.

If you use someone else’s info to get started (like an old copy of Aryan or one of the “some info about #” posts) be sure that you understand what’s going on too and aren’t just copying the names. Granted, some stuff will be complicated at first and you might just want to skip those…it all depends on you.
