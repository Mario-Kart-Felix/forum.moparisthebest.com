I just wanted to post some of my progress with my 194 project.

For those of you who don’t know, which is probably almost all of you, I’ve been refactoring a 194 client for the last few weeks. I’ve been dumping all the data I could get my hands on, since it’s so easy. It’s somewhat fun to look through the old RS2 data, so, erm, enjoy

RS2 194 Items list
RS2 194 NPCs list
RS2 194 Objects list
RS2 194 Textures dump
RS2 194 media indexed image dump
RS2 194 media game image dump
RS2 194 word encoding dump
RS2 194 Interface dump
RS2 194 Interface resource dump
RS2 194 Re-named client documentation

Some explanations to each. The items list previews are based on the inventory images. I can’t explain why some don’t appear, beyond some name problems. As for the NPCs, some of the previews are outside the image area. They are also somewhat difficult to see (just like the objects). This is because they were rendered on a 500x500 canvas, then resized to a 100x100 image. They have empty scanlines that I didn’t bother to remove, otherwise they would be a bigger size. On the objects list, some don’t appear because of the arguments I used to render them. I may be able to dump a more completed preview list later on.

Indexed images are images that use a palette for color referencing. In Mopar’s 317 re-named client, these are known as ‘backgrounds’. Game images are regular 24-bit RGB images, and are known as ‘sprites’ in Mopar’s re-named client.

Two of the four word encodings are a little unknown to me. Though I’m positive badenc.txt is the actual censor list, and domainenc.txt contains all banned urls.

Note for the documentation: I created it right after I packaged up the source. Some of the ‘undetermined’ classes have been determined and placed in their proper places.

Edit: I added the interface dump and interface resource dump. The rendered interfaces you see are only the interfaces with valid children ids. I’m not entirely sure why some of them don’t render anything, but it doesn’t matter too much. Interfaces, like objects, are too diverse to be dumped with 1 common method.

I also updated the items, npcs, and objects lists to include examine info.

If you have any questions, or want the source, just post here. I also have a fixed copy of graham’s Shard Revolutions that peter included with his post.
