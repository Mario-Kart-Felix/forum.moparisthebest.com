You don’t have to agree with me, in fact I encourage that you disagree and debate this with me, I’d like to get something interesting started on this board.

I’ll mention, it would be good if you all could not bring religion into this debate. If you’re religious, that’s great for you, you’ve already found your answer to all of these questions. Unfortunately, bringing religion into this discussion will not work. This is for a few reasons.

My opinions:

Why are we alive?
Because the Universe is big and in a sense we were accidentally created and since then we have clung to life fearful of death, fearful of not existing. (Not bringing religion into this, when I say God I mean existence, not the guy with a beard that’s up in the clouds) In a sense if I were to call anything God I’d call existence God. In a sense humans are like the cells in the human body (human body = universe, cells = us). We are a part of the Universe, we live and we die and on our own we are essentially valueless but when you bundle us all up together you can get an amazing thing (I used the word ‘thing’ because depending on the way the words I could use are interpreted it could be seen as wrong).

Are living organisms more important than nonliving objects?
It depends on who’s answering the question. To other living organisms, living organisms are, but in the grand scale of things we are not.

If the entire human population was decimated, would it ‘matter’, on the grand scale of the universe?
It would not because all that we are still exists in a different form. But again, you need to define what does matter (and other words) in order to answer that question correctly (in relation to what the person defines as correct).

Do living (or even nonliving) objects have any intrinsic value whatsoever? What’s the ‘point’?
None whatsoever.

Every living organism eventually dies. Assuming there is no afterlife, what is the point of living if we are condemned to die?
Their is no point but we try to give ourselves reasons to live with the distractions of life. I.e. Let’s say you have a couple of kids; you would want to make sure they are healthy, happy etc. That is a distraction from the emptiness that existence is.
