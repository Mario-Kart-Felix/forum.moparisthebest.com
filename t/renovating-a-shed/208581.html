R.I.P_RSC July 30, 2016, 10:54pm #4

It is possible, it’s quite normal to do it. I can explain it, but the problem is I don’t know the English names of all the materials.

What you need:
isolating stuff
plaster boards
poles like this: http://www.regiobouwbrunssum.nl/images/Afwerking002.jpg (I hope you can see the image) Not really poles, but square ones. If you don’t understand what I mean, google for ‘houten regelwerk’.
Optional: wooden boards, because plaster boards aren’t really strong. (IDK how to call them and I can’t find an image, but they are somekind of wood chips glued to eachother forming a board.)

Put the poles on the wall, like in the image, leaving enough space between them so that the isolating stuff fits in it. Then screw the plaster boards to the poles and paint or whatever. Of course you have to work a bit accurate, but that’s obvious.

R.I.P_RSC July 30, 2016, 10:54pm #6

If there is a lot of moisture coming up through the floor it can be bad for the carpet, so a underlay sheet is good. Just ask a local carpet store about it, they’ll give you free advise. The door can be a pain in the ass. It’s not really hard, but it’s precision work. I don’t know how to place a door, my father does that and I’ve seen him doing that a few times. But he usually places doors that are extra secured.

I do advise that you put something between the stone walls and the plaster boards, because you really don’t want the plaster boards to get wet.

R.I.P_RSC July 30, 2016, 10:54pm #8

Just empty space between the wall and the boards, google for ‘houten regelwerk’, I really don’t know what it is in English. If you want the walls to be a bit more stronger put chip boards between the ‘regelwerk’ and the plaster boards.

It’s a flat roof right? I hope you tarred it good so it won’t leak, just look for wet spots. If there are new ones that came after you tarred the roof, try to fix it. Then put some wooden plates, chip boards for instance, and then plaster boards. Ask the shop where you buy the materials what materials you should use, I might have translated it wrong.
