I guess first you have to define free will. I define free will as the opportunity to do as you please. Whether or not if someone knows what you are doing, as long as you are doing whatever you want to do, you have free will. However, if the person that was following you could mandate what you could do, then you would no longer have free will.

And as far as those people saying time travel will never exist. Never say never. They told the Wright brothers they could never fly and guess what? They flew. With a scientific mindset, it is impossible to deem anything impossible, for once you do you have omitted a chance. A perpetual motion machine is almost definitely impossible (because of friction), but that does not make it impossible. It is just highly unlikely with our current understandings of physics (which is very rudimentary, mind you) that one could exist. The same goes for time traveling machines.
