Are time travel and free will mutually exclusive?

General Discussion
Ambokile August 4, 2016, 2:12am #1

I’m going to start this off with a hypothetical scenario as it’s the easiest way of doing it.

Imagine that someone, right now, is following me (and has been following me) for my entire life. They have documented my every single action. Also, imagine that in the future time travel will definitely be invented and used. Now, let’s imagine that in the future, the book containing the documentation of my life becomes available. If someone from the future reads that book and then comes back into the present time, they would know what I am going to do tomorrow, right? Surely that means that the action that I am going to complete tomorrow has already been completed as the person who came back from the future would know what I am going to do?
And also, before anyone says that I could simply choose to do a different action, when I did that then the documentation would end up different and the person who came back from the future would still know what I am going to do tomorrow.

So, from the scenario that I’ve posted above, can time travel exist in the same space as free will?
