bloodrayne August 1, 2016, 2:54pm #16

My best tip for learning a programming language is to not do it out of a ‘want’ to learn, but out of a ‘need’ to learn. If you’re trying to learn something just to say you know it, you’re not going to get far. When you’re learning something to build with, you hit all the speed bumps in the road, you have to troubleshoot, you learn the better and more efficient ways of doing something.

I think the majority of people started with html because it’s the most basic practically plaintext language there is, and it gives you a taste for how code is structured, from there you can move on to other bigger and better languages.

The only reason I ever got into anything advanced online was for the purpose of cheating rs that was my ‘need’ to learn
