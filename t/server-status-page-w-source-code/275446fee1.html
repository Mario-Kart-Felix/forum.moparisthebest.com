I didn’t say it depends on how many servers are on the list, I said it depends on how long your server has been on the list. I guess I’ll go ahead and explain it again:

Every 30 minutes, every server is checked. Each time, the field ‘total_count’ is incremented by one. So If you server has been on the list 1 hour, total_count will be 2, if it’s been on there 100 hours, total_count will be 200. Still with me? Good.

Also, every time a server is checked, if it is offline, nothing is done, if the server is online, the field ‘on_count’ is incremented by one. So on_count is the number of times your server has been online.

Percentage is, of course, calculated by dividing: on_count / total_count

So if your total_count is 10, and your on_count is 9, you have 90% uptime, the next time it is checked (30 minutes) if you are online, your total_count will be 11 and your on_count will be 10, making your uptime 91%, that’s 1% in 30 minutes. If you total_count is 100, and your on_count is 90, you have a 90% uptime, the next time it is checked (30 minutes) if you are online, your total_count will be 101 and your on_count will be 91, making your uptime still 90%.

Does that make sense to you guys? It’s the only accurate and fair way to handle this that I know of, if you guys know of another way, by all means, let me know.
