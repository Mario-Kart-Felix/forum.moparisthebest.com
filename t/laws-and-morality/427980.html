TheCyberMan August 2, 2016, 3:30pm #14

A government’s role should not be to enforce morality, but to effectively run a civilization within reason (these reasons are the very basic standards of living that have been accepted since the advent of society, such as no killing and no stealing). Additionally, I believe a government should enforce its rule of law.

However, if the government becomes too involved, moral decisions influence political decisions, which affects individuals differently. For example, many moral decisions involve safety, such as having to wear a helmet while riding a motorcycle. In this case, safety outweighs liberty, but should the government necessarily tell me how to protect myself? If I kill myself, that’s on me, not the general public.

Just some food for thought
