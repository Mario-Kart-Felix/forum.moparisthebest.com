This site became a notorious surprise image and Internet meme, and was—and, through external hosting, still is—used regularly for bait-and-switch pranks, preventing hot-linking in a hostile manner, and defacing websites, in order to provoke extreme reactions. Even though the display of the site was removed in 2004, its images are mirrored throughout the Internet today.
The Goatse.cx image is sometimes used by website authors in attempt to get websites to stop hot-linking from them. By replacing the hot-linked image with the embarrassing image the moment hot-linking has been discovered, an unsubtle message would be sent to the offending website's operators, viewable to all who view the web page in question.[23] As an example, in 2007, Wired.com hot-linked to another site in an article about the "sexiest geeks of 2007"; the said site swapped the hot-linked image with one from Goatse.cx.[24] This action is practiced by website owners either because bandwidth is an issue to them, or simply for the enjoyment of pranking.

Images on the site such as hello.jpg and others have become subjects of parodies, mirrors, and tributes.[25]
