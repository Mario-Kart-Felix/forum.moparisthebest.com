H1N1 is a subtype Influenza type A, which is the most common strain of influenza transferred among humans. The current strains of influenza that are present today include H1N1 and H3N2, both of which are subtypes of Influenza A. H1N1 is not transferred from or to swine. As far as I know, no strain of Influenza A can even be transferred from or to swine. H1N1 was also the cause of the 1918 Spanish flu pandemic. Any of you who took a history course would know that the 1918 pandemic wiped out more people than World War I (estimations range 20-100 million dead). Of course the news wouldn’t say that.
