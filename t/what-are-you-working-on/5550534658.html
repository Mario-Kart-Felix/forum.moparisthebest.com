Z-Order curve is just a spatial hashing algorithm, it turns N-dimensional coordinates to 1-Dimensional coordinates with a special property where you can effectively do 1-Dimensional range searches on N-dimensional space.

You can use a binary tree to model N-dimensional space and do VERY efficient range searching.

Here’s another example but this one doesn’t play nicely with the fact that Z-Order bits are packed like: xyxyxyxyx which makes it very easy to encode/decode. A majority of spatial hashing algorithms are recursive, which means it takes more computations as the magnitude of your scale increases.
