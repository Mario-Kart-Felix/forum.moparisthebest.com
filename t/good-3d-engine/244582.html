zstars July 31, 2016, 3:17am #10

Axiom is said to be somewhat dated / inactive. They chose to make a full port, so they always lag behind the real Ogre and they don’t support all features. If you can use native libraries (dlls), you might wanna use Mogre instead. A few classes are ported for efficiency, but it is mostly bindings only. Can be used from C# and the other .NET languages, and is usually relatively up to date as compared to the main Ogre version. Also, precissely because they are bindings, you have a much easier access to other ogre plugins. (Especifically, there are already a MogreNewt (Physics Library) and a MOIS (Input Library), plus some others).

About XNA and OpenGL, these are indeed much lower level. If you wanna learn the basics of 3D graphics, dealing with things such as transformation / projection matrices, scene managing, etc, you might wanna use one of them instead. They are harder to use though, you get good results much slower, and you need a certain math / gfx background. XNA is slightly higher level than OpenGL (Note though, that it doesn’t use it), being a somewhat “light” wrapper for DirectX, plus having quite a few other libraries.
