The_Un-Named August 4, 2016, 7:29am #5

Mikeyzzz, the question states that the person counted the heads, and asks how many people and horses there are, therefore the number of heads given includes horse heads.

The question provides the following information: the amount of wheels and the amount of heads, assuming that a typical car has 4 wheels and that since the number of wheels is divisible by 4, then presumably there are 49 cars. You also have to assume that for every car there is at least 1 person as a driver, so there are at least 49 drivers, that gives a headcount of 25 left.

Deciding what to classify the remaining 25 heads as is a little tricky. The question is extremely vague and you really don’t know what the question is trying to test. You can leave it as simply, there are 49 cars, at least 49 people, and presumably 25 horses. The question however could be testing to see how well you think outside the box, or how well you think inside the box. How many horses are typically raced at a race track? If the local horse racing only accepts 8 starters, you could explain that you think there are 66 people and 8 horses. The question may trick you into thinking that the car has to be a 4 wheel car, there could be any number of 3 wheel cars so long as they add up to multiples of 4, which would reduce the amount of cars for example. How you answer the question depends on how you interpret what it is trying to test for.

I was assuming the horses had two-wheeled cart with a rider. But why are the cars in a horse race?

Each horse/rider pair is two heads and two tires.
Each car is one head and four tires.

He counted the amount of heads and tires, but I assume he counted himself too, so I’m subtracting him from the rest of the problem.

tires = 196
heads = 74 - 1 = 73

(There is as many heads as tires for a horse driver pair, so I find how many cars it needs to be on the track to fulfil that requirement)
heads - x = tires - 4x
3x = tires - (heads - 1)
3x = 196 - 73
x = 41

41 four-wheeled cars with a driver.

You have then left
32 tires and 32 heads.

16 (2 * tires + 2 * heads) = 16 horses with rider in a cart.
People = 74 - 16 = 58

So there is 41 cars and 58 people (Including yourself).
