This is my new proposed way of handling staff through an RSPS. This is more handling your staff team like you would a team of workers in the workplace and focuses on cutting out badly explained and drawn out issues. This should solve any form of tardiness due to lack of efficiency in a staff team.

Below you’ll find a list of positions and their proposed spaces and roles. These are in decending order of seniority. 1 being the top, 5 being lowest. Also please bare in mind this is made for the server I’m the community manager/chief of staff on at the minute, with around 130-150 players online.

1 - Senior Management.
Decides all decisions and handles all serious and escalated reports and appeal systems. ALL decisions and ideas must be ran past the senior management before being put into action. This should cut out all false acts of developers. Senior Management also deal with any and all queries by users upon staff, e.g abusive remarks then hands out the decided punishment if the statement be true.

You must remember that Senior Management must work as a team or this entire system will not work.

2 - Developmental Sector.
Must be fluent in the programming language of which they were originally recruited with and must not be working on any other major teams and/or projects. Must not have a problem with tight deadlines and must be a mature and trustworthy person. Good programming habits is a must, good documentation, use of conventions etc. Must also have very good grammar and punctual knowledge. Must pass all ideas and suggestions they filter from the forums on to senior management to be inspected and provided with sufficient evidence that it would benefit the server.

3 - Head Of Moderation.
Has the highest amount of rights as a moderator. Controls the entire moderation team in conjunction with the Senior Management team. Must write up rotas and schedules to be passed by Senior Management and then put up months in advance for when moderators must be on the server. Of course they can come on when they wish, but between x and y hours they must be online. Leave for certain circumstances will be allowed of course. Must have great community interaction and mustn’t have a problem with putting in extra work to make sure the moderation team is as efficient as it can be. ALL serious reports and reports against staff must be reviewed by the head of moderation before being passed onto Senior Management as a quality control method.

4 - Senior Moderation.
Senior Moderation have the second highest rights as a moderator. Moderates all aspects of the server and forums, a global moderator if you will. Must be very knowledgable about the server and have enough free time to manage around 12 hours per week on the server doing their part of the schedule. Must have very wide knowledge range concerning the players and their tendencies and how to handle things quickly and efficiently before everything gets out of hand and has to get further members of staff involved. Must handle reports against all members of the community and be prepared to pass any they believe they can’t fulfil properly onto the head of moderation.

5 - Junior Moderation.
Junior Moderation was previously the rank of support staff, this has been scrapped and all of them promoted to moderators. These members are split into forums and in game. The forums junior moderation team must moderate the forums in their entirety and quality control ALL reports. If these members think that they have found a report that could be true, they lock the post and tell the user they have forwarded it on to the senior moderation staff. This branch just generally keep the forums nice and clean and running smoothly. The in game branch of the junior moderation team must have great presence within a community and be friendly at all times but must be able to be firm when enforcing the rules if it is needed. These moderators are the people who fix out the members in game and handle account recoveries, small outbursts and confrontations and be able to enforce the rules on their closest friends.

As I said before, this is designed for a rather large team and hasn’t been put into place yet which is why I’m posting it here to get your feedback and whether to make slight changes or not regarding that feedback.

Regards,
Ruby.
