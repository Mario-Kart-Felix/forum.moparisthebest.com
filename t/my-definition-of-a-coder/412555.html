I come across kids on my server who call themselves coders and when I ask them simple questions like, “how do you declare a variable?” they have no idea. And if you don’t know what that is then you have a lot to learn.

My definition of a coder is someone who can create their own class, understand variables, casting, and types, and can create methods and functions with a keen understanding of parameters. Then they have to know how to use and implement their class in another class without needing help. If you can do those basic things, you can be called a coder.

You arent a coder if you download a source and move some npcs around and then upload it. Thats just looking up numbers and plugging them in. You aren’t a coder if you can follow tutorials on how to add minigames and commands. That’s just copy and paste and takes no effort. A kid came up to me today and said, “I’m making a nazi zombie mini game.” and I said, “Really? Is it all of your code?” he said yup. I went onto google typed runescape private server natzi zombies and the first link was a tutorial on here on how to do just that.

So that is my definition of a coder and please don’t call yourself a coder if you dont know how to do the things said above.

To be called a coder you have to have original ideas, not just ones someone else came up with that you copied and pasted.
