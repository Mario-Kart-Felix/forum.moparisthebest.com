Increase your Download and Upload Speed!

I went from downloading 2.9 Mb/s to downloading 15.23 Mb/s. A lady from the FiOS Verizon DSL company told me about it and it worked very well! Download the TCP Optimizer on this page.
http://www.speedguide.net/downloads.php

Before doing anything, try running a speed test before and after the change so you can see any differences.
You can use this Speed Test to accurately measure your speed. Make sure you’re not downloading or uploading while the test is in progress.

Run the program and backup your settings by clicking on File > Backup current settings. Then change the top bar to your Maximum Download Speed. You can get your Maximum download speed by talking with your ISP. I spoke with mine and they told me the max speed was 15 MB download so that is why I set it to 15 MB. Next click on “Optimal Settings” which is on the bottom. Here are my settings.

Click on Apply Changes, and then click on OK to save the changes. You will be prompted to restart your computer. Do so, and your speed should be much faster. I haven’t tried any of the other settings yet, but feel free to.

Run the speed test again and post any changes. High chance that they will be very good!

If the result is bad, then open the program and click on File > Restore backed up settings (if you backed up your settings) or Restore Windows Default settings.

~Ron

I have a question though... What do you mean "Maximum Download speed"?
The maximum download speed your internet provider says you have. Most people don't download at their maximum speed. My maximum speed given to me by my provider is 15 MB/s so I moved the tab to 15000 KB which is the same as 15 MB. So if your maximum speed is 250 KB, move the tab the 250 KB. Understand now? :)
