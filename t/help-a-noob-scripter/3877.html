var
i,x,y:integer;

This part declares your varibles that you will use in the script, this is helpful when you do random mouse movements, go you can click where you moved your mouse, and is used to repeat scripts several times.

i:=random(500)
writeln(inttostr(i))

Okay the first thing that it does is declares your varible i, the := is what declares it(assignment) the random(500) gives it a random number from 0 to 500, if you did 1+random(500) then its 1 to 500, then it writes this line in the debug box, the line is the varible i, wich is a integer, so we have to convert it to string with inttostr(i) (integer to string).

getmousepos(x,y)
movemouse(x+(100),y+(100))

Okay the getmousepos(x,y) gets the current position of your mouse, and stores it as the varibles x and y, then the movemouse(x+(100),y+(100)), this moves your mouse in a straight line from your current mouse position to x+100 and y+100, movemouse is very detactable, never use it on rs.

wait(100)
movemousesmooth(x,y)

This part will wait 100 milliseconds(1000 is a second), then it will move your mouse smoothly to x,y(wich you defined earlier) this is still detacble becasue its not so smooth(it does a jagged line), srl and squig2 contains a much better script for this.
