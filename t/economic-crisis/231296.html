We are in times of economic crisis.

In August 2007, many banks stopped offering low-rate mortgages, they didn’t want to lend so riskily anymore. They started calling it the “credit crunch”.

Around November 2007, the British bank Northern Rock was given an emergency load from the Bank of England, it almost collapsed as people rushed to withdraw their savings. After a week or so, the British government ended the run on the bank by saying that savings in every bank would be guaranteed for up to £15000.

An American bank called Bear Stearns was given an emergency load from the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, very similar to the Northern Rock situation, except Bear Stearns only does investments, so there was no run on the bank.

The price of crude oil rose to about $150 per barrel.

The bank Merrill lynch has been slowly sold off these past few months, its now owned 80% by Chinese people.

A few weeks ago, the mortgage guarantors Fannie Mae and Freddy Mac were nationalised by the American government to stop their collapse. Their combined debt is worth around 3 Trillion USD.

A week or so ago, the 3rd largest travel company XL collapsed, leaving many people stranded in foreign countries, and many more losing their holidays.

And just now, the bank Lehman Brothers has collapsed.
