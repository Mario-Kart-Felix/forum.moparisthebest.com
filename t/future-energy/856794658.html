Nuclear Fusion-Are you guys serious? It takes 50 years before a powerplant even begins making electricity, and then you have the risks. You have to store the waste somewhere where it is not to harm the environment and stops becoming radioactive. (This could be 100 years to 1000+ years depending on the elements half-life.). And then you have to worry about plant failures, try to remember Chernobyl. Trust me, you do NOT want to get radiated. The side effects are horrific. Mutations occur (as in cancer, not as in “superpowers”). And death.

Turbines-alright, listen up. Turbines are generators (basically). They run on the principle of Faraday’s Electro-magnetic Induction. If you have a magnet inside coiled wire and move it, electricity is produced. Wind turbines, gas generators, dams, and any other things (including coal plants, nuclear plants, and geothermal plants) produce electricity by rotating the magnets inside the wires. The question is, which way is more practical and effective?

Solar power is VERY expensive due to the cost of Silicon (what the panels are made of). There is also giant solar power farms in United States deserts that don’t use silicone solar panels. They use mirrors to reflect heat onto pipes filled with oil. The oil heats up and transfers its heat to water which steams up and turns turbines. This is effective because no oil is burned and it is recycled back to get heated again.
