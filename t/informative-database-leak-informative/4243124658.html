For SMF, the password hashing algorithm is currently: sha1(strtolower($username) . $password) - thus, the a hash of t4secretpassword123; so, unless your ‘word list’ has generated my username (to lowercase) then my password concatenated, you’re going to have a pretty rough time.

The time complexity assuming a 6 character username (lowercase) and 8 character password is now:

Username: 42^6 = 5489031744
Password: 68^8 = 4.571632397×10¹⁴

Concatenated: 2.509383535×10²⁴

So that would mean for a dictionary to be complete with all username that are 6 characters and have 8 character long passwords, it would have to contain 2.509383535×10²⁴ entries. Assuming you’re generating this dictionary at 1,000 hashes per second, you’re left with 2.509383535×10²¹ seconds (795,720,299,000,000 years / 795.72 trillion years).
