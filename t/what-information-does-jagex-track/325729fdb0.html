semaphore July 31, 2016, 4:11pm #64

iirc wL has a character that got invited to be a player mod.

None of my bots were ever invited but then again I implemented the auto reporting late in my rs hacking life. I still stand by what I’ve said in the past. They detect you. It happens. You’ll likely make mistakes that trip some alarms. These triggers/marks/etc are taken in context with other factors. When you get reported your last 60 seconds is packaged into a nice pretty bow and pushed onto a queue for some minimum wage customer service rep to view. There are other factors at play but eventually if your account has tripped enough wires it will fast track to a rep’s viewer. Here they watch the report evidence. From there you have to be aces with your bot to get the rep to believe you are a real person and not a bot. Sometimes, I imagine, it doesn’t matter - ultimately if the rep thinks you’re a bot there’s no amount of good code you can write to trick them. This is where tripping alarms flippantly is damaging. I assume (because that’s all I can do really) that my bots have never tripped enough alarms that when reported have swayed the rep into believing beyond all hope that I was a bot…

Let’s be real - how many of us can be fooled by any of the bots out in public? Your bot doesn’t have to be perfect all the time… It just has to be perfect for the 60 seconds after you get reported. And even then it may be too late based on your ‘history’.

So to sum up, yes player reports are important. But the details involved with you getting a ban are complicated.

For example there are some things that you can do which will get you instantly banned. (i.e. dropping -2 billion gold, and laughing at the poor ass that picked it up comes to mind - it was great one dumbass picked it up, then dropped it immediately having lost ALL the gold in his inventory… then another ass picked it up… and so on… it was hilarious. I don’t know how it stayed quiet - it affected quite a few players and what made it so funny was that -2billion gold looks exactly like 2billion gold on the ground - only if you paid close attention would you realize it was negative) So - I cannot say that I’ve never had a character banned… but that was by far one of the most memorable.

but there are other things that you can do which just chalks marks on your account. All of these things are taken into account (unless they are instaban infractions) when the report comes your way.

To be honest though, from what I’ve observed first hand, I’ve been reported many times on many accounts I’m almost certain. Most of us do the same thing when we’re reporting someone… we go idle and don’t talk and simply sit there. My bot’s have recorded that happening around them many many many times. I’m sure I’ve been reported alot, but the important thing is having very few wires tripped and making for dang sure that when the rep looks at the replay, your bot looks as human as possible. One of the best ways you can find out if your bot looks human - implement your own playback system. (Do it… wL did it and inspired me to write my own). Then watch your own bots. If you can objectively watch them and come to the conclusion they are human - rejoice… if not… keep working. In the end, you’ll never really know when you’re reported… so your bots need to be convincing all the time.
