Ok, so here we go with the report I promised earlier on 2 different threads (Halloween…ugly and I don’t remember what, because it was several days ago). So, here we go:
/////////
The Basic Story
/////////
When party hats were first came to the RuneScape world, they were very
commonly found and had no store value. Actully, they were so common that players
would sell them to the general stores for absolutely no profit. Later, some
players decided to increase the rarity of party hats by collecting them and
storing them away in their bank accounts. These players would go around
buying every party hat in sight. Because of the low value of party hats, they
collected so many that the hats started to disappear. This seriously affected
the RuneScape economy. At first, players were giving up expensive pieces of
armor for these worthless, paper hats. Yet, after seeing how the prices were
jumping, players decided to hold on to their hats and refused to sell them,
which increased the value even more. Soon, these worthless hats became
treasured collectors items that sold for millions upon millions of gold
pieces.

On the night of November 6, 2003, there were several rumors going around that
RuneScape items could now be duplicated. Not much later, a record amount of
party hat owners and sellers show up in world one market and begin to sell
them for very cheap prices. Near the end of the day, several merchants were
selling full inventories of pink party hats for nearly three fourths of the
price. Fellow RuneScape players become suspicious.

On November 7, 2003, Jagex has announced that a major bug has been discovered
and that several RuneScape players were duplicating items, mainly party hats
and Christmas crackers. These duplications are against the RuneScape rules
but even more importantly; it highly effected the RuneScape economy. Prices
dropped dramatically and several players have quit the game because of high
money losses. Jagex announces that they will reward the first person to
report how the bug works a free lifetime membership. Within a couple hours,
Jagex receives over 600 messages and they quickly shut down all the RuneScape
servers at approximately 10:45 A.M. (United States Central time). The bug is
quickly fixed yet no actions have been taken against the duplicators and
duplications. Jagex also announces that they are trying their best to take
care of the unfair wealth and cheaters. They advise their fellow RuneScape
players to neither buy nor sell any party hats and to drop any party hats you
currently own in which you believe could be a duplication.

Unsurprisingly, RuneScape players don’t listen to a word that Jagex says. That
very night, numerous low level characters stand around world 1 market trying
to purchase and sell party hats at very low prices. The RuneScape economy
seems doomed because of the tragic money losses. Very few actually understand
what this bug was and how it worked. It is still unknown as to what actions
Jagex will take against these duplications and tragic money losses. Majority
of the on-going rumors say that the duplicates will be easy to spot and delete
from the Jagex database. Other rumors say that party hats will finally be un-
tradable to control all the unfair wealth and greed.

//////
Weeks later
//////

Still not everything has been taken care of. The Christmas cracker duplicates
have been deleted but duplicated party hats are still among us and the prices
are still at an all time low. Rumors of party hats being dropped again were
going around yet it was not true. Unfortunately, this seems like the end of
the duplication issue. Party hats are everywhere and the prices are low yet
still rising.

//////
Months later
//////

A final update. The results are finally verified. Yes, the few players that
understood the duplication bug were in fact banned. The bug was fixed,
duplicated Christmas crackers were deleted, but party hat duplications are
still around. Party hat prices are finally rising very slowly and will
eventually pass the recent peaks. There are no more worries about value
drops. The whole ordeal is over.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~*
///////
“Future Drops”
///////

Many rumors about future drops have been going around. Some say that Jagex
has put a stop to holiday items because of the “unfair wealth” that they have
caused. Yet, if Jagex does continue holiday item drops, the rules that were
placed upon bunny ears and scythes will affect all future holiday drops.

Yet, after 2 holiday item cancellations, it seems as if this is the end of all
the holiday drops. Certain events might take place during holidays in the new
RuneScape but never in the classic version.

Hope you enjoyed this report
