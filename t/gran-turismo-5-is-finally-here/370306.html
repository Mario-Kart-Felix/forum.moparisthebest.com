tL August 1, 2016, 1:10am #6

And it is an absolutely brilliant game for most of us fans. You’ll see people who jumped on the hype train, seeing the graphics, and then not liking it, but then again, they never would have done - regardless of development time.

There are some disappointments, like the whole standard/premium car thing (some cars are more detailed than others, others don’t have cockpit view, working windscreen wipers, headlights, etc), but the other things like the full weather and time of day features, go karting and other special events all make up for it.

Most of all, and one that I was a bit weary of, was the improvement to the driving physics. Now, GT5: Prologue wasn’t much of a change from previous games and I was hoping they didn’t just keep that system for the full game, and they didn’t - they have much more depth and personality (per car).

I like Forza 3, it is a great game, but it isn’t the same type of game. That may sound a little strange given they’re both driving simulators, but GT always gives you enough and in the right manner to get you involved in the entire experience, A-Spec, B-Spec, Collecting and Tuning cars, etc.
