QUESTION #1
A guy dropped a coing that weights 1 gram from the eiffel tower. The height that the guy was at was 120meters. What “position” energy did the coing have after falling 80 meters?

I also want the coins “moving” energy after 80 meters.

Ok this sounds like some basic physics stuff. Here goes:
If you want to find position and moving energies, you are looking for gravitational potential energy (PE = mgh) and kinetic energy (KE = (1/2)mv^2) respectively.

Throughout the entire process, no energy must be lost or gained, therefore, the inital gravitational potential energy (at the top of the tower) equals the final kinetic energy just before the coin hits the ground:
mgh = (1/2)mv^2

Ok, here’s the initial position energy of the coin:
Variables: m = .01 kg (converting grams to kilograms)
g = 9.81 m/s^2 (acceleration due to gravity)
h = 120 m
PE = mgh
PE = .001kg * 9.81 m/s^2 * 120 m
PE (initial) = 1.1772 Joules

After falling 80 meters, the height is now 40 meters:
PE = mgh
PE = .001kg * 9.81m/s^2 * 40 m
PE (after 80m) = .3924 J (answer to first part)

After falling 80 meters, the coin has both position energy and moving energy and the sum of the two is equal to the initial position energy:
PE (initial) = PE (after 80m) + KE (after 80m)
Therefore: KE (after 80m) = PE (initial) - PE (after 80m)
KE = 1.1772 - .3924 J
KE = .7848 J (answer to second part)
