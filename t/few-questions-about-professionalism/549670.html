stCky August 4, 2016, 2:20pm #7

I’ve never used linkedIn or similar and have never had an online portfolio, other than GitHub which I haven’t contributed to publicly.

My first dev job I was head-hunted straight out of my diploma course and since then all my other work opportunities have been via other dev buddy’s I had met through either work or school.

My honest advice would be to get on Twitter and start following local developers/companies/general techies and joining in on dev conversation there, going to meets/workshops locally will also be a very helpful way to network and meet new people. Without a portfolio or experience working with others it would obviously be hard for someone to gauge your skills, so it would be wise to go ahead and start creating any little helpful tools and scripts you can/want. Small tools are fun and easy and having a range of them available and their source would be impressive.

Oh yo, this guy has a port checker, url shortener, server status checker, calculator etc on his website! They aren’t massively complicated to but his interests and range of tools is impressive etc…
