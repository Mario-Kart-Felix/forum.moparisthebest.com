moparisthebest July 31, 2016, 12:52pm #11

Intel led with pentiums over AMD’s K7 by a long shot. Then AMD came out with their Athlon XP line of CPUs and beat Intel in terms of both price and performance at the same time. Shortly afterward AMD came out with their 64-bit CPUs with a new architecture, amd64, which was excellent because it could also run x86 code. AMD was in the lead both by price and performance, until Intel conceded that amd64 was the best 64-bit architecture and started making the Core2duo’s that supported the amd64 instruction set. At that point Intel beat AMD by both price and performance. Now we are up to today, and as people said above, depending on your price range you would be better off going with one or the other, there is no clear winner right now. If you want top of the line, the i7 is pretty much your only choice. If you want more midrange they are a lot more equal and you may be better off going with an AMD.

They are both good CPUs, and you won’t be disappointed with either of them. The first computers I built in high school all had AMDs, because this was when the Athlon XP ruled. The computers I’ve built within the last few years have core2duos, because they were the best bang for the buck. And the computer I built for my parents a few months ago had an AMD, because it was the best value. Personally I like AMD more because they are the underdog competing with Intel who has much more resources and money, but make your own decision.
