BobDole81 July 29, 2016, 9:54pm #2

An extended version of the Pythagorean Theorem is needed…its called the Law of Cosines (you could use the Law of Sines just as well, but I like this one better :D)

As this is the longest side you need to find, you can use this formula: c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - (2ab * cos(gamma)) - where gamma is the largest angle, ie the one opposite from the unknown side. This is a fairly simple equation so I’ll let you work it out yourself from here

Also, if you’re interested, the way this converts to the Pythagorean theorem is if you stick 90 degrees in place of gamma…cos(90) is 0 and so you end up with c^2 = a^2 + b^2.

Yeah, there are 3 variations of it to solve for any side. The other two are:

a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - (2bc * cos(alpha))
b^2 = a^2 + c^2 - (2ac * cos(beta))

Those two are tad different from the pythagorean theorem, but simply its the unknown side squared = the sum of the squares of the other two sides minus the product of 2, the other two sides, and the cosine of the angle opposite of the unknown side…that description is more complicated than than the formula actually
