Immortal Technique:

Well, even if he decides to ddos me theres not much he can do to my comp anyways its already breaking down, takes 5minutes to open a folder, 30minutes to open internet…

That’s not the point. DDoS’ing can’t physically do anything to your computer. It can’t rm -rf your system32, or anything else malicious. The only thing it can really do is send lots of packets to your computer. Now most routers, when they receive a packet, it contains the source address, the destination address, and the port number. Now usually, if a router receives a packet from 1.2.3.4 going to 2.3.4.5 and a port number of 3456, the router will basically do this:

for(Computer c : network) {
    if(c.getIpAddress() == "2.3.4.5") {
        if(c.isListeningOn(3456)) {
            c.sendPacket(packet);
        }
    }
}

That’s pretty much all the router does. Now you can go into the router config, mess with some settings, and block all packets from 2.3.4.5. If the router send the packet to you anyways, the computers firewall adds another layer of protection and blocks any unwanted packets.

tl;dr: He can’t do anything.
