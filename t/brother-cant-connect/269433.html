No. Each computer will have a different ip address.

If you are using a router, your router has given each of your computers a different private ip address and your router is using the only public address. So from the Internet side, it may seem that your computers have the same address, it is actually the router they see, not your computers. Your router is using a protocol called NAT (native address translation) to allow your 2 computers to use the same connection.
