Hello there, since a lot of people have problems with forwarding ports on their AT&T modems, and that fact that a large amount of people have AT&T as an ISP, I’m releasing this tutorial today.

Open your browser > type this into your search bar: http://homeportal

You should now arrive at a page like this after you type in the password to your modem.(the default password is located on the back of your modem)

Now click the ‘Gaming and applications’ link.

You should now arrive at a page like this:

Click, ‘Applications, Pinholes and Dmz’.

You should now arrive at a page like this:

Now select your computer, it should be in the area where I pointed to mine.

After clicking on your PC, you should end up on a page like this:

Click add a new user-defined application.

NOTE: It might require you to type in your modem password again, so be ready.

Once you click it, you should end up at a page like this:

Type in all the following information, then hit ‘Add to List’.

NOW, LISTEN CAREFULLY.

Do what I did over again. Put in the same information, but this time, for the protocol, CHOOSE UDP INSTEAD OF TCP.

Then add it to the list once again.

Now it should look like this:

Now hit ‘BACK’, at the bottom of the above page.

Now, you should be back at the page where you chose your PC. Choose/highlight your PC again.

Now in the scroll box which is labeled, Application List, highlight any item in the list, then type on your keyboard: RSPS Example
IF you labeled it something else, then type that in.

Once you type it in, it should be highlighted. ONCE IT IS, CLICK ADD, on the right side of the Application list.

Once it says it is successfully added, click save.
DONE.

NOTE: Make sure you make a firewall exception, or turn your firewall off on your PC.

Also make sure your anti-virus doesn’t use a firewall or block ports.

Enjoy. Hope I help out a LOAD of people. :rolleyes:
