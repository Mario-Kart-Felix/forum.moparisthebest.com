Never do this ever ever ever! (hydraulics that is)

Back ontopic, get the VIN number and run it through one of those online checkers to see if it is actually an original 396SS. Also, the odometer on that car only goes up to 99,999. This means that 22k miles showing on the odometer could mean 22k, 122k, 222k, 322k etc etc. Check it out on the title.

Also, on all old cars like this, check inside the trunk (you should be able to see the inside of the quarter panels) and feel inside of all the wheel wells and such for bondo. If everything he says is true (actually 22k miles, original 396SS, perfect body with no bondo/fiberglass etc) then it is worth every penny of $8500, otherwise it might not be.

If you get really serious about it, check prices on similar cars on ebay and such to see what they are going for.
