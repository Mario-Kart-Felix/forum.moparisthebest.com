The mind of a Pessimist

General Discussion
The_Reaper_X July 31, 2016, 4:05pm #1

A piece I wrote at school today.

The mind acts mutual with it’s surroundings. The people find a profound essence in the simplest of things. We exasperate our use of these, and when the true entity is revealed in it’s entirety, we question our establishments prior to this epiphany. A resolution bursts, yet revolution hurts. We’re hazed with our own beliefs so often that we’re oppressed by our own ignorance. We’re incapable of breaching the epitome of the typical thought process. We contain ourselves so compact, we have nothing else to breath but the arrogance we frolic in. We’re blinded by false perception, created by incompetence and misconception. We provoke through antagonistic corruption, and regress to our pawns that support us oh so voluntarily under the same said power. A hierarchy collapses in the distance only to be regulated once again in an inefficient derivation, expelling the originality and beauty it once held. It becomes a game of plucking the flower, only to replace the pedals with an artificial emptiness. The mind will often contemplate habit over choice and vice-versa, only to find that there is no real solution to perpetuation. We’ve created a spectrum within society in which we follow, yet for what is acceptable, we build upon nonconformity. Ironically, we manage to contradict ourselves for as long as we coexist – coexist being the average social minds.

Thoughts?
