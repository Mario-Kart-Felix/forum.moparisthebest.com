There’s a whole bunch of information on wikipedia regarding age of consent and such in America.
Across America, the minimum is 16 and the maximum seems to be 18. It’s quite random, and I don’t know why it isn’t uniform.

According to wikipedia, the age of consent is actually 16 in Ohio.

Ohio

The age of consent in Ohio is 16 as specified by Section 2907.04 of Ohio legislation. However there exists a close in age exception where an offender can be charged only if 18 years of age or older. However in that case, it is possible for both minors to be charged as “unruly” if brought to court {§ 2151.022}.

2907.04 Unlawful sexual conduct with minor.

    (A) No person who is eighteen years of age or older shall engage in sexual conduct with another, who is not the spouse of the offender, when the offender knows the other person is thirteen years of age or older but less than sixteen years of age, or the offender is reckless in that regard. {§ 2907.04}.</blockquote>

Ohio has a close in age exception, so a 17 year old could sleep with a 15 year old and use that defence.
However, if the youngest party was 16 and gave consent, she could sleep with whoever.

I trust the wikipedia information as they seem to be excerpts straight from the constitutions of each state.

Anyway, some examples of close-in-age laws I found:

Colorado

The age of consent in Colorado is 17, however there exists in the legislation close in age exceptions, which allow those aged 15 and 16 to engage in acts with those less than ten years older and those less than 15 to engage in acts with those less than four years older. However a 17-year-old can not legally consent to person that is in position of trust.

18-3-402(1) Any actor who knowingly inflicts sexual intrusion or sexual penetration on a victim commits sexual assault if: (d) At the time of the commission of the act, the victim is less than fifteen years of age and the actor is at least four years older than the victim and is not the spouse of the victim; or (e) At the time of the commission of the act, the victim is at least fifteen years of age but less than seventeen years of age and the actor is at least ten years older than the victim and is not the spouse of the victim;

Delaware

The age of consent in Delaware is 18, but it is legal for teenagers aged 16 and 17 to engage in sexual intercourse as long as the older partner is younger than 30.

Florida

The age of consent in Florida is 18, but close in age exemptions exist. By law, the exception permits a person 23 years of age or younger to engage in legal sexual activity with a minor aged 16 or 17.

In short, it’s not ‘hard’ to legislate exceptions. It certainly gets very wordy though, and reading the legislation of a few states actually did my head in.

Cases like these are on the edge of what is right and what isn’t. If I were the guy, I wouldn’t do it. Let’s just put the question about if it’s legal or not aside for a moment. The law says it’s illegal, and the law is what people are judged by.

Imagine the court case. Especially in the US, where the jury has the final word, a victim can act like a victim and convince the jury, even if the victim isn’t really a victim. What the girl can do here, is to say that she didn’t agree to have sex with the guy, and then there’s no way the jury will let the guy go. The girl can demand a huge amount of money for damages, and she’ll have fun for the rest of her life.

It’s hard to find out if the girl gave consent. Is a 13 year old even able to decide for him/herself? You’d expect that she wouldn’t date a guy that old, but she did. Did she know that he was that old, or did he lie to her? A case like this comes with a lot of questions, and I know that people like to judge, but let’s leave that to the judges. A case like this shouldn’t be done by a jury in the first place. Only a judge can be rational enough to see what’s really there.
