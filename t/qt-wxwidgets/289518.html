QT on the other side renders the controls itself, trying to imitate native controls, being ... more prone to look & feel discrepancies

The primary reason I switched to Qt from wxWidgets was because wxWidgets does focus more on WINAPI than the other platforms supported by the “cross-platform library,” which leads to look & feel discrepancies between versions of the application you’re trying to make. For example, it doesn’t do actual MDI in Linux, it just uses tabbing as an alternative. You’re right, wxWidgets uses native controls whenever possible, even if it leads to discrepancies; they seem to honor speed above the cross-platform aspect of a “cross-platform library.” Therefore Qt does a better job at achieving this goal.
