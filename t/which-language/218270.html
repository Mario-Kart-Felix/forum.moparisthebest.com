Mopman July 31, 2016, 12:04am #5

I recommend Ruby as a first programming language.

  • It has a forgiving syntax, with plenty of ‘syntactic sugar’ to make things that are complex in other languages simple.

  • There is an abundance of newbie-focused material compared to other languages. (There’s a tutorial written almost entirely in drawings of foxes, for example…)

  • It’s a very OO-centred language, which sets you up perfectly for Java/C++/wherever you might go later - language which use the same concepts but in a much less forgiving way, and you will be well equipped.

Of course, you could just stick with Ruby as your programming language of choice, if you never write anything that requires blazing speed
