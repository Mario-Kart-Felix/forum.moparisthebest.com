durial12 August 1, 2016, 11:07am #5

Go into the CONFIG folder and fish out:

NPCDrops.cfg

I’ll explain the layout here.

Yours may look a little like this:

[code]//Man
npcdrop = 1 995 100 15
npcdrop = 1 526 1 100

//Guard
npcdrop = 9 995 30 15
npcdrop = 9 526 1 100
[/code]

I’ll just take the man drops for now:

//Man npcdrop = 1 995 100 15 npcdrop = 1 526 1 100

Right, step by step:

npcdrop = 1

This just defines which NPC you are setting the drop for. The NPC ID for a man is 1.

npcdrop = 1	995	100	15

So here, you can see that NPC 1 (MAN) is going to drop the item: 995. This is COINS. The 100 means he will drop 100 coins. The 15 means 15% of the time. (So that he WON’T drop these every time). If the last number was 100, then he would drop 100 coins every time he dies.

Fire_capez August 1, 2016, 11:07am #11

A float is a type of variable just like an integer. To declare a float you would do the same thing you do when you declare an int.

E.g:

int x; This means that x is a whole number, hence it being an integer)

float x; This means that x is not a whole number, it can be used for numbers that have decimals)

So just look for something to do with your npc drops which creates the percentage of drops and changing any variables that are declared as integers to floats.
