To change a shop price:

I’ll just give you an example.

  1. Open up Shops.cfg. (Using Notepad if you’d like.)

  2. Look around for the shop you are trying to change the price of an item in.

  3. If you see how on the top it repeats: Item, amount, item, amount… you should see this.

  4. Under the “item” spot, is the ID of the item. Then, next to it, under “amount,” is the price of that item.

  5. So to change the price, simply change what’s under the “amount” spot.

Ah, well, then try this. I did a bit of looking myself.

  1. Open up your “client.java” and search for 1.26

  2. Keep searching of 1.26 and change each one to 1 (This is some kind of multiplier)

  3. Now, to physically alter the price of an item, go to “item.cfg” and after the shop name where you see the numbers. ( You know, 1 1 1 0 0 0 0 …) Change the first three.

  • For example, I did 100 100 100 for a Pot, the item pot. Now it’s worth 125 coins. So, I guess you can play around with it.

*I use the TestScape Source, and General Stores have a 1.25% price increase. (Which explains the 125 gp for a pot. In the Varrock General Store.) To fix that, search for 1.25 in the client.java. Change to 1.

  • After more testing, it seems that if you simply change all three numbers to the same price, it makes the item worth that price. For example, when I change the first three number for the pot to 250 250 250, it’s now worth 250 gp. If it’s 1 1 1 it’s worth 1 gp. If I do 100 100 100, it’s worth 100 gp. There you go=)
