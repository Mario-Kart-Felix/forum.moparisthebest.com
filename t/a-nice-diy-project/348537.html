The Fuzebox is a fully open-source, DIY 8-bit game console. It is designed specifically for people who know a little bit of programming to expand into designing and creating their own video games and demos. A full-featured core runs in the background and does all the video and audio processing so that your code stays clean and easy to understand.

[ul][li]Full 256 simultaneous output colors, 240x224 pixel resolution[/li]
[li]Tile & sprite support[/li]
[li]Two player ports, either with Super Nintendo or classic Nintendo controllers[/li]
[li]NTSC RCA composite and S-video out (PAL not supported at this time)[/li]
[li]4 channel output mono audio for music and effects[/li]
[li]SD/MMC card support for future expansion[/li]
[li]Built on an Atmel AVR core, 64KB flash and 4KB of RAM[/li]
[li]Main microcontroller chip is preprogrammed with an STK500-compatible (sometimes referred to as Arduino-compatible) bootloader[/li]
[li]Write game code in C, using fully open source tools on any platform[/li][/ul]
