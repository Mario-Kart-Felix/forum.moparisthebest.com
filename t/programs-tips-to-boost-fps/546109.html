This. A game shouldn’t take up anywhere close to what you have available in RAM, the only time it will effect your gameplay is if your RAM consumption is in the higher numbers. While it’s easy to fill up 4GB of RAM, if nothing else is running in the background while you’re playing a game, and the RAM usage is low, 4GB to 16GB won’t show a change. Other than disabling tasks to free up disk and RAM there’s nothing you can do without replacing parts. If I was you I’d upgrade to 8GB of RAM personally, just so you can handle more at a time. The best thing for improving your FPS, much like Limits said is to get a better graphics card, more VRAM and other such things is how you will achieve a better FPS. Another thing that might improve your gameplay is getting a faster HDD. Read/Write speeds can be a big deal in games that make those calls alot.
