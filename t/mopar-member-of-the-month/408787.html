[size=18pt]What’s the point?
To give real community members more incentive to post quality, helpful and polite posts, or for new members to begin with quality posting, and to submit real reports, instead of petty a pointless ones.

The incentive is, your name to be displayed as the Mopar Member of the Month, to be recognised for your quality filled posts and for help making the community that much better.

[size=18pt]How does it work?
Just go about your normal business poting on MoparScape.org (only), and if someone spots you being exceptional amongst the community, they can post here and recommend you for the Mopar Member of the Month spot.

Then we’ll have 5 days before the first of each month, voting on the candidates been put forward by you, and then the winner will have their name displayed in large, on the title and possibly in people’s signatures.

It’s all about being recognised for being a quality and valued member of the community. I believe that if people have something to look forward to, they may want to put more effort into their posting.

Think of it as Employee of the Month.

[size=18pt]How can you be a quality member of the community?
Good spelling and grammar is a first, although I don’t believe people should really be judged on their spelling, grammar and neatness of their forum posts. I know quite a few quality members of the community who don’t care for capitalizing, and “you” instead of “u” etc., so this is not necessary, but a bonus.

You should post with consideration to others. Remember that before you hit alt + s or the submit button, that what you’re posting isn’t directly offending someone. If someone is abusive or sadistic towards you, you’re best bet is to reply displaying the facts and not using abusive words yourself, to ignore the poster and talk about something else or to simply not post at all. You’ll be more respected if you ignore people’s name calling.

Go out of your way. If you feel someone is genuine with their help topic and they really don’t understand what they are doing, or really can’t find what they are looking for, point them in the right direction. If that person has showed evidence of helping themselves first, then the spoon-feeding rule should no longer apply. Go out of your way for them. Treating people the same way you wanted to be treated goes a long way.

Report topics or posts that really need moderator attention. Posts that could cause harm to other member’s computers, are inappropriate (pornographic or racist), in the wrong section or stupid and annoying. Someone using the server help section to complain about a ban they received needs to be removed. A junior member posting a link to some “amazing” site they are advertising about needs to be removed, in case it contain anything malicious. Someone simply posting “lol” in a post is considered spam, but probably isn’t worthy of a report.

Contribute to the community. Post a topic, a discussion thread, a unique download/tutorial or something never done before in the development section. These sort of things get you recognised and respected. Respect is an important thing, because it can be put to good use in and outside of the forums. Just because it’s the internet and we can’t physically see each other, does not mean we shouldn’t respect one another. It’s the whole point of being a part of a community. Contributing something unique could even inspire some one else to do the same. It’s a like a domino effect.

[size=18pt]The rules
You may only nominate one person a month.

You may not change your vote once it’s been added to the first post.

You may not nominate yourself.
