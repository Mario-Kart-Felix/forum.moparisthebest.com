The fact you call it “soccer” automatically means you are bad at football!

But seriously, what position do you play? Also you don’t need to learn skill moves to be able to dribble. But if I’m going to be honest, and I am not lying, pure elite dribbling you are born with. If you are a striker and you can’t dribble, you should rely on your shooting and aggression, having pace will help too. Look at Diego Costa for example, he isn’t the most technical of players but he is world class nonetheless.

Now regarding your mentality. Believe me, being confident helps ALOT. The confidence comes from being around people who believe in you too. You have to have inner strength in order to fight through the tough times. If you don’t believe in yourself how can you expect others to believe in you too? If you lose the ball in a game, don’t be sad just keep trying, play positively and positive things will occur.

Basiclly, you won’t improve your dribbling too much, your shots can improve by practice along with the rest of your game. Watch a lot of the top matches (La liga & champions league) as much as you can and obviously, play as much as you can. well one problem is you’re very very slow. 4.7 is terrible

i don’t know shit about soccer, but you shouldn’t worry as you’re just a sophomore. You’ve got plenty of time to work on whatever you need. I suggest you reach out to coaches yourself though, through e-mails and what not. Don’t wait for them to come to you, you go to them first.

I’m a collegiate track and field athlete, and i speak from experience that my sophomore year in my sport didn’t mean shit. literally didn’t matter at all. NCAA coaches aren’t allowed to start officially recruiting you until after your junior season, so junior year is probably the most important, followed by senior year. Just keep practicing and getting better. I think that your confidence will go up as your skill level goes up.

As for your slow 40 time. Do resistance training and short speed work.

Ex: running as fast as you can up a hill for 30-100 meters/yards
60 meter/yard repeats

I do workouts similar to this, obviously way more in depth though. these are just simple workouts you can do. I probably run around a high 4.2 or 4.3

you can change the distances to whatever you want, but obviously if you’re only timing yourself for 40 meters, running short distances will help the most.
