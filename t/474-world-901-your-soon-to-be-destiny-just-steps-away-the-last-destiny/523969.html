In normal RuneScape, when you are above level 20 wilderness, you may not teleport. While this is still true in World 901, there is another catch. The catch? You may not view your minimap above that level of the wilderness.

No such thing as ‘training areas’
You know how, in a normal server, you’ll see ‘Beginner Training Area’, ‘Intermediate Training Area’, and ‘Hard Training Area’. Or maybe perhaps you have seen that there is one ‘training area’ that has a bunch of monsters just standing there. Does this look logical? My response, no.

The Start
The Start is partly a tutorial, partly a starter kit, and partly a task system. When the player starts, they talk to the founder of the starting city, Yanille, who will, after a while, give you 25,000 coins to start out. He will then proceed by asking you if you’d like to do a tutorial or not. Players have the option to say, “Yes,” or “No,” to the offer.

If the player says, “Yes,” then they will be in a tutorial that is sort of similar to Tutorial Island, however, we are not on an island and we have only one instructor (Guard). At the end, the player will be redirected to do a task. The player MUST do the task or they may not get out of the city of Yanille. Now, if the player selects “No,” they will automatically be redirected to the task.

The task is randomized from a list of them. They are all easy, such as maybe killing a Goblin, a Chicken, a Man, or maybe even thieving a Woman, or socializing by buying a beer at the bar. This is just to remove people who are just account spammers.

The New Wilderness - A reborn mystery land
For many years the wilderness was though of as just a place of murder. Pointless murder. But World 901 has something to offer that is different. We have made a whole new concept for the wilderness. Its not pointless murder, its a historical landscape, reborn.

Long ago, before the Godwar Dungeon was made, a battle of the Gods took place. The battle was so intense, a whole land was reduced to rubble. Evil lurked the land there, as Zamorak had won the fight. As Zamorak stood, laughing so evilly, so bitterly, Guthix had come. Guthix had put Zamorak at rest, until Zamorak had again arised and terrorized the land of World 901. He had then become encapsulated inside the Godwars Dungeon. Nobody knows where he was placed.

This land that was reduced to rubble then produced lava pits. People built upon this land and made a graveyard. But the people made a mistake. A builder had dug something inside the land. It was a strange aura that prevented anyone who walked passed it from teleporting and jammed their map. Man was saddened that day. The god Guthix had then emerged again to bring peace to the land. Guthix did all he could to stop this aura, but the most he could do was throw it further.

This land today is now called the Wilderness. Where had Guthix thrown the aura? He had thrown it into level 20 of the wilderness. Where is this aura exactly? The world may never know.

Add-ons to combat
RuneScape’s combat tends to be the same. Now we aren’t looking to make a new EoC or anything, but we ARE looking to add some nice features.

If the player is using the slash combat style, the player has a chance to make the opponent bleed. If the player is using Ranged, the player has a chance to make an arrow stuck in the opponent’s skin. Also, if the player is using Ranged, and the combat style is accurate then they have a more accurate chance of getting the arrow stuck in the opponents skin. The victim, in turn, will try every turn of theirs to get the arrow out until they succeed doing so. Now, I will summarize every possibility.

Combat Type (Melee or Ranged) — Combat style (Stab, Accurate, Slash, etc.) — Effect — Percent chance for effect
[hr]
Melee — Stab — The ability to make the opponents Attack decrease by 2 - 5. — 10%
Melee — Slash — The ability to make the opponent Bleed. — 5%
Melee — Crush — Crush the player. Inflicting 1/8 of their health. — 10%
Ranged — Make an arrow stuck on the victim. — 5%
[hr]
Effect — Remedies
[hr]
Bleed — Put a bandage on.
Having an arrow stuck on you — Wear Magic Secateurs or wait for the effect to go away. Your player has a 70% chance of taking the arrow out themseves. However, if he does, it will hurt your player considerable damage.
Having a stat drained — Drink a restore potion.
Having yourself get crushed — Wearing something that neutralizes crush effect (chainbodies, or a sq shield, or defenders).

Day and Night System
Imagine going through the land of Gielinor, and it is midnight. Imagine being able to get a blood rune at night bit a cosmic run in the day. Imagine walking around and then some random creature arises from the dead at night. This is the whole concept of the Day and Night system.

Different areas have a different timing. So, for example, Falador could have night time yet Varrock could have day time.

Transportation
Instead of teleporting, you can either walk, run, chariot, or simply teleport. This option interface will occur as soon as you click the teleport. Chariot is the fastest way of transportation, running is 3x slower, and walking is 2x slower than running. The time it takes is determined by tiles.

[center][/center]

Recoil
After doing a special attack, you get recoil damage. The damage itself is determined by what weapon did the special attack. The basic idea of it will be that it hits 25% of the damage given.

Now do note, only SOME weapons will have recoil, not all. Weapons like the barrelchest anchor, saradomin sword, and the armadyl godsword will have this effect. Simple things like the dragon dagger will NOT have this effect.

If the player kills the opponent from the special attack, there is no recoil damage.

World 901 - The Minigame
Runescape’s worst rulebreakers are put in to World 901, an abandoned and corrupt version of Draynor Village. Only one can survive and win their freedom…

Idea
As soon as you start, a player named ‘Host’ will arrive. He will say in all the contestants chat (like dialogue), “Welcome, to World 901 - The Minigame. however_many_people_are_in_the_minigame of the world’s greatest rulebreakers in the game have been put in this minigame, in order to prove who is the best player. The winner will receive an immense reward, and the rest, will receive a small reward for trying. We wish you luck.”

Of course, the contestants aren’t really ‘the world’s greatest rulebreakers in the game’, that’s just to copy the World 901 movie.

Location
The location of this minigame will be in a fake Draynor Village.

‘Contestants Remaining’
Instead of making it typically that the counter stays on with the ‘Contestants Remaining’, we will make it appear on the screen every time a person dies. So, for example, there are 20 players in the minigame. The ‘Contestants Remaining’ will appear on the screen for 5 minutes, then it will go away. Player 20 dies, so for all the 19 contestants remaining, the ‘Contestants Remaining’ will appear on the screen for another 5 seconds, and then it will go away.

We are doing this for 2 reasons:

[ol][li]The World 901 movie did this.[/li]
[li]We find that it will make more ‘suspense’.[/li][/ol]

TomatoThrowingAction.java:

package org.rs2server.rs2.action.impl;

import org.rs2server.rs2.action.Action;
import org.rs2server.rs2.event.impl.TomatoThrowingEvent;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Animation;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Graphic;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Item;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Mob;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.NPC;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Projectile;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.World;

/**
 * <p>The action in which a tomato is thrown at an
 * <code>NPC</code>.</p>
 *
 * @author Arham 4/RelentlessPK
 */
public class TomatoThrowingAction extends Action {

    private Mob mob;

    public TomatoThrowingAction(Mob mob, int ticks) {
        super(mob, ticks);
        this.mob = mob;
    }

    @Override
    public CancelPolicy getCancelPolicy() {
        return CancelPolicy.ALWAYS;
    }

    @Override
    public StackPolicy getStackPolicy() {
        return StackPolicy.NEVER;
    }

    @Override
    public AnimationPolicy getAnimationPolicy() {
        return AnimationPolicy.RESET_ALL;
    }

    @Override
    public void execute() {
        Mob target = mob.getInteractingEntity();
        NPC npc = (NPC) target;
        if (target.isPlayer()) {
            mob.getActionSender().sendMessage("You cannot throw that at a player!");
            return;
        }
        mob.playProjectile(Projectile.create(mob.getLocation(), npc.getSpawnLocation(), 29, 65, 50, 90, 21, 21, npc.getDefinition().getId(), 10, 64));
        mob.playAnimation(Animation.create(2968));
        npc.playAnimation(Animation.create(2836));
        mob.forceChat("Take that you filthy little " + npc.getDefinition().getName().toLowerCase() + "!");
        mob.face(npc.getSpawnLocation());
        World.getWorld().submit(new TomatoThrowingEvent(mob, target, 600));
        mob.getInventory().remove(new Item(2518, 1));
    }
}

TomatoThrowingEvent.java:

package org.rs2server.rs2.event.impl;

import org.rs2server.rs2.event.Event;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Animation;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Graphic;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Location;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Mob;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.NPC;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.NPCDefinition;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.Projectile;
import org.rs2server.rs2.model.World;

/**
 * When the NPCs throw tomatos at NPCs in the cages of Yanille.
 *
 * @author Arham 4/RelentlessPK
 */
public class TomatoThrowingEvent extends Event {

    private Mob mob;
    private Mob target;

    public TomatoThrowingEvent(Mob mob, Mob target, int ticks) {
        super(ticks);
        this.mob = mob;
        this.target = target;
    }

    @Override
    public void execute() {
        int rottenTomatoTime = 25;
        if (rottenTomatoTime == 1) {
            mob.playGraphics(Graphic.create(30));
        }
        if (this == null || rottenTomatoTime <= 0) {
            this.stop();
            return;
        }
        if (rottenTomatoTime >= 0) {
            rottenTomatoTime--;
        }
    }
}

handleItemOnNpc method:

    private void handleItemOnNpc(Player player, Packet packet) {
        int npcIndex = packet.getLEShort() & 0xFFFF;
        int parentUID = packet.getLEInt();
        int itemSlot = packet.getLEShort() & 0xFFFF;
        int itemId = packet.getShortA() & 0xFFFF;
        NPC npc = (NPC) World.getWorld().getNPCs().get(npcIndex);
        if (itemId == 2518) {
            player.setInteractingEntity(InteractionMode.ATTACK, npc);
            TomatoThrowingAction action = new TomatoThrowingAction(player, 0);
            action.execute();
        }
    }
