moparsc4p3 July 30, 2016, 12:29pm #32
As anybody playing earlier today (Sunday 15 July) will have noticed, there have been some technical problems with the RuneScape game worlds going offline earlier today.

This outage was caused by a complete loss of internet connectivity at the ISP which provides our core hosting. We had one complete outage earlier, and then a second one which has just ended.

What caused these problems the ISP hasn’t yet told us, but this sort of thing is normally caused by a router somewhere dieing.

Because this outage was caused by a 3rd party fault at our ISP it’s somewhat out of our control. It wasn’t a problem with any of our servers. However we are taking the following immediate steps.

  1. When the outage occurred we weren’t even able to tell our users what was going on, because our news posting system is at the same facility! We are now updating our webserver software so we can at least put an explanatory message up if this sort of thing happens.

  2. We are updating our core loginserver software so it handles outages of this nature better. Of course if our main servers can’t talk to the internet you still won’t be able to play, but we think we can at least stop an outage of that type causing a 15 min rollback. Which should make it less annoying.

  3. Obviously we will be chasing the ISP for an explanation of exactly what happened, and making it clear to them how unhappy we are about this, and pressuring them for assurances of what they are doing to make this less likely to happen again.

We have someone in the office now working overtime on Sunday afternoon, making the changes for 1+2.

Please accept our sincere apologies for these problems. I’m finding them as frustrating as you are!

Thanks for you patience
Andrew

Denial-of-service attack

In computing, a denial-of-service attack (DoS attack) is a cyber-attack in which the perpetrator seeks to make a machine or network resource unavailable to its intended users by temporarily or indefinitely disrupting services of a host connected to the Internet. Denial of service is typically accomplished by flooding the targeted machine or resource with superfluous requests in an attempt to overload systems and prevent some or all legitimate requests from being fulfilled.
