zyle1992 August 4, 2016, 1:55pm #1

Hey, I’m going to work on a personal password manager, and would like to know what your thoughts are. Yes, I know there are a plethora of other password managers out there, but I’m just doing it as more of an exercise. A lot of the current password managers use a master password that unlocks every stored password. What I’m thinking of having is having not only the master password, but also a Google authentication token as well. I might make it modular enough that you can add as many layers as you want, and allowing you to specify specific layers per password (ie. having your facebook password use the Google token, and your email use the google token, and a private key stored on your hard drive).

What are some other considerations I should take into account?
