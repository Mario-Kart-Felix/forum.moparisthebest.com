[size=10pt]Hey guys!
I’m going to be showing you how to port forward your RSPS NetGear wireless router WNDR3400v2.

Considering that “Portforward.com” doesn’t have a guide for this specific type of router, I’m going to provide this guide for anyone who needs it.

Step 1:Type in “www.Routerlogin.net”

Step 2:Type in your username and password. By default, the username is “admin” and the password is “password.”

Step 3:Go to the advanced tab at the top of the page.

34ir5af.png1280×1024

Step 4:Click the advanced setup tab>Port Forwarding/Port Triggering

fm85z4.png956×631

Step 5:Click on “Add custom service”

2wqvleh.jpg968×637

Step 6:Type in your service name, port (43594), and keep the TCP/UDP the same. You also need to find your default gateway IP. It can be found by going to your start menu (Windows 7) and typing in “cmd”, (I usually have to run it as an administrator) and type in IPConfig. Look for default gateway. That’s what you’ll enter in as the “internal IP”.

Step 7:Press “Apply” at the top of the page.

Congratulations! You should have portforwarded your RSPS on the Netgear Router WNDR3400v2.

Please, don’t hesitate to ask questions. I’m here to serve.[/size]
