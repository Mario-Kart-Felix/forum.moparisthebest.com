exps35 July 30, 2016, 11:16am #6

A larger example of my simple auto drop procedure

Procedure DropIt; begin Dropped:=0; repeat if(FindColorTolerance(x,y,ItemColor,MIX1, MIY1, MIX2,MIY2,ItemTolerance))then begin wait(100) MMouse(x,y,2,2); if(IsUpText(ItemName))then begin GetMousePos(x,y); Mouse(x,y,1,1,false); Wait(200+ random(100)); ChooseOption(x,y,'rop'); Dropped:=Dropped+1; end; end; until not (FindColorTolerance(x,y,ItemColor,MIX1, MIY1, MIX2,MIY2,ItemTolerance)) Writeln('Dropped ' +inttostr(Dropped) +' "'+itemname +'"s. Terminating script.') end;

This is where syntax comes in handy. You see my repeat that is indented 2 spaces? follow that down to my ‘Until’ which is towards the bottom, and indented two spaces.
This continually repeats EVERYTHING between those two commands until it doesn’t find the item color anymore.

Everything must still contain a ‘Begin’ and ‘End;’ (unless you are calling out a procedure which already has its begin and end).
