Yeah, that’s about how it ends up. I took a haz-mat class one time where the main part of the class was decontamination. We had to practice decontaminating ourselves, another firefighter, and then we were taught how to set up a mass decontamination system. For self decontamination and decontaminating someone else, we had to pull an 1 3/4" line off the truck and spray and cover every inch of ourselves with water. The mass decontamination was two engines parked next to eachother, with a fog nozzle hooked up straight to the pump with no hose (to spray from both sides) and then an aerial with a fog nozzle above. This allowed you to just have people walk through it. And yes, we all had to walk through it. Structural firefighting gear does great against the heat. But it doesn’t do much to keep you from getting wet. By the end of that 8 hour day we were all soaked from head to toe…

One thing we did at bootcamp was firefighting. Learning the classes of fires, how to contain and control them, safety equipment, etc. When you’re on a ship in the middle of the ocean packed with weapons, explosives, fuel, personnel, jets, and not to mention the nuclear reactors the last thing you want is a fire.

I’ve always wanted to get into firefighting or to volunteer. I don’t know much but the bond between firefighters seems somewhat similar to the bond between sailors, soldiers, airmen, marines, etc. Living together, eating, showering, sleeping, etc it’s really a relationship you won’t develop any other way.

One thing I really hate is those people who go out walking on the street carrying weapons just to get a response from the police, I saw a video on youtube where a guy was walking around with a handgun and I believe some type of rifle, videotaping everything and the only reason he does it is to mess with the police. When the officers show up, ask his name he refuses to answer, refuses to cooperate at all. I mean yeah you have your rights but what will giving a name or talking do? If you’re an officer and you see a guy with a gun just walking around and you ignore him, say he ends up shooting somebody, how would you feel? The most the officers would do is run your name to make sure you’re not a felon, have a warrant or anything like that, and check your weapons permits; No rights violation there.

I respect all civil servants weather they be military, police, fire, medical. Yeah not all have the right mindset and may have their own issues but they risk themselves to help others, why judge an entire group on the actions of few, unless a specific individual gives you a reason to not respect them you should show respect where it is due.
