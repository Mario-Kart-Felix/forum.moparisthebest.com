m.dog311 August 4, 2016, 9:12am #18

Volunteer firefighter here. Also a PA state certified EMT-B. I’ve taken and passed the national level skills test, and just need to take the written exam to be a nationally certified EMT-B. I’m currently waiting till August when I turn 18 to finish up my classes to become a state certified vehicle rescue technician (I’ve been trained on everything in vehicle rescue except for hydraulic tools), and I need to finish up classes to become an interior firefighter. As volunteers we put in a lot of time and effort before we even think about beginning to fight fires and save lives. It’s something that you can’t just do as a hobby. It’s something that once you begin you have to be able to dedicate and potentially sacrifice your life for. When people talk about the firefighter brotherhood, it’s something you can’t even begin to understand until you become a part of it. You see a lot of things no one else will ever see. And you do a lot of things most other people will never have to do. I can tell you from experience that performing CPR on someone is one of the most physically and emotionally demanding things a person can do. Riding in the back of an ambulance with a dead body is an experience you hope you never have to do again, but in the back of your mind know it’s something you can’t avoid.

Fire, EMS, and police personnel are some of the greatest people you could ever meet. I can promise you, a real fire depart is nothing like the ones you see on TV in shows such as Chicago Fire and Rescue Me. Those shows were made to entertain, and honestly give a bad name to first responders.

If you want to watch some amazing videos, check out Volunteers: The Series on YouTube.

I haven’t seen it yet (watching it Friday) but “Burn” is supposed to be an amazing documentary on the struggling Detroit fire department.
