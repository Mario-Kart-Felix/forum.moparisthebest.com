What does leech mean?

General Discussion
The_Un-Named July 30, 2016, 12:14pm #1

Well, it seems my understanding of the word “leech” differs from a lot of new people, so I’m going to take a few minutes to write the meaning of this word, so it isn’t horrifically used as it is.

Lets start. Back in the day of Kaitnieks, a leecher was a person to come, download a few SCAR scripts and leave without posting. Now, according to dictionary.com, leech means the following:

2. a person who clings to another for personal gain, esp. without giving anything in return, and usually with the implication or effect of exhausting the other's resources; parasite.

But why did I make this thread? In graphics section, if someone posts an uber sig, some kid with no life has to ruin the posters day by posting the possibility he stole it, usually with “this is leeched”. Let me simplify this: “This is a person who takes something for personal gain giving nothing into return”, this can mean a lot of things, now, onto the second word of the day, “rip”, if someone rips a sig, they simply put their name on it. Lets look at the definition for “rip” on dictionary.com:

[b]9. Rip off, Slang.[/b] a. to steal or pilfer. b. to rob or steal from. c. to swindle, cheat, or exploit; take advantage of: phony charity appeals that rip off a gullible public.

Now, lets say someone posted an uber sig, and the person that said leech before, now said “this is ripped”, I’ll simplify that, “This person stole that sig”.

Conclusion:
If you want to be a noob, and moronically submit your claim of theft, use the correct words.
