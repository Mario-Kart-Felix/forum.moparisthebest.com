serpento July 30, 2016, 6:48am #9

I recommend getting a 512 MB of RAM or more. 512 costs around 40-50$ and the 1GB is around $100.
and maybe a higher processor? Before you go out in the store, make sure what type of DDR RAM slots you have in your motherboard. If your computers warranty is no longer available, open it up and look at the RAM sticks, they should have a label and say what DDR it is.(exp: DDR1, DDR3…). If you don’t know what your doing, don’t bother opening it, before you really screw up your comp.

Okay, without spending a few hundred bucks, lets try these…

When you turn on you’re computer, all those programs such as windows messenger, norton anti virus, etc starts to open and would make a slow startup.

Try this.
Go to start and to run…
In that window that opens, type: msconfig
Go to the startup tab, and disable all.(Don’t touch any other tab!).
Hit ok. It would ask you to restard your computer. click no.
If you want some programs to load up again, go to run, msconfig, startup tab, and look for the program, and check it.

Now go to start> all programs> accessories> system tools> disk cleanup.
If you have more than 1 Hard drive, pick which one you want to cleanup, otherwise it would start calculating… It may take long, probably a few mins.
Once thats done, check the boxes what you want to cleanup. Read the description it gives under the ones you highlight. Some wont be deleted, probably moved or whatever. Its safe though.
Hit ok. Now this may take long too! Once your done, you can do the same thing if you have another harddrive you want to cleanup, otherwise, lets continue.

Disk refragment!
Go to start> all programs> accessories> system tools> disk regragment
Once thats opened, hit the “Defragment” button. This may take really long… probably an hour or more if you never done this before. Its also safe, no files are being deleted. Its just defragments(puts away) the files you dont use on your computer…

Lowering your scree resolution and the windows theme can help the performance on your computer.
Right click on desktop> settings tab> screen resolution. and themes tab> theme. Windows classic theme looks looks ugly(but its up to you).

Finally! When its all done, go ahead and restart your computer and tell me the difference. If still slow, then tell me here.
