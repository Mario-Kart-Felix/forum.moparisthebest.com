3lv3n_snip3r August 4, 2016, 9:53am #36
xxxtilllyxxx:

Ohh. I figured you were selling it. I see. I’m still learning how to ride, it’s really fun. Got any beginner tips?

Uhm, if you don’t know how to countersteer, learn how to do it. Because it makes turning at higher speeds a lot easier. If you don’t know how to, all you basically do is if you want to turn right, push your right handlebar forward (or pull your left handlebar back) making the wheel turn left but the bike will lean towards the right. The same goes for turning left. It will take some time getting used to because it doesn’t seem logical, but it works. Practice on a straight road where there isn’t a lot of traffic and start out with little pushes to get the hang of it and how hard you have to push. The faster you go, the harder you have to push. Countersteering only works if you’re above around 10-15 mph. You can also practice this on a bicycle if you go fast enough. If you have anymore questions, just shoot me a PM and I’ll try to help you out as best as I can.
