[center][glow=red,2,300]For this tutorial ill be referring to my scar script “Gaiaonline Fisher Revamped” so if you dont have it get it.[/glow][/center]

[center]To start off what is a const? Well a const is more are less a choice. In my script [glow=blue,2,300]“Gaiaonline Fisher Revamped”[/glow] I use a const to let the user make the decision of what rod they want to fish with.[/center]
[hr]

[center][size=30pt]How to declare a const[/size][/center]

Ok. i know what most of you are thinking you think "oh man this is easy ill i have to do is:

var
varname:const;

Right?"

wrong. A const is not really a variable therefore cannot be declared under Var.

so now “your wondering how am I going to do this then?”

well its simple. If you would take my script [glow=blue,2,300]“Gaiaonline Fisher Revamped”[/glow] for example
you would see:

Notice how its not declared as a var the const has its own “void”
when you declare a const it goes befor the begin line, just like var.
after the word “const” you put in your choice to be made such as:

weather='?';

being who i am i like to comment the choices that are avalible to the script users
such as:

weather='?'; // sunny or night or rainy

telling them that they can edit that line either to:

weather='sunny';

or

weather='rainy';

or

weather='night';

once there declaired give yourself a pat on the back and continue on…
[hr]

[center][size=30pt]How to set up your const choices[/size][/center]

Ok now that we have declared are const we have to create the choices in a procedure or the computer will get mad at us = O.

once again we will take a peek at [glow=blue,2,300]“Gaiaonline Fisher Revamped”[/glow] to see how i set up my const.

Now after takeing a long hard look at that const what did you notice?
Every choice has a begin and end. Keep in mind if you have two begins in one of your choices you will have to end it a extra time at the end of the procedure just be careful and count your begins and ends.

now a const can vary with looks so im going to try to simulate a basic const that will write a line for the different weathers.

procedure weatherchoices;
begin
if(weather='sunny')then
begin
writeln('Its sunny outside!');
end;
if(weather='rainy')then
begin
writeln('its raining outside');
end;
if(weather='night')then
begin
writeln('Its dark outside);
end;
end;

and that concludes my const tutorial keep an eye out because i may be adding more to it if people just cant seem to understand.
Thanks for reading and please post your errors and until then happy scripting.
