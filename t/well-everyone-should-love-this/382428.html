It's hard to find two vehicles that are more different -- a pickup truck and a Formula 1 racing car. But now Ford is suing Ferrari over the use of the name F150, which both vehicles share.

Ford F-150, America’s top-selling truck for 33 years, probably won’t be easily mistaken for a Ferrari.CAPTIONBy Ford, WieckFord uses it on its F-Series pickups, the nation’s best selling vehicles. The F-150 is the most popular in the lineup. Ferrari has named its new Formula 1 racing car the “F150” as well. Hey, at least Ferrari can argue there is no hyphen.

Ford contends in a lawsuit filed in Detroit that Ferrari’s use of the name is blantant trademark infringement, the Associated Press reports. It probably didn’t help matters that Ferrari has also created a website, www.ferrarif150.com . Ford is asking the court to stop Ferrari’s use of the name.

Ferrari isn’t responding yet, but we imagine it was aware of Ford’s unhappiness even before yesterday’s court filing. It contends that the name f150 is firmly rooted in Italian history. The website says the name refers to the 150th anniversary of the unification of Italy.
