zyle1992:

Only thing I can think of is the weight of the active electrons moving throughout the body, especially the brain.

Electrons weigh almost nothing (9.109382610^-28g), about 1/1000th of what a proton/neutron weighs. In order to have 21g worth of electrons ‘leave’ the body, your body would have to give off ~2.30510^28 electrons, or 38294.27mol of electrons. Considering we are carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen-based, each atom averages probably around 5 electrons (even being lenient here, helping your case). This would total 2.305 x 10^28 electrons given off from 4.6110^27 atoms. A 70kg body has approximately 710^27 atoms (Cite: Jefferson Lab). There is nothing scientifically explainable that could produce the charge to pull this many electrons out of a body in a contained environment.
