Well…

Ak74u - 410 fps - 1050(I think) mAH battery with charger included, 9 lb, 600 round mag: http://www.airsplat.com/Items/ERM-JG-AK741012.htm

Full Metal HFC UZI Propane - 320 fps, propane tank costs about $3 at a Wal-Mart, + $5 for lube, + $15 for a propane adapter. You don’t have to buy the full metal, which will cut you back about $30. http://www.airsplat.com/Items/GR-HGBB-203Z-M.htm

A gun that is $80 that shoots 370 fps and has good statistics is this gun:
http://www.airsplat.com/Items/ER-WELL-R6-M4A1.htm
I definately recommend it if you don’t want something too pricy. I am actually thinking about buying this gun in a few months.

Some people say AirSplat is overpriced, but AirsoftGI will cost you about $200+ a gun, even with the discount code… You can also use these coupon codes to get a discount on AIRSPLAT ONLY:

FREESHIPPING - Free Shipping on all items, ~$3 for handling though.

feedback4 - 7.5% off all items(Cannot combine both of them^)

Only works on www.airsplat.com

I only know one code for www.AirsoftGI.com, which is gitv

Basically it stacks up on the propane adapter, which is about 15$. You only need to buy it once unless you break it somehow, and the lube only takes on spray every few hours or so.

Never buy a gun by looking at the FPS. Always look at the gun, look up reviews for it on Google or YouTube, and then search Ebay if you want. There are tons of other choices on AirSplat. I say don’t look at the FPS because there are some spring guns that say 430 FPS for about 40-60 dollars. I looked up the review, and they are horrible. Watch out for that.
