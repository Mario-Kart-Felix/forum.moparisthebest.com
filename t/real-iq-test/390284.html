jc22493 August 1, 2016, 11:06am #20

If you ever took psychology or have common sense, you would know that IQ means nothing. IQ is calculated by dividing your mental age by your chronological age then multiplying by 100.

IQ = (MA/CA)*100

As you can see there is a huge flaw in this formula, if that. As your chronological age increases your mental age will either stay the same or decrease. When you’re 80 years old, your IQ may be a 30, well below mental retardation, which I believe is an IQ < 70. IQ tests are BS because of all the flaws…

…and as Zyle pointed out, the multiple intelligences theory (Gardner, I believe…?) argues that ‘intelligence’ is not quite as defined it could be on an IQ test. There are multiple types of intelligences; musical, interpersonal, intrapersonal, linguistic, kinesthetic, and others I can’t recall at the moment. You can be intelligent in one category and fail at the rest but still be considered intelligent. (Basketball players who aren’t school smart - kinesthetic, composers - musical, etc…)
