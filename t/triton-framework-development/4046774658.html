You guys might consider following the 80/20 rule [80% design/20% code]. It seems like you guys are diving strait into programming.

I would seriously consider spending 80% of your time drawing up class diagrams, defining network protocol, and discussing the best way to go about this. After that 80% you should have an exact idea of how your framework will be set up [classes, their properties, and how the interact].

This usually produces code that is easily defined [and documented], no patch classes [Classes put in place to fix a design flaw, and after a while become a nearly impossible to replace piece of the system due to how many things depend on it], and is just very reliable and extendable.

Just my thoughts, good luck! [Granted, you may already be doing this]
