Mopman August 1, 2016, 1:31pm #88

In heroics, now probably yes - because everybody overgears the shit out of the content by now and negates the mechanics. When cata was released, they were actually pretty difficult.

In progression raids (ie the newest ones, where people don’t outgear them yet) the fights are tightly tuned to be impossible to beat by just spamming abilities - the bosses have mechanics which must be learned and executed properly.

To take an easy (well, in v2) but well known and fun fight, Heigan the Unclean:

He has two phases.

Phase One: Basically stand there and DPS the crap out of him. He has an aura that increases cast speeds to things closer to him, so obviously casters need to not be close to him. He has a disease that he casts periodically which rapes the shit out of the player its cast on (giving the healers a gimmick to worry about) and also the surrounding players (meaning everyone has to make sure they’re not standing close to anyone else).

Phase Two: He teleports up to the plinth thing in his room and starts casting. Anyone who is still standing on the plinth takes a shitload of damage when he finishes (they’re meant to run, somebody always doesn’t :P) Gas stuff starts bursting out of the floor in a predictable pattern. Players have to move as a big group around the room predicting where the gas is going to come out (it’s always the same, so its learnable.) If you get hit by the gas you’ll probably die, you can take one hit but because of the timing you’ll often get hit by another straight after, and the healers have to try to heal you on the run, they can’t stop moving for more than a split second. A few people getting hit often sets off a chain reaction as other people try to save their ass and end up getting hit themselves.

After this he goes back to phase 1 and everything repeats again.

Probably not the best fight to pick but maybe you get the idea. Every single boss in raids has gimmicks like this, some simpler, many much more complicated. Trying to get 25 people to all execute shit perfectly (or at least handle non-perfectly well) is part of the fun :> …and the source of many ventrilo arguments.
