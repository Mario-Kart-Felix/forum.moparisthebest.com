This will tell us if its true or false. That"s about it for combo boxes. Lets move on to saving settings.

[u]Saving the settings of a Form
Alright, now to saving settings. Firstly, go to the procedure SetupAll. Now lets look at the SaveSetting procedure.

procedure SaveSetting(section, setting, value: String);

Let me explain.
Section = Section!(eg. “UserPassSaver”);
Setting = What part you want to save(eg. “Username”)
Value = What is the string?(Eg. Edit1.Text)

Now in the SetupAll procedure lets add some lines.

SaveSetting('UserPassSaver', 'Username', Edit1.Text); SaveSetting('UserPassSaver', 'Password', Edit2.Text); SaveSetting('UserPassSaver', 'Active', ComboBox1.Text);

So your SetupAll procedure should look like this:

procedure SetupAll; begin Username := Edit1.Text; Password := Edit2.Text; case ComboBox1.Text of 'True' : PlayerActive := True; 'False' : PlayerActive := True; end; SaveSetting('UserPassSaver', 'Username', Edit1.Text); SaveSetting('UserPassSaver', 'Password', Edit2.Text); SaveSetting('UserPassSaver', 'Active', ComboBox1.Text); end;

[u]Loading the settings
Now that we"ve saved the settings, we must load it.
In your script add a line to the following procedures : Edit1, Edit2 and CheckBox1.

*****.Text := LoadSetting('UserPassSaver', *******).

*****.Text is Edit1.text, Edit2.text etc.
The other ***** is ‘Username’, ‘Password’, ‘Active’ etc.

Your final script should turn out something like this:
