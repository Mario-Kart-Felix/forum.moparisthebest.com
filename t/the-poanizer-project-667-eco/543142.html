First thing I noticed was that Edgeville already looks way too cluttered and is filled with NPCs that probably have an infinite amount of decent items, which is going to destroy your economy.

Create custom zoned training spaces located near edgeville. You have the mountain to the west, open field to the east, potentially “risky yet worthy” training spots to the north which could have an increase in rare drops, and the barbarian village, Gertrude’s House (Which would be great for a roleplay bandit home or some sort), and much more. [B]REMEMBER[/B] the monsters you can use are not limited to the popular/generics that already exist (shitty rock crabs, etc.) changing level, stat, and loot variables in decently modeled NPCs isn’t overly complicated from what I understand. I would be a lot more impressed to see low leveled ice warriors, maybe ice fiends, and other themed monsters that are lower leveled, than the shitty/horribly modeled rock crabs that people are so lazy about.

Your server has potential, but you need to own the server and put basic amounts of work to keep your economy alive and stable. Another tip is to not douse your server in GP. Create ways in which gp can be removed from the game without overpowering NPC traders. Things like the lottery, bond trading for “donator items” (EX: 1 million gp = 1 donator bond) which can be used at spawn to trade for something decent/cosmetic). Or something like using donator bonds for temporary access to donator locations/exp boosts.

If you want eco then you need to choose a reasonable exp rate. I would not suggest anything 50x or higher.
