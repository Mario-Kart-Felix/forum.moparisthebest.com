I had a quick scan through the shit you removed and I can’t see how that caused the problem, so I think it’s totally unrelated.

That message means the kernel cannot mount your root filesystem. Either you do not have the drivers installed to mount it, or you have passed it the wrong filesystem. I can’t really help you more than that without seeing your menu.lst and your /etc/fstab, at least.

That said, I highly recommend you change to a different distro. The fact you had to manually remove all KDE packages instead of just doing apt-get remove kde-desktop or whatever proves that ubuntu is a horrible horrible distro.
