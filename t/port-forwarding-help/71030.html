If u find “virtual servers” category u can just follow my tut here:

[center]Forwarding your ports (if it isn’t already made on your computer) is for making others able to play on YOUR server, to authorize access and traffic on your computer.

a) Go to start, choose Run, type cmd. Then in the black screen type ipconfig and press Enter.
b) See the last number (at the right) that appears? Write it on a paper.
c) Go to Internet Explorer and type this number (like where you put the website)
d) Now, the window that appears, it should be your Internet Provider’s (ex. ComCast, AOL, BellSouth, …)
Somehow, like all Internet Providers are different, Manage to enable port forwarding (should be found at “NAT” > Virtual Server) on your server’s computer IP (for example if your computer IP is 192.168.0.3, then put it for 192.168.0.3, if it is 192.168.0.6, put it for 192.168.0.6, …) and enable the following ports:
43594 - BOTH TCP and UDP.
80 - BOTH TCP and UDP.
[/center]
