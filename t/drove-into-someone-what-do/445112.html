Greeny August 3, 2016, 3:13am #3

Mm OK, some minor mistakes here but you can learn from them. My advice isn’t really relevant to your situation but incase anyone else reads over this thread, this is my “2 cents” (and I don’t claim it to be perfect but I believe it will put you in better standing if these kind of things get out of hand).

As most phones these days have cams, I recommend videoing the exterior of your car straight after an accident and the other person’s if they will allow. Take proper pictures of any obvious damage. Make sure to try and include the road and vehicle positions if it was a serious collision (so you can prove your positioning relative to theirs at the time of the accident). People say to jot everything you remember down but I always think a pen/paper may not be handy so either use your phone’s SMS or while recording the video narrate the sequence of events as you remember them.

Ask for all their info, make sure you give them nothing they don’t give you. If they don’t have insurance, make sure you have their registration, name and address (but expect them to be fake so in addition immediately call the police, in the UK driving without insurance is illegal so this is to be expected of you)
[Note: You should probably call the police anyway to report the accident, for minor accidents they don’t even bother coming out usually and filing an accident report straight away may help your claim]

NEVER admit responsibility at the scene of the crash. Let the insurance decide who’s responsible (if you ran into someone the culprit may be obvious but some older drivers will try to fuck over younger drivers in my experience). Don’t admit responsibility to the police either, just tell them what happened.

Make sure all communication with the other party is done via the insurance companies, don’t take phone calls from them, and if you do - don’t say anything you’re going to regret if they happen to be recording the conversation.

There’s a few other tips I’ve picked up but they’re eluding me atm, I’ll get back to you.

In the meantime if there was little to no damage don’t worry, just be careful he doesn’t boot his back bumper and try to claim it was you

EDIT: made a few additions above.
