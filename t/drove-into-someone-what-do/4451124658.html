R.I.P_RSC August 3, 2016, 3:25am #29
runescape3dude:

What I don’t get is that, if stopping distance is longer in rain for you, wouldn’t it also be longer for them, negating the need for a greater time gap?

The stopping distance of the person in front of you also increases, but you still have to take reaction time into consideration. If the person in front of you hits the brakes, you won’t immediately react. By the time you apply the brakes, the person in front of you is already traveling slower. When it’s dry, the remainder of the 2 seconds is enough to prevent a collision. But with rain, it’s not. You will lose grip, and in the worst case you’ll start to slide.

Best would be a calculation or something to prove this, but meh…
