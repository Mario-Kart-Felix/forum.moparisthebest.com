Alright, let’s cover a few things.

[ul][li]We aren’t here to rip out your heart and devour it. The idea is that you post the source code and we point out parts that could have been done better. Nobody expects your code to be perfect. In fact, it’s near impossible to write perfect code. The lovely part about this community is that we have people who have a lot of knowledge in their chosen areas and when combined, they can offer very solid advice. It’s a great resource.[/li]

[li]You said that it’s too late to implement any suggestions and anything additional would require a rewrite. This is a silly way of thinking about things. You’re looking at it all wrong. This project isn’t your end-point. Chances are, you will have many future projects that end up being a lot more difficult than your current. The idea is to learn as much as you can from each one, so you’re capable of handling of the next and more challenging project. It’s perfectly fine if you don’t implement our suggested changes in your project. That’s cool, we get it. But next time you open up your IDE and start writing, you think think back to those suggestions and start producing something one step above what you’re currently producing. That’s progress and it’s a beautiful thing.[/li]

[li]When you get into the real world, you’re not able to hide your code so you can prevent your feelings being hurt. In fact, I have to have my code gutted and spat on in code reviews every time I write anything. It sucks when someone tells you your hard work is terrible but you learn to never write terrible code.[/li]

[li] Making something open source doesn’t mean you’re giving your baby away. Imagine how it would feel for someone to start adding to your project. For someone to love your work as much as you love it. Then also imagine who you’re helping but releasing this. Remember when you started out and wanted to view examples? Same thing. [/li]

[li]As you progress, you will start to figure out why conventions are used. I’m a strong believer that people should learn from their mistakes, otherwise they will wonder why they’re doing something. Let us point out where you’re falling short and explain why.[/li]

[li]We’re strongly against false credit here. This is another reason why we ask for evidence. Once you’ve done this a few times, we will trust your work and there will be no need. Let us help you and let us give you the credit you deserve (assuming you wrote everything).[/li][/ul]
