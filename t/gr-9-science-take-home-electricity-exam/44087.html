39. Quote The blow dryer transforms electrical energy into what other forms of energy: A: Chemical and Mechanical B: Checmical and Thermal C: Thermal and Mechanical D: Mechanical and Electrical
I actually think that one is a. Firstly, the only types of energy I have heard of are kinetic (mechanical here - Kinetic energy gives the object the potential to move, which is what happens in a blow dryer), and chemical potential, based on the fact I've never heard of electric potential energy... Electricity *is* the energy here - It's not where the energy is derived from. An example of explaining electricity simply - A battery produces what you could call electricity.. yet the energy is chemical potential energy. Chemicals produce the energy in a battery. I hope that explains it. Of course, my second choice to that question would be c if I went with one of your two... It's actually a hardish question considering I've been on holidays for the past month and a half and doing everything *but* thinking of school related stuff. :P

As for the last question, I’d go with the alkaline dry cell - It’s chemical potential energy and doesn’t polute. Also keep in mind alkaline is nothing more then water with a high pH level, thus it is probably an infinite resource to an extent. From my understanding, a halogen lamp is a gas lamp, which pollutes the air, significantly less then gas though. As for the electric heater, well in Australia coal is used for power, so that would be causing pollution too.
