OK, quick rundown of what I’ve done so far: Took headset apart, checked pinouts, soldered wires to board, tested results. Steps I neglected to do: Research the Xbox Live audio streaming platform.

Well, I, “successfully” came up with an xbox live radio streaming system, though it doesn’t work. Right now I don’t have pictures, but I’ll take some and post them in a bit. Basically what I’ve done is I’ve soldered a 3.5mm jack to the ports M-, and M+ on the board in the Xbox Live headset. I then plugged the headset into my audio out port on my PC, and tried playing some music. It does infact play sound from the computer, and my party can hear it. The issue is, the quality is nonexistent. It’s just a lot of white noise really, you can discern sounds if you know the song or the video, but it’s still just a bunch of gibberish.

My attempts to fix the issue were to no avail. I tried using Mono instead of stereo, nope. Tried changing level of volume, nope. Different types of media, such as voice, music, sounds. Nope, nothing worked. So really the only thing I can take away from this experiment, is that the Xbox Live audio platform just doesn’t support my venture.

If anyone else has an thoughts on this, or maybe a fix, it’d be appreciated.
