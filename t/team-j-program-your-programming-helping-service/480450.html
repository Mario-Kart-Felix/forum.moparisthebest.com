[size=8pt]Who we are:[/size]
We are Team J-Program. We are a collaboration team, which specialize in any issue you may have, with any and all types of programming. This includes design and development outside the realm of RuneScape private servers as well. We are always on the lookout to recruit new team members.

[size=8pt]Why you should choose our assistance:[/size]
One of our goals is to gather the best of the best involved in the private server scene & general programming scenes, bring them here, and have them become a part of the team. We like to think that we function ‘as one’, or as a well-oiled machine. Any actual questions that are asked of us will be answered to the best of our abilities. If the person you seek help from is not experienced enough, you will be referred to someone who can assist you in your endeavors. Programming and design is our passion, and we would like to share our passion in the form of helping others.

[size=8pt]How to become a member of the Team:[/size]
You can express your interest in becoming a member of the Team, by sending a private message to any current Team member via the forums, or contact us through Skype, e-mail, IRC, etc. Alternatively, you can write a post in this thread expressing your interest. There are only two ways of having your application to join be denied: if TWO or more people vouch against your application, or if you are not being active in the help sections (our team is for people to help the community. If you are not going to help, why be a part of the team?). Providing a reason towards an anti-vouch is not required, yet the Team is free to query the anti-voucher as needed.

[size=8pt]How to phrase your questions:[/size]
The questions asked should be well-worded, and as free of grammatical errors as possible. An ideal question will also be detailed and not a generalization. For example, rather than just posting “Help I don’t know what to do,” post, “Help, I encountered this specific bug/glitch/problem doing this specific action, and in an attempt to solve it, I wrote out this specific line(s) of code, but it is throwing me errors.” If we see that an honest attempt to solve the problem was made, and you have general knowledge in programming (not making us waste time that could be spent helping others), then we will do our best to help. We are not meant to spoon-feed the answers or hold hands. If you don’t know what you are doing, and it is a simple task, go learn basic Java before starting a private server.

[size=8pt]The people we are looking to recruit:[/size]
As a programming/design community, people are encouraged to and should be able to experience and learn new things as a whole. We’re not only interested in having knowledge and sharing it, but sharing it in a way that everyone benefits from this. That is why we hope to expand this group as far as possible.
