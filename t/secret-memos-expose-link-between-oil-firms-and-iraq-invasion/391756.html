The reactors don’t melt down, the waste has a much shorter half-life, and the thorium itself is completely pure from the moment it is extracted from the ground.

And it’s literally everywhere. If you’ve ever held a rock in your hand then you’ve encountered thorium.

Why am I making such a big deal of this? Firstly, the energy is dirt cheap. Secondly, the only other thing that really holds electric cars back is the issue with battery life.
If the Chinese succeed and move to thorium reactors then their energy becomes dirt cheap, and the rest of the world will be happy to follow.
With advancements in battery technology, the electric cars should become more and more feasible as time passes.
