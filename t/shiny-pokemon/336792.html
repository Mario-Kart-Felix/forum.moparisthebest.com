Hello all and welcome to my thread about the famous ‘shiny’ pokemon.

You might think to yourself, what is a shiny pokemon? A shiny pokemon is a pokemon that appears to ‘shine’ when it enters battle at it also happens to be a different color then other pokemon. These shiny kind were first discovered in the metal (gold, silver, crystal) series. The reason these shiny kind are such a big deal is the fact that you have a 1/8129 chance of encountering a shiny pokemon.

Now that we have all of that taken care of, time for the universal shiny rarity system i have made.

[size=12pt]Rule 1[/size]

All pokemon (shiny) in their first stage of evolution are a standard 1.0 points. For every stage it evolves, the pokemon receives a fraction of - 0.2.

Examples:
Scyther = 1.0, Scizor = 0.8
Caterpie = 1.0, Metapod = 0.8, Beedrill = 0.6
Geodude = 1.0, Graveler = 0.8, Golem = 0.6

[size=12pt]Rule 2[/size]

All legendary pokemon (besides Celebi [1.0] and Arceus [0.8]) are worth 2.0

[size=12pt]Rule 3[/size]

The following pokemon have special base point values:

Pichu = 0.6
Pikachu = 0.4
Raichu = 0.2
Magikarp = 1.5
Eevee = 1.6
Ditto = 1.6
All starting pokemon = 1.8

[size=12pt]Rule 4[/size]

The following are fractions that would require you to take points off of your shiny’s point value. Respected fractions are not looked down on, as Non-respected ones are.

Respected

Used Master ball to catch pokemon (unless it is legendary) = - 0.2
Cloned pokemon = - 0.3
Received from Nintendo event = - 0.2

Non-respected

Used cheat to make pokemon shiny = - 0.6
Used cheat to change wild encounter = - 0.4

[size=12pt]Rule 5[/size]

There are many bonuses you receive for capturing your shiny. They follow as:

Used poke ball to capture pokemon = + 0.2
Used great ball to capture pokemon = + 0.1
Pokemon is japanese = + 0.4
Pokemon is level 1 = + 0.7
Pokemon virus = + 1.0
