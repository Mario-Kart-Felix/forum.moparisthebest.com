[b][u]Things Needed:

[ul][li]DvD Burner[/li]
[li]Nero (or some other burning software, Nero is best though :P)[/li]
[li]A movie you want to burn. (*.avi, *.mpeg, w/e)[/li]
[li]ConvertXtoDvD (Optional: Download It Here!)[/ul]

[b][u]Basic Way To Format Movies:

[ul][li]Open up Nero Start Smart, then go to Nero Vision.[/li]
[li]Choose Make DvD> DvD Video[/li][/ul]

[ul][li]Now, add your video files. Select your menu that you would like to use. [/li]
[li]Here’s the fun part. In the upper right box, click on “Write To Hard Disk Folder”[/li][/ul]

[ul][li]Choose where you want to save it, and leave or watch another movie, because it WILL TAKE AWHILE! That is why I don’t use this method, but it is the basic way to do it.[/li][/ul]

[u]Convert DivX (*.avi):
Ok, now most of you who do this alot or get your movies from the internet will know that *.avi formatted movies are the most common movies there are. They are simply the best. So here is a little way to convert them faster, better quality, and smaller then how nero does it.

[ul][li]You will need the program I listed above, ConvertXtoDvD. Get it if you haven’t already done so…[/li]
[li]Open up that bad boy, add your *.avi movie file(s) to the list.[/li]
[li]Drop down the menu tab, change the title, background, font, etc. if you wish.[/li]
[li]Next, go to Settings tab, then Encoding. Now here is what you should do, pick a scenario…[/li][/ul]

[u]One Movie Settings:
Encoding Quality/Speed:= High Quality/Slow Encoding
Conversion Priority:= Above Normal (doesn’t have to be but it’s a good speed, get’s them done in about an hour.)

[u]Three Movies:
Encoding Quality/Speed:= Medium Quality/Recommended Encoding Speed
Conversion Priority:= Above Normal (still doesn’t hurt :P)

[u]4+ Movies: Never tried but you could assume what to do… Low Quality…

[ul][li]Now that you’ve chosen that, go to the General tab in the same window.[/li]
[li]Change the “Working Folder” to wherever you want the newly formatted movie to be saved.[/li]
[li]Finaly, before hitting that good looking Convert button, turn off the toggle preview if it isnt already off. Click on the T.V. screen button next to the convert button.[/li][/ul]

[b][u]Burning The Movie:

[ul][li]Open up Nero Burning ROM, choose DvD-Video.[/li]
[li]Don’t change ANYTHING! The only two things you should touch are, label tab, what you want the movie to be called. And Burn tab, the speed of your burn. (I would recommend the slowest speed)[/li]
[li]After you’ve filled that stuff out, click on “New”. A new ftp like browser will appear.[/li]
[li]Go inside of the VIDEO_TS folder on the very left under whatever you named the movie. Then locate where you saved the formatted version of you movie in the right hand side.[/li]
[li]Once you have found it, go inside the Video_Ts folder and drag every file over to the “Name” box, next to your movie box.[/li]
[li]Don’t worry about the AUDIO_TS box, it’s only there for stability reasons, the movie will have audio. Now click on the “Burn Compilation” button at the top. Wait until it’s done, and now you have burned a movie you can watch on any DvD player![/li][/ul]
