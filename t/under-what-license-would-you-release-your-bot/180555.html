zstars July 30, 2016, 7:37pm #4

If you just want to allow people to use it, without giving them the source, then no much point in using any “standard” license.

If you want people to always have access to the source, and to let them make modifications, then GPL should do.

If you want people to be able to modify the source, and keep it closed if they want to, zlib license might be good.

Other alternatives might be one of the Creative Commons licenses, or a custom license.

Truth is, it is probably somewhat pointless anyway, because given the nature of your program ( a bot ), the license is not too likely to be respected. Jagex’ Runescape client is copyrighted, and obviously reverse engineering it or releasing modified versions is not allowed, but as you can see, nobody really cares. And if they don’t care about Jagex, probably they won’t care much about you either.

On another note, the Aryan developers should have documented the update process much better, so that other people could do it too.

I prefer the GNU/GPL for anything I release. The reason for this is although it is long, lawyer-like and wordy, boiled down it states exactly how I feel about the code I write.
