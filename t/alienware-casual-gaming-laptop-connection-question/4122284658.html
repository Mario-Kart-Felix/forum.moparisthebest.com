SilentCJ August 1, 2016, 2:29pm #27

Out of 3 dell laptops and 3 hp laptops, dell have been my personal preference, don’t know why you have problems with drivers.
All 3 HP laptops run very hot and get to the stage where they can overheat so much that they shut down, 2 of which also had faulty ribbon cables for the monitors, which caused frequent flickering.
I’ve owned my dell studio 1749 for almost 2 years and it’s been brilliant, never had any problems, great value for money.

You’re gonna struggle to get something that’ll play the CoD games in high smoothly (mw1 up to black ops all require very similar graphical ‘power’), with a budget of $1000.

The GT540 card that was in the toshiba laptop mentioned here will be running black ops average of 40fps~ give or take in high settings, not the absolute max with AA turned on which’ll struggle along at around 25fps. You’ll be wanting 60+ fps for fast paced games like CoD so I suggest if you’ve got a bit of spare money then you opt in for something just a little more speedy, such as the GTX560M card. You’ll expect this to be running absolutely maxed at 65+ fps.

I’ve found that whatever laptop you buy, you’ll always be wanting more power in one or more hardware components, and the annoying thing is you can’t just upgrade, you’d have to buy the whole package again, so I’d just get the best you can, without being ridiculous, because you may be stuck with the same system for a while.
