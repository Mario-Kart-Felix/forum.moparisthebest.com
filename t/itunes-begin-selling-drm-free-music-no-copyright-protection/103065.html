iTunes begin selling DRM Free Music - No copyright protection

Source: http://www.usatoday.com/tech/products/services/2007-05-30-itunes-plus-service_N.htm?csp=34

NEW YORK — Apple on Wednesday began selling music from EMI unencumbered by copy-protection software.

The long-awaited iTunes Plus offers tracks by artists ranging from Coldplay to Frank Sinatra for $1.29, compared with 99 cents for copy-protected iTunes downloads. The unprotected tunes can be copied and also can be played on any computer or portable device — not just on iPods or computers with Apple software.

Listeners who earlier bought the EMI tracks from iTunes with copy protection, at 99 cents, can have it removed for 30 cents.

Amazon also plans to sell EMI downloads without protection.

The Apple service launched on a day filled with other changes in music and media. Among them:

• Viacom agreed to sell its Famous Music publishing unit to Sony/ATV— a music publisher co-owned by Sony and Michael Jackson.

The deal adds more than 125,000 titles to Sony/ATV’s 500,000 music copyrights, including The Beatles and Bob Dylan catalogs. Famous Music has standards such as Moon River, Satin Doll and Silver Bells as well as recent hits such as Bad Day and Hips Don’t Lie.

Famous Music also owns music publishing rights to The Godfather, A Chorus Line and The Producers.

Sony/ATV will pay about $400 million, Viacom CEO Philippe Dauman said at the annual meeting.

• CBS, one of the largest radio station owners, said Wednesday that it has paid $280 million for Last.fm, a website that helps music fans find new singers and bands.

Subscribers can create a personalized, streamed music service by noting performers that they already like. The site creates a playlist that includes musicians whom other people with similar tastes enjoy. It also can recommend concerts.

“Last.fm adds a terrific interactive extension to all of our properties and also is a huge step in CBS Corporation’s overall strategy of expanding our reach online to transition from a content company into an audience company,” CBS chief Leslie Moonves said in a statement.

Ad-supported Last.fm says it has more than 15 million users in more than 200 countries. It still will be run by its founders: Felix Miller, Martin Stiksel and Richard Jones.

• Fox Interactive Media has made itself an Internet photo power by agreeing to buy photo and video sharing service Photobucket. It also will get Flektor, a Web-based picture and video editing service.

Fox wants the sites to complement its existing social networking and entertainment sites, which include MySpace, IGN Entertainment, FOXSports.com and Americanidol.com.

• TiVo recorded its first-ever loss in total subscriptions in the quarter that ended in April that was marked by a price increase for its digital video recorders and growing competition from cable and satellite DVRs.

TiVo said that it ended the period with 4.34 million subscriptions, down 2.3% from January.

The company lost 103,000 customers who got service through DirecTV, which now offers its own DVR. TiVo added about 1,000 subscriptions from people who buy directly from the company.

But TiVo was upbeat about its financial results, which beat its projections and analyst estimates.

TiVo generated a profit of $835,000 in the quarter, up from a $10.7 million loss in the same period last year, on revenue of $60.4 million, up 6.8%.

Earnings of 1 cent per share, beat the 2-cent loss expected by analysts surveyed by First Call.

TiVo says it expects to return to red ink this quarter, due partly to expenses for a new marketing plan.

The company was upbeat about prospects for new business from cable operators offering TiVo as a premium service for their DVR customers. Comcast will begin to offer the option in Boston, southeast Massachusetts and New Hampshire in August; Cox will deploy to some customers “toward the end of the year,” CEO Tom Rogers says.

• News Corp. and NBC Universal said their still-unnamed online video joint venture will also offer programming from cable channels Fuel TV, Oxygen, Speed, the Sundance Channel and TV Guide.
