I just realised that I haven’t started a topic in here for a while. I was bored this afternoon, and decided to test a theory that one of my teachers put forth.
Start with an equilateral triangle. Draw a dot at each vertex.
Choose a random dot to start at. Choose another one.
Draw a dot at the midpoint.
Choose another of the starting dots, and draw a line halfway between it and your ‘current location.’
Continue doing this as long as you please.

My program does this. If it lands on a spot that has already been hit, the color darkens.

Apparently it’s supposed to make a great pattern. And hey, it does. It also works with a pentagon.

The coding isn’t exactly elegant in some places, and I really don’t care. I’m far too lazy to fix it.
But here you go.

PS. My favourite is definitely the pentagon, with a color gradient of 1, and about 30 million iterations.
