[size=18pt]General[/size]

Minecraft is a game of creativity and survival. The basic point of Minecraft is to build a basic shelter on your first night because when night rolls in, zombies and other mobs will spawn, and chase after you to kill you. Arrow keys to move and space bar to jump obviously. Left click to attack and punch.

P1: Collecting resources
Resources is the basis of minecraft. In order to obtain them, you must ‚Äúpunch‚Äù them. To do this, walk around until you find a tree. Get close enough for a small, grey box appear over the resource, and left click it and hold it in until it breaks. Pick up the tiny circling square. The wood should now be in your inventory.

P2: Crafting

Crafting is the art of making stuff. Refering to P1, press the letter ‚ÄúI‚Äù on your keyboard. The summary of your character and a small 2x2 box should appear next to your char. Drag your wood into the square, and 4 Wooden Planks should appear. Click on the wooden planks, hence how the other wood disapears, you just crafted your wood into wooden planks, preferably used for the structure of your shelter/house. Now, creating a crafting bench. put all of the wooden planks into all of the squares in your crafting workshop. A Crafting Bench should appear, click it and drag it onto your inventory and place it on the ground. This will provide more crafting space, and the ability to craft anything in the world.

P3: Tools and Weaponry

In order to sculpt your shelter, you need tools. Tools are shovels, pickaxes, hoes, and axes. Shovels eat up dirt relatively fast, depending on your type of shovel and we‚Äôll get to that soon. Pickaxes mines stone at a faster rate than punching it, hoes are used for creating soil to grow crops, and axes chop up wood at a faster pace. In order to make these, you need sticks. Repeat P1 until you have about 6 wood. Craft them into wooden planks. Refering to P3, click on your crafting bench and put 3 of each wood on top and on bottom of eachother (if you don‚Äôt get it, refer to the link above from the minecraft wiki) to create sticks. Then, put the 2 sticks on top and on bottom of eachother and 3 wood on the top 3 squares, and bam you have a pickaxe. (yes I know that was confusing, I think its better just to click on the minecraft wiki link above :o)

P4: Mining
Sooner or later you should come across an indent in a hill with grey blocks, this is a basic entrance to a mine. Mines hold valuable jewels, depening on how far you dig, you can reach gold, diamonds, redstone and more.
Anyway, enter the mine. Left click on a grey box and hold it. Then it will break, dropping Cobblestone. Cobblestone is not flammable. Cobblestone has no really uses other than creating better equipment, refer to P3.

P5: Griefing
Griefing is basically destroying other people work, flooding houses, setting forests on fire, setting houses on fire, and others. This is strongly against the moparscape minecraft server and 99.9% of other servers too, don‚Äôt do this.

P6: Mobs
Mobs are monsters or creatures in the game. There are some safe mobs, such as cows, pigs, chickens and sheep. Those mobs will not hurt you and they are visible day and night. Dangerous mobs come out ONLY at night and they consist of: Zombies, Skeletons, Creepers and Spiders. Creepers explode if attacked or disturbed, its very infamous for ruining shelters and houses. Zombies hit high but they walk very slow, Spiders hit very low but move very fast, skeletons move slow but have a bowanarrow.

[size=18pt]Common Blocks[/size]

There are currently 88 types of blocks, not counting the clouds, lava and water, which are not place-able at this point in time.

Dirt: found everywhere

Stone: found in mines

Gravel: found everywhere, mostly in mines

Clay: most commonly found near water/sand

Sand: found near water

Wood: found on trees

Cactus: found in deserts

Bedrock: found at bottom of the earth, cannot be mined

[size=18pt]Mining Blocks[/size]

Coal: found in mines, also on the faces of mines as well

Iron: found somewhat deep in mines

Gold: very rare, deep in mines

Redstone: common deep underground.

Diamond: extremely rare, found near bedrock/lava
