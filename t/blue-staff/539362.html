amy_webb1 August 4, 2016, 11:38am #5

Nice concept, does it fit the character? It seems a little short on the handle/pole part.

Should probably start with poly, still using too much. There are a few parts on the staff and the claws that don’t need cuts and such. Remember that where ever the shape changes dramatically then you should make a cut, point or line to show it, right now they’re all over the place even though they don’t need to be there.

I see you’re using 4 lines on the front of the model | | | | like so, however its easier to use | | | unless it is necessary to have more for the design’s shape which it doesn’t seem like it.

You might want to try working on your colour palette a bit, try use a few different colours then just different shades of colours. For example this could have had a trim around areas such as white or silver. Or even use a different colour for a handle or such just so it breaks that solid colour you have.

Also, try looking into trying a shading technique where you clone the colour and change the tone of the colour. One being 5 the next 10, 15 so on and then apply it down the model so it gives it a shadow effect.

You did a good job, however, you’re making good progress. Keep it up.

rip4d August 4, 2016, 11:38am #7

I like the concept, and I’m glad to see you have a good understanding of primitives Only thing “wrong” (which isn’t even a problem, its more of an optimization thing) I see is a few extra polygons that aren’t needed. Unless you were planning to use a transitional shading kind of thing with colors, you don’t need three segments for the middle darker blue part of the staff. Since its straight, you can just delete the middle two segments and join whats left together. And with the sphere, you don’t need that many polygons to get a good spherical shape! Its overkill, haha. You can just use a 10/4 or 10/6 segmented sphere and you’ll get a similar effect
