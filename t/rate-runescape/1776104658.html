0/10

The difficulty of building up your skills is nonexistent because of extensive guides available on every game mechanic. The only challenge in Runescape is making yourself actually do the clicking. Since Independent Thinking really only takes place when determining the most efficient methods of gaining experience or money, it would seem a waste to actually do any repetitive clicking yourself. If you just want to have “Fun” playing Runescape, consider what the minigames offer and realize what shitty game experiences they are on their own. It is only their connection to the larger persistent world which would make them ‘fun’. I mean, I’m not saying Castle Wars isn’t wicked awesome and shit, but it really isn’t even as fun as a multiplayer round of Hexen.

The only way you could possibly confuse Runescape with something fun is if you cheat. Even the NPCs in Runescape know that it’s a video game! It has no plot, only random half-baked ideas shoved together! Clearly, it is a game within a game. By playing it, you are losing. By not playing, you are winning.

Why do I say that you are losing Runescape by playing it? It is a game which has no internal life. The game actively stifles the imagination, for there is no true gameworld; there is a fantasy world, inside of a video game world, which is all inside of the real world. The fantasy world is the world of Runescape brought to life by our imagination. The middle ages, with magic and lots of seafood. The video game world is the reality of how the game actually works. You can drink an infinite amount of beer without getting alcohol poisoning and dying, the NPCs know it’s a game, you can mine the same rock an infinite number of times, etc… The real world is the numbers on a Jagex server, which you can manipulate in a in better fashion through your own client rather than the one Jagex provides. And this is the real game; cheating.

Since Jagex actively encourages all three ways of playing the game (they encourage the immersive fantasy aspect of the game as much as possible in every update, they encourage the meta-game-aspect by having holiday events and adding pop-culture references as much as possible, and they encourage cheating by adding easter eggs for cheaters and making cheating harder) we can see that it must be a truly shitty game, for them to try so hard to encourage people to spend time with it. I guess the moral is that anybody can make an incredibly bad game attractive simply by appealing to peoples sense of wonder.
