Strong onshore winds have helped wash up drugs along Volusia County Florida beaches the past two days, authorities said.

A 10-pound bundle of marijuana was found Saturday morning on the beach in the 3100 block of Daytona Beach Shores, said Volusia County Beach Patrol spokeswoman Tammy Marris.

On Friday night, officers found a small block of cocaine washed up along the shore in Ponce Inlet, Marris said.

Beach Patrol officials are working with the Volusia County Sheriff’s Office to determine where the drugs may have come from, Marris said.

The easterly winds, which were measured at about 15-20 mph Saturday, not only brought drugs but rough surf and a moderate rip current risk as well, according to the National Weather Service office in Melbourne. Winds will taper off today but are expected to strengthen by Thanksgiving day.

Anyone who comes across drugs along the beach is urged to contact authorities, Marris said.
