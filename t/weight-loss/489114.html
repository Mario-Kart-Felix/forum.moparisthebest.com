daily calories burned > daily calories ingested = fat loss

daily calories burned < daily calories ingested = weight gain

weight loss != fat loss

Weight loss is reducing how much you weigh, i.e water retention, muscle reduction, whatever results in you weighing less. Fat loss is reducing your body fat %.

Don’t eat less, just eat smarter

Elrick August 4, 2016, 3:31am #14
  1. East healthy sugars. Granny Smith apples are amazingly sweet and tart. You said you enjoy eating so without removing food out of the equation you just need to portion control and eat healthier. Remove good stuff like Ramen, basically all candy, and treat yourself maybe once every other day.

When it comes to eating out, go for the healthier routes. I used to love getting the cheese covered italian bread with ham and american cheese (foot long of course), but when you look at the grams of fat, it’s pretty huge compared to tuna or even turkey. My typical subway meal consists of: 6 in. Tuna on 9 grain wheat with no cheese, a packaging of apples, and blue powerade. I avoid cheese as honestly it offers very little difference in taste and packs on the fat grams.

  1. Remove premade food from your diet. Ramen, Spaghettios, canned chili, mac n cheese packets, all of that crap. My girlfriend and I started working on that and not only are we eating healthier, but we’re finding the food to be much more enjoyable and filling in smaller portions. Instead of heating up that bowl of spaghettios, grab a bowl of salad and croutons.

  2. WORK OUT! Do some cardio for about 30+ minutes every day and you’ll start to feel better. I run 2 miles in 30 minutes every other day and I’m not fit at all. You may not see physical differences, but you need to push yourself. After a week of 2 miles in 30 minutes I began pushing myself. I managed to cut 2 minutes off my 2 miles, then another 30 seconds, and then today I shaved off another 15 seconds. I’m not noticing a lot of difference (I’ve got the damn hip weight too) but I can tell my body is shaping up because of my time.
