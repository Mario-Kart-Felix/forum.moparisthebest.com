Well, for a first guitar I’d say either get a crappy one like The Un Named said or get an ibanez Guitar Center has Ibanez RGs with EMG pickups for 200 dollars, if Im not mistaken. Ibanez’s are easy to play and lots of cool famous people play them. As for an amp…get a line 6 or something, it doesnt really matter just as long as it sounds good. for a tuner, get a Korg CA30 chromatic tuner for about 20 dollars.

As for teaching yourself, there are tons and tons of things you will need to learn. You should really look into getting a guitar teacher, because theres almost always something he or she will know that you dont. But if thats out of the question then I can give you a few tips. For starters, electrics are usually a lot easier to play and way more fun. They open you up to so much more than acoustics. Acoustics are mainly used for playing chords and just about nothing else. But no matter which one you play, you need to first learn ur chords, scales, and notes of the neck. Memorize the notes of a standard tuned guitar and know which notes come after what, like sharps and that E and B have no sharp notes. Learning to alternate pick is very important. Also, always remember to keep your fingers parallel with the frets and keep your thumb low on the neck unless you are playing chords or something. That will help you play faster and easier. Know about harmonics and sweep picking, because they are really cool and used a lot in metal. Try really hard to keep your right hand on the top of the bridge of your guitar so it just pivots to get from one string to another. Learn how to change guitar strings and tighten the truss rod in your guitar. And lastly, know that most of the sound of your guitar comes from your pickups and amp. EMG, DiMarzio, and Seymour Duncan make really good pickups, and Marshall makes good amps. The majority of this I learned within the first few months of my playing guitar and it helped a lot.

Start out your guitar playing with simple things, like some Metallica stuff. Learn like the whole intro to Master of Puppets and the break down towards the middle. Crazy Train by Ozzy is good to start out on too, just learn most of it but the solo. Other crap I started out on were the intro to Raining Blood by Slayer, the intro to Sweet Home Alabama, the intro to Hells Bells by ACDC, various Iron Maiden stuff, and the scales. Scales are awesome for making you faster. You can get gud guitar tabs at ultimate-guitar.com, they have like everything.

Good luck out there
