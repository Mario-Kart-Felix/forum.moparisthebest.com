How To Zombify Your Dead Batteries!

General Discussion
Dregen July 30, 2016, 3:03pm #1

Have you ever been listening to your CD or MP3 player, and your in the mood for music, but then your batteries go dead?

Have you ever needed batteries, but forgot to get new ones at the store, and now your left with just a bunch of dead batteries that you probably won’t/can’t use?

Have you ever had any other battery related problem, which concern your not being able to reuse dead batteries?

If you answered YES to any of these questions, this guide is definitely for you!

Hello, I’m Dregen, your all around battery guru

I will teach you tricks on how to exploit and squeeze some extra minutes or even HOURS of power from your dead batteries! If you find anything in my guide to be faulty, please let me know, as I have only gathered this information by testing, and a tiny bit from word-of-mouth. Thank you.

Please note that the items that use batteries I will call APPLIANCES! This can mean a CD/MP3 player, a Game Boy, whatever.

First of all, why use normal batteries and not rechargeable batteries? I have no clue. But this is for those people who are either too lazy to use rechargeable batteries, or don’t want to use them (Including me, lol ).

Now for the long awaited tips and tricks

The first, and most IMPORTANT step of all is to gather up as many dead batteries as you can! In your vehicle, home, school bus, friend’s house, it doesn’t matter, just as long as they still have enough juice in 'em to use. Having a somewhat large battery storage will help you in the long run with this guide. I once had more then 15 batteries in my coat pockets so I’m assuming they shouldn’t be that hard to find.

Second, find a place to store your batteries. Like i said before, you can use your coat pockets, or maybe an empty pocket on your schoolbag you don’t use. Doesn’t have to be too fancy, it’s just to hold them and keep them somewhat safe from the elements. You don’t need to worry about hiding them, I’m sure no one will steal them unless they are incredibly stupid, they have read my guide

Extra tip: Something that helps is having 1-3 commercial brand batteries, such as Duracell, Rayovac, or Energizer. These batteries usually have a lot of power, and are good to pair up other dead batteries with, whether the commercial brand battery is dead or not. Here’s my reliability/power containment rank for each of the three brands listed.

Rayovac : #1 (Highest)
Duracell: #2 (Lower)
Energizer: #3 (Lowest)

Of course, I mainly use Rayovac so this chart may be way off.

I think Rayovac make these cheaper “Heavy Duty” batteries, so note that these ado NOT contain more power than the more expensive one, but far less. Just thought I would make a note of that.

Okay, now here’s how you do it.

I’m using a 2 battery appliance as an example. Take your dead batteries, and grab whichever one has the most power. This is probably gonna be your standard commercial brand battery. Stick the good battery in a slot. Now take another dead battery, and stick it in. Attempt to turn on your appliance. If it works, congratulations. If it doesn’t try try again. Take another dead battery, replace it with the other bad battery, and repeat this procedure until it works.

Seems pretty simple, huh? That’s not all. Here a few tips to take some of those batteries and give them a temporary power jump, so you can reuse them more and more often.

Changing the temperature of a battery can do harm, or it can do good. Say for example, it’s a cold winter day, and your batteries turn cold when you get on the bus from school, and it turns out your appliance will now have less battery power or won’t work at all.

Stop! Don’t think your batteries are dead. It’s just the temperature of the batteries that is affecting their power. Take your batteries from the appliance, and hold them in your hands and warm them up. Once they are warm, stick them back in. Notice that the battery power is now much higher than before, and your appliance has more energy.

Now it may seem cold kills off batteries, but this isn’t entirely true. Sometimes sticking your batteries in the fridge for a bit will give your batteries about 15-30 minutes of more juice. But it doesn’t last long. Once the batteries warm up, they lose most of their energy.

Now, it’s not just cold. Heat, which I touched on very briefly before, can give your batteries more energy. Even heating up a battery that seems warm, by squeezing in your hand for a bit, STILL can give your battery more power. I usually do this to re-energize my dead batteries. It’s like giving them a shot of steroids.

Here are a few more tips that may be of use to you.

The more batteries an appliance needs, the harder it is to get it to work with dead batteries. Dead batteries seem to affect these more than they do when your using an appliance that requires less batteries. This is probably because these use more power, and it can be quite difficult trying to power up 4 dead batteries to the point where you can use them all at once. And even if you manage to do it, it won’t last long at all. I haven’t tested this much, so if this is wrong, correct me.

Commercial brand batteries may not be good enough for a dead battery pair if it is dead enough. Make sure to figure out how much power the battery is.

If a battery will outright not work at all (in other words, dead… truly dead), throw it away. It will be of no use to you.

That’s it! Thanks for reading this guide. I definitely enjoying writing this, because it gave me something to do in Study Hall at school. I hope these methods help you save money, and also help us SAVE OUR ENVIRONMENT BY CONSUMING LESS BATTERIES! If you need help, or find a problem in my guide, once again, don’t hesitate to ask me.

Have some fun bringing dead batteries back to life! Mhuwhahahaha!

EDIT: Another tip to get batteries more juice below!

I will use a fake story as an example.

Okay, say your listening to your MP3 player on the way to school, and the batteries go dead. You don’t do any of these methods, or switch out the battery. You just leave it in.

Now, when you get back from school, you attempt to try to turn it on again. It works.

The point of that is… That if you stop using a battery for a bit, it will recharge some power! Neato, huh?
