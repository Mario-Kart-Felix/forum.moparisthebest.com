How did you ‘deal’ with them?[/quote]
Guy on watch spotted them (since rubberboat is too small to be spotted by radar). We started taking snapshots from the boat and called spains boarder guard. Since we were around 100km from Spains coast it took 1 hour for them to arrive.

We tried to keep distance from the rubberboat since we couldnt take them to our ship (otherwise they would have become Finlands problem). Our snapshots revealed that they had a small engine. Suddenly we saw something “small” dropping from the rubberboat to the sea. We were afraid that they might start jumping to the sea since in that case we should rescue them and take them to our custody. We were not sure was that something that fell from the rubberboat a possible human being. We mount our rescueboat, loaded our 12.7, and our rescueboat approached the estimated area where something fell from the boat. (All the time this Moroccans rubberboat tried to approach our battleship hoping that we would take them in custody since they had not get anything to eat or drink for several days). So when our rescueboat approached the area where something had seen in the water those Moroccans started to approach our rescueboat and all the sudden our rescueboats enginge shutted down. We got our rescueboat working in the last seconds and we didnt even had to shoot warning shots.

After 1 hour Spains boarder guard came and after that we didnt got any information what happened to those Moroccans. What I do know that it is fairly common that a rubberboat full of Moroccans is trying to cross the Mediterranean Sea.
