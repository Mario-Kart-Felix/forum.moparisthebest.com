Kryptix August 2, 2016, 3:56pm #8

For me, a friend I used to run a website with started playing a new game and he showed less and less interest in our projects. He kept saying he was having a ‘RuneScape break’ which used to annoy me a lot. After a while I decided to check it out. I had never really been a gamer but it always interested me how things were done and made.

I loaded it up and as simple as RSC was, it was actually incredibly confusing for a first time player who was like 12-13 at the time. He told me to train on the Chickens in Lumbridge and gave me some items that at the time I thought was really good. My XP kept going up and I was pretty proud that I could kill Cows all day healing myself with their meat after cooking it on a fire that I had lit.

It got to the point where I was really jealous of anyone who had better items than me and went on a quest to get rich, spending all day sat in Varrock buying and selling stuff…

Eventually all F2P was explored and it got a bit tiring (this was before I knew what the wilderness was really). I got membership and began training at the Guard Dogs in Ardougne. I then made friends with I Wicked I/Zar Family 2/Adx1/Sue Me/Azn Purity (etc) and was able to train all day at the wildrness axes without being disturbed by anyone which was a huge plus as it was really fast XP and usually all the spots were taken on every server, and if they weren’t then you would be kicked out very shortly after arriving anyway. I gained levels quickly and then started to get jealous of them having fun killing people during their training sessions so started gaining magic levels and joining them with pathetic stats like 70 54 85 with 85 magic… Although they were awful it was the magic side of things that helped. I had a pretty much unlimited income on there so just fired as many runes as I could. I was eventually 99 magic before I was even 85hp and it was all purely from the wilderness.

After I had good enough stats to defend myself (88 70 96 or something) I began mainly PKing on F2P with Adx1/Sue Me. It was much more fun than training.

It just got addicting, really wanted 99hp but any time I had available I wanted to PK… I think I got to like 42m magic XP and was still only 93hp or something. RSC was then dying, RS2 was out and everyone was botting… I was intrigued by AutoRune and how it worked, ended up autoing to 99hp on the Scorpions in Legends Guild. My prayer was only like 70 though so level 120 was the highest I reached.

The XP was slow as hell so you was forced to AFK a lot, you wanted to protect your spot, it caused wars, you were forced to make friends and socialize.

Without low XP and lots of competing players RSC can’t be the same.

Id like to go further in depth with how much it meant to the player to gain levels, items and knowledge of the game.

When we all first played rsc it was fresh. You didn’t know what was around the next corner and you most likely didn’t know how to skill crafting. It was a new world and it was an adventure.

I was fascinated with the game when I could see other players. After playing Warcraft II, Starcraft I… I was always interested in playing with another player(s). The multiplayer end of rsc was very new. The first thing I did was pick up bronze daggers and sell them to the shop in lumbridge… I remember my first 100gp I felt accomplished.

What even made me more of a noob was that I thought “attack” raised the amount of damage you dealt … so I ended up having like 70 attack 40 str 30 def.

What really sucks is that since we all played rsc we have it memorized just about. So its lost its value, all we care about is who got what and who is the highest level.

I honestly believe the best thing for rsc (besides a replica low rate) is to just revamp the entire game completely. Different map, different quests, different rewards, bonuses, skilling is different etc etc. It would bring that freshness back to the game.
