iPENGUIN July 31, 2016, 3:22pm #7

Ok, easy fix.

“Start” > “Computer” > Click your hard drive (might have (C:) in the name) > Program Files > Java > jdk1.6.0 (it may not be 1.6.0, just click your lastest version) > bin

Ok, now that “bin” is opened, highlight the address bar at the top, should be something like “C:\Program Files\Java\jdk1.6.0\bin” and copy this.

Now that you have that copied…
Start > Right click “Computer” and select “Properties” > Advanced system settings > Environment Variables

In the first box (should have ‘User variables for (youruser)’ above it)
Click “New…” and for “Variable name” put “classpath” and for “Variable path” put a “.” and select “Ok”.

Now go to the second box (should have ‘System variables’ above it)
Click “CLASSPATH” and click “Edit…” The “Variable name” should be “CLASSPATH” and for “Variable path” put put “CLASSPATH=C:\ProgramFiles\Java\jdk1.6.0\bin;%CLASSPATH%” - Make sure you have your address you copied before instead of C:\Program Files\Java\jdk1.6.0\bin.

Still in the second box, go down to “Path” and click “Edit…” The “Variable name” should be “Path” and for “Variable path” paste the thing you highlighted earlier in that spot and your done. Hope this helps.
