moparisthebest July 31, 2016, 10:53am #8

Here is a good-looking tutorial: http://www.linuxquestions.org/questions/linux-newbie-8/learn-the-dd-command-362506/

Basically, to dump a partition to a file:
dd if=/dev/hda1 of=/path/to/file

and to put it back on, you guessed it
dd if=/path/to/file of=/dev/hda1

Of course, instead of doing one partition you may want to do a whole drive:
dd if=/dev/hda of=/path/to/file
and back
dd if=/path/to/file of=/dev/hda

That is assuming the hard drive is hda, and the partition is hda1.
