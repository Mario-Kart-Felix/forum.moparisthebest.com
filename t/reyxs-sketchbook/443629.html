Frell August 3, 2016, 3:04am #10

if you see light, dont overthink it, just ‘dab’ it where it is. Dont try and detail. Dont choose intense light colors, only go a little brighter/darker and then let brightness and contrast scale them when youre done

NOTE: generally when colors gets lighter, you can replicate that in photoshop by moving the color picker not only up but also to the RIGHT. Do not make the mistake of bringing your colors towards white to make them lighter, youre just washing them out. Darks sometimes do the same, but you can gracefully just go straight down (NEVER TO PURE BLACK) or a little to the left.

Lay down one main color
put down the main colors of everything
lay down darks
lay down lights

It will look sloppy up close but it still should look realistic if your colors are right. Toward the end you can start zooming in and cleaning up alot of stuff if you want, but while laying down the main lights and dark stay relatively far away (unless of course youre putting light on a strap… then zoom in lol)
