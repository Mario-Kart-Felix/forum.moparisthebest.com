the_bank July 31, 2016, 5:11pm #13

Been there done that Ollie, and not on drugs…infact I’ve been having dreams like that from long before I started doing drugs or drinking.

It isn’t exactly lucid, because even though you have semi-control over yourself, you still have to follow the boundaries your mind has set for this dream and can not alter the “rules” so to speak. It is a game, and you lost. Just as I have many times before. It is sometimes impossible to win, other times very difficult depending on the circumstances of your dream.

I would wake up with scratches…cuts…even a bruise once. Each one I would be able to relate directly to the same place it happened in my dream. I even started keeping a journal about these “dream games”. They are very scary indeed.

I seen a sleep specialist when I was 15 for a sleep apnea test. Sure enough, I had a dream that very evening. The specialist woke me up before I could get far into it, and told me I was self-inflicting wounds on myself. Three deep scratches on my chest where in my dream a bear had attacked me.

The way he explained it, is once you hit a certain stage of sleep your body’s nervous system becomes so shutdown (so to speak) that you can’t even feel yourself inflicting damage to your body. However, when you do, the nerves still send the electric impulses to your brain saying where the pain is coming from. Your dream then adjusts to this and creates a circumstance where this injury is inflicted.

I’m very interested and can relate completely to this subject so if you would like to ask any more questions about it I’d be happy to answer. I’ve been there…first time is the hardest :P…but they aren’t that common and not many people can have them.
