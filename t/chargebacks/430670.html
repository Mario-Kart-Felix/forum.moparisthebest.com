whackatre August 2, 2016, 3:33pm #1

Is there any working way to prevent players from doing chargebacks? I really dislike scammers and liars. I’ve had about ten players on my server take back their money (about 600 USD total) this year and it’s kinda annoying. Pretty repetitive procedure. The idiots play for a couple of weeks enjoying their status and items. They quit or abuse. They take their money back. Paypal emails me. I send them the same letter discussing the TOS (agreeing that donations are non refundable), the rules, etc. After a couple of months, nothing happens. I assume I lose. I’ve also lost every chargeback, as I do not think Paypal cares about virtual goods. Why does Paypal side with the customer? I’m sick and tired of these bullshit “unauthorized claims”, (meaning the players’ bank accounts were used without their permission). Very ironic as it is I who is getting scammed, not them! There is no way to prove that I have given someone donator status, items, etc. So I thought of a method that might work (keyword: might), by transferring the money to the bank account after every donation and delaying the donation process by 48 hours so if they decide to chargeback, I will simply stop using my account which will probably be in negative balance. I’ve read quite a few articles about chargebacks and it appears that Paypal only cares about negative balances over $1000. Can I use this to my advantage to stop players from scamming me? Pretty crappy method, can anyone think of anything else?
