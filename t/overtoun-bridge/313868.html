Overtoun Bridge in Scotland, also known affectionately as Dog Suicide Bridge, has been the location of other 50 dog deaths since the 1950s or 1960s. The dogs will, of their own accord, jump to their deaths into Overtoun Burn, the stream below.

However, why the dogs always leap from the same side of the bridge, why it only occurs on sunny days (which are rare in that region of Scotland), and why it has been reported that some dogs who jumped and survived the landing will return to the top of the bridge and throw themselves off again.

In 1994 a man threw his infant son off of the bridge in claims that the child was the Anti-Christ. Later on he tried to kill himself there as well. There were rumors that he was on drugs.

In Celtic mythology, Overtoun is known as ‘the thin place’ where heaven and Earth are said to be close.
