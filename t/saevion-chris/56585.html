[glow=red,2,300]Message from bill (chris’ father)[/glow]

Bill has decided not to be come to the vFuneral if/when it takes place. Below is a message bill would like to share with everyone.

To everyone on the 'Mopar' site:

Hello to everyone,

I am Bill Cobb, Chris’ (Saevion’s) father. I have been following the thread started by Sean all weekend. I honestly don’t have the words to express how touched and moved I am for all of the kind words and thoughts. Although it has been over a year since his passing, I still grieve and cry every day. I must thank you all from the bottom of my heart.

I am sorry that I didn’t try harder than I did to try to find you all. I am so happy and relieved that Sean came across one of the posts I had left out there. There is a bit of closure for me to know that his friends have finally gotten the news.

When Chris died, I turned into a ‘basket case’. I was all I could do to just want to wake up in the morning. There were several times when I considered ‘joining’ him…Chris was my first-born child, the eldest of five wonderful children. Somehow, I knew that he wouldn’t have wanted me to do that. Maybe, just maybe, this was why…

I understand that Sean is organizing a vFuneral in RS for Chris. I think that is a wonderful idea, and I encourage all who knew, or heard of him to try to get there. Chris would have gotten a kick out of it, I’m sure.

However…

I have decided not to attend. My wife (His step-mom, Nise) and his step-sister (Sammi) may attend. Please understand that this is not out of any disrespect for any of you. When Chris died, I had my funeral for him, and grieved as a father should, with family and close friends there.

This is your hour. Where his friends can get together and console each other. I should not be a part of that. I was not involved with you when Chris was alive, I should not intrude and possibly take any focus away in this memorial to him. I ask only one thing: Please don’t get into a ‘grief fest’ (I have cried enough tears for us all…). Celebrate his life and any influance he may have had on you personally or by association.

Chris introduced me to the concept of ‘open source’, and all of the implications. I respect what you do, and what you stand for. To Chris, it was never ‘cheating’, just an alternative solution…

I am proud and honored to find such a respectful community of folks that Chris was associated with. A father always worries ‘what kind of crowd’ his son hangs around with…Well, I have no worries on that matter…

To close: Again, Thank you all for being so kind…You are the sort of person/people that Chris was…To everyone who has emailed my privetely: Thanks, and I WILL answer all, just give me a day or two to get my thoughts straight. To Sean and Jason: Well…you know how I feel…TY…

I am able to go to sleep each night knowing that the last words I heard from my son were, “I love you, Pop. I’ll see you tomorrow”…And the last thing I said to him was, “I love you too, son. Have a good night”. Please, please, try to say that to the ones that are important in your life.

For Chris, through Chris, thank you all, and I love you all as I did my son.

Respectfully,

Bill
