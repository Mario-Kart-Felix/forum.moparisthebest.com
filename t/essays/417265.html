For a strong essay, you’re going to want an introduction paragraph, 3 body paragraphs, and a conclusion.

Lets use “9/11 conspiracies” as an example

For the introduction paragraph, use the funnel technique. Make a broad statement to start with, such as “One of the most impacting days in modern American history would be the 9/11 terrorist attacks”. Then, funnel your way down to a thesis, such as “the 9/11 attacks were actually an attack on America by itself to rally the public so they could go to war with the middle east”. Normally, avoid using “I believe” in any formal paper unless noted otherwise. The sentence after, give 3 examples, which will be the topic for your body paragraphs. “This is because the USA’s need for oil, the lack of plane parts at the Pentagon, and that the towers falling was in a demolition style.”

Now, those 3 points will be the topic sentence for the next 3 paragraphs, which should fill up the majority of your paper. All of your arguements and the support go in here, just remember to take your time and don’t overlook anything. Teachers typically like 3 examples per paragraph. For the controlled demolition paragraph, you could state:
-the force of the planes impact
-the fires heat not hot enough to melt the steel frame
-the buildings falling downward, not toward another building

Then, the conclusion paragraph. It’s basically a reverse funnel. Restate your thesis and the 3 points, then slowly broaden out to a general yet CLOSING statement. Don’t leave the reader hanging.

Hope all of this helped, and no, these are not my views on 9/11.
