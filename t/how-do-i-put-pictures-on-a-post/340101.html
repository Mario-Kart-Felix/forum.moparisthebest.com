environmental variables are quite easy.

Go to [size=14pt]Start[/size]

Right click [size=14pt]Computer[/size], Then click properties [size=14pt]Properties [/size]

Underneath tasks you will find [size=14pt]Advanced/Advanced system settings [/size] click on that

Click [size=14pt]Environmental Variables[/size]

Click New for underneath the [size=14pt]user variables[/size]

Make variable name [size=18pt]Path[/size]. For value put: C:\Program Files (x86)\Java\jdk1.6.0_20\bin; REMEMBER TO REPLACE THE 20 WITH THE VERSION YOU HAVE!

Now make another variable called [size=18pt]Classpath[/size], put the name CLASSPATH=C:\Program Files (x86)\Java\jdk1.6.0_20\bin;%CLASSPATH%;. REMEMBER TO REPLACE THE 20 WITH THE VERISON OF JAVA YOU HAVE!

You have made Environmental Variables. Hope this helped
