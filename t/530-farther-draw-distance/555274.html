@David: No. You have to update the viewing frustrum so that the far distance doesn’t cull fragments beyond the 16 tile area your client renders around you, modify the length of the viewport, update the culling distance so that it doesn’t clip tiles (non-gl), and you have to modify the fog far depth. If you want exactly where those are I wouldn’t begrudge you but this is pretty experimental because of the way they do dynamic shadows you really fudge your frame rate. I need to optimize it more, and play around with what looks best.

530 isn’t a very optimized client OpenGL wise (especially with the technology we have in GL3-4). A lot of things can be done better and you can probably easily get 50 FPS after, especially on newer hardware.

There’s also an issue with the player updating only supporting showing players in your local 16x16 area. But my eventual plan is to completely redo the mobile updating protocols so that you can render people far outside that area (although you won’t be able to interact with them for balance purposes).

Also I want to add ‘render distance’ setting to the graphics interface, so that people on older hardware can’t access the game. That’d be unfortunate.

My client is completely renamed by the way (classes). I will eventually release it when I finalize some naming and fix some of the really nasty nested if-statements.
