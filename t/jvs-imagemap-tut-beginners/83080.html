[color=purple][center][size=16pt]JV’s ImageMap Tutorial[/size][/center]

[color=green][center]The tutorial aims to share with you the joys of ImageMapping! And because most people won’t know how, or are simply too lazy to make IMGM’s the proper way, This tutorial teaches you how using a nifty program called MapThis.[/center]

[hr]

Step 1: First things first! click here to download MapThis.

Step 2: When you have got everything installed and ready, you’re ready to create your IMGM!
Open up the mapthis software, it should look something like this:

Step 3: Press the “New” button, and select your image.

Step 4: Now you’re ready to start adding in your LINKS! :D. It’s a simple process, along the top of the screen there’s a list of shapes, (†). if you’re doing a circular link, use the circle tool, if you’re using a rectangle link, use the rectangle tool. If your link is an unusual shapee, don’t panic! that’s why the ‘Polygon’ tool was born! (Y), Simply draw round your link, and ‘Voila’.

Step 5: Once you’ve drawn out your link area, a box like this should pop up, type in the web address of the place you’d like the link to take you to!

Step 6: Once you’ve drawn out all the links you need, and filled in all the web addresses, you need to save it, this is how it should look (Important: Save as a HTML file):

Step 7: Now, putting this baby on the interweb! First, Open your new HTML file in notepad, and listen to the next instruction carefully.

Step 8: Copy this code into your webpage:

<IMG SRC="Path To Your Image" USEMAP="#Name Of Your Map" BORDER="0" > followed by the contents of the HTML file you created on MapThis. (Not including body tags).

In the end, you should have created something which looks like this:

[code]

[/code]

Step 9: Enjoy your delicious new Linked Image!
