 It's been a while since I've posted here- I have been re-thinking many 'elements' of construction to take for this project and testing little things before I commit fully to the project.  The current engine I had created in C++ and DirectX has been archived for now. Lately I have been inspired of how simple pythons language is and the libraries available. So I went back to the drawing board and more ideas came to mind.. I have also been wondering about the amount of development I could do without turning RS1 into a half-ass RS2 project.

Here is what concerns me at this time…

[ul][li]3d Modeling-- If I used Autocad 3ds max to recreate the sprites and animation it would have to be at least 90% focused on mimicking the character, tiles, walls, player camera, walk flow, animations, skilling etc to nearly exact… I.E My current idea is to start with the same camera view besides being able to completely rotate the camera any angle around the players character… The player character is being modeled in 3ds max (efficient enough however very basic model to start off with.) same textures and clothing/hair style but more colors to choose from. Item sprites in inventory will remain the exact same. the players’ mouseover menus will remain exactly the same (tbd). Skilling will have no animation at all (for now to stick with rs1 bubble). I will replicate the combat animation to best of ability however magic casts and ranging will have new animation simulating archery and magician(tbd). All tiles, walls, and objects will remain the same however they must be converted into 3d objects as well… [/li][/ul]

Once again, the game engine is now being written in python using a few external libraries for handlers and composing well just about everything… Soon as I get done with a model or two of the character it will be previewed here. Pretty soon I should have a fully functional character creation segment of the game with the player having ability to choose its starting role in RS1 Remastered.

Edit: After reviewing the character sprites and few fail attempts at getting a close mimic of the 2d sprites into a 3d form using 3ds max I am currently withdrawing the idea of 3d for now but not entirely. Being that RS1s character design has many flaws in topography- lighting and body posture so oddly coordinated (I wont get into all the details yet.) I will take a break from that and continue to finish the character registration scene and roster everything under a private mysqli server for now with various of encoders to secure player password. I will not require an email to registration as you never needed to back then. I probably won’t do the player reporting segment until lateron I still would like to see a semi functioning game before I start doing the other necessities. I have also designed a color scheme for the 2d registration system that will add 200 more color choosing options to the players torso and pants, without mathematically altering the various shaders. Somebody had requested that I made a secondary client for the game to be played in webbrowsers. I am not saying 100% I will but like I said before I will need to make sure I am even able to correctly place all of the objects in their original positions with character functioning (movement & animation) with camera view with a few objects in place first to further decide where this project really goes.

Guild System:
I don’t care for the “clan” word, to me the word ‘clan’ describes a very small unorganized group. However I’m not going to judge you if you call your posse’ a clan. Anyway, I’m sure this has been brought about somewhere and some time ago. I’m just here to help generalize and help place these ideas into conceptions and well, mainly how I will go about creating this when the time comes and the features I plan on bringing to my project.

Initialization: To create/found your guild you simply hover over the buddy icon and below the ‘Click here to add a friend’ clickable text two options are born. “Click here to create your guild” or simply “Create Guild.” then below that your friends will notice the “search / join guild.”

Create Guild Menu:
Very simple menu , I mean it could practically be the same black box as if you're sending a private message but of course replacing the text, "Enter message to send to so and so" with, "Enter the name of your newly founded guild" or whatever. You get the idea. Then walla- a new guild was born.

Join Guild Menu:
I haven’t exactly put much thought into this menu itself however you would want it to consist of the necessities;
0. Recent Invitations; 1. Search Bar with criteria (Skilling,Pking,RolePlaying, etc); 2. Open Public List ( Guild Name, Guild Description, Member Count, (lol, wealth stack,kill count? random features like such);

When you have either created or joined a guild, those two clickable texts (create/join guild) magically disappear in replace with the Guild Panel. Members will be allowed to make changes to the guild structure depending on his or her rank and the access level (set by the leader or founder. I will explain more about access level later on.

Guild Panel:

[code]
0. Recent Activities (I.E, TechN9ne was slayed by Red Rooster!; or Red Rooster attained 99 fishing! etc.)

  1. Guild Achievements (I.E Fish 50,000 Sharks; or Kill 1,000 Players)
  2. Letterbox (Shoutbox( Need to leave your clan a message? feel free to do so))
  3. Settings -> | Access Levels, Ranks, Description; anything guild editable)
  4. Invite Player
    -In example to Access Levels… Say Access Level in a guild was 1-5. 5 being the highest with the most access to the guilds settings and features, most likely the founder or leader. 1 being the lowest, the newly recruited member, not trusted enough to enter the guild’s bank or leave a message on the letterbox. You get the idea. The guild founder will be able to modify the access levels to his or her wishes. So if the access level requirement to put a message on the letterbox was 1, every member could use that feature and so on.[/code]

I don’t want to get too in depth of my guild system ideas here just yet as I’ve written a lot already however I must at least share some of the main features!

It’s been many months but with out a doubt (alright some) Ive made incredible progress… Yeah 6 months later and the game still isn’t ready for testing however here’s all I’ve done so far… Images soon to display.

Player Character 100% complete : Player characters are now fully customizable from head to toe. New hair styles, torsos, pants shorts shoes you name it all rigged & though seem excessive- I feel it turned out great ! Rune armor looks awesome on men and gorgeous on the ladies.
