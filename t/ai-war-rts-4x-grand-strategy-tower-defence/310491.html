Hey guys, as the developer of the game, I can tell you that I generally fall into a similar category with RTS games. I enjoy stuff set in space (Star Wars original trilogy, Descent: Freespace game, Ender’s Game novel, etc), but I’ve never enjoyed an RTS game set there. So, that was something I aimed to fix with AI War – basically, my inspirations for the gameplay were more along the lines of CivIV, SupCom, and AoE3.

Regarding the other comments about longer not necessarily being better, I completely agree. However, that said, there is something of a difference between 5-minute Speed Chess and a 4 hour game of Chess, by the same token. In the case of RTS, for AI War the extra time lets you have a long chain reaction of effects based on every decision. This is cool because the battlefield is continuously evolving as you play, with reactions to the decisions you make, which ultimately provides more variety and interest.

I built AI War primarily as a game for my own co-op play group to enjoy, and so aimed it at fixing the issues we had with the other RTS game’s we’d been playing over the last 10 or so years. Chief among those were a number of co-op woes, a lack of variety between games, underwhelming AI opponents, games that go downhill after a certain crisis point against your opponents (it’s all easier from that stage on, etc, until you win), and the repetitive nature of playing two one-hour games per night where the general flow of each is always the same. So hence having longer games, that build as they go, was an ideal solution to that last one, and all of the other points (plus others I’m sure I’m not thinking of) were also key things that I made a point to address.

Anyway, I’m glad to see that Greeny and others are enjoying the game so much.

If there are any questions, feel free to let me know, or there’s a load of information about the game on the Arcen site, the wiki for the game, or the various reviews (PC Gamer, Boomtown, Co-Optimus, etc) for the game.[br][br][size=1]Posted on: October 26, 2009, 09:22:54 am[/size][hr][quote=“Speljohan, post:11, topic:310491”]I’d play if it was multiplayer vs players as that’s my main reason for rts’s[/quote]

By the way – that’s cool, this game might just not be for you. Those who are diehard pvp fans certainly won’t find much to love with AI War. However, if you think of AI War as something of a different genre altogether from your standard RTS (and really it is – is a combination of 4X, RTS, TBS, grand strategy, and tower defense all wrapped into an RTS control scheme), then you might enjoy this as something completely new. For instance, in the past I have enjoyed bother Unreal Tournament (and later iterations), as well as Left 4 Dead, and they are two totally different experiences despite the presence of shooter mechanics in both.

In fact, I would argue that the true experience of Left 4 Dead could not be experienced any other way than as solo/co-op against the hordes of AI-controlled enemies (the versus mode their being a minor quasi-exception). With AI War, it’s the same sort of thing. You’re not fighting against bots, you’re fighting against a simulated fleet of opponents (and the AI is extremely good, some of the best in the genre by most accounts).

Still may not be your cup of tea, and that’s quite all right. No game’s for everyone, and there are plenty of beloved RTS games that I can’t stand. And others that I’ve written off because their AI was not good enough or they didn’t support co-op in a good enough way, or were missing random maps, etc. Anyway, but I thought I’d chime in with a bit more about the motivations for this sort of design.

I’m totally with you, to be honest. My assessment of many RTS games that I play co-op was: 15 minutes of rote startup, 20 minutes of nearly-rote buildup (I’m a boomer), 20 minutes of interesting fighting, 10-20 minutes of fun-but-not-challenging smashing of the opponent. Repeat.

With AI War, due to player suggestions we managed to automate the entire 15-minute rote startup period, so that you basically come out of the gate ready to go do something interesting. I guess the overall structure of the game is less like an RTS, and more like a 4X such as CivIV, etc – just with RTS controls and play. I always found CivIV addictive and loved how it built over a similar span of time to how AI War does, so that’s how I had modeled AI War. Obviously, the proof is in the pudding, and no amount of me talking at you is going to convince you, but I think that it’s worthwhile to point out that my goals with the longer games were essentially to address the same complaints that you have with other games. It’s not a case of “MOAR ships, more time, more strategeez!” Rather, it’s all pretty well designed; that’s what netted us some of our better reviews, etc.
