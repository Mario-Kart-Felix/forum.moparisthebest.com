The new RS is certainly a piece of work, but I don’t really like it due to the abilities. They make the game more challenging and interactive due to the situation based use of certain abilities, which is amazing. The abilities aren’t as great as I expected, because they aren’t as efficient as I thought they would be. It seems like most of the abilities are for monsters with more health than usual, due to the long cool-down times of most spells. I’ve taken a look at all the spells, and they are really neat; however, they didn’t really perform as great as I thought.

A couple of builds I thought of were slice-and-dice and affliction. There is no official build names for these because I thought of the names due to the certain activities in a fight.

Slice-and-dice:

[ul][li]Constantly using spells that are at your resource to output the most damage in a short period of time[/li]
[li]Abilities used should be based on the cool-down (long to short), so there will be a possible chance to use more powerful abilities more than once[/li][/ul]

Affliction:

[ul][li]Always use abilities that apply damage over a set amount of time[/li]
[li]The main source of damage is derived from the abilities that have DoTs (damage over time)[/li][/ul]

Even with the builds above, the actual rotation of abilities used should always be tweaked due to variety of gear and monster type, or maybe even personal preference. I’ve tried both builds and I’ve tested different situations and rotations of each build, but I’ve yet to conclude a ‘good’ standing of ability importance. This is just my opinion, because I’m sure other people have adapted to abilities nicely, even if it is due to the type of armor and weapons you use.
