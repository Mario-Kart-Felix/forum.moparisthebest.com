OP: If you are trying to go $500 - $600cheap(like I did), I suggest this.

Heres a few details on the build.
[b]The Case:/b
The Azza Orion 202 EVO is a beautiful case. Leds, great airflow and has a protective lid. The lid on the case is where the power button, reset button and dvd drive is behind. I know this because I have this case and I have had 1 small problem. Unfortunately, the 3.5" HDD didn’t fit for some odd reason. I guess it was a manufacturer problem. Besides this, the case is excellent.

[b]Processor:/b
This processor is the best quad-core, low-priced processor you’ll ever find. It is excellent. I personally prefer AMD over Intel just because of the prices. If you prefer better gaming, i suggest going with an Intel processor. Like the Intel Core i5 2500k. (http://pcpartpicker.com/part/intel-cpu-bx80623i52500k)

[b]Motherboard:/b
So I think this is an excellent price for a motherboard. It’s an ECS A885GM-A2 (V1.1). It has an AM3 processor input, holds 32gb of ram(even 1600) And it has an onboard video (radeon hd 4250) if you don’t want to buy a dedicated one. By the way, this motherboard is not compatible with Intel processors.

Power Supply:
Not much to say about it, just the fact that make sure you use your old power cord, it doesn’t come with one. Also you can get same power supply with the cord for $10 more. (http://pcpartpicker.com/part/compucase-power-supply-hp585dretail) I suggest using your old power cord. It saves an easy $10.

[b]Memory:/b
Again, not much to say but that 4gb is basically all you need. You can upgrade to 8gb, but that’s the maximum you would need for anything. (unless you’re doing high-tech computer graphics)

Hard Drive:
(http://www.ebay.com/ctg/Seagate-DiamondMax-21-320-GB-Internal-7200-RPM-3-5-STM3320620A-Hard-Drive-/71031389?_dmpt=LH_DefaultDomain_0&_pcategid=158826&_pcatid=401&_refkw=320gb+internal+hard+drive&_trkparms=65%3A12%7C66%3A2%7C39%3A1%7C72%3A5848&_trksid=p3286.c0.m14)
Also the hard drives are way expensive if they are bought on tiger direct, new egg etc because of a flood in thailand. So i would suggest a used one on ebay. Only like $54 for a used one… even though that should be the cost of 1tb.

[b]Optical Drive:/b
Nothing much to say about this. I don’t know why someone would spend $50. Just get an easy $16 one.

[b]Software:/b
Just Windows 7… I personally prefer Windows over Mac for gaming. But if you are just doing business or it’s a home computer, I suggest not getting the graphics card and getting Mac software, instead of Windows.

Last but not least:
[b]Video Card:/b
Again, another excellent price for a graphics card. It can still play BF3, MW3, Skyrim and all the latest games on med-high. You WANT this card, especially for a low priced build. You can upgrade to the 6870, which is a little better. If you would rather NVIDIA, good luck finding one this low of a price that could do the same job.

So to sum it up, this build, in my opinion, is excellent for approximately $500 - $520. Total rebates = $60. Yea, that’s my build. I went a little overboard.
