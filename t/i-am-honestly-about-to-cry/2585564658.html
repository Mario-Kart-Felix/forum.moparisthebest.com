I actually had the same exact problem and heres how I fixed it:

Connect your router and type in 192.168.0.1 or 192.168.1.1 or your default gateway.
To find your default gateway go to your command prompt and type “ipconfig”. make sure you have
administrative access or it won’t work. (if your on vista)

It will ask you for a user and pass if you correctly connected your router.
some of the default username and passwords are:

U:admin
P:

U:admin
P:admin

U:
P:admin

You set your user and pass when configuring your router, it might be on your computer or somewhere in your home.

When you connect to your router, (I have D-link) click “portforward” or “applications and gaming”, or something similar to the two.
Now just open ports and click save.
Here is how I opened my ports:

Click the check
Name/application (I think thats what it is)

SERVER IP

Start port: 43594
End port: 43594

Drop down box
computer

pretty sure thats how I did it.
Then, I clicked “Save Settings”.

It worked for me that way, hopefully it works for you
