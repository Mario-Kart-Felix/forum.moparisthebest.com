sini:

But with the vast amount of possible actions it adds up. I think that’s oversight.

In the worst case scenario let’s say each player starts one action per tick (given many actions take several ticks I doubt this would ever happen). That’s only 2000 allocations per tick assuming 2k players are online, and this really is the worst case.

Looking at the updating code:

  • there’s already 6k allocations per tick allocating the SynchronizationTask objects (3 per player)
  • for each player in your local view, one SynchronizationSegment (what was I thinking when I named this stuff…) object is allocated (so let’s assume worst case 255 per player, so that’s 2000*255 objects)
  • and then above that there are the SynchronizationBlock objects themselves, though Apollo does try to re-use those where possible

even excluding the blocks, that’s >500k allocations per tick when you have 2k players. This is almost certainly the area where you’d want to optimize allocations, the actions are insignificant by comparison.
