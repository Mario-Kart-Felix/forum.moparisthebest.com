How do i make it so that my script randomly chooses to do one of two things, I.E. Left click and right click?

King_Keen July 29, 2016, 8:47pm #2

You use the case of command.

Example.

Case Random(2) of 0: ClickMouse(500, 500, true); 1: ClickMouse(500, 500, false); end;

You always need and “end;” at the end of a case of command.

King_Keen July 29, 2016, 8:47pm #4

You can use it for anything really, use your imagination.
Another example.

Case GetTextAt(x, y) of
  'man': writeln('man found.')
  'woman':writeln('woman found.')
end;

The case of command can be used instead of the ‘if then’ command, it helps clear up allot of code and makes it generally easier to read. You can use it for randomly selecting things or for selection things from taking the result of a function.

Good job :), one suggestion. A lot of people make this mistake, even advanced people:

On your Mouse Moving, you move the mosue over the item with random pixels of 3. That is good, but there is one problem: Later you have CMouse(x,y,3,3…) which will also click with a random 3, but the random 3 could be different from the first Mouse Movement.

In order to fix that, so they both end up at the same pixel, but its still random. I do this:

if(FindColorSpiralTolerance(x,y,ColOfFood,560,200,735,465,tol))then
  begin
  x:=x+random(3);
  y:=Y+random(3);
      repeat
       
        MMouse(x,y,0,0);
        if(IsUpText('Eat'))then
        Case Random(2) of
          0: CMouse(x,y,0,0,true);
          1: begin
               CMouse(x,y,0,0,false);
               ClickText('Bury',560,200,735,465);
             end;
        end;
      until(GetHp>EatHpUpTo);
    end;

Now it will still add a random 3, but it is just less detectable because it won’t move after it finds the UpText.
