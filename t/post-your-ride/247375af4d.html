Only it isn’t going to be in a new Civic I’m dropping it in a older one, about 950KG and 220HP… Mad fun, until it’s running on boost.

Well there ofcourse will be a new exhaust under it. It’s all being custome made.
The header 4-1 (for my turbo) and the screamerpiper(downpipe) are made by Wytech and it’s 3" in diameter.
From there it’s running in a straight forward custom made pipe (made by EPS), and the damper is a Tanabe Medallion

I’m still deciding how I want to fix the turbo lag, because when the engine is build, it’ll run to 10.000 RPM, and I’m going to use a rather big turbo that will take some time to spool up so the turbo lag will be quite huge.

I have two solutions:
1: A.L.S (Anti Lag System): They use this in Rally cars, it gives that nice ‘back fire’ and the bang sound, but not only is this system expensive it will cause so much wear to your engine and you have to tune the ECU perfectly.

2: Supercharger: Unlike a Turbo a Supercharger can deliver the power from idle RPM up to the full range. But the down side is, a supercharger is chain driven by the engine which will cost momentum and thus power. But this isn’t a biggy i’d say because I’ll have a turbo that will kick in around 4.000 RPM.

If you want your engine to run to 10.0000 RPM I’d tune it so it doesn’t run TDC, just a little under (unless your rich of course). It will be wrecked within 20000 miles. I’m going to need a new block for my scooter soon, after just 4000 km. When I got it I rev’d it right up past the 12k rpm a lot and there was still only like 100 km on the thing.

How many miles has this engine done? I’d advise to ride it in quite a bit before riding it on top dead centre with a supercharger. You’ll get a lot of more miles out of it![/quote]

10.000 RPM isn’t a problem at all for those engines, I had a B16B before which develired power till 9,000 RPM (out of the factory) thats a 1.6 liter engine putting out 185HP.

Let me give you the specs of the K20A engine:

As you can see the power output tops @ 8000 RPM, the redline begins at 8600.
How ever the engine is going to be completely build, meaning that the internals will be replaced with forged parts. This so that the engine is reliable under pressure.

The reason that it’ll reach atleast 10.000 rpm is because I’m going to do the following:

Camshafts: I’ll put in two (Intake and Exhaust) new camshafts that arew much more agressive and more aimed at turbo my setup.
Crankshaft: The crankshaft will be replaced with a forged, lighter and balanced crankshaft. This is so that it can safely rev up to the 10.000 RPM without engine failure.
Pistons: The pistons I’m using will create a lower compression rate (because with a 11.7 CR you will be doomed to detonation). They’re also lighter and forged so that they can cope up.
Valves: The valves will be replaced with stronger valves, and ofcourse combined with stronger springs so that they wont float at 9.000+ RPM.
The head will be portmatched and flowed.

And ofcourse, the whole setup is going to be tuned with Chrome.
I got a wideband meter to messure the A/F ratio. In the begin I’ll run the engine a bit richer to be safe (better rich than lean!).

The engine has only ran aprox 12.000 KM (it’s a engine from 2008).
The gearbox is a 6 speed gearbox with a LSD clutchpack.
And the supercharger will not apply more stress than that turbo is going to give ;)!
