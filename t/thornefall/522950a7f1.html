sinisoul August 4, 2016, 3:59pm #268

I think a lot of that boils down into my methodology. Learning the process of writing code, writing tests, pulling, reviewing, re-reviewing is a god send to keeping things on track. Doing that by yourself is difficult and I feel objectively its hard to keep track of everything you have to do by yourself.

It’s like if you have to design a soda machine, sure. You can make a soda machine. However if you’re doing it by yourself, you might just create a little machine that dispenses cups, fills the cup with water, adds flavor. Your customers are the people who challenge what you release to them, and if it fits their needs they won’t complain. You may have used gum to keep the pieces together but they don’t care, as long as it works. However, everyone else in the business knows gum is a ridiculous method to keep the pieces together and everyone uses glue. However, you’d have to research into using glue and maybe you’d have to redesign your machine all together. That’s often what happened. In a team environment say, one guy likes using gum, and another guy used glue before so he makes a good case about using glue instead of gum. Your team uses glue, and your machine is less prone to falling apart.

I tried to use the community as an outlet for this purpose a lot of the times but most of the time I’d find that I was the expert on the things I was asking about, and not an expert of the subject but rather the local expert that people would go to ask questions about. That’s the point in time you need to molt.

You don’t know what projects I’ve ran outside of RSPS. I managed the programming side of a robotics team at one point in time, efficiently as possible? No. However we were always the team that had our work done on time. We always had stretch goals finished. Does it matter in the grand scheme of things? I guess not. But just because I guess people are so interested in what I’m doing I guess I could bring in that note.

Putting the responsibility of designing a game on a single person is a huge undertaking especially considering I feel as if I did do a good job and what I did put out was good quality. There were HUGE gaps in my understanding and I never felt like hacking in features was the right way to go about things. My frustration in the Mopar project stemmed from my inability to know how to properly set up the design around what Jagex put forward. I have joint creative control now around a lot of these topics in Thornefall and it feels like coming out of a hyperbolic chamber. The environment RSPS puts you in is more challenging than people would expect because not only do you have to think like a game developer you have to think similarly to how Jagex designed their game. Trying to design your own systems to work with their game client is too time consuming.

A lot of my time committed to the community was driven to try and push the community further, the passion died a year ago when RS programming outgrew me and the experience were more or less a time crunch because people would constantly berate me like you have for as long as I can remember. I honestly just wanted to be remembered in a positive light. Now I realize what was done here doesn’t mean absolutely fuck all considering I used Jagex’s game for 95% of what I called my own work and that’s not a legacy I want associated with me. It was a stepping stone.

These comments are getting annoying. Putting someone into a mold and saying that they can’t do something because you have some inclination to say you understand me as a person, is annoying. I don’t know much about you Silab but beyond what you did years ago, what platform do you have to criticize people on?

I feel like I have to explain my story about this a lot. And you can have whatever opinion. Just please take away from my post that it’s harder than it looks on the surface. I’m trying really hard to improve as a developer, and as a person who has trouble staying not frustrated with some of the more intimidating challenges. But people have issues with selectively reading, and not really empathizing with these points. I wasn’t angry writing this post I think at this point I’m just frustrated. So please don’t think my voice in this piece meant that. I respect you a lot, I think you’re right in some ways, and I am going to improve moving forward. Thanks.
