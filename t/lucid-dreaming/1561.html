Hey, if you don't know what lucid dreaming is, it's the ability to control your dreams.

I'm sure most of you have experienced this at least once, I know I have many times however in the morning I wonder why I didn't do things I always wanted to do, so obviously I'm not in total control.

Believe it or not, it is possible to train yourself for lucid dreaming, I have been training for 2 years and I have learnt a lot. Whether you think it or not, you have many many dreams every night. If you think you don't, your wrong, you just simply cannot remember them. Nowadays I can remember at least 3-5 dreams every night. Someday I hope to have lucid dreams at my will, because I could then accomplish anything. Not only can you control your dreams, but you can control how long you stay in them as well. Obviously not forever, but some are able to spend several hours in a dream, doing as they wish.

Obviously some are going to choose not to believe this, but all I can say is that I have had the experience several times and it is amazing.

So... You can search google for information on training, but before doing so you need to do a lot of stuff. Make sure you get a good nights sleep, every night. 8 hours is a good amount, 10 is even better(weekends are usually good for this). In the morning, write down your dreams if you can remember any. Do this EVERY morning. You need to find a pattern in your dreams, or a re-occuring event. It really helps a lot to do this. For me, swimming pools and long dark hallways seem to happen almost everynight. And, without the ability to remember your dreams you cannot remember if you have had a lucid dream. If you wake u in the middle of the night, jot down part of your dream and see if you can recall the whole thing in the morning. Jot down Who, where, what, and when. Eventaully you'll find a common element in all your dreams, which I believe are called dream signs. Now if you want, go search google for some tips and guides on lucid dreaming, but you HAVE TO master remembering your dreams before doing so.

So, disucss what you feel on lucid dreaming. I have a feeling many people here will flame me or something, but at least give it a try. Try doing this for like 2 weeks, and come back if you notice any trends. Someday I hope to be able to control my dreams, Im not far from it either.

Their are several methods to fly in lucid dreams.

Not everyone can jump up and fly, but it's important to remember that in a dream you can do anything you want, there are no setbacks as long as you practice.

The next time you want to fly, imagine that in the next room of your house there will be a magic wand that will give you the ability to fly. When you go in there, quite creeply it will be there, and you should use it. Don't forget to keep reminding yourself it's a dream, and NEVER close your eyes in a dream, it's an instant wakeup. If you want something to change around you, spin around in circles very fast, or picture going into another room. Your mind will do most of the work, you'd be suprised how quickly it can generate a detailed item.

Btw, the WILD technique worked for me last night, I woke up very excited but I somehow forgot it all by the time I ate breakfast. I should have written it down to help me remember It was really hard though, it's not easy having a single thought in your head while you fall asleep.

Btw, if you have trouble remembering a dream for your dream journal, set an alarm clock for like 5:30 am, and it'll most likely interupt one of the longest REM periods(thus longer dreams) and you'll probably remember it. Write it down and go back to sleep, and re-read it and you'll remember most of what happened. After each REm period, your brain atempts to erase your last dream memory, and when awake it fades quick.
