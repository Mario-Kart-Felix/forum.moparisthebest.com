I thought I would share my experiences on Lucid Dreaming.

I’ve tried to achieve lucidity multiple times. I figured it would help with my depression, letting me do all the things I never could, etc. It hasn’t been until this past month that I’ve been able to truly maintain control of my dreams.

My house was unfurnished; nobody was home. I walk out my front door, and neither God nor man could have fathomed the glory of the land. I only wish I could describe it. It appeared to be night, but there was still light everywhere. The sky was littered with stars, and planets that seemed about as close as the size of a CD at arms length. I walked for a bit. I knew I could do whatever I wanted, like flying, but I felt compelled to walk. I travel and notice a house on a grassy knoll just off the distance. I head towards it, eager to know what it was. As I got closer, I immediately regretted approaching it. I felt darkness. I knew it was a bad idea, but I went in. It seemed like a typical creepy house. Floors creaked, broken windows, and I hated it. I turned around to leave, but I was suddenly rigid. I wanted more than ever to leave, but I couldn’t. Then, the sickest, most wretched laugh came from behind me, and I was awoken. I haven’t tried lucid dreaming since. From what I’ve looked up, usually these kinds of occurrences are something that’s troubling someone in the subconscious, but I don’t ever want to go back.

Have any of you experienced lucidity, and if so, had better experiences than I?

rip4d August 3, 2016, 2:36am #20

Inducing sleep paralysis can help.

Start out laying in your bed. Now breath like you would while sleeping and do not move. At all. You’ll begin feeling a heaviness/numb feeling in your legs and feet, and it will slowly travel up to your torso. At this point, it will feel difficult to breath, and you may begin panicing. Don’t get worried, just continue breathing, despite the restricting feeling. You ARE getting enough air since this is how you breath in your sleep. Eventually, you will become fully paralyzed, and you may hear vibrations. Vibrations are loud sounds that usually scare people, sounding like storms, trains, etc. After this though, you should be able to lucid dream.
