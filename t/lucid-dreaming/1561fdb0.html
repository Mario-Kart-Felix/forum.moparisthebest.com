[quote=“daiki, post:9, topic:1561”]A few summers back I also read about Lucid dreaming. Quite interesting and fun :).

When I read about those I realized I’ve had many Dream Initiated Lucid Dreams (DILD, you find out you’re dreaming in the middle of a dream, and are able to “wake up” or take control of it).

I tried WILDing (Wake Initiated Lucid Dream) many times but never made it :P. Just can’t “fall asleep knowing the next thing you see is a dream”, too hard. But they say some people can do this quite easily.

Anyway, I used to put quite much thought to Lucid Dreaming back then, and I managed to see a Lucid once or twice in one night a few times (3-8?) in a month :). I’ve seen a few this year, but not so many. It really helps when you WANT to have one. Think that you are going to experience one. Keep it in mind during the day and when going to sleep. Try to spot something really weird and you might know you’re dreaming.

I got most of my Lucid Dreams by waking up early and going back to sleep (trying to do a WILD, but fell asleep and then realized it when dreaming, so it was a DILD). When dreaming these it’s too easy to wake up or to forget that you are Lucid Dreaming, so it can return to a normal dream or you might wake up.

So what’s so great about Lucid Dreams? It’s a great experience, your senses usually work so you will be able to feel, hear, see, smell and taste.
You don’t have to know how something tastes, you just taste it and be amazed. Eating something new can be fun :P.
Also the basic one, everyone wants to try it: Flying :). This one usually made me wake up though.
Also killing and dying is fun, really. I like it. It’s just so weird
Sex also works, and feels really nice (until you wake up? :rolleyes:)
