Wet_Dreamss August 3, 2016, 2:36am #22

If you ever want to lucid dream follow this:

Lay down and go to sleep. this works best if you are extremely tired. lay on your back with your arms at your side and eyes closed. STAY PERFECTLY STILL. You must stay awake. Your brain will send signals to your body to see if you are still awake. These signals include: getting an itch, changing your body position, wanting to blink or move your eyeballs(remember your eyes should be closed). YOU MUST IGNORE ALL THESE IMPULSES. After about 20-30 minutes you will feel a weight on your chest; you may even hear weird noises. You are now in a sleep paralysis, if you open your eyes, you will begin to hallucinate(dream with your eyes open) and you will not be able to move your body. Your body is now completely asleep. Now that you are aware that you are dreaming, you can shut your eyes and dream instantly.

You’re welcome.

Any exercises to start lucid dreaming?

Reality checks are a big one. Perform those regularly even if you’re certain you’re awake. Look at your hands, try holding your breath. If you don’t do it regularly when you’re awake, you’re less likely to do it when you’re dreaming. Ask yourself, “Are you dreaming?”

TheHolyGrail August 3, 2016, 2:37am #38

I’ve reached SP but haven’t gotten to LD yet (will be attempting again tonight).

Basically just lay in bed and concentrate on your breathing, take deep breaths. When you suck in air, tense your feet, then when you breathe out, untense your feet. When you breathe in this time, tense your lower legs, then when you breathe out, untense your lower legs. Now your upper legs, your arms, etc. Keep doing this until you reach your head. After that just relax completely and don’t move. Start counting your breaths, so that way you stay focused whilst falling asleep. You will eventually feel your whole body turn numb, you won’t be able to move, and you will start seeing images/hearing noises. Your now in SP.

Idk how to get into LD from there yet.
