Writing assignment

Diary of private mason, England (1893-1915)

June 19th, 1915
Tomorrow is going to be my first day in the trenches, yesterday our team, Delta
Squad 8, had a special training from sergeant Patrick Conneil. He appears to
have a lot of experience with the modern warfare in trenches, our team learned a
lot about what we to do in certain situations. For example, what we have to do
when a gas grenade is thrown near us. Anyways we are really excited to prove
ourself to our country. My team seems to consist of a lot of nice guys and we are
really getting close, now I am going to catch some sleep.

June 20th, 1915
I am at the hospital, the war is absolutely horrible! Today was our first day in the
trenches. I have never been so scared in my entire life, 2 People out of our team
got killed by some German machine gunners and my leg is almost blown of by
some damn motar. Everyone is so scared and we all want to go back home, this
was not as they had led us to believe it was. I am so afraid that I will never see
my family back.

June 21th, 1915
Ofcourse I was already thinking about how it should feel to kill an human being.
Today was the first day I actually shot someone, suprisingly it didn’t do that much to me.
Because of this war I don’t seem to see humans like humans anymore.
There aren’t any norms or values out here. I think that all of my ‘normal’ human
emotions are gone, all I feel is anger, everyone feels so much anger. Anger
towards the government and everything and everyone. The only light in this cloud
of darkness is Jacob, he has become my brother in arms and I surely would catch a bullet for him.

June 22th, 1915
What are we doing here? Today our team, although what is left of our team,
realised that this war is so pointless. Everyone is just killing each other but for
what? Some soldier are so desperate for the war to be over that they just shoot
themself in a random body part so they didn’t have to participate in the fight.
Everything is such a mess, our medical condition just sucks and we are running
out of food and all sort of medical supplies. I have no idea how or when this war is
going to end but I know how I am going to end.

June 23th, 1915
Jacob is death. Now I am absolutely empty, this war is changing me. I don’t feel
any regrets when killing someone nor I feel any sadness for the death of jacob.
All I can think of is surviving. It seems like this war turned off my emotions and
turned on my instinct. I feel so tired but still so alert.

June 24th, 1915
Today we had our first clash with gas. It was really horrible, I was just on time with
my mask but 2 other people weren’t that lucky. We had to put them in a car and
they were sent to the hospital. After that we walked for a couple of houres, tired
and broken but still alert for any sounds. We lost like the half of our team, still
counting. Every 5 minutes I think about killing myself but I just can’t seem to do it.

June 25th, 1915
We are now reinforcing with the french troops but it’s a chaos over here. All I can
here is people shouting and shooting, what is wrong with the world…

So this is my writing assignment, we had to make a diary of a soldier in the trenches.
Could anyone tell me if the grammar/story is okay?
and if not how I should improve it

Thanks

May 23th, 1915
A hard day. We met the Italian regiment today and they were pretty fun guys. One guy, Marconi, showed me how to make pizza with the carburetter of our supply truck… and it was pretty damn good. Never again will I judge a race of people on their skin colour.

May 24th, 1915
Steamy day with the lads again. Mostly training, but we also took a look down at Camp Bastille and shot at some immigrants. Mother always said to me, one immigrant is just another immigrant. Coming here to take our jobs. And in hindsight, she was correct. But the moment I pulled the trigger on that brown man was the day I sold my soul to the US Army. Never again will I look at a brown person in the same way. The look on his face was unbearable and I will never forget the image in my head. However I must now finish the letter as Jeff is calling me for some delicious rabbit stew.
