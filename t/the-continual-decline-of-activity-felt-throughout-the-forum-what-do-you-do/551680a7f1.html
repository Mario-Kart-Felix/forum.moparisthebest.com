The open source, low cost, and conservative ideals that strangle any potential are responsible for the death of good ideas in this community. As far as Minecraft is concerned, there should’ve been real investment years ago to take us away from external dependency on plugin systems like bukkit. I tried to push the idea of developing our own or adapting the bukkit system so we can become self sufficient long before it went south, it wasn’t to be, and now we have a situation where the bukkit dependent server had to close. If we look at bukkit now, they’ve been a year or so behind on releasing updates and their latest version has even been DMCA’d. If we were at all serious about having Minecraft as an integral part of the community then serious decisions must accompany it, in my opinion it’s too late for Minecraft now, but it’s an important note in any case.

The community has a tendency to argue about insignificant details while ignoring the real game ender further down the track. A lot of people here are effectively the cattle arguing about the weather while being transported to the slaughter house. If you want to “fix” the community, then either do something to contribute and make the small difference or come to the realisation that new idea backed by new approaches and money need to be thought up.
