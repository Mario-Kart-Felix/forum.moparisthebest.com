I don’t know why you guys are even bothering. This is a dying community, one way or another. The core members are on IRC, so there’s no real loss there. The forums though, will continue to decline.

Here’s what you have to ask yourself:

[ul][li]What do we offer? We were once the hub of development. This was the center of bot and private server development. None of the other communities had our grunt. No longer the case. When the private server communities branched out, we lost out. There’s not much talent there but there’s something more solid offered. Around the time MITB started, there were a few other big players with a slice of the pie. Sythe for example, was the hub of the black market. They’re still going strong. We also had Villavu which had their little slice of the pie due to their focus on SCAR (or whatever it’s called these days.). Then you had a whole bunch of communities that sat on the outskirts. Impsoft had bot software. Rsresources had a slice because it provided a more generalized place to hangout. Now the scene has been split up into hundreds of tiny communities. [/li]
[li]Are there even people to recruit? I don’t think so. Runescape as a game seems to be declining. I think we’re experiencing private server fatigue as well, where people no longer care about it. That’s understandable though. I’ve never really seen the appeal in it.[/li]
[li]Do we know our audience? No way. We don’t even know what we are. Are we a private server forum? Well, no. Other communities do it better and it’s that creepy cousin that we keep on our boards. Do we produce cheating applications? No. Aryan was how long ago? What about black market? Nope. Alright, so we don’t produce anything. But we are a general forum, right? It’s not cutting it. We have no identity. We don’t provide anything.[/li][/ul]

Listen, we were going to see a decline in the member base either way. The game is declining. Members didn’t want a board on software development. They wanted something they could easily download and use for free. We don’t have that anymore. It’s all good that we have a programming focus and all of that. It’s what I prefer this board having. However, most people can’t develop or will never try to. So what we provide just isn’t being bought.

Here’s the realistic solutions that would help:

[ol][li]Affiliation: Back when Sythe and Kaits were the two big players, there used to be a little link to Kaits at the bottom of the boards over on Sythe. This brought in a lot of traffic and a lot of the members it brought in were decent. They were used to being a member of a large community so it wasn’t too much of a stretch for them to integrate into ours. If we can secure some deals with some of the communities that now get majority of the traffic, I doubt we would lose anything. This includes places like Villavu, Sythe and RS. We’re not longer ‘too good’ for these places. We have been overshadowed.[/li]
[li]We change the focus: We keep the Runescape private server section but start working on other popular games. WoW private servers bring in a lot of traffic. Really, anything that has more players than RS will be an increase in member base. These days, that’s not too hard. What about providing League of Legends ‘hacks’? It can be anything or everything. We need to stop being seen as that Runescape board though. Game dev? That seems to be pretty active.[/li]
[li]We focus on consumables: People are too lazy to develop. We could do a month where we all try to generate as much content as possible. We could focus on youtube videos, game dev guides, cheats, private server tutorials and programming tutorials. We need something that people can get for free. [/li]
[li]Ranks: Internet points matter. Whether they are upvotes, facebook likes, instagram likes or even reputation. We want to feel as though we matter. Sythe used to have a pick-axe system where they would advance through copper to rune. We need something.[/li]
[li]Forum theme: We need alternatives. This is not the golden change that will bring in 99999 more users, but it will help to keep them. Just provide them as non-default.[/li]
[li]Chat: Make a chatbox at the top of the boards that just connects up with IRC. It’ll give the illusion of more activity and it will prevent people from avoiding IRC.[/li]
[li]Become exclusive: Another alternative is to embrace the change. We could make the forum invite only. Make it harder to get in and start focusing on decent content. People would be eager to join just because they’re not able to.[/ol]

The problem isn’t that there members aren’t really friendly. The problem isn’t that staff are sometimes heavy handed. It has always been that way and every community has that. We’re dying. We will never be what we were. All we can do is maintain our member base, transform to something else or replace our audience.

I’ve made countless threads over the past 5 years warning everyone about all of this. I told you that it was coming and you all laughed it off. Now we don’t have the memberbase to play with and you’re suddenly in a panic. You all got too comfortable and now we lose out because of it. Hell, I don’t even care anymore. I’ll probably scrape all the important threads from here and keep them archived and just leave it at that. My community is the IRC.
