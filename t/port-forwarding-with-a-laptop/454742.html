doom_j August 3, 2016, 11:32pm #3

portforwarding is for the most part the easiest thing you can do these days, most routers have an easy to us UI that should bring you to the portforwarding / porttriggering sections. All you have to do is get your ip, given to you by the router for the most part it will start with 192.168.1.(###), the last number is the most important number to know since that is the only real difference between you and another device within your network.

You can get your ip within the command line on windows by using the command “ipconfig”. It will print out in the terminal all the devices you have connected, the one you need is your default ip / ipv4 or the ip that is right above the gateway ip, not the subnet mask!. Again the most likely ip will be 192.168.1.###.

You will now use this with the port forwarding form you will have to fill out in your routers web based configuration tool.

Once you’re down you will now need to add both ports (43594, 5555) to your firewalls exceptions and allow incoming and outgoing packets, if you don’t your firewall will block it, you will need to do the same if your anti-virus includes a firewall in it.
