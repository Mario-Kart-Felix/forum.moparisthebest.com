BobDole81 July 30, 2016, 4:37am #3

Heh, I like that subtle hint in the title

Well, my first issue with this problem is the 300[sup]o[/sup] - if his heading is 300[sup]o[/sup] (-60[sup]o[/sup]), then shouldn’t the polar coordinate be (90, 300[sup]o[/sup])? We’ll see how that goes though.

Anyway, you don’t absolutely need to convert to cartesian coordinates…what you really need to do is add the plane’s vector with the wind vector to get a resultant, from which you can get the time reqruired to travel. So I’ll work out the x and y components for both, and you can take your pick.

(If you don’t remember, x component is rcos(theta) and y component is rsin(theta))

v[sub]150x[/sub] = -77.9 m/s
v[sub]150y[/sub] = 75 m/s

v[sub]300x[/sub] = 75 m/s
v[sub]300y[/sub] = -77.9 m/s

v[sub]windx[/sub] = -20 m/s
v[sub]windy[/sub] = 0 m/s

So

v[sub]150[/sub] = 123 m/s at about 142[sup]o[/sup]
v[sub]300[/sub] = 95 m/s at about 305[sup]o[/sup]

Hope those are right…and so, you have the resultant velocity vectors for both angles in question…and you can easily work out the time it takes to travel 250 km from those
