Well I disagree with the essay entirely I actually attend a public cyber charter school and don’t feel any such concerns. I do spend a fair bit on the computer each day, but I must say that I believe I’d spend just as much time even if I didn’t attend. But I don’t see how staring at the computer kills brain cells to be honest…

Furthermore, there are many programs offered, not all of which depend on being at the computer all day. For example, the Calvert curriculum is done entirely offline with the exception of tutoring, testing, etc. Granted, there are virtual classes where one attends classes in an online setting, but that takes place for limited periods of time. In addition, if one ever needed help, there are always tutoring sessions available, and you are free to email or call teachers for individual subjects. In regards to socialization, cyber schools in general should host social events for students…if not, it’s up to the families themselves to incorporate it. In my case, I take advantage of the early college program, so I actually go into a local college (3 times a week at the moment)…I wouldn’t say I miss out on such social interaction.

Finally, cyber schools typically provide each student with their own computer and offer reimbursement for internet…as long as its a public school, you have to really as you’re paying school taxes…Overall, I see cyber schools as offering more flexibility and opportunities, not problems.

But that’s just my opinion - I’m not asking you to change your essay Your essay sounds good overall, but I think you should remove statements such as “the next reason” and “another argument” just so that it flows more nicely. But I think the content is good.
