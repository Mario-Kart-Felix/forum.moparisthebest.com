How about instead of everyone posting ridiculously specific ‘tuts’ on sig-making, where no creativity can be implemented, the experienced sig makers post general guidelines on what characterizes a good sig as opposed to a poorly made sig.

There really is little benefit for the learners to look at graphics tuts… usually it is some blend of brushes and filters that achieve an effect that is pleasing to the eye. And because no one wants to copy, they cannot ever use those specific steps, so what the reader really gains is a greater aesthetic sense and a better feel of what an outstanding sig entails. Why not skip all the bullshit and post something pragmatic, such as some concrete tips on what makes a sig good?

I’m sure if one of these tutorials is truly extraordinary, FastBawlz will not object to stickying it, though I can’t volunteer it for him.

So I challenge you, expert signature designers, write up a comprehensive, clear guide on how to make a sig.
