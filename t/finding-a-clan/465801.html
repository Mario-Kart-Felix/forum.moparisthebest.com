Loveandpower August 4, 2016, 12:56am #8

the runescape forums has an entire collection of “clans” I personally don’t like those though. It really depends on what your expecting to do. I have linked you below some “rs community” sites to their respectable clan sites. i will then link you to a couple of “suggested” clans.

Forum.Tip.It

Recruitment

Recruitment: Clan recruitment for warring and PKing clans.

http://forums.zybez.net/forum/16-recruiting/

http://forum.global-rs.com/viewforum.php?f=9&sid=ef98c200083b6900db352e6b78ac89bc

Sal's RuneScape Forum

Clan Central

Here you can advertise your clan, recruit new members or post a request to join one.

these are the main places where you will find a more organized clan and a clan that will have their own forums/site.

Finding a clan for the first time can be quite the hard but you can easily resolve this by browsing the different clan sites to get a gist of what they do a lot of. You can also join their corresponding irc channel if they have one and talk with the members in there.

“warring clans” are those who are specific (most of the time but do sometimes host other events ) to clan wars, where you do real warring. (just join a clan to learn about it almost all warring clans have guides for new war members. )

“community clans” are those who generally do a little bit of everything. To be truthful, unless you join a LARGE clan for community it will be crap.

“pvp clans” are those who focus solely on pvp and pking.

“pvm clans” are those who focus solely on player vs monster (like bandos etc ) and sometimes on the rare occasion a skilling event.

The main well respected clan out there that I remember are the gladiators or the legendz.

I myself do RSB Style warring which isn’t really well known, Since the community has died off as lately over the past year every single update slightly affects warring negatively:

Flasks (40 minute rounds, keep in mind RSB style warring is in max gear + Divine/Ely)
Gano Armor
Polypore
Storm of Armadyl
Robes of Subjection
And many other small things.

All I can suggest for you is to take the time to try out everything, you may like one thing on Runescape like PvM but you will never find what you love until you try it all. I myself learnt this the hard way by sticking with a community clan for 5 years on Runescape, just to find out how much I enjoyed RSB Warring.
