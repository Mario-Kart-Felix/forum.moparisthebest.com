[quote=“runeite51, post:20, topic:534054”]It really depends on what you constitute as random. I think we consider spontaneous as something we can’t explain. Example: You might think someone is random. Take that person to a psychologist, and they’ll explain that person’s random actions. If you roll a die, you’d say there is a random chance of it being from 1 to 6. Would you still consider it random if you had a complete understanding of the universe and physics, and knew if you factored everything around you which side it would land on?

So in reality, nothing is actually spontaneous. Even computer functions meant to generate a random number aren’t completely random. With that said, if you had an absolute understanding of the universe, then you should be able to predict the outcome of everything.

But just because everything can be predicted, doesn’t mean we don’t have free will, because we ourselves can’t predict what will happen. So yes, we do have a choice, but at the same time, it’s possible those choices can be/have been predicted.[/quote]
