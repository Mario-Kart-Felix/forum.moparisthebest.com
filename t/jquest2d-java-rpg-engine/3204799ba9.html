zstars:

Lots of monitors actually work at a 60 Hz maximum though, meaning that even if the eye could perceive a difference, the game running faster wouldn’t really make a difference (unless the frames were distributed unevenly in time, and even then the limit would soon be reached).

It does make a difference. Even if the faster rendering itself won’t bring that many advantages, if you update at a faster rate your movements will be smoother. But I’d say for a 2D game it doesn’t really make much of a difference. When I played Counter-Strike I could very well see the difference between playing it at 72fps (the default) and 99.9 (the maximum). It was much smoother and easier to play.
