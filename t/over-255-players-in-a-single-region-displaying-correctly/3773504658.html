Java`, what you haven’t seemed to take into consideration is when player A is visible to player B but player B is not visible to player A. This happens when player A is surrounded by players on BOTH sides, while player B has little to no players on one side, but does have towards the side player A is at. In the following picture, each red dot indicates player A and player B respectively and the black dots (or lines :P) indicate surrounding players:

Notice how player B’s visible radius is much larger than player A’s since player B has no players to its right.

This is a huge problem in, say, the wilderness, where player B is able to attack player A from afar with magic or range and player A will not even be able to see player B. This is where it all falls back to prioritization, which is how the actual Runescape server handles it. Needless to say, creating a system that fully emulates Runescape’s is not the easiest of tasks.
