I’m sorry, but after reading over the posts made by Java`, I think that a few major corrections are in order here.

First off, as many other people have said, player updating and Npc updating, while similar, are NOT the same thing. There are differences, and someone could easily have support for insane amounts of Npcs while not supporting a full 2k players. You cannot claim that you have 2000 oranges and them show us tangerines. They are similar, but they aren’t the same.

The problem with doing Npcs instead of players is that, as Primadude said, the player updates their Npc list for all the Npcs. If you have a bunch of players, for example 255, each of those 255 players must be updated for the other 255 that they can see. This means that player updating processing power increases exponentially, while the Npc updating is not. You also fail to take into account the processing power required by the networking to read and write packets to the players, so this topic is very misleading.

The other problem is that you are not first to ‘benchmark’ this. Frugooscape Ultimate was the first server I know of that had thousands of players on a server at a time, and he did not release any information about this. Graham then posted a topic with 2,000 of my AGF bots connecting to Hyperion, showing the memory and CPU usage of the server. This is probably the first thing that you would consider a “benchmark”, although you didn’t post any CPU/memory usage for the server while you were doing this.
