Flame3 August 4, 2016, 10:13am #1

In response to a recent suggestion concerning the use of IRC for assistance in programming, I’ve created a new channel that is dedicated solely to helping others that join and request assistance.
This channel is called #help, and the main focus for the channel is to provide serious assistance to those in search of help, without the trolling, intimidation or jokes that would follow. Ranks will be assigned to those who are helpful within the IRC and the forums, and those who come to the channel to fool around will be kicked and/or banned. If you’re willing to take part in the experiment and join the channel, then go for it. If there are some things that you don’t want to see in this, or if you don’t want it at all, please say so and explain why. If you have any questions, ask. This way, we can set up the channel in the best way possible infrastructure-wise, and work out the kinks in the idea before the channel becomes more popular.

Some ideas to help the channel, if anyone is willing to create them:

[ul][li]An automated IRC bot that answers generic help questions by providing links and/or answers. Examples can be !question portforward, and the bot would reply with a portforward guide link, while an input such as !question 100errors can answer with “you’ve missed an opening or closing bracket somewhere in your code.”[/li]
