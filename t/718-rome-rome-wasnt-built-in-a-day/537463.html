Rome is a 667 loading 718 that is build on the idea to be different, but to the extent of maintaining real RuneScape aspects. [B]The main idea of the server is to have playability, interactivity, and tons of content.[/B] This server wants to keep aspects of the Roman Empire times but still maintaining RuneScape, making this a blend of medieval and emperor times. The logic of this server is to make YOU a ‘Roman’ not some ‘Player’. We found this a more formal way of talk and found this to be quite descriptive of YOU. Our interactivity will be to produce content that will come from the unexpected, and something that is fun and doesn’t always have to follow the pattern of “get on a server, get 99s, and PK!” We, the Roman team, find this to be quite repetitive, and wish to annihilate that from our server. All-in-all, this server is circulated by YOU and not by the concept of “the World is just turning around”

[B][U]In 11 words: The collaboration of Rome while keeping the main idea of RuneScape.[/U][/B]

[B][COLOR=ORANGE]Server Thought Process[/COLOR][/B]
The server is built to replace the name of “Player” to “Roman”. Staff are "Emperor"s. Donators? Get rid of that name. They are 'Lord’s (or 'Lady’s if they are a girl character). Everyone considers Taverley, our home, to be the main epicenter of Rome, as it has the Central Trading Hub, Skilling Tutors, the Town Crier (one of few), the Squire (one of two), and the Financial Adviser (one of few). It also provides nearly all things that a skiller may need for their journey in Rome. The thought for having it all-in-one, with a few cuts our (such as how rocktails and such are not at Taverley) is to maintain the socialization perspective of Rome.

[B][COLOR=BROWN]Roman Assault - A barbarian to Rome jungle.[/COLOR][/B]

There is a ship at the start, a cutscene coming, showing Roman Guards getting off the ship. Afterwards, you get out. When you get out, you see a battle going on. Barbarians fighting Roman Guards. You must kill as many Barbarians as you can. Once they are all killed, a boss will come. His level will be the exact average of all the players in the game (So if we have a level 69, a level 52, and a level 47, the boss would be the calculated average of their levels (thus, the boss is level 56)). Once the boss is killed, all participants get a reward, just like Pest Control. The person with the most kills gets more money, and vice-versa.

[B][COLOR=ORANGE]Interactivity[/COLOR][/B]
Our prime concern is interactivity. The reason being that we believe that if the server is not interactive, then it doesn’t have the playable feel. Interactivity, to us, is the server having something going on, everywhere. I swear to you, when I was walking down from Taverley to Edgeville, I found things that I forgot I even programmed in pop out of nowhere. That’s interactivity to me. That’s true interactivity.

[B][COLOR=BROWN]We Care About the Little Things[/COLOR][/B]
We believe that the little things count. If you were to walk down as an adventurer in Roman times, no food with you, and suddenly you got attacked and are hungry. What’s the first reaction you’d do? Get food! There is a cabbage field right near you. You run to eat the cabbage. But what do you find? You click it and the boring message, “Nothing interesting happens,” comes up. On Rome, we strive to count the little things in, meaning when you are in dire need, and have no food around, you can click on some sort of field and not worry about that terrifying message, “Nothing interesting happens.”
