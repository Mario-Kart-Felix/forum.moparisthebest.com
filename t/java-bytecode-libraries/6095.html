http://www-128.ibm.com/developerworks/ibm/library/it-haggar_bytecode/

Bytecode libraries basically allow you to manipulate the raw bytecode data of a compiled class. In regards to rs cheating, bytecode manipulation is and has been mainly used by deobfuscators to provide compile-able source code. It’s possible, though, to build a bot through bytecode, but normally method overloading is used instead.

As for learning, BCEL is always good to start with, and they have a decent guide on their site: http://jakarta.apache.org/bcel/index.html

Krichevskoy July 29, 2016, 8:50pm #11

ASM is basically designed for generating classes…it’s small and fast, very good at what it does. Not so good at editing previously existing stuff.
BCEL is the most powerful for editing classes, but it’s more complicated than javassist.
Javassist is the simplest to understand I think, but it can’t do as much as BCEL.
SERP is easy but slow, and iirc it’s only got a visitor mode. BCEL is more flexible there.
