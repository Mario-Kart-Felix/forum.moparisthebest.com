You could make an entire bot from graphics library trampolines. Minus the input of course. Identifying models from vertex is actually common practice amongst hackers; on many many games… Aimbots in CS for example… Or even bots in WoW. Think about it, what changes the least in a released game? The original content… How often do you replace the knight piece of a chess board? Never. Aesthetic updates fall to the wayside of content, engine performance, and engine extension. Use that to your advantage.

There’s enough graphics drivers out there, that you can essentially go unbanned while blatantly hijacking calls by appending a stub to the driver. The majority of “detection” that is applied to these methods is minimal, they usually just query the driver for the information… Which you hijack the query with your stub and just check the register, did the query come from the application? Yes? Return setting from array_of_game_settings else call actual driver routine.

It’s just another approach to the same end, like using packet capture drivers such as winpcap to hijack socket’s data, such as popular hacks like redvex’s man-in-the-middle on diablo 2, or roundwurm for wurmonline.
