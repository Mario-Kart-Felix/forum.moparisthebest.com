Hardly anybody is capable of remembering anything that happened from the age of 0 to 4. What is left of those years is impressions, and things such as speech.

R.I.P_RSC August 3, 2016, 11:53pm #9

I guess the biggest problem for you at the moment is your self esteem. If you feel like your memory doesn’t work you might get stressed, and under-perform at exams even if you could do just fine if you just felt better about yourself. Just like atychiphobia can result in a person under-performing, even though there’s nothing wrong with people who are afraid of failing. Gaining confidence can do wonders.

Are there any other major events you don’t remember? I don’t think it’s a problem your friends can recall mucking around and you can’t, perhaps you didn’t enjoy it as much as they did and it didn’t leave that big an impression.

Don’t think that you’ll fail before you’ve even started. Just prepare yourself very good for your exams. Start as soon as you know the chapters that are required for the exam. Read the chapter the day before class, take notes during class and perhaps reread the chapter and perhaps take notes again or add to the notes you made earlier. Keep reviewing the notes, and now and then open the book to read a chapter or two. Being sure about what you are doing might mean that you have less time for other activities, but it pays in the end. Taking notes is pretty much essential if you want to learn properly. For most courses, I only opened the book the day before the exam. I learnt most just paying attention during class and taking notes. Don’t give up, try talking about it with your parents anyways and just do your best.

I’m no expert, so don’t take anything I say as fact, it’s just my view!
