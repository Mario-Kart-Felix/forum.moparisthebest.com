Tom August 4, 2016, 8:16am #4

It depends what you’re trying to achieve with this interview, if you take Krackkid for example, he isn’t a veteran contributor with extensive knowledge of the scene but rather a popular forum guy, who most people like. It’d be like interviewing a comedian, you don’t interview them because of their extensive knowledge in engineering but because of their persona, it’s a more relaxed experience with general questions, and entertaining answers. Whereas with somebody like Blakeman for example, you’d be more inclined to ask them about programming and RSPS, so it’s less about persona and more about core knowledge and expertise in the field.

The choice you have to make is whether you want to entertain your audience, or educate your audience. Obviously a mixture of both would potentially be possible, but somebody with the professionalism of Blakeman doesn’t really have the same effect krackkid does on the community. I’m biased in my selection, simply because I would knock people who would use this opportunity give extreme and unfair answers, which would cause controversy. My concern about the interviews has always been that people looking to cause trouble can discredit the community, so I do hope there is a certain level of approval stopping people from answering with “This community is shit, the staff are horrible, all join my brothers community at http://badsite.com and get mod” after all this is homepage content.
