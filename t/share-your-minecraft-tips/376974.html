I guess I’ll start:
I’f your digging for diamond and stumble across lava don’t be afraid and put it out with water because its a good light resource and will save you from using your torches.

When you find lava don’t dig through it, find out where the currant is going and follow it mining about 5 or 6 blocks wide because it will usually lead you to a cave or dungeon if you wish

Always take some wooden planks with you when mining so you can build a crafting bench when in need of another pick, you shouldn’t need to take to much coal because you’ll eventually find some on the way down.

Found some red stone but don’t see a reason to mine it? Well if you decide to go cave exploring and don’t want to get lost, red stone dust can be placed on the ground as a red marker to find your way back out

If you keep accidentally pressing the Q button and dropping your items go to the menu by pressing ESC and click options/controls and you can adjust your keyboard controls their.

Fell down a hole while mining onto a zombie and can’t run away? While mining you should not dispose of all your cobble stone, infact keep a whole stack on the side of your inventory because if you fall down you may need to build your way back up to safety.

rip4d August 1, 2016, 4:26am #6

If you’re digging near water, are afraid of griefers flooding your home, or want a good trap against griefers, create an airlock. Since sand will stay on top of torches, you can place torches along walls and along sand being held up, and it won’t fall. It would look like this:

S = sand T = Torch SSSSSS SSSSSS SSSSST SSSST SSST SST ST

As water flows past the bottom layer of the airlock, the torch is removed and the rest of the sand falls, then the same happens to the rest of the torches, stopping all water flow and thus being named the airlock.

Tip #1: You can create an infinite water pool by placing water in adjacent spots in a 2x2x1 hole. For example:

WA AWThe same physics apply for other things. For example, water fill go into the available space if there’s a “source block” adjacent from another “source block”.

Tip #2: You can’t create an infinite lava pool. Don’t try.

Tip #3: Although it seems to be a waste, use steel (or iron, whichever it is) tools for everyday use.
