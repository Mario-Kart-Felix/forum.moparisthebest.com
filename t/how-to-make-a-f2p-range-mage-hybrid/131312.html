How to Make a F2P Range/Mage Hybrid

Games
Runescape

They’re the best kind, with the ability to take on any of the pk triangle. This well-known method keeps your HP lower than the absurdly high HP’s of many hybrids, allowing you to attain an overall lower combat level than someone of equal strength. The key is not gaining any hp levels through training magic. While in F2P, range hp is inevitable, mage hp can be simply avoided by non-damaging spells. As a ranger, your main target is the mage. Twenty percent of your defense against magic is your magic level, so to fight off mages (more) effectively, your magic level should also be a respectable number.

[size=15pt]Mage[/size]

  1. Materials: burnt meat (cows south of Falador), eye of newt (mage shop by Port Sarim), onion (fields north of Rimmington), rat’s tail (Brian’s archery shop in Rimmington); black, yellow, red, white beads (most infuriating part); 272 body runes, 987 earth runes, a water staff, 508 law runes, an air staff, 199 fire runes. Getting these items is the most tedious and expensive part: it will cost you 163k to achieve 37 mage.

  2. Witch’s Potion: Bring 3gp, a bow, and some arrows, and go south from Falador. Pick up a piece of raw meat and cook it on the stove. If it burns it, good. Otherwise, cook it again to burn it. From there, go south until you see the flame symbol of the mage shop. Enter and buy yourself a nice eye of newt for 3 gp. Walk in the direction of Rimmington, past the mines, into the onion fields. Pick an onion. Now enter Hetty’s house in Rimmington. Talk to her and accept the quest. Now to south-west a tiny bit into Brian’s Archery Supplies and range a rat for its tail. Return to Hetty for your prize. Enjoy that level 4 mage.

  3. Imp Catcher: Go to the Wizard’s Tower and talk to Mizgog for this quest. Follow some quest guide, and either buy the beads from a general store or get them with a main. This will take hours. When you’re done, savor the level 10 mage.

  4. Buy runes: You should have 1200 mage exp now. Now you must buy runes. You can get fairly cheap runes from the Port Sarim Mage Shop if you world hop. Never pay more than 7 gp for any rune but the fire and law runes. Get an air staff and a water staff (from Varrock) while you’re at it. Laws you can get for 300-350gp each in a members world.

  5. Confuse: Now go to Varrock Castle. Preferably you should wear some mage-reducing armor. As you enter the first two sets of double doors from the front entrance, you should encounter some stairs. Behind the stairs is a Chaos Druid. Cast 10 Confuses on him with a water staff equipped and you should level to 11. If you wear full iron, his stats will not decrease and you do not have to wait in-between casts, speeding up the process.

  6. Weaken: Now you can cast weakens. Cast them with a water staff equipped until you reach level 19 (128 weakens).

  7. Curse: Begin Cursing with that water staff until you reach 25 mage. You spent roughly 8k so far (134 curses).

  8. Varrock Teleport: For the fun part. Equip an air staff, and hit that Varrock Teleport. Don’t you feel all fuzzy inside? Level 15 teleporting?

  9. Lumbridge Teleport: When you reach 31 mage (199 Varrock Teles), do Lumbridge Teleport, until you reach 37 mage (309 Lumby Teleports).

You can go for high alch (55 mage), but it will take a while (and a couple mil).

Meanwhile, get your range and hp up.

I would high alch when you reach 55, but:

232, 571 exp from level 41 to level 60. That’s 5888 Crumble Undeads at an average of 7.5 damage per shot (max 15 damage). They appeared to have halved the exp of Crumble Undead, no clue when it happened. So 5888 Crumble Undeads times 130 per chaos rune plus 10 per 2 earth runes (correct me if my prices are wrong; I haven’t bought anything but nats in a while). That gives you around 824k, which is honestly an excellent price for mage.

Therefore, if you decided to skip all my math, it costs 824k to go from 41 to 60 mage.

However, you should not be losing money after you hit 55 mage, because you should be alching. It will take you 3177 Crumble Undeads to reach 55 mage, which is 445k. Therefore, ideally, you should only spend around 600k to go from level 1 mage to level 99 mage.
