Thanks for all the support guys. As soon as I finish the level editor though, rather than sticking to the game and adding things like multiplayer support, I’ll be calling it a done project and learning more to build more complex games. For example, I don’t know what half the mathematical functions in the HLSL do (mainly because I haven’t taken any calculus classes), or how to draw a multi-textured cube, or how to actually modify texture coordinates, or how to draw everything with one draw call, despite having different textures (That really impacts performance and because of it, Path Master 3D is a lot slower with big maps than it should be). Still, this was an amazing learning experience in its own right and maybe I’ll make enough money with it to buy a book that explains all I need to learn. Not saying I’ll charge for it, but I have people saying they would pay for it so maybe they’d be kind enough to donate.

EDIT: After a long time, I finally got somewhere with the level editor. Currently, you can place move around and place blocks. No changing or removing them though, although it’s just a couple of lines I have put off adding. I had to create a separate shader for the hotspot (so it appears even when not on a block, as the screenshot shows) which was a pain due to unknown alpha issues, but it’s all working now. Apart from that, I majorly increased the performance aspect and possibilities of the game, making huge maps or large structures now possible.

Lol, well switching to vertex buffers for regions (since there’s only 3 types of map blocks, it’s kinda pointless to make them based on block type), I managed to get a huge performance boost. Previously, ~15,000 cubes in the game dropped the performance down to about 15 FPS, while this new system allows nearly 60 FPS for 100,000 cubes with fast vertex list rebuilding (Btw I have an ATI Radeon HD 3650).

Big thanks to Taharok for helping and providing info.

EDIT: After many hours of rewriting the rendering engine, I gave up due to issues with culling and unknown graphics card limitations that made everything really hard to work with and kinda left me hanging.
