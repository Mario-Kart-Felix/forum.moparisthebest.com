I read your comment after I posted this.

I doubt you need an i7, go for an i5. Considering your budget I’d go for a cheaper motherboard. I doubt you need 16Gb of RAM 8 is fine. a gtx 660 can’t utilize 3Gb of vRAM go for a gtx 760. Go for a seagate or WD HDD. You don’t need a sound card. You should use the voltage step down adapters that come with your fans so no fan controller is needed. In regards to the power supply - 450W is more than enough for your build, I have an RM 550 (550w) powering a rig with a gtx 580 (soon to be a 780 ^_^) with plenty of power to spare. I’m not sure but I don’t think my PSU fan has ever turned on, I highly recommend the Corsair RM PSUs.
I’d think about reworking your budget a bit so you can afford a better GPU though, a gtx 760 is good n all but I don’t think it can max out demanding games (don’t quote me on that). I think my 580 is similar in performance to a gtx 660 which is slightly worse than the 760 and it reaally can’t max out games.

  • Stock heatsinks on most high end intel CPU’s are fine, I ran my i7 3770k on stock heatsink for ages running everything from 24 hour render tests to Metro 2033 on ultra.
  • Do you really need a blu ray player? Just buy an external USB one and then you can use it for any machines you have… 2014 now, internal DVD drives are dying out.
  • All the extra fans you added in that build are unnecessary, the case you selected already provides these. If you wish to overclock and need a bit of extra air flow in the future then you can always add them on afterwards.

Everything else is fine, though if you remove the above you could always upgrade the GPU you selected, it’ll be a bottleneck in comparison to the CPU
