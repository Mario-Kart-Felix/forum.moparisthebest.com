Muroth August 4, 2016, 1:01pm #2

Case: I’d get something big enough to fit atleast 2 120mm fans. I have no clue about water-cooling, however I hear it’s very efficient. It doesn’t take up much space, but if you go that route look up compatible cases.

CPU: If you plan on doing heavy (1080p video that has 2+ hours of footage) multimedia work, i’d strongly recommend atleast a quad core else you’ll be waiting possibly days for it to process. I suggest an intel core, perhaps i5 or i7 or i3.

Motherboard: You want DDR3 RAM compatibility, a long with support for multiple cores, and the ability to add external cards (PCI/PCI-express)

GPU: Atleast 512MB DDR3 imo, hdmi/vga/dvi

RAM: Atleast 8GB DDR3

HDD/SDD: I use a 128GB SSD card for Windows itself, and a 1TB HDD for programs

PSU: I have a 500W, but I feel as if that’s cutting it short. I’d get atleast 750W but that’s just me.

If moderate gaming, light multimedia, then you can cut down on the specs. My first rig was AMD Dual core @ 8GB DDR3 @ 512MB DDR2 GPU and it had no lag when it came to games such as BF3 and COD:BO.

Note i’m no professional

are there any other methods of cooling, besides liquid? are there different types of heatsinks? is there a specific type of fan needed? are some better than others?

Muroth August 4, 2016, 1:01pm #9

There are 60mm,80mm,92mm, and 120mm sized fans. They’re just smaller/bigger size. You have the CPU fan(s) and the case fan(s). Case fans alone won’t do it, they just circulate the air. I have a combination of three case fans and one CPU fan, which keeps my temps at ~30C.

There are passive and active heatsinks. Passive is just the metal piece that dissipates the heat. Active includes a fan. Most custom rigs use active (passive + fan). A heatsink is usually included with the CPU. I bought an aftermarket heatsink and it came with an attachable fan (active). Just be sure you use thermal paste in between the two (IC Diamond is great IMO). The higher airflow the fan has the better it will cool.

No specific size, just as long as you have an intake and an exhaust.

edit: I’d do some research on how it all comes together just so you can get an idea of what you’re actually doing.
