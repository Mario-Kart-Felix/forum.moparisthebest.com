To answer your question about stability, it is true. Larger drives have more platters = more change of damage. Also, I don’t know what the hell you have, but to be 1TB if a lifetime of data (as long as it isn’t HD videos). As for the RAID 5 array, 3 drives is minimal; just use 3 drives.

The formula for RAID 5 diskspace is: 0.77(totalDiskSpace); so 3 320GB HDDs = 740GB that acts as one drive. May I remind you that RAID 5 can support up to 9 drives (10+ is RAID 10); but if you go over 4, you’ll need a 600WAT PSU. Also RAID 5 is striping AND mirroring. If one disk fails, pop another in and all information will transfer to it. Also, transfer speeds on RAID 5 are higher than in a standard configuration; the OS has (in this case) 2 disks to write data to at once, adding a multitasking element to the disks.
