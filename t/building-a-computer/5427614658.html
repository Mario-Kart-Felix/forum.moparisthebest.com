Kidpaparoach August 4, 2016, 1:06pm #23

On board sound is better than cheap sound cards. Sound cards aren’t very useful, feel free to look up reviews/thoughts on them. They were useful back in the day but since on board sound has improved so much the only real use is it can drive headphones that have a higher resistance (I think). I found an old sound card that cost me a pretty penny back in 2011 but I chucked it out as I didn’t see a point in using it (I have a wireless headset so the amp wouldn’t of helped), drivers were a nightmare at times!

Get a better power supply (cheaper psu’s tend to have worse ripple etc). I didn’t bother checking the monitor you picked but get AH-IPS if possible. Viewsonic, asus, acer, dell are good options (you get good deals on viewsonic monitors + they’re great I have a viewsonic monitor).
