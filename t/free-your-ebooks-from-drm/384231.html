Free your ebooks from DRM

General Discussion
moparisthebest August 1, 2016, 7:50am #1

First, I would like to say there are many places you can buy non-drm books, and if your book is available that way, you should buy it that way.

Before last night, I had always read books from books, but had been wanting to try my phone as an ‘ereader’ and bought ‘The Giver’ from books.google.com. I read it with google book reader, but a much better reader is FBReaderJ, plus I couldn’t read it with anything on Linux because they lock down both the PDF and the ePub with some form of Adobe DRM. Luckily, I came across this helpful post:

Apprentice Alf’s Blog – 13 Jan 11

Ebook Formats, DRM and You — A Guide for the Perplexed

Post updated on 22nd March, 2013 DRM: What it is and why you should care about it. DRM is used by publishers to restrict what you can do with your ebooks. DRM controls which devices you can use to …

Basically, you get the books so you can read them on Windows (I used XP in a virtualbox) or Mac, then you install Calibre, download plugins for it that strip the DRM, import your books, and the DRM is almost magically stripped. Now I can read it with FBReader or Calibre under Linux, and with FBReaderJ on my Android phone.

I’m not doing this to circumvent anything, or to pirate anything, I’m doing it so I can use something I purchased with my own money as I want to use it, in a perfectly legal way, now that is freedom.
