Next, you will need to open ports in Windows Firewall and/or the firewalls offered by McAffee, Norton, or other full-system maintenance suites. For Windows Firewall, open its settings file in the control panel. Click on the “Exceptions Tab” and then click “Add Port.” Add the following port: 43594. (If you’d rather not open a specific port or have to worry about Windows Firewall disabling access in any way, you can disable it entirely.)

Now we will need to make the rule for your router, by doing this you need to go into your router. You can do this by going into Internet Explorer (or whatever client you use for the Internet), and then type in the IP address of the router. Now log into the router and go into “advanced” (at top of screen on mine). Then on the sidebar to the left click the “gaming” button (is only left side for me…) Then you need to make a new rule and fill in the blanks with this information:

Name: 1
IP address: 2
TCP Ports to open: 43594
UDP Ports to open:
Inbound Filter: Allow All
Schedule: Always

OMG, why do you guys have to make it so difficult for him?

If you use McAfee I know you dont need to open any ports in the virus scanner, but you have to in you router:

1.Press Start in your task-bar
2.Press “run”
3.Type “cmd”
4.Type “ipconfig”
5. Look for “default gateway”
6. Look for the number beside it, and remember it EXACTLY!
7. Go into your Internet browser and type you default gateway into the address bar.

The rest is separated due to me not know your router brand or configuration, so the rest of the steps my be wrong for your router, but you should be able to figure it out.
(The following steps are for my Mini Wireless Linksys, these are not universal!)

  1. If you haven’t accessed your router before, or maybe you have, the user-name/pass word is:

    User-name:(Keep this blank)
    Password: Admin

  2. Hit “applications and gaming”, or “port forwarding” if there is that option.

  3. Hit port range forwarding or anything similar. (Not port triggering)

  4. Under “Application name”, type RS SERVER. (Just so you know what it is in the future)

  5. Under “Start” type the port that your forwarding (Check the bottom of this post for hints on the right port to forward.

  6. Under “End”, repeat the above step

  7. Under TCP UDP, put both

  8. Under ip address, change it to 192.168.2.100

  9. Tick off “enabled”

  10. Hit “save”

Done

FAQS:

Q: From Step 8 and beyond, they dont match my router
A: Read the insert between Step 7 and Step 8

Q:I don’t know what port to forward!
A: If you are hosting a normal server: 43594
If you are hosting a PKING server: 5555

Q: You said McAfee doesnt need to be forwarded, but I have it, and it does, you have a guide for that?
A: Nope, cause my McAfee doesnt need to, so I never bothered looking.
