Alright, I figured out how to fix it!

9zw1fr.png1366×768

I am going to explain how I fixed this problem in-case anyone else has this problem.

So, open up your command prompt, and type dxdiag.exe and when that opens click display.

It will say the name of your Graphics Card, or chip, whatever you have. Type that into google, and download the Windows 7 Driver, if they do not have a Windows 8 driver available. It may take a few moments, just download and install it. When Windows 8 loads drivers it just gets the basic ones, the ones that are compatible, not the ones that are the best choice. I need to do this with my sound driver now. I have now realized how bad my sound is now.
