What you can try: (if 1 doesn’t work go to 2 etc.)

  1. search in you client.java for “Class68_Sub9.aString2883” or “127.0.0.1”
  2. open your Class68_Sub9.java and look for “127.0.0.1”
  3. search for “aString” until you found the ip (in client.java and Class68_Sub9.java)

You can also change the ip from “127.0.0.1” to JOptionPane.showInputDialog(null,“Please enter your server ip:”);
Ip changer clients use that too.

Class68_Sub9.aString2883 = “127.0.0.1”;
Class68_Sub9.aString2883 = JOptionPane.showInputDialog(null,“Please enter your server ip:”);
