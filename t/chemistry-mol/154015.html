BobDole81 July 30, 2016, 4:16pm #2

Start with atoms -> moles and moles -> atoms. A mole is a certain number of objects (most typically atoms). Just as a dozen of something refers to 12 of an object, a mole of something refers to 6.022 x 10^23 of something.

A mole of a certain type of atom or molecule is equivalent to the atomic/molecular weight (in amu) in grams. Ie, a helium atom has a mass of approx. 4 amu. A mole of helium atoms has a mass of 4g. That’s only a brief example, but should give you the essence of how it works.
