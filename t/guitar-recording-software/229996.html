Mopman July 31, 2016, 1:27am #2

Well, first of all, you want to plug the cable from your amp into the LINE IN on your soundcard, not the MIC IN.

Then you need to play with the Windows mixer to enable the sound to come out of your speakers, because it should.

As for better recording apps, if you just want a simple multitrack recorder/editer, no MIDI or anything like that, take a look at http://audacity.sourceforge.net - it’s pretty good for a freebie.

Mopman July 31, 2016, 1:28am #6

That is what is wrong with putting it in the mic in The mic in is for signals that need amplication, you are putting an already hot signal into it. It needs to go into a line-level input to get proper quality.

Of course, it is possible your laptops sound card just sucks.

Your best option would be to get a cheap audio interface, perhaps a USB one. Or like I said, go without the amp for now and buy/torrent some amp modelling software, there is some pretty fun stuff around
