Quite often, you hear people who have gone through a surgery talking about god doing all the work. Same applies to soccer players: many (Brazilian) soccer players thank god for their goals.

Fresh parents often see their child as a gift from god. A reward for their faith. But if god was the one who gave them their child, why did they even bother having sex? A sin equals a lack of faith, so by saying that god gave them their child, the parents admit being sinners. And sinners don’t get rewards, do they? So god didn’t give them their child. Catch the drift? Ultimately, by saying that god gave you your child, you actually say that you aren’t religious at all.

People give credit to god when they survive an accident. God is the one who saved them. A surgeon did the surgery, but god guided him. Which makes me wonder: why are there surgeons? If god guides the surgeon, why do people become surgeons? A retard can be guided by god, so the retard can do the surgery too. So people praise god when they save their lives. But some people die after an accident. Who is to blame? God? Hell no, god never does anything wrong. It must be the driver of the car that smashed the deceased person’s car. But, as you can conclude from the short introduction of this topic: everyone is guided by god. So the driver is guided by god too.

Again, by saying the driver is to blame, you blame god.
