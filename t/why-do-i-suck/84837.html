BobDole81 July 30, 2016, 8:07am #3

You do realize that you answered your own question? You’re not motivated (and you even said yourself that you don’t pay enough attention). Obviously its difficult to motivate yourself if you have no interest in a particular subject, but you have to decide what your overall goal is.

If you want to do better at those subjects, there must be good reason - whether its because you don’t want to destroy your GPA or you want decent grades to get into college. My best advice would be to really keep your goal in mind why you work…that’s the best motivation. For instance, I’m taking a Business Theory course online that I’m absolutely hating (especially coordinating group projects online - never do it if you have to) - but I want to do well so I’m really just pushing myself through it. But it’ll pay off in the end, and that’s really what I focus on.

Specifically for the subjects, in history and geography look for connections. Try and see how things you’re learning about are interconnected and look for extended causes and effects. I always found that neat in history. Can’t really say much about Swedish, but depending on what level you’re at, try looking for some news websites written in swedish and practice reading those.

Err, can’t think of much else, but I hope that helps some.
