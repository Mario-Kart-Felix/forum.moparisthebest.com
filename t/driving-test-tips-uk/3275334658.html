Greeny July 31, 2016, 2:31pm #40

You check your mirrors when exiting a roundabout for people who are in the wrong lane. Primarily it’s when you turn right - if you’re in the right hand lane going round the roundabout, about to turn off on say the third exit (so the right turn from your original position) you need to cut accross the left hand lane - which should be empty if people have been giving way correctly. However occasionally you get the odd dickhead who snook up on you, and if you weren’t to look into your left mirror a collision would be difficult to avoid.

Obviously if you do look in your mirror, you see them and just continue back round the roundabout or pomp like a motherfucker collide and get a fat insurance claim.

That may be difficult to understand but the reasoning behind mirrors is justified, it’s just when you pass your test you’ll probably forget about all of them except the centre

Remember, you don’t learn to drive, you learn to pass the test. You’ll only learn to drive after you’ve passed and get out there on your own and experience driving.
