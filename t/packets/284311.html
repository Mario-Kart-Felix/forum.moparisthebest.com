Lin July 31, 2016, 8:34am #8

A packet is basically a group of data. I can elaborate a lot more on this, but that’s what you can think of it as.

Since you want to know what they are for RSPS, they carry information over to each other, to which they then process the data.

Each packet has its own ID. How the server or client handles it depends on the ID. After that is the data the packet carried. For example, if you wanted to send a message, you’d send to the client that you’re creating a message, and then send the actual message afterwords.
