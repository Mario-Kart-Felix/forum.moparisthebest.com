As of this morning, Julia Gillard is the Prime Minister of Australia. The Labor Party pretty much told Kevin Rudd to pack his bags and fuck off.
Noteworthy is that she wasn’t actually elected by the Australian people, but she’s technically the first none-the-less.

PRIME Minister-elect Julia Gillard has paid tribute to outgoing PM Kevin Rudd and spelled out plans for the immediate future in her first speech at the helm of Australia.

Ms Gillard, who came to office in 1998, started by saying she was “truly honoured to lead this country which I love”.

She said she believed a “good government” was losing its way, stating she took her fair share of responsibility for the Rudd government’s record … “for its achievements and its errors”.

Ms Gillard said the government’s proposed super mining tax and the introduction of carbon prices would now be reviewed.

She said she would call an election “in the coming months” and between now and then would seek the support of Australian voters.

Ms Gillard also paid tribute to Mr Rudd, saying he was “a man of remarkable achievements and he made a wonderful history for this nation”.

She also praised the work of new deputy Wayne Swan, calling him an “outstanding treasurer (who) I know will become an outstanding deputy prime minister”.
