But then matters were fluctuated to the next level when they updated the ring of wealth. At first, everything seemed to be adequate - no, they seemed to be expected from Jagex updates. You could even say the update was commensurate. But behold! A nasty, hellish bug was discovered by a group of baleful, malicious players manipulated by the mentioned group of players to inaugurate scores and scores of drops and loot. News about the bug started to propagate amongst close groups of player bug abusers, and oh what wonderful abuse! The bug was abused to gather billions of gps worth of drops, and Jagex literally knew nothing about it. Some cunning minds figured they could abuse the bug to relieve the higher leveled monsters of their exquisite drops, one such monster being the corporal beast, where clans spanning huge amounts of players invaded the wild, quadruped critters stomping grounds and proceeded to eradicate the creature out of existence, earning a few hundred spirit shields as a result of the poor creatures demise. Jagex, being their natural tardy self, took over nine thousand multiplied by four seconds to finally catch on and realize there awas a bug in their so called “feature”. What happened following the incident was a series of horror inspiring decisions on Jagex’ part, leading up to them choosing to screw their player base even harder by deciding the ring still had to stay. To exacerbate matters, Jagex announced their most shocking decision: there would be no resets to players who had illegally profitted from the mentioned bug! It was an outrageous scandal! In light of these events, we are forced to conclude that Jagex in fact was compeltely aware of the bug, and they had introduced it into the game on purpose to lure players to the dark side (membership) with promises of future bugs to come.

However, that was not the extent of Jagex’ crimes. A few weeks before, they updated one of their more useless skills, dungeoneering, and unknowingly (was it really unknowingly?) introduced another bug - the infamous null bug! Being the naturally resourceful bunch they are, Jagex’ faithful RuneScape players quickly found untapped potential in such unmined grounds. Adventure logs were soon filled with null kills and item drops, and made it easier to spoof accounts and sell gold. Naturally, warranted, verifiable gold sellers were saddened by such news. They held a meeting on World 81 of RuneScape at the Clan Wars lobby, and they came to a decision: gold prices had to rise - and they had to rise by such a large amount, people would think twice - no three times - before buying gold from a gold spoofer. True to their word, an aggrandizement of prices soon took hold of the black market, and trade dropped to extremely low activity. Was this a coincidence? I think not. You see, Jagex thought of new, innovative ways of how to discourage black trade, and the only way they thought of was introducing a “bug” into the game, then claiming it was all just an error on their part a few months later. To date, this bug has still not been fixed, and gold prices are still overblown.

And now this! They have disconnected the servers, preventing multitudes of players from continuing their lives or botting their accounts! Tell me, Jagex, have you no hearts? No feelings, no mercy, no nothing? If you can imagine the sound of a young, 16 year old teenager crying because he cannot run his bot without cringing and rushing to update your servers, then you are truly heartless, my friends and deserve no place at the top of our hierarchy.

But even that, dearest of my dearest, was clearly not enough for Jagex! They have been subtly adding new bugs into the system, and are waiting for the right moment to spring their traps upon us, their faithful players, dating back to the golden days of 2001!

The meaning of this, my kin, is crystal clear. There is only one logical conclusion, and that is one only a handful of people have come to recognize. My friends, my brothers, my sisters, my dearest… Judgement day has arrived.

but will we stand for this? no, my comrades! We will revolt! We must unite under one banner, as we were before the God Wars seperated us! We must march together, hand in hand, against the common enemy. But who is this enemy, you ask? Well my friends, the enemy is none other than the RuneScape gods.

Surprised? Yes! I was surprised as well, that such treachery has wormed its way into our gods minds, and has taken hold of their thoughts! At first, I was inclined to believe our enemy was Jagex, but after further investigation, Jagex appears to only be the figurehead, the plain view, call it whatever you like. The real driving force behind the company is the force of the gods. They control our destiny, and hold our lives in their hands.

But we will not stand for this madness. We must turn against them. Yes my friends, you heard correct - we will turn against the gods, no matter how absurd that might sound! Guthix, Saradomin, Zamorak, Zaros, Bandos, Armadyl - what are they, if not bitter, merciless creatures of one mind, one thought and desire - the desire to eradicate us all? Even the God Wars were only a small part of the great jigsaw of the gods’ plans. Their only desire was to eradicate the presence of humanity on Gielinor, and they narrowly failed. It was only by a stroke of luck that humanity was saved - Guthix, being the lazy beast he is, was awakened by the combat and bloodshed, and with a mighty roar cast a sleeping spell on the combatants, causing them to slumber for decades to come. Naturally, the gods were enraged with Guthix, because he had caused the plans demise at the peak of its success, but they failed to avenge their bruised prides and egos because Guthix took to hiding and sleeping for another few decades. So now I must ask you, my dearest - who is with me?

cheering ensues

We must show our strength, and combined together, that strength is indeed fearful! I shake in anticipation just imagining the extent of what we could do - we could go beyond all limits, even becoming gods ourselves! But no matter what, we must always avoid thinking like our current gods - players’ lives are their own to handle! We are not cattle to send to the slaughterhouse as you will, gods of Gielinor! We are humans - And We… Will… Show… Our… Strength!
