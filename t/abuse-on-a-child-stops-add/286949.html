Javacisnotrecognized July 31, 2016, 9:01am #10

Let us consider a second what ‘ADD’ stands for: “Attention deficit disorder”. In other words, somebody isn’t paying attention to what you want them to. In a very general sense, people pay attention to two things: Things they find interesting, and things that are relevant to a problem they’re trying to fix. Everything else is considered boring or unimportant. I was previously diagnosed with ADD and all other kinds of mental disorders. But the people who diagnosed me were trying to get me to pay attention to things that had no relevancy to my interests or fixed any of my problems. It isn’t a “disorder” or “deficiency” whatsoever, it is merely bad instruction.

Unforunately, most people don’t have such strong interests that if you try to make them pay attention to something unrelated, they will refuse.
