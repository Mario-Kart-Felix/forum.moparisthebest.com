It comes down to what will hold up in court and what will not. TOS basically work like EULAs, minus legal enforcement. However, in mopar’s case, his website condones illegal activity. It’s not that his website violates Jagex’s TOS (which is irrelevant), it’s that it breaks laws. The TOS does nothing to protect the website from doing illegal activity. Just because mopar has a few words that you are required to say ‘yes’ to doesn’t mean you and him can start doing illegal stuff safely. That doesn’t even make sense. All it means is that if you break something on there, you can be removed from the site (goes for both). Granted, mopar can remove you from his site anyway, just like Jagex can (since it says in their TOS they reserve the right to disable your account for any reason).

So really, there’s no point in having a TOS on any of these sites, other than to maybe stop indexing (which can be done with robots anyway). Jagex has a point in having a TOS, but it can’t be used more than an exhibit of violating in the court of law.

IANAL, but it seems straightforward enough to me.
