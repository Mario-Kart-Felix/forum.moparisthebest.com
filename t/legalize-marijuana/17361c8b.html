The only study claiming brain damage is the rhesus monkey study, done by Dr. Robert Heath in the late 1970’s. The study was heavily criticized for having only 4 subjects to test on, its experimental bias, and misidentifying the brain structure as being damaged when it wasn’t. And in order to achieve these results, doses up to 200 times the psychoactive dose in humans were given. A newer study was done by Dr. William Slikker at the National Center for Toxicological Research in Arkansas, and this time 64 rhesus monkeys were used. The monkeys were exposed to marijuana smoke daily or weekly for a year. No evidence of structural or neurochemical changes were found in the brains of the monkeys. A similar study was done by Gordon T. Pryor and Charles Rebert at SRI International in California. Thirty rhesus monkeys inhaled marijuana smoke one to three times a day for periods of 6 to 12 months. Again, no evidence of structural or neurochemical changes were found in the brains of the monkeys. Some behavioral changes in the monkey were noticed, but wore off when they were taken off the marijuana. Two studies in 1977 that were published by the Journal of the American Medical Association showed no evidence of brain damage in heavy users of marijuana. Contrary to popular belief, killing brain cells is not a prerequisite for getting high.

“But Jme, what about short term memory loss?” Just thought I’d clear it something up since theres so many misconceptions about this issue. While you are high, you may not remember stuff as well. This is all that is. Alcohol is so much worse for this.

It is not advised to smoke marijuana while pregnant; however it is important to note that numerous studies have failed to show any birth defects. Whenever a study shows negative effects, it is widely publicized even if the quality of the study was not good. For example, they may say marijuana is bad to smoke while pregnant on a study, without even taking note of maternal age, socioeconomic class, and alcohol and tobacco use. A study has shown a slight deficit in visual responsiveness among newborns, however no differences were found after six months. Three year olds born from mothers who smoked marijuana actually had superior psychomotor skills.
