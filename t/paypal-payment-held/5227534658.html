back when i ran a server i pretty much social-engineered my way out of most chargebacks. i had a nice win/loss ratio, but consider me lucky. also, remember that the two major types of chargebacks (unauthorized dispute and item not received) evidently have to be dealt with differently. my general strategy was to bs everything for the former (a little ironic because it wasn’t bs at all) and present a ‘this is a virtual item’ case for the latter, which theoretically should work all the time cuz paypal’s buyer protection doesn’t cover virtual goods (unless paypal changed its policy in the last two years.) it worked for me; it might not quite work for you.

you can try to present your ‘no refunds’ policy, but you cannot be certain that it will work.

it helps to possess good writing skills when writing to paypal. just remember to capitalize your sentences and use your best grammar in each rebuttal so you won’t look like a complete moron, and you’ll be fine.
