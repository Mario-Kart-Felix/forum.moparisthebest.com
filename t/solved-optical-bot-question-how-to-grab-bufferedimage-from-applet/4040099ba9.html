While not strictly a reflection-driven solution, you should look into JDI API. If you identify the java.awt.Canvas field successfully, you can listen for mutations in the value via ModificationWatchpointRequests. Find the appropriate Field (com.sun.jdi, not java.lang.reflect) and pass it to EventRequestManager.createModificationWatchpointRequest() and then enable() it. Note that the virtual machine you’re inspecting here must support watching for modifications i.e. VirtualMachine.canWatchFieldModification().

If you need an EventRequestManager just query your VirtualMachine via eventRequestManager().

Despite the apparent “inaccuracy”, I have never had a problem with what Ollie said.

Simply override the Canvas class with your own, -Xbootclasspath the jar and I put a call in the constructor of the Canvas class similar to: “Bot.resetGraphics()”.

It is true that they do generate different Canvas’ throughout the game, but it isn’t as random as people believe. A new one is generated when the login form appears, on login(?), and whenever you change the state of the window (minimized/maximized). On the unsigned client, the canvas also updates (sometimes) when you change regions.

Its true this method could be beaten, but that alone does not make it inaccurate. At the time of posting, it works perfectly fine.
