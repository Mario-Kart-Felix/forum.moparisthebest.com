XxKanexX July 31, 2016, 3:49am #3

I like freedom, versatility, dynamics, a worthy objective and a good story line.

Freedom - Open space, able to control character and move far distances without many boundaries and just a set task.

Versatility - Different ways to approach an objective, with many different items to use.

Dynamics - Needs balance, but is also fun to play because it’s a challenge. Lots of different levels and terrains, as well as weaponry/itinerary.

Worthy Objective - Obviously something that commits to the story line, and that actually makes sense.

Good story line - Speaks for itself imo.

Also, my list goes something like this:

  1. Graphics/Physics : It’s sad, but I really can’t enjoy a game if it doesn’t push my system’s graphics abilities. It needs to make my eyes happy :).

  2. Storyline: I’m not always on multiplayer, so an immersing storyline is great. You are correct about the CoD4 storyline, it is very respectable when you read up on how they made it. Also, it is not the case for CoD5

  3. Multiplayer: This one is HUGE for me. 90% of the time I am on my 360, i am on Xbox Live. If the multiplayer is not fun, the game is not fun. CoD4 had a fun multiplayer, but I wish they had made it splitscreen.

  4. Realism: I don’t exactly mean realism like real to an every day life standard, but if it is a shooter, make it realistic. If it is sci-fi, make it a possible future. I hate games that go all of the place in realism.

  5. Controls: Not all games have changeable controller options, so if they don’t, the defaults had better be good.

PaulT July 31, 2016, 3:50am #8
What makes a good game, good?

In games I look for form over function. If the controls suck, the graphics are terrible, and the game-play is garbage, all can be forgiven in case of mutant monkey ninjas from mercury or whatnot. Games that present compelling universes to explore such as Zork, Escape from Monkey Island, A Grim Fandango, etc… Are more fun to me than games that present a compelling universe where there are just as many boring rules as real life just transposed to digital, like Morrowind, Runescape, Etc…The less rules and the more lively the game-world the better. There should be more games like Beyond Good and Evil and Fable (Peter Molyneux games, basically) that present a world we are familiar with (for example, swords/sorcery/high fantasy world) and then kick it’s ass by letting you do fun stuff, like get married, beat your wife, and then sacrifice her to the dark gods, etc…
