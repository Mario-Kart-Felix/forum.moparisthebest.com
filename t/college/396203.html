m.dog311 August 1, 2016, 11:58am #6

Whatever you can get into and afford… Really, the first 4 years don’t make much of a difference anywhere because it’s mostly bullshit, general ed classes… Get your first 4 years out of the way at a decently local college (not community college, like I live in Pittsburgh, so University of Pittsburgh would be a good one). After that, transfer to a larger college to get your masters, doctorate, w/e. Not only that, but if your college has more than one campus, you don’t really need to get into the main one. It’s nice, but it doesn’t make much of a difference. University of Pittsburgh main campus, Pitt Greensburg (I’m naming ones close to me, I’m not sure of the colleges around you), and all the other campuses all appear as the same exact thing on your diploma, and you’re getting close to the same education at all of them.

The cost of going to these schools is a big thing to look at too…
UC Berkley: http://grad.berkeley.edu/admissions/cost_fees.shtml
Stanford: http://www.howtogetin.com/colleges/stanford-university/expenses.php
I’m assuming you’re a resident of California, based on Berkley and Stanford both being in Califnornia, but look at the CSU’s tuition. http://www.calstate.edu/SAS/fa_coa.shtml
It is considerably cheaper, and definitely won’t leave you in a massive debt like the other to (~70 grand for 4 years compared to ~160 grand at the other two).

Honestly, it’s up to you though. They’re both great schools, but I truthfully think they’re overkill for an undergrad.
