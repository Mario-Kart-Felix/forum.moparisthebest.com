I went back and read some of the posts which mostly discuss about whether time is even real or an abstract term (which at the average human perspective, it is) and I can even recall someone stating that the speed of light is the same for everyone, and that is true. The speed of another object doesn’t always effect the other unless there is a force of some sort acting against it, but in fact, let’s just say there are two objects - one moving at the speed of light and the other moving at 50 km/h, also the objects are not being acted on upon any other force than gravity. This would mean the only thing that could possibly make the other object appear any faster/slower is the gravity that is actually affecting that object, which is determined by the object’s mass. I’m not a guru of physics, but I most certainly can say that an object can only be changed by the act of another force, whether it is some unknown force we have not yet discovered, or if simply it is two objects colliding, which will include friction occurring.

The point is since we are discussing Time, we should consider only the things that scientists believe are possible, such as gravity, which obviously is one of the biggest effects in the universe. I cannot even begin to imagine what the universe would be if gravity did not exist, possibly a black hole itself?
