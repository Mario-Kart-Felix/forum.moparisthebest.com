EMINEM_BEAST August 3, 2016, 11:55pm #16

Spoiler
I’m kind of late to discuss this episode so I wont bore people by repeating the same shit about how incredible the montages were (by far some of the best directing and editing EVER fucking seen on TV. Honestly, that prison montage while Walt was walking around checking the time, knowing with every second that passed another man was dead because of him, was definitely a homage to the Godfather baptism scene), how Jesse being scared of Walt just shows how much their relationship has diminished, Walt mocking Lydia about killing her but having the ricin with him anyways, and the incredible connections made to previous episodes (the painting, Walt’s scan (last time he had a scan in season 4 they didn’t show the results and he just tells his family that it was okay, I’ve been saying that his cancers been back since then for like the last year), the RV, the paper towel dispenser, Walt taking a shower, the fly…holy shit).

What I will say though is that the level of symbolism, foreshadowing and allusions in this show are almost beyond ANY novel I’ve ever read. The painting, when he first saw it after his fugue state, clearly represented a man leaving his family and just traveling farther away literally and emotionally. Now it makes another appearance just as Walt plans to further darken his soul and kill 10 men…in less than 2 minutes, Michael Corleone doesn’t have shit on Walter White…

But it doesn’t stop there…The title of this episode, “Gliding Over All”, is a Walt Whitman poem that mentions the voyage of the soul and a setting sail…Coincidence? Nah…And ON TOP OF THAT, this is the episode where Lydia mentions the plan to expand OVERSEAS, literally making Walt set sail on a voyage…

If that wasn’t enough, when Walt sees the painting he mentions something about how he wonders if all these paintings just sit in some warehouse somewhere…which made me think of the warehouse in the promo pic, but instead in a few minutes we see Skyler take him to a warehouse and show him all the money he’s earned…

This is by far one of the most incredible shows of all time
