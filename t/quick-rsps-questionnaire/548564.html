Elrick August 4, 2016, 2:07pm #9

  1. How important is it to you that a server has a forum? (Rate 0-10)
    7/10 - I think a good community doesn’t just exist on a platform that has no archival medium.

  2. How important is a good economy to you? (Rate 0-10)
    [b]10/10 - You need to find the right way to put money in and pump money out, and tier your drops consistently and effectively. Do not make equipment or convenience based shops because they will ruin the economy for that skill or aspect of the game. Only do necessary things in a few shops IF NEEDED.[/B]

  3. What XP rates do you prefer personally? (Time to MAX one stat)
    Anything from 10-50 for me is ideal. For longevity and difficulty purposes I think lower ended rates are superior because it keeps players from maxing quickly and quitting yourself. This is necessary if you wish to create an effective donation system. HOWEVER, if you are going to do something like a prestige system where players can prestige multiple times and gain perks through it or something then a higher rate may be appropriate. DO NOT do optional xp rates (Easy, Normal, Hard, Extreme) because then your server will suck like the rest of them. It is your server, set your exp rate and take a stand on it. If it is showing to be ineffective for your server then adjust as need be, but I wouldn’t give players the choice to vote on new exp rates and shit like that.

  4. Donator perks, good or bad? Explain if possible? (If not YES or NO)
    I think they’re necessary to keep your server going. But, there are right ways to do it. First of all, do donation packages not membership otherwise no one is going to give you money. Secondly, DO NOT SELL ITEMS, STATS, OR ANYTHING THAT WILL PUMP ITEMS/UNNECESSARY EXP INTO YOUR GAME. If you do this you might as well hold up a sign saying “Get rich quick scheme”.
    The best way to do it is to do a “premium” based package that provides players with a token system that allows them to purchase AESTHETICS, as well as easier access to publicly made content. At most I would say maybe a donator dungeon is appropriate that provides donators with some amenities for skilling and killing but is not overpowered.

  5. Do you tend to donate on RSPS’s? Explain if possible?? (If not YES or NO)
    I do not donate to RSPS because they never prove themselves to be stable or serious enough. If you prove you are serious about maintaining a full constructed server, people will stay and will donate to get those cool perks. I have donated on multiple Minecraft servers because they were able to prove themselves to worthy, so it is possible to get donations out of me lol

  6. What’s your favourite client version? (317, 377, 474, 503, 508 etc.)
    I prefer higher revisions, usually like a 718.

  7. When you first login to a new RSPS, what are the first things you check?
    I check exp rate, what the home base looks like, how many shops there are, and if there is a stupid teleport book that makes the game way too easy and convenient.

  8. What are the basic “Must have’s” on an RSPS?
    A stable economy, no unnecessary stores, no unnecessary teleports for convenience sake, active and humble staff.

  9. What usually makes you stick around on an RSPS?
    If a server owner follows the things I have mentioned above to some extent, I will stay and see how things progress.

  10. What’s your favourite thing to do on an RSPS? (E.G PK, Quest, Skill etc.)
    I enjoy skilling, which is why I always check to see how easy and stupidly convenient it is to do skills on a server. If you are trying to make the game easy and require no effort I will leave within the first 5 minutes. If I didn’t want to have to try then I would go play a spawn server.

  11. Anything you could tell me to help me win the RSPS community over when the server opens up?
    [b]Choose a respectable name that makes sense. If you name your server DankGreenScape I won’t even click on your advert thread because I can tell the server will be immature and geared towards making people happy and screwing around, not creating a stable environment and economy.

Create a well developed “Development” thread so we can see what your vision for the server is, what your current short and longterm goals are, and what progress has been made since the thread’s creation and in what timely fashion you are creating quality updates.

DO NOT jump the gun and try to show off your server before you have at least 25% of the server done. If you do then most people will get sick of your thread and seeing basic updates occurring.

Create a custom environment. Let’s be honest, most servers are leaching off a specific release. Don’t be one of those people who rip a server, change its name and loading screen image and upload it as your own. Take the time to pick and place unique training monsters and skilling areas. For monsters, look at ones that look cool and take the time to alter the stats to scale to what level you want to fight them. I would much rather fight a level 5 ice giant that has had his stats and loot tables changed, than fight the same boring shit over and over again like rock crabs.[/b]
