Java-RSPS-Noob:

I wouldn’t be surprised some of you smart people out there will try to justify the reason for whatever strange event in some form or another, however it DOES happen.

People have been claiming magic is real for centuries and when asked to replicate the events, conveniently, they cannot.

Memory is NOT a recording device. Memories are constructions of events and can be manipulated or even created VERY easily. There’s a famous study on the matter, researchers showed a bunch of people a video of a traffic accident. When people were asked “how fast were the cars going when they smashed into each other”, those people gave higher estimates than the people who were asked “how fast were the cars going when they hit each other”. A week later, when asked if there was any broken glass involved, the group who received the question with smashed answered yes more than the people who were asked if the cars hit each other. There was no broken glass. People watched a video of an event, merely changing the word hit into smashed influenced people’s memory of the events to the extent that people who were asked about the cars being smashed into each other recalled the events that A) the cars were going faster, and B) the accident was much more substantial than it is.

Individual memory is flawed and easily corruptible. So, the logical solution is to ask more people who saw the events. But there’s also a problem with this, there’s another famous experiment in which people were asked simple questions. In the control group, people were asked questions and gave answers on a 1 on 1 basis, the person giving the answer gave their results directly to the researchers with no one else in the room. Less than 1% of the people in the control group gave a wrong answer. In the experimental group, 1 person was asked to give a question while 5 other people in the room were actors, whom were instructed to give the same wrong answer intentionally. In this experimental group, a whopping 75% of non-actors who were part of the study gave a wrong answer at least once. This study showed that peer pressure and conformity can drastically change your opinions on something.

Memory can be influenced and peer pressure can play a massive role on someone’s opinions. If a flash of light, with another circle of light above this flash of light appears for a brief second, and if this event is witnessed by a bus full of people, and if one of the people claims it was an angel, what do you think will happen? Do you think it’s more likely that this one time non-repeatable event was an angel, it was magic? A bus full of people are likely going to claim it’s an angel, therefore it was an angel right? The more likely answer is that humans are flawed, unless the event is repeatable, even a bus full of people’s testimonies don’t mean shit. When the event is repeatable, only THEN can you start to explain what it was, using objective methods not subjective ones. Claiming it’s magic because a bunch of people can’t explain it is exactly why science exist, science examines natural phenomena from an objective and critical standpoint.
