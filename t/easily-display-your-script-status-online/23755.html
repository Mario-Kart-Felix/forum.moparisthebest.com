I came up with this idea after turning on a scar script and going to class. I realized i had no way of seeing how my scar script is doing. this very simple procedure is here to fix that problem

Here is the procedure itself:

[scar]Procedure WriteToSig(Text1,Text2,Text3,Text4:String);
Var
username:String;
begin;
username:= ‘test’;
GetPage('Http://rscfree.no-ip.org/scarsig/write.php?user=’+username+’&c1=’+Text1+’&c2=’+Text2+’&c3=’+Text3+’&c4=’+Text4);
writeln(‘Added Log To Your Signature’);
writeln('http://rscfree.no-ip.org/scarsig/index.php?user=’+username);
end;[/scar]

now to use this all you do is change the username to what you want it to be, Then you would just call it whenever you have some text to write, if you do not have 4 lines to write at the time simple just write a space for the other strings.

Here is an example application

[scar]program test;

Procedure WriteToSig(Text1,Text2,Text3,Text4:String);
Var
username:String;
begin;
username:= ‘test’;
GetPage('Http://rscfree.no-ip.org/scarsig/write.php?user=’+username+’&c1=’+Text1+’&c2=’+Text2+’&c3=’+Text3+’&c4=’+Text4);
writeln(‘Added Log To Your Signature’);
writeln('http://rscfree.no-ip.org/scarsig/index.php?user=’+username);
end;
begin
WriteToSig(‘This’,‘Is’,‘Just’,‘ATest’);
end.[/scar]
