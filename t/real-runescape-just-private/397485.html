zyle1992 August 1, 2016, 12:09pm #4

Jagex has never, and will never release their code. Private Servers are at the simplest form, a protocol. Just like HTTP, FTP, and IRC.

What we do as developers, since we don’t have any of their code, is observe how the client interacts with the server. From those interactions, we can make a “best guess” on how the server functions, and emulate that behavior. This is why I prefer to use the term RuneScape Emulator, instead of Private Server.

To learn how emulators work, I would first take a look at how the 317 protocol functions. However, due to the nature of game programming, if you don’t already know the language, you will quickly learn bad habits. Evidence of this is the many server bases that exist today.

The reason that most private servers are #317 is simply because it’s the most documented, and most understood. Lin is/was working on a #355 just to do something different. He had to work harder however because there was no existing documentation. The way 317s function today is unchanged up until #469 (or #474 if I’m mistaken). After that, Jagex threw the many communities a curve ball when #503 arrived. Instead of all the objects in the world being found in the cache, the client then requested the map data or more appropriately, XTEA keys, from the server when the player entered a new region. This is also another reason why #317s are more common than other revisions. To have any objects appear (trees, crates, etc), you had to have the map data. You didn’t have to worry about that with# 317s.

Anything after #503/#508 I didn’t care to look into. In my opinion, if you didn’t have a complete map, you didn’t have a complete game.
