BobDole81 July 30, 2016, 4:17pm #5

Supposing you had 2 perfectly smooth and perfectly reflective mirrors, I don’t see why it wouldn’t if you aligned it properly (this would have to be such an ideal scenario that I’m ignoring the difficulties of aligning the light beam perfectly). Discounting quantum effects (like photon-photon interaction), a single photon could bounce back and forth for all eternity for all anyone cares. Note that you would not be able to see the light at all…if all the light is trapped bouncing back and forth, none would approach your eye and so you could not see light coming from that area.

Anyway, Light does not simply dim over time. In terms of relativity, light moves through space at the speed of light (obviously), but thus moves through time at a speed of 0. Light does not age at all. If you’re referring to a Doppler shift, I don’t believe it would really apply here since the system is at rest. But even a Doppler shift is just an illusion of loss/gain of energy. Light does not “lose” energy unless it interacts with particles.
