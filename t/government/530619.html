I had a dream last week that I logged into Moparscape Classic and there were several hundred players online. Having ran two RSC servers over a period of years, I realize how important it is going to be to devise a functional server team from the beginning as all eyes will be on MoparClassic. As this server will be tied to this forum being the official RSC server of Moparscape, players will flock in droves. Maybe not at first but given time, it can happen.

Keep Jagex’s invisible style of RSC days government in mind. We rarely saw jmods. I personally never enountered a pmod until RS2. A few screenshots of Mod Paul and Andrew may still survive still but for the most part, players did not interact with Jagex directly. This led to minimal confrontation, high integrity of the economy and production database, as well as a game where players could sort out their fights with other players themselves through conventional means - clan warfare in the wilderness. Without having the admin’s ear, player #1 who accuses player #2 of botting when player #2 is simply far better at timing the rune ore rocks will not be able to convince their close friend who is in the role of admin to ban player #2. Player #2 will then not burn bridges by quitting in a fury and going on to post ranting insults about the server in general, causing new players to not give MoparClassic a chance and instead leave to play an alternative server. Often, it is the player mod who abuses their power and causes the series of events to unfold the same way.

The best government is a formal, nonserver-based government with a small number of player moderators in-game covering all time zones with very limited powers. The ability to mute and the ability to offer assistance to unstuck a player (with the player accepting on their end, similar to a trade window, before the teleport to Lumbridge can occur to prevent abuse for any reason) should be the only two powers assigned to a player moderator. Banning should be a formal process that a council vote on the forum should address. Kicking should not be permitted as it can be used as a form of player abuse. A player that is disruptive was never jailed for 20 minutes in RSC. That only serves to make them quit and it abuse of powers.

The modroom was never furnished with a desk, a plant, and a jail cell as common RSC servers have. I recall a picture from around 2002 that showed a player teleported there with one of the jmods and it was simply an empty room for the screenshot. Things have changed with RS3 though. Take this photo, displayed on the Runescape website as a meeting place for player moderators to converse with Jagex moderators:

Botting should be handled through something resembling Jagex’s botwatch. Here is how we can solve the problem:
Step 1.) Create a website, tie it to the database, take weekly snapshots of all players and graph it. Show a graph for all players who are either over level 50 or have x amount of gp/high value items in their inventory+bank spanning three week snapshots. For any who have an abnormal overlay showing a much higher wealth or skill gain than the others that could only be indicative of botting, place them under surveillance.

Step 2.) Utilize another form of player moderator account named invisimod that cannot engage in combat, trade any items, and is completely invisible to traditional players. Local chat should not be visible to traditional players either. Only private messaging should. The invisimods must remain as ghosts and not interrupt or interact with the game other than surveying suspected bots.

Step 3.) The invisimod should private message the suspected player and strike up a conversation with them, acting as if they are a regular player for all intents and purposes. If the suspect does not respond or does not make any reasonable human reply, the invisimod should take a screenshot and submit it to the council to vote on a ban. For players who might disable their private messages to appear offline, invisimods should be granted the power to override the player’s private message setting to enable it in order for their messages to reach the suspected player. This could be done through an in-game command or a menu overlay within the invisimod’s game client.

Step 4.) For players condemned to a ban, the punishment should be timed at two weeks with the player’s account name placed upon a list. Their tradelogs should be investigated utilizing a website connected to the database showing dates, times, trade partners, and items traded. Trade partners should be able to be clicked on to see a live view of their stats, bank, and inventory as well as IP addresses used. This information can be used to find all mule bot accounts which may also be given a two week ban at the discretion of the council. Upon the second documented attempt at botting with screenshot evidence by an invisimod, a permanent ban should be set in place for all associated accounts along with an IP ban for all IPs used in both account creation and last login.

By following the four step process outlined above, player moderators should not ever need to kick or ban players at their own discretion at any time. This will enable a timed response with just as much effectiveness but with much more accuracy and integrity than traditional methods.

I write from years of experience in dealing with this very problem tens, if not hundreds of times. It can and will be a server killer if the cancer is allowed to run rampant. We have all seen this time and time again and this can completely end that. The methods listed above, ranging from invisimods to functional website panels have all been created in one form or another over the years and are not difficult to implement. I still have the entire Lupus Regnum website that Elite created, as well as all of the DarkQuest features that can be shaped to cover everything listed above. I believe in a strong core game developed in an open source environment without a drive for monetization. MoparClassic is building on that foundation. Let us make that dream a reality!

lothy August 4, 2016, 10:32am #5

My in-game account is a staffer account? This is news to me.
Was it like that before when the server was first online?

By the way, I’ve always believed their should be restrictions on moderator accounts. Namely the same restrictions that Jagex employees are subject to - no trade, no dueling, dropped items are immediately removed from the game, etc.
For bot detection, one idea I’ve recently had (and liked) was an ability for moderators to occasionally force a captcha screen to appear. They would be notified that it had appeared, and if the captcha was ever completed they would be notified of how long the completion process took (a strong indicator that there’s nobody at the computer if the player has just gone from powermining iron to not solving the captcha for five minutes).

I would be happy with the following ‘powers’:

  1. Muting.
  2. Forcing a captcha (with some arbitrary limit).
  3. Invisibility.
  4. Teleporting to a target player.
  5. Teleporting to an arbitrary location (either co-ordinates, or a named location such as ‘varrock’).

And the following limitations:

  1. No trading.
  2. No teleporting other players.
  3. No dueling.
  4. No dropping items on the ground.

Forcing a captcha could obviously be used to harass, so there needs to be limits. Specifically, a target player should only be able to (initially) only receive one every 30 to 60 minutes. The cooldown needs to be on the receiving side, otherwise two or three moderators could captcha-gank someone and bug the fuck out of them.

I like the sound of an account management system. At the end of the day tooling really matters. It’s the game changer when it comes to usability.
