amy_webb1 August 4, 2016, 9:52am #13

First of all, you can never expect to have a perfect team which is effective in helping members on your server. Anything that involves a group of people can never be perfect because there are too many factors.The best you would hope for is an effective staff team, with this, you should base it off, as others have said, time zones and players. You need a balances ratio between players and staff in order for players to feel included but restrain from breaking rules. You need people your player base can respect, but cannot manipulate.

I would say the best way about it is to have yourself as someone who watches for outgoing players and follow up on them, rather then just hiring random people who apply. It shows that they were once players, and gives them a better connection with the community. Personally, i don’t think i would ever make developers staff as they tend to assert their importance a lot. They should be given separate badges if any, to show their dedication to the server but still refrain from giving them any rights to abuse.

These are just my views, things i’ve seen happen first hand and what i’ve been told by other owners.

Tutorial August 4, 2016, 9:52am #14

I personally wouldn’t want more than 2-3 people helping out, at least until you reach a big player base (100+). If you want the right people, make sure you spend time monitoring potential players. It would be better to keep a sharp eye on players that are not only active, but interested in the community. There are tons of people that are capable of performing tasks, running by a strict manual, and even being completely trustworthy, but a select few are only going to be interested in the job itself. I would also suggest that you try to keep applications out of the picture, and focus on invitation only. If people know that you are trying to find staff members, they are going to make it their full priority, even if it means breaking rules in an attempt to so. The difference between this and a real job is obviously the payment; applications apply in that situation because people know they need the money, so they will do everything it takes to keep their job. If you give an immature and incapable person the duty of enforcing rules on your server, they will know they don’t really have to worry too much about any serious consequences.

Elrick August 4, 2016, 9:52am #15

As most have said, so long as you have a few dedicated moderators in different timezones, you won’t have much of an issue until you begin to see more and more reports.

Moderator A is in -5 UTC (East Coast USA)
Moderator B is in 0 UTC (UK)
Moderator C is in +10 UTC (AUS)

If you can find 3 moderators that are able to moderate well, you should have very little time slots where there isn’t a moderator on. Then when you see a rise in reports or a majority call for more moderators, begin making a list of active, helpful, and MATURE candidates for moderators. In my opinion you should see little to no profanity, almost no vulgarity, and absolutely 0 tolerance for blatant racism, sexism, or any other form of group-oriented offensive stances from a moderator.
