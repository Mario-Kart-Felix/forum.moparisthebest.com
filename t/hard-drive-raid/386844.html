moparisthebest August 1, 2016, 8:16am #4

The RAID that is built into most motherboards is generally really crappy, it’s called ‘fakeraid’, because most is implemented in software anyway. I’ve only ever set up Linux software RAID1 for my servers, not on my desktop. But you have to understand it won’t make it ‘save and load quickly’.

With 2 hard drives, your choices are this:
RAID1: Data duplication, each of your drives are a copy of the other, this makes writing slightly slower but reading almost twice as fast. If one of your hard drives fails, you don’t lose any data. However, it’s not a replacement for backups, because if something goes wrong with your operating system, drivers, or even if you accidentally delete or destroy data it is still destroyed on both drives.

RAID0: This stripes your data across two hard drives, it makes writing and reading about twice as fast, but also makes you twice as likely to lose data, since if either disk fails you lose everything. I wouldn’t use it, too risky.

For more detailed info, look here:

en.wikipedia.org

RAID

RAID (Redundant Array of Independent Disks, originally Redundant Array of Inexpensive Disks) is a data storage virtualization technology that combines multiple physical disk drive components into one or more logical units for the purposes of data redundancy, performance improvement, or both. Data is distributed across the drives in one of several ways, referred to as RAID levels, depending on the required level of redundancy and performance.
