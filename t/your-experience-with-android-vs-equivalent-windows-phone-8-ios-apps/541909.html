And you wonder why…

Android’s major problem is version fragmentation and not so strict design policy - ultimately these two things go hand in hand. You’ve obviously used all the Google apps, aren’t they absolutely great? Simple, clean interface that’s nice and smooth. The reason: Google develop them with their current iteration of Android in mind and actually follow their guidelines (It’d be quite weird if they didn’t)

Some may argue the freedom Android developers are given to design their applications in almost any way they feel like is a plus side. For the majority of apps this level of customisation is entirely unnecessary and leads to awfully equivocal interfaces which even tech-literates may find difficult to adapt to. iOS and Windows phone have a much healthier approach by making developers use their own native components to build apps - I love how WhatsApp and normal text conversations look almost identical in Windows Phone.

tl;dr Android devs are given too much freedom and some get it wrong hence make shitty slow interfaces.
