Work in progress registration process:

After it’s finished it should be pretty much impossible to do anything wrong. Absolutely everything has been checked to make sure the person signing up knows exactly what can and can’t be done.

Other things I want to do is validate the password when typing to check they haven’t accidentally put a space at the beginning or end etc. It will also check the password strength to check they haven’t used any common phrases. The reCAPTCHA can be checked with AJAX too so it doesn’t let you submit the form unless the captcha is correct.

I can’t think of anything else that’s required really. As far as I can tell it’s fool proof.

The main reason for being so anal about this is I’ve seen a lot of people try and register characters with spaces at the beginning or end of their name only to find out they were removed with PHP’s trim() method. I also don’t like players registering names with more than 1 space in a row as it creates a lot of confusion when someone called “Il Ill I” is walking around. Plus, players would sign up with “THIS NAME” and be disappointed when it got corrected to “This Name” so hopefully the client sided preview will make them realise this is going to happen.
