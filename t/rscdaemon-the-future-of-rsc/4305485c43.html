Kryptix August 2, 2016, 11:54pm #326
-Nothing:

I think he means that we want to see the progress on RSCD.

People (players and junior developers) are used to fast progress (in terms of content) as everyone starts with a working base so our progress probably seems really slow. I know Zilent has been investing roughly 6 hours a night without skipping a night for a few weeks now though. The problem is I’m having to have a normal GMT based sleeping pattern due to work so we only communicate a few hours a week. Luckily we’ve split the project up so we each have different responsibilities so not much communication is needed.

I’ve been idea mapping more than anything, Pompano kindly gave me the tryrunescapeclassic.com domain so we’re going to use it as a landing page to advertise RSCD with screenshots and a basic description of what it is before allowing them to proceed to the webclient. When a first time user accesses the site the client will automatically load and log them in as a ‘guest’ account. It will then teleport them around the map invisibly showing them the popular areas, the PvP side of the game and explain what RuneScape Classic is before dumping them on the tutorial island as a new player. They can then play the game as a normal new player would and if they wish to save their progress they simply visit the RSCD website and sign up choosing a username at the same time.

I think this will help bring new players into the game as a lot of people just click onto the website and can’t be bothered to sign up unless they were referred by a friend or have experience with private servers. I know when I first signed up to Reines’ RSCD I found the whole thing a bit confusing, I had already signed up to the forum so was confused why it was saying the login credentials were incorrect when logging into the game only to discover I had to go into my forum profile to create an in-game character. I know I’ve had to explain how to sign-up and get in-game more times than I can remember so I’m convinced a lot of people have given up prior to actually seeing the game itself.

For those of you that aren’t aware, Pompano has been doing some great work with SEO recently getting his rskingdom.com domain to 4th spot on Google for the term ‘runescape’ and has offered to do SEO for RSCD. As you can imagine a lot of traffic comes directly from search engines so a nice simplistic landing page with a sleek promotion video will draw a lot of first time players in.
