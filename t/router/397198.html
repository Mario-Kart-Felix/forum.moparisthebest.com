A 5mb/s internet connection is actually represented in 5 megabits per second. They do this so it sounds like it’s more than it really is, and it’s pretty shady in my opinion. But it’s become industry standard. Divide 5,000 by 8 (there are 8 bits in a byte) and you’ll get 625kb/s which is your maximum download speed in optimal conditions.

A router can affect download speed, ping, and other things. But I wouldn’t be so quick to blame your router. Try connecting a computer directly to your modem and analyze the speeds you get then, before you invest money in a new router.
