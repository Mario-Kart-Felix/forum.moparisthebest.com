No one likes runecrafting. It’s a dry, dull, and boring skill.
Especially on a rsps. That’s why typically server’s will set it’s xp rate higher than the rest.

But lets change that. Lets change Runecrafting as a whole. Let’s “reinvent the wheel”

I was thinking, maybe the skill could start like this:
You get the essence, and the correct talisman (or tiara or what ever) and then use it on the correct altar.
From there, you’d be teleported to a new location, based off the rune you’re making.
Example:
I grab 27 rune essence, a air talisman, and use the essence/tali on the altar.
I then get transported to a place where I’m tested on my wits using air.

What could we do for challenges? Like, what are some good idea’s for challenges and such that could be used here.

Restrictions: They cannot be combat based (so skillers can do this too).
if gfx/anims are used, they must be based off runescape’s. (I don’t have custom ones nor don’t know how to make em).
I only have revision 377 mapdata.

JavaTC August 4, 2016, 8:52am #16

I’m fine with how it is really, but that’s also because I never do it, I always have enough money from doing other skills first to buy all my runes anyway.

To make it more interesting, I’m not really sure. I wouldn’t be too interested in a skill where I’d have to do puzzles or ‘use wits’ to gain xp. It wouldn’t seem to fit in with the rest of the game.

One reason I think it’s so dry and dull, at least for me, is lack of communication. Whenever I’m fishing, woodcutting, crafting, cooking, etc, I’m always able to type messages to other people. You can’t really do that with RCing, you’re always running, always clicking, and can’t really stop to type out things, or stay in the same area as other people.
It’s the same issue with agility, firemaking, and such, you’re doing too much to be able to communicate. I don’t think making you do more would really help that aspect out much.

The way I see it, you either have to make it less demanding so people can communicate better, or you have to make it more entertaining to distract them from the lack of player interaction.
