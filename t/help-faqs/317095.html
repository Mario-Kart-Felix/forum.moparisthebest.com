FAQ: How do I make a item go to the correct spot, such as a shield?

Answer: Go to item.java look for shield and add the id in there like it looks for the others. Ex: 12, 13

FAQ: How do I add a command to my server?

Answer: Go in ‘Client.java’ search for “command” and there should be a line called customcommand()
Or something along those lines.

FAQ: How do I change my starting area?

Answer: log in on a test account, do not move. type ::pos or ::mypos, or whatever the command is for your server, then open “Player.java” search for the first number that popped up for the coords, and there you go, just change them.

FAQ: How do I make npc’s open a shop?

Answer: Considering you have the npc id, go in “Client.java”- normally correct spot. Search “wanneshop” And it should pop up like the npc id, and WanneShop = 1, etc… Just copy and paste from there. with the shop id you want.

FAQ: How do I make a client/server work with 64bit?

FAQ: How do I change prices of the items?

Answer: “item.cfg” Before the bonus’s, and right after the last word… You will see the prices, change them to what u want.

FAQ: How do I change the teleports?

Answer: Most servers use it by action buttons, figure out the button id, search for it in client.java and there are the cords.

FAQ: How do I change npc drops?

Answer: Depending on the base, most of them use drop.cfg, npcdrop.cfg, etc… Just go in there and look at what the other ones, and make your own.

FAQ: How do I add npc’s?

Answer: AutoSpawn.cfg, normally looks like //------NpcID---CoordX--CoordY--Height--RangeX1-RangeY1-RangeX2-RangeY2-WalkType-HP-Descrption

FAQ: How do I change what a npc says above their head?

Answer: Considering your doing this because they already say something, open “Npchandler.java” search for a word from what they are saying, and replace that line with what you want, Copy and paste it and change the npc id, etc… To make a new one.

FAQ: How do I change the crowns, from the default ones? Or make custom ones load?

Answer: Here Click there to see the tutorial on how.
