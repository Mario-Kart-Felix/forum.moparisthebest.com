STREET CLOSES AFTER TRAILER SPILLS LOAD OF LIQUID ASPHALT

By Ray King/OF THE COMMERCIAL STAFF
Thursday, October 19, 2006 8:21 AM CDT

A tractor-tanker truck carrying liquid asphalt lost part of its load Wednesday morning and forced the closing of a Pine Bluff street for several hours.

Wally Hunt, coordinator of the county Office of Emergency Services, said the trailer contained 5,000 gallons of liquid asphalt and some of it spilled onto Michigan Street, resulting in the street being closed while cleanup operations were under way.

Police Lt. Bob Rawlinson said the truck was on its way to Pine Bluff Sand and Gravel when its driver, Jeff Dumas, 41, of El Dorado, missed the turn off the Martha Mitchell Expressway onto Michigan Street, and tried to use a side street to turn around.
