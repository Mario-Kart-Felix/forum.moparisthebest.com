Ashi:

Okay, what would you do?

That 400mb figure you’re seeing is all of the game objects loaded into memory. You can safely unload regions that haven’t been touched in a while.

The actual size of the clipping mask is very small. Assuming a region of 104x104x4 that has a mask for every tile, you’re looking at ~170-340kb of ram. In reality a normal region uses ~40-80kb, and can even be reduced if you unload tiles that would never be accessed.
