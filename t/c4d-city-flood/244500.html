I modeled a city kinda, and gave it some shitty ass textures cause I didn’t plan on having close ups. I faced the hardest part of visual effects, water. It’s like a puddle. No big waves, just little jumps.

Tico what you’re trying to say is what I was explaining in my first post about nonscalable water. Even though this is a 3D model, it was built relatively small (3mx3m maybe) and importing into realflow makes it smaller as well. So you have a tiny scene filled by water. So this is how a 30cmx30cm (.1 scale in realflow) model would look filled with water. But with the camera being so close forced perspective makes it seem these buildings are life size. The water is not however. Those circles are just drops of water. If the city was bigger than real scale and turned out real scale in the simulation, those drops would be miniscule. But since the simulation used a tiny ass version of it, it’s like letting water loose in a lego city.

Water can’t be scaled. That’s the easy explanation.

SomethingToHide July 31, 2016, 3:16am #16

keyframe it. You can do it multiple ways. I used to make a spline for it to follow and hook it to that because I didn’t know how to work everything. But there’s a much easier way.

The automatic keyframing buttons. Turn it on on the first frame. Move to the frame where you want the camera to be later on. Move the camera to that point. Move further along where you want it next time. Move/rotate camera. etc etc.

It makes a bezier curve for you that it follows and makes it seamless. But I always open the timeline and do some adjustments in the end.
