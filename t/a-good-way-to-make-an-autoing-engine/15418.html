Krichevskoy July 29, 2016, 10:11pm #9

I don’t know what people do now, but here are two ideas offhand:

Passive:
Do not touch the client. Write accessor methods for (or reflect) things like NPC/object position. Use this data in your cheating program (located outside the client threads) to control input methods. This constrains you to using only the human game interface and passive client-code interaction. It also requires your program to get the game state every time it wants to do something and requires you to create your own control flow (as opposed to the next one).

Event-based:
Insert hooks at places in the client like object creation / NPC tracking / inventory handling / etc that inform your program of the current game state. This has the benefit of allowing you to make a bot that responds to events rather than follows a script.

No matter what you do, detectability really depends on your implementation (so you could use ideas/snippets from Aryan, just don’t copy the whole thing…)
