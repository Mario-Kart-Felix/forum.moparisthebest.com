r0ckstar96 August 2, 2016, 6:50am #1

I need some help telling my parents about my true religious beliefs.
Story time: I was born and raised a Christian, however around the age of 13, I started to question my beliefs and a few weeks later claimed to be an Atheist. I changed my religious beliefs (on facebook) to Atheist, and my family members saw, whom then reported the sudden change to my parents. My parents called me into their room and a baww-fest commenced, and then they started making me go to church with them every Sunday, and made me have weekly talks with their pastor and I had to write down why I was questioning my beliefs. It was honestly hard to explain, so what I wrote down didn’t seem convincing to them. My pastor then went on to preach to me from his bible saying quotes from it and saying how evolution was false, etc. I also had to have daily conferences with my parents about my beliefs (an hour each day), but really all they did was tell me stories about what’s going to happen if I don’t believe in Christianity (they used the good ol’ scare tactics). They also, along with multiple other families they’re friends with, bought me a giant bag full of books on why Christianity is the way to go, and why evolution is wrong. One cold day in December, my mom angrily called for me to come into the computer room where she was sitting. She wanted to know where I started to get these ideas that made me question my faith in Christianity, and so off the top of my head, I said science class at school (8th grade at the time), however looking back on it, it really wasn’t the school’s fault, because I questioned my faith on my own time, not because of school. Anyways, she proceeded to write a very angry e-mail to the school saying how they shouldn’t be teaching evolution (even though they hardly even touched on it at all, just said the word once) because it’s causing me to question my faith and all this other stuff. Then a few weeks later my principal had a meeting with me saying that he’s sorry what was in the textbooks caused me to question my faith, and some more awkward talk happened that I can’t remember (he was actually pretty calm about all this like it’s happened before), then I went back to class. Eventually, after a couple weeks of these stressful events and daily conferences with my parents and weekly conferences with my pastor, I just gave in and claimed to magically be a Christian again, and all went back to normal. Although a couple months later, a couple weeks after I got confirmed, I secretly took my place (religiously) as an Agnostic. Over time I looked into other religions and philosophies like Buddhism and Taoism, and as of late, I’ve been really liking the ideas of Taoism and would like to look more into it.
So my question to you is, how can I come out to my VERY Christian parents that I don’t share the same religious beliefs as them, but without getting pressured and scared into believing in their religion like they did last time?
