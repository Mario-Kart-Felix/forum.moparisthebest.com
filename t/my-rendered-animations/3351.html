I'll keep track of all the animations I've uploaded here, and for you to "enjoy."

These scenes are made in C4D. I simulate fluid particles with another app, then import into C4D. Set up camera and scene and render it all. I've got one I'm rendering right now, but it took 8 hours for the first 30 frames. However that's just the beginning, the middle and end only take a minute or two per frame, so that should go by faster.

Sorry for the horrible quality, google video really fucks up the pixels in these. But that was because I was rendering straight from C4D to a movie. Now I'm importing JPEG sequences into Adobe After Effects and touching up/exporting from there. Doesn't give bad quality on google, as you can tell from the newer ones.

SomethingToHide July 29, 2016, 8:18pm #3

Thanks.

It's REALLY user friendly IMO. Everything is right there, and organized. If you're going for rendering, you might as well get another renderer also. Advanced Renderer (C4D's renderer) is pretty good, but the GI system in it BLOWS. Takes hours on hours. I've bought finalRender and I am extremely pleased with it. GI solutions are more accurate tenfold, and increasingly faster.

A simple scene with a ball and lit by HDRI with GI on in AR takes quite a few minutes. In fR, it takes less than 30 seconds almost. Scale that up for a big scene, and it's a huge improvement.
