And the artifact is there because of the GI calculations. Things like that appear when you have GI undersampled, and other settings low.

The GI will bounce the light around like normal light rays. Normally I think it does 4 rays per cubic inch or something like that. I can’t really remember. But when undersampled it does say, 2 rays. And on big open areas, that leaves lots of areas where rays don’t hit correctly, or at all. Because the GI is set to use lots of calculations on many polygons, or a highly detailed objects. But when there’s a just a few large ones it doesn’t use as many, to save time.

So when all that’s happening, it can screw up in some placed.
