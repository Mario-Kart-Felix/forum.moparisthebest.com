Oh yeah, sorry for getting off topic on your thread…

Here are rough steps to getting your server running and accessible, assuming you already have it running locally.

Port-forwarding

  1. Go into your router settings.
    1)a) You can do this by going into your browser and typing 192.168.1.1 for the URL (usually) but sometimes also 192.168.0.1
    1)b) The default login information is, username: admin, password: password, sometimes it can be variants of those, like administrator instead of admin. But if you have a Wi-Fi connection that’s protected with a password, it’s sometimes the wifi password.
  2. After you’re on your router settings go to port-forwarding.
    2)a) Sometimes a port-forwarding link is on the home page, other times you have to look for “Advanced” or equivalent.
  3. Create a new instance under port-forwarding. You should select TCP, and for port put 43594, and for local IP, put in your local IP. Your local IP can be found by going to Start > Run… > cmd > when a black box appears, type in ipconfig. Your local IP is your IPv4 address.
  4. After you have created the new entry, save everything and log out.
  5. Go to whatsmyip.org and take note of this IP. This is your EXTERNAL IP, the one that other computers use to communicate with you. Copy it down and open your client folders.
  6. In your client folders, you need to change the IP that your client connects to. This can sometimes be in client.java, GUI.java, Jframe.java, but it depends really on what revision client you have. After finding where the current IP is, replace that with your external IP, exist out of the files after saving changes, and compile your client files.

After following the steps above, just make sure your server is running, and then send your client to your friend.

What you just did: port-forwarding - That was just a quick pass on how to do it, if you wanted more in depth instructions or images, there are a few tutorials on these forums and other sites for port-forwarding.
