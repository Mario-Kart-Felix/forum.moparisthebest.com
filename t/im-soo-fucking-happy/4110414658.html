The Core i series stand out because of their hyperthreading and because of turbo boost. Wich makes them pretty much faster than any other same priced regular desktop cpu.

With turbo boost, the processor looks for what is best for the application and adjusts itself for that. For example, a game that has no multiple core support, what i5 and i7 do is, shut all other cores off and boost the frequency of the active core. Awesome. Hell yeah. And for those who don’t know what Hyper Threading means, it’s when you have 4 cores, but by many complex ways they managed to run 2 threads at the same “time” on 1 core wich gives us 8 logic cores. Let’s say they have an 8 core processor, that makes 16 logic cores.
