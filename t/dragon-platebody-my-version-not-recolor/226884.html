system July 31, 2016, 1:03am #6

No one has a better converter, let me explain.

The model converter works perfectly.
You simply failed to read the thread and don’t understand the model format.
The converter converts one model format to another, thats all it does, and it does it perfectly. The only time it complains is when you try giving it lines, but why would you give it lines. It’s a triangles only format.

What your seeing in your model is your failure to provide it with triangle priorities, which you need to do with a hex editor. The converter can’t do this because every model is different, so the priorities will be different for each model. So you need to do it. Manually. With a hex editor.
You will have to load an original plate body into a hex editor to work out what priorities are needed.
Your also going to need RSMI to see what triangle skins and vertex skins are needed, you then need to hex edit your model for these, otherwise your model won’t animate correctly.

Sound complicated?, well you chose one of the hardest things to edit.
(It isn’t that hard when you get used to it.)

There isn’t a dedicated modeller to create RS models with, i doubt if one will be produced, so we are stuck with doing things the hard way.
(or cheating)

If your really bored maybe you could learn about, and then produce a general ‘map’ for, triangle priorities, triangle skins and vertex skins. You might gain some insight into why only basic tools were given with the converter. (dat spec given with models.zip).
I’ll produce an RSMI that shows triangle priorities as well.

It’s early days in custom modelling, we are all just starting to learn how the models work. Be patient. http://www.moparisthebest.com/smf/index.php/topic,288988.0.html

BTW Awsome model, best i’ve seen yet.
