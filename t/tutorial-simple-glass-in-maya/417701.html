I don’t know if some of you are using AutoDesk Maya, but nonetheless this could also be used in other 3D modelling software I guess.

Today we will be modelling a simple 3D Glass (You can also construct vazes using this method)

First off, Open Maya.

Before you even do anything, set your status at ‘Polygons’

Once you have done this, you need to press ‘Space’ on your keyboard.
Once you have pressed ‘Space’ this screen will pop up.

You will be using the left view from down, head over to it with your mouse, and simply press ‘Space’ again.

Then go to ‘Create’ and select the ‘CV Curve Tool’

Now just click on your grid and start making a rough sketch for a glass.

Now that we have this, we need to rotate the sketch to -90 degrees, do this by going to ‘Modify > Transformation Tools > Rotate Tool’ and rotate the sketch just as you would do in Metasequoia (by example)

You should come up with something like this :

Alright !
Now we have our sketch on the right angle of the grid, but it isn’t looking like a glass, is it?
We surely can’t make a glass out of this by just using this sketch, or can we?
Let’s find out.

Set your status up to ‘Surfaces’

Now go to ‘Edit’ and ‘Select all’ like so :

Look for the ‘Surfaces’ tab and click on it, then click on ‘Revolve’ like so :

Now you have the wireframe :

Hit ‘5’ to show your model with materials. Like so :

Would you look at that, you have just made a simple glass within 2-3minutes !
Thought that we couldn’t do it just by this method, did you?

I hope that this tutorial is usefull for those of you that find Metasequoia lacking in design or quality with models, AutoDesk Maya is a magnificent programme to model in, and by learning this method , you are in the beginning of getting the heck of it.
