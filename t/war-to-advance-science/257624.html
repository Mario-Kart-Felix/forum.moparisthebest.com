yakman July 31, 2016, 4:55am #1

history teaches us that war causes massive advances in science.

in the second world war for example, there were many areas of science that benefitted massively from the huge amounts of government spending into technologies that would help with the war.

after the war, most of these technologies can easily be adapted to piecetime.

for example, the British mathematican Alan Turing built the first computer in WWII to crack german codes, After the war, he simply rewired it and used it to try to solve the Riemann hypothesis, play chess and write love letters (true story)

after the second world war, the same technology used in nuclear weapons was used to build nuclear power stations.

i can think of many more examples, in almost all fields of science.

in war, governments spend 20x their normal budget on scientific research.
Also, in Britain at least, scientists do not get sent to battle, they are far too valuable as researchers.

i dont mean shitty little wars like iraq and afganistan, where there is no danger to the aggressors, and public opinion is against it.
the right kind of war needs to be a threat to the people of both sides, the whole country needs to feel like everyone is “doing their bit”. The world wars were exactly like this.

Is it right/ethical/moral to hope/pray for this kind of war?
think of the massive scientific advances it would produce.
