*A bank contains four dimes, seven nickels, and three quarters. Two coins are selected at random. Find eac h probability.

a. P(quarter and quarter) with replacing
b. P(dime then nickel) without replacing
c. P(dime and quarter) with replacing
d. P(quarter then quarter) without replacing

Please show me how you got the answer to a. and d. because those are the ones I am most confused with.

regecks1 July 30, 2016, 7:48pm #2

d. (3/14) * (2/13)

d. The probability for a quarter is 3/14. However, since there is no replacement, after we get the first quarter, there are only 2 quarters left out of a new total of 13 coins. So it becomes (3/14) * (2/13), you just multiply the probability of getting the quarter the first and second time.

Notice the use of “and” and “then”. And implies that order is not significant, but it is significant when the question specifies “then”. This should help you solve a.
