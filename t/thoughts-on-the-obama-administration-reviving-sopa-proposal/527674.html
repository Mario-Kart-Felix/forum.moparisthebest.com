Almost everything about your post is incorrect. What they mean by unauthorized streaming, is that you can’t stream content that you do not own/not authorized by the owner. This is ALREADY the law. So if you make a tutorial for something and post it on youtube, that is just fine because you own it.

Youtube relocating servers to countries without copyright laws will never happen. youtube is estimated to have between 50,000 to 1,000,000 servers. They can function and profit better by following the laws. Nothing will change besides the fact that law enforcement will be able to remove copyright content quicker and easier than before and block americans from connecting to whichever sites they don’t like.

.eu is just the domain name and has nothing to do with where servers are located.

So as I said your concern about not being able to stream unauthorized content is already a real law. This bill is basically going to force websites to be responsible for their users and what their users post/upload. Law enforcement will be able to shut down access to any site violating copyright laws and also ban them from advertising networks.
