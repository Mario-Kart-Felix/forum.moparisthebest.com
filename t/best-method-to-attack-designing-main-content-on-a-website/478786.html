Ruby_Forums_Coder August 4, 2016, 2:15am #2

I do it depending on what the website’s main function is, for example: RuneRebels. They’re a runescape private server and a very important aspect for their players is the announcements. Therefore I listed their two latest announcements from the database using the SMF SSI’s boardNews() function. This gets across the main information. I then took advantage of a 3 column layout below that. This then gets across the three secondary points of their server: how to play, communicating through the IRC and donations. They wouldn’t have any players if they didn’t know how to play. Since it’s a remake there’s no yell command or what not so communicating through the forums can take time, so I gave them information on the IRC channel/applet and then without the voluntary donors we wouldn’t have anything to pay for the hosting costs besides AdSense so that’s another main part, without hosting we’d not have a server for the player base.

However, I also often put an image slider just below the navbar followed by a three column layout and sometimes with thumbnails on the columns, depending on what flows. That’s the main way I look at it.
