The_Web_Guy August 4, 2016, 12:58am #32

Sorry but this is a horrible tutorial, uTorrent isn’t the best way to port-forward something, and I would highly suggest not doing so. See, with uTorrent the connection is like so,
Client > ISP > Your Network > uTorrent > Your Service
There are other steps in between, but that’s the bulk idea of it, if you were to port-forward through your router, it would be…
Client > ISP > Your Network > Your Service
Making a much faster, and probably a more secure connection from client to service.

Same thing goes for no-ip, I’m not sure who started the belief that to run a Runescape private server you need to set up no-ip. All this does is create an extra step for the client to connect to, meaning that if you have no-ip set up the connection would be,
Client > No-Ip > ISP > Your Network > Your Service
Just like uTorrent, eliminating no-ip and having people connect directly to your external Ip Address, will make the connection stronger. The only use I can see with no-ip is to disguise your IP from hackers, which doesn’t do anything at all. Even a basic hacker knows to simply go into command prompt and type, ‘ping noipaddress.no-ip.org’ giving the IP Address hidden behind the no-ip address.

Also, another thing is that you wouldn’t change the Ip in your source files, as you side. You’ll want to change the IP client-sided so that others connect to the correct IP.
