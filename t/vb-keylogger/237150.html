Hookless keyloggers, that is, Keyloggers that use GetAsyncKeyState / GetKeyState / GetKeyboardState, do have some advantages over Hooking keyloggers, such as being harder to detect, and often more reliable and easier to program without using DLLs.

zstars July 31, 2016, 2:29am #17

Most AVs don’t really have proactive protection so they have a hard time detecting keyloggers (assuming they are not in their current malware list). Those that do will usually detect, to some extent, both hooking and hookless techniques. However, there are more programs which legitly need or at least can use a call to GetAsyncKeyState (such as for hotkeys) than programs that need to put a global keyboard hook, so there’s a good chance the hookless one is only “suspicious”.

Only indetectable keyloggers are really hardware ones, and I suppose kernel-level ones which directly hook the keyboard are probably hard to detect too.
