Radeon:

I know if they block websites you can get around that using a vpn… But can a vpn help prevent them from recording your browsing history?

Well you’d connect to the VPN using encryption, the VPN would send all of the HTTP requests and then return what the server sends back to you over the encrypted connection. So the ISP is only privy to an encrypted exchange of data.

In my opinion, there’s no way that this approach to crime fighting can ever work. Sure, the government might catch a few dumb-dumbs before everyone manages to smarten up, but the worst criminals are all too aware of the climate they live in and they’ll become even more paranoid and take every protective measure under the sun – something they may not do already.
People get sloppy when they don’t necessarily know that they’re in somebody’s sights. But if you know you’re under surveillance then you’re going to work damn hard to cover your tracks.
Bottom line, it’s going to make policing tougher, not easier.
