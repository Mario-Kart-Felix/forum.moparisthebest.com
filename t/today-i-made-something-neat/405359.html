No because the top of the structure is where the mobs spawn, with this the top levels are pitch black and the mobs drop down into the water (without the water there they would die, optional etc…).

See:

I’ve made them all drop into the room with the water because when pistons are added I’m going to create a control room where I can choose to let lava into the chamber, and change the water level, or even potentially crush them. You can even close the gap where they fall with pistons, the possibilities are endless!

A much funner way of collecting your drops, watching 30+ mobs in a small room suffer at your hands by pressing a button. None of them spawn in the bottom room, the glass was just if I ever needed to kill them all if it went wrong, I could remove the cobble from the outside manually or with pistons, kind of like a failsafe because if something went wrong I’d be screwed with all those mobs coming

The one myself and Evil_ created only has 3 levels and is already capable of filling that room to the absolute rims. You could probably get a good 10+ levels, a common mistake people make is by making their levels really wide, it takes the mobs a LONG time to find the bit where they drop down, having a lots of small levels is a lot more efficient in my view.

You can then do it multiple times to take advantage of the land mass, (excuse my horrid drawings =/)
