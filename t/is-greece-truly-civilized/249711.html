SomethingToHide:

No I did read, I was referring to when you were saying something along the lines of a civilized person would never…

Oh I see.

A civilized person is someone who thinks before he does something. In this case, the reaction (riot/plundering) has nothing to do with the action (death of the kid). A civilized person keeps thinks about what he is going to do. If his actions are truly justified, he does it.

An example: A serial-killer is arrested. He admits that he is guilty. Someone from the crowd attacks him, and punches him so hard the serial-killer gets permanent brain damage. The puncher probably gets arrested, but his actions are justified. He might be sentenced to a few months in jail, but it doesn’t matter. He isn’t a great example of a civilized person, but at least his reaction was justified.

Now another example: The same serial-killer gets arrested. Another person thinks that now the serial-killer is arrested, he can break into a shop. He is arrested, and his actions are not justified. He certainly is not civilized.

The last example seems quite far fetched, but it isn’t. The action: the killing, and the reaction: burglary, have nothing to do with each other. But the latter is a reaction to the first.

In the Greece situation it’s the same: The action, the death of the 15 year old, has nothing to do with plundering and rioting. A civilized person knows that, the reaction is not justified by the action.

Now to answer your question in short: The reactions of civilized people are justified by the action.
