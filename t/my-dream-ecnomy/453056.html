I have been attempting to design the greatest economy for over a year now. The #1 challenge I have always stumbled across and what I do have to give kudos to Jagex somewhat battling is the factor that items just become too populated.

Before I begin with what I have come up with to battle this let me go into some detail in what I call “a real economy”. I have suggested this on several servers and well quite frankly I am surprised there aren’t more spawn servers, considering all the servers I have seen players are point blank - LAZY.

I can understand that this is a server, and nobody want’s to have to gain stuff like on Runescape. But in reality it’s the one and only true way of keeping an economy somewhat stable.

So let me explain…

I believe an economy is stabilized by low to somewhat medium level armor etc in the stores, limited number of runes in the store, no skiller resources such as ores in the stores.

A real economy depends on the players to make the resources to sell to each other. A mage buys runes off of the grand exchange or whatever the trading is(forums etc )

Furthermore, a real ecnomy depends on not only npc drops, not only skiller drops, but a random drop table or in what I consider my version of “slayer rewards.” These rewards would be like for example nosepeg may be a drop from spectres, and you can get the pieces to make the slayer helmet.

Another part of an economy is just like jagex, they make new models and bring them into the game. Well many of us are not modelers like the wanderer and such so what I designed were to make what I call “rotating shops”. At random times between once a week to once every two months one or two stores would have an entire different stock or it would have for example a magic robe shop with low level stuff may have blue wizard robes one week, the next it will have the black robes.

Now, when I ran my server I had what I called “gold or legend items” these were actually trim products. These items were only put into a store for perhaps an hour or two once a week. There would be no notification of it being added etc. The item would be essentially a money drainer from the economy. Meaning, the item will cost a fair bit. I had it where about 200-300m was an average money gain for about 3-4 days work on my server. The trim item would cost around 165-175m if not a little more. You make it so players do keep a little gold but you hit them hard.

Another good idea is to give regular items sometimes a small bonus or an odd effect that isn’t in runescape. For example, I made it so the different dragon masks would have a stat bonus depending on the color mask. For example red mask - 15% extra chance of better accuracy while mask was equipped. The masks were gained similar to the shop gold/legend item except instead of a store it was once a week for about half a day it would be a random drop from a random monster. Sometimes I would give a hint sometimes I wouldn’t. The drop needs to be VERY rare.

Now, let’s continue on to “how do we efficiently drain the economy without upsetting the players” I have always found by making one item discontinued for let’s say six months - to a year if the server lasts that long that is, you replace it with another item that can be gained in the similar fashion it was originally obtained.

I don’t claim for this tactic to be perfect, nor do I claim this is the best economy. However, I can say with confidence that if you start this economy and stay solid with it. Players will eventually join, and players will go by the economy. It makes merching actually happen on a server.

I hope this long topic has helped you understand how to design a proper economy. If you have any questions feel free to ask. If you have any comments or changes that you believe would be beneficial feel free to comment on that as well.

The dullest factor is simply making players earn their items. It shouldn’t be necessary to add temporary items; however, the biggest thing that affects an economy is the amount of players a server maintains on a daily basis. If you are a server with around 50 players, maybe you should consider having a higher drop rate, but if you have a server that is more populated it would only seem logical to decrease the rate.

There are tons of factors that affect the economy, but the only piece of advice I can give is to: limit item accessibility, creating an ideal drop rate for the community, and ignoring complaints about the economy. If you give in to one person, you will most likely give in to others. Also, if the majority of your players are complaining, you might want to either: A) reconsider your rates, or B) getting more active players.
