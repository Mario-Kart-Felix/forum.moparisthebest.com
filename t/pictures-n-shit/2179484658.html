jme July 31, 2016, 12:05am #22

Well I know a lot about photography…

I think this is your best:
http://j0nny0000.deviantart.com/art/Evening-Wind-90882010
There’s a few reasons why. The rule of thirds is used in several ways. When your subject is in the dead center, it tends to be boring. Notice how the wind turbines are to the right third of the picture… And also the same is with your horizon. When the horizon is in the dead center, it’s just less flattering and doesn’t look as good. In this picture your horizon is in the lower third. This puts great emphasis on the sky and gives it a sense of freedom. This would be better if you had some more clouds, especially dramatic clouds. I’m only into black and white photography though, so even without clouds it does look nice being able to see the gradients of color in the sky. The sun is also in the left third. Typically you want to avoid lens flare but if it’s on purpose you can have some nice effects from it. I see a lot of elements and principles of design, and your composition is great. Now there kind of is a problem with exposure with it, but the silhouette does good with this imo. Because the sun is infront of you, it will trick your cameras light meter into thinking there is more light then what there really is. If you wanted to show your wind turbines and the landscape instead of the silhouette, you would need to let in more light. I don’t know if you have a manual camera though.

There’s a million rules you can follow in photography though, and the best pictures are usually the ones that break the rules.

But yeah all of these are basically snapshots. If your school offers photography courses, you should take them.
