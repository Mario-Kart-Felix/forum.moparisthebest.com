R.I.P_RSC July 31, 2016, 4:47am #14

Stealing in the digital world isn’t as bad because you don’t steal something physical, or not something that can become physical. So hacking someones bank account is just as bad as stealing someones money, because the money on your bank account can be made physical. But other then money, digital stuff can’t be made physical.

You type a poem on your laptop, and someone hacks you and copy’s your poem. You don’t lose anything. You still have the poem, but now someone else has it too.

The same applies to you writing down a poem on paper. Someone comes into your office, and writes your poem on another piece of paper that he brought with him, with a pen of his own (just to make sure he won’t be accused of stealing some ink or a piece of paper). You don’t lose anything.

It’s called copyright infringement.
