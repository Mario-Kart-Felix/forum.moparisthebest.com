davidi2 August 1, 2016, 2:02am #14

I feel like I’ve seen this type of thread before…

But anyway:

I started out a few years ago, a nerd with no knowledge of RS hacking at all. I didn’t even know it existed. Then one day, my neighbor (3 years younger than I, but we have a lot in common) showed me the MoparScape client, and how he was playing by himself, and he could spawn whatever items he wants.
tl;dr - My best friend neighbor introduced me to private servers.

I was intrigued. I started perusing over the forums, reading random tutorials and the like. I had never programmed before, besides some Visual Studio Visual Basic stuff (ps vb is shit), so this new Language was very foreign to me. I played one server for about a day, then decided that I wanted to make my own. (I don’t enjoy playing them nearly as much as I do putting work into them)
tl;dr - Java was new to me, but I decided I wanted to make my own server.

I downloaded a base (I don’t remember which) and tried to get it to work. I, like many others, had not port forwarded correctly. It was a week until I figured out that was the problem (not that I spent a lot of time on it during that week) When I finally got it to work, I had 20 players within the first day (ahh the times before golden servers) and we had a blast. It was called DavidScape 317. I was still very new to Java, so I considered almost anything I did a good accomplishment. I changed the hay bale defense training to a portal, and we had a party to celebrate that. I remember my main update was a beer keg that you clicked, and it would give you a beer. Then you could drink it and you would walk funny for a while.
tl;dr - I started hosting DavidScape 317 (my first server) after some portforwarding troubles. There were plenty of players, but I still sucked at Java and my updates were pretty bad.

After hosting this server for a while, I discovered that there were more than one type of Private Server. I was hosting a 317, but there were more types! I started reading up on what they were, and immediately decided to switch to a 508. I gave my players a few days to download the new client, but I still lost about half of my playerbase on the switch. (If I knew as much as I do know, I would have written something to switch for them) The base I chose was Bulby, a run off of Palidino. This was probably the biggest mistake of my private server career. I did not know the stability flaws of Bulby at the time, and had I chosen a different base, DavidScape would probably have been a lot more popular than it turned out to be.
tl;dr - After a while, I switched to Bulby, and renamed my server DavidScape 508. I deeply regret that base choice because of its instability.

I started releasing my bases, even though at first they sucked. After taking a break and learning some real Java, I look back at even my latest DavidScape and I can see how unclean the code is. Over the months, I slowly started to fix some of the major problems of my unstable base. I fixed player resetting, and a lot of the connection issues. I even started adding good real updates that had never been seen in a RSPS to date.
tl;dr - I slowly improved my sources, and started releasing them.

Eventually, a few months ago, I shut down the hosted DavidScape 508. The player count was dwindling, and with the new status page, the chances of me ever getting a good number of players was low to none. I started learning more Java, and making programs not related to RSPS at all. It was fun. Now I’m getting ready to officially revive DavidScape, because I found one of the main stability issues. Hopefully this will allow me to keep a higher uptime, and use the status page properly. And this brings us to the present, where I am now hosting and administrating the official MoparScape Minecraft server.
tl;dr - I shut down DavidScape because the new Status Page was killing my player base. I moved further into Java for a few months, and now I am preparing to relaunch DavidScape (because it is more stable now) Right now, I am hosting the MoparScape Minecraft server.

Thank you for reading the Davidi2 autobiography.
