I have a small underwater turbine rig me and my brother built (we’re both trained in mechanical engineering) to harness the power of the river running through my garden, it works a treat. We also have 4 wind turbines down the bottom of the garden, which were built by us also. His main base is chemical engineering whereas mines is environmental engineering; both require mechanical engineering skills. The windturbines don’t really churn out much, maybe .2/.3 of an amp. Which for a home-made wind turbine is pretty good, since the motors aren’t actually made for that. The only way of which we could make the motors we used work better is to add a gearing system to them, but that’s just not worth it. The underwater turbine however churns out between 2 and 3 amps, depending on the current of the water, which is extremely good for home-made equipment.

It doesn’t even take all that much of an engineering knowledge to create any of those, the only bit that really tried us was optimizing the underwater turbine, other than that it was more or less plain-sailing. Anyone who’s studied Electrical and Mechanical engineering for about 3 months would be able to do it. It’s also great fun.

We have a geothermal pump in our lake house, it was 110% worth the money to have it fitted, definitely recommend it to anyone who looks for engineering solutions.
