I know where you’re coming from, but windfarms at sea are much harder to erect and much harder to maintain. I’m part of a maintenance team which is a seven man outfit, because I’m at university through my job, the days I have off, they have me working. I’m still only an apprentice just going into my second year, but I do work in a team, when I work. I’m more on the mechanical side of things than anything else, in our team there’s 2 mechanical engineers, 2 electrical engineers, a labourer, the team leader and a fibre optics engineer. To train all of us offshore, that costs around two thousand british pounds, so two thousand times 7, you get 14000 pounds, just for that one team. You then have to pay us, the majority of my team is on 60+k per annum, the team leader is double that. You then have to buy a helicopter, or at least lease it from a company. You then have to hire a helicopter pilot, or employ one. You then have to get a team that erects wind turbines, train all of them for off shore work. You then have to rent huge boats to carry the parts out to see. You then have to get sea cranes and operators plus crews for the boats.

The list is truly endless.

There’s a famous saying “When you have a boat, throw in another thousand!” well “When you have a wind farm, throw in another ten thousand!” These things are extremely expensive and there’s nothing we can do about that, the government only allocates so much money for them and the rest is paid for by private investors. They are a great thing to have, and they will make the money back in the long run, but what I’m trying to say is, it’s not just like you erect them and leave them there. We have to perform maintenance checks every 4 weeks at least because if a 100metre blade comes flying off, things are going to get ugly.
