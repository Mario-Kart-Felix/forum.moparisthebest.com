mod_taharok August 3, 2016, 5:17pm #2

I’ve done a few school term papers on certain types of renewable energies. There are a lot more solutions out there than most people realize. For example, my personal favorite is algal oil (that is, oil derived from cultures of specific species of algae). Algal oil is immensely fascinating. They are able to produce it in large quantities and in short time periods (don’t quote me, but I think they can gather something like 70% of the oil from the cultures every day before starting a new culture). What’s more fascinating is, the phosolipids from the algae form the same hydrocarbon structure as petroleum, which means that algal oil can act as crude oil in the same refinement infrastructure that we currently have setup for petroleum.

For those unaware, because we would be producing oil from algae, such an energy source is considered carbon neutral, meaning that we are neither adding nor subtracting carbon from the atmosphere while using that method. The minuses is that the algal farms take a lot of upkeep and equipment and, of course, a ton of land. Nevertheless, the production of a barrel of algal oil is drawing pretty close to that of a barrel of petroleum. Since petrol is going up and algal is going down, I’m pretty sure which one will win within the next few years.

Now, the reason I’m a big fan of algal oil is because it allows us to stop increasing greenhouse gases from petroleum consumption while we work on even more efficient solutions (such as cars that get more miles per gallon) or using nuclear power plants instead of coal. Hell, we could even burn switch grass, which is one of the fastest growing plants that we’re aware of (it’s a good candidate for replacing corn in the production of ethanol).

There’s definitely plenty to consider.

@Ruby: my secondary solution would be pure hydrogen vehicles. Hydrogen vehicles are generally built to be safer than a lot of modern gasoline vehicles (where they have a safety valve to expel the hydrogen if the tank is ever ruptured, plus the tank that holds the hydrogen can be dropped from like 8 stories and be perfectly fine). Hydrogen-electric powered cars allow people to drive for thousands of miles on a single tank of hydrogen. Beyond that, if you still wanted to use gasoline, there are catalyst systems that convert gasoline directly to hydrogen without using combustion. Combine that with algal oil and we would bypass the need for a super safe hydrogen tank and reduce the amount of oil consumption, even if it is renewable and carbon neutral.
