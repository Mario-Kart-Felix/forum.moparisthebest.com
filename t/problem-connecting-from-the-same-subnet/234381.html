@ Olzax:

IT DOESN’T WORK LIKE THAT…TRUST ME
Plus you don’t have to port forward for you household computers to join…

This happened to me…

First of all, your other computers cant use 127.0.0.1, 0.0.0.0 or leave it blank because their computer isn’t running the server. Next you need to find YOUR local ip address for them to connect to. One way to do this is to open the start menu, press run and type “cmd” without the " 's when you cmd is open, type ipconfig. It should say:

Local Area Connection: IP Address . . . . . . . ###.###.#.##

use that ip adress for the other computers to log into your server…

If it still does not work continue reading…

If you have any security program blocking/disallowing your other computers to connect to your computer disable them or make it so they can connect by allowing them to…
