Heres a few resources that may tip you over the egde if your interested in building your own machine.

http://www.davesite.com/computers/system/checklist.shtml (old but the list is good).

http://www.pcmech.com/byopc/ (pretty comprehensive from scratch guide)

http://www.build-your-own-computers.com/ (alternative to the above)

A couple of tips:

  1. Be sure to check for compatibility between the specifics of your parts. For example, if your buying an IDE Cd or hard drive ensure your motherboard comes with IDE and Sata. If your CPU FSB(front side bus) is lower then your motherboards supported FSB then it will clock the cpu down to a slower fsb and cause potential system instability.

  2. Motherboard mounts are important. A motherboard mount is a small mount you put on top of the screw between the motherboard and the case. Not installing them before turning the computer on will usually give you a nice motherboard shaped frisbee.

I assume your going to use Newegg? One particularly good thing about newegg is how helpful they are when it comes to products arriving dead on arrival (god forbid).
