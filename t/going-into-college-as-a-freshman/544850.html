Tree August 4, 2016, 1:25pm #9

Treat it like a full time job.

  • Get to know fellow students.
  • Put in 10 hours per unit. For you, 40 hours a week should be fine, including lectures and labs/tutorials.
  • Finish an assignment completely and don’t swap from one to another, without having completed what you first started on.
  • Get passionate and research everything.
  • Start assignments early and read up before classes.
  • Don’t skip lectures. They’re important.

Elrick August 4, 2016, 1:26pm #20

Oh gosh O_O who does 3 session courses!??? I couldn’t stand doing those. I’d rather do 3 hour courses than that. And Casey is right, Friday courses suck. What I did was take MW or TR classes and kept my friday free. There is seriously nothing like a 3 day weekend when you’re in college. With a whole free day you can focus on gaming, hanging out, or simply finishing all that homework before your weekend truly starts.

As far as difficulty goes, it depends on what type of school you go to. I went to a state university and was easy. Shoot, I’m in a Masters program and it’s still easy. Most important thing to do is give yourself enough time to get your shit done so you don’t get stressed. Also, find out your studying habits. If you never studied in high school, you better figure out the best way for you to study real quick. For me it was flash cards. I am tactile and memory focused. Try to create associations in your head about what is related to what. An example of this was the ICD9 code for Anxiety is 300.2 in the movie 300 they were forced to fight off an entire army, so they must have been anxious. And you know what? That actually stuck with me and I remembered it on the test.

In short, save your Fridays, prepare for the worst and hope for the best, don’t slack or you’ll regret it, and figure out how you can best study. Oh, and don’t be afraid to use school’s services. Using the writing center or the math center is going to get you a much better grade than struggling for hours trying to get shit done.
