Right, no one likes someone who tries to be someone else, but, charismatic people do have traits in common. I think there’s a line between outright copying the way someone acts, and sharing a goal someone achieves by acting that way. Bear with me now.

Example:

Jesse says “woah” when something interesting happens, and his goal is (to show he is interested).

Nathan acts like other people, and thinks Jesse is cool, so when something interesting happens, he also starts saying “woah”.

Now Aaron, shares the same goal as Jesse (to show he is interested) and he raises his eyebrows when something interesting happens. He achieves the same thing as Jesse, but isn’t acting like someone else.

Now replace (showing interest) with a charismatic trait.

See where I’m getting at?
