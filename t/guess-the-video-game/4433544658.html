Somewhat off topic but it relates to obscure games…

I used to play a puzzle game with my dad (it was one player, but we both enjoyed playing and watching each other) that I can’t remember the name of, and it’s been bothering me all day.

It was 2D, in a grid based layout (like 3x3-5x5 or something) with score and such as well. The point of the game was for each player (you vs computer) to ‘dig’ out one square at a time, collecting ‘artifacts’ (worth points), special items (like glasses that let you see what was under a square), or bad items (there was one that closed the ‘mineshaft’ exit, which meant you had to find another item to clear it, and then there was a bomb that ended that round and all points were lost). If the mineshaft exit was open, you could leave on your turn and ‘bank’ any points you had collected from artefacts. If it was closed you had to find the way out before you could leave. You could also defuse the bomb using the special water bucket item, so if you found it and used it properly after the other person left the mineshaft you could collect all the rest of the items without worry.

Kudos to anyone that knows the name!
