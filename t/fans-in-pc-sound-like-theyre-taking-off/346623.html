Firstly, what processor do you have?
Secondly, what socket do you have?
Thirdly, you should reapply some thermal paste to the cpu and then, more importantly, you should make sure the heat sink is tightly secured to the cpu.

I personally was a bit bored so I decided to take my processor out for lulz but after I didn’t screw my heat sink back in properly and had the exact same problem. (temps exceeded 65C, fan was constantly at 5000rpm). I then simply screwed the screws tighter and everything was fine.

ps, 62 is safe but is still hot.

Ollie July 31, 2016, 5:27pm #5

check in the bios they are not set to max, I thought my processor was overheating all the time because it was running ridiculously fast all the time, whilst being extremely loud at the same time. All I did was go into the bios and set my fan speed to auto-configuration based on the CPU temp, it found its max and min speeds and chose what would be best. I have the P5Q Deluxe motherboard, but the option should be available on most motherboards. If not use SpeedFan.
