0/10.

Images took a while to load, probably because their format is bmp. Web development is about fast, clean and simple webpages, you don’t need an over elaborate website with highres images, and having the wrong format images can make it slower as well, png or even a highres jpg would be better. As well as that, bright red and blue hurt the eyes, you got blue links on a red background as well, and you didn’t do a good job on the graphics.

Main page:
Images need more work.

And finally, use Notepad++ or something to make a website, don’t use an “automatic generator”, or a “WYSIWYG(What You See Is What You Get)” editor. They won’t display the same on different browsers/operating systems, or different resolutions, and by the time you’ve fiddled with it enough so it works on all 3 combinations, you could have done it in a text editor and have 100% cleaner code that would work properly first time, if not, after a few tweaks. On top of that, maintenance and upgrading would be easier, and you’ll save time in the long run.

As for images, use gif, jpg or png, never use anything else. bmp may look better than jpg, but png is another lossless format, the only downside is transparent pngs won’t display correctly in Internet Explorer.
