ilyaostr9 March 4, 2019, 3:07am #3

Errm, not quite. Verizon and CDMA and Cingular (AT&T) is GSM. You’d unlock a Verizon phone so it could use Nextel or Sprint, and you’d unlock a T-Mobile/Alltel/Rogers/AT&T phone to use another carrier on the same channel-space.

Unlocking the phone just means that you are removing the SIM card block that T-Mobile placed on your phone, so that you’ll be able to use other GSM carriers (The Sidekick is a phone that operates on the GSM protocol on GSM frequencies)
I don’t recommend that you do it, though, as your phone will lose the ability yo do a lot of things, such as email, etc. Those are stored on Danger’s servers (Danger Inc. made the Sidekick), and, if I recall correctly, you’d need a SIM card from T-Mobile for your phone to access the servers. Just get an unlocked Motorola phone, or buy one and unlock it yourself (it’s really easy)

Hopefully that helps.
