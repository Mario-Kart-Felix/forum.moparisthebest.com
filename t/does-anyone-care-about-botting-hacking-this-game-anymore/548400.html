One way would be to tell the user to do the task legit for 20-30 minutes, generate parameters based on what you have recorded from them [clicks, delays, etc], then perform that task. I’d hazard to guess that this is banproof assuming the bot itself isn’t detected/somebody in-game doesn’t get you banned.

Some things which get bots banned:
Obscure mouse movements - random zigzagging in an attempt to simulate whatever the fudge these dumbasses think they are. Make mouse movements less circular, they should be streamlined with a couple of points of deviation from the main course (obviously not a damn straight line).
Stop moving the mouse off screen to simulate AFK behaviour, by using all 4 corners of the fudgeing applet - what does that even show? Do you really run rs like this?

Simulate afk behaviour by moving towards one side of the screen only (preferably left or right, i’d say go for right since most people are right-handed and if they’re using a split-screen their other content probably tends to the right? - may be the inverse)

Botting for 3 days in a row at the same place
If when performing a task you aren’t with the ‘crowd’, ie if when cooking this scenario occurs, you can safely assume person a is botting - clearly path finding is at work here or social autism - easier to spot outsiders & some may notice your bot is breaking (if you stand around still for too long)
