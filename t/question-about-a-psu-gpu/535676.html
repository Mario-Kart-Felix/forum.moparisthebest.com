You’ll be fine, but it’s not recommended for mid-high level range cards. The GPU should’ve came with adapters in the box from what I read on their website. Just be sure you can supply enough power, at least 12A on rail.

tl;dr it’ll work, but i recommend a better PSU that’s 80+ bronze certified along with a higher wattage and more connector cables.

Kidpaparoach August 4, 2016, 11:03am #4

You’ll be cutting it close to the max power output of that psu (maybe it throttled for your uncle but he didn’t notice). It’s recommended you should get a psu that can provide 50 more watts of power than you need (capacitor aging etc leads to more electricity usage too). Rough estimate because I have no clue what other componuts you have or even what psu it is but I’d estimate you only have 20w to spare which isn’t a lot at all + you’ll probably hear a coil whine from your psu since it’ll be at like 95% usage while gaming (assuming your system isn’t loud as hell).

You can buy the cables online though. I’m sure if you contacted Rosewill and claimed they got damaged (in a way that isn’t your fault) if it was still under warranty they’d replace it for free. Should only cost a few $ online to buy the cables elsewhere if not from Rosewill. In all honesty it’d probably be better just to get a better psu which you can use in your next system as well. It’s up to you though, I don’t know your financial situation.

P.S. An adapter like that should work, they just get hot. I have used voltage step down adapters in the past no issues apart from their hot so you should probably avoid buying them from … random sellers online because if their not constructed to a high quality they might catch fire etc.
