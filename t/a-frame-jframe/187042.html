I initialized a JFrame with its painting methods overwritten. Inside of them, I filled a rectangle to cover the entire canvas (white with about 30% transparency) and it worked. The problem was, if you move the frame you would see what ever was underneath it when it was first initialized. If you minimize the frame and maxmize it, it does the same thing- you can see through it to whatever is underneath it. So, I added a component listener to listen for when the frame was moved. Whenever it was moved I would repaint the frame. The problem with this was, if you fill a rect with partial transparency over a rectangle with the semi transparency then it will just become more opaque. Thus, the only way to ‘clear’ the canvas of the JFrame was to minimize and then maximize it again. Transparency is possible, its just you cannot make it constant.
