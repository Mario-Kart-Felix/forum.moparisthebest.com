Frell:

If I go into color management and remove the profile there the monitor tints yellow pretty hard, but I calibrated with the windows 7 thingy and got it to match decently but its too dark. And it seems like the gamma from ccc is wayy too blown out

The only thing I can suggest is try going to a friend/relatives house with the monitor, and see if it happens when plugged into their machine. If it does, then that indicates a problem with the monitor, and you can probably get a replacement from the manufacturer. If it works fine on their computers, then that might indicate a problem with either the video cable, or perhaps a problem with the graphics card itself.

There were two instances in which something similar happened to me. The first incident was with an ATI Radeon HD4890. It had a blue tint to it. I tried everything I could think of, and finally ended up plugging the card into my cousins machine, and his monitor had a blue tint. I sent it back to newegg, they sent me a replacement.

The second incident was with my current card; A Zotac GeForce GTX 460. It had more of a red tint, and I went through the same procedure as the ATI card, but it worked fine on my cousins machine. Turns out I had installed the drivers that came with the card, only to find out they hadn’t been updated since 2009. So I updated it with the official drivers, and the tint was gone.
