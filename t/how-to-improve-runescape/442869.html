Speljohan August 3, 2016, 2:47am #10

Some notes: Graphics being limited by running in a browser is the absolutely worst myth i know of in the world, because the game ISN’T running in a browser. It is still running on the same old hardware as any other hardware accelerated game, they just choose to render the canvas within a browser. In fact, the downloadable client doesn’t use any kind of webbrowser components at all, just a virtual machine packed into an application and rendered in a C++ Window.

The argument that they don’t give a fuck about community is obviously straight out bullshit. No community means no income, no income means no company. In fact, very few people actually complain about most updates, and this is why they aren’t retracted (the exception being removal of free trade/wild which proves that Jagex WILL adapt if a great portion of the community are upset).

The economy CANNOT be fixed without breaking it. People don’t seem to realize that RuneScape uses a Free Market economy, and given that there are few gold sinks the inevitable outcome is that items are devaluated linearly for an unlimited period of time, especially as new items that are better get released. The exception to this is Rares, which have a limited supply, and as such will always go up in price long-term.

Addressing some of Tutorial’s claims:

[ul][li]Drop rates? What do you mean? Make them more common or more rare?[/li]

[li]Common misconception there, i have seen plenty of games with far worse communities (Call of Duty, Any blizzard game including World of Warcraft to mention a few).[/li]

[li]They still run polls regularily just as they always have. It would just seem that people have stopped caring about them, and as such most people dont even know how to find them.[/li]

[li]Instancing for the big bosses is a horrible idea if you value the economy at all. It would pretty much ruin the prices at an even faster rate. For new bosses maybe, but for the current ones it would be atrocious. I think they should add a few bosses only available in free for all pvp areas, this would lead to the drops not dropping in value very quickly since most people would avoid them altogether (excluding the “brave” ones who actually dare challenge faith)[/li]

[li]Stop adding content? This is probably the absolutely worst thing they could EVER do. It would lead to stagnation, and as a result the majority of players would leave the game.[/li]

[li]About Mounts: You are correct in that WoW was late to the party for that. Ultima Online had it in 1996, and a far superior system to the gold sink crap that WoW uses. It wouldn’t work in RuneScape though, because it would lead to prices on crafted good crashing even further (see: Runecrafting). And if they eventually did add mounts, i would want a system where you tame your own mounts and make them part of the market economy altogether, where they will die if you die in combat etc.[/li]

[li]Stereotyping: What i find really interesting here is that people draw comparisons to WoW when RuneScape is probably the only MMO that actually DEVIATES IN EVERY SINGLE WAY from the WoW-formula (which isn’t really a WoW-formula either because they stole all concepts from Everquest and just polished them more).[/li][/ul]
