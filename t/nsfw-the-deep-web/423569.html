U try to access a website through the TOR-browser, the TOR network send the data through hundreds of TOR servers, everyone who uses the TOR browser is a part of the network, it dumps your data in form of cookies on all the computers the data has been send through.

It doesn’t have anything to do with cookies or dumping data on other computers (in fact, the best thing for it to do is probably to not leave any data on the computers, as it makes it harder to trace then.)

What it actually does is encrypt your data, bounce it around lots of other “relays”, and then sends it off to its intended destination how it normally would be over the Internet. It’s difficult to trace back because the relays it is bounced around are probably configured such that they don’t keep any records of the traffic, and they’re probably in different countries too - so it’d become difficult for law enforcement of one country to even get access to any logs that do exist, as laws differ widely for these sorts of things.

Interestingly enough, TOR was originally sponsored by some research branch of the US Navy.

And yes we get the point of the main post, people do illegal activities by using the anonymity something like TOR can provide e.g. exchanging child porn. Not something that anyone is interested in here I hope.
