Tree August 4, 2016, 1:36pm #7

It’s a gaming laptop so you have to remember that you’re giving up a fair amount of battery life. I don’t really see why this is such a problem for most people. Anywhere that I have taken my laptop, there has been a power source nearby. Hell, I have no problem rolling out an extension cord whenever I need to. It’s most likely going to get very heated and I suggest a cooling pad. Especially when gaming.

The 760M is a bit outdated now (off the top of my head) but it will play the simple things. If you’re looking to play LoL or something like DOTA, I doubt you’re going to have too many issues. If you do, just tone everything down. This doesn’t mean you should go installing Crysis 3 with ultra graphics and expect 60fps. No, that’s unrealistic. There’s plenty of places that you can check the performance of individual parts and I suggest you do so. I think a GeForce GTX 870M is suggested when buying a new gaming laptop so that it can run well for the next 2-3 years.

Depending on how you use your laptop, 16gb RAM will possibly be overkill. 16gb will carry you over the next few years so I suppose it provides a little more future proofing. It’s certainly not going to be too little right now. It shouldn’t give you any trouble. Most gaming mid-range gaming laptops offer 8gb of RAM at the moment, so that should give you some idea.

Downgrading is completely up to you. I’ve only gone in the upgrade direction, but I’m sure it’s fairly easy once you grab a key and google a tutorial or two. It seems as though the main use of your laptop will be forum browsing, reddit, youtube and perhaps some school work? For that, the laptop is overkill and I don’t suggest selling it because it does what you/will continue to for a long time. As for the stickers, most likely not. Who knows though. In the end, it’s up to the individual. If you’re implying that the laptop has a little bit of damage or is showing some age, then the value will decrease. Yes.

I’m not a hardware guy so take what I say with a grain of salt.
