Hi to all,

Semaphore (interesting name for a java coder), your story has eerily parallels to my own experience with Pplsuqbawlz back when we first developed scarscape. Keep in mind, of course, I did not know java at the time and neither did pplsuqbawlz.

First pplsuqbawlz contacted me about something concerning scar scripting. His basic problem was colour changes, he wanted to know if we could stop them. I remembered a post I had seen on rscheatnet (yes this was a year ago) about bytecode manipulation and decided to look into it.

What followed was a few weeks of what you might call research. Nearly immediately I had the idea of nulling all the Math.random calls by changing their multipliers to 0. That was the basic idea behind scarscape and always has been. We both did independent research into other possible features and how to get the modified client to load. Of course this is all very simple to me now but at the time we didn’t know what we were doing.

Eventually I managed to just reuse their (jagex’s) loader to load the new modified client. Then I wrote an automatic bytecode modifier (unfortunately it was writern in the only language I knew well at the time, visual basic. Meaning it relied on jfparse’s asm and disasm classes which are both slow and rather crappy) . Pplsuqbawlz kept promising to develop a loader and an updater but never managed it until 6 months later. So mine were used.

During the business aspects of scarscape I did most of the managerial work. We had decided to employ cheater’s services in securing hosting (as I had none at the time). We had a successful run, the client lasted about a month then cheater’s host pulled the plug in his server. Which it turns out he had not paid for.
