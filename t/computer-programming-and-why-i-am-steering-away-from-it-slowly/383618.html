Computer Programming and why I am steering away from it, slowly

General Discussion
z August 1, 2016, 7:45am #1

Throughout my last five years experience writing programs (mostly extensive PHP stuff and minor C++ now), I’ve noticed that there is a similar trend with every time I tend to program now – a lack of ideas. Because of this trend, I can speculate that computer programming will never be something that can keep me busy for a long period of time, as I thought so in the past. It seems to me as though computer programming is best to be something you learn over time and get better at, but never actually use on a daily basis, since that would be boring and ruin the fun of it. Because of this, I’ve changed the focus of my career towards Electrical Engineering, where it seems that I will learn a whole lot more and I can actually work with physical systems and soldering, etc. Thoughts on this?

MujaheD August 1, 2016, 7:45am #4

I changed my mind about CS simply because of the notion that most jobs in that sector are out-sourced. I decided on electrical engineering but I eventually moved to mechanical because it is far more practical. I imagine there are a lot more jobs in mechanical because mechanical apparatus need maintenance and exist all throughout our lives. Electrical systems require little or no maintenance, e.g if your graphics card is not working, you would normally buy a new one. Hence it is my belief that the majority of jobs in electrical are in design, that being a bad thing because not only does it limit the number of jobs, but it also means you would require a lot of experience prior to the job.

Just in case you’re still open to new ideas.
