When should police use lethal force?

General Discussion
JavaTC August 4, 2016, 6:00am #1

I saw a clip of a firefight here in Missouri today, and it made me question things.

A man who was in a car crash walked away from the accident unharmed, and the police tracked him to a nearby park a couple minutes later. When the officer got out of the car, the man randomly opened fire without even saying a word. The police shot him and he went down, basically bleeding out and was going to die if he wasn’t treated. The officers couldn’t help him, though, because he was still pointing the gun at them, ready to fire after they would come out of cover. He eventually died that night in the same exact spot, still holding the gun, the police still have weapons on him.

Now, it sounds like they did the right thing at first, right? They weren’t going to risk other lives to save one. But as it turned out, the man was under the influence of both alcohol and drugs. He didn’t have any other violations with the law beforehand, and was mentally stable, meaning his most defiantly wouldn’t do this if he wasn’t on the substances.

So my question is, should the officers have tried to disarm and save the man because he isn’t under control of his actions and thoughts, or did they do the right thing by letting him die?
