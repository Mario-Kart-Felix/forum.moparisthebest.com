yakman July 31, 2016, 1:00pm #17

A hidden assumption here goes something like this. “There are two waves, each containing energy.”

This is where you go wrong. If destructive interference causes complete cancellation of the wave phenomena, there are not two waves. There is no wave.

When there is no wave phenomena, there is no energy. It doesn’t matter if it is because of destructive interference or because no waves were created in the first place.

Remember this key point – if two waves completely cancel each other out, you do NOT have two waves. You have no wave.

hope that answers your question.
