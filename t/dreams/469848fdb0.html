When it happens, you should accept the fact that it’s a part of your sleep cycle, considering you go through it every 24 hours, and move your toes/fingers/facial muscles. If you want, you can turn your lucid dreams into amazing adventures. Most people who experience vivid lucid dreams for the first time claim it was the best experience that ever happened to them. Next time, do a reality check and look at the finer details of everything (especially your hands) that you wouldn’t naturally see in a dream. Get into the feel of being lucid and a new life will just begin.

If you get scared in a dream when you know for sure that you’re dreaming, just remind yourself that this is all taking place in your own mind. Your own organ you developed over the years, how can it scare you? Also, dreams only transition with same storylines because you think they were the same storyline, yet they were separate dreams you just don’t remember transitions of. Most of the dreams you remember are during REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep while your eyes move rapidly, hence the name. This is the state most lucid dreamers want to lucid dream in because REM dreams are much more vivid and last longer.
