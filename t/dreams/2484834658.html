A message I received from someone knowledgable in these things:

Lucid dreaming is a huge subject, and one where much rubbish proliferates. I will just say the following:

i. not all dreams are the same. Some are very important, some not important at all. The first step is to learn to differentiate between them.

ii. Most people seem to think that the goal in lucid dreaming is to somehow “control” what happens in the dream. If you succed in doing this, you will have transformed a dream into fantasy, and will have robbed it of any value.

iii. Reflect on the relationship between dream and memory. Also reflect on the relationship between the so called “real life” and memory. We have a faint memory of our dreams when we are awake, but do we have even the most faint memory of real life while we are dreaming? The establishment of a “dream memory” (that is a memory of real life while we are dreaming) is crucial to this process, without it no success. This means sleeping with a notebook and pencil nearby and writing your dreams the moment you wake up. This process may take many months before you achieve anything concrete.

iv. “dreaming” is not something that we do while we are asleep. Dreaming is a continuous process, that is happening constantly. It is just that when we are awake there are other things fighting for our attention.

This should get you going.

It means that you shouldn’t try and forcibly change your dream by thinking about something else. I have done this before when I became lucid and jumped into the air, flying all around. This may be fun for a few seconds but it interrupts the flow of the dream and makes it meaningless as you will most likely wake up shortly after or drift into a non-lucid dream, losing your awareness. If you can gain awareness of yourself without disturbing the flow of the dream, your dream will make much more sense and be more visually clear and easier to remain aware in. The hard part of lucid dreaming is to remain aware, as gaining the first split second of awareness is easily accomplished by most, but prolonged awareness is only attained by those with true control over their minds.

It’s like a game or a sport that requires more focus than you could imagine, but the rewards are high. Once full lucid dreaming is attained, one will have complete control over his or her will power - arguably one of the greatest and most challenging accomplishments a man can make in a single lifetime.
