In sleep paralysis, your body forbids your muscles from moving so that you don’t act as you would in your dreams, and stay still. Only parts such as facial muscles can move and your chest (to allow breathing). A simple way to knock out of sleep paralysis is to accept the effect and slowly move your toes and fingers. Move your facial muscles and flex your bigger muscle groups.
