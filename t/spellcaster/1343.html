ShadowSyntax July 29, 2016, 7:56pm #16

Iruleants’ script will work, although it isn’t too undetectable.
One of the easiest things you can do to make a script undetectable is to make where it clicks random using the random() function.

e.g.
movemousesmooth(740+random(3),188+random(3));

This would randomly pick one of however many numbers.
random(3) would pick one of three numbers (including zero)
so: 0,1,2

So in theory the command could move the mouse to:
740,188
742,189
741,190
740,189
etc.

To break down the commands more:
movemousesmooth(x,y); - Moves the mouse smoothly across the screen (without jumping around) to the co-ordinates x and y
clickmouse(x,y,true); - Clicks the mouse at x and y. To make the script less detectable move the mouse using movemousesmooth to where you want it to click before using this command. To make it even more detectible use an include (dont worry about that for now)
wait(k); - pauses the script for k amount of miliseconds (replace k with however many miliseconds you want the script to pause for). 1000 miliseconds = 1 second.
getcolor(x,y)=1773070 - finds what the colour is at the co-ordinates x and y and checks if the colour is 1773070.

The finding colour thing:
if findcolor(x,y,ppc,17,16,494,319)
The 4 numbers show the script which area of the screen to search in (I’m going to assume you already know how to find co-ordinates).
For example, to make it search for that colour in the inventory screen you would move your mouse over the top left corner of your inventory and get the x and y co-ordinates.
Then you would make the first two numbers of the command whatever the x and y co-ordinates you chose were (respectively).
Now move your mouse to the bottom right hand corner of your inventory and pick the co-ordinates. Replace the last two values of the findcolor command with the new x and y co-ordinates.
