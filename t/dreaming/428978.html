rip4d August 2, 2016, 3:15pm #8

I’m guessing you either watch TV or go on the computer or something immediately before bed (can be within 30 minutes of going to bed), right? That’s why you don’t remember dreams. Every night, you have multiple dreams. That’s why you’ll sometimes remember a dream go from one distinct instance to another. But back to the inability to remember, its caused by alpha brainwaves, which oscillate at ~30Hz. This state of mind generally occurs when you are focused on something strongly, such as a video game or the computer. With the high oscillation, you are unable to recall certain things, in this case your dreams. I can just about guarantee you that if you go an hour before bed using nothing electronic, you will at least partially remember your dream. That is because when you aren’t so heavily focused on something, your brainwaves are slower. When you are asleep and haven’t recently been in an alpha state, you should end up in a beta - theta state, where your brainwaves oscillate ~3Hz - 8Hz if I remember correctly. This is the state in which you are able to recall dreams best, and sometimes you do so well that you can even go lucid, where you take control of your dreams.

Hopefully that will help you understand why you couldn’t remember your dreams at all :rolleyes:
