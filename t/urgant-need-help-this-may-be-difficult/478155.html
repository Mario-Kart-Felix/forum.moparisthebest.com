First, you can extend the bandwidth (or allow more bandwith) by asking your service provider, which may often result in them wanting more money. Unless you have unlimited bandwidth but there are a lot of devices using your internet.

Secondly, No-ip is a 3rd party application that takes a numeric IP such as 8.23.224.90 and turns it into a textual representation such as skaarjpk.servegame.com. This thought is generally to protect from hackers though, I went into cmd and typed ‘ping skaarjpk.servegame.com’ and it displayed that it is now pinging the numeric IP (8.23.224.90). If skaarjpk.servegame.com is set to disguise the numeric IP ‘8.23.224.90’ and that’s not your IP, this could be the problem. I’d recommend getting rid of No-IP and instead of typing in skaarjpk.servegame.com, type in your IP to connect to (the 121.***.***.29) let me know if any of these suggestions worked.
