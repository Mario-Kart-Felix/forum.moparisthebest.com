amy_webb:

The problem with IRC is no new members are ever accepted because of the community that IRC attracts. The one time i logged onto IRC i wasn’t exactly welcomed with open arms, how do you expect newer members to join and fit in with that attitude? A chatbox would allow members to communicate and not be treated differently because they’re “new” to the community. IRC just proves that having a chat system separate to mopar isn’t beneficial, how do you expect to update IRC to fit standards when people are too intimidated to join?

The difference between IRC and a chatbox in this situation is time, IRC is merely an already established chatbox. Once you get members, moderators, regulars to the chatbox it will be no different, people will be flamed for asking inane questions that are answered in a dozen places etc. As for IRC being hostile, I won’t lie and say that #mopar smells like roses, but in most cases the newer folk that do get flamed are usually people who tend to act with a sense of self-entitlement which just doesn’t belong on IRC. Plenty of new people come and go on #mopar without incident, people just don’t hear about those cases because they aren’t there.
