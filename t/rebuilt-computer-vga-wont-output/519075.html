The_Un-Named August 4, 2016, 7:20am #4

Confirm that all the cables you are using work correctly, sometimes a cable may not work depending on a position that can create very specific problems such as this. Secondly, use a completely different monitor and cables if you can. If that doesn’t work, completely turn off the computer and remove ALL of the cables and then wait a few minutes, plug everything back in except power, then plug the power back in and turn it on. Either make sure the monitor is turned off before turning the pc on, or make sure the monitor is turned on before turning the pc on.

I have an old monitor that will display no signal unless the pc is turned on with the monitor turned off, even if I unplug it and plug it back in, I have to restart to solve the issue, it’s incredibly frustrating.

Additionally, if the motherboard has additional video slots try using them, or if you have a video card use that(the connector will be horizontal not vertical).
