whackatre August 4, 2016, 10:58am #2

i wouldn’t dare to call myself proficient, but anyway, here are my opinions on the dialects i’ve used, subjective or not:

cl: a pretty big and bulky language, and probably considered more ugly (the macros especially) by schemers. nevertheless, it’s quite powerful and there are great implementations (clisp and sbcl are the most common ones, and they’re both great). think the ‘C++ of the lisps’. a good read is Practical Common Lisp.

scheme: elegant; clean. it’s a lot more pure, because it was historically designed as a teaching language. like cl, there are tons of impls (chicken, gambit, etc. etc.), and you’ll spend a lot of time choosing one. racket, a scheme dialect that has deviated from scheme, has tons of great libs and is both a great teaching language and ‘real-world’ language. it’s pretty awesome. anyway, sicp is the definitive book for scheme. an alternate and unorthodox learning approach is the little schemer.

clojure: if you want a lisp that runs on the jvm and has all the familiar java std lib features, use clojure.

i usually use racket. regardless of whether your main goal is education or getting things done, all are applicable for the task.

also, you should check out paul graham’s links. visit #lisp on freenode. the people are pretty nice to talk to there and will help you out with matters even not related to cl.
