[size=15pt]Enkrona 2.6 For Your Information[/size]

Enkrona 2.5 Overview:
Enkrona 2.5 had horrible bugs and lag from the start. It had an area loading bug where people would not be where they really would be on the X and Y plane of the server map, which made it look like they were noclipping. Enkrona 2.5 had serious fighting bugs that would cause people to lag out, have the ability to dupe items (if done right), and, occasionally, crash the whole server. Enkrona 2.5 had NPC bugs, where you could attack the NPC, and walk away, and attack again, and it wouldn’t attack back. It had serious bugs with timers for fighting, and allowed you to “mass” while attacking, which means you click repeatedly and you would repeatedly do damage. Enkrona 2.5 was entertaining, but just a fine icing smothered over a piece of shit cake, the icing being the NPCs, the challenge, the entertainment.

Enkrona 2.6 Overview:
Although Enkrona 2.6 had a few combat bugs at first, all were fixed. Enkrona 2.6 now is coded by me while I know what I’m doing. In Enkrona 2.5, I was still learning Java. Enkrona 2.6 has perfected counters, and math. Fighting damage vs. stats are exactly perfected like real Runescape. That means a pure would actually work as a pure, and not be weak like the bullshit of Enkrona 2.6. Armor and Weapon bonuses actually work 100% well, instead of the weird math in Enkrona 2.5 that caused low defence armor to get hit exponentially higher then high defence armor. Basically, all the stability of combat and items in Runescape apply to Enkrona 2.6. I worked very hard to make the server stable, yet still fun. In Enkrona 2.6, you get more rewards from your skills, you don’t hit 30s at level 30 strength. You hit just like you would in real Runescape for your strength level. In other words, level 99 strength with a whip will hit in the mid 30s. Enkrona 2.6 is much more stable, and rewarding then Enkrona 2.5.

[b]A Tour of the Training Dungeon:
Island Enterance:
[i]Here you will enter the dungeon by climbing down the ladder. You will get to the island by clicking one of the portals in town.

Enterance.jpg769×557

Down the ladder
[i]Once you get down the ladder, there will be rats nearby. I highly recommend you train on the rats until you are at least level 40. The rats drop steel armor.

LadderEnterance.jpg770×556

To the fire giants
Head a bit South and you will reach the Fire Giant training area. The fire giants drop adamant armor, and are not easy to kill in a long-term manner.

FireGiants.jpg772×557

Side-Step to the Black Demon
Head a bit West from the rats, and you will reach the Black Demon. It drops rune armor. Be warned, it is not an easy kill.

blackdemon.jpg772×557

Zombies
A bit East from the Fire Giants is the Zombies. They drop mithril armor, and are a pretty easy kill.

zombies.jpg770×555

Vampires
Further East you will reach four vampires. They are a mid-range kill in difficulty. They drop amulets.

vamps.jpg773×559

Hounds from Hell
A bit South from the vampires lay the Hellhounds. They drop staffs, and are not an easy kill.

hells.jpg771×556

Around the corner
Around the corner from the Hellhounds are the Abyssal Demons. They are hard to kill, and drop abyssal whips, ancient staffs, and a few “unknown” items.

abbys.jpg770×556

Steel Dragons
Past the Abyssal Demons, and at the end of the Dungeon, are the Steel Dragons. They drop dragon armor and weapons, and are an incedibly hard kill.

steel.jpg772×556
