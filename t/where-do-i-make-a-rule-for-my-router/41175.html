Follow what he said, but go onto the Advanced section of your [/b]Routers configuration[/b]. There will be options like Port Forwarding, Virtual Server, Application Rules, etc…

If you are confused with the above post, and do not understand it…follow this.
Go to start > run > cmd > ipconfig
Then where it says Default Gateway - [/b]IP Address[/b] (192.168.0.1)
Remember that IP address and open your browser. Type the IP Address into the browser. For example, I would put 192.168.0.1 in the address bar and then press enter.
Something should come up.
It will ask for the username and password. The username and password are usually not assigned, unless a username is assigned, which you would then just press enter or continue to further on.
Once on the Routers Configuration page go onto the Advanced tab.
There will be a lot of options, and you can choose, which one best suites your needs.
