I’m sure you’d be happy to know that science will bring us immortality soon. For example, a scientist managed to make a yeast cell live for 10 weeks, when its usual life expectancy is only 6 days. This may not seem like a lot, but that is the equivalent of us living for 800 years. The real “secret” to longevity lies within our DNA structure. This science also repeated the experiment with mice and got the results of doubling the mice’s lifespan from 2 years to 4 years.

http://concen.org/forum/showthread.php?tid=31108
That thread is a bit old, but it talks about them going to try mice next.

So, what is to say they can’t get rid of these “aging DNA” in humans and make our lifespan even longer? In a sense, we are just speeding up evolution.

This scientist also has a theory as to what causes us to age. I found a magazine with an article on him… If you don’t feel like reading the whole entire thing just go to page 74 and 75 and read the bottom.

http://books.google.com/books?id=wgKpEb86UPIC&pg=PA70&lpg=PA70&dq=junk+in+lysosomes+may+cause+us+to+age&source=bl&ots=JbFjZZpMSn&sig=XGAD4ma4EuysbZbhbPd_YEdZkEg&hl=en&ei=51gzTq2WAuTgsQLr9bmQCw&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=1&ved=0CBUQ6AEwAA#v=onepage&q&f=false

Basically he states that our body accumulates “junk” and is broken down inside the cell. When the cell cannot break this “junk” down it goes to the lysosome.

Lysosomes are cellular organelles that contain acid hydrolase enzymes to break down waste materials and cellular debris.

These lysosomes are basically a super powered machine to get rid of junk, but sometimes these lysosomes cannot break down the junk. This junk then is stored inside of the lysosome and stays there forever. He is searching for a bacteria, or a sequence of genes which can break down this junk inside of the lysosome. So basically you would just go for a checkup, get the bacteria to get rid of your junk, and then be on your way. Maybe come back in 20 years again and repeat. I found that very interesting.
