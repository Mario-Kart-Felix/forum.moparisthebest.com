Time travel is split into two departments: past and future. Time goes hand in hand with space, because without space, there would be no time. This was the concept that helped Einstein develop general and specific relativity.

Theoretically speaking, traveling into the future is physically possible if one can travel faster than the speed of light (3x10^8 m/s). In other words, you would have to travel faster than 300,000,000 meters per second to break the time threshold and arrive in the future, whether it is arriving in a future of 1 ms, or 1 hr, or 1 day, or 1000 days. Time = Distant/speed, which means the farther you want to travel in the future, the FASTER (speed, m/s) you would have to travel. The fastest spaceship in the world right now cannot even travel a fraction of the speed of light. In addition, imagine the strain that would inflict on the human body at that rate of travel. It’s unimaginable.

And you may ask, what about traveling in the past? You would need to be more specific when asking this question. The correct phrase to ask would be, “what about traveling in the past in OUR UNIVERSE?” There are multiple theories that support the existence of multiple universes. Hence, if something cannot be disproved, we should not exclude it from influencing our educated opinion. Time traveling into the past may lead to an array of problems. We all understand that history makes the present and future. The butterfly effect dictates the cause and effect that the simplest change in the past can engender an entire new future. Therefore, if traveling in the past is possible, the ripple in time and space would be stupendously unstable.

To answer your question: “Is Time Travel ever going to be possible?”

Presently, I believe that nobody on earth can answer this question because our technology is not at that level of sophistication. As we progress into the future, I think this question will gradually become easier to answer. We’ve only begun to harness the power of the sun and other extraterrestrial power sources. Time travel is million of millions magnitude greater than the concept of solar and nuclear powers. However, what we do know is that even if there was a 0.00000000000000000000001% chance that time travel is possible, our existence would be in more danger than all the nuclear bombs in the world detonated at once. Time travel should not be for humans; only a God should wield such power.

Disclaimer: every word, every sentence, and every paragraph in my statement are based on facts and verified physics laws. I did not mention blackholes or wormholes because neither have been confirmed with any experiments or mathematical verification.
