I think it depends what kind of game you like playing. Some gamers prefer to play more realistic games. They enjoy them a lot more because maybe they understand what is happening and it can relate to something they know in real life. For example, in BattleField 2, you could easily relate this to a war between two sides. (And it is quite a realistic war if you take away respawns.)

Then, on the other hand, you have CoD. Don’t get me wrong I like a game of CoD but for completely different reasons. This game is not realistic and you cannot relate it to anything that really happens in real life. Snipers don’t go around quick scoping. People don’t do 360’s in Iraq. That would be stupid (maybe someone has done it, I don’t know.).

So these games, in theory, could be placed on entirely different platforms. As other people have said; Battlefield is a strategic game that often requires teamwork and this resembles what it would be like in a real war. Modern Warfare is much more of a fast paced FPS, where you are a computer character with a gun, not a marine fighting a war.
