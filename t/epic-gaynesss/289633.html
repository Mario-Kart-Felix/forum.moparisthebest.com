the_bank July 31, 2016, 9:22am #14

Antihistamines do it for me. Claritin 12 hour works the best for me, taken about an hour and a half before bed. 24 hour medications tend to be shit because they deliver such small dosages over the long period of time.

I’m allergic to grass, trees, horrible ragweed allergies and cats. I’m also deadly allergic to Ibuprofen. Claritin works for all of my seasonal symptoms, even ragweed.

Reactin worked for about a week for me, then stopped.

Aireus is great, and I use it when I don’t have any Claritin.

If you’re like me and also get horribly itchy eyes with your allergy symptoms, Visine is the best eye drops to get. They will heal the redness and numb the itch, without causing it to come back stronger later.

Don’t try nose sprays, they’ve always made it worse for me, and have too many side effects to risk their small benefit.

Dust the room you sleep in. Vacuum. CLEAN. You’re exposing your body to the air in your room as you sleep, which is exposed to everything else in your room. Keep it clean and free of allergens. Keep windows closed, and use air conditioning. Avoid going outside when your symptoms get very bad, and after spending some time outside have a shower and wash your clothes (or atleast keep them away from you or your sleeping area) to free yourself of any allergens you are carrying.

If you find that even with medication you are still not even able to breath through your nose, there are a few home remedies. The most well known is spicy food, it does a great job at clearing out your nasal ways. My personal favorite is warm lemon water. Boil some water and when it is at a steady boil, take it off and pour some in a bowl. Let the bowl sit for a minute or two, so it is still very hot but not boiling. Squeeze a lemon in and let it dissolve in the water. Place your face directly above the bowl so your nostrils face the lemon water, then just wait until you start to feel the freeing effects.

All that caffeine you are having also isn’t helping your sleeping patterns. As hard as it is, STAY IN BED EVEN IF YOU CAN’T SLEEP. Don’t fuck up your internal clock.

I suffer absolutely horrible from allergy symptoms, and have been feeling and doing the exact same way as you for many many years, but all of the solutions I listed help GREAT. I was never able to find anything on it either, which is why I spent so long writing a stupid post on allergies. I can’t stress enough how well Claritin 12 hour worked for me, you should try it.

Good luck!
