eXemplar July 31, 2016, 2:22pm #63

Just my 2c on this whole idea of community moderation, a lot of you probably already know what I mean if you use any form of social media/news aggregation sites (ie, reddit).

The #1 problem with community moderation is it turns everything into a popularity contest. For example,
User1: runescape is shit because x/y/z
User2…n: omg ur shit, - rating dikhed
Net result: Thread burried, user receives bad rep purely for saying something which isn’t necessarily wrong but against the popular belief. In this example, although maybe on this topic would be unlikely to happen, you could lose a really interesting conversation and back/forwards between both sides. Then you end up getting popular users who attract more attention for no real reason other than they appear popular (read: have higher votes) and a backlash to lower voted users, which only attracts more negativity. Also you end up with down rating cliques forming who can bury things just because they hold sway over a few users.

It’s a community anti-magnet, where + attracts + and - attracts -

Also, where do you draw the line between comment and post? It’s far too open to individual interpretation, and I’m pretty sure if you looked up reply in a dictionary a few would probably list see: comment.

Further edit:
Personally, I think a better approach to this would be to move forward from using flat, linear and, let’s face it, ancient bbs style forums and move towards proper branching/threaded forums (again, reddit). As far as guaging users and input, I don’t know, maybe some form of signal to noise ratio? A representation of post count vs rep/rating per post or overall vs length of time browing forums vs how long user has been registered for. Anyways, that’s more idle thought than anything.
