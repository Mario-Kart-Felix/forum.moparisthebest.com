The_Web_Guy August 4, 2016, 1:13pm #10

I use PHP, and I haven’t had any problems with it, and I haven’t had problems accomplishing anything with it. There may be better alternatives, but if you can program with it, and have effective applications and you find it easy to work with, why not? A majority of sites (even large sites) use PHP.

After taking a look at this article:PHP: a fractal of bad design, I found something I can argue against…

Part of what makes a good developer is the ability to choose the tools that work best.

This guy used an analogy of a carpenter using either a rock or a hammer, a good carpenter can use either, but they’re going to choose the one that’s easier. If that carpenter started doing work with a rock, and has done so since he learned how to be a carpenter, why try something that’s foreign?

tl;dr - PHP is amazing if you started with it, and if you can program applications with it, efficiently. Some of the functions are broken or don’t give the required results, but that’s when you just add in a couple more lines to get what you want.

How about this: if I wanted to write a ten minute program, and leave it at that, PHP wouldn’t be too terrible of a language to use.
