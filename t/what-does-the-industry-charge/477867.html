Ruby_Forums_Coder August 4, 2016, 2:06am #6

It depends on the skill level involved, when I freelance I charge between 7 and 10 dollars for a page using just HTML and CSS. If they wish a script to be used, they can then purchase the script which I’ll incorporate. If the site is over 15 pages its 7$ per page. If its under 15 pages its 10$ per page. I don’t really freelance much since I’m rolling in cash from work but that’s what I used to do. It keeps you living comfortably. Once you get a name for yourself then you’re gonna have business left right and centre and could quite easily be charging roughly 150 per simple site and if you do 4 of those a week you’re coming off at 600 dollars. It’s just finding the right kind of people to pay those amounts. Usually small businesses that are just starting. Try handing out some form of leaflets or flyers to local shops and asking if they can put them in the windows or what not. Freelancing is what you make of it, if you want a lot of money then expand into advertisements. Once you make your name heard the money will make itself.
