It’s probably not 100, it’s actually probably more than that, but the compiler stop giving a damn after 100 (“if you don’t get that something’s wrong after me telling you 100 times, there’s nothing I can do for you”).

Decompilers are not perfect. A decompiler is basically a translator between a primitive, and modern tongue (analogous to latin-english). It’s not going to do everything right, and even if it gets the words right, the concept might be totally missed (for example, “You’re pulling my chain”).

Granted, decompilers have come pretty far in recent years, they’re still far from perfect.

The best you could do is look at the first error (which is what usually causes the cascading of future errors), and try to fix it.[/quote]
