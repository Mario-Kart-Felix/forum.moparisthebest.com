General Discussion
The_Reaper_X July 31, 2016, 2:57pm #1

Not all bad people are in jail, and not all people in jail are bad. So, what defines a bad person? Of course we have the degree of murder for the sake of killing someone, but where do you draw the line of good and bad?

There are a number of individuals that believe marijuana and other drugs render you in the category of negative individuals, but what if it were for the sake of experimentation? You only live one life, what’s wrong with trying as much as you possibly can within the small time span we’re given?

There are others that believe steeling opens the door to greed and deficient morals. But what if it were to continue your family’s health? What about the whole man, the family and the bread story? If one were to steel bread in order to save his or her family, is it still considered bad?

There are a number of construed reasons for pending someone to a “bad” list, but among what judgment is someone aloud to pass this test? What do you feel defines a bad person?

Discuss.
