tL August 1, 2016, 12:02pm #7

ATI have improved massively. When they brought out the 5000 series cards, and then NVIDIA brought their initial 400 series cards (470 and 480), ATI’s cards were reviewed considerably better for price to performance, and efficiency.

Nvidia have brought it back with the 500 series cards, and at the top end have better performance - but overall, I’d say you wouldn’t go wrong with buying either brand of card these days.

An example for the top end single GPUs from both sides, while you may not be looking for those specifically - it is a good indication of the quality of both brands now:

http://www.anandtech.com/bench/Product/305?vs=292 (play around with the various benchmark categories on the left) - As you can see, the 6970 does well against it. The prices:

NVIDIA 580: http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814127567&cm_re=Nviida_580--14-127-567--Product ($499)

ATI 6970: http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814127554&cm_re=6970--14-127-554--Product ($339.99).

NVIDIA still has the better drivers, but ATI are also improving. There are still some annoyances, but having had my 5850s since they came out in 2009 I’ve seen them improve and haven’t had any significant problem with them.
