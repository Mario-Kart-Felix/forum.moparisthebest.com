Vain1 August 4, 2016, 7:30am #37

you concern about security should not be based on the extension or location of where the data is stored (remote servers, flatfile, binary, sql, anything)

as stated previously (“what if a virus gets a hold of my files”, well then, they could also get the mysql connection string, or the compression/encryption methods for whatever data storage you use)

the only thing you should worry about when debating the storage system, if speed/ease of access.

any file can be implemented into a ‘highscores page’ or ‘forum’ as long as you read/parse correctly.

hell, you could use json to save accounts if you’d like (and it has been done before)

you should pick something you know how to parse (for what you’re trying to accomplish) or something you are looking to make.

xml, flatfile, sql, sqlite, binary, json, dat, gz, anything.
pick what your goal is first. then look about your best options on achieving that. after so, worry about speed/compression.

if you’re actually worrying about security; don’t look at the extension at all.

also, look into huffman, like stated by sinisoul.
