One example of what I am talking about is with the new ubuntu (jaunty, still in beta). Everyone talks about how fast it boots with the new ext4 filesystem, and that is true. However, what most people don’t take into account is the new kernel configuration, in which many of the modules, instead of being compiled as modules, are now built into the kernel, which is the real reason load times are decreased.

On a side note, they thought it best to compile in the ipv6 module into the kernel, reasoning that they always load it whenever the network is used anyway, which makes it impossible to disable ipv6. Luckily a new kernel is so easy to compile. Thanks ubuntu developers! :rolleyes:
