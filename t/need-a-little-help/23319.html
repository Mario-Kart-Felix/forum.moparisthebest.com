a²+b²=c²

Pythagoreans Theorem. With Pythagoreans theorem you c is always The Hypotenuse (side opposite right angle.)
The Black Point in the farthest left corner will be represented by B1. The other Black Point is B2. The Red Point R.

For this question, I will give you what you need. What you half to do is split the triangle into two, so you have two right triangles.

a=Black point to Black Point divided by two. (A=Dist(B1,B2)/2)
b=Distance from Red point to half way between The Two Black Points (a). [B=Dist(R,A)]

Then you need to calculate a² (Multiply ‘a’ by itself.) Do the Same thing with b. Add the results together.

You will get a ridiculously high number. That is obviously not the answer. Find the square root of a²+b² (What I was just explaining) to find the answer of c. Now you have the side length of the Hypotenuse, or the distance between the black and red points.
