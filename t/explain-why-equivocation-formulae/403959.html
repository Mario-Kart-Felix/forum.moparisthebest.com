Question: Suppose there exists a natural number n such that for all natural numbers a, b, and c, (a-b)/-c = n. Prove that (b-a)/c = n.

Direct Proof P=>Q:

Assume there exists a natural number n such that for all natural numbers a, b, and c, (a-b)/-c = n
implies a - b = -cn
implies a = -cn + b
implies a +cn = b
implies cn = b - a
implies (b - a)/c = n
Therefore given (a-b)/-c = n, (b-a)/c = n Thus proven

Note: Not only is this a messy proof, but I think you have to prove the converse is true as well… that is prove P <=> Q not P => Q but oh well fuck it.
