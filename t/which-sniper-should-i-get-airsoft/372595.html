Even though you have decided, I thought I would throw my feedback in.

I have the Well L96 and personally I love it. Many reviews say that the accuracy is very jumpy but I haven’t noticed a problem with it. It delivers a nice punch at long range and is accurate as long as the wind is down. However, many times the bullet doesn’t seem to align with the crosshairs but the sniper does not change the shot direction for me.

Buy some extra magazines if you can, the magazine is very annoying to reload even with the speed loader. If you run out during a war you will probably be out before you can reload anyway.

Some reviews may also say that it has a feeding problem. I honestly can’t say much to this as I haven’t used it enough to see a problem, but otherwise the feeding is pretty smooth.

Truth is, you get what you pay for usually. Most airsoft guns will encounter problems sooner or later, because plastic and cheap metal models can only last so long. I currently own (too lazy to look up the other brand names):

-Well L96
-AK47
-Uzi (propane)
-Several cheap plastic Wal-Mart guns. Honestly the only Wal-Mart gun that is worth the money is the shotgun. Even for a $20 white plastic gun it delivers a pretty strong force.

I can get pictures of my guns if you want. Also, sorry for the long post.

Airsoft

Airsoft is a competitive team sport in which participants shoot opponents with spherical plastic BBs launched via replica weapons called airsoft guns. Airsoft is commonly compared to paintball, which is a recreational shooting sport similar in concept but with some key differences. Unlike paintballs, airsoft pellets do not mark their target, and hits are not always visibly apparent.
