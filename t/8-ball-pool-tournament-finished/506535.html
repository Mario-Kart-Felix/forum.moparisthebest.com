Game overview:
Pool games are played on a table with 6 pockets, balls and a cue to move the balls around. Pool games are great to play by yourself or to play against others. There is a nice selection of different pool games for you to choose from.

Tournament explanation:
Round 1 is the preliminary round. Each player will be randomly paired with another. Each pair is given 36 hours to submit a screenshot of a winner of a game of the round. The loser of that round is eliminated while the winner advances to Round 2.

Round 2 is yet another preliminary round. The winning player of a pair in Round 1 will face the winning player of another pair in Round 1. Each pair is given 24 hours to submit a screenshot of a winner of a game of the round. The loser of that round is eliminated while the winner advances to the semi-finals.

Semi-finals is a round of 4 where players fight it out to qualify for the finals. The winning player of a pair in Round 2 will face the winning player of another pair in Round 2. Each pair is given 24 hours to submit a screenshot of a winner of a game of the round. The loser of that round is eliminated while the winner advances to the finals.

Finals is the ultimate round. Beat the other player and you win!

Note: if all players complete a round before the round expires, the next round will begin! Additionally, because the previous attempt at this competition went to shit, any participant that disappears/doesn’t reply will be disqualified.

Frequently asked questions:
What happens if I am disconnected during a battle? If you have overwhelming evidence that you were disconnected (ie, a screenshot), talk to me about it and you will be entitled to a rematch before the next round begins.

What happens if my partner doesn’t reply to me during the round time? If you can’t contact your partner, provide overwhelming evidence of your activity to me. If I also cannot contact your partner, you advance to the next round. Otherwise, which one of you continues to the next round will be randomly chosen.

Am I entitled to a rematch? Yes. However, you cannot force your partner to give you a rematch. Both of you must be willing to have a rematch. In short, you can play as many games as you wish but only post the screenshot of a game if your opponent does not want a rematch. If your opponent screws you over, (ie you win, he asks for a rematch, you lose the rematch, he refuses to rematch) then it’s too bad.
