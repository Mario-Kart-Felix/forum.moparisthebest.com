Lothy:

Just to clarify, computer graphics as I’ve mentioned it has nothing to do with game development. It’s processing imagery for the purpose of computer vision, among other things.

Uhhh, that’s image processing, not computer graphics. The are entirely different fields of study. Computer graphics is the means of rendering and processing image data in real-time, whereas image processing has to do with things like edge detection, filtering, etc. Modern server clusters can do a lot of complex image processing algorithms in real-time, but they are still radically different in intention to computer graphics.

Computer graphics inherently does not have to be linked to game development or CGI in general, but it has a ton to do with both.

To get those Higher level courses you will need to attend a 4-year rather than a 2-year college. Not many community colleges offer such courses. Odds are the first class your taking is Introduction to programming (a level 100 course) and next is a 200. 200 will get you into Some things like GUI’s and other simple graphical things. Ours we made Pong, and are working on O(n) for sorting techniques. odds are had you done some basic programming before, you could of skipped your level 100 course by going to the professor and explaining you already know the basics. They would probably have you write a simple program, or have you explain a bit of code to them.
