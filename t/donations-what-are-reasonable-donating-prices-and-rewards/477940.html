sk8rdude461 August 4, 2016, 2:06am #16

Personally, how we set up the current ‘donations’ isn’t really considered a donation, in my opinion. When you donate for a cause, you aren’t donating for the item, you are donating to help. When you put rewards up there, such as spins on a SoF, party hats, etc, that’s just giving the user a change to get ahead of the game, something RuneScape has done wrong now-a-days. In RuneScape you can buy spins, get free XP, and level up using the lamps won, and use money earned to buy items for skills and so on. RuneScape doesn’t call it donating though, they tell you to buy spins. That is essentially what you are doing with your server. You are selling items to users for the upkeep of the server. Just like the RuneScape market outside of the actual game.

I think you should set up a cosmetic donation system, if any. Meaning you give them items that are unique and not tradeable, Also making sure they don’t give an advantage over other players overall. Adding a timed xp boost, or PvM rewards, is a good idea, as long as you don’t over power it. Double XP rings are a big mistake in servers, because they are generally sold for about $15, and the player would be maxed on your server in a week. Then they stop playing, and stop donating. Say that happens to 75% of your players. Well, now how are you going to pay for the server since only 5 people play.

It’s really up to you how you setup a donation system and what items you give out, but don’t make it so it kills your server.
