amy_webb1 August 4, 2016, 7:19am #7

If your model is too small it will make it look choppy, i’ve experienced this.

Try sketch out ideas before modelling them, it gives you a better understand of what you want to achieve and you can edit the idea as you go along.

Basic shapes i mean, try to avoid adding every little detail, just get the base so you don’t poly over kill or make the wireframe look unbearable. Primitives are not shapes you throw into a model, they’re the base of a shape. Look at a primitive as you would a block of clay, it is your foundation, you mold it to the way you want it. This is achieved using the tools provided, knife is a good tool to use, as it allows you to cut across faces.

Instead of using ball primitives along the shaft, make cuts for either side of the ball you want. once you’ve got as many cuts as you need, go back and make cuts in the middle of these two lines. You will then need to select and use the yellow scaling block to expand the middle, this will give it the ball type look without having a lot of poly. If you need an example, i’d be happy to draw it out for you.

I’ve tried to make videos multiple times, but have always run into a problem. One day i’ll get around to making a good one, but it may be awhile. Until then, just ask an advanced modeler in this section for help. We’re all willing to help, we want to see people improve. No question is ever too silly to be answered, we just require you to seek help.
