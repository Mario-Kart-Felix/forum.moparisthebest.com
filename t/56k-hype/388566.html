[size=24pt]About
[i]I’ve named my server 56k-Hype for two reasons: 56k because I’m on a 56k dial-up connection and Hype because I initially was going to develop a Hyperion server, but decided against it; kept the name though as I thought it sounded cool.

I am developing 56k-Hype as a cleverly balanced PK server (because this is one of the most fun aspects of RuneScape private servers). I also plan to take full advantage of the combat system. Apart from PKing of course, 56k-Hype will have skilling, minigames, new and original activities/things to do, quests and lots of information so that you know what you are doing and where to go.

The source is Project No-Doze, created by killamess - I highly reccommend the source as it has the most beautiful of combat systems. Better than Pali’s in my opinion. Best one I’ve seen so far. I chose Project No-Doze for the combat (because I believe this is the most fun and important part of a private server), it has the basic core system for skilling, dialogues via XML, object spawning via XML, MySQL login, MySQL npcspawns, MySQL npcdrops, and various other things. I haven’t worked out how to really use the XML files yet though.[/i]

[i]I will also make the economy balanced. High end stuff has a rare percentage of being dropped, whereas Mithril for example, is moderately obtainable, so it’s enough to sell to pures for decent cash, but not worthless that you have to leave it.

There will be a massive variety of weapons and armours to choose from.

Clue scrolls with a massive variety of rewards (unique to treasure trails).

All, or at least the vast majority, of ladders, doors, caves, dungeons and portals to be interactive. A choice to go absolutely anywhere so people may find quiet spots to camp while they train.

Secrets hidden around the entire world to throw people off, confuse them, get them thinking or to reward them for venturing so far away from places you’d expect.

Nothing too over the top, but a little system that gives rewards for doing certain tasks/acheivements (i.e. killing 1000 Abyssal demons gives you double XP in Fletching for a day, stuff like that).

Level 99’s to be very special. Some examples I have in mind are 99 defence, you can spawn 4 body guards that surround and follow you and 99 Thieving you can pickpocket other players (with limitations of course).

More to come…[/i]

[size=24pt]Current Features
Please note, this features are not necessarily created by me. I’m just adding them as a whole.

Locations
-Ash Cave. You slip down the manhole cover outside Varrock’s Fancy-dress shop, and the dungeon contains a trail of ashes to follow and reveals certain types of monsters to kill. So far, there are Lesser demons, Greater demons, Black demons and Abyssal demons.

-Ports. There’s a Ghost Sailor outside every major port (Karamja, Kazhard, Phas. and Sarim). This is the way to get around the map faster (rather than walking). Will also be useful for when I implement clue scrolls.

Items
-Holy grail. Get it as a rare drop from a monk and sell/alch it for a quick 750k.
-Ring of wealth. Get it as a rare drop from an abyssal demon and sell/alch it for a quick 750k.

Magic

-Perfect graphics (literally).
-Perfect timers.
-Perfect level and rune requirements (except teleports).
-Steady leveling with generous rune spawns/drops.
-Normal teleports are free to any level. Ancient teleports are free to any level but the letter on the teleport icon represents where you will go (i.e. Dareeyak = Draynor, Lassar = Lava maze etc.).
-Low alc and High alch work perfectly.
-Bind, snare and entangle work perfectly.
-God spells work perfectly.
-Teleblock works perfectly.
her
Need to work on: Superheat, Telekinetic, Jewellery enchanting, Orb charging and teleother.

Miscellaneous
-You can spawn a pet dog (German Shepherd) and it picks up valuable items you get from monsters for you.

-NPCs cannot stand on top of one another , basically NPCs are clipped wiht one another and depending on the situation, if you are standing on top of an NPC and decide to interact with it, you or they are forced to move and face the NPC (just like RuneScape) - no more attacking a monster on the same tile as you.

More features coming very soon…
