Yes, this is debatable. I’ve always thought that smf and vBulletin were the better of the options out there. But I know not everyone agrees with me

So have at it. I want to see actual reasoning behind everyone’s idea on what they think is the best.

Here’s mine:
SMF: Open source. Easy to use. Customization. Community based modifications, customization’s, and themes. Typically not bad on the eyes. And it’s pretty secure for free software.

vBulletin: (Can be) clean. Sleek (depending on who’s in control, some just throw everything everywhere). Powerful (in terms of options). Unique (I’ve seen a lot on vB that hasn’t been done on others). And it’s very restrictive (Meaning the forum admin can add lots of controls and edits for the forum to not allow people to do things).

Here’s some of the thousands of forum software out there:

phpBB, vBulletin, SMF, IP.Board, XenForo, MyBB, ASPPlayground.Net, Vanilla Forum, YaBB, Tal.Ki

Ruby_Forums_Coder August 4, 2016, 9:56am #9

I would never go anywhere near vBulletin really, the code is fudgeing atrocious alongside the fact that their hashing algorithm is absolute bollocks. Anyone with half a brain can crack [php]$hash = md5(md5($password)+$salt)[/php], I’m friends with a villavu admin and he recently had to change the password hashing on there because they were hacked, it’s atrocious that people even use vBulletin anymore, especially when you’re risking a forum as large as villavu (1m+ posts, 130k users). I’m personally a giant fan of SMF, most of the reasons have been stated above but no-one seems to have included the fact that they offer the SSI, such an amazing tool for any website developer and a really, really big time saver. Their password hashing is relatively secure, it’s what you’d be expecting from free software really. Their theme system is a little hard to get your head around at first but it’s nothing difficult once you understand it. I’m also becoming a fan of the aesthetics of MyBB lately (blame RevengePK), it looks pretty nice because he can actually create themes for it. I’ve never really messed with the code but I don’t really want to since it’s pretty much free vbulletin.

SMF is obviously my favorite forum software because it’s easy to use, easily customizable, lightweight, and has many modifications which can help you customize your forum. A downside to SMF is the amount of decent themes out there, and anyone who is really talented who starts developing nice themes for it will be quite successful over there.

vBulletin is a mess, seems to break or look inconsistent at times, and is just terrible to try and customize. Making plugins for that thing has never been so complicated. The code is obviously a mess as Ruby stated, and I have yet to see why people buy vBulletin for their private servers. There are much better free and paid alternatives out there. I would say it’s one of the worst.

Haven’t used MyBB too much but making plugins for it is nice to do. The themes for it are nice, and I don’t know too much about the plugin community but I hear it’s decent.
