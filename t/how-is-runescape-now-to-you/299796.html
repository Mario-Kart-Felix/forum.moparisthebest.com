lothy July 31, 2016, 10:40am #2

Speaking as a player with top-end melee skills on one account (not quite flat 99s, but getting there) and a maxed ranger for a secondary account I can say that the game is still very much playable.

I will agree with your opinion of certain weapons though; I’ve likened Runescape PvP to Russian Roulette on numerous occasions, and I simply don’t take part in any form of PvP on p2p worlds because of this.
I don’t own any of those weapons at the moment because I have no real use for them, but they’re well within the money-making capacity of my account.

I also agree with your opinion on the availability of higher leveled armour and weapons.
Runescape is apparently geared at both hardcore gamers (who spend endless hours playing) and casual players.
The problem is that casual players are often locked out of buying the higher end items that they may be able to make use of simply because they can’t play enough to acquire the money.

As for summoning, it’s just as trainable at low levels as slayer is. I think that keeping your summoning level in line with your other stats (ie, 50 attack/strength/defence, therefore 50 summoning) is quite achievable. Proof of this is the fact that Villandra trained an account, Lorica, to 99 summoning without maxing any of her other skills while doing so.
If lower level players train their combat through slaying then they’re even more likely to acquire charms as quite a few of the summoning monsters are, at the very least, good gold charm droppers; gold charms are sufficient for a low leveled player, and they’ll acquire green charms too.

If you want easy charms at a low level, kill flesh crawlers. They’re level 25 and can be found on the second level of the security stronghold - I can guarantee you that you’ll receive an ample amount of green charms, fire runes, noted iron ore, and ranarr weed - sppt was playing there just last night and can tell you this from experience… Or you can take my word for it, because I afk’d my ranger there for quite a long time.
