Graham July 31, 2016, 1:28pm #10

That sentence was written quickly and I’ll explain it more (in terms of ‘ruining’ it). It will probably make more people use it, however, the way it’s all nicely object-orientated and stuff is going to gradually be ruined - I predict that the Player class is going to turn into a massive gloop of methods put in to retain compatibility with Winterlove - and people will be less aware that they can call getInventory() and do all sorts of cool stuff with the container class (this is just one example).

With the structure there is at the moment things are still relatively easily.

Instead of having to write player.addItem(…), you can have to write player.getInventory.addItem(new Item(…)). It’s a bit more verbose, but much tidier and cool stuff like transferring items from an inventory to bank is super easy thanks to the container class.

Rather than making it compatible with winterlove, doing to reverse and making people compatible with hyperion by writing guides would get more of my support, however, I can’t do much about this due to the license it’s under (and I do believe in people freely using my work anyway).

Regardless, I’m glad that Hyperion is at least getting some more attention now and this, if done properly, may well protect newbies (perhaps protect them too much - they won’t be aware of some of the power hidden away in other parts of the source as it’s all hidden in the wl-style methods) from making mistakes and improve the number of people using it - at the expense of breaking OO-principles and good programming practices.
