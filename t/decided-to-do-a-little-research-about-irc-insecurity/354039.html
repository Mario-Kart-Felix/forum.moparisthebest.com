Many IRC servers also include service users. These service users are essentially robots which provide services to users. These service bots are usually named in a fashion that makes them easy to recognize such as Chanserv, Nickserv, Memoserv, etc. One common service is that of a Channel bot. Chanserv (most commonly used name) can help maintain channels even while no users are currently in that channel. Each implementation of a channel bot differs; therefore, features present on the bot may differ from network to network. Since information is relayed in IRC, this type of communication is not secure. There are many possible ways for attackers to probe for information and initiate attacks. Trojans, viruses, and backdoors are some of the malicious tools that hackers on IRC use to compromise computers.
