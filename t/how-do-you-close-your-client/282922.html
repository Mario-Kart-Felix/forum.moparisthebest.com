pesterd July 31, 2016, 8:21am #6

This is how to do it, this is to download keytool http://start.ubuntu.com/8.10/
open KeyTool UI.jnlp and sign your new rscd.jar file.

  1. Open KeyTool UI!
  2. On the left collumn, find where it says “Create” and double click on it.
  3. Click on “Keystore”
  4. Look to the right, it says “*Keystore File:” with a floppy disk icon beside it.
  5. To the right of that is a list of choices with radio buttons. Choose “JCEKS”
  6. Click on the floppy disk icon
  7. Navigate up to Desktop and type “secret” beside where it says “File Name:” then click “Save file”
  8. Below find where it has the mask icon beside "Keystore password: and click the mask
  9. Enter a password. Repeat it and then click “OK”
  10. Now at the bottom of the program click “OK”
  11. Back to the list on the left, double click on “Keystore’s entry”
  12. Double click on “Private key, with vers. #3”
  13. Double click on “RSA”
  14. On the right, it says “*Keystore File:” with a folder icon beside it.
  15. To the right of that is a list of choices with radio buttons. Choose “JCEKS”
  16. Below find where it has the mask icon beside "Keystore password: and click the mask
  17. Type in the password you used before and click “OK”
  18. Now scroll down to where it says “Common (or domain) name:” and click on the pencil icon
  19. Type in your website URL. If you don’t have one, make one up and press “OK”
  20. Scroll down to where you see “2-letter country code:” and click the pencil icon
  21. If you are in the US, type “US” and press “OK”
  22. Now a window will pop up. It says “Enter new private key entry’s alias:”
  23. Type in an alias of your choice. Type in a password and repeat it. (Use the same as before to avoid problems)
  24. Click “OK” and then get out of those extra windows.
  25. Go back to the list on the left, double click on “Sign file with private key”
  26. Double click on “Embedded signature”
  27. Click on “JAR”
  28. To the right where it says “*Unsigned JAR file:” click the folder icon.
  29. Navigate over to the Open RSCD Webclient folder you have your rscd.jar located and select that file.
  30. Below should be “*Keystore file:” To the right of that are the radio buttons again. Choose JCEKS
  31. Click the folder icon and navigate to your desktop where secret.jce is saved and select that file.
  32. Below should be a mask icon asking for your Keystore password. Type in your password that you have been using.
  33. Below that should be “*Signed JAR file:” with a floppy disk icon to the right. Click that icon!
  34. Navigate over to where the Open RSCD Webclient folder is that has rscd.jar.
  35. This time don’t select rscd.jar! Instead, type “web” in to the “File Name:” box and click “Save file”
  36. At the bottom of the program click “OK”
  37. A window should pop up. Look at the top where it says “Private Key (keypair) & Trusted Certificate Entries:”
  38. You should see an alias of your own making right below. Select that.
  39. Now at the bottom of the window where it says “Enter respective password:” type in your password from before.
  40. Click OK and you’ve done it

Thats for RSC but It kinda works same way for RS2
