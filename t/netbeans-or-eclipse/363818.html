Fat_Cookies August 1, 2016, 12:19am #20

This is like a windows v linux debate, it really just depends on the user and their attitudes.

On the bottom line, both netbeans and eclipse manage to all the basics: a source editor, syntax highlighting, display possible methods and javadocs, source formatter (indent), refactoring, debugging and some sort of gui developing. It’s just all the small things that make the differences.

I am personally a netbeans fag because I prefer the ui; everything is where you expect it to be. The class navigator is brilliant! I also find the management of projects is very easy to use; you can add, delete and modify multiple projects with your eyes closed. The workspace thing with eclipse just pisses me off.

Most people say: “netbeans is bloated 0mg0mg0mg” but I think that’s just bullshit. I will not deny that it takes a long time to load up on windows but on arch linux it loads up as fast as any other big application. For me, on arch it uses 180mb of ram and runs fine on my shit 1.6Ghz atom, 1gb netbook.

My love for netbeans is probably gained because i tried it before eclipse but I still haven’t changed mine mind. OP, you should also consider idea because I’ve heard it has many useful features but obviously you’ll have to “aquire” the full version yourself.
