American SATs actually mean something.

British SATs aren’t called SATS anymore, they are National Curriculum Evaluations or something - they mean nothing to the student, they are evaluations of the school and curriculum used to develop it from year to year and rate the school on the performance tables.

However, in an attempt to get you to actually try your best, most schools use the SAT results to place you in classes for the next couple of years, which might affect which GCSE you sit (Higher/Intermediate/Foundation) - which IS fairly important.
