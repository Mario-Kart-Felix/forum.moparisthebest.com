[center]Mini-debate Death Penalty[/center]
It is resolved that the death penalty is cruel and unusual and it is not a fitting punishment for the crimes of the citizens of the United States of America. There are many arguments against the death penalty. First off, many people who are sentenced have been exonerated in fact 122 people have been exonerated from death row since 1971. Another problem with capital punishment is that many defendants where underage at the time of committing the crime; as stated by deathpenaltyinfo.org, twenty-two people who where underage at the time of the crime have been executed since 1976. The rate is increasing too. Mental retardation and illness can be a large factor in a humanÃ¢â‚¬â„¢s decision to commit a crime. According to deathpenaltyinfo.org over 5% of all convicts are mentally retarded or ill. One of the largest factors in the argument that the death penalty is cruel is the methods used to kill the inmates. Electrocution cause violent convulsions, spasms, severe mental pressures, and in many cases the convict may be in so much pain that the inmates eyes will pop out and come to rest on the inmates cheeks. Many psychologists think that the drug used in lethal injection, pancuronium bromide, may actually mask the pain that the inmate is feeling.
