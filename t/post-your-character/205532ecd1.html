mod_taharok August 2, 2016, 4:27pm #4558

If you’re low on money, why would you do frost dragon bones? You need to calculate opportunity cost, here. Frost dragon bones are about 2.7x more experience than dragon bones, which means they will allow you to get 1-95 in 37.1% of the time (so about 1/3). Awesome. Is that really worth ~85-90m? If you can get 95 prayer in 100 hours using dragon bones, then you would be able to get it in about 37 hours using frost dragon bones. Does that mean you are able to get the extra 85-90m you need in 63 hours? Because if you can’t, then you are spending more time in the long run, not less.

I feel this is a common misconception for people deciding on how to approach “buyable” skills. Sure, you may not want to spend as much time actually training, but you end up spending a significant amount of time overall due to needing to raise funds. Some people who realize this are fine with it (such as me), because they enjoy getting the money more than actually using it. For someone (such as you) who are low on funds and means of getting funds, you should always pick a middle to low cost solution, in order to minimize overall time.

@ectofuntus, unless you have high agility, it’s not viable.

Hope this helps.
