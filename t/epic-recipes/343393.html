lothy July 31, 2016, 5:00pm #6

Macaroni and cheese is easy enough. Cook the pasta on the stove until it’s done, strain it, and then put the pasta back in the saucepan. Now add sauce, stir it through, and allow it to heat for a moment on the stove. Chuck some grated cheese in, stir it through, let it melt, and you’re done.

Make sure you don’t walk away after you’ve added the sauce, because it can burn if you have it too hot or don’t stir it enough. It’s awesome, because you save on dishes which means less washing up.

Chicken Stir Fry: (serving 2)
Ingredients;
- 2 White Onions
- 2 Peppers (Preferably Red & Yellow)
- Stir Fry Veg (purchased cheaply at supermarket, I think my ASDA stocks them at 70p)
- 2 Chicken Breast (you can miss this out if you want to go cheap)
- Egg Noodles
- Sauce (Sweet and Sour is one of my personal favourites)

dice the chicken to small pieces
add a small amount of oil to a hot pan, can be veg or olive
dice onions and pepper
fry the onions until partially cooked (1 min)
add the chicken, cook thoroughly
add the stir fry veg, peppers and sauce
fry for a further minute

then you can either mix in the noodles, or lay the stir fry on top of noodles. Doesn’t take too long to do this and is a v. tasty meal, great if you want to share with a few house mates or something.

Pork Steaks and Sweet Potato

Ingredients:

  • Pork Steak
  • 1 Sweet Potato (can be swapped for a standard if you dont like sweet but I urge you to try it)
  • Peas & Sweetcorn, and any other veg you may want. such as broccoli or carrott

peel the sweet potato, then dice it to small cubes. put to one side.
prepare the steaks however you want, i usually add garlic salt and pepper and just rub it in.
put in the oven at 200c this should take about 15 mins when left.
have a 2 min break to sort out getting the other veg out or something.
then boil the potato for about 5-10 minutes until soft.
drain the water then add butter and a small amount of milk to the pan, use a masher to create sweet potato mash, season with salt and pepper to suit, I personally like lots of pepper.
now you want to put your veg in a boiling pan and turn the oven off (assuming the 15 mins are up), but leave the pork in the oven whilst your veg boils off.

once done, drain veg and plate up.

for the enchiladas I use the old el paso pack and they are v v easy to follow.
