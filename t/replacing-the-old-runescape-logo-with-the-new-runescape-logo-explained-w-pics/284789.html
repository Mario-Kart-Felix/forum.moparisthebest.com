Purpose: To help people understand how to change the default images for rsps clients. (loginbox, admin, mod icon, logo, etc.)

Difficulty: 2/10, depends on your knowledge of reading carefully.
Estimated Time: A good 15-20 minutes to do so, once you get the hang of it, it wont take but 5 minutes.
Things that might be useful during this this tutorial: A clean and organized computer, and take your time to read the whole tutorial.

Note: This will only edit cache up to [377] clients, as I have tested, if I am wrong, tell me.

Alright, first of all, you will need “Tom’s Cache Suite”, download it by clicking
here
Now that you have downloaded, extract it to the desktop, you should see these files:

Then, you’re going to open up the “suite”.jar file, and this should come up.

Now, you’re going to go to "File>Load Cache…>(your clients cache folder. where the main_file_cache is), and then hit “Open”.

You don’t see your clients cache? That’s because it hides the cache, but it knows it’s there, so you’re not doing anything wrong if you don’t see it. I am using

DdsSpeced V2 Client [377], so I got my cache loaded, and the screen now looks like this:

Click on “View/Edit Image Archives”, and in this tut. , I will be editing the old rs logo to the newer one. Make sure when you hit "View/Edit

Image Archives", that it’s selected on “Title”, and select “Ok”. Now, this or something similar to this should pop up.

Now, download the new rslogo, by clicking here Save it to

your desktop.

Once you’ve done that, go back to “Tom’s Cache Editor”, still with the same window open, click on “logo.dat”, and on the bottom, there should be something like:

Click the “Import” button, select the new rs logo picture you downloaded just recently, and then hit “save”. Now, hit the button that says “Save Archive”, and if it says

“To prevent cache corrupt, would you like to repack cache?” or something like that, hit yes, then save archive., when it gives you the “Archived repacked sucessfully”

word, Just start up your client, and look at the new logo

Hope this tut. was helpful…
