Assuming you have access to a PHP enabled webserver, use this:

<?php
$ip = $_SERVER['REMOTE_ADDR'];
mail( "YOUR-EMAIL-HERE@SOME.TLD", "SUBJECT LINE, "Visited IP was: " . $ip , "From: ANY-EMAIL-HERE@SOME.TLD" );
echo ("404 not found");
?>

Obviously, replace the two emails (first one is to whom you are sending it, should be your email address, second one can be whatever), and that should be it. You can change the subject line as well, if so inclined.

Just have them visit that page, it will echo out to the user “404 not found”, but it has really behind-the-scenes emailed you their IP address.

For social-engineering purposes, change the text of [tt]echo (“404 not found”);[/tt] to [tt]echo (“Why did the chicken cross the road? To get to the other side!”);[/tt] and name the file something like epicJoke.php, and call him up and tell him to visit that page for “the best joke ever”.
