Although you could do this, there is so many fields, Command Prompt wouldn’t even have enough space. It is also impractical because fields change name and positions from update to update.

The practical way is to write an “updater.” This updater would use a bytecode library (preferably ObjectWeb’s ASM or BCEL) to open up the .class structure (a.k.a. bytecode) of RuneScape’s classes. Then, it would look for patterns that stay consistent on each update to locate certain fields (e.g., maybe the energy field is multiplied by 20, divided by 5, and then printed to output).

basing your entire search for the identification of certain fields on field monitoring during client runtime is highly impractical
