Power_of_Truth July 31, 2016, 4:00am #4

Don’t even bother with a PCI video card, the bandwidth on the bus (133MB/s) can’t handle any kind of gaming, and is sometimes even worse than on-board graphics. If you really need a new video card, I’d suggest going with a PCI-Express slot, where a full x16 lane is 4GB/s on 1.0 and 8GB/s on 2.0, or buy a motherboard that has one available. If you are really on a budget and can handle playing most low-requirement games (World of Warcraft, HL2-based games, etc) at low settings, you can get a motherboard with an AGP 4x (1067MB/s) or 8x (2133MB/s) slot, but I wouldn’t try as AGP is really showing it’s age.

Personally, if I were stuck with just PCI slots, I’d go ahead and buy a new computer. You’d get more performance out of it than any PCI slot can give you, and you don’t have to pay too much (you can get a new gaming computer with decent specs for under $1000 USD).

If I were to take a stab at making a few suggestions, I’d say go with an ATi HD4670 if you’re just going for games like World of Warcraft or Runescape. Cheap (sub $100) and the same performance as an HD3850 (which as quite a beast in it’s time a few months ago).

I hope my post was helpful.
