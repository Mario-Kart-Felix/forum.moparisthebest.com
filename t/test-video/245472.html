Thanks guys
This is relatively easy I think. After Effects is just like Photoshop, with a timeline.
Most of the work here was rotoscoping. Hand matteing the hands over the film. As for it growing and stuff, just animated the scale, rotation, position. Think of how you’d do this in photoshop, and then do it every 3 or so frames in the right place. Hope it interpolates correctly. If it doesn’t just add another keyframe.

My movie, I think it will be epic. It’s a mockumentary. Highly influenced by The Office. US Version.

It might be quite long. I wrote the intro, and what I think will be 20-30 minutes of it. I’m still adding bits and pieces. I’m doing it with my friend Kyle. He’s writing the rest of the story and I’m writing a bunch of funny “incidents” that happen during his story.

Don’t want to give away many of the funny parts so here’s a quick rundown.
Kyle thinks so highly of himself he has requested a documentary be done on him, which I am to film.
He’s actually a loser, and his mind ticks in a very different way. Within the first 10 minutes, a meteor crashes around the house, and when we go to see it we see a zombie on the street. He’s convinced the apocalypse is happening and that everyone’s a zombie but us.

So we live in his backyard forest for a while.

I’m getting my HD Camera within two weeks max so I’ll have more time to organize it. I’ve got a beginning storyboard and script, and then a few incidents I want to happen. Everything in between them will be improv maybe, and picking the best. With the scale of what we’re doing it may turn into a series with a few shorts every now and then.

But I plan on doing a complete storyboard and documenting all my ideas, and an unofficial script because the way we talk to make things funny is different than how I write.

So if you guys have a sense of humor like me, you’ll probably like it a lot.
