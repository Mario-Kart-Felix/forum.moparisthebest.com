The reason the mouse moves to the corner is because you havn’t include the ClickMouse in the if.
Example:

if(SomethingHappens)then Writeln('Something has happend!'); Writeln('Something might not have happend');

Should in your case be:

if(SomethingHappens)then begin Writeln('Something has happend!'); Writeln('Something might not have happend'); end;

Cheesehunk July 29, 2016, 8:39pm #4

Yep, as Spky said, if statements only allow use for the condition after it.

So, the variables x, and y will be reset after the if statement. In order to remember this, I usually put my if statements with one condition after it on the same line.

Example:

if(ThisIsTrue) then MoveMouse(x,y);

Instead of:

if(ThisIsTrue) then MoveMouse(x,y);
They both do the same thing, but I find it helps me clean up my code, and remember it easier.

But if I was trying to excecute multiple conditions if something is true, then I would do what Spky said, and use if…then…begin…stuff…end.
