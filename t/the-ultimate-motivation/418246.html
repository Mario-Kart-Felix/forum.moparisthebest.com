The Ultimate Motivation

General Programming
Game Development
Lin August 1, 2016, 3:35pm #1

There are many factors that lead to the urge to work on a game, or any project in general, but one stands out the most. To develop a game, you obviously need graphical resources, sounds, music, and the ability to make a game. Regardless of whether or not your game actually uses graphics and sounds, there still has to be a drive to work on the game. Assuming you’re not getting paid by the hour, this drive is provided by other people. While one might eagerly work on a game on his or her own for a little while with no support or criticism, the project is likely to eventually die.

I believe that over time, game developers lose interest in only seeing themselves satisfied and this is what leads to project failures. So, in an attempt to find a better reason to work other than self-satisfaction, they seek attention from others. I guarantee most of the project development topics on any forum that had dead projects will have the author being the last poster in the thread or few replies. Had the author received more attention the project would have likely lived longer.

So what does all this mean? When you see a project topic, post on it, and not just once. Even if it’s something as simple as “Looks good”, or “nice job”, the project developer(s) will have a smile put on their face and feel an urge to work. Personally, there’s nothing I love more than showing off my progress on something I’m working on and getting criticism, regardless of how brief it is. I take screenshots of my work often so I can view them for personal reasons and get feedback, and it keeps me going.

~Lin
