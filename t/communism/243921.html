Ok, lets look at bowserjr2’s statement: company’s (and thus the market they operate in) need money to grow. If you want to prove that this statement is either true or false, you have to consider the complete economy. So let’s remake a small economy, or atleast markets that are needed for the production of planks.

Materials needed:
Chainsaw.
Tractor to transport trees out of the forest.
Truck to transport the tree to the saw mill.
Saw mill.
Truck to transport the planks to the marketplace/costumer, with low demand this can be the same as the previously mentioned truck.

Humans needed:
Costumers
Woodcutters
Saw mill workers
Truck driver
Chainsaw producers
Saw mill producers
Truck manufactorer

Let’s say that in one year, there is a production of 1000 planks. To produce these planks, you need 1 chainsaw, 1 truck, 1 saw mill, 1 tractor and 50 man hours. Again, it is really hard to make an exact estimate, because to make a chainsaw you need steel. To make steel you need ore, etc. But let’s say that it requires 10 hours to make the chainsaw, 100 hours to make the truck, 50 hours to make the saw mill and 50 hours to make the tractor. That’s a total of 210 hours, or €2100 at €10 an hour. The total cost to produce 1000 planks is €2600. €2.6 a plank.

A researcher has developed a new kind of chainsaw, that allows a production of 1100 planks a year. This researcher spent an average of 10 hours per chainsaw to develop it. It’s an average, because it would be stupid if he developed a new chainsaw, and only sell one. So now the production is 1100 planks (the market has grown), the cost was 10 hours, and the gain was 100 planks. In hours, this is 10% of 1000 thus 10% of 260 hours, so a gain of 26 hours. As you can see here, the cost was 10 hours, and the gain was 26 hours. The market grew, and it didn’t cost anything. But we forgot one thing: the chainsaw was developed, but it hasn’t been made. Let’s say that it takes 16 hours to produce this new model.

So, in year 2 we produced 1100 planks. What did it cost?
Chainsaw: 26 hours
Truck: 100 hours
Saw mill: 50 hours
Tractor: 50 hours
Manhours (saw mill, forest, truck/tractor driver): 50 hours
Total: 276 hours, €2760, or €2,51 a plank.

The market grew, and planks became 9 cents cheaper.
