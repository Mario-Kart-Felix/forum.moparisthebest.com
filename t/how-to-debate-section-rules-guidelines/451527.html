Purpose of the thread
The purpose of this thread is to preserve the quality of discussion and debate in this section. Throughout this thread, there will be material on various topics that shall enable you to post constructive, concise, and meaningful replies. This section contains material which can be seen as offensive to some, and it’s for this reason we employ laws and regulations on what can be posted here. You’ll learn about the various rules and associated punishments that are in place in this forum. Moparscape Staff take the content being posted here very seriously, so it’s imperative that you don’t blunder, as the consequences may be unforeseen.

How to debate
Believe it or not, knowing how to debate is a sure-fire way of boosting your reputation within this forum. Too many times, you’ll see people posting threads with no actual intent to provide for debate. The purpose of this section is to discuss, debate, educate, influence, persuade, or inform. As the original poster, you can ensure your thread stays alive and flourishes simply by the hypothesis you put forth. Let’s take a look at a couple of examples.

Thread With No Potential Or Purpose

abortion is fucking sick. all you peopol who would purposly kill your own baby are mental.

Thread With Meaningful Content & Quality Hypothesis

[code]Hi,

I’d like to discuss the hot topic of abortion with everyone today. Personally, I don’t think abortion is right and I believe it should be made illegal. I don’t see how you can intentionally kill an alive human embryo for your own selfish needs.

What are your thoughts on abortion? Have you ever had any real-life experience with abortion? And why do you feel the way you do?

Thanks[/code]

You can immediately see the difference here. The second thread would be acceptable and would, most likely, stay alive for quite some time. The first thread would probably be locked by the site’s staff, as it can be deemed offensive.

Offensive posting in this section is a really big issue. People will often post in the Intellectual Discussions & Debates forum because they’re passionate about something. Or, other times, they’ll post simply out of anger or frustration. The first example was, as I’d like to say, an angry thread. The user did not think about what they were saying and just said what they were thinking at the time. Warnings, bans, and negative reputation are the most common results from creating such ranting threads.

When creating a topic for debate, you should remember a few things. Don’t make your side of the argument too overpowering. If possible, start with a fairly neutral statement on the topic, and then pose your hypothesis or opinion. Use evidence, examples, and thought out material in your thread. You might like to use the following structure.

[code]Abortion is one of the most commonly discussed topics in today’s society. It’s so commonly discussed because the results of abortion can effect so many lives; people will spend hours tearing their hair out deciding what the best option for themselves is. <<>>

Personally, I feel as though abortion is a good option for those who have mistakenly fallen pregnant and feel as though they couldn’t or shouldn’t give birth to or care for a baby. <<<This section goes over your personal opinion and doesn’t ever escalate to the point of offensiveness.>>>

I feel this way because I’ve been through the abortion process a number of times. I’ve had tough times contemplating what option is right, but each time I’ve come out at the end thinking I did the right thing. The International Federation of Birth and Parenthood has proven that 90% of people will carry out an abortion if they have to (2010). <<>>

What are your thoughts on abortion? Do you think you’d kill your own baby, or would you put up with the labour and give up your personal aspirations to parent the child? <<<In a relatively neutral statement or two, pose your question to the audience.>>>[/code]
You aren’t required to post using this structure: this is simply a guideline you might like to adopt.

If you want to become a reputable member in this section, there’s a few things you should strive to maintain.

Grammatical Accuracy
This is really easy to do. Proofread everything you post. If you’re unsure of something, use Google. There’s hundreds of excellent resources available on the Internet that will help you correct your spelling and grammar. Being able to post with grammatical accuracy not only saves you from lingering in the low quality area, but it also makes people take you more seriously. No one’s going to actually care about what you’re saying if you can’t even spell it correctly.

Remain Neutral
You’re allowed to post your opinion. You’re allowed to argue your point. You’re allowed to say your point of view is better than theirs but only if you are able to back up your argument. Don’t expect everyone to believe you when you say, “Abortion is the only way” unless you legitimately know for a fact (and provide examples and evidence) that you’re correct.

Remain Calm
People are always going to troll you. People are always going to get angry at you for having an opinion. Let the staff take care of these individuals. Simply report their post and it’ll be dealt with. Do not reply to these people, as you’ll derail your own thread and end up in the wrong with them.

Keep It On Track!
You’re the boss. It’s your thread, so you need to maintain it. If someone has said something, and there’s a few replies relating to what they’d said instead of the original topic, the chances are, the thread’s going to be derailed sooner or later. Simply post telling everyone to stay on topic.

My unofficial rules
Respect other peoples’ opinions. If you don’t agree with them, that’s your own prerogative, however, you must respect others’ thoughts and feelings.

Don’t mindlessly rant. I’ve seen a lot of people going on about how they hate a particular religion or race. No one cares unless you’re making a valid point, which you’re not.

If you’re going to use someone else’s article, for god’s sake, reference it. Leeching is not permitted on Mopar. Also, if you’re going to do this, don’t just quote the whole thing and hit Post. Actually make a point or valid argument that will spark discussion.

Low quality posting & flaming is not allowed. This doesn’t just apply to this section, but perhaps it’s taken a little more seriously here. Pointless, mindless, or just plain stupid threads will be closed or moved.

Now I think you’re ready.
