PaulT August 4, 2016, 1:42pm #136

Runescape was popular in the early 00’s because it had very low system requirements and a very structured and straightforward gameplay environment which appealed to many young people. Interest in the game was mostly spread by word of mouth, on BBS or through young people. The idiosyncratic nature of the gameplay does not translate well into marketing schemes or “Improvement” teams efforts to “Balance” or “Upgrade” the game.

The original game should have been left alone; Andrew Gower should have made it open source and let people run their own servers. RS2 should have been totally open source. You should have been able to play on the official servers or do your own modding etc.

The low system requirements and portability of Runescape would have opened up a whole world of gaming and development for many young people. Unfortunately people were very greedy and basically it’s all just a commercial scheme now. If people wanted to play a cheap java clone of Everquest or Asherons Call, they would. And with free MMORPGs like NWN or LOTR:O there’s no reason to play RS with the current state of affairs.

And to bring this back to the original question, the reason private servers didn’t take off (including when the original PS was posted by Winterlove on the Kaitnieks forums) is because the motive for development is so often wrong. The development on private servers is geared towards trying to fix something that was never really broken in the first place, just conceptually flawed. It’s not about making money or attracting the most players, it’s about making a game that is fun to play.
