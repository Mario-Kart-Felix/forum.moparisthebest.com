So, as not anyone of you might know there’s a PMod room on RuneScape for PMods and JMods where they have surgeries in.
http://runescape.wikia.com/wiki/Player_Moderator#Surgeries_and_the_P-Mod_room

It looks the same as the Dungeon below the Varrock West bank, just without the huge gate and the pickables. However, it was added in 474 or later, but I felt like doing something for a few minutes when I was bored so I opened my Delta and started.

Tutorial[size=14pt][/size]
Files edited: client.java || Difficultly: 0.1/10 || Time needed: 60 seconds || Abilities: None, like myself.

First off, I disabled the Varrock West Bank Dungeon Stairs that lead to it (unless you’re doing a remake and you want the stairs to work you should be good with it, noone will climb down there anyways…)

The rest is self explanatory.
So once you have added your dialogue cases (tutorials: http://www.moparisthebest.com/smf/index.php/topic,522617.0.html, search for dialogue tuts) just follow my steps

So first off the dialogue itself

if(NPCID == 2244 && playerRights >= 1){ talk2("You will now be sent to the @red@PMod Room@bla@.", "Leave by using the stairs!", 2244); NpcDialogueSend = true; toX = 3190; toY = 9828; sM(" You are now in the player moderator room. Please remember that anything"); sM(" discussed in the room has to be kept secret. Leaking any kind of information"); sM(" will lead to a demotion (--> Moderator Guidelines)."); }

That’s where Clicking the NPC is opening the dialogue interface, teleporting you to the Varrock Bank dungeon which isn’t accessable at this point (other than talking to Lumbridge Guide with Player rights of 1 or higher) and sending three warning messages (sM" ")

Next we need the Stairs, that’s where I failed. And I am a beginner, so far I’ve never coded anything good.

So, I had my CMD open and when I clicked the stairs in the varrock dungeon it wrote the exact coords and the ObjectID.
I searched for it in client.java, and didn’t found it, so I added it myself:

if ((objectID == 1734) && (objectX == 3187) && (objectY == 9833)) { resetPos(); toX = 3230; //back to lumbridge toY = 3230; //guide heightLevel = 0; break; }

So yeah, that’s it basically.
For some reason there’s a small issue, but it doesn’t change anything in the usefulness of the feature, as it fully works.

Alright, that’s my first work so far actually, I hope I don’t get too much flame on this, I couldn’t get to work on this kind of stuff too much yet.
