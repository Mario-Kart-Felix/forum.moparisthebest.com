Well, first a comical suggestion - sell answers to past test papers.

A more serious suggestion - like regecks mentioned, soft drink. Back when I was one of those 14yr old junior students, soft drink cans were sold for $1 each on Friday sport afternoons.
Everyone brought them, and they were always sold.

There’s a few advantages to this:

  1. You can buy in bulk - bulk buying power is often what can lead a business to prosperity, because it puts them in a more competitive position; they can sell for cheaper.
  2. Soft drink isn’t like milk… If it wasn’t all sold, it’s safe to keep for next week.

Now with a thing like soft drink, which is low risk (low risk like banking your money and collecting interest compared to share investments), you know that they will sell.
So say you had a school of say… 1000, since you’re a high school student, and England (If you’re english :P) is a lot bigger country then Australia.
You could expect at least 70% of those to buy soft drink at a dollar a can. What you could do, is buy direct from Coca Cola for several weeks at a time.

The above is assuming you have capital though.
