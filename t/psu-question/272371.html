Mopman July 31, 2016, 6:49am #2

Link to the PSU and mobo and someone will be able to tell you if its suitable.

A “rail” is a single voltage supply. Most PSUs (all, if theyre atx compatible) have a +3.3v, a +5v and a +12v rail. Some PSUs advertise as having multiple +12v rails, this is pretty much a scam on every PSU I’ve ever seen, maybe its true for some high end supplies but I doubt it. It’s marketing bs you don’t need to worry about.

moparisthebest July 31, 2016, 6:50am #4

There is a plug that plugs into your motherboard from the PSU, and it will either be 20 or 24 pins. Count them, and that is the one you need. (the majority of PSUs today have what they call 20+4 pins, which means they will work for either 20 or 24, but not all of them do, so you have to pay attention.)
