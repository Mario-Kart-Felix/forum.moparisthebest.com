Try to cut your teeth working on some open-source projects or modding teams. Experience is the best teacher. Also, you are going to need to learn math.

The best advice you’re going to get is: Do what you love, and if you can’t, then love what you do. Pick a major that genuinely interests you.

0/10 would not recommend.

You have to get real lucky to get teachers at a community college who actually give a fuck about you. I went to one of the most well known community colleges and my experience sucked ass and I did not keep anyone from there. IF you have the money to afford all of the expenses, go to a university. Chances are you are going to get a better experience all around, plus you will be able to make connections that will last for 4 years instead of 2 years AND if you’re living close to campus/on campus you will be able to attend more clubs and it will be more convenient.

As much as it sucks to admit it, 80% of getting a job is who you know. Yes knowing your shit is important, but if you don’t make strong connections with both staff and peers it will be much harder to land a job you want, especially in today’s job market. Also, it will be easier to get recommendation letters if you want to continue your education. Pick a couple teachers that are relevant to your field and take them repeatedly, and introduce yourself the first day. It might feel weird just going up and saying “hello, I just wanted to introduce myself.” Let them know you’re highly interested in the field and their class.
