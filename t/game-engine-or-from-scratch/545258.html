davidi2 August 4, 2016, 1:32pm #17

Two very different beasts to tackle. Writing a game engine (even 2d, assuming you are actually creating an engine and not just a canvas with an X-buffer method) is a project in itself. It’s something you could use for many projects to come.

I too was one of those people that was like “I dont want to use anything that isnt MY code” (still am, to an extent), but after you actually start making games you realize that there are people out there better than you that know what they are doing, and if you want to create a game without going crazy you should probably use a library, or many of them. Writing a game is about the content, writing an engine is the more technical side. So it’s up to you what you want to do, either of them are great experience. Just keep in mind writing an engine is not easy and you might not even get to game development before giving up.
