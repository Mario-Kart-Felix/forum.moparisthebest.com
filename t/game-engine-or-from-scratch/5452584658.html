You have to look at time efficiency, time constraints and how much extra work you’re doing that isn’t required. The wheel you’re using has generally been battle tested and is written a lot better than what most standard game devs could manage. So if his goal is to create his own game, it’s pretty obvious which route is the more efficient one of the two. Considering one of the biggest stumbling blocks is underestimating the work required to actually create something, this hugely decreases the pain that the developer will feel. Not to mention, there are some great engines and libraries out there that will do what OP wants.

If it’s a learning experience that’s desired, then sure. Go write your own engine. That’s not what the OP wanted though. I assume from what he has posted, that he wants to be able to implement the game ideas as easily as possible. There’s certainly enough room allowed for customization and optimisation there without having to build everything yourself. Everyone uses existing code because it’s logical. It doesn’t make you less of a developer, lazy or terrible at your job. It’s just the better way of doing something.

I stand by what I said. There’s nothing wrong with using existing libraries. Reinventing the wheel when you’re aiming for efficiency (time) is idiotic and understanding your goals/constraints is vital. If it’s for learning purposes, then go write your own. I agree with that part. It’s all good to throw around ego and claim that you’re developing your own engine, but you start to realise how silly that is if you’re working on a large project. Don’t make life more difficult on yourself.
