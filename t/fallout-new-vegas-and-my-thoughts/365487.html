Fallout: New Vegas and my thoughts

General Discussion
kihomura August 1, 2016, 12:32am #1

[center]
[/center]October 19, Fallout: New Vegas, a game developed by Obsidian Entertainment was released. Another game in one of the greatest RPG series of all time. This game features many changes such as the ability to look down the iron sights of your gun, a feature that the previous game, Fallout 3, lacked. There is many other interesting additions such as creation of chems, foods, and ammunition, rather than relying on the wasteland and creatures for your items.

[center]

[/center]
My review

I think the game could have been great. I don’t believe in sequels having to be ‘bigger and better’ than their previous title. This game is alright overall, but not anything special. The voice acting sounds like a gargling asshole filtered with the moist cloth of a thousand orphan tears. It’s worse than Fallout 3’s (where every character was voiced by like three dudes).

The iron sights are nothing to look forward to, they are just a let-down; when you go to look down the sight of your gun, your hand teleports to the middle of the screen and when you release the sights, it displays a smooth animation of your hand going back to it’s regular position on the screen. Why is this? Did they not check this game for quality? It feels like I’m playing Fallout 3 with a shitty iron sight mod. The sad thing is, iron sight mods for Fallout 3 are better than the iron sights included in this game.

Ammo creation is alright, but it’s unorganized in a way that you can easily mess up and end up losing ammunition. Basically it’s just a big unfiltered list of names. Examples of the list could be “9mm ammo” and “Breakdown 9mm ammo.” A new player isn’t going to understand what the fuck this means, and nobody reads the damn help. It should be user-friendly. At first I thought “Breakdown 9mm ammo” was some sort of hardcore 9mm ammo, but no, I just ended up breaking open some bullets, getting some resources and then make 9mm ammo again, except less. The menu is confusing also because you can’t click on any of the lit up options, you have to hover over it, and then press the accept button. Why can’t I just click the goddamn option, is it really necessary for me to precisely hover over it and then click accept?

I don’t have much to say about chem/food creation. To me it’s not particularly useful. Maybe it is in hardcore mode, I haven’t tried. From the looks of it, it is just like the ammo creation interface and you can make stuff like cooked gecko hide steak. Cool story bra.

The new weapons are crap, they left in older weapons, but added new weapons that do not match the old ones. For example, there is a 9mm pistol that actually looks like a damn pistol from our time and the 10mm pistol from Fallout 3 that looks like a futuristic handgun. It feels like they slapped together this game rather quickly and it just doesn’t work. Modding weapons is sloppy, to mod your weapon you buy the part from people throughout the game and then go into your Pipboy’s weapon menu, find the weapon you want to mod, hover over it, and press the mod key (default X on PC) and then select the part. It’s very uncreative and I wouldn’t doubt that there is a mod for Fallout 3 that adds this feature much better than this.

The storyline is uninteresting and just overall boring. Shot in the head, and now you want to find this fucker and shake hands with him? The only thing I really like about this game is the character Victor. He’s a cowboy robot and extremely friendly. Seriously, out of this entire game, that is the thing I enjoy the most.

The intro to the game is worse than Fallout 3’s. First you wake up in a creepy dude’s house and he starts talking to you. It’s like he raped you while sleeping. Do you remember the GOAT exam from Fallout 3? Well the quiz is even worse. You just reply with retarded responses “Smelly cheese.” You can just select random shit and choose your tag skills afterward, like the GOAT exam. He has a mustache.

Fallout: New Vegas is bug ridden, even worse than Fallout 3. More crashes to desktop on PC, and they actually managed to fuck up the “Continue” button on the intro screen sadly enough. NPCs around the world can randomly fly through roofs, and heads can go into exorcist mode and start spinning around. Walking paths are also messed up and creatures get stuck in boulders.

Overall, I’m just going to go back to Fallout 3 and play it over and over until the next game comes out. Maybe it won’t be made by careless dicks.

What did you guys think of New Vegas?

Written by John Gonzalez,[10] Fallout: New Vegas takes place during the year 2281, four years after the estimated end of Fallout 3, and 204 years after the Great War of 2077, making this installment chronologically the latest in the series thus far.[11] The game is set in post-apocalyptic Las Vegas, Nevada and the Mojave Desert known as the "Mojave Wasteland" which is roughly the same size as the "Capital Wasteland" in Fallout 3.[11] Unlike other cities in the Fallout series, [b]Las Vegas was not struck directly by a nuclear attack. Its buildings remain intact, and mutation of its inhabitants is minimal.[/b][11] The city is divided between various factions, most notably the New California Republic (NCR), the slave-driving Caesar's Legion, and the various factions of New Vegas itself.[9][12] Landmarks featured in Fallout: New Vegas include the Hoover Dam that supplies power to the city[12] and the Helios 1 solar energy plant.[13]
