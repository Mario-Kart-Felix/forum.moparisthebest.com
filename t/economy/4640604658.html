zyle1992 August 4, 2016, 1:13am #28
tyb97:

Contract system? Explain.

I got the idea from EVE Online. It wont replace an economy (nor should it), but it provides a buffer between the price of an item, and the amount of effort needed to get it. I’ll try to explain it the best I can.

A player can issue a contract, which entails either buying or selling a certain amount of given items. So I could create a contract, in which a person, or a group of people, mine for me 10k rune ess. Each contract would have an expiration time, by which the contract must be completed. If someone has accepted the contract, and the expiration time is near, they could either request an extension from the issuer, or pay the collateral for not completing the contract in time. After a contract has been successfully completed, the person who accepted the contract would receive the reward.

Contract:
Issuer: zyle1992
Expiration: 11/23/2012 17:00
Requirements:
    Rune Ess: 10,000
    Air Runes: 1,000

Collateral:
    Coins: 50,000

Reward:
    Coins: 1,250,000

After a player sees the contract, they can either accept it then, and get to work, or accept and complete the contract if they already have the required items, or contact the issuer, and negotiate a different deal.

This would work best in addition to an Exchange type system, as it provides the players with more control

EDIT: I should also note that it would be wise to “reserve” the detailed items. Let’s say a person drafts a contract, with a 22m coin reward. If they don’t have the 22m as a reward, they should not be able to create the contract. Likewise, if they have 22m, create the contract, they shouldn’t be permitted to have less than that amount at any time (killed, trade it, buy other items, or anything else that brings them below 22m). Or you can just have the items removed at the time of creation so exploiting it doesn’t become possible.

Another idea is to have contracts, or mini-quests given by random Npcs. Let’s say there’s some guy in Lumbridge, the player talks to him, finds out that some goblins in the abandoned house took something from him, and he’ll pay coins/items to get it back. Or the guards of the local city can hire you to kill goblins/black wizards/* and will pay you for it.

tyb97:

Basically yes. This might require some client-side interface changes to be made (new sidebar interface for contracts available to the player, contracts in progress, completed contracts, etc) for it to be a concise functionality.

There’s loads of different ways that you can augment an economy, but the most effective one would be something similar to the Grand Exchange, where people are able to set their own prices. Giving people options is what makes/breaks an economy.

And as said earlier, an items “true value” is also taken into consideration. You don’t have to have party hats, or santa hats as rare items. You could sell them in the general store as gifts for 10 GP if you wanted. The reason they’re rare in RuneScape, is because they were only given out one year, at the very beginning, and a large span of time has passed since then, making the net amount available very little. If you had trimmed armor as a 1% chance in a treasure trail/difficult boss, then it would be more rare than party hats, and thus more valuable. The same applies to gilded armor, or anything you want.
