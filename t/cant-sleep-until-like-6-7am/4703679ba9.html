Ambokile August 4, 2016, 1:31am #54

Actually, your body doesn’t “need” heroin to function. Inhibitory neurotransmitters are usually released, and these neurotransmitters prevent dopamine neurotransmitters from being released into the synapse. When the dopamine neurotransmitters aren’t released, the dopamine receptor is not triggered and so there is no effect. However, when the body’s natural opiates activate opiate receptors, the release of inhibitory neurotransmitters is stopped and therefore dopamine is not inhibited and is released. Heroin merely mimics the natural opiates, turning off the release of the inhibitory neurotransmitters and so releasing dopamine. The human body will then get used to this increased level of dopamine and will begin to crave it, because such a level will not be reached in everyday life.
