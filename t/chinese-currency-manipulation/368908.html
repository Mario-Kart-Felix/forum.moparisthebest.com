theUnforgiven August 1, 2016, 12:59am #1

You may hear in the news about how China is manipulating the RMB or how Obama’s proposal for regulations on currency trade with china was rejected by the G-20 members. So I’ve decided to make a thread about it and voice my opinion.

First of all a little background:
The U.S. claims China is manipulating its currency by buying American bonds, this in return inflates the American dollar, while keeping the Yuan low. Why is this good? Because this takes advantage of the low-cost of the service sector in China, and makes exports look more attractive.

As a result, the U.S. claims that jobs are shipped from the U.S. straight to China, and that’s why the whole entire world should gang up on China, probably bankrupting the whole entire country. The problem with this theory is that the jobs were never in the U.S. In the United States corporations have already developed hi-tech machinery that can do the job better than any american worker. And simply stiff-arming China isn’t going to fix the problem of out-sourcing, corporations will just move to other developing countries like India, Vietnam, Indonesia, and Brazil.

Not to mention, the U.S. just printed $600 billion dollars that’s obviously some form of currency manipulation don’t you think.

Sorry guys, this is the price of Capitalism, survival of the fittest. When we signed the constitution and laid down the stepping stones of what would become the free-market, we acknowledged that one day we would have to compete with other nations.

What the Obama administration is doing makes me sick. They have analysts that have a far more broad understanding of this than a 14-year old kid in highschool, but all he cares about is getting re-elected. He finally hit a topic that both republicans and democrats agree on and is taking advantage of the close-mindedness of the country.
He needs to accept that he isn’t getting re-elected and go back to trying to fix the economy. I mean seriously his approval ratings are lower than George Bush’s were during his second term.
