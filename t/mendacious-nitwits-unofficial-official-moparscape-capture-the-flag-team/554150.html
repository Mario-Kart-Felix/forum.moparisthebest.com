I decided we should enter 32C3’s Capture the Flag this year, so I registered a team - ‘Mendacious Nitwits’. Sorry, mdog, you will never live it down…

You can find out more about 32C3’s contest here: https://32c3ctf.ccc.ac/announcements/

If you’re not familiar with ctf, essentially they are hacking/information security challenges - a little like the one I posted that I made a few months ago, except actually well-run and entertaining, because these guys know how to make a challenge fun.

Winning qualifies the team for Defcon 2016 in Las Vegas. It runs from the 27th Dec, for 48 hours, and the aim is to get as many points as we can in the 48 hours. More points = higher on the leaderboard.

32C3 is a jeopardy style challenge, which means we will receive a bunch of ‘hints’ on the 27th. For example, one may be a link to a web service with a hint on some flaw that lets us break into it, one may be an executable running on a remote server we have to write an exploit for, etc. Usually, a wide variety of skillsets is covered, so if you’re hopeless at writing shellcode but are really good at breaking web apps, or know your way around Java bytecode or crypto or anything really, you are probably going to find something to spend time on. Absolute newcomers are totally welcome, as long as your mindset is ‘I am going to try to figure this shit out’ and not ‘I am going to complain as soon as I hit something I don’t understand’.

You can find the team here, PM me for the invite code: https://ctftime.org/team/21200

Bring your best boolean lag handlers and clinet.java.
