2.) Preparing
Before you reformat, you may want to backup stuff you want to keep, etc. Not much here.
Just zip the stuff up and burn them to cds.

3.) Clean up!
Ok, now that you’ve backed up what you want and burned it, insert the cd then reboot, when its at the loading screen thing (Dell, HP), hit ESC or F12 to get to your BIOS boot screen, choose your CD\DVD driver, then it’ll say “Press any key to boot from CD…”, press a key. Afterwards (when the drivers are loaded), hit F10, get in recovery mode (Hit the R key), type “1” and enter (assuming you have windows xp installing on C:), enter your system adminstrator password (if its not set just hit enter),type “map”, hit enter, type “format C: /fs:ntfs” and enter (assuming your system is NTFS, if your on FAT32, just type “format C:”), MAKE SURE YOU HAVE WHAT YOU WANT, AFTER THE NEXT STEP, THERES NO GOING BACK!, type “y” and enter, let it finish reformatting, when its done, type “exit” and enter.

4.) Installation
It should have rebooted when you hit exit, again go to boot menu, choose cd drive.
Let the drivers load, choose option 1 and your good to go. On the set-up choose “NTFS Format” (not the quick option either, just NTFS format) and let that finish, next, it should be copying the system files, the it’ll reboot (I think), then your on a graphic set-up. You should be able to handle it from there.
