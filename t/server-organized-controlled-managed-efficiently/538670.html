What revision you like?[OLD: 0 to 400][NEWER: 401 to 600][NEW: 601 to present]
Old, as most on Mopar.

What makes a server unique?
The content it contains. Less like RuneScape, more like private servers.

What you expect from?

  1. Staff - Professionalism, helpfulness, friendliness. Nobody wants to be driven away by the staff members.
  2. Developers - Consistent updates. Their job description is to do just that, not tend to player needs. If they do not communicate with the community, that’s fine by me. As long as they’re doing their job making updates, I’m fine with it.
  3. Website - NOT a frequently used template that you see every other server with. Organization.
  4. Client - To be able to connect to the server. To run smoothly.
  5. Server - Unique content.

How updates should be managed?
As long as they’re updating, manage it however the owner likes. But always perform the ‘System Update:’ instead of randomly resetting the server. Once updates are complete, post them on the forums for the server.

How advertising should be done and where?
Gold memberships in toplists. Sponsored spots. As your answer to ‘where?’, in the larger toplists. I suppose Google advertisements couldn’t hurt, but, in my opinion, they’re a waste of money. And when I say “sponsored spots”, I don’t mean the big 728x90 pixel banners. They should look like a listing in the toplist, just above all the other servers in the list. If it’s a big banner, it looks more like an advertisement and will receive less clicks.

What content must be present?
Private servers are a fun way to kill time. Time consumption content must be present. Though, nothing like staring at your character woodcut for half an hour. They must be interactive and enjoyable. Such as pking, minigames, etc. However, skilling is still a large factor. There are quite a bit of skillers in the RuneScape private server community, believe it or not.
