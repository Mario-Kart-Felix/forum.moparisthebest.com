Unhandled August 1, 2016, 12:28am #2

Buy a new drive (1-2tb’s are piss cheap now) and create one partition for your OS and one for your data (a 20/80% split works well for most). Install the OS on that drive and start copying whatever you need from the old drive to the new data partition. You can even redirect the documents/music/pictures folders of your new OS to the data partition. With this in place, you’ll be able to reinstall your OS as often as you want and never have to worry about losing data. You can use the old drive for backups and whatnot.
