lothy August 4, 2016, 9:57am #9

I think global chat is one of those things that grows more important as the game’s population density decreases. I wouldn’t put a price on it because then you effectively lock newcomers out of it.

Perhaps player-based moderation would be key. Players, if they come across a spammer, can enter a command to vote in favour of banning him. If a certain percentage of online players vote in favour of the ban then the spammer is muted temporarily (perhaps 30 to 60 minutes). The mute could apply to the logged in account only, every player account associated with the person’s forum account, or even the IP address.
There’s the risk of abuse I suppose, but that’s always the case with ‘mark as spam’ approaches. Even if there is a miscarriage of justice though, it would be short-lived. And if certain people were obviously being victimised then perhaps some sort of exemption could be applied to their account.

EDIT: That, or you could just make the ignore list functional for global chat as well.
