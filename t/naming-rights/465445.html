there’s a lot of misunderstanding in your questions, firstly you generally cannot copyright names or logos, logo artwork can be copyrighted and that copyright may also be protected as a trademark for disputes, an interesting tidbit is that the coca-cola logo for instance is not copyrighted but is trademarked

secondly, trademarks can be disputed as part of the registration process and unregistered trademarks still carry some rights, first-to-use and first-to-register disputes aren’t unheard of

it would help if you explained why you want to trademark something and what you hope to get out of it

as far as I’m aware, you can’t copyright ‘services’

yes, you’ll automatically have copyright to the contents of the page + the artwork of the logo but that’s all the copyright extends to, by creating a logo in paint or photoshop you don’t necessarily get the same rights as a trademarked logo

there are exceptions of course, if you go ahead and start advertising this service and actually get a market for it, you’ll have a stronger case if someone trademarks the same logo/brand name and starts providing the same service

MistaBigBoss:

Second question is simple, say I had an unregistered trademark/service mark, could someone register my trademark/service mark as a registered trademark? Even if I could prove that I was the first one to use that trademark/service mark for my products/services? Would I be able to defend myself (or sue him) under common law’s passing of tort?

yes, as I stated, there are cases where it has been “first-to-use” an unregistered trademark versus “first-to-register”, you can dispute the claim for the trademark during the registration process (note: it can take up to 6 months) and potentially you could sue them but I have no freaking idea

if you want a real answer go register a trademark and see a lawyer

something I want to stress is everything is fair game if you don’t have a registered trademark, if a dispute occurs the mediators will look at who was the first to use the trademark and first to start the registration process, they will look at marketshare etc
