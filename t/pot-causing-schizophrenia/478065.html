jme August 4, 2016, 2:07am #3

I don’t think there’s any credible evidence to suggest cannabis alone will cause schizophrenia but it still shouldn’t be ruled out. Its widely accepted that it could make pre-existing mental problems worse. Some people seem destined to get schizophrenia later in their life. You could be one of those people without knowing it, and cannabis use may quicken the onset more rapidly. This seems to be true with most drugs though, including alcohol.

If you smoke a lot of weed and are diagnosed with schizophrenia, your doctor won’t even blame it on the weed. He will tell you it was probably inevitable.

It’s a mental disorder, and we don’t understand as much about them as we’d like to. However it does have real physical effects, including too much dopamine receptor activity. With anti-psychotic meds this activity will be reduced usually fixing the problem as long as you take the meds.

Smoking it is okay for millions of people, but others would disagree. It’s not something I suggest you start, success and money will give you a better high than pot ever will.
