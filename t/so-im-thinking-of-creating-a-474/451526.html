Lin August 3, 2016, 7:43pm #1

Recently, I teamed up with my friend to help him on his server in hopes to make a couple bucks. After packing all sorts of data to the cache and improving the “on demand fetcher”, not to mention fixing a ton of poorly-done sprite customization that came with the client, it felt really dry. I wanted to make an “HD” version of one of the servers, but he wasn’t really up for it, and plus the one he had in mind was a PK server - one I thought was a waste of the 718 client. So, I started looking some stuff up to get a 474 client with 474 or 508 maps, and I actually found one with custom-made 474 maps. Pretty neat. So after trying it out to see if it worked, I started thinking about the server.

Not too long ago I had stopped working on Rarity v2 for a number of reasons I won’t get into. Rather than start a whole new server from scratch, I figured, why not build off of that? It’s got a really strong core and would be easy to convert. So, that’s what I’m doing.

What I want to know is what kind of server should I make? The reason I chose 474 was because it has construction and hunter (the two funnest skills IMO), no grand exchange, no summoning, no ugly gameframe, and no HD stuff. It’s my favorite revision by far, and I feel now is a good time to make a server. The best server I ever played was, I’ll admit, a Delta server. But it was just so well-done - it had my favorite home, my favorite custom items, a public yell command, and so much more I just loved. This pretty much includes a specific home with a bunch of custom objects, multiple areas to train against monsters, bosses, and a skilling area. I have no problem with this type of server, but it just feels like I could do so much more with a 474 client.

I really want to make some sort of RuneScape recreation, but that’s a huge task and I don’t really remember jack shit about how it was around the 474 era. Basically, what I mean is no true home, a player-run economy, plenty of areas to train skills, quests, and a lot of places to visit and train combat.

What do you guys think? I’m really lost here and could use some guidance. Thanks,

~Lin
