[center]Hello, my name is Brandon. I’m 16 years old and I’ve been dropped out of school for a few months now. When I dropped out, I started working. I’ve been doing things that involve carpentry, painting, and other things that would try my strength and endurance. Now I may be forced to go back to school, but the 2nd semester will be over in about a month, and if I go to school in fall, I will be a freshmen again (I’m supposed to be a junior in fall).
I’ve been living poor for years now, so I know how it feels to stress about groceries (even with food-stamps), etc. I realize I’m much too intelligent to have myself and my future family stranded in poverty, and it scares the fuck out of me.

You’ve likely already noticed, I’m a big bitch about school. However, I do have sufficient reasoning. My schooling has pretty much always been screwed up. I’ve always missed more days than allowed, in fact I went to truancy court for the first time in kindergarten. In the past 2 years, I’ve been to 4 different high schools, of various peoples. In order, I went to a school with a total of approx. 800 that were all country (so I’m an outcast), another which was 75% black (hood black) and the white people were so childish that I had to leave, a large one that I consider to be a cluster fuck, and the last school I attended was in the middle of a hood, with around 300 students, and only 4, yes 4, other whites.
On top of this, my personal life has been a train wreck for years. I don’t have much family. My mom & dad aren’t doing well at all, and it of course hurts me to even know that. Any other family I have is only ‘family’, and doesn’t even know anything about me. I’m simply not focused when I’m in school on account of all the other bullshit that’s going around, and I can easily find myself ditching classes to smoke some dank or a cigarette on account of my personal issues.

I feel if I went to one of those special schools, where you just go for 4-5 hours a day and do work on a computer, I would try my hardest and succeed. It’s my belief that those schools were originally created with the intent to help those who couldn’t take the conventional approach to an education, however I was told back in fall that in order to get in I would either need to be sent there for being an uncontrollable pest, or I would need to have 10+ credits. I have 2.
I’ve no idea what career field I desire, but I’m well aware that many jobs (like oil field worker) pay great for young people, offer good benefits, but age your body exceedingly fast. I’m hesitant of this. I’d like to be 50 when I’m 50, and not 80 when I’m 50.

I really need some advice, preferably from someone who’s already succeeded. Any ideas or suggestions?[/center]

I fudgeed up in school last year as well, I decided to play around more than I should have and when it came around to doing exams, I scrapped by and passed most but it wasn’t enough credits to pass the year. It shouldn’t be hard to find a good school where people actually care about their education, go on a search, make some appointments, and get a tour during school hours.

When I was young, before NCEA, I never really understood how important school was, and in order to do what you like, you’re going to need a plan b which is where your education comes into play. For example, I want to go into the movie business, it’s a hard deal and it’s not 100% guaranteed, so while I work towards my goal I will need to get a decent job (that you or I may not even like) to pay the bills. Even the army requires a good literacy and numeracy level, so don’t expect to get any jobs if you don’t have a background in any education. Schooling is the very very minimum requirement, succeeding with anything (if anything) below that is just a miracle.

If you’re really stuck up on not going to school then the only thing I can think of is correspondence, you should be able to find something near you. It really is just school online, I’m doing it right now to get the small amount of credits I need to NCEA level 3.
