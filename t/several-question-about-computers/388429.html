  1. https://www.moparisthebest.com/smf/index.php/topic,483858.msg3544840.html#msg3544840

  2. Ok, crash computer course:
    To build a computer, you need these things:

  3. Motherboard, RAM, and CPU, these have to be compatible with each other.

  4. Case with PSU (power supply) to put it all in, the only thing you must worry about here is that the PSU has enough watts to run your entire system, if you get a top-of-the-line video card, it might pull most of your power.

  5. Video card, unless you have one integrated into your motherboard, but for a gaming rig, you probably don’t want to do this. Nowadays you will want a video card that has a PCI-E x16 v2.0 interface, and your motherboard should have an empty slot for this, it probably will.

  6. Optionally, you can have a dvd-burner, or other optical drive, it will run about $20 at newegg.

  7. You need a hard drive too, you can buy a good one at newegg, or use the one out of your old computer.
    Then it just all snaps together, it’s really easy, and we can help you here with it.

  8. Whether or not drivers are available is your only concern here. The only place I’ve ever ran into large problems with this is with wireless card drivers, hopefully your desktop will be connected with a wire though. Either way, a quick google of your set up with ‘ubuntu’ will usually tell you if there are any problems.

  9. I don’t use an anti-virus, but then I spend 99.9% of my computer time using Linux.

  10. Basically, it’s just making your hardware (CPU, RAM, or Video card) run faster than advertised. It generates more heat, which can damage things, and sometimes makes it less stable. Generally if you have to ask what over-clocking is, you don’t want to do it. I’ve never had the need to overclock, another few hundred Mhz on your 3+Ghz CPU isn’t going to make a noticeable difference.

  11. For a computer you already own? You should probably call HP and ask.

Indeed. I tunnel using ssh from my Macbook Pro to a box at my house.

ssh -D 4567 user@domain.com -p 80 (Many schools/colleges block all traffic except port 80, so run sshd on port 80 on your host box).

System preferences > Network > Airport or Ethernet (whichever you’re using) > Proxies > Socks > SOCKS PROXY SERVER: 127.0.0.1 Port: 4567 (or whichever your chose to create it on above) > Apply

And you should be ready to go! Once that information is in there, you can flick the proxy on and off (assuming you’re still connected to your sshd) with:

networksetup -setsocksfirewallproxystate Ethernet off
networksetup -setsocksfirewallproxystate Ethernet on

Replace ethernet with the network interface you’re using.

Saves you having to go through menus each time you want to configure it.
