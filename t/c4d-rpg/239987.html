SomethingToHide July 31, 2016, 2:44am #6

Yes there’s plenty of stuff.

I on the other hand just pretty much learned myself.

Look for UVUnwrapper. It’s free and great. Or you could pirate the pro edition, but the free one does it for me.

Or you could do it all in C4D. Most of the time it’s easier that way. You could get some bodypaint tutorials on that.

Pretty much just Windows/Layout/Bodypaint UV Edit.

Pull out the UVPolygon Selection tool and select polygons, and in the UVManager you can set what type of mapping to apply to it. Then you can move and scale them around to all fit. Then what I do is:

  1. Select all the polygons
  2. If you haven’t, create a material and apply it, and under Color for the texture pick Create New Texture and make it pretty big.
  3. Do a Layer/Outline Polygons
  4. Save that texture

In photoshop you can open that and then you can see the UV Layout and you can paint on that.

It’s pretty much the materials and lighting. The lighting makes it look really small. And the materials make it seem light.
The lighting makes it look like it’s almost floating. You need to give it like harsh sun lighting so it appears correct size. And make the tiles a lot smaller. IRL that thing would cross many more tiles. That makes it look small.
