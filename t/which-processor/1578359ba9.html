brownsymcard March 12, 2019, 3:28am #53

The quad is 2.4 ghz while the e6850 is 3.0ghz. That in itself doesn’t say much, but the essential reason is because very very very few programs even touch the 3rd and 4th core as of right now. Benchmark tests show the e6850 out performs the q6600 on all but 3d rendering programs, very recent games, and a few other side applications. Since 2 cores are unused on 99% of todays applications, the e6850 outperforms it because its 2 cores at 3ghz, not 2 at 2.4ghz.
Also a quad would require a p35 chipset, which means he would most likely need a new motherboard also which would be another $150.

Normal gaming and design/daily use; get the e6850. Crysys and professional 3d modeling; get the q6600.
