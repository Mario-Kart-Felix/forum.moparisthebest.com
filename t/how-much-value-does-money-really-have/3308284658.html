[quote=“ollie123, post:22, topic:330828”]The sad truth is, money makes the world go round.[/quote]But, does the world need to go round in order to exist peacefully?

Money was developed in an early age to determine the value of items. I know I wont hit every key point in the matter, but say your a nomad wondering South America. You fish for a living while others farm. You want crops while they want fish. Back then it was based on bartering and trading. But how do you determine how many carrots equals a fish? And what if it’s the middle of winter and you’re unable to grow anything? Upon this crossroad, currency was established along with a universal value of all things. Trading irrelevant items became obsolete due to how different the circumstances for each item was. This is the only true established purpose of money, and without it we would resort back to some form of systematic trade, lowering ourselves to cavemen.

Now, you look at society’s philosophy on the way it’s distributed, how it’s put on a pedestal, favored over all things. We’ve become tied down to material, no doubt. But, without a physical form a value for anything, I don’t believe we’d be as far as we are in society today.
