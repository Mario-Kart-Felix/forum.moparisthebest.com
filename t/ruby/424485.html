[quote=“The Outsider, post:18, topic:424485”]What kind of things are programmed in Lisp/Clojure? Never heard of them before.[/quote]there was a period of time where they were used for AI, but that boom died after people started accomplishing the same things with imperative languages. overall, most lisp dialects are general purpose and can be used for anything really… although you do sometimes get dialects specialised for one job (the guys that made Crash Bandicoot and Jak and Daxter used a Lisp dialect for the scripting side of their engine, Ypsilon Scheme was written as an addition to a game engine and is fast) and the Movitz dialect of Common Lisp which targets the Movitz OS project’s runtime Muerte, which runs on top of a small assembly core and allows the majority of things in it to be written in pure Lisp. the emacs text editor uses Lisp for scripting too, and is pretty damn good for Lisp development as a result.

there’s also a few nooks and crannies where you’ll find Lisp used tdday, but it’s becoming slowly a language of the past which projects like newLISP and Clojure (and certain CL/Scheme dialects) aim to defy, and you’ll find the majority of Lisp programmers both seasoned and fresh only use it because they like it. hell, Lisp is great when you beat the parentheses and weird abstractions
