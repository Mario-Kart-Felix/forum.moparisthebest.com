Regarding the OP, inflation is the big important thing.

This is one of the points of clash between different classes.
High inflation disproportionately hurts rich people because it makes their fortunes worth less. High unemployment disproportionately hurts poor people because they find it harder to go for a long time without a job.

So as a general rule, usually if you have a left wing government in power they will promote full employment, if you have a right wing government in power they will try to lower inflation.

Nothing to do with helping or hurting the economy, just helping their core voters.

Related but separate is how unemployment is seen by the different classes. The owner class like high unemployment because it drives wage costs down. The more unemployed workers we add to an economy, the more wages fall. An example of this correlation can be seen in the U.S. recession of 1982, when unemployment nearly hit 11 percent in the fourth quarter, and real hourly wages fell nearly 50 cents from three years before. The flip-side to this example is the 1980s “Massachusetts Miracle,” when unemployment fell to a phenomenally low 2.7 percent, and McDonald’s began offering twice the minimum wage ($7 an hour) trying to attract workers.
