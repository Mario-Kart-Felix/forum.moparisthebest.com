JavaTC August 3, 2016, 10:33pm #35

Here’s something interesting about the whole “We need guns to defend from a government takeover” arguement.

[quote=“Mr_Quick”]it doesn’t work, either in theory or in practice.

On the theory side, the central fallacy that Americans tend to make when discussing how to prevent government overreach is that we act as though a country’s populace has no power to influence government activities. We prepare ourselves for a situation where the “gubbament” tries to take control and starts an all-out war between government and populace. This is probably a cultural holdover from our experiences as a colonial possession, but in the modern era it’s a silly way of viewing things. Even the most despotic governments don’t oppress all their citizens equally through a blatant show of force. Rather, they tend to prey upon the majority’s fears in order to divide the populace, then quietly oppress the hated few at the behest of the many, slowly stripping away the former’s rights while bleeding the latter dry to fuel their schemes.

Simply put, a government is not some alien force exerting power on the citizenry from above; it is a part of society, and it exists to benefit its supporters, since having the support (or at least acquiescence) of large numbers of people is the essence of power. As such, believing that the citizenry will rise up as one if the US government becomes tyrannical is absurd, because the citizenry is the US government. If tyranny comes to our shores, it will come in a manner that the majority of us find quite agreeable. Thus, if liberty is allowed to die in the hearts and minds of men, no amount of weaponry will save it.

On the practical side, look at Iraq. The Iraqi insurgency wasn’t able to forcibly drive the US out, despite having access to better weaponry than the average American and being comparatively united against a hostile foreign occupation, not to mention the fact that the US was trying to hold foreign soil while maintaining a strong presence across the rest of the globe. In light of this, what are the odds that the American populace could drive the US government from its own soil, aka the one place it would be most determined and best equipped to defend?[/quote]
