So, I have a VPSland VPS aswell, and I’m having alot of issues with TCP and UDP portforwarding with them.
To login amd see your details you have to enter your VPS IP in your browser.
Like mine: http://64.22.121.116
Alright, you will find your address in your e-mail which VPSland sent you after your registration.
It is called Virtuozzo Panel. You also have another plesk, which is and does exactly the same.
You should see your page by yourself, like I did.
Now first you will need to upload your server to your VPS by using FTP.
FTP connection details:
Host: (VPS IP) e.g. 64.22.121.116
Name: administrator
Password: ********* (your signed up password)
Port:
NOTE: VPSland requires no FTP port. (You could use 21)

Select your source folder and start uploading…

After uploading is done, login to you panel again and see if your server has been uploaded succesfully.
Go to: File Manager>C:/InetPub/ftproot/(YOURSERVER)
When you find that, everything is OK for now.

Now find the support e-mail of VPSland and ask them to portforward your VPS with 43594 or 5555, I saw alot of posts on this forum that VPSland doesn’t portforward 43594, but they DO!
They will ask you if you want to use TCP or UDP, you want to say both cause that’s most common.
But you will say TCP because you don’t want trouble
Now they ask you to what port you want to forward 43594 (I have no idea what they mean with that lol). e.g. Forward 43594 to 4633.

That is as far as I know how to use it.
