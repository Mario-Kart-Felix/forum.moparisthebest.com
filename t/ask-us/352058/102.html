Jython August 1, 2016, 12:10pm #106

objectdef is a name for a representation of the definition of a specific type of object entity, complete with name, actions, model reference, etc.
the object1-5 are “objects” in the sense they are entities of a scene… they represent characters, projectiles, ornaments, and other various kinds
landscape files have not i believe changed very much since 317, as i recall they store a 64x64x4 grid of tiles. in the past map_idx was used as a resolution table to map between region coordinates (in the form of a uid/hash, (y << 8) | x iirc) and “type” (map (tiles) vs landscape (objects)) to the corresponding cached resource files (main_file_cache.dat & idx5). now however they use a higher-level interface for cached resources and use base-31 encoded names for look up (mX_Y, lX_Y, nX_Y, umX_Y, ulX_Y) as well as support npc spawn maps and the under-water maps as well. the structure itself has not changed drastically afaik minus accommodating for new parameters in the game engine and variable region sizes
