Just so you guys know, this is for final-Render Stage 2 for Cinema 4D.
However if you’re smart enough you can also use this for the standard Advanced Render.
fR2 just has a MUCH better GI algorithm, which produces better results and much faster.
So with that said, lets go ahead.

This is good if you have a very simple scene, you prefer it, you suck at normal lighting, you’re compositing something onto a backgroung, etc.

You have a crap scene like this.

So you toss in a light. Still isn’t too grand.

HDRI! Here’s what we’ll do. Make a new material and take off all channels but Luminance. In that channel, open up a HDRI you have. Turn the effect to Multiply, and make the Luminance 100 and the HDRI a lower number, usually a number that changes the brightest white to something you can see. This will take some playing around with.

Make a sky object, and put that material on the sky.

Now, open the Render Settings, and goto Options and turn off AutoLight. In Effects > finalRender Stage 2, goto Global Illumination and turn it on. You shoulddd be set =]
