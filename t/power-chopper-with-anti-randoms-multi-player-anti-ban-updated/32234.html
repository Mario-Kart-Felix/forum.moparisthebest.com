da_professa July 30, 2016, 1:54am #13

What do you mean? Do you mean in my script , or in a script you are making… It’s probably in a script you are making, so let me just give u the basics…

First you tell it to find a colour… You can do this by man different commands… You choose which command you want, whichver fits your condition… To find out the list of commands, look in either the scar help file (press F1 after opening scar) , or look in srl docs, which is in /SRL DOCS/ Srl Manual.CHM

So for example once it finds the colour it will store the location in the x, y cordinates… For example you used FindColor(x,y,thecolor,0,0,100,100)… This is the main command
FindColor(var x,y: Integer; color, xs, ys, xe, ye: Integer): Boolean;

I’ll explain the basics… var x, y… This is where it stores the cordinates of where it found the color… So in my example I used x,y… So you have to tell the script at the beginning that var x,y : Integer; So that when it gets the cordinates it knows what to do with it… The color is the color. The xs, ys , xe, ye… Are points on the screen you want to look for the color… For example you want to look in the main screen then you choose the cordinates of the main screen OR (this is where SRL comes in handy) you could just use the command FindMainColor from srl… BUT to use a command from srl you have to include srl at the beginning of your script… This is done by writing {.include /SRL/srl.SCAR} And also you have to setup srl… This is done by putting the procedure SetupSRL somewhere in the beginning of your script…

Hope I helped you,
