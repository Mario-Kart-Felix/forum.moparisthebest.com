Two things are most important when buying a computer for gaming and Stability.
The Processor and the Graphics Card, next comes the Memory and the Disk Space.
Then the cooling/heat ratio, and finally the chassis and misc. items.[/quote]

Not really. You need a good balance between processors and RAM. And for processors, it’s not just the frequency that matters. You need to look at how big the caches are, how many cores it has, hyperthreading, architecture (whether 32bit or 64bit), boost/overclocking capabilities, etc. You need to check RAM types and speeds. For graphics cards, you aren’t going to get anything that great in a laptop anyway, unless you buy a total powerhouse (but why would you? just buy a desktop and save a ton of money).

Disk space isn’t really important anymore either. Disk speed is really important, especially if you want to have large amounts of virtual memory without slowing down fast RAM or a fast processor.
