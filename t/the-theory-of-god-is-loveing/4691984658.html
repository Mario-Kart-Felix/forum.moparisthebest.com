I think it’s this simple,

When you reproduce, there are random mutations in the offsprings. Those offspring can reproduce and pass on those mutations/genes. Anything that increases their survival chances will populate the gene pool. Anything that decreases their chance of survival, will fade out.

So the only factor left is time. It all boils down to whether or not the earth is billions of years old. It is. We can be certain that the bible is wrong as we have proof of the earths age. We also have fossils of our ancestors. But evolution didn’t start with apes, all living things share an ancestor. That includes plants. The only alternative argument is, “god planted those fossils to trick us”

Rabits turn white in the snow/winter. Why? Because by pure chance he was born that way. While his brothers were brown and getting killed by predators, this white rabbit eluded his predators with camoflauge. He lives to reproduce and pass on this survival trait, while the others fade out. This type of change in an animal can happen in no time at all, so just grasp what can happen over millions/billions of years.
