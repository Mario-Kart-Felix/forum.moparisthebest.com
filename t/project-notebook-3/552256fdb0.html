I think MITB is a fair neutral party to hold the money, so long as we can keep in contact with him regularly enough to make it work. We should organize all shipping in advance (it’s really not hard). We just setup pickup and dropoff times for everyone on the list. This gives us a fair schedule to get the book from location to location. Once we know how much shipping will cost, we can try and get an estimate for creating a nice, quality copy of the book. This cost multiplied by the number of members plus the total cost of shipping (which includes shipping the copies to each participant afterwards) will yield the total cost of the project. This number divided by the number of participants gives the cost each person puts in. One person organizes the shipping purchases and each member is responsible for picking up the book and dropping it off, on time.

I’m not sure how we can make a high quality copy of the book, either. That’s something we could possibly contact office max or staples to see if they can do that sort of thing. The cost for the book is also a deposit for people who might have malicious intent. There’s little incentive to steal it because they will be receiving a copy of the completed thing, anyway.

I think if we’re going to put this much effort into the project that we should try and get a decent number of people involved (maybe 30).
