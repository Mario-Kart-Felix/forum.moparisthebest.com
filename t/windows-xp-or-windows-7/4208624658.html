zyle1992 August 2, 2016, 1:47am #40

If you plan on buying a new computer, it will more than likely come with Windows 7. I’m not going to say that one is better than another, because honestly it does boil down to a matter of personal preference. However, there are pros and cons for either. I’m sure a quick google search of “Windows XP 7 performance comparison” would result in a nice list of factors that you can then take into consideration when making your purchase.

I have used both for many years, had good and bad experiences with both. But over the years, I have come to the conclusion that people will prefer whichever they use more. Some people I know started with XP, switched to 7 (new computer, work upgrade, etc), and came to detest how XP operated, and had the same thing happen with people who used 7 and switched to XP.
