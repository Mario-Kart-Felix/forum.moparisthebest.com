I’ve had a similar idea to this recently, in particular I was thinking about writing a domain specific language (similiar to runescript) in which users can upload scripts to a repository and add them to their server, I believe this was the original intention with Apollo actually. The idea of a plugin system is great in that it combines the efforts of the community into one location and users can simply integrate these into their own servers. The idea behind a DSL (domain specific language) was to aid those who aren’t able to comprehend Java, most of us know that runescript was wrote intentionally to tackle this problem among many developers who worked on RuneScape itself. I think that by isolating the core and having game logic wrote through plugins it could make it less of a hassle for newer users to write content.

Without further ado, I wish you best of luck with this project and hope to see it become a success.

Drafted up how adding new packet decoders will look 

<!-- 

	File to produce all the packet decoders for the server.

	Notes:

			Blocks are parsed in order in the way that they are listed.
			Decoders must have the following information listed about them:
				- service, descriptor, [blocks*]
			Services are listed in org.nova.net.ServiceType

		 	* Blocks may not applicable to zero sized packets.

 -->

<packetdecoders>
	<decoder>
		<service>GAME</service>

		<descriptor>
			<id>0</id>
			<size>VAR_BYTE</size>
		</descriptor>

		<blocks>
			<block type="numeric">
				<name>popsicles</name>
				<value>INT16</name>
				<translation>A</translation>
				<order>LITTLE</order>
			</block>

			<block type="string">
				<name>gum_drops</name>
			</block>
		</blocks>
	
	</decoder>
</packetdecoders>
