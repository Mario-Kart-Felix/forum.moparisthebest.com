Most Realtek cards have two settings:
Make front and rear output devices playback two different audio streams simultaneously
Make front and rear output devices playback the same audio stream (default)

They also have front panel jack detection turned on by default, which will disable the back one when it detects something plugged in up front. Again these settings can only be changed with the Realtek drivers, and using their audio manager. If you use the default windows drivers you won’t have access to these settings even with a card that supports it
