That’s an interesting stance. I would agree that at least a good portion of drug users sometimes use drugs to escape reality, even recreational users without a hint of addiction or mental issue - but I wouldn’t agree that’s necessarily a bad thing. Humans do all sorts of things to escape reality (literature, video games, fantasy, kids playing imaginary games etc.), it’s pretty much encoded into us from the start. I assume that’s because albeit without will or intelligence, even evolution realized that sometimes, life just plain sucks and it’s good to get away from it for a bit as long as you’re ready to be back and fighting at some point. Hell, it can help with the fighting spirit rather than detract.

Your tone also suggests that you believe all drugs either dull the senses or turn gritty reality into some kind of happy wonderland where you’re oblivious to real life. I very much have not found that the case. That describes opiates, to me, though I’m gonna get some ope fiend bitching at me now, all drugs are subjective blah blah. But opiates, to me, do mask the harshness of reality sometimes… in a way not dissimilar to alcohol, but without quite as hard a rebound effect. Other drugs do not work this way however, at least for me… even MDMA, which most people would consider the most “fake happiness” inducing drug, doesn’t work this way for me. I’m well aware that life isn’t perfect on MDMA, it just helps my brain work out that it doesn’t have to fight it and it’s OK to be retardedly, ecstaticly happy for a few hours even in an imperfect world.

I really wish I could recommend MDMA, just once, to hardcore “anti drug” folk, because I think it has the best chance of showing them what it’s all about. But, given the current state of prohibition and the drug market and whatnot I don’t really feel comfortable doing that to someone who is potentially very uneducated about drugs. Maybe one day eh.
