I have two pieces of advice:

Buy “Exploring the world of Lucid Dreaming” by Steven LaBerge (sp?). It’s a good book, lots of background reading + howtos on the MILD and WILD techniques (aimed at getting to a lucid dream state but WILD can also be used to enter hypnagogic states, since a hypnagogic state is essentially what you get before sleep, and WILD aims at keeping you “awake” during this period).

Don’t be afraid of Sleep Paralysis. While it is an odd feeling, it’s not dangerous in anyway, it can’t hurt you. If you play with your sleep you will almost certainly experience it at least once, just don’t worry about it.
