Just bought an alienware M14X laptop

Technology
mwiebelhaus August 1, 2016, 11:58am #1

Well I am going off to college next year and I need a laptop for school since ive been living with a desktop only for over a year. I wanted something that I could use to do school work and also use while I’m away from home for gaming (for instance when I visit my parents and there is nothing to do in the small town they live). I was looking at getting a custom notebook from xoticpc.com (a sager notebook) - 15 inch - same specs but higher resolution screen (1920X1080) vs (1600x900) and it had a slightly better card (460m) vs (555) but I questioned the build quality of the notebook and the size was larger than the 14inch, it had a couple more pounds of weight, and note to mention it didn’t look the best. But all in all with taxes it came out to be the same price (maybe a tad more) than the custom notebook I was going to get. I thought the alienware (dell) was going to be overpriced but I think it was good for what I am getting, theres not another 14inch notebook on the market with the graphics card that mine is coming with.

Here is the exact specs.

Alienware M14X - Stealth Black with Soft Touch Finish
Intel Core i7 2630QM 2.0GHz (2.9GHz Turbo Mode, 6M cache)
4GB Dual Channel DDR3 at 1600MHz
Alienware M14X 150W A/C Adapter
14.0 High Def+ (900p/1600x900) with WLED backlight
3.0GB DDR3 NVIDIA GeForce GT 555M using NVIDIA Optimus technology
500GB 7200RPM SATA 3GB/s
Custom Nameplate
Genuine Windows 7 Home Premium, 64bit, English
Slot-Load Dual Layer DVD Burner, DVD±RW, CD-RW
Internal High-Definition 5.1 Surround Sound Audio
Intel Advanced-N WiFi Link 6205 a/g/n 2x2 MIMO Technology

EDIT: Also I just wanted to let everybody know that I ripped myself off because I could have got 2gb more of ram (6b) and 250gb more of storage (750gb) for the same price but I didnt see they had a M14X in the featured system sections off the alienware site which had $175 (the ram and the storage) Its probably not a noticeable improvement over mine but it would have been nice to get all of the extra space for free (no biggie) I am still happy with what I got and I was planning on switching to a solid slate drive eventually anyway.
