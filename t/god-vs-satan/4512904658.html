evolution in the real world is not akin to how it is in pokemon

first I want to explain what a species is, a species is a group of animals that can generally interbreed and produce a successful offspring, if you fuck a human and they have a baby, you and your partner are the same species and the baby shares half of each parents genes, if however you fuck something else and cannot produce an offspring, it is likely you aren’t of the same species (extremely oversimplified example)

now, consider for a moment that a a group from a species gets separated for a long period of time (thousands of years plus), the species doesn’t necessarily have to be separated they just can’t interbreed, given enough time and selective environmental pressures the new group species may develop slight but different features(thicker coat of hair etc), these new genetic features may thrive in the new group but may remain relatively isolated in the parent group (but still there nonetheless if they originated from the parent group), genes can be bred out given enough time in the larger population isolating the smaller populations genes even more

given enough time it will become clear to anyone studying the remains of the animals (timespan: thousands, tens of thousands of years) that a group of the species was isolated and genes that better helped the group survive (thicker skin, hair etc) were selectively kept through environmental pressures while the parent group who didn’t have these pressures weren’t under any selection to make those genes prominent

you now have fossils from two groups of the same species, albeit you will have fossils of a species and a diverging subspecies, which can readily be confirmed by environmental factors along with genetics and observations of the remains, given enough time and the isolated species may inbreed enough that it is no longer capable of breeding with the parent species and you’ll have a new species and a transition of fossils to show such

a real world example of this although not applicable to my hypothetical scenario is wolves and dogs, believe it or not wolves and dogs belong to the same species and can interbreed and while dogs are a merely a subspecies it’s an interesting observation, you’d think wolves are wolves and dogs are dogs and that was that but the reality is that dogs are still wolves for now despite having some glaring behaviour and appearance differences

another real world example is horses and donkeys, and this is where it gets a little complicated, horses and donkeys are technically different species but are in the same family and are very closely related, so much so that if you mate a female horse with a male donkey you (may) get a mule, on the other hand if you breed a male horse with a female donkey you will get a hinny, the chances of successful hinny offspring are extremely slim compared to mules which from my understanding is pretty uncommon as well, another interesting fact is mules are almost always sterile, mules are not a species but are a hybrid of horses and donkeys

tl;dr what the hell am I getting at? one whole species does not simply evolve into a new species and any speciation is a slow and gradual change occurring after hundreds and thousands of generations
