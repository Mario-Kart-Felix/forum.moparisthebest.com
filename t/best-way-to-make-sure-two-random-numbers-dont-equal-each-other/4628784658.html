[quote=“zyle1992, post:6, topic:462878”]I’d say make one base RNG, and 2 RNGs, based with seeds generated from the base.

It wont make it impossible for collisions to occur, but it will make it highly unlikely.[/quote]

This is called non-uniform PRNG, and it still won’t guarantee nonconsecutive equality.

@OP: You have no reason not to want two equal consecutive results. Having that is essentially random. If you take that away, I would start to argue your PRNG is not random, because there is always probability of getting the same number twice. You are making it less random by predictably removing one number for every iteration of the PRNG.
