This post is all about surviving your first night in minecraft, it comes with basic information on blocks and mobs, also it shows FAQs, tips, tricks and possible mistakes which are commonly made. Minecraft is a block based game made by Notch. It creates a randomly generated world when the map loads. The general aim of the game is to explore, survive and make cool buildings! Some servers can be put into peaceful mode, this way no dangerous mobs spawn and the aim of the game is to create cool buildings and have fun - meaning you don’t need to worry at night.

Minecraft can take up A LOT of your resources, so it is advised that you end non-vital processes before you play as they can generate a lot of FPS lagg.

What is Minecraft?

Minecraft is a sandbox building[1][2] indie video game written in Java originally by creator Markus "Notch" Persson and now by his company, Mojang, formed from the proceeds of the game. The game is focused on creativity and building, allowing players to build constructions out of textured cubes in a 3D world. The game is divided into two main modes – free Classic and paid Beta – where Classic is focused entirely on construction with unlimited material supply, while Beta requires players to acquire resources themselves, and contains mobs, player health, and additional features and items. The gameplay is inspired by Dwarf Fortress, Dungeon Keeper, and Infiniminer.[3][4]

The game is currently in development and Beta is the only continuously updated version of the game. Minecraft was developed for about a week before its public release on May 17, 2009 on the TIGSource forums, where it gained a considerable level of popularity. It has been continually updated and patched since then, and while it was still in alpha release, it garnered several hundred thousand sales and received critical notice and acclaim from many reviewers. It passed a million units sold on January 12, 2011,[5][6] less than a month after reaching Beta.

[size=14pt]Part 1 - Introduction[/size]

Surviving your first few nights in Minecraft

Okay, before I actually explain to you how to survive there is some vital information you must know, firstly, when the sun goes down you must be in shelter, this is when the commonly referred to mobs come out. These mobs are zombies, killer spiders and what not.

The mobs will kill you, it’s what they do. They will chance you for as long as possible. The only mob which will still attack you in day light is the creeper - they explode on you and are very sneaky about it. Sometimes mobs can hide in the shade when it’s day so always keep your sound on to listen out for them. When daylight comes, mobs will still spawn in dark areas such as caves, so never wonder into caves without the correct equipment or you will probably die.

Actually surviving…

Okay, now you all know the basic usages and working of the minecraft world, we can move on to actually surviving. Once you log on you will spawn at a place known as the spawn area. This area is protected, so you can’t get any resources from it. So simply walk away from a few minutes, then try.

Once you get to a usable area, you need to get around 20 mud (This will be a backup plan - always be sure and safe) after you’ve done this, get yourself 10 - 20 lumber (wood). You can obtain lumber by cutting down trees.

How do you cut down trees?

Easy, hold your left mouse button down until you successfully get a log, keep going this until you get 10 - 20 logs, you also might want to get some tree saplings so you can plant your own trees when you are well established. Do this by cutting the trees leaves.

Now you’ve got lumber, and mud you NEED to find coal, this resource will allow you to create torches which will allow you to survive. Coal is commonly found at cliff openings, and at cliff sides. Or in underground mining. Here is a few examples of where coal can be found,

[size=24pt]Minecraft - Do/Don’t[/size]
[/center]

In minecraft there is a lot of things which can go wrong, such as being griefed, flooded, etc. This will teach you how to avoid those bad things.

1) - Don’t build near spawn, I mean really? The griefers will logon, and you’ll be the first thing they’ll see.
2) - Don’t make a house next to a river, you’re asking for it then.
3) - Always password protect your chests!You never know who can get into your house.
4) - Only build near a cave if you know what you’re doing, this can be a bad idea as there will be A LOT of mobs.
5) - Keep time in mind, NEVER loose track of time.
6) - Don’t mine directly above or below you. Gravel might fall on you and kill you or you might fall into a cavern or into lava and burn to death. (K0 Twin)
7) - Always use /sethome in your home location!
Tips:

  • You can only fall from 3 or less blocks/meters without taking damage.
  • Water is very handy to carry around with you, in case you accidentally touch lava
  • A compass points towards your spawn point, and is great to have while exploring, in case you get lost.

[center] [size=24pt]Mini-Guides[/size] [/center]

Alright, in this section I’m going to be writing advanced/beginner guides which will come in handy to you in your minecraft experience. They won’t be really long as this guide is pretty big already.

Quick Situation

If you’re on a long trip to another place in minecraft, you will find yourself traveling for days sometimes. So you’re going to have to spend the night away from the safety of your local community and home - I like to keep traveling for as long as possible then dig a 2x2 hole and fill in the top. This means no mobs can get you and it takes literally 4 seconds to do meaning more travel time and less messing around! It also requires no torches so is good if you’re in pretty much any situation.

A mob cave

I’ve recently stumbled upon a mob cave on the server. I can tell you now, I was happy. I found coal, iron, diamond, lava, the lot! But it came with a risk, mobs. The way I like to clear a mob cave is simple - peacefully. Sure you could go in there with weapons and risk a death or more mobs but I feel my method is better. I take 60 torches, 60 blocks of dirt and a few pic axes and head down. I make a 2x2 gap to ensure the start is clear of mobs and then light it up. I then block the opening of the next part of the cave with dirt to ensure no other mobs try to attack me while I light the current segment of the cave I’m in up. Once I’ve lit it up and mined all the resources in that segment I check the next and do the same - This ensures you cannot be ambushed or long ranged by mobs while you’re mining valuable resources.

If you happen to see a mobster you simply put blocks to create a wall and go around it. Or you could use the wall to your advantage and kill it. This creates a much safer environment for cleaning out a mob cave.

Grow your own wood

Really! I’m sick of having to risk it ever morning incase a zombie is hiding in the shade. Just grow your own. You have a roof, right? Put dirt on it plant some saplings and you can even cut them at night - it’s much more safer and makes your house seem all in one.
