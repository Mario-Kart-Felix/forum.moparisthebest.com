Lin August 3, 2016, 11:48pm #10

I started with C++ way back in the day developing on what I believe was Visual C++ 6 with my cousin. His dad knew C++ and occasionally helped us out. Later I got into Game Maker, which helped me with game stuff, and then found RSPS. Here is where I got into writing working code and working with other sources. I never read a single tutorial, but rather just looked at other code and was able to piece together what did what.

Then I found Visual Basic 6 and stuck to that for a long time because that’s what ROM hacking tools were generally written in. I figured most of the stuff out on my own, and like before, looked at other code. Eventually I moved onto Visual Basic .NET, but because of it being harder, I stuck with VB6 and it wasn’t for a while that I actually decided to move up. Here is where I started getting into what I currently do now, which is designing windows, creating controls, doing other drawing stuff, making text editing applications, etc…

One day I decided to download Visual C# 2005 and randomly opened it and started writing code. I figured most of it out based on the intellisense, error correction, and what knowledge I had from VB .NET. For no real reason at all I stuck with it and here I am today.
