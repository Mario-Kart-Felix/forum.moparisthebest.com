Secure OpenVPN increases speed

General Discussion
moparisthebest August 1, 2016, 1:58pm #1

I’m at a hotel with awful free, non-secure wifi in Gatlinburg, TN, and needing to purchase tickets online, and with things like FireSheep on the loose I don’t trust this connection, so I connected to an OpenVPN I had setup at home that I never got the opportunity to use before, and I was surprised when it seemed like pages loaded faster!

No VPN, Atlanta GA server (nearest):

VPN, Cincinnati OH server (nearest):

VPN, Atlanta GA server:

So I was even more surprised when it was confirmed, and the VPN is configured with security in mind, not even speed. I’m using bridging (as opposed to routing), tls-auth, and 2048-bit keys, all of which should slow things down. The thing that must make the difference is lzo compression is enabled on the line.

Also it was easy to setup, on the server, the laptop under Linux and Windows 7, and on both my and my wife’s Android phones (running Cyanogenmod 7).

So, I’m impressed, awesome!
