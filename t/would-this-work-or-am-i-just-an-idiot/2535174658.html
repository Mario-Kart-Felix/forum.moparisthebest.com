lothy July 31, 2016, 4:36am #31

Lethal snow is on the right track.

Humans have evolved over time. Back in the day humans would go for periods of time without eating large quantities of food. The body adapted.

In short, if you starve yourself then your body begins to break protein down - it will actually do all that it can to avoid breaking fat stores down.
This is the trap that most people fall into, which induces the yo-yo effect: they don’t care about what they’re losing to cause the loss of weight, as long as they’re losing the weight.
Most people undereat and lose their protein (which muscles are built from), which means their metabolism slows (due to the reduction in muscle size and density) - when the diet ends they eat again, only this time they’re burning fat at an even slower rate, which in turn means they put the weight back on and then a little.

The real solution is actually quite simple: a lifestyle change, meal-wise.
Ideally people should eat around 6 small meals per day, at 2 - 3 hour intervals. They should eat grainy foods in the morning, such as breads (grainy breads, not white bread), cereal, etc. At the end of the day they should be eating lean meat, such as ham, along with foods that have complex carb chains (foods such as lettuce and brocolli are great).
To optimise results you should drink double the daily recommended intake of water. This keeps your system clean, and also stops your body from storing water, which reduces water weight.
