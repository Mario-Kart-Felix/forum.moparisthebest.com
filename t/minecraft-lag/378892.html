Tekk123 August 1, 2016, 7:02am #19

Minecraft processes chunk updates in the same thread as the render loop. Therefore, a bad network connection can cause FPS drops, because it is struggling to process all the chunks and get a frame drawn. Same thing with lots of redstone circuits in one area.

I am not saying that all frame drops are because of network connectivity. It is just a possible cause, along with graphics hardware, CPU, amount of RAM, etc.
