xx_matty_xx July 31, 2016, 6:27pm #8

On Ubuntu, it tells you your battery’s actual capacity and actual charge. If the battery’s capacity has been reduced to 13%, obviously you’ll need a new battery.

If the capacity is still high you probably need a replacement charger. This happened to my brothers Dell, and I can fix it sometimes if I wiggle the cables around a bit. I read somewhere that some of the strands of cable in the wire can easily snap when the wire is bent, and after a certain amount snap the voltage drops below a threshold which stops the computer from charging the battery. I also read something about interference with the data pin in the charging cable, so you could unplug your everything for a while to let static discharge then plug it back in with the cable away from other electronics.

If getting a new charger doesn’t work, the chip on the motherboard that detects the chargers data pin is probably damaged. I thought this was a Dell only problem… but I guess not =/
