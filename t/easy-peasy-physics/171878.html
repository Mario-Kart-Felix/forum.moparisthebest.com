Right now in science we’re learning physics, and got a problem that I’m stuck on.

Here it is:

Identify the action/reaction forces in each example described below.

  1. You may have watched the liftoffs of the space shuttle on television. When the booster rockets carrying the space shuttle rake off, their engines push fuel exhaust downward. The exhaust pushes back on the rockets, sending them upward.

  2. When you bang your toe into the leg of a table, the same amount of force that you exert on the table is exerted back on your toe.

  3. Action and reaction forces do not always result in motion. For example, if you press down on a table, the table resists the push with the same amount of force, even though nothing moves.

Please help.

Thanks in advance.

xxlegitxx July 30, 2016, 6:37pm #2
  1. Gases pushing on rocket. Rocket pushing on gases.
  2. Toe to table, table to toe.
  3. Weight pushing down on table. Normal force exerted on you by table.
