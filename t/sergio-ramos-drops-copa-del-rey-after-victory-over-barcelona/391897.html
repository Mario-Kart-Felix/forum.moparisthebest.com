Sergio Ramos drops Copa Del Rey after victory over Barcelona

[size=12px][font=Arial]Real Madrid finally ended their three-year wait for a trophy on Wednesday night... and then immediately broke it.[/font][/size][font=Arial][size=1.2em]After Cristiano Ronaldo's terrific header settled a cagey Copa del Rey final in extra time, Real took to the streets of the Spanish capital on an open top bus to celebrate not just the claiming of the trophy but also getting one over on the old enemy, Barcelona. [/size][/font] [font=Arial][/size][size=1.2em][b]Scroll down to see video footage of Sergio Ramos dropping the cup[/b] [/size][/font] [/size][size=10px]

[font=Arial][/size][size=1.2em]And it seems those jubilant scenes got all too much for defender Sergio Ramos who, as he balanced the giant piece of silverware on his head, somehow let loose his grip and dropped the cup beneath the wheels of the bus.
[/size][/font]
[font=Arial][/size][size=1.2em]World Cup winner Ramos later claimed the trophy was ‘ok’, but television pictures showed emergency services gathering up the broken pieces and returning them to the team on board. The cup was then absent from the traditional celebration spot of the Plaza de Cibeles in central Madrid.
[/size][/font]

[font=Arial][/size][size=1.2em]Madrid’s victory in the second of four ‘clasicos’ secured a first domestic cup in 18 years and handed Jose Mourinho’s side the momentum going into next week’s Champions League semi-final showdown with their Catalan rivals.
[/size][/font]

[font=Arial][/size][size=1.2em]The victory also maintains Mourinho’s record of having won a trophy every year since 2003, excluding 2007/08 when he was not in club management.
[/size][/font]
