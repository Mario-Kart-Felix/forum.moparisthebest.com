Cheese_Police August 1, 2016, 7:16am #15

You could use nginx alone, sure. Some reasons for proxying script requests I can think of are vhosts, caching, resource/load distribution. From what I remember nginx does have some level of support for a lot of those and other “standard” httpd features, but depending on what you need it might be beneficial to use the proxy setup.

I guess it also depends on how much of a mess your current setup is, or how much of a mess it needs to be. Only way to really find out how effective any solution will be is by testing it, I suppose.
