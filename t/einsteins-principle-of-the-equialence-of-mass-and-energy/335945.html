xx matty xx:

so i heard that nothing can go faster than the speed of light. does this include light itself?

…

if i had a fast rocket with headlamps on the front, would the speed of light relative to space increase, or remain at c? paraphrased, would the speed of light relative to the rocket decrease, or remain at c?

one of Einstine’s postulates is that everyone observes light to be travelling at c, that means an observer standing still and an observer in the rocket would both measure the speed of light to be c.

It turns out there is nothing special about light in this. The significance is that there exists a maximum speed and all massless particles and fields travel at this speed in a vacuum. According to special relativity, if something could go faster then c it would lead to an effect happening before its cause. Also it would make some times become imaginary in the sense that they involve sqrt(-1).

i would of thought that the light would now be traveling at the speed of the rocket + c. but because nothing can exceed c, does the speed of light relative to the rocket decrease? and does the mass of something increase due to e=mc^2?

no, the rocket still travels at v, but the concept of length and time changes so that the light from its headlights still travels at c instead of c+v.

e=mc^2 arises because the energy of a body depends on its speed amongst other things, as our idea of speed has been changed due to Einstine’s postulate, our idea of energy must change too. When you do the maths, there is an extra term that doesnt depend on the body’s speed and it is equal to mc^2. The e in e=mc^2 only refers to the rest energy, that is, the energy of a body when it isnt moving.

e=mc^2 isnt anything special, its just that the media hyped it up 100 years ago when it was discovered and the hype has lasted until today.
I think the phenomenon of time dilation and length contraction are more amazing, intriguing, awe-inspiring and more capable of creating a sense of wonder at the universe. Go to youtube if you have time and type in special relativity, videos are normally quite good at explaining this.

No experiment has been found where special relativity didnt work, it seems overwhelmingly likely that our universe really is like this…
