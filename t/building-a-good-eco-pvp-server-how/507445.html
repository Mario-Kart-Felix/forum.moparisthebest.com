Flame3 August 4, 2016, 5:42am #2

Make your players work for it, while still providing the motivation for them to continue doing so.
Key factors such as:

  • The player’s taste in PK’ing
  • Economy:PVP ratio
  • Access to spawning
  • What levels are important,
    play into a server like this.
    Motivation-wise, there should be some sort of achievement, be it an item, a yell title, a specialized PK’ing zone, that should be at some point accessible to all.
    This would bring me to my next point; donators/members. Members should not be able to have access to superior items in a PK’ing server, in my opinion. If they did, new players and old alike would lose motivation because of an ‘elite PKer’ roaming around the wilderness. Give them an increased percentage of PK points per kill, or maybe allow them to have a point-free member’s PK’ing zone.
    Recently, point shops have become popular, yet they only play their part in a short term span. Once players have access to these items, they find themselves losing the motivation to keep on earning them.
    You should definitely invest some time in periodic promotion events. These could be double-point weekends, PK’ing tournaments that reward, say, an ‘Elite Pker’ player title, or Bring-a-Friend events. With something to look forward to, players find themselves willing to stick around and see what fun event the administration might hold in store for them.

Again, you need to have your server be flexible enough that it can adjust to the many types of PKer’s that there are out there.
Here’s an idea that I’m thinking of:

  • At your home town, there is a group of NPC’s that you can speak with.
  • Speaking with the first one will set your levels to that of a ‘pure.’ You will then be asked if you wish to teleport to the pure’s PKing zone. To stop hoarding and massacre, only other pure’s have access to this area.
  • Do the same for maxed pkers, hybrids, no food PKing, etc.
    Alternatively, you can implement code that will stop another player from getting points if the level difference between the two is too great.

Flame3 August 4, 2016, 5:43am #6

This depends on what route you are willing to approach.
You can have new players join in a tutorial island sort of environment and require them to make their first set of armour and then their first batch of bread or food before they can dwell into the actual PKing world.
You can have your players simply log in with a practical PKing starter and have them level up by fighting other players and neighboring NPCs.
You have have your players earn XP lamps while fighting, which they can either use to the skill of their choice, or have the XP lamps be assigned to certain skills.
An idea I just thought up of with construction is:

  • Players must cut their own logs and bring them to the mill to have them made into planks.
  • Players must mine several metals and smith them to make nails.
  • Players must use a log with a bar of metal to make a hammer.
  • Afterwards, players may begin to create their own PKing rings, where:
    ~ Players can set points for their own rink.
    ~ Players can make use of an NPC running the same concept as player-owned-shops, but they must use the points they earn from that player’s ring to get the items in that shop.

I always thought to make skill levels 0 - 6 available for anyone to change their combat levels freely from 1 - 99. You could also add a few items that cost pk points and make a pk point currency (like the money pouch) that can be accessed and taken-away-from whenever the player decides and placed into a trade window. Additionally you could have NPCs drop pk point items and a few items that can only be gained through killing monsters and put together with skilling (such as crafting 2 item pieces together like 2 shield halves).
