Mopman July 31, 2016, 4:08pm #3

of the two you posted, the E3120 is very slightly “better” but theres not much in it and it runs quite a bit hotter and is power hungrier

the xeon family and pentium family are two completely different lines, its not really possible to give a list of differences or comparisons for the lines as a whole. generally xeons are used in servers, though high end workstations that are likely to be running apps that are well optimised for parallel processing also love them (think video editing, digital audio). the mac pros are xeon based, for example.

one big difference is that the xeon family support multi-cpu environments (usually), except you picked 2 from the series that doesn’t
they also tend to be beefier cpus all round rather than focusing on clock speed, more cache faster bus etc

beware that you wont be able to drop them into a pentium specced system though, even the LGA755 ones use different ram
