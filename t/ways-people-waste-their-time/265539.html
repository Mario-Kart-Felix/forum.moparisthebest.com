I have been thinking alot recently about why there are issues in in the world that have been around for awhile, that many people want to solve, and that go unresolved. Part of this has to do with people who live their whole lives and never accomplish anything worthwhile.

I’ve observed that, in most people’s free time, they have some particular activity or set of activities that does nothing but waste time. The defining characteristic of this activity are it’s aimless, and you could do it forever without ever making progress. So, if you watched a documentary on TV on yesterday and then did something else when it was done, don’t count that in this list. If you were browsing a forum and aimlessly reading whatever interesting topic you happened to come across, then that would count.

Something interesting a friend of mine pointed out to me: In general, the time wasting activity is related to the generation of the person doing it, because they grew up in its time. For example, my father and grandmother (In his 60’s and in her 80’s) both watch TV. The people at my campus, mostly in their teens, waste time on forums and sites like facebook.
