I like the layout of the image you linked Pure but I’d like to stick with the awards that most people agreed on. Justaguy has a great point, website development is far too broad a term to use. It’s been needing partitioned for a long time, I remember talking to Taharok on the IRC before I got mines and even back then he was wanting to partition it. Since then website development has opened itself up in a more public form to more than just PHP.

Django is popular enough by a long shot. Once again there isn’t just one framework, though. RoR, Sinatra, Rack are all good Ruby frameworks. Django, Flask, web2py are all great examples of Python frameworks. It’s bigger than just RoR and Django, too.

My point is that generalising these things is good, Ruby award for all aspects of Ruby, not just web sided obviously. Python award already stands, why bother arguing over that? A PHP award has been needed for a while, along with a web designer award and since the growth of Node.js, Express.js etc it’s warranted enough (IMO) for a JavaScript award.
