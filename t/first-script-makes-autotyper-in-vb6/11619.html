ya, sorry, forgot to add the variable part.

Also, use this procedure instead, it has less detectable mouse movements…

[scar]procedure findColor;
begin
if(findColor(x, y, color, x1, x2, y1, y2))Then//If color is found at given coords, continues.
begin
MMouse(x, y, 3, 3);//Moves mouse smoothly to the coords of the color.
wait(100);
Mouse(x, y, 2, 2, true);//Clicks on color
end;
end;[/scar]

And remember to define x and y as integers, and if you have problems with the clicking, do what janilabo said.

Now for the explanation of the mouse procedures.

MMouse(x, y, 3, 3); --x and y just the coords that the mouse will move to(retrieved by the findColor… procedure). The two 3’s means that the mouse will move somewhere within 3 pixels of x and y. It just makes it more humanlike, not clicking in the same exact spot all the time.

Mouse(x, y, 2, 2, true); --x and y are again the coords that the mouse will click on. The two 2’s are once again the same thing as MMouse, this means the mouse will click somewhere within 2 pixels of x and y.

Hope this helps!
