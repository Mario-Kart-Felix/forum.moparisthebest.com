Is it hard to fix RROD, I haven’t looked into it, my friend wants me to fix his.[/quote]As long as no one has done anything stupid like the towel trick, penny trick, x-clamp fix, eraser mod etc, then it should be easily fixable. But you will need the proper equipment.

You will need:

  • A reworking station
  • A preheater for your board (if the station doesn’t have one).
  • 40x40mm and 20x20 mm nozzles
  • Some new thermal paste (preferably Artic Silver 5)

Also, you will need the following tools:

Torx 10, Torx 8, an xbox opening kit (optional, but helps a lot. you can just cut apart a dvd spindle lid), and a small flat head screw driver for removing the x-clamps.

The xbox uses lead free solder which liquidates at 217C and it is recommended to reach a temperature of 230C for atleast 25 seconds but no more than 90 seconds. Note that this temperature should be BETWEEN the chip and the motherboard, not above or below it.

Keep the board suspended 1-1.5 inches from the preheater, which should be outputting 400F
