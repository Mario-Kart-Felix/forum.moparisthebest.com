Blizzard sues another bot ... in Germany

General Discussion
eXemplar August 1, 2016, 1:21pm #1

So some of you may remember Blizzard sued the wow bot Glider which operated under an American company, and was successful in stopping sales, distribution and got some crazy ruling about DMCA and circumvention of warden.

Now they’ve gone and sued the (currently) best bot on the market Honorbuddy which operates under a company in Germany (yes, that means filing the lawsuit in Germany itself, too - gl/hf DMCA) which may have quite a few implications depending on what Blizzard are seeking damages for. There’s a touch more info on their forums, however not much more than the fact that the suit was filed under reference 310 O 260/11 on 11/07/2011 before the District Court of Hamburg, and the details are not yet available.

Bot licences are still being sold, however their model has changed slightly - they no longer sell ‘lifetime’ licences under the guise of not being misleading - heh. Other than that it seems business as usual, at least until all the details of the case are disclosed.

You’ll probably see (some) mainstream media pick up on this more once these details are forthcoming.
