tL August 2, 2016, 11:55pm #27

Yeah. Good luck building a decent laptop yourself. It’s possible, but laptops contain all sorts of custom parts that were designed to fit within the case, to work within the thermal constraints of that case, not to mention all the battery, lcd and expansion considerations.

The manufacturing of many of todays laptops is quite a specialised process. It’s easy to built a fantastic desktop machine because parts are all standard and cases are built to those standards (motherboard sizes, etc). Not at all the same as a laptop.

The AMD A8-3520M isn’t actually all that bad. It has a low stock clock, but turbos up to 2.5Ghz iirc. It’s supposed advantage is in it’s low power consumption so you do get better battery life. It isn’t intended to be a workhorse.
