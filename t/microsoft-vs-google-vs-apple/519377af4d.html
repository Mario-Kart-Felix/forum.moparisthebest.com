The PS3 wasn’t overpriced when you look at it on paper though. Sure, the PS3 was ridiculously expensive at launch, so much so that Sony sold the PS3 at a loss for quite some time. But for that initial £425 price tag, you got the cheapest Bluray player, you got a removable hard-drive, you could play PS2 games, you had built in wifi, and you had HDMI from launch. The launch Xbox’s didn’t have HDMI until 2007 (conveniently when the PS3 was released, WITH HDMI), launch Xbox’s had no wifi (with the official dongle being $100), and depending on the model the launch Xbox’s didn’t have a harddrive, at £209 and £279 respectively.

The PS3 dropped the price to £399 quickly after launch, and 8 months later in November 2007 Sony released the 40gb PS3 at £299. In August 2007 Microsoft had came out with the Xbox Elite, which I believe finally had wifi, being priced at oh, look at that, £299.

So sure, the PS3 may have been overpriced at launch compared to launch 360 models, but the 360 models were gimped and when they finally weren’t gimped they came out competitively priced with each other.
