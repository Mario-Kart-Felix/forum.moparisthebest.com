[center][size=20pt]Burning downloaded movies to a DVD[/size][/center]

Okay, after j4m3s’ request, i’ll describe how to burn movies onto a DVD.

What you need

[ul][li]A (downloaded) movie, in a common movie format (e.g. AVI, MPG, RM etc)[/li]
[li]WinAVI (see below)[/li]
[li]DVD burner, including blank DVDR disk[/li][/ul]

Download
First, get WinAVI from here.
http://www2.winavi.com/download/WinAVI_Video_Converter.exe

Registering
After install, enter one of the following serials:

No, this is not illegal, just, erm, dont use them :rolleyes:

Converting
Okay, press the DVD button, as shown in the picture.

Browse to your video file, and double click it.

  1. Select output directory, like C:\MOVIE_NAME
  2. Select DVD
  3. Press advanced, you’ll see this:

  1. Disc size should be 4300MB, 1 disc
  2. AUTO is most common for the video size, but if you have a widescreen, you’d better select 16:9
  3. Audio bitrate, 192000 is fine
  4. If you have a PAL dvd player, you’d select PAL. (Most european dvd players have this). American people have NTSC. If you’re not sure, ask your dvd supplier.
    Also, if your movie has an AC3 codec, check the box “Enable DirectAC3”

Press [OK]


It’s converting woohoo!

Burning
Okay, fire up your burning program (i use nero) and make a new DVD. Add two folders to it. The structure must be like this:
DVD_ROOT
|-AUDIO_TS
|- [no files, leave it empty, just create folder]
|-VIDEO_TS
|- [ALL files WinAVI outputted go here! Including every vob, sub whatever file]

Press BURN (or whatever it’s called) and let it do it’s job. Open your drive after it’s finished, and watch a movie on your TV!
