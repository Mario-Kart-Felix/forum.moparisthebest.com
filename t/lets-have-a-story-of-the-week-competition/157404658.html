frost21:

When I have time. I have a very imaginative, and creative mind.

Anyone can write a story, but it might not be interesting. If you want to write good stories then you have to practice. For those who want to write a story, but don’t know where to start, this for you.

You can write a story about anything you want. All you need are the basics. You wouldn’t bake a cake without knowing the ingredients, right?

So before you write, it helps to know the ingredients of a story. That means you know what you want to write about.

Story Structure
It has a beginning, a middle and an end. Some stories even end up in the same place they started. It is like a snake that has its tail in its mouth, the head is the beginning, and the tail is the end.

For example, in The Wizard of Oz, Dorothy starts out in Kansas, travels to Oz in a cyclone and ends up back in Kansas.

Ready to start your story? Then grab a pencil and paper, or open up a new file on your computer, and away we go!

Main Character - The person, animal or thing your story is about
Every story starts with a main character. It can be any animal, human or anything else you want it to be. Make sure you focus mostly on the main character in the story, but add other characters to make the story more interesting.

Start by asking yourself some questions:
• Who or what is your main character?
• What does he, she or it like/dislike?
• What is your character’s personality?
• What does your character look like?

When you start getting answers, you can write a brief description of your character. Describe his/her characteristics.

Example: Josh, a student, attends Benix High School in Urville, Newcountry. Althought he is shorter than most 15 year olds, he can win anyone in a arm wrestling match. He plays for the Benix Tigers, the school football team. Etcetera…

Setting - Where your story takes place
Every story needs to take place somewhere. Of course, you might say. But your story’s setting can have either a big or little impact on the story. What would The Wizard of Oz be without Oz? Just a story about a girl and a dog in dusty old Kansas.

Ask yourself these questions:
• Where does your story take place - on the moon, in Hawaii, inside your dad’s nose, or someplace completely different?
• When does your story take place: past, present or future?
• How much does your setting affect your main character’s problem?

Problem/Conflict - The challenge your character must face and overcome
Without a problem, your story would be very boring. But when you give your main character a problem to solve, your story comes alive. Be sure to make it a big enough problem. In stories, the main character solves the problem, but then more problems come. This is called Complication.

Important tip: The more times your character tries and fails, the better.

Ask yourself these questions:
• What is your main character’s problem?
• Is it a big enough problem that it will take a whole story to solve it?
• How does your main character try and fail to solve her or his problem?

Resolution - How the character finally solves the problem
The most satisfying resolutions come when you think your hero is about to give up. When they’ve tried everything else, they finally solve the problem. It’s best if your main character solves the problem on his or her own, but you can also use other characters to help the main character solve the problem.

*Remember to have fun and be creative. If your not having fun then make it fun.
*Be descriptive, and explain the most important parts of your story in good detail.
*Use adjectives, to express the characters.

Your friend,
Leader
