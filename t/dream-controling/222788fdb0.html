Zero187 July 31, 2016, 1:54am #67

Lucid dreaming takes much discipline and practice. One must increase their overall awareness to a certain level before he or she is able to lucid dream. If you are very self-centered and don’t notice much going on around you, but instead are always in your own day-dream then you will not be able to lucid dream very easily. Practice by bringing out your awareness in every day life, and turning off the internal dialogue. Once the internal dialogue is silenced, you will start to notice more things and your senses will be keener. If you practice this daily, you will start noticing an increase in awareness in your dreams, to the point where you can control what you do.

At first when you become lucid in a dream, it will only last a few seconds and either fade to darkness and you will awake, or you will go back into another dream and lose you lucidity. The way to prevent this is to actually “spin” your dream self while in the dream if you are losing lucidity. When you are done spinning you will have a completely clear environment around you which will look as real as ever.

I’m 19 years old and have been practicing lucid dreaming for years now. If you have any questions feel free to ask me.
