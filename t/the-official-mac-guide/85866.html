About Macs

The Macintosh Operating System
is the most user-friendly software commercially available.
How can I make this claim? Personal experience. The Windows OS may
seem easy to use at first, BUT as you use it more and more you’ll
find it’s a whole lot more complicated than it appears! Windows gets
bugs. Patches are constantly being released and are often large files
you need to download. Windows gets viruses. You need to buy expensive
programs to protect your computer, as well as run frequent scans that
take up a great deal of memory.

Frequent Problems and how to fix them

This is a RuneScape forum so people will be reading
this for help with their RuneScape-related problems on a Mac.
Here are some of the very, VERY few issues that may be encountered.

  1. When loading a page on the RuneScape website, you may encounter a
    totally white screen. Click anywhere on this white space and the page
    will appear. Fairly straightforward.

  2. When loading the RuneScape game, you may get a white box with a
    little red X on a document symbol in the top left of the box. This is
    a failed classloader (if you care enough you can find out the exact
    string in the Java console) and can be fixed by closing Safari and
    reopening it with the default settings. Again, very simple.

  3. While playing the game, you may suddenly notice that your character
    and everything else simply stops moving and you can’t click anything.
    EDIT: Jordanweb has a great solution to this problem on page 3

  4. When playing with music on (the RS music) you may notice it
    suddenly just holds a random note, which can get very annoying.
    Follow the instructions given by Jordanweb on page 3

The Macintosh OS doesn’t have bugs. And if a small issue with the
software is ever reported, Apple will release a patch (this is rare).
Also, Macs don’t get fragmented and are immune to approximately
100,000 identified viruses. Macintosh laptops pick up a wireless
internet signal anywhere there is one, right out of the box. Plus
there is no setup of hardware to use a Mac . Any questions about the
information above can be asked later.

Other Information

Playing RuneScape on a Mac is very straightforward, and quite easy.
However, do not go PKing with a one-button mouse. That’s the one and
only drawback, sorry. Oh, wait. I forgot-Macs can support a two-button
USB mouse! Touché, PC . Also, remember that if your in-game settings
(the wrench icon) are set to a two button mouse when you really only
have one button, all your clicks will be the equivalent of a left
click. When set to one button, you will click something once and it
will be like right clicking with a PC.

EDIT–Helpful tip from Lumberslashe–Pressing the Apple(Command)
key and clicking is like right clicking. Thanks a lot, I didn’t know
that

If I find something else to add, I’ll add it later.

Frequently asked questions

Q-What browser should I use to play on my Mac?
A-Safari is a fine browser. It even supports tabbed browsing,
a helpful feature.

Q-How do I remove cookies in Safari?
A-Go to the Safari menu, click Prederences, and then click the
Security tab. Click Show Cookies and then click Remove all.

Q-Is there an Official Mac client I can get from the RuneScape
website?
A-No, not at the moment. Safari is fine.

Q-Can I download stuff while playing RuneScape on my Mac, or will it
make me lag?
A-Yes you can. Download music, movies, whatever, and you will
experience no lag.
Disclaimer-Always download stuff LEGALLY. If it’s free, don’t trust
it.

Q-Don’t I have to download Java?
A-Nope. Macs come with Java preinstalled. No need to download
anything in order to run RuneScape.

Q-The RuneScape music doesn’t work on my PC. Will I have to configure
anything to make it work on my Mac?
A-No again . The music and sound effects should work with no problems.

Q-Can my Mac get keylogged?
A-Technically yes, it is possible. But as Macs will automatically
detect any file that even had the remote possibility of being
dangerous, you’re chances of getting a keylogger or a virus on a
Mac is extremely slim.

Q-What about Spyware?
A-See previous answer!

Q-Yay, I just got a fire cape! How do I take a screenshot of it?
A-Make sure you can see the fire cape, and then press Command-Shift-4.
This will make your cursor turn into a crosshairs. Highlight the area
you want to take a picture of and release the mouse button. The picture
will automatically pop up on your desktop as a PNG image.

EDIT–please be aware that if you want your question answered,
make a new thread as help threads aren’t allowed on the forums.

If you have a solution to a problem you’d like me to include, please
post that!
