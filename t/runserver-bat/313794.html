Ebowne24 July 31, 2016, 12:28pm #6

Maybe try this?

This guide is to help, or at least try, the known JDK problem. This may not be the solution, but I think it's better to do it regardless.

First. Find your System Information and then click on your Advanced System Settings

It will then pull up the left table on this image. Click on the Advanced tab and click Environment Variables…
Find the CLASSPATH variable and edit it to this:

CLASSPATH=C:\Program Files\Java\jdk1.6.0_16\bin;%CLASSPATH%;

Then scroll down a bit and find the PATH variable and edit it to this:

C:\Program Files\Java\jdk1.6.0_16\bin;;C:\Program Files\Common Files\DivX Shared\(Not so sure what the “C:\Program Files\Common Files\DivX Shared” is in there for… but I don’t want to change it. o.O

–EDIT–
You must have JDK 1.6 Update 16 or higher.

Hope this helps!
