lothy July 31, 2016, 11:09am #50

Seriously, I lol’d while reading this. You’re doing it all wrong.

Firstly, if you’re planning to steal things that are actually worth any money then you do it out of town. As the antagonist from ‘Four Brothers’ puts it, “You don’t pay a hooker to have sex with you - you pay her to leave. You don’t pay out of town shooters to shoot somebody; you pay them to get the hell back out of town”.
If you do the deed out of town (and remain faceless while doing so) then the odds of it ever catching up to you are pretty slim; batman notwithstanding, everyone has jurisdictional limitations, and this is why you do it elsewhere and get the hell out again.
iPods, laptop computers, car head units, lithium batteries - these are all small, light-weight, highly valuable pieces of electronic equipment - you flog them out of state, or at least a couple of hours from where you live.

Secondly, you mentioned nothing with regards to the planning phase. The bank kinda did, but 2 days doesn’t cut it if you plan to siphon thousands of dollars out of the shop in the longer run.
Generally speaking, I would recommend planning over a few weeks, perhaps even using different (reliable) people. As the bank said, they found themselves on security camera footage - why?
Why not map the shop, identifying all real security cameras (and all fake security cameras - the completely opaque domes that you often find on the roofs of grocery stores).

Thirdly, consider having a ‘fall guy’ - someone who, ostentatiously (ie, on camera), pockets things. If another shopper sees then even better.
This guy then, off camera and out of the public eye, ditches EVERYTHING and exits the store looking as guilty as possible. When caught he obviously has nothing on him and, as such, hasn’t committed any crime. He has however soaked up security resources (as people watching the video feeds have focused on him, and then pursued him after he left the store).

Finally, rely on the nature of the training employees receive when they start working for the company.
A staff member will virtually be instructed to never follow a potential criminal; with this in mind, keep any getaway vehicles out of view of the store. Do not park right in front of the entrance.
This one should be a no brainer, but if the car isn’t stolen and the plates aren’t stolen (ie, it’s a car that belongs to you or someone you know) then it can be used to link you to the crime if someone sees and memorises the plates.
It is best to park the vehicle in such a position that is possible to drive away without going along any stretch of road that is viewable from the shop.
If the area is low-traffic in nature, you could even consider just concealing the plates until you drive to a backstreet that is perhaps a half mile away.

I’ve written this for academic purposes (as I am personally interested in both computer security and physical security and do a lot of reading on the subjects), mostly to show that the OP is an idiot.
Don’t shop lift, because there always is the chance that you will get caught and it’s an eventuality that all criminals will run into at some stage.
