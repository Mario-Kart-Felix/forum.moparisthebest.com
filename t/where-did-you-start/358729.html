After working on private servers for awhile, a friend and I decided the best approach to this is to make our own from scratch. Taking others pre-made sources and clients is what most people do. Yes I know what your are thinking…

“OH EM GEE this guy is such a loser, he can never learn what I do. You will never make anything. Go lick a tomato!”

Guys don’t be harsh, I’m watching a video series right now (87 Java Programming, 27 Intermediate Java Programming, and 35 Java Game Developing ). Is this the right approach? You think I would learn enough to make my own server from scratch? I’m at lesson 35 right now and I’ve literally memorized all of it. No exaggeration.

Next Question I have is about modeling. There isn’t much that you can do with modeling private servers, there’s a certain limit to what you can do. You can obviously make custom weapons.

Is Blender good for making private server customs? I know this much, it is very advanced and has animation options.

Metasequoia is ok, but from looking at it somewhat briefly, it doesn’t have much of an animation built into it. Apparently it’s made by the Japanese so there’s not many guides out there, half of the ones that I happen to come across are in Japanese.

Again guys, don’t buzz me off. I’m not some desperate 12 year old kid who wants to be popular for making a private server. From the programming that I’ve done so far(around 3 weeks or so) I do it because it’s fun.

Rixxx July 31, 2016, 7:08pm #4

People learn differently. To quote this sentence, “If you study to remember, you will forget, but, if you study to understand, you will remember”, this can be applied in every way. The only way for you to learn something is to understand its concept.

Get the basics, read documentation (or/and tutorials) then test your knowledge by application. Since you want to write a private server from scratch, understand how the core of it works; write your own networking for the server, read tutorials on player- as well as NPC-updating etc and you’ve got yourself the best server you’ll ever like.

Don’t worry about modeling now though, take one step at a time. If you want questions related to modeling answered, then visit this board because IMO the regular people there could answer your questions better.
