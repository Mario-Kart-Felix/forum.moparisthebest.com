Elite2142 (Skating On Java):

Or just highly modify Linux’s source?

You don’t understand. The way to make a linux distro is to start blank with the kernel (which is free for download). You should then add packages and applications based on what you want.

The easiest way to do this is to start from an already installed linux distro, make new linux partitions and copy the kernel over to it along with said applications. If you know what specific machine the distro is going on (say an xbox), you can take stuff out of the kernel and recompile it and take out drivers for hardware the xbox doesn’t have.

The only real reason to make your own distro other than to make it into a big project (like fedora, suse, ubuntu, etc) is to customise linux for your machine.
