moparisthebest August 1, 2016, 3:09pm #6

Since you said it fixed itself if you reset your router, I think it’s pretty clear something is wrong with your router. Can you install DD-WRT or similar on it? The problem probably manifests itself with torrents because of the sheer number of TCP connections it has to hold, it probably over-flows some buffer or something.

Alternatively, you could turn off TCP torrent connections and make your torrent applications only use the new uTP protocol, it goes over UDP, which is stateless, so there are no connections to hold and no buffers to over-flow.
