matzie August 4, 2016, 2:07pm #4

A lot of programmers run into the issue of implementation bottlenecks, not execution. The way the network traffic and game processes are handles is going to be your greatest nemesis.
Also note the access rate on your HDD may slow you down, you can easily bottleneck if there are a lot of (dis)connections simultaneously(game load/save). The more players, the higher chance of this.

As far as network traffic goes, i have no clue as to a cap on what to expect on per player basis. As players rise, so will the traffic per player (Live sync for player displays). This will also be part with your implementation (Old 317s felt the need to send a interface update on every game tick, lot of wasted bandwidth there)

Also having your server on the same host as your gamesaves can help with latency, but also prevents your growth and adds more vulnerabilities. A servers only purpose should be the performance of what it is hosting(Ergo, your RSPS), having the server ask a database for player data will help you in the long run for larger and more versatile scalability, maintainability and modifications.

Good luck on your endeavor!
