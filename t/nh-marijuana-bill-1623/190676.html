NH Marijuana Bill 1623

General Discussion
Ron July 30, 2016, 8:49pm #1

Today was a great day for New Hampshire! A marijuana decriminalization bill (HB 1623) was passed in the house. Before it becomes a law, it needs to be passed by the senate and finally signed in by the governor. If this passes all the stages, it should be issued by January 1, 2009.

Decriminalization is not legalization, it just makes marijuana possession up to 1 and a quarter ounce (35 grams) will just be a violation and not a misdemeanor. New Hampshire has always had a huge amount of pot smokers and since I live in southern New Hampshire where weed is affluent, I also unfortunately know a lot of people who have gone to jail for possession/trafficking/selling. And it really sucks!

A few people may criticize me for saying weed should be decriminalized. But hey, people who abuse themselves should not go to jail. If someone smokes pot, that’s their problem/concern/prerogative. Live and let live, and pass the blunt.

So I was just curious what other people think about bills like this. Please vote and please post your reasoning.

Website for more info: http://www.nhcommonsense.org
The bill: http://www.gencourt.state.nh.us/legislation/2008/hb1623.html

~Ron
