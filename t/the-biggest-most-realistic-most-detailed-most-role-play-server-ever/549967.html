The Vision
Imagine a server, where every click means something. Every step taken has a consequence. To live a story, your own story, as a being of many sorts, which simulates real-life (with a fantasy-medieval twist, of course). One life.

Forget about the traditional point, click and wait combat. Forget the skill and grind aspect we all know. Take the skin of a game that is familiar to us all, rip it’s guts out and give it a new heart. This is what you get.

Once you log in you are born, figuratively speaking. Decide what you want to be and the rest is up to you. Law-abiding citizen, a crazy nomad wizard, dragon-slaying hero, or maybe a law-upholding kingsman? Go find some Vampires and live the cursed life (I know you secretly fantasise about it). The possibilities really are endless.

Every action has a re-action. Murder, stealing, tax evasion and choosing to be rude in dialogue has its consequences. Even down to the small details like eating food and simply using coins will effect your character in some way. Everything is documented so you will know what you can do.

Choose your style of play, whether it be heroic, evil, a bit of both or something in between, you get to decide everything. But die, through being killed or, if you are boring, old age, and your account will literally remain dead. Your gravestone will be placed near your location and you may even go down in the history books.

Still not convinced this is the game for you? That’s fine, I know it won’t be everyone’s cup of tea, but let me give you some insight on a few misconceptions that might persuade you otherwise.

About/Credits
In the end, the poll as to what base to use was in favour of the old school Arios 498 project as opposed to the 718 loading 742. But then someone suggested to me to use a 317 framework and it made me think. After a day, I decided this would be my best option as I could write all the content from scratch and wouldn’t have to worry about building off of an unfamiliar system or having to clean the source first. So I chose lare96’s Asteria 2.0 project.

I am creating this server for no one else but myself, so when you ask why, why not? Collaborating ideas and collecting data as I go. Some days may even be purely preparation and not actual development in code. I also would love to discuss with you concepts, ways to implement systems and ways to make the entire game involving, interactive and immersive regarding a RuneScape-style world, so please strike up a conversation with me at any time.

I have one rule. Talk to me as players (not developers). What do you like and dislike in games such as RuneScape, and all other simulation or RPG types.

I’m not here to revolutionise RuneScape Private Servers. I want to change the idea completely.
