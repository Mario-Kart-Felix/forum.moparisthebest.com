I’m talking about actual Runescape when it put the grand exchange was put in

It basically worked like this
Merchanting clan has spambots advertise their clan "Make mils in minutes"
Players join it, and buy whatever item the clan leader (and his friends) tells them in bulk
Supply goes down, demand goes up, therefore the price rises
At a peak, where the demand starts to go stead as people catch on the price rise, the leaders flood the market with the item, making their huge profit, and then tell the merchanting clan to sell.

The issue is that the leaders have massive amounts to spend (cash stacks upon cash stacks), and by the time the normal players get word, the prices have started to fall. Supply not only meets demand, but creates a surplus, making it impossible to sell for roughly 80% of the clan.

They promised a low risk, high return investment (an impossibility) in order to profit themselves.
Basically, they fucked over the economy to get more money themselves.

If you think comparing the grand exchange to the stock market is extreme, just look at how similar the grand exchange webpage from 2009 (or so) looked when compared with the Yahoo! Finances page from the same time.

Of course, servers are vastly smaller in comparison, and typically more regulated, though this doesn’t rule out the possibility of abuse.
