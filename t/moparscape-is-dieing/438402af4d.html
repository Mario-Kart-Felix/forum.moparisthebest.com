MEGA POST! ok! there have been many proposals for community-unifying projects and they have all failed, mainly due to disinterest and/or poor planning. what you need to take into account is the range of people here (this will be very speculative!):

first off, there are two types of active members

[ul][li]those that care about the community[/li]
[li]those that don’t care about the community (very probably the majority, many just want their own RSPS and that’s it. fair enough)[/li][/ul]

and out of those that care about the community, there are

[ul][li]those able to contribute (they have a relevant skill)[/li]
[li]those unable to contribute (likely the majority)[/li][/ul]

now through those two deductions we can conclude that there probably is a minority that do care about the community and are able to contribute. now, you have to take into account other things

of those that are able to contribute

[ul][li]some can contribute greatly (time, effort, resources)[/li]
[li]some can’t contribute too much (large majority, because there are more important things than this)[/li][/ul]

and if the project was a community server (which is the most frequently proposed project and also the most likely to be chosen as it’s the most unifying), you have:

[ul][li]those that can program proficiently[/li]
[li]those that can’t program well (probably just the minority if i’m optimistic)[/li]
[li]those with something to contribute that isn’t programming (graphics, web design, advertising etc.)[/li][/ul]

from this, we really can see that there is probably a smaller number of people that are actually going to be of use to this project. usually, most of the people that will join the project will probably be those that can’t contribute too much, but want to help in little ways. you will probably have a smaller crew of people who can contribute often with lots, but they will very likely become demotivated due to the lack of contributions by a lot of the people that can’t contribute too often, and work will slowly desist. this is the main problem, and it can be observed with almost every community project that has ever started here, or on similar sites. proposing that everyone helps is just not going to work, someone is going to have to assign roles and find dedicated people to get the project well on the track to success.
