“Client and Server’s revolutions are different.

What?

First off, the client and source have a revision, not they aren’t holding a revolution lol.
Second, you’re restating yourself when you say “versions between client/source are different”.

"Cache isn’t in the correct place."
Did you even check your signlink to make sure this wasn’t the issue?

"Outdated Java/JDK."
If this was the issue you’d get an unsupported major/minor version error.

"Client IP/Ports didn’t match Sources."
9/10 sources don’t require an IP anymore, I’ve told you this. Just make sure the client is trying to connect to the ip where the source is. If you’re running it locally, use “localhost” or “127.0.0.1” (they’re the same thing).

"Client/source don’t match."
This, again, would not be a huge issue. As long as they have generally the same revision and the packets are being handled the same ( like player/npc shit usually) your only real errors would be that the server is trying to load something the client doesn’t have, or vice versa.

"Didn’t set your environmental variables"
You don’t need environmental variables, they are encouraged though.

And yes, this is client sided whether you want to believe it or not. There is nothing here that is saying it’s server sided. Explain to me where the proof is that it is server sided.

Your issue (like I’ve said before on teamviewer) is that the client is not connecting to the source.

Like I said, make sure the cache is in the appropriate place in your client via the signlink.

Your issue (whether you believe me or not) is client sided. How? Because the client cannot/will not download the cache. Being that it isn’t downloading the cache, it cannot load and throws the error_js5 whatever.

Manually put the cache in the appropriate directory in the signlink.

ALSO: Make sure your server has the cache in the correct folder. It needs to be in like data/cache or something. I can’t remember exactly.
