moparisthebest August 4, 2016, 7:01am #1

I have recently got new SSL certificates for all the domain names, and activated support for the next-generation web protocol SPDY with a few patches to shrpx, a SPDY -> HTTP proxy.

What this means to the end-user (you) is that you can now access all forums over https if you like, and in addition to being secure, it should even be faster as it will default to the fastest SPDY protocol that your browser supports. Newish versions of Firefox and Chrome support SPDY, I’m not sure what else does.

I also get an A rating on the ssl test:
https://www.ssllabs.com/ssltest/analyze.html?d=moparisthebest.com
Which means you can be fairly confident that what you are seeing or sending actually comes from or to this website.
It’s kind of sad when a forum like this where security really doesn’t matter that much, has a slightly higher rating than major banks:
https://www.ssllabs.com/ssltest/analyze.html?d=53.com
https://www.ssllabs.com/ssltest/analyze.html?d=usbank.com

Of course, if you notice any problems with the new system, let me know ASAP. Otherwise, enjoy!
