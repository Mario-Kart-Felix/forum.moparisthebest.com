First off, I’m completely aware of the fitness thread. Thanks.

Just some back story stuff, a couple days ago I purchased gym membership to continue losing fat and start building muscle. Over the past 6 months, I’ve lost just over 40 lbs and am at 160 now, with a body fat % of 18.8. I talked to a guy there, and my goal should be roughly 8%. Rather than do cardio to lose it all, he said to do lots of muscle-building/strength exercises so the fat eventually burns itself off.

So I thought OK, I’ve lifted weights for about two years in high school so I think I can manage keeping track of a full-body workout (not that I can afford the outrageous price of a personal trainer anyways). But last night I went and exercised for the first time, following a routine I found that splits full body into 5 days, but it didn’t go as I had planned. Initially, it started off alright, since it was all benching and free-weight exercises, but then it started listing machine exercises and there had to have been like 20 different machines and lots of them.

When I went, it was very late and there was almost nobody there, so I couldn’t just watch someone else or ask. Specifically, “cable crossovers”. I found the machines, but the right side didn’t have the right (or same) part you grab onto. It looked like the bar you use for pulling the weight down to your legs when sitting (I don’t know the name). Then I moved onto dips, but couldn’t find dipping machines, but probably just didn’t look hard enough. I prefer not to use a bench.

Another problem I had is adjusting the inclined benches. I saw little knobs and such to adjust them, but the bench seemed pretty resistive and I didn’t want to mess anything up so I just worked with it somewhat too low.

TL;DR
Anyways, I’d like to get some advice from people who were brand new to the gym at one point, had never used anything other than free weights, and can’t afford a personal trainer. It’s very intimidating to go during the day when I’m assuming there are lots of other people, so I’d prefer to go at night when I not only feel better but get to experiment without feeling like I’m keeping anyone waiting.

Also, is it common to see people at the gym who look like they have no idea what they’re doing? It seems like everyone who attends is crazy fit and knows what they’re doing. But everyone has to start somewhere I guess.

Thanks,

~Lin

Happy to help. Honestly just updating progress on the fitness thread Frell created really helped me maintain momentum. I live a perfectly regular life still in terms of eating what I want when I want, I just try to sometimes go for better alternatives or smaller portions, and I still have lost 25 pounds after putting on muscle since July.

I have pretty much cut out McDonalds completely though, always get spicy chicken sandwich from Wendys instead of baconator, and always go for medium size instead of large. If you’re not trying to be hardcore about it, choose one fast food place you love the most and don’t sacrifice it. For me, it’s Chic-Fil-A. It’s the only place I will get 8-piece nuggets with a large coke and waffle fries lol. Just remember, it’s calories in vs calories out.

Oh, and when you’re at home cut the soda and energy drinks out of your life. Switch to filtered water and lipton tea with 1 Stevia sugar packet. Most people seem to hate drinking regular water, so tea with Stevia packet is a way to get around that and only taking in 15 calories. I’ve also cut beer out at home and moved to mixed drinks like Cranberry and Vodka.

Ah yes, losing fat is all about dieting. Exercising just makes the dieting portion easier. I gained about 40 lbs when I went off to college and then lost it all starting like May or something, and I didn't stick to salads or anything. Instead I just cut out all the high calorie stuff, never mindlessly ate food, and burned like 1000 calories at least 3 times a week to get to and from school. Also, I don't drink soda very often and don't even consider touching energy drinks. I just drink water all day, but thanks for the advice.

Although, to be a body builder apparently you have to eat a lot, and having muscle basically makes the fat burn itself off, so that’s the route I’m taking. I’m tired of being skinny with a gut, to be honest.

When I was at my peak, 175 pounds and less than 10% bodyfat I could bench 250 and do 100+ pushups in a row. I could run a mile in 6:30. I had started out in terrible physical shape, ex-smoker and malnourished.

It’s all about numbers. I never ate sugary treats, I avoided products with “Enriched wheat” “High Fructose Corn Syrup” or sweeteners. I ate vegetables, whole grains, meat, yogurt, milk, protein shakes. I worked out 1.5 hours every other day. Basically a quick run so I was fired up, then right into sets after sets of heavy weight and high reps. Monday was chest day, Wednesday I did back and legs, then Friday I hit my arms and maybe did abs. Sometimes I did two days on, to shock my muscles and confuse them. Sometimes I would skip the workout and just do some light cardio and stretch, sometimes I did circuit training or martial arts. At the end of every workout I did a “Suicide” style workout where I made sure that I had worked my muscles until they were screaming for oxygen, so I knew I had torn new mass every workout.

That’s just my personal fitness journey, everybody is different. I wanted to train for crossfit or something like that, but I ended up just enjoying the process. I think exercise makes you happy, as well as helping you shape your appearance and physique, so I highly recommend you get some good books and follow a set exercise routine and dietary guidelines. Best of luck!

Before I give you any tips, I have to say that depending on the gym, you could either have too many alternatives or too few. There is always going to be one piece of equipment at minimum that will work the muscle group you are targeting, but it is important to understand that a lot of the alternatives are simply going to boil down to preference. I would suggest going to www.bodybuilding.com and looking up some beginner workouts. They are usually pretty detailed and have videos demonstrating how to execute the exercise, and some even provide alternatives.

Also, I highly recommend you make cardio something you do on a daily basis, as it is not only vital to the development of aerobic ability, but will play a key role in dropping your bf%. I actually have a workout I would suggest you take a look at (it’s the current plan I’m using), as it has a lot of information about the exercise program, nutrition, etc. It’s one of the best programs I’ve come across, although it might not necessarily be for a beginner, I think it would be a great starting point for research, and potentially, learning exercises.
http://www.bodybuilding.com/fun/jim-stoppani-six-week-shortcut-to-shred-training-overview.html
