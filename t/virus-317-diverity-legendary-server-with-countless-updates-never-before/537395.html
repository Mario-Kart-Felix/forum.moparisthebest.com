Again, the penguins aren’t too smart, so you can hop into the portal yourself to face Shanty Claws… if you dare (safe fight).

[B][I][SIZE=4]Achievement System[/SIZE][/I][/B]
Diverity offers over 80 achievements to unlock. Unlocking an achievement grants a certain amount of achievement points, and one achievement coin for every 10 achievement points you earn. Achievement coins can be spent in a store. Certain achievements also grant you special titles to use on the ::yell channel!


[B][I][SIZE=3]Daily Achievements[/SIZE][/I][/B]
Daily achievements consist of many categories and each category can be completed once per day. Every category completed grants coins, achievement points, and during certain events, bonus items. When any 5 categories are completed, a [B]daily achievement chest[/B] is rewarded, which contains IP, unique crafting materials, as well as a chance at auras and other rare items like the recipe item for the glacial whip and certain premium items.

Recipes come from various sources - some come from the Vote Point store, some come from the IP store, some come from monster drops, etc. Can you hunt down the recipe and materials to make your dream weapon?

[B][I][SIZE=4]Server World Events + Difficulty Scaling[/SIZE][/I][/B]
The server occasionally spawns a number of different kinds of events. These events scale difficulty and volume with the amount of players that attend the event (or, in certain cases, total players online), and after the event concludes, ALL participants receive rewards depending on their contribution level (bronze, silver, gold, or platinum). The scaling is designed in such a way that all participants can get a gold-level contribution.

A couple different events are detailed below:

  1. Shooting star
    A shooting star falls from the sky in a general location (e.g. East Ardy, Seer’s Village, etc), and players have to hunt down the star. The first person to find the star will receive 25% bonus rewards at the end of the event. Mining the star grants stardust and mining experience based on the size of the star. Stardust can be sold to the general store or exchanged for rewards. Players also receive a 25% XP boost for 30 minutes after the event concludes, as well as IP.
  2. Bandos Invasion
    Bandos has always been the most popular in the Godwars Dungeon. However, consistently killing his generals doesn’t make him very happy. After his anger reaches a certain threshold, hell breaks loose and he sends forces to invade Falador. Players have 30 minutes to clear out the generals and minions inhabiting the city, or Bandos will capture white knights to fight for his will. After clearing all the normal Bandos monsters, an elite version of General Graardor spawns in the castle and scales in strength to the amount of players fighting him. After defeating him, players can loot a Bandos event chest which has a very high rate to give Bandos items, as well as a small chance at a legendary recipe for an infinite GWD key, which gives you and your allies full GWD killcount infinitely.


[B][I][SIZE=3]Bosses with Difficulty Scaling[/SIZE][/I][/B]
Sometimes, it is annoying to compete for bosses. However, certain bosses are made under the event framework to scale in difficulty with the amount of players fighting it, such that it is justified for the boss’s loot to be distributed to every person via a chest. Long story short - bosses with difficulty scaling can be fought solo or in a group, and everybody always get the same, if not more, loot than normal!

[B][I][SIZE=4]PKing[/SIZE][/I][/B]
PKing is one of my favorite aspects of RSPS, especially when it isn’t just arranged fighting. There are high-value resources in the Wilderness, such as Frost/Mithril dragons (they drop Frostbite/Hailstorm dagger) and bosses with incredible drops (Bork drops corrupt Vesta and hand cannon), and if you want to obtain these drops, you might just become prey for the PKers out there.

[B][I][SIZE=3]PK Systems[/SIZE][/I][/B]

There are many systems that come with PKing.

  1. Influence Point gain
    You gain a variable amount of IP per kill, depending on many factors, including you and your opponent’s killstreak.
  2. Multi-kills
    Killing multiple people back-to-back sends global multi-kill messages to the server and grants you bonus IP (while making you look like a total BA).
  3. PK rating
    After killing 50 players, you are given a PK rating starting at 1200. This rating increases or decreases when you die/kill in the Wilderness, depending on your opponent’s rating. Reaching certain rating unlocks achievements and titles.
  4. EP
    Killing players, monsters, and bosses in the Wilderness all grant EP. When killing a player, EP is cashed in for bonus drops. You can even receive legendary recipes for items such as the primal longsword if you have the maximum EP.
