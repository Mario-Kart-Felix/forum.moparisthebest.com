lothy August 1, 2016, 12:33am #9

Each group is effectively one of each type, but there’s some crossover. Specifically, we have groups for just badges, groups for just viewing a given section (or sections), and groups for moderation roles. These groups are, with the exception of Community Member and Moparscape Vet, used only as secondary groups.

The moparscape moderation group also doubles as a badge-displaying group, so each new moderator is made a member of two secondary groups: One that grants them the ability to view appropriate hidden forums, and one that grants them their ability to moderate.

It wasn’t always like this; in fact, Moparisthebest put some time into fixing it all up – it used to be a real mess.

Fat_Cookies August 1, 2016, 12:33am #19

ok, you don’t seem to understand how smf permissions work altogether:

Every member has a primary membergroup. This is normally based from post-count (cba to post, junior member, member, senior member) but can changed to a higher rank by an admin (to global mod, admin, vet, community member). A member can only have one primary member group and the name of this group is shown under the username.

Members also can have additional members which can only be assigned by admins. These are exactly the same as primary member groups with the exception that you can have more than one additional group and they do not appear under your username.

This forum has over 50 member groups. Most just display an award in a members post. Others grant access to specific boards that’s normal members can’t see or the right to moderate certain boards.

In the case of Kihomura, his primary group was “Senior Member” but he had the moparscape moderator as an additional group which allowed to moderate. Similarly, community members have the primary group “Community Member” but also belong to the moparscape moderator group as an additional group.

You could even assign a Junior member with the administrator membergroup as an additional group. This would mean that no standard member would know that this member is actually an admin
