the_elve_age August 4, 2016, 1:25am #2

I’m an experienced runner and I’ve been running for 4+ years.

• Stretching. You need to become better at stretching, I’ve heard before that the better you can stretch the more athleticism you have and the more your muscles can input and output.

• Have you started working out? Some good exercises are “calf ups” when you go up and down just raising your foot up and working your calves out. Once your calves start burning, keep going (not too long or you could hurt them). Once you’ve gotten basic calf raises start doing them over stair cases or over something that gives you more gravity on the back of your foot, continue to go up and down.

• If you get into a workout room, do squats. Not just any squats, but speed-squats. They are squats but you only put about half of your weight on the bar. Go up and down as fast as you can and do 3-4 sets of 12. Doing this will increase your muscles “fast-twitch” response (surely you’ve heard of fast-twitch/slow-twitch muscles).

• Start running long distance. I can’t tell you how important it is to get a good balance of both sprinting and running long distance. Long distance will make you a better runner.

• Try sprinting up hill. Think about it, if you get really good at sprinting up hill, then on the flat ground you’ll kick ass.

• Technique, have you started looking for better techniques or ways to actually improve your running form?
