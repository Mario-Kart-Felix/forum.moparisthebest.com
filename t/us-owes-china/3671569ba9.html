Frell, you really need to read up on subjects before you start spouting bullshit. For instance, a welfare system does NOT mean that every unemployed person becomes a lazy obese Democrat.

The Dutch system is as follows. Depending on how long you’ve been working before you lose your job, you can get an unemployment benefit for a maximum of 3 years and 2 months. The first 2 months you get 75% of what you earned, and the rest you get 70%. So you lose 30% of your income.

For every year you have worked, you get one month of benefits. So you have to have worked 38 years to get the maximum of 38 months of benefits.

You can only get the benefits if you are fired, so quitting doesn’t pay. If you are fired and you say “great, I don’t care” you don’t get benefits either.

The benefits are not paid for by the state, and aren’t paid for by the working citizens. Employers pay for it, so they can save money by employing people.

When you receive unemployment benefits, you have to apply for a job once a week. The application is reviewed by an institute, and if you don’t take it serious you may lose your benefits. The longer you receive benefits, the less picky you can be.

All this means that people stay unemployed for a very short period. In 2009, 269860 people received unemployment benefits. 144780 Got a job within 6 months. 65750 Within 6 to 12 months, and 59330 needed 12 months or more. In the same year, 377000 people were unemployed.
