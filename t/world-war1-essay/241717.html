I think you need to develop your structure first.

First thing you need is an introduction. In your introduction you need to give the point to your argument.
You can start off by saying… world war one was started because… . the reason there are different versions of the storyline is because… .

  • It’s good to start off with something that grabs the reader’s attention such as giving a rhetorical question to start off,
    or mention the word “you” to make the reader think that the writer is talking to them, etc.
  • Don’t write too much on the introduction, just a plain description of what you’re trying to point out in the argument.

Then you start with the main paragraphs
1 - causes and reasons leading to world war one
2 - why there are different versions of it
these are your points you’re trying to make, correct?

  • so you should start on your first main paragraph by saying “Firstly, world war one is caused by… the article…Wikipedia… (or whatever) states that… this happened…” etc (which links your sources into your essay)

  • then you should start on your next main paragraph by saying “Secondly, there are many versions of the war because… the article…Wikipedia… (or whatever) states that… this happened…” etc (which links your sources into your essay)

  • and in those paragraphs you say something to oppose your side of the argument, but rebuttal it (to balance both sides)

  • you should give the direct link of the sources (don’t just say “this source states world war one was caused by…”, say “the non-fiction text [Name of Text] by…[Author] states that world war one was caused by…”

Then when you finish explaining everything, you go to the conclusion (new paragraph) where you give a summary of your essay.

And finally you have 1 more paragraph on your judgment after the conclusion and give an opinion of what you think caused it and why there are different versions of it, according to your sources.

Don’t just give a whole lot of information on how it started and why there are different versions of it. If you want to do a proper essay structuring is important.

Intro
Main Paragraphs
Conclusion
Judgment

Basically, you want the introduction to contain the question and list the various details that need to be explored so the reader can gain a better understanding. Once the introduction has been done you need to follow SEEL.


S - Statement. Basically present your point in a few sentences.
E - Explain. Go into detail as to why that statement is correct. Expansion of the statement is the key here.
E - Examples. List various examples and sources that provide evidence for your points. Your trying to sell your viewpoint here.
L - Linking. Basically, you want to make a small statement that links this paragraph to the next. You need to link them up with something that your current paragraph touches upon.

Your current work lacks detail and a severe amount of depth. It looks like you have done very little research and barely no sources have been used. If you hand that in, i can guarantee you will struggle to pass.
