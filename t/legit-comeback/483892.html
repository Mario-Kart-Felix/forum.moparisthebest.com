Tom August 4, 2016, 2:55am #7

I’ve slowly become to hold the opinion that vanilla minecraft (or, anything near vanilla like mcrft) is a pointless venture to undertake. I think the way Mojang has gone with the game is streamlined with mistakes, and flaws in the content they’re adding. To be quite frank I don’t know why I expected anything different - They have a 1 man development team who would appear to add content without thinking about the multiplayer implications.

Long gone are the days where MineCraft could be played without the need for excessive damage control plugins, and laggy modding APIs. The level of maintenance that is needed for something that is aimed to replicate what Minecraft should be out of the box is shameful. I think Minecraft is a good singleplayer game, but they’ve really not considered the implications of what they’re adding to the multiplayer community.

I like what FTB does, while it doesn’t actually fix any of the issues relating to multiplayer it saturates the game with content by opening up an entirely different dimension of gameplay which prolongs the multiplayer experience, and the health of the world.
