[quote=“Waffendennis, post:20, topic:225213”]I myself have owned allot of cars, mostly Japanese cars (<3 JDM).

Let me advise you on a car when you just got your license.
A Nissan S13 (I would prefer a S14) is very nice and fust, but it isn’t a car for beginning drivers.
The reason for this is first of al: Power + turbo and rear wheel drive.

If you don’t have any expierience at all with driving you will end up crashing sooner than you think with RWD, this is because the slightest error in a corner will be punished HARD, for example: You’re cutting a corner, and in the apex of the corner you push the pedal like you would with a FWD car, with FWD this is possible but however with RWD your backend will breakout and if you don’t correct you will find your self reversed, or sliding off the road.
Plus those S13/S14 are mostely 2.0 Turbo engines, I don’t know if you got any expierience with normal turbo engines (not diesel) they aren’t easy to drive for a beginning driver, because you don’t get the full power from idle RPM like a supercharger, you get a kick in the back from a turbo and in the case of a S13/S14 it starts at 3.500 RPM.

I’d say go with toyota, or better yet go with Honda.
Few months ago I got myself a black Honda Civic hatchback from '92. It’s totally remodded.
The car came with a 1.6l D16Z6 SOHC VTEC engine (good for 130HP on 900KG), I swapped it for a 1.8l DOHC VTEC B18C-R engine (good for 220HP on 900KG). At the moment it’s getting turbo mounted, so far the car runs 469HP @ 0.2 bar. The car is ment for circuit because it isn’t quite handy on the streets.[/quote]
I was definately thinking of going japanese and as for driving exxperience that is something i do have i did some days at rockingham race circuit, driving on and off the track but i agree with getting a rear wheel drive, there is no point in making a front wheel drive car it gives the wheels too much to do resulting in understeer.
