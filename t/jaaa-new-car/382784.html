.Unlimited:

How about comparing MPG on all of those cars.

Actually, like I said, the 5.7 Hemi will only run 6 or 4 cylinders if that is all that is needed, so you get V8 power when you want it, and 4 cylinder gas mileage when you need it.

For the 2007 Charger R/T AWD with the ~380 HP/~385 ft-lbs/torque 5.7L Hemi, the EPA estimated gas mileage is 17 in town and 24 on the highway, but generally it gets 20-22ish. That’s really good for such a powerful, large car, especially with the fuel-sucking AWD.

The SRT8 is all performance, so it has a lower 14 city/20 highway EPA estimate. It will get 15-16 if you drive it like you stole it, and you can squeak 20-22 out of it if you drive it conservatively. That’s what you get for a race car though.
