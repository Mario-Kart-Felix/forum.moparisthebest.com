Everything happens for a reason. Bowser made me think of a great point I always like to bring up with mentioning feudalism.

After the Bubonic Plague hit Europe and the Mediterranean area, over 40-50% of the population died. Now, surely nothing good can happen from 50% of the population dying, correct? Wrong. The current system set in place was called feudalism. With feudalism you would have a king with a bunch of land. He would then give this land to lord’s with the job of protecting his land. These lord’s would then hire knights to protect the land and would give them land in payment for their services. Lastly, these knights would enforce peasants to sow the fields and grow the crops. Peasants had no choice but to agree to do this, for if they didn’t they wouldn’t be under protection from bandits and would most likely die. When 50% of the population died, there wasn’t enough peasants to grow crops. Thus, peasants were able to ask for a wage to farm the land and hence the Renaissance period.

So to end this history lesson and to have a little moral of the story: Everything happens for a reason. Although everyone died on Earth with the great flood, more good may of come of it than harm. We just may never know.

Note: I do not consider The Bible to be a history book, but more a book of morals. I highly doubt the world was ever flooded and was most likely a story telling of God’s great prowess or whatnot.
