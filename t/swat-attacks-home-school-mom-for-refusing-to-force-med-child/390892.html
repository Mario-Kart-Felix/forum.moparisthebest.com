I’ve been following this case (haven’t looked into it in a week or two though, need to get on that), and frankly I find it disturbing. The state of mental health law here in Michigan is absolutely horrible, and this incidence in particular is extremely abhorrent. First off, there was no warrant in this case, and the mother had the permission of a doctor to take her daughter off of medication - it had been causing extremely adverse side effects (I believe it was risperdol but I’m not entirely sure.) She had originally gone to a somewhat ‘radical’ group that recommended it, and when she visited another doctor who agreed that it wasn’t beneficial for her child, the original group worked with Child Protective Services (another fucked up organization in Michigan, at least with my experiences with them) to attempt to take the child away. This led to a standoff in the home as the mother didn’t want to let the child be forced onto harmful medication (seriously, antipsychotics can cause permanent injury), and for some reason a gun was fired. The mother was arrested (last I checked she was released on bond) and the child was put into Hawthorne (last I checked she was still there.) Some background on Hawthorne: it’s a shitty, shitty, place, even as far as American mental institutions go. They’re mostly a money-suck and they’re generally a long term facility. They drug their patients and aren’t very helpful, from what I’ve heard they don’t do much at all. This is a travesty and an abuse of power by both the Detroit Police and the Michigan Child Protective Services. I could go on and on about the problems presented by this case/other instances.

tl;dr: Mother had doctor’s permission to take child off of harmful medication, corrupt doctor’s group convinces corrupt CPS to try and take child away without warrant or court order, standoff results in shot being fired, mother arrested, child forced into a shitty mental institution and onto meds that caused adverse side effects on her.

I know that if a judge had ever tried to force me onto meds I would feel violated, and I would feel even more violated if no judge had given permission for such a thing and my home was broken into without a warrant to try and force me to take them.
