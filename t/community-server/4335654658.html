mod_taharok August 2, 2016, 4:05pm #37

Well my arse remark pretty much reveals the problems I’ve been seeing with these topics. I asked for a reason to do a community server, and I got several different replies. No offense intended to anyone, but most of the reasons provided were just variations of “it would be cool.” A valid reason is the difference between servers designed to earn revenue versus freely shared servers, but does that really make much difference in the end? In terms of organized content, perhaps. Really, the best reason supplied has to be the learning experience. Nevertheless, is an entire community server needed for such a feat? Could we just have a series of tutorials instead? What do we achieve by teaching the community in this way?

I’m mostly playing devil’s advocate here. I have, as far as I remember, always backed the idea of a community project, though at my most cynical times, basically dissuaded its possibility with many questions, sorta how I am now. I don’t want to discourage the project, but I do want to try and evoke some list of differences between this proposition and its many predecessors. Those ones, most obviously have yet to succeed. The only project that’s come close to succeeding was hybridscape. As we can see, it hasn’t.

I ultimately propose our most successful route would to simply be adapting osiris, rather than taking the near-impossible journey of trying to develop our own base server all over again. Many of the programmers in this topic, however experienced they may be, may not really understand the bureaucracy of designing a server framework (again, no insults intended, these are simply my observations). There are a lot of design factors to consider, actual implementations, more design factors in regards to content systems, more implementations, testing, and so on. The regular pattern, but for tens of thousands of lines of code that need to work perfectly before any actual content can be written. That’s exhausting.

The only way I can endorse the idea this time around is if there is some agreement to use osiris. I’m not sure if my opinion even matters, since I probably won’t have time to provide contribution other than to give feedback once in a while and maybe try and manage part of the team, but I do say osiris is the only way to go.

As far as legality is concerned, the license is pretty clear. Moparisthebest has exclusive rights to use it on his website at this point. Whatever problems exist between blake and boomer are independent of his decision. The only thing that is gray area is boomer advertising paragon as the official implementation of the framework, which is entirely independent of this proposition.

two cents…
