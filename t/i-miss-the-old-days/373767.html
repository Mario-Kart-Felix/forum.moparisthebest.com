I miss the old days

General Discussion
system August 1, 2016, 1:37am #1

Do any of you remember when you could host a server, throw it on the server status, and get 10-20 players without even trying?
Back when having over 90% uptime was considered an accomplishment? Back when players trained attack on dummies, strength on doors, and defence on hay bales.

Nowadays, RuneScape emulation has turned into a business. If you have 99% uptime, you’ll be on page 3 of the server status. Everyone has dedicated servers, some people make thousands of dollars, etc. and I feel like RuneScape emulation has lost the “innocent fun” aspect that it used to have, and now it’s a commercial operation where you have to work your ass off just to get some loyal players and spotlight.

What happened to us? Can we ever get back? Maybe I should start over, and make a completely separate website and sever all ties with the current RuneScape community (MoparScape, R-S, etc.). I could make my own source and give it to people to use and build off of. It could be an open-source community based around one server and one central repository, started off properly.
