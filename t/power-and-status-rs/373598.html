system August 1, 2016, 1:37am #9

I take pride in all that I’ve done and what I continue to do for RuneScape emulation. I take pride in everything I know and my ability to help others. I don’t think that my capabilities and what I’ve done makes me better than any other person here, though.

I’m an administrator on my server, but I’m the most laid back administrator on the whole team. My community has one rule because I don’t get anything out of “babysitting” players and making them do things that I want them to do. That’s not what’s important to me. I’m here to kill boredom and to do something that I believe to be worth my time. I like being at a level of understanding to the point where I can use what I know to help others succeed as well.

When I first came here, I had a plan to get to the top, to conquer all of RSPS and to monopolize it. To be the very best at everything, to have an excuse to be an egotistical, arrogant asshole and to have people envy me. Above all, getting to where I am has been a learning experience to me: that the plan I had is not how I want to live my life and interact with others.

So now I just chill here, doing stuff for people when I’m bored and helping out wherever I can. A vast majority of my projects are open-source, and as such a vast majority of my time that I put into RSPS is donated to the communities that taught me RSPS in the first place.

I never sought out MoparScape Vet status or the Java Guru badge. From what I’ve been told, super_ made a veteran poll for me (out of nowhere) and also suggested that I get this Java Guru badge. I didn’t ask for any of it haha, it just sort of happened.

CoonHunter August 1, 2016, 1:37am #11

The reason I first came into programming a RSPS was because my friend called me out of the blue after not talking to me for 6 months and told me to go on this website, that there was a private version of runescape there. I, being a no-life for runescape at the time, decided to go check it out. It was an All-Star base, but the fact it had custom items that were not in RS made it appeal to me. When that one was shut down, I decided to look into making my own, not even knowing the first steps of doing so.

I contacted the owner of the one I was playing, he informed me I would need JDK, suggested to have Notepad++, and even pointed me to a few different websites to get a server and client from. Generally, I was thinking this was going to be a piece of cake, I mean cmon, a 13 year old kid (he is now 16) was hosting this, how hard can it possibly be? I soon learned that this was actually a complicated process. Looked for minor help, for example, making max level to 120 instead of 99, and minor things like this.

When I first opened up with a barely edited base, and only advertising on 2 websites I ended up with 10-20 players logged in regularly. I felt a sense of pride that people were even on something that I was trying to create (edit, or whatever you will). After they started asking for features (after i figured a bunch out), I actually felt a sense of minor importance.

Anyways, these days I help out others when I can, but that’s about it. The entire thing has gotten boring, and nobody seems to care about it when they play anymore, except for its bugs, glitches, and anything else to complain about that is not perfect.

I first started out RSPS when a friend told me about them. I’d heard about them from before but never bothered to actually try them out. I found an Allstar based server and got addicted to it and I was on it for a long time when it shut down. I began looking into running my own server a few months later when my friend told me that RSPS were becoming bad, so I thought I’d make a good RSPS with a lot of players on it. I quit hosting the server when I discovered how hard it was to add customs, and I’m not into graphics design anyways.
