BobDole81 July 30, 2016, 9:39am #4

Heat is the effect of the activity of particles - the more energy contained in a system, the more active the particles, and thus the greater the temperature (ie, there is a larger amount of heat). There are three types of heat: conduction, convection, and radiation. Conduction is the movement of heat (or energy) between solid objects directly through the particles (ie, molecules/ atoms). Convection is the transfer of heat through flowing particles, in gasses for example. Radiation is the transfer of energy using electromagnetic radiation (ie, light). Although all radiation causes the transfer of energy, only infrared radiation is realized as heat to us.

There are many types of energy…but it depends on what you mean. Typically energy refers to mechanical energy and is divided into kinetic and potential energy, although potential energy can be split into electrostatic, elastic, gravitational, and thermal potential energies. There is also a lot of energy held within an atom, and can be described with nuclear energy, binding energy, and bond energy when you get into molecules.
