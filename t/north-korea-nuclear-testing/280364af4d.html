I don’t know about other countries, but in Australia if you kill someone in self defence then you lack the intent required for a murder charge, which means you’d be charged with second degree murder, or manslaughter, at most.

Now this is the ‘tricky’ part. If you can convince the court that you believed that the force with which you responded to the threat was reasonable and necessary then the jury would most likely find you not guilty.

So in short you’d be tried by the courts, but you’d get off.
