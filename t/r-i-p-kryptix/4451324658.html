Yes, they actually are. A motorcycle helmet has 3 layers, a harder outer shell layer, a softer, inner layer, and a padded layer.

They work by the hard shell outer layer absorbing and spreading the force over the impacted area, which is why generally when you have a bad crash you will notice your helmet will ‘crack’ or split. The outer shell’s only job, (other than to be painted nicely for da chickz), is to spread residual kinetic energy over a larger area.

The softer inner shell works to absorb and decelerate the head to try and soak up what energy has gone through the shell.

And the padded layer is there to secure the helmet, comfortably and securely. Basic helmet safety laws (ones that are the very basic of basic, helmet laws that aren’t cleared for competition for that matter, have a bare minimum of 400g.

New helmet technology is also emerging rapidly. Look at the new brand 6D, that I am luckily enough to have their equipment support. They use a ‘suspension’ in their helmet shells.

So you saying that a helmet is only there to prevent glancing blows, is very very very stupid.
