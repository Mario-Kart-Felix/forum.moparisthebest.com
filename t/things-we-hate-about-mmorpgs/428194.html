davidi2 August 2, 2016, 3:07pm #19

Right now there are only 2 MMORPGs I am playing, and the main things I dislike about them:

World of Warcraft: Questing. It’s the stupidest quest system ever invented; if I wanted to explore 40 different map areas doing the exact same cookie-cutter five different type of quest, I’d do the same quests over again. And the fact they based leveling off of this ticks me off, I can’t stand the leveling in that way.

RuneScape: I love the quests here, if World of Warcraft had some real storyline quests like that it would be much less of a bore to train. I dislike how slow the xp is though, but I’m guessing that’s because it’s based around the whole ‘one maxed account’ concept, instead of different classes you can max with different play styles and such.

Personally I think RuneScape would do good with a sort of class system, and maybe even limiting the amount of skills you can train like WoW does.

The very same reason games such as EvE and Ultima Online have an avid following. In the case of Ultima Online it was designed in such a way that you maxed out easily, and instead the endgame began instantly. In the case of EvE you are likely to never max out all the skills due to their linear time progression system.

The big con with class lock-ins is that PvP becomes far less interesting as you know what to expect. In a freeform skill system with a hardcap, you have far more interesting choices which gives a nice, emergent experience
