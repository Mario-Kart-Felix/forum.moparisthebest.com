Now to explain.
“if (itemUsed == * && useWith == * || itemUsed == * && useWith == *) {” Basically means… “If you use (this item) and (this item) together” And then the { means “,”. If you know good English, this should kind be like grammar, but it’s grammar in Java. Similar to English ;p kinda.
“if(c.playerLevel[c.playerHerblore] >= 80) {” Basically means that it’s making sure the player has 80 herblore before making extremes.
“c.getItems().deleteItem(useWith, c.getItems().getItemSlot(useWith),1);” Means it’s deleting the item you used with the first item, and making the slot free.
“c.getItems().deleteItem(itemUsed, c.getItems().getItemSlot(itemUsed),1);” Means it’s deleting the first item that you used on the other item, and making the slot free.
“c.getItems().addItem(15308,1);” means it’s adding the outcome (The extreme potion) to your inventory.
“c.getPA().addSkillXP(40000 * Config.HERBLORE_EXPERIENCE, c.playerHerblore);”… this one’s kinda obvious. It means it’s giving you 40k herblore exp for making the potion
“} else {
c.sendMessage(“You need 80 Herblore to make Extreme Magic (4).”);” Means that if they DIDN’T have 80 herblore in the first place, that message will be sent to them, and no items will be deleted, and no items will be added.
“}
}” If you didn’t know, if you have one bracket, you have to close it. We had 2 brackets already opened, so we had to close them.
