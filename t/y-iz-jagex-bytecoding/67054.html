semaphore July 30, 2016, 6:04am #9

Is it far too much to ask of ‘community members’ to refrain from idiot speak?

If you look closely, unpackclass is only needed if you do not have Pack200. Pack200 is a sun approved/developed/sanctioned jar packer. It is quite efficient (compared to what you or I could do). If you are using any jre/jdk below 1.5, you will likely not have Pack200 as it first started shipping with 1.5+. unpackclass is basically their implementation of pack200 for jdk 1.4.2.

byte was on the right track. There will be a problem loading that game code from their cache because they try to load Pack200 first. That will fail, and invoke the downloading and execution of their version. Again, this will only happen on jdk/jre 1.4.2. If you use 1.5+ you’ll avoid any usage of their shared unpacker.

semaphore July 30, 2016, 6:06am #11

Method counting would only work if you are injecting code ignorantly. One of the things I do every time I decide the only way to get around some issue is through injection, is figure out the ways my injection can be detected. The most basic defense you have is closely monitoring the ClassLoader. Know what is and is not loaded. Look for signatures in the bytecode. Know how often some things are supposed to happen and if they happen more or less than you expect, raise a flag.

Keep in mind, if they want to catch you, they will. There is very little you can do to be prepared for every possible change they could make. So stay on your toes.
