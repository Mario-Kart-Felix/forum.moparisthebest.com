Mr_Yankee August 1, 2016, 11:12am #24

I don’t like the theme of this, as money isn’t everything, no matter how much everyone says it is.

If you make a “good” game, it’s going to get good results, as long as you show it to someone.

One of my new favorite games of all time: Demon’s Souls. Originally, this game was expected to sell less than 75,000 copies, and that’s probably a bunch of the anime-freaks who followed the game developers’ other games (anime games, basically). Anyway, Demon’s Souls was ridiculously successful, moving from Japan and Asia, to North America, and eventually to Europe, where many people on all of these continents still play it. The thing is, this game was extremely budgeted, and probably lacked some planned content because they didn’t want to piss off their publisher any further (had been pushing back the dates).

tl;dr money isn’t, and never will be, the only option to success. It’s just the lazy-way-out.
