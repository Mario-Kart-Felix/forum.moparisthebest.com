Let’s put it this way, I’m guessing your an adolescent thinking game development is pretty darn cool (which it is), however, coding in general is becoming ever so advanced, and the concepts of game development are being driven by world leading industries in game development (e.g. SONY) that strive for perfection, so it’s unlikely that you will be making the new “perfect” Call Of Duty game any time soon.

Now, don’t get me wrong, all game developers started from somewhere, but not without the fundamental basics that are needed to succeed in making a decent game.

Perhaps your wondering what the hell I’m talking about? Well, in order to make a successful game than you will need most of these things:

[size=14pt]Large Scale Development[/size]

Knowledge:

  • Intermediate knowledge of general coding; the most popular gaming code languages are C++, and JAVA, so I’d start learning these thoroughly before attempting to draw guns on GIMP.

Team’s:
Have you considered the effort needed to develop a game, no? A major company such as Infinity Ward usually takes 8-14 months to successfully develop a game (e.g. Modern Warfare 2). Now let me tell you, Infinity Ward is not run by one person, it is a company of several teams working day-by-day consistently. Imagine trying to accomplish what 50 people can do by yourself. That would take you a VERY long time. So you will need to come up with a team, which then brings me to my next topic.

Cost:
You will need to provide for your development team, this means an office, multiple computers; hundreds of thousands of dollars will be needed in the development stages of your game. The budget for large scale developments varies from at least $20,000 - several millions of dollars.

[size=14pt]Small Scale Development[/size]

Small development team dairy log: (Very useful for individuals wanting to start understanding what a “small” scale development team attribute to)
http://www.gamedev.net/page/resources/_/reference/103/postmortems/small-scale-development-grand-scale-ideas-r2314

I hope I have enlightened your mind of what game development consists of; not just cool animations or graphics, but the determination to create something amazing. Please, i hope i haven’t effected your dreams in the future, but keep in mind that game development is only attempted by the determined, not script kiddies who know how to make themselves spawn items on a Runescape private server.

Will be adding more game development related topics asap.
