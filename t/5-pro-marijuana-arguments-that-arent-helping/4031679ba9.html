lothy August 1, 2016, 1:07pm #51

For what it’s worth, impairment is induced by sleep deprivation as well. In fact, the graphs that describe alcohol-induced impairment and sleep-dep induced impairment are both exponential, and virtually identical after the 24 hours of being awake.

In Australia, we have a legal driving limit of 0.05 BAC (blood alcohol concentration). You can have two drinks and stay under it if you’re male of average weight and height.
The point I’m making is similar to Greeny’s, I suspect. Ultimately we accept mild levels of impairment, otherwise it’d be a zero BAC across the board.
The problem with marijuana is that, being illegal, nobody can quantify ‘acceptable’ or mild impairment levels.

I don’t agree with intentional drink driving, even when it is below the limit. I think someone should go out of their way to avoid driving if they’ve had anything to drink.
But I do think acceptance of low range BAC isn’t the end of the world for those people who have no alternatives.
My opinion for marijuana is similar, but the problem is the lack of information on the quantity that you can smoke before passing that mild level of impairment that society allows.
