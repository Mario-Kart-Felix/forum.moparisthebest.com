slavemaster August 2, 2016, 1:43am #1

Since the Bot-nuking update on the 25th, the game client code is now distributed in “gamepack” archives like so:
http://world1.runescape.com/gamepack3fadvOgm4xaXPSh38voAXUdsGXP6MLgX_8154080.jar
http://world119.runescape.com/gamepack6KMn7fFI8q31zZGBofP5Xw96MdhR97Lk_5323730.jar

To find them on your own, go to any world page (world.runescape.com) and examine the source code. The relevant snippet is located as a body of a tag; for example:

document.write('<app'); document.write('let '); document.write('name=runescape '); document.write('id=game '); document.write('width="100%" '); document.write('height="100%" '); document.write('alt="Please visit http://www.runescape.com/kbase/guid/the_game_won_t_load for assistance" '); document.write('archive=gamepackGhvTXnsR6fvGErnTayPiaQavHdorWn6J_1992758.jar '); document.write('code=client.class '); document.write('mayscript>');

What is strange, however, is that only some of these URLs appear to work. The general structure of the names is as follows:
gamepack_.jar

random-suffix appears to have no bearing on the existence of the file; it is equivalent to the suffix of the older archives, used to avoid JAR caching. Changing it to some other string, such as “jagexsmells”, appears to work just fine, therefore it is apparently and semantically random.

session-mark, however, is strange. It is also passed as a parameter to the applet, with the name being “7”. For example:

Changing even one character of this value causes it to change; attempting to reach http://world1.runescape.com/gamepackGhuTXnsR6fvGErnTayPiaQavHdorWn6J_1992758.jar (I changed a single v to a u) turns up Jagex’s 404 page.

Reloading the page, regardless of world-id, however yields a brand new session-mark on its own:

This variation changes among both different players as well as different page loads; such behavior seems consistent with some sort of per-session watermark used by Jagex.

Is session-mark really such a watermark? If so, what is its significance? What can it be used for?
Discuss.
