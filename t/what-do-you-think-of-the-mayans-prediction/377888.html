What is the b’ak’tun?

1 day is 1 K’in
20 days are 20 K’in, or 1 Winal
360 days are 18 Winal, or 1 Tun
7200 days are 20 Tun, or 1 K’atun
144000 days are 20 K’atun, or 1 B’ak’tun

In other words, one B’ak’tun equals ~395 years. It’s just a way of denoting long periods, just like we use days, weeks, months, years, decades, centuries and millennia. Only the Maya’s didn’t use multiples of 10 for periods longer then 1 year, instead they multiplied everything by 20. Except the Winal, which they multiplied by 18 to get close to a solar year.
