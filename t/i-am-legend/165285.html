Well, after seeing the previews every time I watched TV I decided that it was necessary to go see the movie, I am Legend. It is the new Will Smith movie and I have to say it was pretty decent in quality.

Will Smith portrays a man who stays behind in New York after it has been quarantined because of the “KV Virus.” The virus is created when a cure for cancer mutates killing nearly everyone who has been “cured.” The virus becomes airborne killing most of the population and changing the rest into monsters of sorts. These monsters are described as being in an advanced rabies like state, they only come out at night and pretty much attack anything that moves. Will Smith’s character, Robert Neville, and his dog Samantha “Sam”, are the only sane survivors in New York. Robert chose to stay behind to try to develop a cure for the KV Virus.

[Spoiler Warning: Skip the next paragraph if you don’t want the ending ruined for you]

[size=7pt]I have to say there has never been a movie that has been emotional enough to make me cry, but this movie did succeed in doing so. The part that made me cry is when Robert basically goes off the deep end putting him and his dog in danger and resulting in Sam getting infected. He rushes her back to his lab and tries everything he can to save her, however, holding her in his arms she starts trying to bite him and her fur starts to fall out. He restrains her and eventually ends up having to strangle her to death. This causes Robert to snap, he goes out after dark and tries to kill many of the infected and almost dies himself until he is rescued by two other survivors, Anna and Ethan. Anna and Ethan are survivors from Maryland that had heard Roberts radio message. You find out that the infected followed them back to Roberts house and attack, this causes Robert, Anna, and Ethan to become corned in the lab and in a final act of self sacrifice Robert saves Anna and Ethan. He hands Anna a vial full of blood and says “The cure is in her blood”. He than kills himself and all the other infected in the lab using a hand grenade. Quite heart touching, eh?[/size]

[/Spoiler]

I’d give this movie a 9/10.
