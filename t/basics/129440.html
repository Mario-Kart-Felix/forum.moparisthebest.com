Well thanks, I appreciate it, but it wasn’t requesting it for myself. I was thinking more of some general guidelines for beginners to really catch up to speed. More text-based rather than a tutorial format. Something like this (I personally hate Do’s and Don’ts because of its limitations, but it was the easiest way to promptly organize my thoughts):

[b][size=10pt]Do:[/size][/b]

[ul][li]Choose a general color scheme. This is a very useful tool (I would use the setting: analogic)[/li]
[li]You can go by the most common ‘template’: background with brushes (or filters), then a render, then text, then filters.[/li]
[li]Make sure the render blends. Nothing is worse than a render that sticks out like a sore thumb in your asshole.[/li]
[li]Get familiar with the various filters. Get a stock photo, and play around with the settings and all the filters. Try remembering the shortcuts (Ctrl+F) and even the names for the filters.[/li][/ul]

[size=10pt]Don’t:[/size]

[ul][li]Don’t use a fancy font that can be found on your system. Either go simple, or find some fonts online.[/li]
[li]Don’t use a font simply because it looks cool by itself. Don’t use anything (render, filters, brushes) just because it looks better by itself. The most important part is how well it meshes with the other parts of the sig. There’s no ‘I’ in ‘team.’[/li]
[li]Don’t ever claim you made it in 5 minutes. Be proud of your work, or don’t post it.[/li]
[li]Don’t think complex is necessarily cooler looking. Certainly C4D renders are breathtaking, but you could achieve a similar effect off a few filters.[/li][/ul]
