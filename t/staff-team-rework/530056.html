Staff Team Rework?

Community Suggestions
davidi2 August 4, 2016, 10:28am #1

So I made this post on Tree’s thread, but it really doesn’t belong there seeing as his is more about overall changes to the forum as a whole and mine is more direct.

The problem:
Lack of moderation in some sections. Staff position viewed (and in some cases actually treated) as an award instead of a volunteer position (for example, there is no reason section moderators in their current state should have a ‘moparscape moderator’ badge. They do not have any power at all in other sections of the forum, and the award is misleading).

My proposed solution:

[ul] [li]Migrate to a more sectional moderator based system. No visible award except in section. Able to warn and moderate users for offenses in that section (the ability to warn/moderate is all debatable, not dependant on whether this actually goes through. I do recognize that ability to warn for only offenses in their section would need a SMF mod and as such probably unrealistic). Much larger number, probably 1-5 per section depending on the sections activity.[/li]
[li]Much smaller number of global moderators (I was thinking 5-7). Deal with the occasional forum spammer, people that really need mutes, etc. I personally feel like this number of admins (3-4) is fine.[/li]
[li]All staff are required to be active or they should step down, but they can easily return if they feel like being active again.[/li]
[li]Discussions are more open with the community to increase the marketplace of ideas. This works well with the community suggestions board. Sectionals would not have access to ‘staff’ discussions, and have hardly any more weight in the public ones than a normal user. Of course staff (globals/admins) still make the final call and have their own private discussions as well.[/li][/ul]

Sectional moderator can be an opt-in position, much less selective. You remove almost all of the ‘perks’ and it seems more like a volunteer job instead of an award, so the people that apply are the ones that actually want to help, rather than ones that want the awards.

I’ve always agreed with MITB that having inactive staff doesn’t hurt the forum, but it does change the whole dynamic of the staff team if it appears like you ‘earn’ a staff position and then you don’t have to actually do anything once you get it (also just applies to those with blasé attitudes). I see nothing wrong with a post saying “Today we say goodbye to ___ from our staff team while s/he takes some time off, but would like to welcome ___ in to help fill the gap!”
