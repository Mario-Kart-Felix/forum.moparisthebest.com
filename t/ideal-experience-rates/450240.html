mod_taharok August 3, 2016, 3:01pm #10

Well this is essentially a time problem. Using the same experience growth problem as jagex (where the experience needed for each subsequent level is approximately 12% more than the previous level), then one could easily create an exponential growth function to achieve level 99 within an exact number of days. Skills in RuneScape don’t grow their experience exponentially, they grow it linearly (with possibly an exception to dungeoneering). For example, one level may allow you to get 50 experience per item done, but the next level may allow you to get 60. That’s pretty nice, nice that’s 18% more experience for that item (assuming it’s doable). Now, let’s say you go from an item that gives 200 experience to one that gives 210. That’s only a 4.88% experience increase. Now the experience needed is increasing at a rate much greater than what you can keep up with.

The above is why it takes so many hours for 99 or for 200m experience in RS. Jagex could use only their own numbers (without even analyzing the player base) and come up with ideal times to reach 99 and 200m experience in each and every skill. These ideal times are probably impossible to achieve by a human, but nevertheless, it’s fairly straightforward to calculate.

Back to the problem, though. Let’s say that you want the user to do almost exactly 20 item iterations for each level to reach the next level. If you start off with (83/20)=4.15 experience per item and apply a growth rate of 12% for the next item (assuming there is a new viable item to perform at each level), then the next item for level 2-3 will give 4.648 experience each, the one from 3-4 will give 5.21 experience each, and so on. Once you reach level 50, you will be receiving an optimal 1070.856 experience per item. Levels 50-51 require 10,612 experience, which means this item would only have to be done 9.9 times in order to achieve 51. Now we see some discrepancy here. The growth rate for your items actually is greater than that of the skills themselves (which is to be expected, 12% is an overapproximation). You could derive an exact growth rate function for a parameter l (representing level) which uses the exact same growth rate as the skills, but that’s up to you if you want to do it this way.

Of course, with a system that achieves exactly 20 items per level for each new item at that level, then you would need to do, at minimum, 1,960 iterations to achieve 99, if there’s an optimal iteration at each subsequent level. How easy do you want to make it?
