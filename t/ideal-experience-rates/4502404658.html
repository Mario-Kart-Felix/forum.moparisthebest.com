mod_taharok August 3, 2016, 5:17pm #34
The Wanderer:

Just make the experience scale slower than the rate of the experience going up. So say 10 interactions to get lvl 1-2, but at levels 98-99 it may be 150. Still worth more experience and a lot less interactions if it stayed at a static amount.

That would be an interesting idea. You could start at 10 interactions for 1-2 and add 1 more interaction for each subsequent level. You need nearly 100 interactions for 98-99. The arithmetic growth rate results in an increasingly smaller percentage difference each time, never keeping up with the growth rate for experience and thus needing some pretty massive exp drops for the higher levels (kinda obvious, since 100 interactions for 98-99 would lead to around 10,000 exp each interaction). Also noting that this creates an arithmetic series of the form 10 + sum from index n = 1 to 97 of n, if you want to find the total number of interactions. For those curious, it comes out to 4,763 interactions for level 99. If each interaction takes 3 game ticks, it comes out to about 142.89 minutes, or 2.3815 hours (2 hours, 22 minutes, and 53.4 seconds) of gameplay for that level 99.

Taking into consideration this pattern would be used for all the skills, a user would have to play roughly 57 hours, 9 minutes, and 21.6 seconds in order to achieve 99 in 24 skills. This doesn’t count idle time or losing game ticks as they are leveling. For someone that plays a private server 4-5 hours per day (which I would consider a ton), it would take them a week and a half of continuous game time in order to max out, not to mention missed game ticks. I would just round up to 2 weeks of private server training.
