The processor will support that RAM speed, and that RAM isn’t shipped at that speed. They’re sold at what they’re tested as high as, it’ll actually run by default in 1066, it is up to you to overclock it, but you can do safely to 1333Mhz.

Most motherboards, such as x58 chipset ones will have the XMP profile in the BIOS to enable you to easily overclock the speed of the RAM to the maximum tested at. Though, if you plan on overclocking your CPU too, then I wouldn’t be using XMP.
