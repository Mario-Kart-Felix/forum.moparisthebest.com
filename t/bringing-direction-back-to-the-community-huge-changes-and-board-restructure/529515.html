Community Suggestions

[center][size=24pt]Fast Changes - Huge Results[/size][/center]

Change or Decrease.
A huge overhaul of the forum is required if we wish to maintain our presence in the Runescape3/private server scene. It’s no secret that we now rank very low compared to other communities which are now running their own successful servers. Once known as the central hub for development, Mopar has now become a general forum. I fear that if this trend continues, our membership numbers and influence will decrease very soon.

The Importance of a Backbone
As it stands, we have very little to offer. We once had SCAR as our backbone. Villavu have created something nice with SIMBA but unfortunately that means we have lost out. Private servers? Once were a huge draw but now other communities have taken that discovery and expanded on it considerably. Client ‘hacking’? The sections have become inactive and anyone capable of contributing has left or has lost interest. Our core theme and direction has been lost. We must reconstruct a strong foundation, to allow for the community to reach it’s full potential.

These are the changes I propose:

Unification
Removal of the Gave Development section. We shouldn’t separate a community that’s on the decrease. We should aim to unite the members and then push forward.

Exclusivity to Stroke Egos
Hide and password protect MITB. This will create some exclusivity. Passwords will be awarded to active members or anyone interested in development. When it seems more like a secret club, individuals will be more motivated to contribute something so that they can gain access. Passwords will be acquired rather easy, if interest is shown over a private message to a staff member.

Fresh Development and Teamwork - Unification of the Scene
Key staff members of other private server communities should be approached and offered the chance to join a central private server development crew. There will be a proper award to entice them and the crew will be limited to a certain number. Large scale and fresh ideas will be explored and developed upon. Other communities can use anything that is created, as long as credit is given to the crew. This provides free advertising and fits Mopar back into the role of central hub. Files will be hosted on Mopar’s server. As an incentive, mention of their individual servers will not be censored and server list benefits will be provided.

IRC Support
This one is a no-brainer. Tutorials should be provided on how to connect to the IRC server and an explanation of what it actually is. The official channel should be #moparscape, so no swords are crossed with the long-term members over at #mopar.

Board Re-Structure + A Purpose/Theme
Inactive sections will be culled and the community will shift focus to becoming a general gaming community, with programming sections and a private server section. This section will not just be limited to RS private servers. Other games with decent private servers will be allowed. Structure will be as follows:

General

[ol][li]Announcements.[/li]
[li]Community General[/li]
[li]Programming/Hacking[/li]
[li]Possible: Graphics[/li][/ol]

Gaming

[ol][li]General Gaming[/li]
[li]Minecraft[/li]
[li]League of Legends[/li]
[li]Day Z[/li]
[li]Personal Projects[/li]
[li]Runescape[/li][/ol]

Private Servers

[ol][li]General Private Servers[/li]
[li]Help[/li]
[li]Sub-Forum: Tutorials[/li]
[li]Official Crew Projects[/li]
[li]User Projects[/li]
[li]Sub-Forum: Advertising[/li][/ol]

Award Changes - Staff Changes
This will obviously require awards to be removed. As well as this, it should be very difficult to acquire an award/cup of any sort. Each individual section should have one or two sectional mods who are responsible for the content and encouragement of new ideas. They shall be held accountable by global moderators (once again, a decrease is required). Specific members looking after their specialized sections will allow us to focus more on the content of each individual part, as well as decreasing abuse.

[sub][size=8pt]These are just a few ideas that should be toyed around with. Apologies for the lack of writing style, grammar and possible spelling mistakes. I’m writing like a toddler on speed. It’s 12 A.M and this was a five minute job. I also welcome debate, as long as your arguments provide alternatives. No doubt some idiot will claim that numbers aren’t decreasing. This is more about the feel and longevity of the community.[/size][/sub]

Things I do agree with:

merging a lot of the shit boards that have been recently added
merging minecraft into one board

However, I had a different idea for the staff changes a while back.

The problem:
Lack of moderation in some sections. Staff position viewed (and in some cases actually treated) as an award instead of a volunteer position.

Proposed solution:
Migrate to a more sectional moderator based system. No visible award except in section. Able to warn and moderate users. Much larger number, probably 1-5 per section depending on the sections activity.
Very small number of global moderators (probably < 5). Deal with the occasional forum spammer, people that really need mutes, etc. I personally feel like this number of admins (3-4) is fine.
All staff are required to be active or they should step down, but they can easily return if they feel like being active again.
Staff discussions are more open with the community to increase the marketplace of ideas. This works well with the community suggestions board. Sectionals would not have access to ‘staff’ discussions, and have hardly any more weight in the public ones than a normal user. Of course staff (globals/admins) still make the final call and have their own private discussions as well.

Sectional moderator can be an opt-in position. You remove almost all of the ‘perks’ and it seems more like a volunteer job instead of an award, so the people that apply are the ones that actually want to help, rather than ones that want the awards.
I’ve always agreed with MITB that having inactive staff doesn’t hurt the forum, but it does change the whole dynamic of the staff team if it appears like you ‘earn’ a staff position and then you don’t have to actually do anything once you get it.
