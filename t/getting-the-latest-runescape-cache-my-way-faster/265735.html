Introduction
There are a couple of tutorials on this, and all that I’ve seen are long (in terms of downloading) for no reason. These days, most people I downloading the cache straight from the a RuneScape server. This is fine, but it can take up to a few hours, depending on bandwidth, and there’s no need, you can get it in 10 minutes flat. So, here, I’ll explain my way, which doesn’t vary that much from other tutorials, but will make a hell of a lot faster.

Requirements:
A RuneScape account.
270mb + of disk space. - It’s a big cache, but you can delete what you don’t need.

Credits:
- Defyboy
- Miss. Silabsoft
- Benski
- Flappy Paps Software
[i]- And of course, MrPotatoHead.
- Me

Step 1
Take your RuneScape account and log into a world. I’m not sure if some of the cache is limited to members worlds, but I don’t think it is. If all else fails, I frequently update my models list so you can get it there. After an update; when you log into a RuneScape world, as most of you know, the cache is downloaded to “C:\WINDOWS.jagex_cache_32\runescape”. Don’t worry, you don’t need to touch this. After you’ve logged in, stick around for a few minutes. I suspect it continues to download files after you’ve logged in. Though I don’t know.

Step 2
Now that you’ve acquired the cache, you need to extract it. For this, we use a tool by Defyboy that’s been around for years. You can download it here;

http://grabber.download.solumstone.com

Next, extract the .zip and then run the SetUp.bat. You’ll now be given a folder that contains CacheExport.bat. Run this file. It’ll begin to extract the models. This’ll take about 10 minutes. It varies from PC to PC but there will be a noticeable pause when extracting cache 7, which is saved into the ‘model’ folder. This is because this is the largest folder, and contains your models.

Step 3
Now that you have the models, you need to put them into a usable format. For this, you need DatMaker by Flappy Paps Software. What this program will do, is import the models in their current format (.MDL) and allow you to save them (export them) in .MQO (Metasequoia Model) or .DAT. You can get that here;

http://datmaker.download.solumstone.com

Next…
[i]There are lots of tutorials on editing and adding models, so I won’t go into that here. But this at least has shown you how to get all the models, and quickly. If for some reason this doesn’t work, check out my thread in the model section. The lastest models are posted there.

Hope this helped,

Syrillia[/i]
