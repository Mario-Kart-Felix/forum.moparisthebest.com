a very simple approach is round robin dns as you’ve stated with failover removal (although many would argue against this) and perhaps even geo-balancing…of course this causes problems with cookie sessions and those few isps that ignore ttl though. this also means that your dns should be fast and stable as fuck. you could also use a simple http load distributer and use a simple master-slave design; apache’s module is fairly decent.

better than a dns failover system would be a bgp approach, but it’s a pain in the ass to set up
