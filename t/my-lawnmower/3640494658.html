Steam Engine Lawnmower Running Nicely

With the new (huge) boiler, it runs for a very long time and runs fairly fast. It goes as well as a normal lawnmower at 50 PSI, tomorrow I will get another 4 M of copper piping to make it run better still.

UPDATE:

Had it running this morning, it started to gain pressure almost instantly.

I cut a decently long strip of grass with it.

Then, as it started to run out of water, I made a video of it running down from 90 PSI (it could barely make any pressure near the end.)

UPDATE:

I filled it up, lit it up then cooked my lunch on top of it while it was warming up. Got it to hold steady at 80 PSI, then cut the lawn (only about 20% was left to cut). After I finished cutting the lawn, I got bored so I just let the engine run at full speed - cycling through the speeds for the fun aswell). Then my dad came home to take a look and I turned it on to full speed. It was running perfectly for about 20 seconds then it was like ‘WUSSH’ and lost all power and stopped. First I thought the blade came loose, so I checked that. It was still tight. Then I checked to see if the valves were being lifted. They were. I then removed the spark plug hole bung to see if the piston/con-rod broke. That was still working correctly. So that just left the inlet manifold gasket to check. I knew that it would fail some time in the next day or two because it was already leaking. So I pulled the inlet manifold (a plate with two bolts and the copper feed pipe coming out of it). When I did that, the Epoxy Glue gasket fell out in like 3 bits.

I think the reason that it broke was because I didn’t clean the old Epoxy Glue gasket off the surface correctly last time that I took the manifold off and the glue couldn’t form a very good bond. The head gasket made from Epoxy Glue seems to be holding up perfectly well still.

So yea. It is a 5 minute job to fix it as soon as I get some new Epoxy Glue to seal it all back up.

No video sorry.

UPDATE:

I replaced the engine on my lawnmower. I decided to swap it because the threads were just too far gone on the old engine to make it worthwhile to use decent gaskets. I had to swap the cam, pushrods and bottom end of the con-rod into the new engine (was still an old engine and couldn’t run on petrol at all). It took about an hour and a half to get it all done (it was fiddly work when I couldn’t be screwed to take the sump plate off completely - that takes about 20 mins to remove a certain part to get it off). I used some gasket maker from the neighbour to seal the intake flange. I’m not too sure if the gasket will hold, the neighbour says that the gasket maker is pretty shitty with pressures, and I run the engine at 100 PSI.
