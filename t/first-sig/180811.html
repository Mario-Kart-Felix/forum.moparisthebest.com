Wow, if you’re going to sit there tell me you didn’t put much work into a sig everytime it looks bad, just uninstall PhotoShop now. Honestly. Who cares if it looks terrible. It’s your first. But saying you didn’t put much work into it isn’t an excuse. It won’t make the sig look any better in my eyes.

It’s pretty bad, but you were experimenting with some intermediate techniques. I assume you’ve looked at some tutorials. The text is terrible. Lime green is just awful, and then putting a cyan glow and a shadow, ew. You’ve used some good brushing techniques, and you can use a render, however it isn’t blended at all. It looks to me you were making a border. Just a tip, don’t ever use emboss as a border, it’s hideous.

I’d also like to note that the text is larger than the render. Which is a very bad choice.You want your render to be your focal point, what everyone’s eyes first are directed to in the image. My eyes went to the zigzag on the edge of the sig first, and then to the very bright and blinding text second.

Text is suposed to be minimal, it just tells a person who made the signature, it’s the signature of your signature. You chose that render for a reason. (Well, in your case you just found one.) But usually you choose a render for a reason, whether that represnts you, or represents a community you’re in, whatever. It tells a story, as the rest of the sig does.

I’d suggest using a grunge tutorial, a text effect tutorial, render blending tutorial, a fill effect tutorial, and a abstract tutorial, or a tag.

2/10
