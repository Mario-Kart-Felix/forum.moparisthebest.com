[quote=“I Vain I, post:1, topic:371517”][quote]Damage Soaking and New Hit Splats

We have recognised an imbalance between the potential damage per minute (DPM) and damage capacity (DC) of higher-level players. The rate of damage that you can deal at high levels is far greater than the rate of damage you can withstand, especially when in a multiplayer environment. NPCs and boss monsters can deal with this issue because their life points can reach far beyond 990, but players’ life points cannot. Dealing with this imbalance will be done in several ways, with the first of those ways being introduced this month in the form of damage soaking.

In what will be a huge addition to the Defence skill and defensive equipment, we’re aiming to add damage soaking stats (currently only applied to the chaotic, eagle-eye and farseer kiteshields) to the majority of head, body, leg and shield slot equipment. Damage soaking will help reduce all incoming damage values over 200 life points, with higher grades of equipment offering a greater percentage of ‘soak’, and with members’ equipment offering the greatest percentage of all.

We’re also looking forward to releasing a brand new set of hit splats to give you better information about any combat you’re in. We’ve had some great conversations in the forums after the dev blog about hit splats a few months ago and have changed our approach thanks to your excellent feedback. Here’s a screenshot of what they will look like:

Once launched, you’ll be able to:
[ul][li]Identify your character’s damage in multiplayer situations, so you’ll know which splats are yours when fighting boss monsters with your clan or group.[/li]
[li]See visually how much damage your armour is soaking.[/li]
[li]Identify the type of damage being dealt to you, allowing you to adjust armour and prayers more accurately.[/li]
[li]Identify reflected damage.[/li]
[li]Use the ‘max hit splat’ to easily spot when you’re dealing huge swathes of damage.[/li][/ul]

lothy August 1, 2016, 1:19am #9

I’d just like to mention that I have a tank ranger, which means maximum DPS through maxed range skill and ice barrage as a secondary means of attack, near maximum damage soaking (90+ defence), and high health capacity – all at 101 combat. XD

The only way the account could improve would be the addition of 92 prayer, which I can’t afford. Unfortunate.

The only problem that remains is the vast price gap between mid range armour (ie, rune armour) and high end armour. Unfortunately they can’t solve this with the current armour sets, which are ‘special’ in that they can’t be created by players.
If they re-evaluate the smithing skill, and rebalance it in such a way that a smithing level equivalent to an attack or defence level allows the creation of weapons/armour appropriate for that level, then they partially alleviate this problem.

The remaining problem is simple: Do we want to add more value to the player-created items by making their stats slightly higher, or do we want to encourage boss hunting by making monster-dropped armour/weapons better?
The problem is that if they’re identical then their demand, and therefore price, is coupled together – the arguably easier to acquire player-created weapons/armour will drop in price, and no reason will exist to pay for the exotic monster-dropped gear in the eyes of your functional player, thus inducing a drop in value that reflects the drop in value of the player-created gear.

tl;dr: Big step has been taken towards fixing a very old problem, but there are still some deeply seated flaws.
