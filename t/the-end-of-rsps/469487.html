Today, I was going to start a project with my good friend KronIxz_. We searched for a source for at least an hour and come to find all of them either said the file didn’t exist, it was off limits or JaGeX has removed the links. It hasn’t been for the servers, but I saw it a lot for the caches. This included a 317 Delta - 317 loading 600+ PI. Now I haven’t checked any legit 508+ downloads out yet, but if JaGeX is getting on 317s, I’m assuming they’re getting on even more to the higher revisions.

So, big deal! My RSPS is already online! That may be true, but if your cache was hosted using host outside your webhost (such as DropBox), they may be getting your cache link, and sending it in to DropBox to get rid of it. This means new players won’t be able to join. And we all know at some point, players get bored and quit. When players quit and no new ones are able to join (reason in enough to shut down) people start loosing donations which means they can’t pay for the VPS unless they do it out of pocket. Also, with the way they’ve been taking away the files, people won’t be able to start “creating” [size=3pt]cough[/size] new servers to host.

Anyway, I believe the end of RSPS is coming near, and JaGeX is helping it along by removing files, kinda like giving all of us a silent warning to stop programming the servers.

What are your thoughts? What do you think will happen? Do you think they’ll take legal actions against people who ignore their warning?
