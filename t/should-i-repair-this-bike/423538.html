Others have basically said this, but I’ll echo it.

You don’t need to remove the rust to ride it, and if the tires and tubes will hold air, you don’t need to replace them either, and if they won’t hold air, you can also just put a $5 tube inside them if the tread is sufficient for your needs. All that chain/gear setup needs is a few sprays with a penetrating oil like WD-40, that you can get a generic version of from the dollar store around here for less than $3. The helmet is probably the most expensive part of the ‘repair’, but don’t be an idiot, wear one. It doesn’t look like a super-expensive bike, so don’t spend a lot on it, just have fun.

For exercise, you’d really want a smooth ride. If it can give you that in this condition, then don’t bother replacing the gears and chain. I started out with a €150,- bike. It was in better condition than yours is, but it still is no match for my new one, which only costed me €700,-. Clean the cassette and chain, and take it for a spin. Maybe just removing the rust will suffice.

The bike doesn’t look like it’s been used for racing though. When racing, you put slicks on your bike, not tires with profile. You put a saddle on it that will make you sterile if you sit on it too long, and pedals that give more grip.
