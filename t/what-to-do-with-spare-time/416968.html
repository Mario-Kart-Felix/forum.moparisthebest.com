abraham2119 August 1, 2016, 3:22pm #18

Depends on what your goal is: for pure strength training, bodyweight exercises are sufficient but for hypertrophy, they are not.

Don’t listen to m.dog311; instead of buying equipment, get a gym membership – not that expensive really (I’ve seen Ballys with $10/month offers).

Anyways, I currently do a three day split (triceps and chest, biceps and back, and shoulder and legs) and workout every other day. As for my caloric intake, on workout days I eat around 600+ my BMR and try to save all of my (complex) carbs (and calories really) for post workout (think of a IF carb cycling hybrid sort of thing) and on rest days a caloric deficit of around 300-600 with little to no carbs. Simple carbs I absolutely avoid except around 15 minutes pre-workout for energy. Stick to 1g of protein per pound.

I’ve been at it “strictly” (little bit of cheating) for around 3 months and have had great gains: I’ve put on 15 pounds of muscle and cut from ~12% to ~9% body fat.
