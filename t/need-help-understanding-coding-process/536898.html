First of all, what you did to the delta before is not coding… second of all try Udemy, it costs money but the tutorials are VERY useful. Start with RuneSource, easy to understand. Answer to #3, anti leeches are last, usually it is the IP that is incorrect, Port is almost never incorrect. OR the client is not compatible with the source. Answer to #4, try to begin by changing variables and strings that are called by methods to see what they do, then try to add your own methods that do special things. To answer a few of your questions:

[ul][li]1. There’s an extensive amount of tutorials, guides, and textbooks available at your leisure. Not only that, but they’re free. The most reliable source available roots directly from Oracle, the javadocs, more specifically the ReallyBigIndex. The terminology might throw you off at first, but googling these terms will prove useful and make it easier to understand.[/li]
[li]3. The help sections are here for when you need help with a specific problem that you’ve encountered and can’t seem to fix on your lonesome. Remember that a majority of those who help in this section will not give you your code on a silver platter, they will expect you to have at least a basic understanding of what it is they’re showing you.[/li]
[li]4. Don’t start learning to program with a private server. Start with reading the Java documentation and begin to write your own programs. Once you feel like you have a decent grasp of the basics of Java, and if you’re still interested, then try your hand at a private server. Also. if you want to make your life a lot easier while programming, be sure to do all of your programming in the Eclipse IDE or other Java IDE’s.[/li][/ul]

If you have any questions regarding help and you don’t want to post in the help sections for any reason, myself and a lot more members are willing to help through private messaging. Good luck.
