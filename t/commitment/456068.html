amy_webb1 August 3, 2016, 11:43pm #2

The thing about choices is there are many of them, you don’t have to decide on one. I’m always having that ‘what do you want to do when you’re older?’ question pushed one me, i say at least 2 or 3 things i was to do. That way you never really commit, and it also beings up a broader range of topics for conversation.

When it comes to small things like food, you don’t need to be worried. The smallest things are always the most difficult. Instead of becoming frustrated, narrow it down. Then you can pick one of the others, maybe even the first on the list, and get the rest later. There is always time for the others, it isn’t a life or death situation.

When picking a future path, don’t get to picky. There are always opportunities that will allow you to change your job if you decide to, you’ll never be nailed down to one place forever.
