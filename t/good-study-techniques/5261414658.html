Of course it depends on the actual subject/how long you have for the project and etc.

What I used to try to do though would be to divide each day to a devoted time for studying that particular class.

Example: I might have a history test in seven days. So if I was to estimate I need to study at least for three hours total to successfully get this standard or grade on the test then I need to divide that three hours into seven days.

This would give you about 25-30 minutes each day to study. Which really isn’t a bad time period. Other tips I have learned over the years are:

-Don’t try to study late at night. Your body is already weakened and thus will not be at 100%. I know you may not like that the sound of this but it’s important to do it at it’s peak.
-Don’t try to focus on one part of that subject too long in that period. If something is just not clicking at that time. Write it down and ponder on it later when you have a chance. Don’t forget about it either though! If you need to confront your teacher/professor about it.
-Never, never, never get the attitude of “I know everything about this”. You may be a java guru for example, but ten years later you know what you may come to find out about a new way of doing something etc.
-Read each line to yourself “out loud” in your head. This way you are really reading it twice.

Yo dude, I’ve finished all the math requirements for any engineering major (Straight A’s on the vector series ) and in the process of transferring to double E.

For math, here’s how I prepare for exams and the class in general.

I usually skim the sections covered prior to lecture and attempt a few problems and jot down some notes on parts that the book does not explain too well. I usually get to the lecture hall like 30 minutes early and reread my notes of the previous lecture just to jog my memory of what we were previously doing. After Lecture hall, I do the assigned problems for each section and then I look back and see if I can do them differently than the way the professor presented (this is very very important as you get to the high level courses, new ways open up of doing problems).

For the exams, I give myself 3 days to prepare.
First day, I go through my notes and rewrite all the major formulas + theorems presented in class on a single sheet of paper (if your professor gives you a study guide, finish it on this day)
Second day, I redo every single homework problem assigned only using that single sheet of paper and the problems professors usually recommend in lecture but are not required
Third day, Spend a couple hours reviewing before bed, sleep, wake up and review for the two hours before lecture!

Hope this helps!
