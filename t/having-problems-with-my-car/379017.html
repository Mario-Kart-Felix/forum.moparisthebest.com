Unfortunatly, I had to rush back up to my uni and couldn’t take it back to the garage (Although, they didn’t charge me a lot for doing this).   • Low revs when idling
  • Exhaust sounds different/not normal (hard to explain the sound, but like a chug)
  • Loss of power at low revs
  • Noticeable rise in vibration when idling

moparisthebest August 1, 2016, 7:02am #2

all of those symptoms seem to point to the engine not firing on all cylinders. it would cause low power, vibrations, and low idle speed. you need to track down which cylinder isnt firing, it could be the spark plug, plug wire, distributor/rotor button/coil, or the coil pack, or possibly even your computer. but start with the simplest thing first, check for broken wires or sparks jumping through cracks (look under the hood with it running in the dark, makes seeing sparks easier), if you dont see anything, take off one wire at a time ans, while the engine is running hold it near a ground point to see if the sparks are at regular intervals like they should be.

alternativly, if you have a place that will scan your car or loan you a scanner for free, and your car is new enough, chances are the scanner will tell you which cylinder is misfiring. i wouldnt pay for a scan though, because its easy to troubleshoot the old fashioned way.
