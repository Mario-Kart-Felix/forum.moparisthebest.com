Well, I wanted to create this thread because I want to see if others like poetry as much as I do, it takes out a lot of emotions from me and I enjoy doing it, here what I have so far as far as having poems and what not:

I Love You

[QUOTE]The sound of your voice is music to my ears, so soft, sweet, and clear.
The kiss from your lips words can not explain
It takes away my worries and pain
The caress of your hand sends shiver down my spine
Everyday I thank God that your mind

The sight of your lovely face takes away all my worries
You’re the one I want to love through out the years
For eternity I want to send in your arms
Everyday graced by your beauty and charms[/QUOTE]

Dying Inside

[QUOTE]Something so hard
Goes straight to the soul;
It seems impossible to get over
My heart is left with a big hole

I’m trying to be happy, wearing a smile, but I’m in fact dying inside
The world seems to be fading, I want to run and hide

Everywhere I go I see the pain
I see the ending road
I see… the answer
The truth to be told
I see the opening gate
I see the death of dreams[/QUOTE]

Down Again

[QUOTE]I’ve been pushed down so many times
I feel this time will be the end, as I lay here dying
My thoughts are to an end

I feel the pressure of shame, sadness, and rejection building as I fall
Not the strength to get up
I feel the need to fall
I crave the need to burn, to ash if I could
I’m not even worth it anymore, I do say if I could[/QUOTE]

This poem does NOT have a title

[QUOTE]We all want a bit of peace
Something we cannot keep
Wherever the world takes us
There is but the slightest of thee
We cannot comprehend such pain
Though we cannot stop at such paths
Our will continues to drive us
Though our physical body will always stop us
We will always strive for that feeling
Of Peace and Harmony[/QUOTE]

Dark

[QUOTE]I float in a pool of darkness
Cold presses upon me
I am but alone in such a dark world
Floating aimlessly
I reach for spark of hope
But rather than warm, I sense burn and ashes

I ache with sorrow and hunger to leave
I strive for escape
To leave this prison as free as I can be
To crave to be free as free as I can be
To be heard as free as I can scream
To be loved as free as I can deem[/QUOTE]

Broken

[QUOTE]In my soul lyes a broken crack
A piece that can not come back
What can I do, nothing but live my hell
The grief
The crying

To what may consume me
I lay there dying of it
In a wheatherless place
Nothing but Hell

As I wake
I lay my eyes upon the Devil
With him doing…
My soul to take

I bum with nothing but a broken heart[/QUOTE]

[CENTER][B]Oh my darling is buy away Her pain is gone but I worry I plan on seeing her again on day Nothing Keeps me here although I stay

I smile as I see the sunrise come up
Oh I bless the heavens for seeing her again
But oh how I sadly frown as she goes down
And I get on my knees and say amen

Oh the sunrise may leave us about one day
And as I lay crying and to dye madly
I wait and see how to get to her way
God suddenly warms me and says goodbye[/B][/CENTER]

  I looked before me to see nothing more then an endless canvas of white. The cold pierced my body like a thousand needles, jabbing into my skin at once. Searching for hours for my prey, I continued into the vast wilderness of ice that surrounded me. The only existing color was that of my eyes, a deep green reflecting that of an opposite season. Searching for hours, I had yet to come about my target, the creature that would bring me satisfaction for days to come. The frigged air around me could not stop the quest I set myself on.
  Suddenly, in the corner of my eye, I spotted the reason I was out here. It's brown fur was completely blanketed with white; the horns stood strong above the beast. It was the North American Whitetail, the most majestic of all North American deer. Beyond the amount I had reached, I froze for a moment, then realized this would be my only shot. Slowly but surely, I reached for the rifle on my back, making sure to stay was quiet as the wintery land that engulfed me. The metal of the trigger stung my fingers, right through the glove. I leveled my eye with the barely visible scope, but just enough to place a bullet through my destiny. I took a deep breath in, the air slicing my lungs, and pulled the trigger.
