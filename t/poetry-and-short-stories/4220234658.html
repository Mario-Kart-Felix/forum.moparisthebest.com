[CENTER]A Razor, A Knife, or the Penalty?[/CENTER]

[QUOTE][B][CENTER]What is there left?
A razor, a knife, or the penalty?
A wound in which I foresee
For it is the blood that drives me

You act as if he is but seen
A particle in which we cannot see
How about tell me where to look
A particle in which I cannot see

Try and explain your wounds to him, do it and see
You will only be left with in-san-ity
Oh how I tried to warn you
For it is the blood that drives me
I told you not to leave, I can show you light

You remain here and tell me he is there
Now stop telling me how it is to be
But tell me how to save my san-ity

Now tell me of thee
A razor, A knife, or the penalty?[/CENTER][/B][/QUOTE]

Svitkona August 2, 2016, 6:49am #35

I opened my eyes to the green, overhanging foliage around me. Something had moved; I was sure of it. Right before I had opened my eyes, I could have sworn I had seen a shadow flickering like the light of a candle at the edge of my vision. I wearily got out of my sleeping bag and looked around me. There was nothing there and yet… I couldn’t shake off this strange feeling of being watched, this feeling of an invisible presence observing my movements. Suddenly, I froze. Right there in front of me in the darkness, I could see the outline of a four-legged creature moving. Its movements were strange, as if it was trying to avoid detection. Abruptly, it stopped moving and slowly turned on the spot until it faced me then lay there, as if waiting for something. I started sweating. Somehow, I knew that if I saw that creature’s face bad things would happen, but my curiosity could not be stemmed. Hesitantly, I brought my flashlight up and turned it on, my shaking arms causing the light to dance in my field of vision. I froze in terror as I saw the creature clearly for the first time. then frantically started running away. I felt the creature leap on my back and let out a final cry as I fell and dropped my flashlight.
