How the universe is how it is today!

Well, it all started out with the big bang, right? Thats partly true, it didn’t first ‘start’ with the big bang, the big bang was actually going on for plenty of eternities. What? How is that possible? Well to understand this, you would have to know something about the 11 dimensions.

=================

Dimensions

0: There is nothing, there is a point of indefinate size, its just an idea.

1: If theres a line, with no width, and no depth, only height, this is the 1st dimension.

2: The 2nd dimension is, well, think about when people say 2D, its the same thing, height, width, but no depth.

3: This is easiest for us because this is what we live in, a 3 dimensional object has width, height, and depth.

4: What we can describe the 4th dimension as is duration. If you imagined yourself from the time you were 1 minute ago, to the way you are now, the line you could draw between those points in time would be a line in the fourth dimension.

5: If you imagined bending the line of time in the fourth dimension, to meet another point in time, you would do this in the 5th dimension. In theory, time travel is possible then, and I believe we’re not far from time travel in the future.

6: The 6th dimension holds all possibilities for your life. If you did something in your childhood that made you famous and rich by now, you would want to see what would happen if you hadn’t, you would do this in the 6th dimension.

7: You can bend the 7th dimension to meet the 6th dimension imitating time travel in a different sense.

8: Forgot…

9: Ah, now I remember, this holds all future possibilities for an infinity. Subotomic particles vibrating in the tenth dimension make up our vision, what we see. We cannot view the dimensions above us, so we don’t know whats happening behind the scenes. If you have looked at all possibilities for an infinity, every single possibility to ever happen in the universe, and put them into a dot, this dot would be in the ninth dimension.

10: Now, in the ninth dimension, we spoke about having all possibilities of an infinity into one dot, right? Now, it is possible to have multiple infinities, by changing what happened before another infinity started. Now, if you put every possibility, from every infinity, from all of the universe, into one dot, you would put this dot in the 10th dimension.

11: Recent breakthroughs in how atoms are made and what happens in our universe adds up to a final dimension, an extra one. When you imagine our universe, you think “Well thats it, we’re the only universe, the universe is everything!”, quite contrary. The 11th dimension holds different universes, they are called membranes. It is like a thin wavy disk that floats around in the 11th dimension. Our universe is one of those disks, but there are other disks floating around, holding other universes, where the laws of physics that apply here, may not apply there, it could be entirely different.

Now, back to the main topic. How does the big bang happen? Well, scientists used to say super nova, but thats not true. If you imagine those membranes flying around in the 11th dimension, what do you think if one of them hit eachother would happen? Say hello to the big bang. When one of those membranes collide, it causes the big bang, and this process has probably been going on since the beginning of time, which is, well, forever. We came from tiny bacteria thriving to make us. We’re insignificant beings on a planet, we think somebody cares about us but they don’t, we just live, and die, and then we get blown up, whats the point?

Imagine yourself sleeping, you don’t know your sleeping, you can’t feel yourself rolling over, your just, well, dead.

Well, if their balls of a definate size, they have to be made of something. They are made of ‘strings’. Strings are indefinate sized squiggly always moving lines, all attatched together to make an atom. They make up everything, from the air we breathe, to the keyboard we type on.

They are all just stuck together in half circles. The air we breathe is made up of full circle strings. They control everything, gravity for example. A graviton is a string which is in the air all around us, pushing us down. Scientists are trying to harvest a graviton, for with a graviton, for example, if you put one modified graviton inside an upside down bucket, the bucket would shoot towards the sky, as if falling up.

That was 69 million years ago. Gravity

According to my previous fact that gravitons are what gravity is, those are in this dimension, but they are around us, an invisible force that might be able to control gravity, but why is there gravity, and easy way to think of it is, you know how people say “Ripping the fabric of the universe” (tell that to the black hole, which I’ll get to in a minute), well its not all sci-fi. When a huge ball of rock is floating in space, its not moving up, or down, only maybe left and right with extreme force, think of it like this, their is a blanket, stretched tight, and you put a rock in the middle (our sun), it would bend the blanket down, creating gravity, and if you put a whole bunch of other rocks around it, (this might not be the most accurate recreation because the rocks would all collide into the ‘sun’, space works differently but anyways), the rocks would be the planets and they would create their own ‘gravity’ around a bigger gravitational pull, the ‘sun’, in real life, these ‘planets’ might just roll into the ‘sun’, but in space, this is different because gravitons are holding it in place. Why do the planets rotate around the sun? Well, think of when you throw a basketball in a hoop, and it circles around it a few times, then goes into it, think of the hoop as a gravitational mass, and the basketball as a asteroid, it gets spun around it a few times then flies into the mass. Space is the same, only the planets are big enough, and far enough away, that ‘the basketball is too big to go into the hoop’, and its only place to go is out, where the pull is a little less daunting, and they will move away faster and faster and maybe eventually collide into something that might be thousands of lightyears away.

I say this because scientists know that the planets are moving away from their gravitational ‘daddy’ slowly, inches a year, but its happening. We’re like the sun to the moon and the moon is a planet, the moon can’t collide into us, but it is moving slowly away.
