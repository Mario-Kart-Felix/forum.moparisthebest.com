moparisthebest August 3, 2016, 2:19am #32

The coolest vacation I’ve ever been on, hands down, is a road trip to Yellowstone National Park my wife and I went on when we were dating. It seriously looks (and smells!) like you are on a different planet when you get there, and each and every natural phenomenon you see is beautiful and completely different from all of the others. Some of the water and springs there reach 400 degrees F, but doesn’t boil due to weird pressure factors and stuff.

And that’s just the geological stuff. There are also vast amounts of really neat native wildlife, pronghorn antelope, buffalo, elk, and others, all of which are a common sight. Also a few you DON’T want to see, like Grizzly Bear, and mountain lion.

Also, even though the temperature gets very warm to hot there in the summer, the nights are cold, and yellowstone lake, being so high up, generally stays around 40-50 F even in the summer, so even in the heat of august, if you fall out of a boat on the lake, you have something like 3-5 minutes before hypothermia sets in.

Anyway, the road trip there from Ohio was amazing too, seeing all the sites along the way, we also dropped by Mt. Rushmore on the way back, and that was pretty neat too.

So, go on a road trip to Yellowstone.
