zyle1992 August 3, 2016, 2:42am #7

Yes.

Although technically it isn’t stealing, because stealing implies the removal of the original copy.

For example, if someone stole your pink fuzzy slippers, then you wouldn’t have them anymore. But if someone copied them, then you’d still have a pair, and they would as well.

zyle1992 August 3, 2016, 2:42am #9
Rodgerwilco:

…it’s still stealing.

Stealing implies the deprivation of whatever is being stolen. For example, when somebody steals your pink fuzzy slippers, they are depriving you of them.

I’m not saying it’s right, but it’s really just the unauthorized possession of property.

Also, doing an in-game command should not give anyone access to the machine. Unless he knew the machine password, or you were sharing the source (dropbox, etc) then he can’t have the source.
