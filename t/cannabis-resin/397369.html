The only resin I know of is that which is commonly seen on trichomes, also colloquially known as “hairs.” The resin itself is where the majority of the THC is stored, which is the main psychoactive cannibinoid what causes the perceived high. Those who cultivate their own marijuana attempt to pick a “harvest” time such that trichome development has peaked and therefore THC production has maximized, attempting to create an optimal recipe for recreational usage. The resin being on your hands probably would have had no effect, as THC is not efficiently absorbed through the skin, but diffused in through the lungs.

the_bank August 1, 2016, 12:13pm #12

I’ve never heard of the term “hairs” being used for trichomes, however I have heard the term used to describe the pollen collecting part of a female marijuana plant.

As for resin, although it is technically the same resin which is referenced in “resin-production” through a plants growth cycle (ie. trichomes) it is most often referred to (atleast around here) as the black “resin” found in for example a pipe, or along the tip of a joint or in a heavily used bong. This black, tar like substance will infact get you high, but is also famed for its common residual effects of short lasting high and headache.
