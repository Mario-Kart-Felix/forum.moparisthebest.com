For a perfect mirror, the angle between the incident ray and the surface normal is the same as the angle between the reflected ray and the same surface normal. The surface normal is just the normal of the surface on the point where the ray is reflected. But, in reality, no perfect mirror exists. So, a mirror is essentially light rays propagating through some media. Which concludes that, even if we had no eyes, “mirrors” would still exist. Our eyes are just a means to observe things, such that it’s easier for us to survive.
