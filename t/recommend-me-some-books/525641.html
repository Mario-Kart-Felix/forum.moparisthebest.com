  1. The Ciricle Universe
    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamora_Pierce#Circle_universe
    When I was younger I LOVED her Circle of Magic books. I would suggest reading them in chronological order, that being:
    1.The Circle of Magic
    2.The Circle Opens
    3.The Will of the Empress

I didn’t read anything after the empress, but i sincerely loved the storyline.

  1. RedWall
    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redwall
    The Redwall series is an excellent, EXCELLENT series to read as well. Depending on how you would like to read them, it is very interesting. Chronological order is not by date released, which makes it a bit confusing.

  2. Perloo
    http://www.amazon.com/Perloo-The-Bold-Avi/dp/0590110039
    This is an all time classic for me. I could not remember the name and it bothered me so much that I had to start digging through my closet to find the name of it. Well worth the read again :).

  3. Eragon
    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eragon
    One of my most favorite series of all time. I believe there are 4 books, all of which are pretty big. They have a lot of imagery and completely immerse you into the world. If you are like me when you read, then when you zone out of the world and focus on the book you literally feel like you’re there. Plenty of exciting moments, mixed with a few dry moments which usually end up well anyways. It yanks your reigns back and forth so you never feel like the situations are the same, or that the storyline is overly predictable. I would highly recommend you at least pick up the first book

PS: For the love of all that is good and even slightly okay in this world, DO NOT WATCH THE MOVIE. DO NOT, I’M SERIOUS!!! The movie made me so mad when I watched it that I spent the 60 minutes driving back home on a full out rant to my family about how much garbage was pumped into that movie.

reyx7 August 4, 2016, 9:49am #4

Are you looking for just general classics? Some of my favorite books are…

1984 - George Orwell -> Favorite dystopian novel with a great love story, a classic indeed
Animal Farm - George Orwell -> another dystopian novel, just generally well written
Watership Down - Richard Adams -> it seems odd, but trust me it’s a great book.
Slaughterhouse Five - Kurt Vonnegut -> Vonnegut has a great, sophisticated sense of humor. Some like it, some don’t.
The Great Gatsby - Larry Fitzgerald -> READ BOOK, THEN MOVIE. If you’ve seen the movie, you’ll get a weird perception of the book.
The Count of Monte Cristo - Alexandre Dumas -> Although written in the 19th century, it was in French, therefor when it was translated it was put into modern English. An amazing story, just an epic read. 200 pages in of the roughly 900 and I was hooked.
The Catcher in the Rye - JD Stalinger -> Another one that goes both ways. Some see it as a kid complaining for 300 pages, others really take the message in and relate to it.
Fahrenheit 451 - Ray Bradbury -> Another dystopian novel. I really like dystopian novels…
Catch-22 - Joseph Heller -> Wasn’t my favorite, but it’s where the phrase comes from. A decent novel non the less
The Giver - Lois Lowry -> She does a lot of dystopian novels as well, this one, however, is the best
The Kite Runner - Khaled Hosseini -> Seriously the saddest fucking book I’ve ever read. It gets worse and worse and worse and you just feel like you’re worthless. Manly fucking tears dude, manly fucking tears

That list should keep you busy. Enjoy!
