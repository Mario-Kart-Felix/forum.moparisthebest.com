[quote=“reyx7, post:4, topic:525641”]Are you looking for just general classics? Some of my favorite books are…

1984 - George Orwell -> Favorite dystopian novel with a great love story, a classic indeed
Animal Farm - George Orwell -> another dystopian novel, just generally well written
Watership Down - Richard Adams -> it seems odd, but trust me it’s a great book.
Slaughterhouse Five - Kurt Vonnegut -> Vonnegut has a great, sophisticated sense of humor. Some like it, some don’t.
The Great Gatsby - Larry Fitzgerald -> READ BOOK, THEN MOVIE. If you’ve seen the movie, you’ll get a weird perception of the book.
The Count of Monte Cristo - Alexandre Dumas -> Although written in the 19th century, it was in French, therefor when it was translated it was put into modern English. An amazing story, just an epic read. 200 pages in of the roughly 900 and I was hooked.
The Catcher in the Rye - JD Stalinger -> Another one that goes both ways. Some see it as a kid complaining for 300 pages, others really take the message in and relate to it.
Fahrenheit 451 - Ray Bradbury -> Another dystopian novel. I really like dystopian novels…
Catch-22 - Joseph Heller -> Wasn’t my favorite, but it’s where the phrase comes from. A decent novel non the less
The Giver - Lois Lowry -> She does a lot of dystopian novels as well, this one, however, is the best
The Kite Runner - Khaled Hosseini -> Seriously the saddest fudgeing book I’ve ever read. It gets worse and worse and worse and you just feel like you’re worthless. Manly fudgeing tears dude, manly fudgeing tears

That list should keep you busy. Enjoy![/quote]
