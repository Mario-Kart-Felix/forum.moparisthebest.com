If the Federal government brings in a law that is beyond the scope of their powers then no, the state governments don’t have to follow it – it’s unconstitutional for the federal law to even exist.

The American constitution, much like Australian’s constitution, delegates law making powers in various areas to the federal government and state government.
For example, in Western Australia radar detectors (which are used to detect police radar) are perfectly legal to own and use. This isn’t the case in any other state. Now our federal government couldn’t come in and legislate with regards to this to introduce uniformity because they aren’t legally allowed to, as per our constitution (which grants the power of law making with regards to roads and transit to the state governments).
If, against all odds, the Australian government did try to legislate in such an area then the state governments wouldn’t have to enforce it, because the federal legislation is unconstitutional. However they may choose to enforce it anyway, but that would be at their discretion.
