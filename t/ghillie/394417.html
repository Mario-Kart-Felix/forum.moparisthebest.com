chassy13 August 1, 2016, 11:44am #6

I have a net ghillie suit that I use in airsoft skirmishes for sniping. It’s decent but a lot of brush gets into it and it’s practically impossible to clean. You can buy everything needed to make one at Walmart and it’s A LOT cheaper if you put your time into it.

Unless you’re going to use it often, don’t spend $100 on it. I kind of regret buying mine because I don’t go to wooded skirmishes often and it’s full of brush and leaves, but I don’t regret it at the same time because it can still be helpful. You can also get burlap instead of netting so you won’t run into as bad as a problem.

Try finding one for a cheaper price or try making your own is the best advice I can give.

chassy13 August 1, 2016, 11:45am #8
reyx7:

And I was thinking that maybe the environment sticking to me could be camo? Or is it just annoying…

For the most part, it’s annoying. It won’t affect the effectiveness of the ghillie suit but when it’s time to pack it back up the plants dry out. It’s extremely hard to get the plants and leaves back out and the smell of plants will linger in the suit, but it’s nothing extreme.

Having extra vegetation in your ghillie suit is not bad at all. In fact, most airsoft snipers purposely add vegetation to improve the natural look. The only problem I have is a large amount of broken leaves falling out sometimes (which shouldn’t matter if you’re outside), and some of the yarn/whatever is used gets lost. Just remove the large twigs and plant stems and then reinforce the ghillie suit with matching string if needed, problem solved.

Mine is one layer of netting. The ones you posted look heavy and uncomfortable, not to mention you’ll probably roast sitting in the sun.

I’m pretty sure this is the one I have (woodland version): http://www.ghilliesuitwarehouse.com/products/Swiss-Link-Ghillie-Set.html

chassy13 August 1, 2016, 11:51am #18
m.dog311:

So I’m sitting here with about $400 in my pocket and I’m trying to make a decision… Originally the money was to buy a PS3, but I am currently leaning more towards a nice airsoft gun… Any suggestions?

Depending if you’re a serious airsofter and are willing to spend that entire budget or not. If you are, head towards a Tokyo Marui. These guns are pretty much packed out of the box requiring little to no upgrades, but they are a hefty price.

If you’d like to go cheaper and are just starting out, go with JG or Echo 1. You can’t go wrong with the JG G36C:

http://www.airsplat.com/Items/ERM-JG-G36C-638.htm

It’s probably one of the most reliable and cheap guns out there. JG has good prices and usually good guns, I have an AK47 from them and have had 0 issues with it. They are easily upgradeable and it’s usually easy to find extra mags and attachments.

Others based from a budget of just $150 (Pulled off a website, always make sure to watch reviews from people who know what they’re talking about before purchasing airsoft equipment):

JG MP5 JG G36 series JG M4 KS P90 Echo 1 basic MP5 CYMA AK line CYMA M14 G&G Combat Machine (eww) JG M16

Take a look around AirsoftGI and Evike before moving onto other retailers. With $400 you can get a lot of cool stuff, but try and save some money if you want extra magazines, optics, or internal/external upgrades as well. Feel free to ask on here or PM me if you need any help or have questions regarding guns/other equipment.
