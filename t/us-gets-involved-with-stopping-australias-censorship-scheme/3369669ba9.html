If you were to read the internet news articles on Australian technology news websites, you’d find that many people share my point of view (or, alternatively, they find the whole filtering idea absurd like I do, but disagree with the idea that the US is getting involved).

The problem with Australia is that we have compulsory voting. This means that we have what is called a ‘safe seat’, among other things. A safe seat is one where the likelihood of an opposing politician being voted in for that electorate is virtually zero, thus many parties won’t run a candidate for that electorate. This is an automatic win for the candidate of party X.
The other problem is that John and Jane Doe down the road have been voting for party X for the last 30 years of their lives, and will undoubtedly continue to do so ‘just because’, regardless of that party’s policies.
These are two reasons why no democratic nation should have compulsory voting.
Do you consider it your ‘right’ to pay taxes, or your obligation? Down here, voting has never been a right simply because we MUST do it (or pay a fine).

Oh, and I strongly believe in the freedoms that Americans have, but a bill of rights is simply not happening in Australia. Note that we are the only 1st world, democratic nation, that doesn’t have one.
The reasoning for this is that it’s ‘dangerous’ in the sense that times change, and old rights may not be appropriate (America’s right to bear arms is notably used as an example when making this point).
Of course, if we had citizen-initiated referendums then that would never be an issue, because if something truly was inappropriate then a future generation would almost unanimously strike down the law.
