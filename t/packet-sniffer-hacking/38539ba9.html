You can packet sniff hardware you’re interfaced to, as frank_ said. For example, I could sniff my network card and see MY rs password (encrypted) being sent out as I logged on, and the forum posts I posted and read. I’d also see my cookie being sent to the forum, which I could use to get MY password.

The reason you can also sniff your local LAN is because most home LANs are using cheap hubs which are just repeaters, everything that goes into the hub from one machine is broadcast to all of the other machines, and then the networking software looks at it and decides if it was aimed at your pc or another one. Using a sniffer’s driver instead of your normal network driver, you can just look at everything.

If the internet did this, it would all be very inefficient, slow and insecure, so it dosen’t, so you are most definately not getting other peoples passwords - I suggest you look at the data in your sniffer a lot more closely.
