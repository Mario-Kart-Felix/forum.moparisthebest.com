zyle1992 August 1, 2016, 12:08pm #5

The practice of developing a bot is trying to make it act as “human” as possible. And being human comes with certain constraints. For example, we have to eat, we have to sleep, we have to go to the bathroom. Now, we can change our behavior to reduce the frequency, and method at which we do these things (ie. keeping your water intake and salt intake similar to reduce the frequency you go to the bathroom. And in extreme cases, inserting a catheter so you don’t have to get up). You can alter your sleep schedule so that you can stay up for a longer period of time, and have people bring food to you, but the chances of doing all of that is slim.

There have been studies that after a few days without REM sleep, the subjects started hallucinating, and any more time after that there brain simply couldn’t handle it anymore and died.

So the most likely outcome is that Jagex checks the last time they were idle, determines the probability that it’s a human or bot, and goes with their best guess.

If you had a bot running for 1 week without any breaks, then it would be very likely that it’s not a human playing.
