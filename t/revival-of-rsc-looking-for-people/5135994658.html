CodeForFame August 4, 2016, 7:11am #33

The problem with being a for-profit in this community, is that people don’t like that, because of what happens every single time…

There are really two scenarios, though there are some variations, but these are the primary ways it happens:

  1. The owner starts off really actives and starts up a “donation” system, which typically claims all the money will go towards the server. After that, the owner becomes less active and people get mad. Meanwhile, the owner is using the donation money for things other than the server (buying video games, nudes, etc.). Then, once people realize the owner is less active, they start saying the server is dying, which causes people to quit and the owner to become less active. Eventually this kills the server.

  2. The owner starts off really actives and starts up a “donation” system. Once the owner gets a certain amount of money, the server is shut down and the owner runs with the money.

At this point, people are tired of being scammed/lied to and are much more hesitant to join a server that involves money. Although, to this day, nobody has even tried to be transparent, so I’m not quite sure how it would work out.

lothy August 4, 2016, 7:11am #34

I’d say that subscription services (where everyone has to pay, not just people who want some benefit) are superior because then people get what they pay for.
If they pay their $5 - $7 for the month and they can login and play during that month then they’ve gotten the agreed value for their money. If such a server goes belly up then, at most, someone would stand to lose any remaining subscription time. And if payments are taken on a month-to-month basis then that loss is capped at $5 - $7, worst case (i.e., the server goes down one day into a newly paid month).

With 200 subscribers each paying $7, you wind up with $1400 per month. That level of income, in my opinion, would cover hosting (which is quite exorbitant in cost) and administrating the server but not a lot in the way of content updates.
Mind you, I’ve never understood the insatiable demand that this community (which is supposed to be a retro gaming community, of sorts) has for fresh content. Fuck off to newer games if you want that.
