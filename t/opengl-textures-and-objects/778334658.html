/unimpressed

Not that impressing me matters in any way.

First, let’s talk about your signature as it’s been mocked quite a bit. For clarity’s sake, anyone can be a ‘developer’ on second life, it’s an open ended game that allows users to ‘develop’ widgets. So I suppose in the loosest sense of the term you can call yourself an ‘official secondlife developer’. The one tidbit that I find somewhat amusing is that you added the tag ‘CodeManagement’. I don’t know a single developer, in all the years I’ve written code, that would willingly put the tag ‘CodeManagement’ in their title. Code Management is for the poor souls that can’t code. They handle builds. They manage CVS and CMS. They are most definitely not developers. They are scripters at best with maybe a modicum of understanding of high-level code. So that tag in your signature doesn’t really surprise me.

And now for the point.

What are you hoping to accomplish? I don’t expect the community should expect much from you because all you have managed to do is figure out the format for the models in the game. Even that is debatable since bentski released his code quite a while ago and anyone really can just peruse his code and surmise the format. Also, editing the model files will be absolutely pointless for you because, if all you, seemingly, have done is figured out how to put the models onto a screen and maybe even edit them, there is no way in hell you will get them into the game. Is it possible you have figured out how to bypass the crc check on cache files? Sure, but do I think you did? Highly unlikely. What’s more, even if you do manage to modify the models in the game, what in the hell makes you think that is not detectable? If you change them and go about clicking on your new models, for example, making a tree a large cuboid, all it takes is one click that registers as a model hit on your machine but when it gets to the server, the server does a very simple check to validate the click location against 1. object location, 2. your location, 3. camera angle, 4, camera pitch, 5. real model data. Granted, the check very likely does not happen real time. But, it does happen, and when it does you are caught.
