mod_taharok August 3, 2016, 7:36am #238

Well I have to agree with tL here more than anyone else. I’ll admit I didn’t thoroughly read over every post, but either way. Our job is to simply make sure this forum succeeds mechanically and structurally. Whether that requires updating the rules based on what the community wants or creating new boards to suit new interests. Our jobs are to encourage people to follow the rules and to ease into the community. Beyond helping users get used to this community, our job has little left. We ban people that disrupt others and warn people who seem to be on that track. Our jobs are supposed to go no further than curbing people’s behavior in a way that reflects the better interests of the community, not the staffs.

The reason this board exists is to try and encourage this philosophy more. The staff do not know what’s best for the site, we simply have the decision powers. I would say staff members have no other power at this community other than making decisions that affect everyone else (because in reality, we’re just on an internet forum, we have no real “power” over people). Yet, making decisions has an impact on thousands of members. Having proper judgement and a clear head is extremely important when making those decisions. If for no other reason, that’s probably the greatest criteria I look for when choosing moderators.

There are times when the staff’s opinions do not reflect the community’s. In extreme cases, we must say no to a certain suggestion simply because not all the users at this community can see the entire scope of what’s going on (another important aspect of staff here). The most difficult part of being staff is figuring out where to draw the line. In fact, the way some moderators deal with users is much different than others. This often causes great distress in the community and can lead to even bigger issues. Nevertheless, it’s times like this when we have to sit down and again reflect the reason why we are here and why we are giving our time to this community.

I respect every single opinion of every member on this forum. My volunteered time at this site is used to make decisions and make sure other decisions don’t cause issues. I have no magic list of things I want the community to believe or think and I do not spend my time shoving it down their throats. This may have been a popular practice a few years ago, but that’s not how we do things anymore. There are still times when a moderator’s decision is questionable, but we deal with it, learn from it, and eventually move on. After all, the staff is just a panel of normal members who have the unfortunate expectations of the community to make decisions that reflect their better interests. At least, that’s the idea.

Edit: After reflecting a bit, I missed one of the most key components of being a staff member. Our job is to make decisions, but it’s also to help users. We should essentially strive for a better community by first trying to help people on our own. We can’t help every user, but we can make a valiant effort to try. Tasks as simple as replying to every topic posted in this section or replying to every private message received is a fantastic start. Every little effort by both the staff and the general community helps improve the community and keep it alive for as long as possible.
