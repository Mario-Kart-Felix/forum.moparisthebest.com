It’s all relative really and people have different measures of success; there’s also considered to be different types of success (academic success, personal success), I personally take a combination of a utilitarianism viewpoint (that happiness and bringing happiness to the most people is important) and an epicurean viewpoint (that the fundamental aim is pleasure, and not the modern notion of pleasure).

To most people success is measured by how much money you have or your position of authority; I generally find these views to be really shallow if i’m honest, money is just a man made invention used by the state to keep items flowing and shouldn’t be the centre of your entire life, and authority i’ve seen plenty of clueless people in the position of authority and I wouldn’t call them successful.

To me success is living a life of achievement, and achievement is to live your life how you want to and benefit others through various means such as research into a academic field.
