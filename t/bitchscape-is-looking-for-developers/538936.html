[anchor=entitle]What does each role entitle?[/anchor]
Of course you’d ask this question. No one wants to join a development team unless they get something out of it. Below is an explanation of what each role is entitled to, both in game and on the forums.

[ul][li]Server Developer[/li][/ul]

   A server developer is entitled to all privileges both on the server and on the forums. This will include access to all commands, teleport locations, and hidden sections of the forums.

[ul][li]Client Developer[/li][/ul]

   A client developer is entitled to all privileges that a server developer is. Both of them are considered equal ranks, and will have equal power.

[ul][li]Forum Staff[/li][/ul]

   Forum staff are the backbone of our less-than-active community. You are entitled access to [u]all[/u]* forums and will have an in-game icon representing your rank. However, forum staff will only have one 'power' command in game, and that is the power to kick.

[ul][li]Server Helpers[/li][/ul]

   As a Server helper you will receive an in-game rank as well as access to a few of the staff forums. You will not have power-based commands in game. However, you will have access to valuable information that will help you in your role as a server helper.

*Some boards will be off limits to forum staff, such as server/client developer boards.
[iurl=#top][abbr=Back to Table of Contents]Back to ToC[/iurl]

[anchor=do]What do I have to do?[/anchor]
Now that you know what most (as I haven’t fully decided on what the staff will receive/have access to) of the benefits are for becoming staff** you’re going to want to know what you are required to do, right? Below is an explanation of what each staff member must do.

[ul][li]Server Developer[/li][/ul]

   You will be required to do constant updates to the server. This includes bug fixes, adding/removing content, as well as general maintenance. All work must be logged in some form of a development log. You are [b]not[/b] required to be online daily, nor are you required to do daily updates. I request at minimal a [u]weekly[/u] update from you. I understand that some work requires the client developers to finish their end first, but generally there should be something for you to work on.

[ul][li]Client Developer[/li][/ul]

   Just like a server developer, you're required to keep a development log. Most of the client work will be working with packets, creating new interfaces, and adding/removing new content.

[ul][li]Forum Staff[/li][/ul]

   This job isn't as interesting as server/client developer, nor will it require as much work. You will be in charge of removing forum spam, giving users forum-based ranks (at least, until I find a way to add an automated system), and (depending on how much I trust you) adding/removing new boards. While I expect you to be on the forums a lot (since it really isn't hard to log onto the forums, look at the new content, and log off) you aren't required to be in-game often.

[ul][li]Server Helpers[/li][/ul]

   You play a vital role in helping the community in-game. You will be in charge of helping players find their way around the server, and answer general-knowledge questions about the server (such as: where are ____ monsters?). I trust you to help build a good community. Server helpers are required to log in to the game at least [b]three[/b] times a week. The reason for this is because they are expect to [u]help[/u] the players.

**In-game staff such as admins and moderators will be picked based on their performance in the community. There will be no application what so ever. Applications for said roles make people drive for only that role and usually after that they stop actually caring about the community and overall about the server. While it’s voluntarily, members are appoint by the staff.
