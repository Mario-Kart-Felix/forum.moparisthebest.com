-> The Beginning Steps

    [*]The first thing you should do, is go through your list of installed programs. Click on the Start Menu > Settings > Control Panel > Add or Remove Programs. Now, look through the list and get rid of everything that you don't want anymore (provided you get your parents permission unless you have your own computer). Do NOT remove programs you know absolutely nothing about, because they could be Microsoft Updates or something just as critical.
    [*] The next thing you should do is go to where all the crud left behind will probably be from the installed programs. Go to My Computer > Local Disk (C: ) > Program Files. Now look through the folders names and see if any of those names were the same names you removed from the Add or Remove Programs and if nothing but that particular program you uninstalled was in it... then delete it.

-> Disk Cleanup & Disk Defragmenter
One last thing you should do without the aid of programs is to do these things (again, provided you get your parents permission unless you have your own computer).

    [*] Start menu > Accessories > System Tools > Disk Cleanup (this will take a while, Do NOT run anything else, go to a website, or anything. Just leave it alone.
    [*] After that is completed, you may defrag your computer now. Start menu > Accessories > System Tools > Disk Defragmenter. Again, this will take a while, Do NOT run anything else, go to a website, or anything. Just leave it alone.

-> Turn off Hibernation to Save GBs of data!
Hibernation is a tool that most people who have Microsoft don't even use it. Hibernation saves a lot of energy on your computer when it's idle, but it stores a lot of data on your hard drive. The file is called Hiberfil.sys and it's in your C:\ Drive right in the beginning. Right click on it and select properties and you will see how much space it takes up. Took about 1 GB of space before I disabled it.

Follow these steps to disable it safely.

    [*] Right-click on the desktop and click on "Properties".
    [*] Click on the "Screen Saver" tab.
    [*] Click the "Power" button under "Monitor Power".
    [*] Click on the "Hibernation" tab, then uncheck the box next to "Enable Hibernation".
    [*] Press "OK" and you have just saved a load of hard drive space!

-> Saved Passwords and Forms
If you guys save passwords or form information for searches and stuff... DO NOT! You must turn them off immediately or any hacker on your computer could easily get access to them.
Turn it off for IE: Tools > Internet Options > Content > AutoComplete > Uncheck everything and click on Clear Forms and Clear Passwords.
To turn it off for Firefox: Tools > Options > Privacy > Saved Form Information > Uncheck Save Information I enter in web page forms and the Search Bar. Also in the Privacy Panel (where Saved Form Information is), click on Saved Passwords and uncheck Remember Passwords.
