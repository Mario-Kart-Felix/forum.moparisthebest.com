Religion is always tempting. There are many questions, such as why are we here, how did we get here, and what happens when we die. People have been thinking about this as long as they exist. They tried to answer these questions, but obviously, they couldn’t. So the best explanation would be a higher power. Something that created everything, something that looks after us. And, if you go back a few thousand years, explains natural events such as thunder, lightning or even the sun and the moon.

If I’m correct, karma is part of Buddhism. So is reincarnation (it doesn’t really matter what religion it is). This is another way to explain what happens after we die. It’s tempting too: especially when you think about it longer then a few seconds. I thought it over a while ago, and stumbled on something: How to explain the increasing number of humans? Where do all the souls come from? But the answer to that is simple: animals die. Which would mean that the number of humans and animals are connected, and never change.

But you need karma, and that’s where it derails. There is no such thing as karma. Some people don’t have luck in their lives, does this mean they did something wrong? African children, whose parents died, who suffer from aids: Did they do something wrong? I doubt it.
