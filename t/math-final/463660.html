Alright, keep in mind I might not be using correct terms, but this is how I understand it.

[ol][li]9x^4 - 24x^3 + 16x^2 = 0 — Original equation[/li]
[li]You can tell how many answers you need by how[/li]
[li]x^2(9x^2 - 24x + 16) = 0 — Take out the x^2 as that’s the most you can currently simplify it.[/li]
[li]Pretend that the x^2 that you just took out isn’t there for a second. We’ll come back to that later.[/li]
[li]Take the (9x^2 - 24x + 16) = 0 and factor it out like normal. How I would figure out what the 2 expressions in the middle is by taking the first number in this case 9 and multiplying it by the 16. This would give you 144. Now, you look at the middle number which is 24 and see what two numbers multiply to equal 144, yet add to equal -24. With that in mind, you only have 2 sets of numbers that would work: 12 & 12 or -12 & -12. Since you have to add the set of numbers so that it would equal the -24, it is obviously the second set of numbers -12 & -12 as the sum of the two equals -24.[/li]
[li]Now your equation will look like this: (9x^2 -12x -12x + 16) = 0.[/li]
[li]Now basically look at this equation as two seperate expressions that split here: (9x^2 - 12x | -12x + 16) = 0[/li]
[li]Part A is: 9x^2 - 12x and Part B is: -12x + 16.[/li]
[li]Now let’s start with Part A, take out the greatest common factor between the 9 and the -12. The greatest common factor would be 3x. Now divide 3x from the 9 and the -12 so Part A will look like 3x(3x - 4)[/li]
[li]Do the same for Part B, take out the greatest common factor from it which would be -4. -4(3x -4)[/li]
[li]Now, take a look at the expressions you have in the parentheses; the (3x-4)s. Since the parentheses of Part A and Part B match, that’s one of your answers*.[/li]
[li]Then take a look at the numbers to the left of the parentheses in Part A and Part B. For Part A it would be 3x and for Part B it would be -4.
Combine those together so it would be 3x-4. That would be your second answer*.[/li]
[li]The reason I put an asterick next to the word answer is because you’re not done yet.[/li]
[li]Remember you put aside the x^2 earlier, now put them all together. x^2(3x-4)(3x-4)=0, that is as simplified as you can get generally unless told otherwise by your teacher. That is your final answer.[/li][/ol]

Also, if you need to solve for X rather than just simplifying, this part is easy as well. Just set your previous two answers* to equal zero. Since both of your answers* were the same, this part is very simple.
3x - 4 = 0
3x = 4
x = 4/3

Keep in mind, I mapped almost if not ALL steps. It is usually a lot faster, but as you wanted a list of steps, I tried to explain all of them to help you understand. I hope it doesn’t confuse you.
