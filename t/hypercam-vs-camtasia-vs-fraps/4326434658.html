mod_taharok August 2, 2016, 3:58pm #28

I feel like there is a lot to learn in this topic.

First of all, Hypercam’s “shit quality” has nothing to do with it being hypercam. Its default settings use low quality lossy compression algorithms. You can change it to use maximum quality in raw format, and it’ll have just as good quality as any other recorder.

The reason the files get so large is because they are uncompressed and retain 100% of their original colors. For all purposes, if your computer can handle it, this is the superior choice (regardless of what program you use–all the programs do is take a screenshot, who cares what it’s called?) You can then decide what sort of compression algorithm you wish to use and re-encode it, using something such as media coder (which is my personal preference, since it is excellent).

It’s worth noting that there are lossy compression algorithms that are very good at retaining most of the quality (such as xvid). These are generally unavailable to simply recorders, or they are used poorly. Furthermore, by recording in raw format, you are able to distribute the video in multiple qualities (which is especially useful nowadays).

So my answer to the topic…it shouldn’t matter yo.
