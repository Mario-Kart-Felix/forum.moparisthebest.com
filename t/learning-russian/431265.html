Frell:

Why does there have to be a motive? Why cant I learn a language just to have credit for knowing that language? Tons of people here know English and Spanish because they’re the only two languages that are seen. To be able to say you speak Norwegian or Russian really stands out on your resume (although it isnt useful) youll stand out from the crowd. Why did he need to learn ______? No reason. Did he go out of his way to do it? He did.

I’m not saying that a motive is an absolute necessity. But a motive or goal is something that will keep you motivated. As I said earlier, learning a language isn’t done overnight. You’re having fun now, but how will you feel in half a year, or a year? Will you still be as motivated?

I’m not trying to persuade you not to learn a new language. I admire your willingness to gather knowledge and gain a new skill. But you have to realize that at some point, the progression slows down and you’ll get bored and frustrated. At that point, you need something to flip the switch and continue studying. Perhaps a better resume does the trick for you.
