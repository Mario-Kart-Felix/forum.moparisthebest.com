Lin August 4, 2016, 2:55pm #4

Usually warranties for electronics only apply if you don’t tamper with what’s inside of them. So if your computer randomly stops working, they have proof that you didn’t just open up the inside and mess everything up yourself. That’s why the sticker was peeling off as you were opening the case, which means you voided whatever warranty you had. Since it sounds like you had your computer pre-built, that specific warranty was exclusive to the company that put it together. If something inside goes wrong, that company will take care of it - provided you didn’t mess up or try to fix anything yourself. But since you ignored their policy and opened up the case, they won’t cover anything for you now. However, that doesn’t mean your individual hardware warranties were voided.

It’s worth noting that damaging your processor by over-clocking isn’t covered in the warranty anyways, and you’re usually given warnings before doing so. The warranty that automatically comes with your hardware is usually if it stops working, not if you messed something up on your own. The only kind of warranties that cover that are ones you purchase ahead of time, like with phone plans you’re given the option to buy “accidental” protection, which would include fixing your phone if you dropped it or spilled something on it. By default, the limited warranty does not cover this.

In conclusion - anything that goes wrong with your computer now, you’ll have to fix yourself. You should still have individual warranties for your processor, hard drive, etc but you’re on your own.
