This is a living thread meaning it is constantly evolving, check back every now and then for Updates.

Well, 3 days ago (Update: it’s been more than 3 days now) I started research on the Runescape client and decided to see what the Bot development process was like. After gleaning information from IRC #mopar with the help of people such as frank_, Thug-life, Rictoo, yakman, Miss Silabsoft, and newbiehacker I was able to come up with a simple bot that loads the RS client using the default applet and has the capability to print out the number of Players on the current field. Despite the fact that I knew BCEL/reflection and Java fairly well, I still found it difficult to make progress from scratch. I refrained from referencing other open source bots as that would defeat the purpose of this learning experiencing. The people who developed the first Runescape bot with nothing to reference but your own minds (whoever you are, and you know who you are.) have my utmost respect.

At any rate, I do plan on making a very detailed tutorial about the Bot development process once I learn the ins and outs of the Runescape Client. It won’t be a tutorial on BCEL, or reflection, or Java. You’ll need to read it with those skills already available. What it will be is a document to launch new developers into the Runescape Bot development process because as of now there doesn’t seem to be any real enthusiasm for the subject in the first place because of in-game updates as well as just plain not caring about the game anymore.

In the meantime, the source (and a compiled version) for my simple program is attached to this document. It was built not to be anything special (at least not yet), but as a way to see what the development process is like without copying directly from other bots.

Final Note: For those of you that attempt to jump into the Runescape cheating scene so late (like myself) I feel extremely sorry for you if you do so without first reading the document I am preparing (if I get it finished haha.) because getting the proper people to discuss the development process (when they have gone through the task themselves several times over) when they benefit virtually zero from anything you accomplish except to have their name mentioned briefly somewhere in some type of docs/Credits.txt is a complete pain in the ass. I’d also like to say that of the 3 days I spent trying to research i.e; bothering frank_ and the others, only 1 of them was spent coding. Once again, I have the UTMOST respect for those that were at the root of bot development.

If you have any questions feel free to catch me on IRC under the nickname dafan

If you are an ancient bot developer or a slick new upstart, I would love to here your stories as well as development process. PLEASE Contact me and .dump() all the information you can on me so that I can incorporate it into my document or at least put it to practical use, I can virtually guarantee that I won’t let your information fall on deaf ears as I am serious about this. Everything I do will be Open Source as I have no intention of actually playing Runescape, I just believe this to be a fascinating subject which I am fully prepared to invest much of my spare time into despite the current slump that the game seems to be in.

~unlimitedorb
