Designing a Dialogue Tree

General Programming
zyle1992 August 1, 2016, 12:04pm #1

Okay, so I started working on a simpler way to implement dialogues between Players and Npcs. I’ve got most of the nitty-gritty done, I’m just trying to determine which of the following ways of “tracking” the dialogue would be best. I’ve narrowed it down to either single-step notation, or dot notation.

Here’s the example of the single step:

[ol][li](next: 2)Npc - “Help I’ve fallen and I can’t get up!”[/li]
[li](next: 3)Player - “What happened?”[/li]
[li](next: 4)Npc - “What do you mean? I just told you! I’ve fallen and I can’t get up! Go into the chest over there and grab my replacement hip!”
There would be an option screen here(4):[/li]
[li](next: 6)Option: Okay, Hold on![/li]
[li](next: 8)Option: Sorry, but you really should have invested in Life Alert.[/li]
[li](next: 7)Npc - Thank you so much! Here take this[/li]
[li]You go some hard candy… Great…[/li]
[li](next: -1 (or end of dialogue)) Bastard![/li][/ol]

As you can see, it’s messy. You can kind of see a logical order, but it seems like there would be a better way.

This is an example of the dot notation:

[ol][li](next: 2)Npc - “Help I’ve fallen and I can’t get up!”[/li]
[li](next: 3)Player - “What happened?”[/li]
[li](next: 4)Npc - “What do you mean? I just told you! I’ve fallen and I can’t get up! Go into the chest over there and grab my replacement hip!”[/li]
[li]There would be an option screen here:
[list type=decimal]
[li](next: 4.1.1) Option: Okay, Hold on!
[list type=decimal]
[li](next: -1 (or end of dialogue)) You go some hard candy… Great…[/li]
[/list]
[/li]
[li](next: 4.2.1) Option: Sorry, but you really should have invested in Life Alert.
[list type=decimal]
[li](next: -1 (or end of dialogue)) Npc - Bastard![/li]
[/list][/li]
[/list][/li][/ol]

This one is much more logical, but it may get to be confusing with longer dialogues (7.4.1.1.8.6.2)

So which do you think would be the better choice?
