General Discussion
sp11k3t3ht3rd August 1, 2016, 7:59am #1

My algebra 2 and trig teacher had us do an MCAS prep day. We got separated into groups and had to figure out if the game was fair or not. The game is: There are two players and a spinner. Player 1 adds the numbers together, and player 2 multiplies them. The players spin the spinner twice to get the two numbers. The player with the higher number wins.

The numbers on the spinner are 1, -2, 3, -4, 5, and -6.

Every group decided the game was unfair because the person adding always had a higher chance of winning. Among the groups 3 of 8 said there were 36 possible combinations and the other 5 groups said that there were only 21 combinations because the numbers can repeat. (EX: spinning a 1 and a -6 is the same as a -6 and a 1.) We were asked to figure out which option is right. I personally think that it is 36 combinations because even though the numbers repeat, it’s still a possible option. I’m just looking to see what others think.
