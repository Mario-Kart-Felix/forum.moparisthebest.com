Just trying to provoke debate.

Too many of us are trying to be aspiring businessmen or professional MMORPG developers, when in reality we are very unprofessionally ripping off Jagex and RuneScape.

What are we trying to achieve? Thinking back to the origin of private servers, they were more or less an accident. It was just a point of concept/place to test the earliest RuneScape bots. Not a place to emulate, customise or express our ideas and concepts of the real game. And definitely not a place to earn a modest living off.

So why are we still here? How has RSPS evolved over the years, and why is it at the stage we are at now? Private servers are shit, to be frank. They do not work, they look ugly most of the time and are usually an insult to the original game. Some people see it as a cash cow, and 9 times out of 10 fail miserably. Only a handful last for years.

Don’t get me wrong, I am a part of this, and I do get personal satisfaction messing around with servers and building a community, but we just need to remember who we are from a moral point of view.

So, why are we still here, doing what we are doing? Well, one does not simply quit private servers.

There is no general point to make in this topic, just wanted to hear people’s views on the subject(s) raised.
