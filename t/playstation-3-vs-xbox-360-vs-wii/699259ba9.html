Wrong. It comes in 20, 40, 60, and 80GB models. Specifically speaking in the US (but most likely a fact for most other countries as well), the 20GB and 60GB models are no longer built and shipped off to stores for shipping. The 60GB model is the best when it comes to backwards compatibility with PS2 games, but the only way to get one nowadays is to buy one off the net for more than the 80GB model. As the 40 and 80GB models are the only ones available in stores in the US (as far as I know), the 80GB model is usually picked (for a mere 100 USD or 67 EURO more) as that specific model can be considered a gaming device AND a media device. This is so because the 80GB model has three extra input slots for a memory stick, SD memory card, and CompactFlash (CF) card.
