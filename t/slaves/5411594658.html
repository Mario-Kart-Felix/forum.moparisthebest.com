If you’re looking for historic accuracy and lore friendly additions to this, the tribal NPCs on Karamja would be a good addition to this. NOW, If you could create a way to enslave children and make them do things like farm herbs for you, my mind would be blown.

Also, adding a new “potion” called Chloroform, and requiring the player to use it on the weakened barbarian, then using a rope would make things a bit more challenging. Esp if you found a decent way to have players make the Chloroform, or made them buy it from that poison salesman from Seers.

Just to correct a few people, slavery is absolutely the correct word for this. Indentured servants implies that there is a chance of release once they have worked off their debt. slavery has nothing to do with racism, that is just the historical context from which society has now derived it from. the enslavement of any type of people does not involve racism, it involves overpowering and establishing ownership over them. We read science fiction novels about how aliens enslave worlds to do their bidding, yet that in itself is not racism, it is struggle for power. Stop trying to be politically correct about a term that is being used in its politically correct form.

On top of that, this is in no way related to Summoning. By definition Summoning involves SUMMONING an entity. Yes the concept of fighting for, following, and attacking is the same, but beyond that this idea is its own individual concept. Water and Ice are made up of the same thing, yet they are their own things and serve their own purposes, just like this.
