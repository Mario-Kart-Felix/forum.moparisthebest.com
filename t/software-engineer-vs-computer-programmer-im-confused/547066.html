Software engineers, often called software developers, plan and create programs. They analyze user needs, design software and then test it to ensure quality. They give instructions to programmers who write the software codes. Engineers who specialize in the operating systems needed by computers are called systems software developers. Those who focus on applications, such as games, video editors, word processors and databases, are called applications software developers. They require a minimum bachelor’s degree in software engineering, computer science or a related field. Though they do not usually write code, they also need strong programming skills to communicate properly with programmers.

From what sense I can make out of what I’ve read, it seems like software engineers are either (a) The persons who develop the virtual machines and programming frameworks and what not, i.e. .NET and Java itself, or (b) The persons who mainly deal in developing base API’s for companies so that developers can utilize them for their tasks. Protosstribe August 4, 2016, 1:50pm #2

It’s usually just a role given for a specific job. So you can probably be whatever you want. My education was called Application Development, but I could become Systems Developer, Project Leader or whatever else there is.

People called Systems Architects are usually paid more, for whatever reason (as far as I know, it seems like they don’t program at all, but I’m not sure).

Then the main difference between Systems Developers and Programmers are that Systems Developers are Programmers, whereas Programmers are not necessarily Systems Developers. By that I mean Systems Developers are planning the project and programming, but Programmers are just programming. Well, that’s probably the truth simplified, but I hope you get the point.

Software engineering is just engineering applied to software. That is, planning, developing, designing or whatever else you can think of. All related to software. So a Systems Developer do all that.

Protosstribe August 4, 2016, 1:51pm #20

Yz85Racer: By that you’re probably referring to either a project leader or a systems architect. Not that it really matters, though, as a project leader of one project may very well be able to take the role as a systems developer of another project. In other cases there’s no difference at all, and everyone can take the role as anything. That’s usually the case if the company doesn’t have that many employees, like smaller companies.

I have worked for six companies so far. One of them with around 800 employees, whereas the others were pretty small ones. As of yet, only one of those companies had a systems architect (not the large company). We also used Scrum there, so he was also the Scrum Master. On the company where I work now, we have a project leader whom barely write any code for the project that he leads. Although he is a systems developer in other projects, so he does write more code than that.
