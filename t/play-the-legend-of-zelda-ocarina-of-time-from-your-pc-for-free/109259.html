Okay. I am a pretty huge Zelda fan myself, and this is my most favorite game of all time. So I am here to tell you all how to get The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time to play on your computer for free. You may already have it, but you’ll still want this one.

Benifits

[#]Built in GameShark
[#]Pause whenever you want
[#]No Controller Needed
[#]Save whenever wanted
[#]Do other things while playing
[#]Fast Forward
[#]Full Screen

There are many more, but those are the best of them. Here’s how to get it.

Step One: First, go to REMOVED website and click on/download both Download Parts under The Legend Of Zelda: Ocarina Of Time - N64.

Step Two: Next, go REMOVED and click/download the 1964 0.9.9 and save it. Hopefully you made a folder for all this stuff.

Step Three: Extract the two Zelda files. Try to extract them at the same time. Then, you’ll get a folder containing The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time.N64 and a .txt file. Then, extract 1964 0.9.9 and open it. You’ll get a popup wanting you to specify a location, but it doesn’t say to what. So make it find The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time.N64 which should be in that one folder you got from extracting the two rarred files. You can double click on the text to play it, but unless you have an N64 Controller Adaptor and it’s hooked up to your computer, along with the controller, then you’re gonna get a No Controller appearing at the bottom.

Step Four: Go to REMOVED site and go towards the bottom. See the thing that says “DesktopMan’s Basic Keyboard 1.1”? Well, to the right of it is a floppy disk. Click on it to download. Download to your 1964 0.9.9 Plugins folder and then extract it there. Make sure all of its components are in the Plugins folder, and not another folder or else Step Five won’t work.

Step Five: Go back to the 1964 program, and you should be at the page where you first start up on. Right click on ‘Legend of Zelda, The - Ocarin…’ and click on ROM Plugin Settings. Next, where it says 'Input Plugin For Current ROM : ’ ( Is the game), click on the Arrow, bringing down a list of options. Click on ‘KeyBoard plugin by DM’. If we didn’t do Step Four, then it wouldn’t be there. Next, click OK and you can double click the text to play it without a problem.

Questions & Answers

Q. My graphics are all bad, and it’s like everything is overlapping itself and showing things that don’t exist.
A. Repeat Step Four, except in the middlish topish, you’ll see ‘Rice Video plugin’ and download that. Then do Step Five, except change the thing under Video Plugin. Change that to ‘Rice’s Daedalus 5.2.0’ and click OK. Then goto Video Settings… From the Plug-ins at the top and change Render Engine: to OpenGL and the combiner type to the same. Make sure the Color and Depth Buffer are both 32-bit or higher. The colors will change on some things like the C’s, and sometimes concrete things, but overall the graphics are the same and look like the N64’s.

Q. I don’t want sound, and I can’t get the ‘No Sound’ to be available.
A. Repeat Step Four, but this time, download the No Sound one and then repeat Step Five, but change the Audio Plugins to No Sound.

Q. How do I save whenever I want?
A. File > Save State > To File

Q. It doesn’t show a saved state I saved. How do I load it?
A. It doesn’t show it, you just need to remember what you saved.

Q. When I went to the start menu, the game froze.
A. It didn’t freeze, it usually takes a bit to load it. You know it froze when the Video % is at 0 at the bottom left.
