Closing something will close underlying streams as well (in this case, System.in is closed along with the Scanner object as it is the underlying stream being used by the Scanner).
Most of the time it’s a good thing, because you don’t have to make a whole bunch of method calls to close all of the underlying streams. In this case, it obviously isn’t.
