Well I used to have this problem in the bat that gave me this error:

“main” java.lang.NoClassDefFoundError: client
java.lang.ClassNotFoundException: client
…

Here is the solution.

Rightclick on “my computer”
then pick “properties”
then in the tab click “advanced”
then at the bottom you will see “variables”
Click it.

Then you will see somthing like “System Variables”
Now if you already have a “Variable” called “CLASSPATH” skip this.
If not, press “new” , name it CLASSPATH and the varriable should
lead to your client map.

Now for the variable.
It should lead you to your map.
For example, I play ploxagescape.
Now my variable is
C:\Doccument and Settings\Glenn\Desktop\Ploxagescape

Now “C” stands for your hard drive.
Mostly it will be “c” unless if you have another HD.

“Doccument and Settings” is a map in my HD. This map will make you able to pick files from your desktop.

“Glenn” stands for the my username. This is varries for everyone.

“Desktop” is where my file is saved on. For you it could be the same or “my documents”. It varries.

“Ploxagescape” is the name of the map where my client is in.
