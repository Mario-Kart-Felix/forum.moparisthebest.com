No, the original poster is correct.

When you delete a file, the space that file was using is marked as free, and other programs can now write to that space (thats why your hard drive space isn’t taken up) - however, the data is not gone until something writes to that space.

EDIT: This is why, if you corrupt a hard drive and call a data recovery company, the first thing they will tell you is to turn the PC off and do not use it until they have seen it - because the more you use the PC, the more likely it is that you will overwrite the data.
