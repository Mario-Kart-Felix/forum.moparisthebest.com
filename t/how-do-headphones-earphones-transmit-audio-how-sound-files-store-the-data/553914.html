Well it’s most likely that these three cables represent the left channel, right channel and finally the signal ground. In the particular song you were listening to, I presume the “background” was panned to one channel and the “foreground” to the other which creates this effect.

The format of the digital music irrelevant - any sound, regardless of how it’s stored, will end up being converted to an uncompressed format like PCM which is sent to the Digital-Analogue-Converter (DAC) where the digital information is converted to an analogue signal which are needed to drive a pair of headphones.
