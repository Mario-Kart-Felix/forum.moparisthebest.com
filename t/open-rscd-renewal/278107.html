[s]I have come to the most logical conclusion that DARScape Classic must now become an open source development project.

I have always believed in the quality service demonstrated by the staff to be what makes or breaks a private server. What we “have” specifically should not be of a loss by releasing what we have developed to the rest of the community.

I began Open RSCDv25 several months ago only to find my work go un-aided by the rest of the RSC developers. While I understood that the majority of users did not want to go the distance to install a MySQL based server, I also discovered the additional time it took to keep it updated with each successive update of DARScape was too time consuming. That is now no longer a problem and I believe it is absolutely necessary to keep our source open to the public so that the following occurs:

1.) More developers to be attracted to development of stable, interesting server sources
2.) Support the continued interest in RSC development
3.) Nullify the reason to DDoS other servers to try to shutdown the competition

I will be releasing everything we have developed minus our player data, IPB forum, (licensed software, a replacement forum must be supplied by user) and webclient. In the coming weeks this will be made public.[/s]

Second thought, never mind.
