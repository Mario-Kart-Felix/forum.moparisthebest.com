Here is what I thought.

  • It does seem to have an awkward layout due to the black and white. There are more colors. You could also add insignias near the edges or over the corners.
  • The login.php has 2 login areas. I saw it after I registered.
  • I registered as Ron but when I logged in with ron in lowercase, it showed “Welcome ron! You’ve been successfully logged in.” I suggest having an extra spot in your database for how the user wants his/her username shown to others in the correct case. So when people register, they will be able to set how it’s shown to add more of a user-friendly vibe.
  • In members.php, if an avatar is used, it widens the height of the cell. Your text is not centered meshing with the feel poorly.
  • The layout moves strangely when clicking to the next page. Click each of your navigational links from left to right and you will see the entire layout in 2 different locations.
  • Instead of putting “Click here to logout” maybe just put “Logout”
  • Your website doesn’t validate w3’s XHTML validator or their CSS validator
  • Don’t use tables, it’s just a waste of page load time. They can easily be replaced by divs and you know how to use them so that shouldn’t be a problem. Using divs instead of tables is also good for Search Engine Optimization (SEO), so even more ad revenue because its easier for search engines to grab your content and keywords.
  • I liked how it resized the avatar if it’s too big, it resizes it.
  • I also liked how images are backgrounds because it makes selecting text without selecting the images.
