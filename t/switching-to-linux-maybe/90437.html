MOST distro’s have;

Firefox
A messenging service, the *ubuntu range uses GAIM

Wireless is another thing, I have it and when I go on my laptop on linux I just can’t be botherd with the thought of wirelss, never have been, so I just unplug the ethernet from my main computer, although as mopar stated ndiswrapper is supposedly very simple and effective when setting up wireless on linux, if needed I can provide some links to tutorials if you specify your card model.

Any off the *Ubuntu ranges are good for someone with any level of knowledge that is to do with Linux, some people argue that some of the more simple distro’s may be better though, I personally tried using Puppy Linux before my swap over, this is only around 80mb and is a fairly stable and nice distro to use.
