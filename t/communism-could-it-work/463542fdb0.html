The pilgrims (in Plymouth) weren’t socialist, while the pilgrims did agree to common ownership, it was always intended that they move to private ownership. But the farmers did work, and they weren’t lazy, you have to keep in mind that the pilgrims that settled in Plymouth came from all over England and had different dialects and different ways of doing things. Take a group of farmers from all parts of a country in colonial times and tell them that they have to work together, see how far that gets you. William Bradford reported that the pilgrims were unwilling to work because they didn’t want to work with each other, that they were confused because herpderp different accents and different backgrounds, single men didn’t want to farm to feed the wives of married men, the wives of married men didn’t want to wash the clothes of single men, husbands didn’t want to see their wives in the fields planting corn for the community, etc. But the communal ownership worked, and in 1621 the pilgrims had the first reported thanksgiving, a year after they had landed. If the communal ownership hadn’t have worked, they would have had their thanks giving after 1623 when they switched to private ownership, not before.

After the pilgrims switched to private ownership (which was the plan all along), the situation improved because there was no more squabbling between farmers that came from different backgrounds and didn’t get along with each other. Wives weren’t complaining about having to wash anyone but their husbands clothes, single farmers weren’t complaining about having to feed married men’s wives, etc.
