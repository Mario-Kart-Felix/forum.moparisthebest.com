lothy:
Lol… there’s no such thing as a “KO point”. A knockout is achieved when the head is hit hard enough to cause the brain to hit the inside part of the skull. Yes, you can hit them in the jaw. But hitting them anywhere with enough force will cause their head to jerk back. When the head stops moving back, the brain can still hit the inside of the skull, and that’s how it happens.

EDIT: Better way to explain it - Think of it as inertia, one of Newton’s laws. If you’re on a bus that stops quickly, you’ll feel yourself go flying forward. If you’re in a car that hits another car head-on, you’ll either a) Get thrown out the windscreen or b) you’ll get jerked forward hard, the seatbelt will keep you there, and afterwards you’ll most likely have whiplash.

Apply this to fighting. The fist (car 1) hits the head (car 2) head on. Wink The head flies back, and then stops, and the brain keeps going, until the skull (seatbelt in the car) stops it going back further. But the brain hits the skull hard and you get knocked out.
