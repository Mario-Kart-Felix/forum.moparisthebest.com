OCT 24: Jeffrey Skilling, Enron Corporation’s former CEO, was sentenced to 24 years and four months in prison and must forfeit $45 million for directing a massive fraud that destroyed the company and made its name synonymous with corporate wrongdoing.

US District Court judge Sim Lake sentenced Skilling Monday in Houston after seven victims described how the fraud upended their lives. Lake recommended that Skilling serve his term at a prison in Butner, North Carolina, while denying a prosecution request that he be jailed immediately. Skilling, 52, received the minimum sentence he faced under federal guidelines that suggest between 24 and 30 years.

“Skilling and his attorneys argue that the guideline range would be tantamount to life in prison,” the judge said after hearing a defence request for a seven- to 10-year term. “But as the many victims of his crime have poignantly explained, his crimes have imposed on them a life sentence of poverty.”

Enron, once the seventh-biggest US company by sales, had a market value of more than $68 billion before its bankruptcy in December 2001 wiped out more than 5,000 jobs and at least $1 billion in retirement funds.
