All the models for the items, and information on the spots on your character where they get wielded are stored in a large random access file called the cache. When you log on to RS using Jagex’s official client (basically, the only way they encourage one to join RS) the client downloads the file and waits for you to press the “Login” button. When you log in, the client preextracts the contents of the cache to that it can automatically render models and images as you see them. Now, the MoparScape client is basically version 317 of Jagex’s RS client. Some time after version 420, Jagex heavily updated their client and the cache so that their client and cache both work together “properly”, however our old 317 clients can’t work right [with the new cache] anymore.

You may say “Well, why not just use Jagex’s client instead of MoparScape ?” well, that is because Jagex’s new client responds extremely differently that the 317 does to the same signals that the 317 responds to. So, for example, if your server wanted the client to update the image with “Draw a picture of a dragon 2h on the ground at tile 453,6533” then the 317 would do just that. However, if your server told a 420 + client to do that, the client would crash.
