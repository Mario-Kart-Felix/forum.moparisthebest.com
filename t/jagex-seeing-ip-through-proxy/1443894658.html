First off, it would be stupid to run runescape as a signed applet because, as it has been said, it can do whatever it wants.

I suppose that would work fine. So you and the 8 or 9 other people that do that will be safe from them executing protected code on your machine. That won’t look suspicious at all… If I were them, I’d closely monitor everything anyone that ran an unsecured client did. I think running an unsecured client gives you less protection from their watch-dogs and not more. It just raises their threat level against you. But if you get a warm-fuzzy thinking you are keeping yourself from being detected by running the unsecured applet, go for it…

Keep in mind, the only real reason for tracking ip addresses is to identify unique machines and/or clients. There’s likely a number of reasons for this. I can promise you that the most important of which has little to do with preventing cheaters or hackers. The number one reason involves advertising. The more unique individuals they can show that play the game, the more they can charge/increase the cost for advertising. Every web-based company with a vested interest in advertising has many eyes on unique page-views.

That said, there is also the side-benefit of detecting known cheaters. All-in-all though, from my experience, these guys care very little about your small hacking/cheating operations and care far more about game stability and advertising dollars/pounds.
