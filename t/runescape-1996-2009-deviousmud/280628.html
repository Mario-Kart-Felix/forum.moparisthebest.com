I’ve been looking around the internet about the evolution of RuneScape. I’d like to share what I found with this community as I believe it’s an interesting story. A student named Andrew Gower decided to make a MMORPG for a college project in 1996. He decided to ask his brother Paul Gower to test the game for him. Paul Gower believed this was an interesting game and should be made public. Andrew Gower decided to name this game “DeviousMUD”. DeviousMUD was an excellent game in it’s time but was not really introduced to the public. Andrew Gower decided to make the game public for one week. The game managed to reach 1200 accounts within the week of publicity. The game was then removed from the internet.

In 1998 Andrew Gower decided to re-write his MMORPG game to re-introduce to the public. Adding membership and basic buttons Andrew Gower began to think this was a great idea. He re-wrote “DeviousMUD” (1998 - 1999) which was then called “RuneScape Classic”. The game became public and after two weeks the game reached it’s limited users. Andrew and Paul Gower decided to expand the servers and use the tradename “Jagex”. RuneScape has expanded to RuneScape 2, RuneScape HD.

Don’t believe me? Google it.

I played DeviousMUD on the week of publicity but didn’t manage to grab any footage of the game because there was no video recording equipment on the internet. I got one picture on my old computer, sorry for the background desktop.
