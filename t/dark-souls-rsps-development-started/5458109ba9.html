[quote=“Death Style, post:40, topic:545810”]if you’re going to add multiplayer you need to use instances like DS

the trick to make an “instance” is to just change the floors the players use
0,1,2,3 are the standard height levels
4,5,6,7 are physically identical but entities on the former do not appear here

when player 1 joins the server, you give them floors 0,1,2,3
when player 2 joins the server, you give them floors 4,5,6,7

in that sense they are on their own instances
the idea I posted ignores the fact that they are on different instances and renders them as ghosts anyway

and like DS each instance needs their own NPCs, because if player 1 killed a goblin it should still exist for player 2
which is what I mean when I say the real challenge is making unique instances, since your source doesn’t natively support it
I don’t mean that NPCs can’t spawn on levels 1,2,3, just that they would have to spawn on those levels for each player

if you’re seriously considering it you should create a separate set of NPCs that can show on all instances (traders, etc) and modify their updating function so they can also show across all instances

and then create “areas” with their own bundle of NPCs and items that spawn on an as-needed basis

i.e. if you have 100 players on the server, you should not be spawning 100 goblins for each of them the moment they log in, only when the player is in the area that the goblin exists (and you should delete them when the player leaves)[/quote]

the client has a packet for this, you can construct map regions for a specific player using a tile palette, the way you have described is the wrong way to do this
