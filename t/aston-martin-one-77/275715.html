Aston Martin One-77

Aston Martin will reveal the first working concept of its £1 million One-77 supercar to the world today.

The covers will be pulled off the concept at the exclusive Concorso d’Eleganza show in Italy.

This marks the first time the new Aston Martin has been seen in public. Aston Martin describes the show car as a fully-complete concept, which we’re sure will closely mirror the ultra-exclusive production car.

Billed by Aston Martin as the ‘definitive sports car’, there is no denying it’s dramatic. It’s an Aston Martin on steroids, with impossibly pumped bodywork and taut, bulging wheelarches. Looking ready to pounce, the drama alone is sure to fully justify the immense price tag for a very lucky few. Indeed, just 77 of them.

Aston Martin has also opened up the doors of the One-77. This gives us a first peek at the interior - and it looks like it’s pretty bespoke to MSN Cars’ eyes. No missing that huge, stylised air vent, nor the use of cutting-edge, modern materials. Exposed construction structures also hint at latest-generation underpinnings.
Aston Martin One-77 (Image © Aston Martin)

Speaking of the new motor, Aston Martin boss, Dr Ulrich Bez, said: ‘The One-77 demonstrates the unique capabilities of our company - it encompasses engineering excellence with a great passion from our engineers, designers and technical partners. This is a major step in One-77’s engineering programme.’

In showing the very first complete car, Dr. Bez says Aston Martin is demonstrating ‘new benchmarks in many different fields of automotive design. The craftsmanship and design that has gone into the creation of the Aston Martin One-77 lifts automotive engineering into the realm of art. It surpasses any Aston Martin that has gone before it.’

‘With its finely sculpted forms and sleek lines, it is an expression of unparalleled beauty that embodies everything Aston Martin stands for.’ Which sure sounds like a call to arms for us. This genuinely is an exciting supercar announcement, and a super-significant new car that could, just maybe, set new supercar benchmarks.
