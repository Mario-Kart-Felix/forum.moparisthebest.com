When making models, tracing can only take you so far. You’ll get to the point where there will be no more for you to travel. Thus, I am making this quick guide on making a sword using the button, primitive.

Step 1: Open up meta, go to file and uncheck basic functions.

Step 2. Go over to primitives, select cylinder, and work it to your liking. This will be your handle. Hit the create button.

Step 3. Make another cylinder, this time wider. It will be that “guard” thing that protects your fingers. Hit create.

Step 4. Make a square. This will be the blade. Put it ontop, and segment it to look like a cross. Hit create.

Step 5. Go over to select, hit normal, and click in space. Then click on the side one of your segmented spots, select move, and move that over to the side. Do this for both sides. Congratulations, you a have a sharp cleaver-like weapon!

Step 6. Click move on the tip of the cross part, and move that upwards for a sharp point along the Y-axis.

Step 7. Getting now into some detail for a nice looking weapon. Use the select tool and use the “shift” key to select multiple points (shown below). Go over to move and move it in a little bit for a nicer look. Apply to both sides

Step 8. Go over and select the knife tool. Make 2 lines across your handle as shown below.

Step 9. Select the move tool and move the points in to your liking to something like this.

10oi4xg.jpg313×502

Step 10. Go to primitives, select sphere, and add it to the bottom.

Step 11. Add color!!
