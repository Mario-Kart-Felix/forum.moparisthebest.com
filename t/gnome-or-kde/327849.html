moparisthebest July 31, 2016, 2:30pm #10

Here is how I look at it.

For a complete, good looking, and excellent functioning Desktop Environment I choose KDE 3.5, and I have been using it on a day to day basis since 2006. I don’t really think it is bloated, as you can run as little or as much of it as you want and any computer that can handle windows XP decently can handle KDE 3.5 even better. It does take more resources than Xfce, but to me it is worth it, especially if you have the few resources to spare.

I’ve tried Gnome in the past, but it seems completely useless to me when there is the alternative of Xfce. Gnome probably uses the same resources as KDE (3.5) and (to me) doesn’t work as well or feel as integrated or complete as KDE. Xfce looks and functions identically to Gnome as far as I can tell, but is much faster and consumes far fewer resources. I don’t see why all Gnome users don’t just use Xfce instead, perhaps someone can enlighten me on the issue?

KDE 4 is a different story. It seems bloated to me (although, again, if a computer can handle Vista or Windows 7 then it can handle KDE 4 with no problems), and I don’t like it near as much as KDE 3.5, which I continue to use. However, if you tried KDE 4.0 or 4.1 like I did and hated it, it really has improved much since then, especially with the current 4.3, which I tend to actually like but still have yet to switch to. I feel by the time KDE 4 reaches 4.5 or so it will probably be time for me to switch to it.

Summed up:
I like KDE 3.5 the best, and don’t see why anyone would use Gnome at all instead of Xfce. The difference between KDE and Xfce boils down to opinion, both are fine DE’s.
