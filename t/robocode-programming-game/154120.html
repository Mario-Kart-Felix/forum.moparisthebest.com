lothy July 30, 2016, 4:18pm #16

Chess couldn’t be that hard. I’d imagine that you’d have a collection for pieces that were ‘in danger’, and those pieces would be given a priority. The bot would obviously search so many moves ahead, and keep this collection up-to-date. It’d count the moves required to attack that piece in its current position, and if two pieces were in danger at the same time, it’d move the piece with the highest priority.

It’d do the same when attacking - it’d aim to take out the most powerful pieces (queen, followed by rooks/bishops and so on) in order, where possible. Of course, it might want to consider pieces already removed so that it can take pawns into account (before they reach the opposite end of the board and get replaced by a previously removed piece).
