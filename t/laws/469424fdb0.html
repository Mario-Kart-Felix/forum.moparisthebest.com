to understand why drug testing isn’t effective you need to look at the methodology of how drugs are tested, as I’ve stated many drugs don’t stay in your system for a long time and to find these drugs you actively need to search for them (costing money for resources and many, MANY man hours)

codeine, ghb, heroin, many opiates and oxycodone all stay in your system for as little as 1-2 days, meanwhile amphetamines, cocaine, ketamine and methamphetamine all stay in your system for up to 4 days, benzodiazepines only stay in your system for 3 days while lsd and ecstasy stay in your system for up to 5 days

so, in as little as 5 days, most of the drugs taken recreationally will be out of your system, it’s only a few other drugs that last longer such as cannabis, methadone and phencyclidine that generally stay in your system for longer than a week, so while we can test for those substances and find them if they were taken less than a week ago, you can’t actively search for those drugs unless you do random drug tests on a frequent basis and even then you’re looking at a good lot of the population who aren’t going to be caught by these tests unless you ramp up the tests, so to find a most likely minority of welfare recipients who take drugs you are now subjecting all welfare recipients to frequent drug tests where false positives may occur and if the welfare recipient can’t make a test (people on welfare have lives too you know) then they’d be suspected for taking drugs and possibility lose their only financial support
