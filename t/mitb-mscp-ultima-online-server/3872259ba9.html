Perhaps i should add some more interesting contrasts from the modern MMORPG’s.

First of all, there are no “Levels”, so it’s like RuneScape in the sense that you have a pool of skills to choose from. Now, a big difference is that you have a stat cap, meaning you can only get x amount of skills to 100 (7 by default). This is to diversify the population a bit and have people take on different roles. In addition to this, you have 3 stats, which are Strength, Intelligence and Dexterity.

By far the most interesting skills in this game (in my opinion) are Animal Taming and Stealing. Animal Taming will allow you to tame pretty much anything that isn’t Humanoid in the game (sorry all of you interested in a pet-orc ). This includes horses, which brings me to the second point: Mounts. Unlike the current line-up of MMORPG’s, mounts can either be free or can cost. If you want it for free, no problem! Become an animal tamer, tame one, name it and do what you wish. Or maybe you’d rather want to sell it for profit to people who are not tamers!

In addition to taming them, you can control them using text commands, which will allow you to have them: Follow You, Attack Things, Guard You, Guard Someone else etc etc.

As they fight, their skills go up as well, so you could see it as a pokémon-pseudo combat thingie! Of course, animals can and WILL die, so always be cautious, and have bandages ready for healing

Secondly, Stealing. My favourite skill by far out of the two. This will allow you to steal things from other players. Yes you heard me, it’ll allow you to open up their bags and steal things directly from their inventory! Of course they can detect this, and most likely will unless you’re at a very high skill (or good at distracting them somehow). Here comes another fun metagame element into the picture: Trapping, Tinkering and Alchemy skills.
These can be used to trap containers either with explosives or with poison (as well as lock them). This was a popular method of avoiding pickpockets in the old days of UO, people would have all their valuables in bags in their inventory, and then they would trap these bags with deadly poison or explosives, so when someone tried to check it for loot, they would die and you could just loot the thief. In other words, Karma Baby!

I’ve only scratched the surface here really, but i feel like i’m typing a fucking essae, so enough for now!
