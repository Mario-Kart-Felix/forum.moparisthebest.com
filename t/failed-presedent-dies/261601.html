Well actualy it was a mistake!

A TV station in South Africa mistakenly broadcast that former US President George Bush had died. For three seconds, ETV News ran a moving banner headline saying 'George Bush is dead'.

Apparently, a member of staff wanted to see how a rolling banner headline looked. The error happened when a technician pressed the ‘broadcast live for transmission’ button instead of the one for a test-run. “The technical director pressed the wrong button, it took a second for the words to appear and then the words were on screen for only three seconds before they were taken off,” said spokesman Vasili Vass. “We’ve learned from it, all test banners will now be done in gobbledegook,” he added. He would not comment on whether the person responsible faces disciplinary action.
