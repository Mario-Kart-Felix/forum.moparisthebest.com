Your form; touching dumbbells at the top of a flat press puts a lot of stress on your shoulder/elbow joint. Not that 90lb is a weight at which will cause too much problems straight away, but overtime you can injure your shoulders. It weakens the shoulder joint.
Locking the arms out on any form of press takes the force of the movement away from your muscle and puts it entirely on the joint. Best I can describe it as is this, seeing as you seem fairly tuned on in terms of weight lifting. Next time you’re at your gym, anywhere will suffice actually, perform a shoulder width overhand chin up. Now, let yourself drop down, letting your arms lock out. Feel that jerking motion/pulling feeling on your wrist? That’s the force of your weight transferring through your wrist joint. The only difference in this situation is there is your entire body weight + gravity transferring through it, not just 90lb.

Dumbbells are best for inducing hypertrophy which is desired when building muscle. They aren’t the best for raw strength (in this case anyway). As far back as you can remember the best exercise for building upperbody pressing power is, and always will be, the bench press. Dumbbell benching is good for hypertrophy, bench pressing is good for strength - DON’T confuse the two.

Now, if you’re trying to induce hypertrophy which I am going to assume you are, there are better ways to perform a dumbbell bench than the “flat bench” you performed. That exercise has a bad habit of producing “man boobs” so to speak. Ofcourse I am only merely speculating at this point because I have no idea what you train for/want to achieve. If it’s too feel better about yourself because you moved some weight and posted a video than kudos too you. But, there’s a reason why bodybuilders do not perform the flat bench.

Not that I can clearly see in your video, but it looks like on 2 or 3 of your reps your scapulars protracted. This either tells me the weight is too high, or you have poor scapular retraction. However, take this with a grain of salt because I can’t really tell without watching your form from the side.

ps I’d like it known I don’t advocate for either bench or dumbbell press. They just both have their uses, but only doing one of the two is not wise either. Incorporate both, but focus primarily around your goal.
