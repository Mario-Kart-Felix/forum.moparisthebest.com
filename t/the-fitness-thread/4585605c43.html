I would argue from a sport science point of view that compound exercises do actually infact induce increased muscle hypertrophy. It’s a well known fact that recruiting more muscle mass with a challenging motor pattern (the correct term for a compound exercise) will induce increased metabolic stress; and in order to account for this increased stress and to maintain homeostasis the body will release more testosterone. As a result of this increased testosterone level, a rise in anabolism would naturally occur (provided you’re in a calorie surplus).

The general agreed method is 4-6 reps of a compound exercise is optimal for increasing test-levels. 5 sets of 5 reps is good, 10 sets of 5 reps is going to be better; however there is a point to which there is too much of a good thing. This is considered the ‘catabolic threshold’, and each individual is different.

There’s plenty of evidence out there to suggest that all you need is to perform 6-10 sets of 5-6 reps for Olympic lifts, squats, and deadlifts. Then use isolation exercises to bring up a lagging area intermittently.

There is nothing magical about a compound lift that induces growth more than an isolation exercise. Sure it may release a tiny bit more gh but it isn’t that significant, if it were everyones legs would grow fast and every highschool football player would be jacked. In beginner lifters compounds help create a mind-muscle connection to help you activate more during exercise, this in turn helps you grow faster. A seasoned lifter can do isolations or compounds and still make progress.

The best thing to do for size is to do compounds and then top them off with isolation. For strength don’t even waste your time with isolation unless your form on a compound is incorrect due to a muscle imbalance

When I went on a 5 day split for one summer I started every day with a compound, and the rest were isolations (but you have to sort your days so certain muscles aren’t worked adjacently at high volume/intensity). I grew very fast during those 3-4 months. Because of work I had to slow down to a 3 day split, I do even more compounds now because they save me time but I’m not growing nearly as fast because my volume is suffering.

Yz85Racer August 4, 2016, 11:02am #340

The overtraining phenomena is so overplayed in the fitness community it’s amazing.

There’s also a difference between overtraining and physiological limits, people mistake the two - working out twice a day is hard when you start and you’re usually a lot more exhausted/sore when you start; but you were exhausted and sore when you FIRST started working out. If you actually do ‘overtrain’ you will feel like you were hit by a truck, and you will physically see it.

Overtraining’s medical name is rhabdomylosis. I have had a case of rhabdo. My urine was brown and I could physically see the swelling around my lower back.
