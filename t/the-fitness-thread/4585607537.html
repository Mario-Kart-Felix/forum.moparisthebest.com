I agree with your second last statement. Best thing you can do is change your diet and perfect it, however perfecting it without certain supplementation is rather difficult -not impossible-, but difficult. But using protein supplements, for example, provides other benefits (quick insulin boost + protein synthesis spike post workout). Rather small and insignificant, but combined with a solid diet and a solid supplement regime (fish oil, protein) it amounts to a significant advantage.

My take on pre-workout supplements has always been the same. Be careful. They’re perfectly safe if used correctly and safely, provided you’re using a safe one (i.e ones that don’t contain new flavor-of-the-month ingredients). For what it’s worth, I predict a lot of new formulas for preworkouts will contain the new flavor-of-the-month ingredient called “Pterostilbene”. When these new ingredients are released and formulated is when you should be careful. Hence why I always avoided the DMAA hype, the ingredient profile was so close to a amphetamine it wasn’t funny.

That’s why if you do choose to use a preworkout supplement make sure to be rather anal about the ingredients, and do your research; don’t be lazy. I always advise only using non-stimulant pre’s, but if you do choose to use a stimulate based formula, make sure to drink lots of water after to flush them through your system.

Bottom line; don’t become a junkie on supplements. Get your training, diet and the basics perfect, then work supplements into your regime. I occasionally use a pre-workout for strength training sessions (most of my sessions are very similar to crossfit so a pre-workout is not a smart idea) but I only use formula’s I have meticulously researched. (i.e MP Assault, Platinum Pre).
