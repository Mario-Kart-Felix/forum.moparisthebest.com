Yz85Racer August 4, 2016, 9:03am #236

It’s not risky for your health. It’s beneficial for your health. I’d cut the coke zero/diet coke out entirely, but that’s me personally. I have not drunk any soft drink for over 4 years (only thing other than water I’ve drank is Hydralite, and an occasional beer). I am in the process of trying to lose some body fat, and I found it extremely hard to only eat so little calories a day. I felt cold, angry and frustrated because of calorie restriction, and given that I ride motocross regularly it was not working well because the intensity of motocross quickly shows you why restricting your calories can love you up when exercising intensely.

So I was suggested to look into changing my eating pattern into a IF-based protocol. After a week my body adapted perfectly, no hunger pains, and I had so much more energy when exercising fasted and the increased insulin sensitivity is amazing. Basically, here’s what I do
4pm Training
6pm Largest meal (this is sometimes upwards of 1500+ calories)
8pm Second meal (smaller than first)
10pm Last meal (Try to aim for about 50g+ protein, for me this meal is usually 2 tins of tuna + some chicken + a scoop of ON Pro Complex)

Following MFP my TDEE is 2210. I then take my calories burned (calculated from my heart rate monitor) from my training session and enter them in to get my daily total allowance.

Now, days that I ride, I completely throw my diet out the window because I know I will never be in a surplus of calories on these days. For example, when I do a 30 minute moto I burn upwards of 1400 calories.

During the daily ‘fasting’ period, all you consume is water and green tea. I drink 4-6L of water during this time.

After 16 hours of fasting your body burns almost pure fat.
