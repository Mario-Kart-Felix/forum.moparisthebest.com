There really is no technical science about working work. It’s a simple equation (perhaps it’s the engineer in me), but it can be broken down simply as this.
constant muscle exertion & strength progress + eating daily macronutrient needs as per your goals = progress.

To expand slightly, how you workout simply comes down to this… more weight = more fast muscle fiber exertion. less weight = more slow muscle fiber exertion. Fast muscle fibers generally recruit larger fibers (hence why your muscle grows in size), and quicker (provided you’re in a calorie surplus). Slow muscle fibers generally recruit slower fibers (your muscle may still grow in size, but minimally. It will however become more efficient at nutrient partitioning [i.e how well your muscle can utilize nutrients/oxygen, which you might know as ‘muscular endurance’, or ‘lactate resistance’]). Slower muscle fiber recruitment is also a lot longer, and quite a larger process of recovering torn adenosine triphosphate in the muscle (i.e that muscle soreness you feel)

There really is no such thing as ‘toning’, or ‘lean muscle gains brah no fat all muscle bruh u mirin?’. It’s muscle, and bodyfat - don’t overcomplicate it. Now once you go even further from here, you learn that there really is no such thing as ‘cardio’ either, but I digress and attempting to explain that would cause a shit storm larger than trying to explain it too someone doing squats on a exercise ball and calling it ‘functional training’.
