[quote=“asshole_rule, post:256, topic:458560”]how can i get a good chest workout without weights? what exercises would I do? I guess push ups, but I don’t really know for sure.

I googled it and a bunch of bs came up.[/quote]
Well I don’t know about most people but when I workout my chest area I never use weights to be honest. I train for motocross, so unless I plan on having a bike laying ontop of me and I need to press it, bench press/weights aren’t important. Bench press is also a very unfunctionable exercise; it builds muscle sure, but if you’re bench pressing hoping for functionality you’re wasting your time.

I do 20x push ups, 15x atomic push ups (with the TRX machine, put your feet in the handles, push up, then crunch your knees into your chest), 5x vertical pushups (put your feet up on the wall and push up like that), 10x weighted push up (perform a push up, but with a 20kg plate on your back). This is one set, no break between exercise, 2 minute break between set. My heart rate never gets below 165 doing this, and just yesterday was the first time I decide to try bench pressing (if you didn’t know, I’ve had a lot of surgery on my shoulder and arms lately so I didn’t expect much), and I can still out bench 80% of people I know and train with.

Now for me, that’s a definition of a good chest. For you, a good chest might be bulging pecs, etc. Well, remember this, the decline of an exercise (i.e a bench press, the “going down” of the bar) is what induces hypertrophy. So, in a push up, it’s the going down to the ground, not pushing back up. Slow push up down, quick up, could develop a decent bit of muscle - but without weights muscular attributes are hard to develop.
