RSC hasn’t had the same feel in years, not since it was hosted by JaGeX (for free), in all honesty. When I first started, it was all new, fresh. Things to explore, things to do. There was a great deal of curiosity involved which made it interesting. There was an “exploration” feel to the game. That can never be recaptured by replicating the game, as we already know everything to the game. I even remember when it was possible for me to get “lost” whilst walking the map of RuneScape Classic.

It’s definitely lost a qualitative aspect I can’t quite find the right words for. I remember when simply hitting high numbers felt cool, when having adamantite felt accomplishing, when being lent a few hundred gold was great. That was probably like 2001 for me, when I was a total noob. PKing was fun, actually caring about how many Ruby Amulets you had. That’ll never happen again, and even if people try, nobody’s going to put forth the effort that was once required with RuneScape Classic. It’s not a new game anymore.

Merching was great, there once was a time when there was 1000s of people doing every aspect of the game simultaneously, so the economy just came naturally without any effort. Where have all these other people gone? A lot went to RS2, a lot moved on, and a small percent who shared the memories either went seeking for or were told about a private server. My first private server other than the one I ran with you and Protosstribe, was RSCA when I came back a few years later and heard that RSC private servers were a bit more established. That kinda hit the spark for me for a bit, but it got old after a few months. The games okay, the magic is just gone. But like you I still occasionally lift a hand for development, if somebody needs help.
