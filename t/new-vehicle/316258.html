That is the closest you can get.

AWD is not 4WD, at all. With AWD it is still possible to get stuck with only 1 wheel spinning, with 4WD you are at least guaranteed that one of your front and one of your rear wheels will spin at the same rate.

Unhandled:

Can’t you get vehicles that have AWD/4WD enabled manually? My dad’s truck is 2WD but it has a switch to change to 4WD.

Of course, my S10 had this as well. Problem is the tires moving are still spinning the front axles regardless of whether it is engaged in the transmission or not. Some (mostly older) vehicles have locking hubs that allow you to disengage the front axles from the wheels by getting out of your vehicle and physically turning a knob on each wheel, but most people just leave them engaged all the time regardless. No matter what a 4x4 is going to have more rolling resistance than a 2x4 and therefore less gas mileage.

And I’m not saying it’s not worth it, depending on where you live, but you won’t get as good of gas mileage as with a comparable 2x4 vehicle.
