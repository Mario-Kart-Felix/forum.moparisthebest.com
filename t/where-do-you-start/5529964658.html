I sometimes start at the platform level (algorithms, db, etc) but usually go straight into the visual stuff as a fast prototype. Whatever I do, I usually end up throwing it away after a few weeks, and that gives me a better idea of how to architect everything and start again from scratch. It also helps me get over the one aspect that interests me the most about the project (e.g., super efficient multiplayer handling, cool terrain generator, etc), then I can build the thing properly without getting distracted and going on a tangent.

I don’t think making a big plan is a great way to build software, especially if you don’t deviate from it. I like doing it because it makes it easier to track progress and I find it more motivational, but often find myself going down a fundamentally flawed route just because it was the original plan.

Agile isn’t a method, it’s a methodology. It’s a set of optional, core principles you base the foundation of a team on, not a means of creating software. I find a few of the principles to be fundamental, one of them being open to adapting and changing the way you are currently developing software (which includes ignoring parts of the Agile manifesto that do not apply to your particular team). As to that, I am very much open to changing the way I do things. I consider that the “Agile-way”.
