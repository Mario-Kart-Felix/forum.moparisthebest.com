bowserjr2 August 3, 2016, 10:31pm #12
Niall:

Copyright should belong to the author for 10, 15 or 20 years max, allowing the author to profit from his work and then allow society to profit from the eventual public domain works.

It isn’t a need to profit from the works, but using it for what it is worth. Giving everyone a part of literature without charging them. Make them freely available on the internet, and sell printed versions for those who prefer paper.

I wouldn’t object to profiting from public domain works, as the availability only get higher as you give someone some incentive of distributing it.

The reason for my view here is based on why we have (should have) copyrights. We need something to inspire people to write and make good works. And why should we inspire people to write books and make movies? To entertain or inspire people. Therefore it should be available when the author has gained a some profit from it. It might even inspire more works to be created, as people can’t just lean back after a successful book and get money for something you did 12 years ago.

PS: I’m not speaking of protecting of characters and the world that the book is creating, as that is another subject.
