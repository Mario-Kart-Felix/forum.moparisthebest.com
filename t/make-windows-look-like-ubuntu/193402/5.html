This tutorial does not require any major installations, just make your windows look and feel like linux without having to use those risky os editors or anything like that

NOTE: your dont need windowsblinds or any other type of 3rd party software for this its just to make it look somewhat like linux

[center][size=15pt]Installing the theme.[/size][/center]

Before you can use any themes, you must add a patch to windows that allows unassigned themes to be used. Download the theme patcher.

When you start the program, click ‘patch’, keep going until you get to the part where it asks you to insert your windows boot CD. Cancel at this time, by now, your theme should be windows classic. Next go to C:\WINDOWS\resources\themes and just leave that open.

Here are 2 decent themes for your linux look

Clearlooks - http://www.deviantart.com/deviation/18591720/
Ubuntu - http://uppit.com/9G6F6J
Or if you downloaded the pack already, ‘Ubuntu’ is in there.

once downloaded, extract the folder to C:\WINDOWS\resources\Themes.

restart your computer then move on.

[center][size=15pt]Perfecting your theme.[/size][/center]

Ok, so now you restarted? Good. Now right click on your desktop and click properties, then go to the appearance tab and wala! your theme should be listed under there.

Now alot of other things may still look windows-ish, so if you would like, you can make firefox look a bit more like linux.

Now for the icons, http://vertigosity.deviantart.com/art/Tango-Patcher-2600-7-08-2-27940418, this is also in the pack at the beginning of the tutorial, and is really the best i can find for right now.

when your installing the icons, the first set is blue,
second is orange,
3rd is industrial
4th is gnome,
when your installing the picture on the installer left side is them in order.

Install the icon patcher and you will have linux looking icons.

[center][size=15pt]We’re almost done![/size][/center]

Now all you really need is the background

I have a decent amount attached, enjoy!
