lothy August 4, 2016, 7:10am #66

Jagex has a reputation as a failed gaming company. In over 12 years they’ve only shipped one successful title (that being Runescape).
If they didn’t want people to bot then they would make the higher levels more accessible (i.e., adjust the experience given by everything, or change the experience curve so that it isn’t exponential in nature). In doing so, fewer people would reach the ‘love this, I’m just going to bot it’ point.

The other problem is their economy. There are three routes to the best armour, which is now a necessity if you don’t want to be completely outclassed by someone 20 levels lower.
You can spend $150 - $200 USD on the gold (which in Australia, as a professional, can be earned in a working day) OR you can get very very lucky after killing a monster OR you can grind out over 400 hours of gameplay doing something that’s worth at least 1 million gold per hour.
To put that into perspective, 400 hours is over ten weeks of full time work at a job (in Australia, our full time work week is generally 38 hours).

So yes, people are going to cheat in their shitty virtual world.

NOTE: I’ve referred to ‘best armour’ as in the best armour available in EOC Runescape. This armour set is worth more than 400M. My point is still relevant in OSRS though, where there are surely very expensive items that take at least tens of hours of work to afford.
