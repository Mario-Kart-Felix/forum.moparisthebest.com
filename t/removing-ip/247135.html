Introduction:
Today I am going to teach you how to host games (including RS2 servers) without using Hamachi or having to access your router. Using this method you can automaticly forward up to 1 port temporarily without needing to know your router info, all by using a familiar and unexpected program: uTorrent.

How does it work:
uTorrent works by automaticly forwarding a selected port to ensure your computer will accept incoming and outgoing connections require to download .torrent filetypes. The new versions of uTorrent conveniently allow you to modify that port. This got me thinking, would I be able to use this to somehow temporarily port forward?

Step 1:
Figure out which port your server is listening on. This is pretty easy to discover, it’s usually displayed right in your runserver.bat upon start-up. As you know, many of the servers created today include the ability to modify your port, but 43594 is the default java compound gaming cache port.

Step 3:
Once uTorrent is installed correctly, boot it up. Browse through the first-run setup, put in the obvious things. When you have completed this task, navigate to the top of the screen and select Options -> Preferences -> Connection.

Step 4:
You should see a screen with the title Connection and a box for “Port used for incoming connections”. Simply modify this to 43594, or whichever port you happen to be using. Make sure “Randomize Port every time uTorrent starts” is UNCHECKED and that “Add uTorrent to firewall exceptions” is CHECKED UPnP and NAT-PMP are a good idea to have CHECKED. Afterwards Click apply.

Step 5:
Once your settings are in, close uTorrent and open/run your gameserver. AFTER your server is up, re-open uTorrent and make sure that the settings you put in were saved properly. MINIMIZE uTorrent and you should be hosting.
