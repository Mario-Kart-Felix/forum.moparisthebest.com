[size=16pt]Using the Search Button by: therealjdb[/size]

Have you ever needed help with something or just can’t remember something you saw on here?

Now usually you just create a topic and ask your question.
Now what happens?
You get flamed, people tell you to stop being a noob and use the search button.
Search button? What in God’s name is that?
Well it’s this thing in the upper right corner of every page:

Now you think to yourself “WOW, I saw that thing before! But how do I use it?!”

It’s simple, you can learn how to use the search button in a few simple steps. This will make your life much easier.

Step 1. Think of something you want or need to know.

For this tutorial we’ll say that you need to know Moparisthebest rules(i hope everyone should know these).

Step 2. Goto the section of the fourm you want to search, or just goto the Index :P.

Step 3. Click in the Seach Box and Type in “Moparisthebest rules” (without quotes) so that it looks like this:

Step 4. Click Search or hit enter.

You should now get a page saying Seach Results with a list of topics or posts that contain the keyword(s) that you have typed in.

Step 5. Find what you want or something related to what you want.

Congratulations you just used to search button
[size=16pt]
Update: Using the “advanced” search.[/size]

By now I’m sure you have noticed the little magnifying glass icon next to the search button:

It is used to refine your search to give you better results. Today you will learn how to use the “advanced” search feature.

Step 1. Click on the magnifying glass.

This screen should come up now:

Step 2. Enter in what you want to search for, I’ll used moparisthebest rules again.

You start with:

And then you enter in what you want to search for and the user((optional) leave as * to search all users)

Step 3. Configure the options (optional)

Set these to what you like, I don’t use these here:

Step 4. Set what part of the board you want to seach.

First click the plus(+) symbol to expand the your options.

Then select what board(s) you would like to search, here I select the Check all option which searches the whole site:

Step 5. Click Search and browse your results for what you need, if you do not find what you need, change your search options to be more general.
