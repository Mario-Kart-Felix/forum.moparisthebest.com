Cheesehunk July 29, 2016, 8:42pm #6

If your statements with the MouseMovement pattarns have more thaan one thing they are asked to do, you must have a begin… end.

I will give you an example:
Bad Code:

var FX,FY : integer; begin FX:=325; FY:=56; if (GetColor(FX,FY) = 53324) then MoveMouseSmooth(FX,FY); //Would only excecute one statement after it, therefor Clicking the mouse, even if the color isn't found. ClickMouse(FX,FY); end;

Good Code:

var FX,FY : integer; begin FX:=325; FY:=56; if (GetColor(FX,FY) = 53324) then begin MoveMouseSmooth(FX,FY); //Excecute both, because the if statement tells the begin to start, and inside the begin it asks to move, and click the mouse. ClickMouse(FX,FY); end; end;

The spacing is called standards. They are not required, but they make your code look cleaner and more readable. If you want to learn perfect standards, look at some of the Kaitnieks examples that came with SCAR, and watch where the spacing is done.
