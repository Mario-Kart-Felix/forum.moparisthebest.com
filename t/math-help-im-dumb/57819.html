All you have to do is follow the formulas. Notice the formulas have A and that means area. For a.), the cylinder has circles on the sides and a large rectangle wrapping around them. 2 areas are said here: circle and rectangle.

First, find the area of the circle: A = pi*r^2, r = 5
(A = pi (5)^2)

Then, find the area of the rectangle: A = l * w, l = 12, w = 5
(A = 12*5)

From the formula A[size=9pt]1[/size] = A[size=9pt]1[/size] + 2A[size=9pt]b[/size], replace A[size=9pt]1[/size] with area of the rectangle and replace 2A[size=9pt]b[/size] with the area of the circle. The reason why replace 2A[size=9pt]b[/size] with the area of the circle is because there are 2 circles. Now add them up and there is your first answer.
