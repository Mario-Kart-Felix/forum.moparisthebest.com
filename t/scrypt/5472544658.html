[quote=“wildskiller, post:23, topic:547254”][quote author=RSCShadow link=topic=666160.msg4453713#msg4453713 date=1413318444]

Why is it that people are trying to find better ways to encrypt messages when shifting a sequence by a randomly generated key is enough. You have like a 1/1000 trillion million chance to find the key to decode the message.

Also, maybe if the key has been used a certain amount of times, the key could be shuffled.
[/quote]We aren’t finding better ways to encrypt messages, we’re trying to find better ways to store passwords. Encryption implies decryption, which is not something one wishes to do when storing passwords.

Well, how could using password security be better than a private algorithmic encoding? Taking more time to decode shouldn’t be an excuse.[/quote]You can’t decode a hash. That’s the point; it’s one way. Now just because you can’t just follow an algorithm to decode it back to its original form doesn’t mean that it’s not possible to find the original input.

If I have an MD5 and I wish to find what input was used to create it, it would be trivial to test millions of passwords in a very short amount of time. This time’s cut even shorter when you can use multiple GPUs spread across multiple machines. Even if you added a salt, all it does is increase the amount of passwords to try (and when you have a beefy enough machine, it will make little to no impact).

With something like bcrypt, the idea is to have the amount of time it takes to compute be adjustable, and not capable of being performed faster using special hardware (like GPUs). Doing this adds the extra security of not being able to calculate hundreds of hashes within milliseconds.

The point is even if there are trillions of possibilities, if you can test them all extremely quickly then it’s not as secure.

And in case you aren’t familiar where passwords are used, think about how you logged into this website.
