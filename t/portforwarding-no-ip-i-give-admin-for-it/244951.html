Chapter 1 - basic infomation!

Well i made this chapter because if your new to private servers then im guessing you wont know much about them so what is ‘portforwarding’ well if you have a router then your server wont work unless you portforward or it came with open-ports but im not going to go into that but back to the point of this tutorial portforwarding so heres a quick few questions you need to answer before knowing if you need to portforward!

Questions!

  1. Do you have a router?

  2. Do you you know your administrators password? [silly question just bare with me ;)]

well if you can answer them your good to go i will explain about number 2 in a minute

Chapter 2 - Lets get started!

Ahhh! here we go goodluck and before i start if you have any problems please feel free to pm me
Okay now folow these simple steps…

Step 1) Go to run…

Step 2) A box will appear put ‘cmd’ in it…

Step 3) A black box will appear type ‘ipconfig’… infomation such as ip adress will appear!

Step 4) Type your default gateway in your search provider e.g in your adress bar on www.google.com

untitled4546.jpg864×36

Step 5) Almost there now you need to log in! ( i will not provide a picture due to security reasons )
but all you need to do is login with your routers name ( what you named it) and then your password which is most likely ‘admin’

Step 6)
Now when you log on there will be a list one will say ‘portforwarding/port’ or something simular (Once agen for security reasons i can’t post a picture )

Step 7) Well now you need to press ‘add’ or something simular and add your port! it will also say ‘name’ just type any name you will remember then for the port put in ‘43594’ or what ever port you wish ( but i strongly recomend 43594 )

Step 8 ) Now you will see your default gateway at the bottom of the ‘add’ page there will be at the bottom of the number list a empty box just fill the last number of your default gateway in there and click ‘add’ and your done.

Step 9) Try your server! see if it works if it doesn’t there i suggest you have a look in your firewalls
