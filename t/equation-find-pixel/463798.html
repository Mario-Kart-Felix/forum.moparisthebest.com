Am I missing something?

tileWidthPx = screenWidthPx / 11.0
tileCoordPx = playerCoordPx + tileWidthPx * relCoord

Where relCoord is a vector (4,3) in your example and playerCoordPx is the center (x, y) pixel coords of the player’s tile.

Aftermath_961 August 4, 2016, 12:41am #6

They Y value is just the y coordinate of tileCoordPx. There’s potentially an error in what I said above since it assumes the tiles are square.

Basically,
tileYPx = playerYPx + tileHeight * relCoord

relCoord is known (say, “5 spaces above”)
playerYPx is presumably known
tileHeight is calculated by screenHeight / numRowsOnScreen

So we find tileYPx, the pixel value of the center of the tile 5 spaces above you.
