While splines are a great way to make mouse movement look more realistic ask yourself this question: Can I be fooled by splines? If you can watch a mouse and tell the difference between a human-powered mouse and a spline-powered mouse do you think they can too?

Remember, the game can be recorded and played back with a bit of work (on our side) and with ease (on their side). I know, for a fact, that client recording has been done (I have done it and wL has done it). Despite what you may think, the recorded playback files are very small compared to an AVI or mpeg of the same length of time.

With this in mind, splines are nice, but they look hilarious if not done perfectly. If you don’t believe me, use snagit or some other recording software to watch your own mouse movements and tell me you don’t chuckle a little when you play it back. (of course if you have the client playback implemented use that )
