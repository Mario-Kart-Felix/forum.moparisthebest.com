It’s been a while since I last visited my Tupac section of my music library, this made me revisit. He did say once that the stuff he put out like what you see in the video was always the stuff for the clubs, to sell the albums in which he put his other songs on that he actually wanted people to listen to.

I am not a rap or hip hop fan. I used to think I was, but I literally only enjoyed one or two artists and the only rap in my music library is Tupac. The rest of rap is, mostly, juvenile shit. But I like a lot of Tupac’s stuff because he had lyrics you could listen to and find meaning in the lyrics, it is what I like. Of course it isn’t relatable to me, I didn’t grow up in poverty in the ghettos of the US, I grew up well in a good area in the North of England.

These stories or lyrics aren’t relatable and I don’t care. In the same way I don’t need a book or a film to be relatable to me, I enjoy them for the story or the message they’re trying to tell.

Just take the time to listen to a few of these, actually listen to the lyrics. Even in the music aimed at supporting the image the record label was selling, It was certainly far better written than the typical stuff you see in that category.
