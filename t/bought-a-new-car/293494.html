Waffendennis July 31, 2016, 9:53am #12

Thanks everyone.

I only want cars for performance, however the Supra offers best of both worlds.
0-60 4,1 seconds. Top speed 200mph (after you defeat the speed limiter of 250 km/h).

Engine size is big enough for me, I mean it’s pushing out a fair enough of WHP to ‘smoke’ almost any car I see on the road (even talking about some Porsches, Lamborghini’s and Ferrari’s).

Fat Cookies is right. Compared to a American muscle car the Supra is relatively light.
It weights about 1.500KG (3.307 pounds). Out of the factory the car pushes 330 WHP.
The previous owner(s) already did some performance upgrades such as:

-Cold air intake filter
-Free flow and bigger diameter exhaust
-Blitz BOV (blow off valve) with uprated spring
-Removed the two catalytic converters (part of the free flow exhausts)

Supra’s are well known that their power can be uprated very easy (but not cheap hahaha).
According to fellow Supra owners those performance upgrades are bringing the car to near 360/370WHP.

I already have some extra upgrades laying around for the car which are:

  • Upgraded FMIC (front mount intercooler) to replace the OEM intercooler
  • Upgraded intercooler piping to replace the OEM piping
  • Upgraded couplers for the piping
  • Speed limit defeater
  • Boost controller
  • Boost, oil and temp meters to monitor what the car is doing (it won’t be a good thing if I have boost creap or the engine runs to hot for example hahaha)

When that’s installed I will bring the boost a couple of PSI up, let the ECU be remapped to fit the upgrades. Then I hope I will be around 450WHP.
That will be enough for now. The Getrag 6 speed gearbox can hold upto 600BHP. However sources claim that they’re running their Supra’s on 800+ BHP with the Getrag gearbox without any failures so far.

As for the steering wheel, for me it’s on the ‘wrong’ side.
We have the steering wheel on the left side, and drive on the right side.
But I’m used to driving on the ‘wrong’ side (Skyline and the Integra both RHD).
