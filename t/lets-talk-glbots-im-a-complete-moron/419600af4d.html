Aftermath_961 August 2, 2016, 6:46am #86

Speaking of computer vision techniques, I was thinking of using Hough transforms and SIFT to find objects and such - then I realized that it was moronic to use these techniques in circumstances where we have perfect information about what is being drawn or even have simpler visual methods - e.g. color picking or template matching will work just as effectively, if not better, in most cases.

I think graphics library interception + forwarding is the way to go, possibly with a reflection / injection / agent / JVM interceptor approach to get stuff that is not represented on screen [e.g. skills when the tab is not open].

The key benefits:

[ul][li]Perfect graphic information: deeper information than merely what is being drawn on the screen: rather, we know what is being REPRESENTED on the screen [and as a result, this works across all graphics mode, for one][/li]
[li]Reliable and low-maintenance: the same bot code works even after repeated obfuscations and updates of RuneScape[/li]
[li]Limited ways of detection - you know exactly how it can be detected, unlike with approaches involving injection or reflection.[/li][/ul]

Disadvantages:

[ul][li]You are limited to what is being drawn: can’t get otherwise-hidden information as you could with reflection / injection.[/li][/ul]
