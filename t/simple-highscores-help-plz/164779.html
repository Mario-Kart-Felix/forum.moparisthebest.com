Lets start of with creating the menu method it self

Code:
public void mymenu() {
sendQuest(“YourTextHere”,8144);
sendQuest("@yel@YourTextHere",8147);
sendQuest("@gre@YourTextHere",8148);
sendQuest("@cya@YourTextHere",8149);
sendQuest("@red@YourTextHere",8150);
sendQuest("@blu@YourTextHere",8151);
showInterface(8134);
flushOutStream();
}

This is just a starting menu (an easy one in other words)

Q: Where to add it?

A: Add it under

Code:
public class client extends Player implements Runnable {Which is in client.java of course.

Explination about the above menu:

Take a look at

Code:
public void mymenu() {where it says “mymenu” is the name of the menu. It could be similar to “slayermenu” or what ever you wish.

Now look at this:

Code:
sendQuest(“YourTextHere”,8144);This is the title of the menu. (Look at sample at end of tutorial.)

Now the
Code:
sendQuest("@yel@YourTextHere",8147);and all of the sendQuests are the ones showing what text you want to be displayed. As you might have noticed, i wrote @yel@, @red@ and such as which changes the colors of the texts:

Code:
Blank (no @@) = Black
@yel@ = YELLOW
@red@ = RED
@blu@ = BLUE
@gre@ = GREEN
@cya@ = cyan
@whi@ = white

Making a command show the menu:

It’s time to make a command to show your beautiful menu !

Code:
if(command.equalsIgnoreCase(“mymenu”))
{
mymenu();
}
Now the
Code:
if(command.equalsIgnoreCase(“mymenu”))As you probably already know is the name of the command, which you can change to what ever you wish to have.

The
Code:
mymenu();makes sure your menu will show. And if you named your method (your menu above) to a different name, you should make it say your name of the method instead of “mymenu();” it would be “YourMethodName();”.
