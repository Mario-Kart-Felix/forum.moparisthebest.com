[u]Step One: Connecting To Your Xbox
Ok so there’s two ways you can do this. Either FTP the game to your PC, or download it from the internet.
Now here’s how you would FTP it to your PC.

[ul][]Your Xbox has an Ethernet Cable port on it, so connect your Ethernet Cable to it on one end, and on the other, to your router.
[]Start up your Xbox, let it get to it’s Modded Dashboard. Now, open your disk tray and insert your game.
[]Open up Smart FTP, for address, type in your Xbox’s IP Address (It should say somewhere on the dashboard, usualy 192.168.1.xxx. Now xxx can be between 100-140)
[]For Username and Password, it is xbox. It’s always going to be that.
[*]Once it has connected to your Xbox, go to the D drive. This is the cd drive, you will see a bunch of data on there. We are going to Download it to your PC.[/ul]

[u]Step Two: Getting The Game To Your PC

[ul][]Now that your hopefuly connected to your PC, and in your D drive folder, lets move on.
[]Right click on your D drive folder, select Download>Direct>Select Folder.
[]Now it will ask you were to download the stuff to, so tell it, I have a special folder on my PC especialy for games, it’s a good idea to do this. Keeps you organized.
[]Ok, so once you specify a folder, it will just start downloading everything in the D drive, which is what we want. Depending on how big the game is, this could take 10-30 minutes. But since you are directly connected to it, you should get around 10 mb/s.[/ul]

[u]Step Three: Creating The Iso
In order for your Xbox to read the data off the disk, you have to have it as an Iso. So if you have been just burning the data straight onto a disk, you need to read this.

[ul][]So you have the game on your PC, lets take this stuff and make it useful.
[]Open up Qwix. Go to “Create Iso”. You should see three options. I will explain what these are and how they work.
Option 1.Local Folder - This is where if you have just the regular data on your PC, it will take the folder specified and create an Iso out of the data given.
Option 2.FTP Folder - Here, it will take the data specified off of an FTP folder on your Xbox, and create an Iso out of this. I cannot seem to get this option to work, therefore don’t recommend it
Option 3.Convert Iso - Now let’s say you download an Iso off of the internet. Well there is a slight chance that the Iso ITSELF could be damaged. But not the data (In some cases) So I recommend doing this everytime you download an Iso off the internet. Select the Iso you downloaded, then it will create a new Iso with the data off of the old one.
