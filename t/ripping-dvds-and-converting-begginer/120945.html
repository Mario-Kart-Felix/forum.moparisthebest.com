Very basic way of ripping and converting use DVD Decrypter to rip the main .VOB movie file to your HDD and then use a converter to convert it to your preffered format.

Step 1:[/size]
First insert the DVD you want to rip and convert into your DVD drive and open DVD Decrypter when it is inserted:

Then select
Edit > Select main movie PGC

Then click the Stream Processing tab. And Enable Stream Processing

Then finally click the Decrypt button

For me it usually takes 4-6hours to decrypt a DVD but this will vary because of DVD drive reading speed (How fast it can read the disc).
When it is done find where it ripped to (Usually a folder on C: Drive). And find the main VOB (Will be between 4-9GB).

[size=10pt][b][u]Step 1: Installing[/b][/u][/size]
Install:
-CladDVD
-CladBRC
-VirtualDUB
but not DVD2AVI because it’s an executable no need to install. Remember you need the Xvid or DivX codec. (You must probably have it if you can play .avi files)
[b][u][size=18]Step 2: Ripping the DVD[/b][/u][/size]
Remember you may want to use DVD Decrypter instead
Open up CladDVD:

Choose the tab: “PGC Mode [AVI]”. Normally CladDVD will select the files for you. The file you are looking for is usually the longest (time) one. Click on “Options” on the right and choose your destination folder. Then Click start rip and the ripping will begin.

[size=10pt][b][u]Step 3:Calculating the bit rate[/b][/u][/size]

Find out the length of your movie (Own knowledge, back of DVD cover or you can use http://IMDB.com/). Insert the number of minutes into the textbox and click go.

Now you can choose which configuration you want. The 1 and 2 stands for the number of files you will have. 1 Means 1 avi file, 2 means 2 avi files. Choose for yourself what’s best. Off course, when you choose 700Mb, the quality of the file(s) will be better…

[size=10pt][b][u]Step 4:From VOB to .AVI[/b][/u][/size]
Open DVD2AVI:

Click File > Open, Then select one VOB file. DVD2AVI will automatically select the other VOB files needed unless there is only one.

Note: If you move the slidebar a little you will see the movie.

If you notice black borders on the top or bottom or on the sides. You may want to get rid of them. If you do follow these steps:
Click on video > Clip & resize

66emvxg.jpg725×514

63s245i.jpg727×517

Choose the video aspect you want.
Set the filter type to: “precise bicubic”
Close the window

Now save the AVI file go to file > Save AVI

66txg1k.jpg727×517

and type in a filename

After you have done that a pop-up window will appear, asking for compression methods. Choose DivX or XviD for the compressor (Or whatever you want)Then click Configure.


(This ^^ screen may vary depending on your codec). Now change the bit-rate box to what you calculated earlier and any other things you may want to change.
Press OK
Then press OK again
This process can take a while 1-3hours+

[size=10pt][b][u]Step 5:Combining the AVI and WAV[/b][/u][/size]
Open VirtualDUB.
As you may have realized DVD2AVI has created two files. The video file and the sound file. You must combine these two.
Click file > Open Video File (choose the file you just made)

After that click Video > Direct Stream Copy

After that, click Audio > WAV Audio

And find the WAV audio DVD2AVI just created

Now go to Audio again and click “Full processing Mode” (Because you have to change some things)

Now click Audio > Interleaving

Now change “Interleave Audio every 1 frames” into “Interleave Audio every 500 ms”

Now go to Audio > Compression

And Select: Mpeg Layer-3; 128kbits/s 48kHz Stereo 16kB/s. Press “OK”.
Note: Keep the kHz at 48 but you can change 160kbit/s instead of 128kbit/s but remember this will make your file larger.

Now go to Audio > Volume, Set it to 200%

Now go to File > Save AVI

After this just type the file name in and you’re done. Any Questions? Just post.
