To learn your way around the sequencer, and to gain basic knowledge of the hardware if you’re using it and plugins if you’re not, yeah sure you can do that in a month. However, the talent in electronic music production comes from composition and sound design. (Sure, you need that for traditional music too, but electronic producers tend to have more awareness of structures etc. than your average guitarist in my experience.) You can tell a novice producer from a more experienced one from the sounds, especially if they do what most newbies do and use presets.

I never said, and nor will you ever hear me say, that playing a real instrument does not take practice, skill and dedication. (I play guitar and piano). However, I only ask that other people drop this silly idea that making electronic music is just “learning a program”.

By the way, by “synchronising all the instruments” I can only assume you mean playing in time… not to be harsh, but that’s the easiest bit, if you can’t keep time you really can’t call yourself a musician.
