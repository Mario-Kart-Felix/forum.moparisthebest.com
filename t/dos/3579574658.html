Without knowing what type of attack you’re being hit by it is neigh on impossible to help you mitigate the attack. Some things you can do to find out what attack you’re being hit by are:

Trying to ping your server’s IP address when you’re being attacked - if you get high packet loss or complete packet loss then your server is either running out of bandwidth (udp flood) or shitting itself which could be caused by almost anything tbh ;p, it’s best to keep a remote desktop connection open to your server at all times so that you can see what it is doing (or not doing).

Checking apache’s log files - What you’re looking at here is anything which looks ‘out of the ordinary’, like the useragent string (if you’re not logging it, log it). A lot of bots won’t have any user agent (grab their IPs from the log and ban them, I did it for >1 year w/o a problem). You may also see lots of hits from custom UAs hitting the same page, you should grab their ips and ban them and then look at the page they’re attacking to see if you can recode it to be more efficient and therefore less susceptible to attack.

There’s way too many things that you can check for me to list them all here, but If anyone wants me to seriously secure their boxes as a network consultant or other adhoc work, give me a shout (:
