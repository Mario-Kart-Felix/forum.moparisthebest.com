SomethingToHide July 31, 2016, 4:28am #12

Everything that was just said was wrong basically.

GPU’s do not help in the rendering of this. Google some info about the aspects of rendering.

GPU’s ARE used in the rendering of games. GPU’s AREN’T used for rendering from a 3D app.

It’s WAY more complicated than I think a GPU can handle. Millions of rays are sent from sources and have to bounce around realistically according to complex algorithms and sometimes double when bounced. Each ray is influenced by the angle of a bounce, all the material properties, how many times it’s bounced, etc.

It’s actually a lot slower for a GPU to handle. Game engines are heavily optimized and baked and made for GPU’s to handle, while the CPU is doing a bunch of other shit. Render engines are CPU and RAM users. Not GPU’s.

None of this stuff is real time. I see almost no use for a GPU in rendering besides an aid. I highly doubt it will be GPU centered later on, unless we start seeing some monster multicore fast as shit card.

SomethingToHide July 31, 2016, 4:28am #14

No. It’s not what games do. There’s a difference.

GPU’s work with games by rasterization.

CPU’s work with calculating everything for the final image by raytracing.

It’s all done building on the basics of raytracing. GPU’s don’t do it that way.

Raytracing is more realistic. It’s how reality works and that’s why it’s superior in this case.

GPU’s rasterizing uses a shitload of layers of things like shadows, lighting, atmosphere, everything to make it look real. It fakes reality.
Not what we’re trying to achieve in this case.

There’s also a lot of reasons why it’s taking so long for GPU’s to even be thought of for stuff like this. GPU’s are proprietary. CPU’s aren’t.

GPU manufacturers don’t really release all the stuff going on in the inside to make it programmable.
