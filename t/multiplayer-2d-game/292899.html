ownagesbot July 31, 2016, 9:48am #7

Yeah, you could do something like this:

length / opcode / operands

(example): 2 56 79

Where 2 is the length, 56 is the opcode (maybe the code for “Attack”) and 79 is an operand (maybe the players index i.e. 79).
Obviously, this small example has limitations, the fact that the length may not exceed 255 bytes, nor may the opcode be larger than the byte 255. If you think this will cover your needs then by all means go for it. Another approach could be the following:

length (2 bytes), opcode (2bytes), operands

This is pretty similar to before, only this time you are going to have 2 val for length/opcode this allows your length to be up to 65535. And opcodes up to 65535. (Providing you read into an unsigned short)
(Clearly each packet you send like this will be an additional 2 bytes, so it may effect performance).
As far as im aware, RSC atleast had no more than 255 opcodes and manipulated the raw data to see if the length of the next packet was 1 or 2 bytes. I guess they did this for optimization.

Anyway, gives you something to think about I guess.

To be honest, the way I would go, is somewhat like RuneScape. You could also steal the Stream class from there, too. Basically, have a packet ID. Send the needed data from there. For example, you could do something like this to draw all the other people on the screen (Send it to each client).

The packet ID (Byte) Amount of players in the area (Byte) (Start looping for each player) The player gender (Byte) The distance (X) from you to the other player (Byte) The distance (Y) from you to the other player (Byte) The player face (Byte) (End loop)

If you wanted to make things easier, you could include the packet size after the packet ID, or you can just store the packet sizes in an array or list for later use.

Basically, upon a new client connection, after the login handshake(s) have been fulfilled, the server sends a packet similar to the one Lin posted. This packet contains all of the players that are within the current region (in other words, the players that are drawable) along with their characteristics. The client will have an array of “Player” objects, and will add each one according to the packet’s specifications. Then, in your drawing method, you will iterate through the player array and draw the players one-by-one according to their location on the screen and map.

Each time a player walks, the client will send the respective walk packet to the server, the server determines which players are within the region and sends the packet to them. The client will proceed to read the packet, identify the moved player and changed its location.
