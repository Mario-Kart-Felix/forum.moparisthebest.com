Yz85Racer August 3, 2016, 11:33pm #24

Despite the fact I hate this topic; I’m bored and thought I’d compose a post. For your goals, get into the habit of developing Micro/Macro cycles. Micro cycles are your week long goals, meso cycles are your 6-8 week goal, and macro cycle is your annual goal.

Develop strength first. Do pure strength strength. I’m talking, 1MR sets, with long distanced breaks in between.

Turn this strength, into power. Yes, your strength, and power, are completely different.
Strength - the extent to which muscles can exert force by contracting against resistance
Power - the ability to exert maximum muscular contraction instantly in an explosive burst of movements. The two components of power are strength and speed. (e.g. jumping or a sprint start)
This means, lower your weight to about 80% 1MR, and perform the weight lifting action hard and fast 8-10 times. Decent breaks in between.

This complete your second meso cycle, and you will be stronger, more powerful, have developed decent muscle and increased your muscle’s workability.

Now, your 3rd meso cycle should be to develop tone. This will turn those sized muscles, into chiseled pieces of body.
This means, lower the weight even more, about 60-70%, and perform the weight action slow - especially on the negative. Your sets should consist of 15-20 reps, performed slow.

Congrats, you’ve arrived where you are. Never forget the importance of cardiovascular endurance too. Not only does it assist in training your heart, it will actually help you tone up. Also, your diet should be #1. Remember, you don’t grow in the gym - you grow in the gym. “tear the muscle in the gym and have the discipline to repair it in the kitchen.”
