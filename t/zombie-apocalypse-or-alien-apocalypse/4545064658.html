Elrick August 4, 2016, 2:38am #40

Zombie apocalypse. Assuming genetic mutations/Everyone is infected ordeals are out of the question, the zombies would have a rather short lifespan. Surely a decent amount of damage would be done and it would take a very long time before society was even able to recuperate to the point where government began reinitializing itself and regaining full control over its people. However due to muscular degeneration and lack of thought process, these zombies would be going on feral instinct and in its later stages would be going after people at a very slow rate.

Obviously their most basic and lasting sense would be hearing, so one would need to be quiet when attacking/hiding. In its earlier stages zombies may be able to smell and track humans, however they would still need to be able to find a way into households that do not involve cracking the code to the garage and opening the back door. The only issue I would be concerned about is muscle memory and such, as in their body remembering what specific things such as door knobs do when turning them. In the end they would not pose much of a threat compared to aliens.
