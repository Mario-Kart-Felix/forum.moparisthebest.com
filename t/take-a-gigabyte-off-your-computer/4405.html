Take a Gigabyte off your Computer! by turning off hibernation. You can take more than a gigabyte off your computer depending on your memory. Some people have a gig, 2 gigs, 3 gigs, and more of memory!

First to see that I’m not lying. Go to My Computer and right click on your C:\ Drive and write down how many gigabytes of used space you have. I have 12.5 before I did hibernation.

Now to turn off hibernation (completely safe don’t worry) just do this…

  1. Right-click on the desktop and click on “Properties”.
  2. Click on the “Screen Saver” tab.
  3. Click the “Power” button under “Monitor Power”.
  4. Click on the “Hibernation” tab, then uncheck the box next to “Enable Hibernation”.
  5. Press “OK” and you have just saved a load of hard drive space!

Source: http://www.geekstogo.com/forum/Easy_XP_Tweaks-t2780.html

Restart your computer, and then go to My Computer and right click on your C:\ Drive once again and compare your new used up space to your old one.
