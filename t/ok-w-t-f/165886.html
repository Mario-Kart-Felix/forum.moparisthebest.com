akgamer0615 July 30, 2016, 5:52pm #16

If it’s completely shutting off your computer, then either there is software (maybe moparscape, or a program someone running with moparscape) telling the computer to shut off, or there could be hardware errors.

To test if it’s a software issue, delete moparscape and reinstall it; if it works, software was the problem. If not, it’s probably your computer.

How many hertz does your computer run at (start-control panel-system)? And how much RAM (listed next to hertz) do you have?

If moparscape requires more hertz to run than your computer can provide (remember, the CPU is processing other tasks at the same time, so if the amount needed to run moparscape and the amount of hertz your computer runs at at close, you may not have enough hertz).
It could also be caused by overheating. After your computer shuts off, do you have to wait a little bit before it will work? Does the computer case feel hot at all? If either of these is true, then a computer part is overheating; could be your CPU.
