New ubuntu failed market ttly

Technology
Ron July 31, 2016, 1:07pm #21

Yeah to be honest, I’m not too happy with Karmic Koala. It’s probably the worse OS to come from Canonical ever. I wish they would stop their 6 month release cycle and only release a new OS when it is STABLE. I’m so tired of them hyping up a new OS, it comes out and it’s filled with bugs. This is the reason I left Microsoft several years ago.

In my experience of the 9.10 on my HP pavilion laptop:

  • I upgraded for one reason, they finally fixed full screen flash video
  • No sound - Kind of fixed, I have to reload the alsa drivers each time
  • Sleep mode crashes the computer - Fixed
  • Wireless inactivity crashes the computer - Fixed
  • Cannot change the login screen without killing the GDM - Not fixed
  • the new boot loader is kind of annoying that it doesn’t use menu.lst and you need to actually edit the bash code to remove entries or delete the old kernel files from /boot/. - Not fixed
  • GParted will not let me resize my ubuntu/vista partitions unless I reinstall the partition table which wipes out all my information. I might as well run fdisk and reinstall both operating systems.
  • Banshee included to get rid of rhythmbox. I am not a fan of banshee, they will stick around for a while unlike rhythmbox but their current version needs work and their LastFM plugin is an abomination compared to rhythmbox’s.
  • Since 9.04 (maybe even before) the bug report tool is in the menu list but refuses to start up. Used to work fine but now they say it’s strictly a command line tool and needs a few more passing arguments for it to launch. That’s retarded.

Also, I reinstalled Mac4Lin and rhythmbox stopped working. Worked fine on previous versions of ubuntu with Mac4Lin installed, but Karmic still has problems. If anyone wants to have a stable system for a while, stick with 9.04.

I’ve read a lot of those articles and honestly, I do not think they’re bullshit. It was kind of shameful that so many laptops like my own, experienced the same shit right when Canonical was competing with the new operating systems from Mac and Microsoft. On a college campus, everyone has a laptop and if the young are the ones experiencing the most problems, promoting Linux would continue to be a problem.

I may switch to fedora.

~Ron
