BobDole81 July 30, 2016, 2:10am #7

Kepler worked on the orbits of the planets around the sun, while Newton provided an explanation for the force holding the planets in their orbit.

Kepler stated that the planets move in elliptical orbits, that speed around a specific part of an orbit is proportional to the distance from the sun at that time (worked with the area between two regions on the orbital plane), and that the overall period of rotation is proportional to the average distance from the sun (one is squared, one is cubed…forget which).

Newton’s law of universal gravitation explains, mathematically, the gravitational forces between two objects in an isolated environment (obviously in real life there are many forces exerted on a single celestial object). This law allowed for explanation of the orbits of the planets, the orbit of the moon, and even the changing tides on earth.
