Third grandmaster quest -- a review

Games
Runescape
mod_taharok August 1, 2016, 12:13am #1

For lack of a better place to put this right now, we see the third arrival of a RuneScape Grandmaster quest. Worth it? I’d like to think not.

If you head over to the wikia, you can see the requirements of the quest are phenomenal. You need 80 magic, the highest requisite of any quest to date, among other level 70 requirements. You end up going through some seemingly challenging logic puzzles (easily remedied by a decent quest guide), and fighting a “monstrous” level 599. Comparable to Nomad? Not in a lifetime.

Don’t get me wrong, the queen seems pretty tough. She likes using all forms of combat, fights among elite pest control defilers, and has a special attack that hits 500 (but this can be blocked using a special from a weapon you get in the quest). Based on a video I watched, the queen seems to have lifepoints similar to Nomad, which would explain the high level. I would even argue she may have more lifepoints than Nomad, but this is to be determined.

As for the rewards, not so good if you ask me. You get about 120k experience spread over six skills, then two sets of 50k additional experience for any combat levels over 75. You get an elite Void torso, which is an “upgraded” version of the regular torso (which is, to say, it adds a decent +4 prayer bonus). You get a lousy one quest point, and Korasi’s sword, which is used against the queen.

In fact, out of all the awards, the only remotely decent one is Korasi’s sword. This new weapon replaces the leaf-bladed sword as the highest stab attack weapon in the game, outside of Daemonheim. It has slightly better accuracy than the leaf-bladed sword (+8 slash, -7 in stab), but has a whopping +20 difference in strength. Korasi’s sword actually surpasses the dragon scimitar in strength by +4. It is weaker than the abyssal whip by 12. Nevertheless, it has the same speed as all three.

Some quick opinions about the quest seem to be along the lines of a disappointment that no new boss monster was added and it being considered grandmaster. Otherwise, I think the sword is the only reason worth doing the quest in the first place, and quite a good reason at that.
