[center]This guide is to teach those of you who need help how to edit/make models. I will try to explain this in the simplest way I can, but I will also be willing to help if you need help.

Now to get started.

[SIZE=4]Step 1 - Getting your basic “supplies”[/SIZE]

There are a few key items you will need in order to get into model editting/making. The first thing, is a model editor. There are several model editors, but the best one for beginners is most likely Metasequoia. This 3D model editting/building program is very basic, but it gets the job done.

Download MetaSequoia 2.48 by clicking this link: http://www.metaseq.net/metaseq/metaseq248.zip

If that doesn’t work, go to www.metaseq.net and scroll down until you see MetaSequoia 2.48, download that.
[center]________________________[/center]

Now, you will also need a model grabber. I use a tool I downloaded from a post, and it works very well. It grabs all the models from the current RuneScape cache and places them in a folder named “models”. It also has about 16 other folders with models from other caches, most likely sub caches, but they are all in the models folder.

In this download I have also included ClientHax’s RSMV modified to view all models, so no more invalid formats.

Download this tool by clicking this link: http://uppit.com/XI5PH6, or http://www.mediafire.com/?jjnyyj2jnix if one doesn’t work, try the other.
[center]________________________[/center]

Here is an itemdump.txt that tells you what models are for what item. Heres a simple guideline to follow.

class8.anIntArray174 - Ground/inventory model
class8.anIntArray165 - Male equip model
class8.anIntArray200 - Female equip model

Download itemdump.txt by clicking this link: http://uppit.com/FKFDD7
[center]________________________[/center]

You will also DatMaker by Mr Potatohead in order to convert each .mdl to .mqo in order for you to edit.

Download DatMaker 1.3 & More by clicking this link: http://uppit.com/AFF3G7

[center]________________________[/center]

Make a folder name “Models” or something like that anywhere, desktop, documents, etc. And extract all those tools into that folder, this way they aren’t spread out everywhere.

Once you have downloaded all the tools, open the model grabber and start running “export new cache”, the dos file. Wait for it to finish, and then you have all 42k models from the latest RuneScape cache plus a model viewer to view them all.
[center]_____________________________________________[/center]

[SIZE=4]Step 2 - Getting a model you want and converting it to .mqo format[/SIZE]

Now that you have all your models, your tools, and everything you need, lets learn how to convert a model you want into a format for you to edit it in MetaSequoia. Open up RSMV and open the “models” folder in RSMV. Start looking through your models.

Now lets say you want to convert a Dragon chainbody into .mqo format for you to edit. You see that the model is 3820, now open up the “models” folder and copy that model, 3820.mdl.

Now go into your DatMaker folder, and paste(ctrl + v) the model in that folder. Open DatMaker(the program), and select that model, 3820.

Now, select File, and hover over Export, and select MQO. Like this:

I suggest making a folder named MQO Models right now so you can put all your .mqo models in here for easy access. Copy the converted model (in this case 3820.mqo) and paste it in your MQO Models folder.
[center]_____________________________________________[/center]

[SIZE=4]Step 3 - Editting the model in MetaSequoia[/SIZE]

This step is going to be sort of a long step, because there are many things I am going to show you to make your model editting/building experience easier for you.

First of all, open MetaSequoia. Make sure you have a converted model in your MQO Models folder. You may be asked if you would like to start up in basic mode or some other mode, choose which one you think you’d work best in for a beginner.

Now Open the .mqo model(ctrl + o, or File -> Open), and it will open the model you have selected. Now is the part that is tricky to people, so I’m going to explain how to select a tool, and what the most commonly used tools are, and what they do.

[u]Navigation

This is probably the hardest and most confusing part of MetaSequoia for people to get used to.

To rotate your screen/view of the object, hold down your right mouse button, OR click and hold this button at the top right hand corner of your toolbar, and move your mouse.

To Zoom in/out, press and hold the left and right mouse buttons, and drag your mouse in or out. You can also press and hold this button and do the same thing.

To move the position of the screen, or where you are looking at, press and hold this button down, and move your mouse around where you want to go.

Try fooling around with these to get used to them, for faster movement and navigation.

[u]Tools

I will be posting links for most of the pictures, due to the 10 pictures per post limit.

To select a tool, go click the Command panel, on the left side of the screen. It should open up and you should see this:

Now, to explain what everything means and does.

The Select tool - Allows you to select one point on the object, also allows you to deselect one point when holding down CTRL if more than one point is selected
http://i34.tinypic.com/2ivbrfd.jpg

The Rect tool - Rect stands for Rectangle. This tool is like the Rectangle tool in MS Paint, or PhotoShop. It allows you to select a certain area of points, lines, or faces all at once.
http://i38.tinypic.com/wlzm7q.jpg

The Rope tool - This tool allows you to select multiple points, lines, or faces, but it allows you to select any “shape” you want, so you are not stuck with a rectangular selection.
http://i38.tinypic.com/2qnyzio.jpg

The Move tool - This allows you to move any selected point(s) anywhere you wish.
http://i34.tinypic.com/1y64xj.jpg

The Scale tool - This tool allows you to change the scale of the selected face(s), or object overall. Scale is another word for size.
http://i35.tinypic.com/2gshbnc.jpg

The Rot tool - [i]This tool allows you to rotate any selected face(s) in any direction using the tools handle, which you will see when using the tool.
http://i34.tinypic.com/i23qrk.jpg

The Local tool - This tool is a very handy tool. It allow you to move, rotate, or change the scale of the selected object without having to select all the points.
http://i33.tinypic.com/2rm6y38.jpg

The UV and Map tools - You will not be using these tools just yet
Picture not required

The Primitive tool - This tool allows you to create objects of any shape, size, color, scale, segments, anything you wish. Experiment with this and you’ll be on your way to making custom models.

The Create tool - You won’t be needing this for now
Picture not required

The Del tool - This tool deletes any face you click on

The Invert tool - Deletes the face you click the first time, click again to add the face again

The Extrude tool - Pulls or pushes the selected point(s)/face(s) in or out
http://i36.tinypic.com/vyljrl.jpg

The Mat tool - Changes the color of the face you click to the color selected in your MAT Panel
http://i38.tinypic.com/23ijsc0.jpg

[u]Editting the Model

Editting the model is the most fun part of model editting/building! Its where you can actually put your creativity to work. I’m going to show you how to take a simple platebody and make it into your desired creation.

You can start with a basic platebody model, model 306.

Now, lets say I want to make a version 1.0 of a dragon platebody. Well, we need the “Dragon” red color, so press CTRL + I to insert a new object, and insert that dragon chainbody, or any dragon item you have. After inserting it, you will have the red(s) required to make your platebody look like a platebody. Delete the dragon object from your object panel, and you’ll still have the colors.

Now select the Mat tool, and click on all the faces you want the “lighter” red. So it looks like this.

Now, color the rest of the platebody how you want, perhaps so it looks like this.

Now, you have the basic concept of your platebody. The platebody itself, and the colors. Now, you need something else… But what else… Spikes! You want some spikes on the shoulders! Well, that can easily be done!

Go to your object panel and click new, it should say “obj1”.

Select your Primitive tool, and select the triangular prism. Mess around with the scale and segments, so maybe its going to look like this.

Click “New” in your Mat panel, and select the new mat, its white, make sure its selected

Now click “Create” in the Primitive panel. You have now created a spike.

Now you need to rotate it to fit the shape of the shoulder. Select the Rot tool, and rotate it in the directions you need to make it fit the shoulder. It should look something like this.

You now have a spike on your dragon platebody. Now, if you want one on the other shoulder, simply right click obj1, and select Mirror Seperated. This will mirror the spike perfectly positioned on the other shoulder. Now your dragon platebody is complete!

I’m not going to spoon feed you every little thing, but by now, you should have an idea of how to customize models to your specifications.

Enjoy your model editting/building, and if you need help, simply post on this thread; I’m sure someone is willing to help!
