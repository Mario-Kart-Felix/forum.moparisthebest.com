dylan_0789 August 3, 2016, 2:50am #14

Love how it was modeled for the most part. Design, not so much. Just nit-picking, but the blade (specifically the “floating” section) has too many edge loops. I get that you want it as rounded as it could be, but you always need to think of what the model is going to be used for. In a RS-client, you will never notice the extra loops-worth of smoothing, and in some cases RS-client rendering will merge close edgeloops that are insignificant, causing a “rippled” effect along certain edges (which is a problem I had with my oldest model releases here). That being said, you could remove two edge loops in the handle with a miniscule difference in how its rounded. Same with the handle now that I think about it. Keep in mind that my nit-picking over loops is just to teach efficiency to some extent.

All in all, nice work. I would say your modeling skills are fairly decent, although I can only judge so much from something basic.

the_wanderer August 3, 2016, 2:50am #15
dylan_0789:

RS-client rendering will merge close edgeloops that are insignificant, causing a “rippled” effect along certain edges

that’s cuz it uses fixed-point arithmetic and will join the vertices together if the distance is too close (modeling software uses floating-point, so the values are truncated).

Kind of a funny thing, but in the rs client they use 4 sides to represent a cylinder. In reality it is more like a cube.

Protip: Turn degree of shading to 120 for rs shading
