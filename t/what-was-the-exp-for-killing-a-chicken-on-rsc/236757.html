system July 31, 2016, 2:22am #8

The exact formula for any NPC in RSC is the following (the formula I added to Geminus):

(Comabat Level * 2) + 20

And yes, for killing a Chicken, it is 19.5. I get that by using my formula aswell. Here is how to calculate it.

A Chicken is level 3, so the formula for a Chicken would be the following:

(3 * 2) + 20

If you know math, you can see that it will end up to 26 pretty fast. 26 is the total experience for a Chicken. But that also includes Hits. So what you have to do, is the following:

Hits = 26 / 4, which is 6.5.

There are two options for the other skills (Attack, Defence and Strength), namely, the skill, or controlled…

Controlled mode: The experience = 26 / 4, which is 6.5.
The skill mode: The experience = (26 / 4) * 3, which is 19.5.

Also, don’t ask whether this formula is accurate, or not, because it is totally accurate (I did check against the real RSC when I developed Geminus).

Zian: Your formula is very redundant… Why do you use “getCombatLevel() * 0” anyway? This will always end up 0, so removing it would never make any difference. Also, if you only keep it at “10 * 0.8”, then it will always become 8. So really, I don’t see what is so good about this formula? It is everything but close to the real RSC-formula.

Zian: Okay, I will do that… You know there are four different skills (Attack, Defence, Strength and Hits), right?

You can get experience to all of these skills, or to two of them (at any given time). The different combinations are shown below:

  1. Attack (3/4) and Hits (1/4)
  2. Defence (3/4) and Hits (1/4)
  3. Strength (3/4) and Hits (1/4)
  4. Controlled: Attack (1/4), Defence (1/4), Strength (1/4) and Hits (1/4)

So, as you can see, you need to divide by 4 to have the base-experience.

Take for instance the Chicken you were talking about…

Chicken is level 3, whereas the total experience will become “(3 * 2) + 20”, which equals to 26. Now I guess, 26 isn’t really what you expected, is it? But if you divide this number (26) by 4, you will have a base-experience of “6.5”.

Now you can apply the base-experience differently, depending on what you are training… The following are the only possabilities you have:

  1. Attack (19.5) and Hits (6.5)
  2. Defence (19.5) and Hits (6.5)
  3. Strength (19.5) and Hits (6.5)
  4. Controlled: Attack (6.5), Defence (6.5), Strength (6.5) and Hits (6.5)

This is all information I can give, I am afraid (as there really is no more), so I hope you can work it out from there.

Also, NOTE: You do only get experience after you have killed the NPC, not by every hit. I don’t know where you got that impression from, by reading my message. Now you know that, atleast.
