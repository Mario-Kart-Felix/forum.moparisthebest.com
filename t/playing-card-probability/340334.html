A playing card is drawn from the pack of 52 Cards, It is then replaced, The pack is then shuffled and then another card is drawn. Find the probability that :-

[ul][li]The two cards were both Clubs[/li]
[li]The two cards were both Red Kings[/li]
[li]The first card was a two and the second card was red[/li]
[li]One card was a Jack and the other card was red[/li]
[li]One card was a Club and the other was a Queen[/li][/ul]

bowserjr2 July 31, 2016, 4:31pm #2

  1. The two cards were both Clubs

  2. The two cards were both Red Kings

  3. The first card was a two and the second card was red

  4. One card was a Jack and the other card was red

  5. One card was a Club and the other was a Queen

A = Clubs.
P(A ∩ A) = 1/4 * 1/4 = 1/16
2.
A = Red King
P(A ∩ A) = 1/26 * 1/26 = 1/676
3.
A = Two
B = Red
P(A ∩ B) = 1/13 * 1/2 = 1/26

Now, do the rest yourself.
