RiiPiiNFtW:

Wish I was advanced as other people. I just have simple CS5 knowledge and how to make a cool logo.

Here are some basic tips that helped me learn before I took classes for photoshop.
[ul][li]Use layers as much as possible, makes it easier to make changes, and able to make more advanced images[/li]
[li]Shortcut keys are your best friend, know these to speed up[/li]
[li]Filters - Apply these multiple times for different effects (layering different filters can make a unique effect)[/li]
[li]Opacity + different layer modes can be very useful[/li]
[li]Masks - These are your best friend when it comes to limiting the picture in a layer to a certain area, applying an antialias effect etc.[/li]
[li]Pen tool - Possibly the most complained about and most useful tool I have found. You can make some amazing shapes, or pull your hair out trying to get used to it. This tool is very useful after you get the hang of it.[/li][/ul]

Other than that, look up small videos on youtube, download some brushes. Its really just getting used to layers and the pen tool that will be your biggest downfall.
