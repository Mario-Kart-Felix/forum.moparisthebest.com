I must not have made myself clear, sorry. I was trying to summarize everything up. Here, let me try a little more detailed summary:

First of all, everyone will have the oppertunity to help out, if they so desire to. This includes people from BOTH websites. Each person (except higher leveled coders such as MoparScape CMs and Community Members) will have to start off at an early stage of the subsidery development team. Now, he or she needs to work their way up if they want to be on a higher-level development team.

Now, one might think that, “Hey, they will just ruin everything up because they do not know anything”. Two very easy solutions. The first one is quite simple, indeed. They most likely will not stay on the team very long if they do not know what they are doing, or are not serious about it. The second plan is to include a suggestions board for all members to post suggestions. We will have a Desiging Team (harder team to be accepted on) that will handle all of the suggestions. This will allow those who cannot code still help out in creating it.

“What about releasing it too soon and there be a load of errors, or little testing” Well, that is why we will be using a system of ‘Release Candidates’ to organized and plan which versions are released to the public and which are kept for Alpha/Beta testing. Alpha testing will of course be the testing right after the code is implemented. This testing occurs on every update. The Beta testing stage will be a test for all MoparScape CMs/Community Members and the leaders of all the Development Teams and of the Designing Team. If it successfully passes Beta test, it may be held until another version is made, or it will be a new Release Candidate.
