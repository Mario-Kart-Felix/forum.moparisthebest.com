T41 August 1, 2016, 12:46pm #1
Moral nihilism, also known as ethical nihilism, is the meta-ethical view that morality does not exist as something inherent to objective reality; therefore no action is necessarily preferable to any other. For example, a moral nihilist would say that killing someone, for whatever reason, is not inherently right or wrong. Other nihilists may argue not that there is no morality at all, but that if it does exist, it is a human and thus artificial construction, wherein any and all meaning is relative for different possible outcomes. As an example, if someone kills someone else, such a nihilist might argue that killing is not inherently a bad thing, bad independently from our moral beliefs, only that because of the way morality is constructed as some rudimentary dichotomy, what is said to be a bad thing is given a higher negative weighting than what is called good: as a result, killing the individual was bad because it did not let the individual live, which was arbitrarily given a positive weighting. In this way a moral nihilist believes that all moral claims are false.
tl;dr: are there really such things as universal morals and such?

lothy August 1, 2016, 12:46pm #3

Morality is certainly artificial in the sense that it is a social construct of humans alone.

Consider these four dichotomies: good and bad, right and wrong, strong and weak, winning and losing.
Obviously this discussion concerns itself with the first two, but these four dichotomies are ingrained in every member of society from a young age through education and discipline.

In the animal world, none of those exist. Animals merely do what they must to survive.
For example, the lion hunts and kills various wildlife when it needs to eat. But at other times, it doesn’t. It doesn’t hunt them at certain times of day because it’s bad, nor does it demonstrate compassion at other times of day when it leaves other animals in peace – it merely eats when it needs to so that it survives.

At the end of the day, humans weren’t so different. Nowadays we centre our existence around compassion, and so it is natural to teach people that it’s wrong to harm others.
If you hear or say something often enough, you come to believe it.
And that’s what morality is really – a set of ideals that most people come to accept as true through education and the right type of upbringing.
