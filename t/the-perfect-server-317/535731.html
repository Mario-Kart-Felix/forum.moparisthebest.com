Hey guys, I’m just wondering what people consider to be their perfect server content wise. Forget about things like respectful and mature staff, no lagging, constant uptime, etc, because we all know they are important.

Flame3 August 4, 2016, 11:03am #2

Don’t attempt another remake because there is already servers like RuneRebels and 2006Redone which have been in development for a while and still don’t see a very satisfying player count.

As for me, I’ll highlight a few things:

Staff team:

[ul][li]Have ‘junior’ moderators and ‘senior moderators’. Junior moderators can kick and mute, and Senior moderators can 24-hour ban.[/li]
[li]Administrator positions should be strictly reserved for developers and the occasional community administrator (which is, in my eyes, just a Senior moderator in disguise).[/li][/ul]

Content

[ul][li]In beginning stages of public access, please don’t provide a full-access map. The community gets spread out, and with nobody to talk to outside of a PM system, the game can get boring pretty fast.[/li]
[li]I’ve always been a bit of a fan of a spawn/home area that followed a server’s theme. Edgeville has always been a good spawn area for PKing servers, while Miscellania has been a favourite for general themed servers. If you’re going to choose to develop a server that revolves around one major aspect of the game, make the spawn area have some sort of relation to the game itself.[/li]
[li]Let us at least know every once in a while that development is actually occuring. By this I don’t mean tell us that NPC’s have been added to a new area, but let us know if you’re designing a new NPC and give us a preview/concept description of it.[/li]
[li]Make sure that the majority of P2P features don’t have a significant impact on the game or forums. The last thing I enjoy seeing is donator crowns, obvnoxiously visible forum ranks, and abilities to do what regular players cannot (i.e. access to commands that F2P players cannot access).[/li]
[li]Quests/dynamic minigames.[/li]
[li]Holiday/community events. It’s also interesting sometimes to see a discontinued item circulate after time goes on, and have that helmet from the beginning of 2013 that you had to be there to get.[/li]
[li]…[/li][/ul]

Just a few things, I could go on if you’re still willing to read.

-Provide options to have multiple characters under one account. This way I could enjoy having a skiller account on Mondays, a pure on wednesdays, etc etc.
-A unique idea I’ve thought of in the past is being able to “concoct” paint mixtures, and colour robes, boots, certain hats and armour, etc. Players could express their mood in their armour, which would always be interesting to see.
-Have your webite relate at least somewhat to the theme of a forums if you’re going to use one.
-I’ve never stuck with a server that made overly visible the “Become a Member” link. Just my personal pet peeve.
-If you’re going to provide a ::yell feature, have channels that people could join. This is essentially just clan chats with a nice little twist on them.
-If you’re going to provide a generalized training area, have any attackable NPC in that area have little to no drops, and when you encounter those monsters outside of the training area they have their regular drop tables. This way you can prevent drop farming and actually have a person get involved with the game to earn their items.
-…

Ruby_Forums_Coder August 4, 2016, 11:03am #10

Having ran a pretty successful server in my younger years I look back now and see what I done, in my eyes, and the players eyes was in fact correct. A flawless server in my eyes has nothing to do with custom content, or donator ranks, or anything like that. To me a flawless server is one that is managed correctly, you can have skills like woodcutting that have all the trees in one place with a fast multiplier yet if it isn’t glitchy and you don’t have the game lagging with a shit ton of ddos attacks then you’re doing it right in my eyes. When myself, and multiple others came to private servers we were looking for a runescape experience, not a spawn in and PVP for hours on end type thing. I like the skilling aspect, I like the community aspect.

Make a clean, smooth, professionally run server and devote your time to it. There’s nothing worse than having 13 year old moderators who are representing the servers staff, constantly abusing their powers and acting like they own the place. Keep it professional, I can’t stress that enough. Making a server for monetary gain isn’t a bad thing, but you must run it as a business. If you half ass it and don’t feel dedicated to the project then don’t run it for money.

Just my views, I probably went way off track but oh well.
