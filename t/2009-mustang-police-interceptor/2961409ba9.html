Think about the law of reflection. Trace a ray from the camera to the surface. An exact line will bounce off that normal, at an angle equal to the exact angle you look at it, measured from the perpindicular face of the normal. You know:

The only faces that can then see the outside from you looking at this angle would be, the only ones that are actually reflecting the outside. The front of the hood and the bottom of the bumper.

It makes sense. The only way the front tops could be reflecting the outside is if the car was poking out more.
