semaphore July 31, 2016, 9:58am #20

If by lazy you mean under explicit obligation to share none of the code I’ve written… guilty. But even if I was not bound quite tightly from releasing code I’ve written for another party I don’t know that I would share a whole lot of my (now quite old 1yr+ untouched) system.

I’ll admit that I have released very, very little; however I do not receive validation from mitb. Some folks feel that releasing things somehow validates them or strokes their ego. Who knows? Perhaps they release things for the benefit of the community but I think that group is very very small. I find it perfectly fulfilling to actually do great things with the game without needing to share anything with anyone. The fact is, I know what I have done. Why should I feel obligated to prove it? It doesn’t change anything. If I ever felt the absolute need to prove something I have claimed, I know enough about the client to prove that I have the wherewithal to do it.

I think the core lies with what you really value. If you value true achievement, you really don’t care one way or the other if people see what you have accomplished. If you value praise of others, you release your creations (for better or for worse) all over the good green earth.

To each his (or her) own.
