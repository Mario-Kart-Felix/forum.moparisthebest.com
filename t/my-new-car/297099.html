A V4 engine is a four-cylinder engine with its cylinders arranged in a "V" configuration. The V4 configuration has seen use in automobiles, motorcycles, marine propulsion, and for industrial/commercial applications. Conceptually, a V4 is a pair of V-twin engines mounted end-to-end. Most V4 designs support the crankshaft with three main bearings and have two crankpins that are shared by opposing cylinders.
