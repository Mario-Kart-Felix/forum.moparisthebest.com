Learn to word your posts so they flow off the palate easily. Learn to know when you need to use quotation marks. Learn the difference between a single quote (’) and a double quote ("). When you want the reader to read a word as if it being yelled, put that word in bold text. If you want the reader to put strain or emphasis on a word, italicize it. Making capital every letter in every word for emphasis produces a text that is rather difficult to read, takes up more space, and is, quite frankly, annoying. It is also conducive to reading your post in the tone of an angsty eight year old girl. When typing the letter ‘I’ in the case of referring to oneself, capitalize it. When referring to ‘I’ as a letter in the Latin alphabet, capitalize it as well. In no circumstances should an ‘I’ be alone and lowercase, with the exception of referring to a lowercase ‘I’ in a context where simply saying ‘lowercase ‘I’’ is inappropriate. Also, questions end with a question mark. The exception to this rule would be rhetorical questions, or statements worded in the form of a question. After these put a period, unless you want emphasis on it, in that case put a question mark followed by an exclamation mark. This is not a half-ass attempt at trolling you, MITB. This is me expressing that if you want me to take a post of yours, or a post by anyone for that matter seriously, you ought to learn about the English language a bit more.
