iPENGUIN July 31, 2016, 3:22pm #7

Log into your router through your internet address bar using your default gateway. If you don’t know what that is: Click Start, and search for “command prompt”. Then type “ipconfig all/”. A lsit of information should come up, just scroll down and find your default gateway.

Moving on, most default logins for routers are User: Admin, Pass: Admin (or just blank). if you changed your pass, then you know what it is. Find something on your router that says something like “Gaming”. Fill out the form. Name: servername. Ip address: if you use pfportforwardchecker, it tells you at the top, something like 192.168.0.162. Then whereever it says port put 43594. Then make sure its TCP or whatever. If it still doesn’t let you through, go into Control Panel > Security > Firewall > Allow a program through firewall > Add port or Add exception > Name: servername Port: 43594. PM me with problems.
