Learning music theory is never easy, you landed yourself right in it by stopping playing piano.

I love piano, not as much as guitar, but you get my drift.

If you’re willing to come into blues, I’d suggest just getting say a 3-4 minute blues shuffle backing track, probably in A. This way you can show a whole load of licks and riffs. In a blues shuffle, I could do an entire solo using 3 notes for like 2 minutes without it getting boring, blues riffs are really easy to pick up and sound really impressive, so along with impressing your teacher, you’ll also impress your classmates.

For example, here’s a quick 3 bar blues lick I’ve just tabbed out:

|-----------8b-----|-------8–10br----10-8-|---------8b-----------------------------|

----10-10--------- —10------------------ –10------------------------------------
------------------- ----------------------- ----------------------------------------
------------------- ----------------------- ----------------------------------------
------------------- ----------------------- ----------------------------------------

Ignore the hideous tabbing, please.

If you find any A blues shuffle track, you’ll be able to use that to start it off.
I also suggest sliding into the first position.
