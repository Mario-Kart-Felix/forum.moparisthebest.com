semaphore August 1, 2016, 2:33pm #120

It is not illegal to sell currency from a game. Period. TOS are basically contracts. All this means is that the company can execute the penalties they put in the TOS. This amounts to a ban at worst. Breach of contract is not a crime.

One thing they /can/ do is file any number of lawsuits against you. At least in the USA frivolous lawsuits are very common. You don’t even have to have a case to file a lawsuit. This doesn’t mean you’ll win, it just means you can file one. In order to WIN you need to ‘prove harm’. If the company can prove that you harmed them then they will probably win. The judgement is a whole different thing. For example, if a company can prove that your bots disrupted service for X number of customers which led to downtime which led to engineers having to work specifically for investigating you, the company can come after you. If they have a team of 10 engineers working a 40 hour week to investigate you fully AND your bots were responsible for a server load to cause other paying customers to be unable to play, AND they can prove all this, you will probably be in a little bit of CIVIL trouble.

Civil != Criminal. There is a /huge/ difference.

Mopman is right. TOS are not laws. They cannot go after you criminally for breaking TOS. They CAN go after you for breaking the law by distributing IP and/or copyrighted work. Though IANAL, I had to confer with one for 40+ hours with the company I worked with on this exact topic.
