lothy July 30, 2016, 5:20am #19

Whether it’s useful is always going to be an issue. There are thousands of regulars, and all will have varying views as to what is useful and what isn’t. It’s all relative to who you are, how laid back you are, and how much you know.

For example, lets say I posted a program I made in the Java section. Mopar, k00dr, Krichevskoy and pplsuqbawlz might see it as the most useless topic out.
On the other hand, maybe it’s my first GUI program and I spent a while working on it and thought it could use some feedback.
Commenting on things like that is fine, but as I said, people should keep it constructive.
Someone saying “You suck noob” isn’t going to tell me ‘which’ part of my Java program isn’t that great. Nor will it teach me anything.
Compare that comment to this one:
“Hey Lothy - Your program is ok. But I think you could work on the GUI a bit, it looks a little messy. That, and…”
See which type of ‘comment’ is helpful?

This forum isn’t an all adult forum. There are people at the age of 12 and younger, all the way up to people in their 20s. There may even be older people.
It will never be spam free. That’s like expecting an entire group of year 7 students at a public school to have the complete and correct school uniform every single day. It’s just not going to happen.
