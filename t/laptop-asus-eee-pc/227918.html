Well after a hard working summer, I’ve saved up a bit of cash. I am going to college in September, which means a train ride every day to and from college.

My current laptop is simply too heavy.

While on the usual browse of the interwebs, a tiny laptop caught my eye. Weighing just 1.1kg (compared to my current 3.5kg) and having an 8.9" screen. It also boasts 7.8 hours running time (with lowest settings). Comes with 1gb ram standard, able to upgrade to 2gb and a 1.6ghz processor which can change to high power mode to strain it to 1.8ghz, and also be run at 1.2ghz for power saving.

All this was so great, I thought; oh lord that has got to be expensive.

I checked the price and to my astonishment it was only £292 including VAT.

So guys, I am pretty set on getting this, just wanted to post it up here in case there is something that is going to keep me from buying it.

The version I’m buying comes with standard Linux, but you can buy it with XP, and of course you can always install XP if you want, although apparently it runs slower. Comes with 20gb hard drive. However no optical drive (which is fine for me).
