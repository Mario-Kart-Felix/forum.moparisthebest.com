Radeon:

I thought homeless people in the west are homeless because they choose to be. The government always supports people who can’t find jobs etc.

Not true at all. There are numerous hurdles for a person to overcome before they get any assistance from centrelink (our social services).
One of them is having an address to send paperwork to.
Secondly, housing assistance has thousands of people that are in line to receive some sort of accommodation.

There are services like food vans and ‘hostel’-type places where you can seek shelter, but they’re overcrowded to the point that they have to turn people away all the time.

Virtually nobody chooses to be homeless. You get beaten or stabbed, robbed, you freeze and so on. There’d be maybe 1 in 1000 that goes out of their way to continue living on the streets; everyone else is there due to a series of unfortunate events, be it job loss or struggling to pay the rent despite having some form of income.
