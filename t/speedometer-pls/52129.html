BobDole81 July 30, 2016, 4:27am #3

Hmm, line coordinates…well, the easiest way would be start at the center of the (presumably) circular gauge. Obviously, it will depend where in your panel the speedometer will be, so I will suggest to write a method assuming basing the center of the speedometer at the point (0, 0) of a coordinate plane.

Then, say that 0 mph is like 225 degrees (relative to the +x axis) and the max speed (probably 120 mph in many cars) is 315 degrees (-135 degrees and -45 degrees respectively). That means for your 120 mpg range you have 270 degrees of the circular speedometer to work with.

The angle of the speedometer marker would therefore be: 225 - ((speed / 120D) * 270). If you know the radius of the speedometer circle, then, you can determine the points by getting x and y components: (r * cos(angle), r * sin(angle))
