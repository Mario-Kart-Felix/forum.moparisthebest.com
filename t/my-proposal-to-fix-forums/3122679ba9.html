I’m not really concerned about the forum software used, but most of the points, when applied to vBulletin, are moot.
vBulletin (being a commercial application) is probably subject to penetration testing before major revision releases.
And even if there are multiple entry points, they probably all depend on the same permissions checking function. Who knows, maybe they even mimic java’s SecurityManager check methods.

There’s no reason that multiple entry points should be taken as multiple implementations of a security checking function.

As for the whole sha1 vs md5 debate, that’s moot too – both have been declared cryptographically insecure and put into the “DO NOT USE” basket by government bodies such as the DOD, who now encourage the use of SHA-2 algorithms.
