I don’t exactly need help, I just need some advice. My class just started to sum finite geometric series, (sigma etc.) and I was starting to notice a pattern, besides the one that the book gave. The normal formula given, is:
S[sub]n[/sub] = (t[sub]1[/sub](1 - r[sup]n[/sup])) / (1 - r)

S[sub]n[/sub] being the sum of the series,
n being the number of terms,
r being the common ratio,
and t[sub]1[/sub] being the first term in the sequence.

I noticed that if you took the common ratio to the power of n (r[sup]n[/sup]), and r being 2 (2[sup]n[/sup])you were only a subtraction away from the sum of the series. To show my point:
1+2+4+8+16; r=2; t[sub]1[/sub]=1;n=5;
Using the normal formula (or your head), you get 31. But, r[sup]n[/sup] (2[sup]5[/sup]) = 32, and t[sub]1[/sub]=1.

I thought it was just coincidence. But I kept trying and trying, and I realized that this formula also works, as long as the ratio is 2:
S[sub]n[/sub] = (r[sup]n[/sup])(t[sub]1[/sub]) - t[sub]1[/sub]
I emailed my math teacher, and she said it was good, but that it didn’t have much use if it only worked with r = 2. But I realized that
S[sub]n[/sub] = (r[sup]n[/sup])(t[sub]1[/sub]) - t[sub]1[/sub]
is the same as
S[sub]n[/sub] = ((r[sup]n[/sup])(t[sub]1[/sub]) - t[sub]1[/sub]) / 1
and r - 1 = 1. I then changed my formula to this:
S[sub]n[/sub] = ((r[sup]n[/sup])(t[sub]1[/sub]) - t[sub]1[/sub]) / (r-1)

My question is, which formula would you use:
S[sub]n[/sub] = (t[sub]1[/sub](1 - r[sup]n[/sup])) / (1 - r)
or
S[sub]n[/sub] = ((r[sup]n[/sup])(t[sub]1[/sub]) - t[sub]1[/sub]) / (r-1)

Please explain why
