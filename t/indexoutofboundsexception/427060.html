Protosstribe August 2, 2016, 2:51pm #4

Your problem is the expression “Player.inventory.get(i)” in your while-loop.

You’re expecting the expression “Player.inventory.get(i).equals(Player.ore)” to return false, but a List will throw an IndexOutOfBoundsException if the index is out of range (index < 0 || index >= size()).

In your code, both your items will be removed in your while-loop. When your third loop starts, that’s when your code fails.

[quote=“sp11k3t3ht3rd, post:3, topic:427060”][quote author=imsuperman05 link=topic=529760.msg3867047#msg3867047 date=1325091291]
Use an iterator instead of a loop.

Don’t iterators get screwed up once you remove and add things to the array though?[/quote]

No, iterators throw a ConcurrentModificationException, letting the programmer know they are trying to modify something while iterating across it, which is exactly what you are doing. It’s by sheer chance your code happened to be setup to throw an exception in the first place, to let you know. Java collections are designed to stop bad behavior such as that. If you choose not to utilize this functionality, you need to be immensely careful when modifying arrays while reading them. You can get some very funky behavior when you concurrently modify a collection or array, and not always in a way that will crash your program.
