I’ll explain it.

So you have 0.999~. In an equation, you can substitute this for a variable, x is most commonly used. So in this case, 0.999~ is equal to x. Multiply x by 10, and you’ll have 10x, or 9.999~ exactly. The decimal point is simply moved one position to the right, so actual multiplication isn’t necessary. Now, if you subtract 0.999~ from 9.999~, you’re left with 9 exactly. This is the same as doing 10x - x, leaving you with 9x. Having 9x equal to 9, you can deduce that x = 1 with simple rationalizing.

So yeah, 0.999~ = 1. screw common logic, hello math.
