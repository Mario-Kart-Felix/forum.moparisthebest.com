PaulT August 4, 2016, 3:22pm #22

The argument is usually “The minimum wage workers will go spend the money right away and put it back into the economy, so the business won’t have to fire people or raise prices”. Yet this is not the case. Most people below the poverty line in the US actually don’t work, because government benefits are more substantial relative to the effort required. A majority of minimum wage jobs are in food service jobs. Raising the minimum wage is just going to mean you get served by a robot at McDonalds, and food is more expensive at nice restaurants. Also, many restaurants pay workers under the table, and many employees send their earnings back to their homecountries, so there’s no reason to believe a minimum wage will be effective at increasing available capital in the marketplace.
