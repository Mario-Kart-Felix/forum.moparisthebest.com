Even if that were the case, there’s still a short term benefit of the increase in buying power. From this perspective it has the same pros/cons as inflation.

I think the real problem with raising minimum wage is that small businesses just can’t afford it, so you end up with more unemployment and more people on zero hour contracts. People who think the same minimum-wage employees are going to keep the same jobs and get a pay rise are deluded.

Maybe the logic is this - we raise the minimum wage so that burger flippers are paid more. McDonalds have to increase the price of a burger, and so do all the other fast food restaurants because they have to pay the same. Everyone on a high wage is still earning the same amount, but their burgers cost more - shifting wealth from the “rich” to the “poor”. The high wage employees aren’t going to demand pay rises because of the rise in the cost of a burger, so you don’t get widespread inflation.

The effect of government wage controls, specifically on the minimum wage allowed, is actually basic economics. There isn’t a real question as far as I know. It’s been very well described by many economists, many times.

The minimum wage law is most properly described as a law saying that employers must discriminate against people who have low skills. That’s what the law says. The law says that here’s a man who has a skill that would justify a wage of $5 or $6 per hour (adjusted for today), but you may not employ him, it’s illegal, because if you employ him you must pay him $9 per hour. So what’s the result? To employ him at $9 per hour is to engage in charity. There’s nothing wrong with charity. But most employers are not in the position to engage in that kind of charity. Thus, the consequences of minimum wage laws have been almost wholly bad. We have increased unemployment and increased poverty.

Moreover, the effects have been concentrated on the groups that the do-gooders would most like to help. The people who have been hurt most by the minimum wage laws are the blacks. I have often said that the most anti-black law on the books of this land is the minimum wage law.
