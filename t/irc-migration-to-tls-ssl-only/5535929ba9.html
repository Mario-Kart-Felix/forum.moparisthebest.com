Correct me if I’m wrong, but isn’t it true that if you use SSL on your end then even if you are in a non-ssl required channel, your connection is still encrypted? Meaning anyone connecting from an outside country or work or whatever need not worry that other people aren’t using SSL as long as they enable it on their end. So then what the worry would be about is someone snooping on someone elses connection. So what can someone get from that? Private chat messages, and then the public channel log with no way to link people to usernames. I’m fairly certain they wouldn’t have your IP address since you’re not P2P, and since it’s not listening on your connection there’s nothing that can be tied to you. Sure, private messages being snooped on could be annoying, but I still don’t believe that it outweighs even the fringe cases like sinisoul’s when there’s so many other human security flaws anyway. you know, I have absolutely no idea why I was under the impression that using ssl doesn’t encrypt your own connection, but that is indeed correct (and verifiable)

in sinisoul’s case, recent versions of mirc do come with ssl built in so in his case it’s a matter of running outdated software, and fixing it only requires running an openssl installer (which can be installed to mirc’s home directory so if he needs alternate versions of openssl, having mirc’s provided openssl won’t affect anything else)
