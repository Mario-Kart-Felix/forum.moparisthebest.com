Step one: Proposal written into a bill.

2: Bill introduced to one of the two houses of Congress.

3: Bill assigned to appropriate committee for debate.

4: If recommended favorably, the bill is read to the entire house of Congress (Senate or House of Representatives) for debate.

5: If it is passed, the bill goes to the other chamber of Congress.

6: If the bill passes, but amendments change the wording so different versions pass in the House and the Senate, the bill goes to conference committee to iron out those differences.

7: The revised bill is voted on by both chambers.

8: The bill is then submitted to the president, who can sign it into law or veto it.
