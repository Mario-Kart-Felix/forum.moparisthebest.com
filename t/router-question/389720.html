NewbieNewty August 1, 2016, 11:01am #3

In regards to someone changing the password on your wireless it is possible given that you left your router with the default username and password. If the ‘hacker’ (not literally) was even somewhat computer savvy they would realize with a quick google search that the default username and password for most routers are simply “admin”, “password”.

Once they are logged into your router they can do pretty much anything they want with it, including setting a password on your wireless. It probably varies from router to router but on mine you don’t need the previous passkey to change the current passkey. If the ‘hacker’ did indeed change the password and you do need the current password to change the password then you can simply reset your router. There is usually a reset button on the router somewhere.

As for which protection type to use; I’m not really sure which is more secure, a quick google could probably tell you, but at my house I use: WPA-PSK [TKIP] - Wi-Fi Protected Access with Pre-Shared Key, use WPA-PSK standard encryption with TKIP encryption type

Good luck with figuring everything out
