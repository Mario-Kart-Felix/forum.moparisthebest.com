mod_taharok August 4, 2016, 10:52am #20

Honestly, I feel bad here that StringTokenizer was only mentioned once… You really should use StringTokenizer over a BufferedReader that goes through looking for tokens. Why? Because that’s exactly what StringTokenizer is designed to do. You could go to a distance of using regular expressions which RSCShadow pointed out (but obviously in Java in this situation) and use capture groups to extract your information. With a loop, it becomes dynamic, as well. I would recommend you use StringTokenizer over regex, though. If your format starts becoming more complicated and has specific rules (like an integer must go here, etc.), then you can move onto regular expressions. If your format becomes extremely complex, look into implementing a parser like JavaCC.
