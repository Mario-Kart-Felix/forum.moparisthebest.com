“Bob” is a “look and don’t touch”, albeit brilliant, person. Eventually he has some time on his hands to surf to a forum in order to view current hot topics in a community that he cares for (though he may be unwilling to admit it.) Seeing a conversation that he believes to be a worthy cause he makes a seemingly innocuos post to influence the direction the conversation is headed toward. Though his argument is wonderfully put, there is something even more special in his post than the words that he painstakingly typed out. Beyond the words, there is the revival of a new idea: footnotes. The application of something so obvious yet helpful seems to have slipped through the cracks of the community. Eventually “Bob” comes back to see that many of the posts after his own contain footnotes, and as a result, “Bob” is able to understand what other people are saying with far more clarity. It seems that both parties have benefited: Bob AND the community.

I personally believe that many of the arguments presented thus far are fundamentally flawed in that they are based on the idea that the said person releasing code is doing it out of the kindness of his/her heart, and can benefit no longer once the code is released publicly.

Releasing code is an investment…plain and simple. If one releases code to a community, they are not only encouraging people that benefit from the code to act in the same manner, but they are also guiding the community toward their idea of perfection. The problem is that one person’s idea of perfection can conflict with another person’s idea of perfection. In some cases, they can combine to form an entirely new (and far stronger) reflection of perfection (ultimately, this is the investment that I speak of).

By releasing code to the masses, one can find that there are two outcomes: the release is a total flop and nobody learns from it, or a special few are able to rise above the general masses and see the code for what it truly is…an investment in their future. When all is said and done, those few in the latter case are able to break out of the norm, destroy circular reasoning, and keep the community fresh with new ideas. Giving birth to new life in the form of ideas is a strenuous task when it really comes down to it, but if even one of those baby ideas grows up into an adult and gives birth to new baby ideas then it was all worth it; everybody can learn something new.

Philosophical
Guys, if you have ever wanted to give birth, then releasing your code is probably one of the closest things you can get to doing it. So go ahead and give birth, some of your babies are bound to die, but there’s a chance that a few will survive and follow in your footsteps…
