Yeah I did it over the weekend, really easy so I felt like putting a tut on it.

I WILL NOT TAKE RESPONSIBILITY FOR ANYTHING THAT HAPPENS TO YOUR COMPUTER!!
its not my problem if you jack up your computer and forget to make recoveries. even order recoveries from your manufacturer BEFORE you do this if it makes you feel safe. Don’t do it after as it may void your warranty.

BEFORE YOU START create a set of vista recovery disks, dual layer dvds do the best as they hold the most (8gb I think). I almost had to use them because my computer would spontaneously restart during the second stage of the installation (after the reboot).

  1. find an XP disk. I don’t do stuff illegally so I just got an XP professional from my uncle.
  2. Back up data. I just backed up my documents, pictures and stuff (stuff I’ll probably never put back up on my computer)
  3. Pop in the disk and reboot from it.
  4. It will give you the option to delete partitions. Delete your windows one and do a quick (or full, quick is faster obviously). It gets iffy here when you have linux. Leave your swap partition and your linux partition, grub will need to be reinstalled later. I believe you can do this either with super grub or your linux disk. http://www.supergrubdisk.org/
  5. Let it install.
  6. Your computer will then reboot, when it turns on it will say to press any key to boot from the cd, don’t press any key.
  7. XP will finish installing, prompts will come up for you to enter passwords, users, and whatever.
  8. There you will have your windows XP

The glory of this is that you have a completely “new” computer with no start up items, no pre installed junk, or any junk for that matter. I’m using my XP now and it runs better than any vista I’ve used.
