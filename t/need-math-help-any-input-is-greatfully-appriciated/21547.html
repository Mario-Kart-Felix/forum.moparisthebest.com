easiest way to do it, put in test values of X that will cancel out as many terms as possible… we use this for partial fractions in my Differential equations course, by FAR the fastest way to do it… you can do it by solving the 3 equations and whatnot, but its a pain in the ass…
so x=5 cancels out the last two terms leaving you with (5)(5) + 31 == A(7)(6) A=4/3.

x=-2 cancels out the first and last term, leaving you with 5(-2) + 31 == B(-3)(-7) b==-1
now just substitue a=4/3 and b==-1 into your original equation and solve for the necessary values of C. so from the x^2 terms you get that a+b+c ==0, so 4/3 - 1 + c == 0
c = -1/3.

if your looking to do something with division of polynomials, try using synthetic division.

ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d and you want to divide q +wx
put it into a table like this

                                        w | a        b        c         d

now drop the first value a a

w*a and subtract it from b. w | a b c d
-wa
then take that answer, multiply it by your w again, and repeat until you get to the end. what you get in the last box is your remainder, and all of the other terms are x^(n-1). meaning that your first digit on the left side corresponds to the x^2 now, second to x^1, and so on…
