I guess those are good reasons for implementing it, but what are the longterm results from this system? Reputation is an entirely subjective system. Everyone treats it differently and considers it differently. Someone may think trivial tasks are worth reputation points, yet others may consider only long-term contributions worth awarding reputation. In such a system, how can any formula work? The underlying values are still subjective, no matter what you do with it.

Using this formula, you are still going to have problems. Someone who has a lot of rep but not a lot of posts will have a poor activity index. When they realize posting affects the index, they will start posting a lot of things, even if those things are completely non-contributing to the community as a whole. Post count is directly tied with membergroups, so a lot of people go after posts just as much as rep. The combination in a short amount of time results in a great activity index, based on this formula.

In fact, I think the only way of having a remotely accurate karma system is doing that Aura thing lothy posted about, but it’s unnecessary complex for these boards.

Just my thoughts.

I bet MITB loves the Aura system because it’s based on smiley votes.

I don’t really care about the rep system, but if you want it to be representative you should base it on the current rep, how long the member is registered, and how active the member has been in the last few weeks. Members can rep++/-- someone like we can now. The rep given by members is a separate number, which is only used to calculate the actual rep number shown on a profile page.

It’s pretty much the same as eXemplar said, but I’ll change it a bit and make another useless post:

Say a member has made 1000 posts, of which 50 were made in the last 2 weeks. He has been a member for 30 weeks. He has been awarded 100 rep.

Let’s say that the average member makes 20 posts a week. So the member has made above average posts in the last 2 weeks, but less then his own average of 33,33 a week. So he’s 25% above the overall average, and 25% below his own. His final rep is 100*1,25=125, 125/.75= 93.75 > 94.

Another member has made 300 posts, and has been a member for 6 weeks. In the last 2 weeks he has posted 120 posts. He has 10 rep. He’s 200% above the overall average, and 20% above his own. His rep would be 36.

Obviously, new members start with 0 rep, so people that come here to spam don’t look like reliable members.

This may sound like a stupid system, and it probably is. But it makes rep a representation of a members current activity, and how useful he is to the community at the moment. You could add a green or red arrow, a red one showing that a member is less active, or has started spewing bullshit.
