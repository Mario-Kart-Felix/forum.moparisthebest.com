Post metric: signal to noise ratio over time

Community Inside Talk
eXemplar August 1, 2016, 12:10am #1

So I was just idly thinking, as I’m very apt to do, about why people use reputation (or, more specifically, upvotes on reddit). My thoughts were reputation doesn’t necessarily gauge a persons contribution because someone who posts 10 posts over time and gets 1 reputation per posts isn’t necessarily contributing as much value as someone who makes 1 post and gets 10 reputation.

My thoughts first were just rep / post count, but I’m not sure if that measurement gives any more indication than just the two metrics by themselves as it doesn’t give any indication of the time span involved. But if you use the posts per day, that might work better.

For example, say person1 makes 10 posts and each post gets 1 reputation and they’ve been registered for 10 days, this person has an average of 1 post per day at 10 rep - this person has an snr/t of 1 - 10 rep (signal)/10 posts (noise) * 1 post per day (time)

If person2 makes 10 posts and each post gets 1 reputation and they’ve been registered for 100 days, averaging 0.1 posts per day - this person has an activity index of 0.1 - 10 rep / 10 posts * 0.1

This falls down having a rep ceiling of 200, but yeah, there’s some idle thought for you. Not sure if this would work well but I enjoyed wasting about 10 minutes on it.

lothy August 1, 2016, 12:12am #18

I suppose it wouldn’t really make sense to follow the Aura approach unless you went back to unweighted reputation, which means 1 point per vote no matter who the vote is from.

The trick with this system though is that you could check who the rep givers were before applying the textual representation; if Joe Forumuser has 500 votes but those votes come from just 10 different users then you can give him a Tentative Aura title. On the flip side of the coin, if someone has 200 votes from over 100 members then you might give them a Pervasive Aura.

The underlying logic can be changed to whatever suits, but you can certainly stop people from climbing the ladder by having their close group of friends rep them endlessly.

Other than that, maybe consider a cooldown period after account creation that stops new accounts from voting.
If each new account had to wait one month before being allowed to vote then most people aren’t going to see the value in cheating the system by making 100 fresh accounts to vote their one main account; a month is a long time to get caught and therefore undermines their efforts. At the same time it obviously allows them to build their own Aura up by contributing positively to the community.
