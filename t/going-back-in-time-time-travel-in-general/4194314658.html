zyle1992 August 1, 2016, 11:06pm #21

Let’s say that there was a planet in a distant galaxy, and it took 5000 years for the light reflecting off that planet to reach us, and that somehow, we had a telescope that could see what was happening on that planet (or rather, the reflections of light from that planet). Now, let’s say that they were a modern planet (industrious, etc), and let’s say that with this high power telescope we have at our disposal, we see a rape occur (an arbitrary event. It could be a robbery, mugging, whatever you like). Now, you couldn’t very well stop the crime from happening. Why? Because the event happened 5000 years ago. If you tried to send a warning to that planet, it would take 5000 years for it to reach them.

In order (according to my knowledge) for us to really influence an event like that, we would need to send that signal at 5000 times the speed of light. In that sense, you could potentially alter the past. However, although you sent the signal, and they certainly received the signal, that does not mean that they had the technology to interpret that signal.

If we on earth ever create a telescope powerful enough to see every single detail on a distant planet, and we have a space ship that is capable of traveling faster than light, we still can’t change the past on that planet. The light that reaches us is a result of events on that planet. If for instance the planet is exactly one light year away, the rape or whatever obviously happened a year ago. If we then decide to travel to that planet, and have a telescope on board that is fixated on the target planet, all we will see is everything fast forwarding, like a videotape.
