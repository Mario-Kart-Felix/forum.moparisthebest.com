TBH its near impossible to fully protect yourself from DDoS. That’s a terrible strategy. I don’t know why everyone thinks IP addresses are somehow the magical key to ddos protection. If you change your IP address your hostname needs to point to the new address otherwise your users can’t connect. And if the hostname points to the new IP address then the attacker already has it.

Shorty626:

TBH its near impossible to fully protect yourself from DDoS. Afaik cloudflare is just a web CDN, so great for websites but not something you’d use for an RS server. You’re correct in the sense that it’s not possible to fully protect yourself, because the whole idea behind an attack is that the packets look that that of legit users. The only way to defend yourself is to buy more servers, buy better hardware, or use some software/hardware that drops bad packets before they reach any bottlenecks. But this is much harder when the attack is very distributed and the attackers behave almost exactly like real users.
