Chances are, that won’t work very well, either. Specifically on moderators, the amount of effort needed to review a tutorial is far from trivial. The person critiquing them needs to have a strong and recent background in servers (which I don’t believe any of the staff can qualify for anymore), an accurate understanding of the Java language (or any other language the tutorial targets), and a very thorough understanding of the rules outlined which the tutorial must follow. They need to have common knowledge of the section, so they can be preventative of duplicate tutorials, unless one outdoes another. And, being the least used of all, such a moderator would need to be willing to offer constructive criticism on every single tutorial, whether it be denied or not. In the old system, we would send private messages to users whose tutorials got denied, explaining to them what they need to improve for it to be accepted later on.

Conversely on the members side, a restrictive system requires very detailed and sometimes verbose rules which the tutorial must follow. A few of the rules we’ve used in the past include:

[ul][li]post your work only[/li][li]provide detailed explanations to any and all code snippets provided (no “copy and paste” tutorials)[/li][li]tutorials must follow an outline…[/li][li]titles need to be clear and accurate[/li][/ul]

Some of these rules are impossible to enforce (such as determining if the work is genuinely anyones, determining if explanations are “detailed” enough, determining of titles are “clear” and “accurate” enough, etc). Others are far too restrictive (you must explain your code, you must follow an outline, titles need to be clear and concise). There’s no breathing room in a system like that.

The tutorials section started off as a place where any and all developments were posted, sometimes in an effort to actually help people, but probably more often in an effort to gain respect, popularity, and karma points. The section began to transform into what many would call a “shithole,” as it was full of people attacking each other, spamming nonstop, and hardly any worthwhile tutorials (worthwhile being anything a user could find informative and educational, relevant to RSPS). The staff eventually took control of the section, but immediately things got out of hand in the opposite extreme. Entire pages were locked, rules of control were created, and no user member input was considered. These harsh actions only continued to grow steadily worse over the year following.

Afterwards, the moderators seemed to grow more lenient in the section (meaning they still handed out the same harsh punishments and enforced the same rules, but at a far less rate). After more transformations of rules, harsh punishments, driving people out of the section, etc, the filter was added. I think the general thought behind the filter was to allow moderators to enforce the strict rules easier. The thought was good, but the setting was terrible. The filter only drove more people away and added another number to the moderators’ workloads.

I may have went a bit out of context in order to try and explain my own point of view here. We have made too many mistakes in the past regarding that section. To be completely honest, it should never have become this complex in the first place. Since it has, however, there are far too many mixed opinions on how it should be run. It seems the MoparScape community is almost bent between whether the filter should be there, many of them not even being here before the filter was put in place, or even back when all the strict rules were started.

The only scenario which has yet to be tested in that section, or even on the site as a whole, is one where we build up guidelines to counter only what is a nuisance, rather than trying to be preventative of scenarios which likely no longer exist, or never will. I have spent too much time theorizing what our actions could do on the site, and too little time actually trying the way where users get the most benefits possible, even if it means an extra workload for the moderators. I no longer want to do what I think is best for the site, but rather find out what is best.

Another long post. :rolleyes:
