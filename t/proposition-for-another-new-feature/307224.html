system July 31, 2016, 11:37am #1

Well since we’re posting all these great suggestions that aren’t ever going to be addressed, I figure I might as well add another to the list: thread-level moderation.

There should be privileges for people (or better yet, certain people, like CMs) who create threads that allow them to delete posts of others that are intentionally spam. Deletion would result in a small message left in its place stating that it was deleted by the OP, and a reason for deletion (which would be mandatory) would be listed. Obviously moderators would still be able to see the deleted posts, which would make the thread authors liable for their actions. Another feature could be added to prevent deleting really old posts; if the post is older than 2 weeks, for example, then it cannot be deleted by a thread author, because it is assumed he has had more than enough time to review it and determine its relevancy. This, obviously, would serve to prevent abuse on buried threads.

Ultimately the idea is to help move the burden of monitoring dumbass posts that people constantly make from moderators (who really don’t give a shit) to thread authors (who actually do).

Go ahead and discuss, but please don’t troll. It’s not like there’s a feature that lets me force you assholes stay on topic.
