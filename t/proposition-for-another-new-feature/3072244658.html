Well, as abraham pointed out, the irony is overbearing because I would’ve already used the feature discussed to clean up this thread. But, I think I can push that aside because this topic is actually interesting.

This community is full of assholes, but I think in some cases the outcome can be good. When you’re being an ass to someone who has posted something, one of two things will happen. Either he will leave the community if he doesn’t like being insulted by people or if he thinks the people are complete morons, or he will stay if he wants to improve himself to their liking. (Well, he could also not be bothered by it, but there’s only so many posts one can make before he is affected by what they’re saying.) I think the trend leans more towards the latter, which means that more people will try to improve themselves to everyone’s standards, which, I think, makes them better programmers as a result of being here.

We might ask the question of whether replacing that entire community of assholes with people who are encouraging and helpful will result in more people improving themselves. It’s difficult to predict, and since that is an unrealistic suggestion we can leave it at that. Perhaps the most driving force isn’t even the people directly but rather the features for recognition (awards and reputation). In that case, it would probably be better to have a formal system. That is, if one of the goals is improving the quality of output.
