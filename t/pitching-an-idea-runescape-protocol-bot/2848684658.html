It seems that a proxy bot is impossible to create, because you cannot get the private RSA key in order to decrypt the ISAAC ciphers. However, the Java Math.random() is relatively predictable and there are also theories that ISAAC can be broken after a few hundred keys. I would say that emulating the client would be a better approach than acting as a proxy - it wouldn’t be too hard because most of the incoming packets could simply be discarded.

It’s very possible, and would work better than RSBot or any other bots of the sort, because Jagex can only know what is going on through the protocol - through what you tell them. If you have a packet bot, you can completely control what you tell them, and therefore completely control what they know about the “client” (bot) actions.

Runescape completely killed AutoRune and any other proxy-type bots by RSAing the login block. The only way to get the private key would be to brute force it - and good luck bruting a 128 bit key, that would take several millennia.
