Point one :
Too many connectives causing uneeded breaks. At a part of suspense it really should flow, breaking it up lowers suspence a bit, however adding certain pauses can increase it. For example :

He came into the house, took out a knife. He was going to kill me. I had something he wanted.

philodespotos July 30, 2016, 2:38am #15

It was good. Pat yourself on the back, and indulge in congratulatory orange sounds if that’s your thing. What follows is advice from someone who writes obsessively every bloody day.

The first thing you need to do is cement your editing process. To do so is tedious, typically boring, and doesn’t immediately bear any fruit. But it makes the thousands of times you will repeat it much less cumbersome. I haven’t the slightest what a professional editor does, and don’t much care. My process is: a slow reading for misspellings, grammar, punctuation; a second reading and some chickenscratch to eliminate the most egregious of my 900 word sentences that amount to “the grass is green;” throw everything away and rewrite the entirety in very broad strokes – what do I actually consider important enough not to discard? – 50 pages can typically best be written in 5; with that in mind, go back and trim the fat from the original; now that I’ve got a product that doesn’t make me want to throw in the towel, note what words/tenses/phrases/constructions I’m overusing, and, rather than search for synonyms, get some new fuckin imagery. If I’m repeating the same words in a thousand spots, I need better ideas. This is just generic editing, without any significant changes to content.

I have made only a couple comments on content in my marginscrawling. Mostly, I’ve confined myself to the first two steps. The compliments are genuine; the insults largely suppressed. No need to seem like an ass right out of the box. I’ve also gone easy on punctuation, because it’s so tightly tied to style – as anyone who reads my posts can see, I overpunctuate, but productively. Others underpunctuate, but smoothly. Some follow the prescribed usage. They should be shot.

After many years of effort, it’s been decided that there’s no reasonable way of expressing editing changes without being able to scribble idly, so I’ve done it in the same manner that my friends and I use for each other: print, belittle, spill coffee, dry, scan. My handwriting is a toss up, depending on how early I started drinking. If you can’t read something, you’ll have to ask, or guess.

Anyhow – I do this quite literally every single day, whether with my works or a friend’s. Perfectly happy to look at yours, too, if you’d like. Just email it if you want it read in something approaching a timely fashion. me at gmail.
