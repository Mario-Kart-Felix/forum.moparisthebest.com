When I want to improve (off season) I look to change something.
my 5k went from 17:30 to 16:20 (freshman to sophomore PR) off changing to real racing shorts (short ass ones) and racing shoes, then from 16:20 to 15:15 (junior PR) from changing my diet and being consistent with it. I don’t eat crap food like chips, soda, greasy or fatty foods, etc. Also dedication works as well, such as not shortening runs, running 7 days, and going through an optimal week running the hard days as hard as I can, while making sure the recovery days are nice and slow.
This summer I’m planning on upping my mileage to improve upon my cardio, and run 14:30’s by september.
The diet probably worked the best though. My mile this year went from 4:52 to 4:22.
Your energy/warm-up before a race matters as well. I ran a 57 in my first 400 this year right after the 2-mile, and didn’t warm up at all.
the next week I skipped the 3200 and ran the 4x4 only, stretching and getting a bunch of striders in, and ran a 53.
Oh yea, and form is a given. If you don’t have that, then work on your core alot and work on good form in practices.
