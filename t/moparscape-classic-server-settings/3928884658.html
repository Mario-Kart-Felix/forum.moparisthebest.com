Yoshie August 1, 2016, 11:31am #28

As I recall, quite a while before RS released RS2 and RS was forced into being called “RSC,” I enjoyed staking in lumb on a friend’s defense mage, imageudie. Staking existed, it just wasn’t as big as it has become in so many of the private servers that have surfaced lately.

There’s a “fan-base” or “demand” for p2p features, but it’s countered by the fact that there’s just not enough of a population on most private servers to warrant separate worlds. Personally, I like the concept of only having one world because it keeps us all closer together in the end.

Having the wild as f2p for the majority of the time, and p2p every Sunday and Wednesday or something along those lines is a solution found on other servers. If it can be coded, I suppose a separate wild, one for p2p and one for f2p could be done, so both exist simultaneously on the same server, as separate maps, but again, that’s dividing up the population and invariably “killing off” the wilderness.

I think, however, the greatest complaint people have against p2p is its effect on the wilderness, namely by the use of godstaves. I do not see how this should equate to eliminating p2p items, skills, and its world map from gameplay entirely. Someone earlier (Gety4some) suggested that the vote of p2p versus f2p was flawed, and did not include enough options for people to express what they truly want.

Personally, a “true to original runescape” server, in my mind, is going to encompass as many elements of nostalgia as possible. This is going to include members quests I began but never quite got around to finishing. Stealing cakes and feeling like a badass twelve year old once more. PKing in Varrock’s low level wilderness and getting shot at from behind the fence. Even rushing to shops and trying to buy up elemental runes or bolts before someone else does.

I’m all for this idea either way though, and my apologies for rambling on.

Ah yes, one last thing I took note of and just remembered: It was mentioned earlier about wanting a f2p world due to having players spread too far apart in a p2p world, and not having enough interaction with one another. People will always find places to congregate, be it in Varrock square or next to the castle, in Lumbridge, in Edgeville, at the castle, etc. Add in a global chat and the community will be well interwoven.
