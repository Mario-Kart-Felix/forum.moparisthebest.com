Yz85Racer August 4, 2016, 10:57am #39

Why would you fast without a clue about it? Don’t talk bad about fasting if you do not know how to do it properly (yes, there’s more too it than just ‘not eating’). I fast every day of my life. I follow an intermittent fasting protocol, particularly the one made popular by Martin Berkhan (see; www.leangains.com, the 16:8 eating protocol, however I do 18:6 and occasionally go to 20:4).

While this is different too what you’re describing, I have sometimes done the ESE protocol. (Eat, stop, eat). Basically it’s 24 hour fasting every other day. I didn’t particularly like this protocol but lots do. What is making you feel bad when you first start fasting is the ghrelin response; you’ve been regularly eating all you life and all of a sudden you stop - your body is not used too this. It takes a little bit of time to adjust your ghrelin hormone.

I can honestly say after adjusting to an IF eating protocol, I have never felt better. I never feel lethargic during the day, I sleep amazingly well (who doesn’t like to sleep after gorging yourself on food?), and I am so much more mentally clear and energetic during my fasting window. Now, for the first month of switching to a fasting eating method, it did suck - I felt ill a fair bit. But after 2 weeks I started feeling better and better. Now I can eat, not eat, and it doesn’t matter. During my eating window, I can literally eat and eat and not get full, but don’t get hungry.
