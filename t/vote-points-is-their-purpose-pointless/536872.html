Vote Points; Is their Purpose Pointless?

Games
Runescape
Ykcud August 4, 2016, 11:13am #1

As I search through the server advertisements I notice that about 95% of the servers “force” their players to (at some point) vote. What do I mean by force? Many servers employ a creative way to have their players vote. Some have a vote point shop with items that should be attainable within their world through what should be normal means. Some servers give cash for voting to the point where if you wish to buy anything worth while you will likely need to vote. some servers restrict their players in some way such as reduced xp amount or not being being able to trade or access shops. The latter sometimes (if rarely) can be taken to the extreme, like not allowing the players who do not vote access only to the home and no xp gained, ect ect.

Some people may not see this as “forcing” the player to vote, and that’s just fine. But I feel that a vote for a server should be voluntary and not used as currency. Its a vote that says “I think this is a good server that is worth looking at” and not “Boy do I want that pernex, I better vote 25 times to get it, I dont care if I skew the vote system for it”. I just feel that the point of voting for a server in no longer valid and that maybe something should change. Maybe I am wrong. Maybe that was the initial point of the server voting system. All in all I believe that a truly good server does not need any kind of incentive to have players vote for it, at most an infrequent reminder of how and where to vote is all it should need.
