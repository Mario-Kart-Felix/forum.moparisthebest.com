BobDole81 July 30, 2016, 10:25am #12

Well, I see two ways of looking at this personally.

First, following the scientific approach, consciousness is (according to the theory I like best, at least) is merely an emergent characteristic of our increased mental complexity…ie, through evolution, the capacity of our brains has increased to a stage at which consciousness and the ability to rationalize comes spontaneously (which is by no means saying that our mental complexity gives us the ultimate level of consciousness). So in this sense, consciousness and our sense of reality should have simply been realized some time after conception/birth. So when we die, that’s it…nothing after that.

A more spiritual approach would say that consciousness has been given to us for a reason. In regard to the Christian/Islamic dogmas, our consciousness would obviously be our tool to strive for a seat in heaven…it explains well what should happen when we die, but I don’t like it very much because it ignores what happens beforehand. Like Bebemycat2, I much prefer the idea of samsara, or reincarnation…it gives you more of a sense that one is on a long path to achieving something as opposed to being given a single lifetime in which to accomplish everything. It also explains what goes on before our current sense of consciousness.

That’s basically how I feel about it…
