mod_taharok August 1, 2016, 2:25pm #29

If you want to understand what everyone is saying here…start off smaller. Honestly, you are way over your head. This is something I’ve run into over, and over, and over, and over again. I start massive projects and get a good chunk through until they become overwhelming. You need to have experience with the building blocks, a plan, organizational strategy, and a thorough understanding of what you are trying to accomplish. At that point, it doesn’t matter what the project is, so long as you remain dedicated.

Your shortfall lies with the building blocks part. You are starting to learn C++ and expect to design not only a 3D game engine, not only an advanced and sophisticated 3D game engine, but a game engine with the capability of making a “better call of duty.” You need to take into consideration the sort of capital that goes into developing a game like call of duty. A person who does not even know C++ can’t just wakeup one day and decide to recreate a game better than a team of programmers who have probably had decades of combined professional programming experience.

I am not doubting your capability, but I am doubting your experience. Don’t just shoot down the advice in this thread. It’s given by people who have not only been where you are, but have also made mistakes you are about to. You are basically wasting the majority of your time doing a project like this until you’re ready. I doubt anyone on this website could step forward and complete the goal you just set. I know even triton wouldn’t try it until he was entirely satisfied with his own engine/editor, and had every aspect of the game drawn out to a T so he knew exactly what he was about to go into.

Start simple, build on previous concepts. This is a technique used in schooling systems around the world because it works, duh. Schools don’t assign you massive projects on the first day and expect you to finish it in 2 weeks (because comparatively, that’s the deadline you set yourself). Sure, you might be able to do a 3D engine in 2 years, but it will not be anything like you are expecting. I’d also recommend what frell said about using other engines. If you aren’t happy with them, then make your own.
