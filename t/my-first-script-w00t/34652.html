This doesn’t actually send the keys to RuneScape, it just writes them in your debug window.

Instead of writeln, use sendkeys(message + chr(13));

I’ve rewritten it for you:

[scar]
//THE OFFICIAL FIRST CRIMSON KNIGHTS AUTO BAN URESELF\
//Created by Edvinfalls10\
//all credit goes to him\
//Edited by Aftermath_96\

program Banning;

const
message1=‘jagex sAwkcs ballz!’;
message2=‘report me bizatches!!!@@@@@@@!’;
message3=‘Im a jagex mod ohh im special!!!’;
message4=‘Wats ure pass i wont hack you?!?!?!’;
message5=‘go to wwww.gold4rs.ciom to buy runescape gold cheap!’;
message6=‘Im gunno scam you all you lil noobs’;
message7=‘F’;
message8=‘U’;
message9=‘C’;
message10=‘K’;
message11=‘Jagex’;
message12=‘And your mother you little retards!!!@@@@@@@@@@@#@#$#$%’;

procedure typekeys(themessage:String; waittime:integer);
begin
sendKeys(themessage + chr(13));
wait(waittime);
end;

procedure ban;
begin
typekeys(message1, 5000);
typekeys(message2, 5000);
typekeys(message3, 5000);
typekeys(message4, 5000);
typekeys(message5, 5000);
typekeys(message6, 5000);
typekeys(message7, 1000);
typekeys(message8, 1000);
typekeys(message9, 1000);
typekeys(message10, 4000);
typekeys(message11, 4000);
typekeys(message12, 4000);
end;

begin
repeat
ban;
until(false)
end.
[/scar]

I added a typewait procedure to type keys and wait, to reduce repetition a bit. When you learn a bit more, use arrays for the messages and the waittimes, and then use a for loop to do it. This wa, you’d be able to do this in less than 10 lines.

Cheers.

By the way, if you’re just going to repeat ban over and over, you can add a line that says ban; at the end of the ban procedure and take out the repeat loop.
