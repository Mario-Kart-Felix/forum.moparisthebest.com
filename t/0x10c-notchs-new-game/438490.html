0x10c, notch's new game

General Discussion
_s11 August 2, 2016, 5:02pm #1

[center]

http://0x10c.com/[/center]

Simply put, it’s a game, by notch (creator of Minecraft), where you have spaceships (like EVE Online) and you control them by programming them with a primitive language called Assembly. What I believe notch expects is a community to pop up around this, where people buy/trade ships and software and general space-piracy occurs. I expect it will be quite open-ended and player-driven.

Various people have been busy on working on emulators for the game’s ship’s 16bit CPU architecture and planning out various things to aid in-game development. Each ship will have a programmable virtual CPU of it’s own. The general entry-bar to the game looks like it’s aimed at a more mature, technically-adept crowd than Minecraft was, as learning to program will probably be a necessity if you want to get far into the game and the learning curve will probably be steep because of this.

For more information, check notch’s twitter. General discussions are on reddit (It also has a subreddit, /r/dcpu_16_programming) and HackerNews.

If you want get a bit of a head-start by experimenting with the language that the spaceships will use and have a bit of assembly programming knowledge already, I recommend reading the CPU specification and download interfect’s assembler and emulator. I expect more tools will pop up over the next week or so, since it’s still early days. Plans are being made for potential programming with BASIC dialects, Pascal, C etc.

So, what do y’all think?
