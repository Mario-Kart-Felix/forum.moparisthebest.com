Mopman July 31, 2016, 11:08am #23

a bit contradictory, as those are both steel-string guitars

while it is true that steel will be a bit rough on your fingers to start with (as in, the actual skin on the end of you fingers will be a bit scuffed and sore for the first week or two that you’re learning) that goes away pretty quick as the skin callouses up, and steel-string guitars are often much easier to play after this happens because they have lower action (the strings are closer to the fretboard so less movement is required to push them down)

lower action also makes barre chords easier (which is going to be one of your first stumbling blocks, probably)

personally i’d recommend, if you’re not that bothered eitherway, to get a steel-string. everybody always suggests nylon strings to young people/people starting out, and i’ve never understood why, theyre harder to play and they don’t sound like most people are expecting unless they want to play a lot of classical stuff. literally the only thing they have going for them is that they don’t make your fingertips sore, and thats hardly worth the trouble since it goes away after a few weeks
