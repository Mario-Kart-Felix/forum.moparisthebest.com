I don’t think you should ever sacrifice architecture for speed.[/quote]abstraction is achievable through many more means than just logic encapsulation and inheritance really is a tradeoff when used extensively; inheritance hierarchies lead to lots of inter-class dependency which makes code reuse a chore due to the lack of modularity. i go by the rule of thumb that inheritance trees should be smaller and independent where classes are only dependent upon a concrete definition and polymorphism & dependency injection do the rest, which is something that Hyperion (for example) does not do well at all. try reusing any class in Hyperion and you’ll end up having to solve all sorts of dependency problems.

you are right to believe that architecture should not be sacrificed for speed, but architecture is a much more complex thing than ‘stick classes together!’
