using software to make games isn’t really good practice, and you don’t get a whole lot out of it, for you, or your game. you are limited to what you can and cannot do. on the plus side, your game may be a bit better than what you could do with a programming language and a graphics library. the cons outweigh the pros, so i’d suggest (don’t just take it from me, ask anybody) writing simple games with a programming language such as java or c++ with a graphics, sound, and input library of your choice.

here are some portable full game development APIs, meaning they handle input, sound, graphics, text, raw rendering, or anything else your game needs. you can write engines, frameworks, or a simple unreusable pong clone if you wish:

SFML
SDL
Allegro
DirectX

great, you read my post and understand it, so I can see that you are a lot more interested than I thought you were.

Those are APIs specifically made for helping people write games. If you didn’t use an already-made API, you’d be stuck with reinventing the wheel, even though some companies actually write their own, but only because they are professionals and doing it for a purpose other than it being already there and you don’t want to use it.

SFML is a great way to start for newbies. It was written in C++, and it is object-oriented. It is also faster than Allegro, and faster than SDL without the use of OpenGL rendering. It has a graphics core, a sound core, a system core, and a window core. Each of these were specifically written to help you write a game, rather than writing these all by yourself, which would literally take years of research and development for a starter. If you ever decide to use one of those APIs, I’d suggest donating to them for their help.

for more in-depth information: http://www.sfml-dev.org/
