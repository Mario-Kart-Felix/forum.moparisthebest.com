In a major city or more rural? Your plan is going to be very hard to beat without jumping to a carrier like Straight Talk that runs on someone else’s network. T-Mobile and Sprint are the only ones to buy you out of a contract, but both don’t have nearly the same coverage unless its a major city.

T-Mobile is $60/mo for the same plan as yours, but they don’t do subsidized phones. You’ll either have to buy it at full price, or do it on a 24-month installment plan which is around $25-35/mo for top tier phones. They don’t charge you for data overages, so you will get 2gb at 4G LTE speeds, and anything after that is throttled to 2g speeds.

Should also note how the contract buyout works. You will still have to front the early termination fee, and bring your new carrier a copy of the final bill. They will process it and send you a prepaid Amex or Visa within a few weeks.
