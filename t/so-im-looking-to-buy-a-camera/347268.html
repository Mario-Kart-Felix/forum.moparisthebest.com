SomethingToHide July 31, 2016, 5:33pm #15

For anything other than amateur photography, you’re going to need a DSLR. Of course I’d recommend the ones that I’ve had along with the best cheap lenses.

In that market, it’s less about quality (a good photographer is going to take good pictures with any equipment) and more about features and price.

The a100 I started with was a nice little camera and I still use it from time to time, and they are probably pretty cheap by now. But the ISO performance leaves a LOT to be desired once you use something better.

For P&Ss, I have no recommendations. Try and find websites that compare them (I always use http://www.dpreview.com/). You’re looking for good high and low ISO shots with little noise, especially if you’ll be shooting indoors or without a tripod.

Manual controls should be a must to an extent. You want to be able to control at the least either the aperture or the shutter. Controlling both would be awesome but a little daunting to the new photographer. Features like an awesome flash and face detection and direct printing capabilities aren’t needed.
