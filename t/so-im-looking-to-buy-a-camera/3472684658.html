SomethingToHide July 31, 2016, 5:34pm #25

Auto focus is fine, I rarely use manual focus. Manual controls like Aperture and Shutter Speed are a must, and every camera should have manual ISO control.

The lens on there probably isn’t a macro lens. It won’t be like a P&S where you have one lens which goes from wide to semi zoomed and also does macro shots. You’d need a good macro lens for macro shots. Can’t tell you much about Canon lenses, but I know the “plastic fantastic” macro lens I use is also made in the Canon mount.

Cosina 100mm macro. Build quality is awful, but the optics are incredible. Thus, cheap lens with really good pictures.

It’s also rebranded, so there are other "plastic fantastic"s under a different brand name but same optics. Look into it.

One thing not to get caught up on is how bright the pictures look, house interiors are actually MUCH darker than they look in real life, so ideally you want a camera good in low light off the bat, and this usually means getting a good camera with a lower mp count(not quite sure they make these yet… lol)
