in digital electronics we worked with basic logic gates most the time and later got to flip-flops and programmable logic devices. if you are interested in this stuff u can look into maybe an electronics hobby store or something. ik frys(on the website im just finding cds with info for IC chips but in store they had actual chips and stuff.) has electronic chips and stuff u can make little things and everything. but the PLDs we used some language similar to assembly, im not sure its been like 3 years since i was in that class. and the class before that we used this(http://www.amazon.com/Fischertechnik-GMBH-93293-ROBO-Interface/dp/B000WXD6VA) to make a marble sorting machine which used a flow chart type thing to program it.

as far as the light dimmer stuff they have opensource home automation software(e.g. http://www.linuxmce.org/) that does what u were thinking. u just need to buy the hardware(e.g. this site has lots of that hardware: http://www.smarthome.com/_/index.aspx?src=WG1002329&gclid=CLWw45Xav6oCFeoEQAodJks06Q) to use it

i think if u just wanted to do the light dimming thing u could just get a digital potentiometer and a PLD and have it change the amount of resistance based on the input going to the PLD. idk just ideas and info. the idea might have more stuff then that going on though maybe find an electric engineer, or possibly a software(for the programming part) and hardware(obviously the hardware side of this subject) engineer, i think they would know about this stuff.
