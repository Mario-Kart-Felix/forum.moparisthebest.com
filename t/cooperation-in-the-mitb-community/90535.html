First, we need to decide on a single version of the client to use. I suggest a version from before the additional obfuscations (inverse conditionals, awkward shifts, etc). This is partially because that’s a pain in the ass to read and the last thing we want is to screw up the client trying to fix it, and partially because all of my renaming has been done on a client from 16 May 2006.

We would also need to agree on a standard naming convention. What I for some reason called “ObjectWrapper” (an example of a bad name) is psb’s “DataHandler”

It’s not a necessity, but I would suggest that in addition to a renamed source submission, a you are also required to write a brief explanation/walkthrough explaining the functions of the code you’re documenting and how you identified it. This would allow people to double-check and to learn how to trace for themselves.

Another good measure might be carefully choosing the people with submit access. What’s the use of renaming if you rename it incorrectly?
