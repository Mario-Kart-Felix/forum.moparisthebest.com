Mopman August 2, 2016, 3:27pm #33
R.I.P RSC:

To be honest, the whole idea of prosecuting (ab)users of the internet is something new to me. It’s been around for a while, but I haven’t given it much thought yet. But to me, extradition only applies to a crime committed in one nation, the perpetrator travels to another, and is arrested there. So if I murder someone in Germany and go back to the Netherlands, I get arrested by the Dutch police and I’m extradited to Germany.

It doesn’t need to involve travel - the idea is that if a crime has been committed in the country that wants the guy, they can try extradition. Doesn’t matter whether he was physically there or not. The conditions of the extradition depend on the treaties in place between the two nations, not any sort of overarching international extradition “law”.

For example, the US-UK extradition treaty allows for us to send criminals to the US if they commit a crime in the US, even if they were on UK soil at the time. This is what allowed the extradition of Gary McKinnon - he hacked into US government servers while living in the UK. What matters is a) Dual Criminality (the offence must be a crime in both countries, usually, not just the receiving country) and b) That we believe the accused will be treated fairly and not subject to cruel or unusual punishment, or a biased/politically motivated trial.

If MegaUpload had servers in the US, and NZ has a similar agreement with the US, then that is plenty to legally extradite them.
