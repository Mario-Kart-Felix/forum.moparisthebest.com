Depending on where you live, $2000 shouldn’t be hard to raise in 4-6 months. If you work 20 hours per week at a local food joint at minimum wage (obviously in the U.S.), it will take approximately 14 weeks to raise $2000, not including any taxes being taken out (if you’re a student, you shouldn’t have to worry about taxes anyway, otherwise it’ll depend on how much you make by year’s end). If you can manage 40 hours per week, you’re looking at less than two months. If you can manage to find a job that pays > $7.25/hr, then you’re doing great. Worst case scenario is 14 weeks.

Note: I did not take into consideration SS or Medicare.

Sell your car for either $300-400 or see how much you can get from a local junk yard/metal collector places.

After that, if you even get that much, you should be set to go for picking up another job from the sound of it. I would stay away from jobs that rely on tips and a low pay per hour, as the money is not constant. I don’t know what the minimum wage rate is for you, as seeing people say values that are less than mine. In Michigan, the minimum wage is $7.40 for all jobs. So, your best choice is a local grocery, hardware, automobile, or other miscellaneous place. The closer to your school/house the better (you want to save as much money as possible, and gas prices are only going to go up). You should aim for a minimum of 20 hours per week, and shoot for as many as you can handle. You have to keep in mind that you have additional expenses that you have to pay, and some that may even be unexpected.

That covers what you can do with employment situation, but for additional money saving I would quit any habits you currently have that costs you money. Smoking, drinking (both alcohol, pop beverages, or anything that costs money that you do not need), and any memberships you pay for personal enjoyment that you can live without.

Depending on where you live in the world, you may be entering a season where you have additional money making opportunities. I’m currently approaching Spring so I will start with examples for that. People are rather lazy, they don’t want to handle lawn care if they don’t have to. They tend to overpay for rather simple labor jobs. So, take advantage of this laziness. Start mowing other peoples’ lawns and doing yard clean up and more (this applies to Summer as well). If you are approaching Fall, once again another opportunity. People absolutely hate cleaning up leaves, they get annoyed by the sheer amount of them, and the labor required with cleaning them up. Rake/Leaf blow your neighbors, or other people in your town for additional money. If you are in or approaching Winter, shovel or plow drive ways.

If you need additional ideas for money making, just ask.

Edit:
To expand, the amount of money you need is rather small. I make over $95,000 a year after taxes :L
