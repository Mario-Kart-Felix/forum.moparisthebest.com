Mopman August 1, 2016, 4:37am #3

In my opinion (and this is just my opinion, remember, I’m not a game developer) game development is a good choice of profession if you LOVE it. And I mean, LOVE it.

It pays a lot less than equivalently-skilled development positions in other industries, and it’s harder to get into, and often much harder work. But you get to work on cool games. Whether that makes up for it in your opinion is your choice.

As for the english problem, just speak to people a lot. There’s a website, www.lang-8.com, which hooks language learners up with native speakers for practice, in a tit-for-tat fashion (you correct me in English, I’ll correct you in my tongue etc.) It’s aimed more at the written word, but I’m pretty sure I remember quite a few people being up for skyping and stuff.

If not, just make a point of Skyping with more people instead of IM’ing them/email’ing, and make it clear that they should ruthlessly correct your pronunciation or mistakes. Most people don’t mind doing it, I’ve found, but they’re shy or embarassed or whatever of doing it without your permission, because they sound like a dick. If you make it clear that you want to improve they’ll gladly help.
