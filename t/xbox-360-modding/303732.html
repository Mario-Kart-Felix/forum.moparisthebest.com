er… afaik the chips are just placed on a type of ‘switch board’ so you can switch between the real chip and the modded chip. This is a good method if u want to play normal games on live and backups without. If you want to play both on live, then of course there is a risk of getting banned, there is also no point in chipping, do a softmod. The chips just offer ease as they allow you to ‘turn off’ the mod. My xbox is still modded, it has one of the first softmods ever on it. They can’t detect the mods directly, they only become detectable when you run backups (something to do with timings and all that shit). Last time I read there was speculation that your xbox cached these discrepencies so that even when you unplugged eth cable they cud still detect u next time u went on live. Which, if cached on the chip itself (I cant see this unless they blow some efuse or sumthin???) the hardware mod wud be good.

I say: Softmod, its cheap easy and easily reversible. Just not so easy to turn on/off. Keep up to date with all the updates (You can do a case mod and allow for a sata cable to be plugged in from the rear, relatively easy to do). Be wary when playing backups on live / playing on live after playing backups. I haven’t kept up with the scene but it was real easy back when I did it.
