Not 100% accurate, I made myself a cable with a parallel port on the end that I soldered to the xbox mobo, this allowed me to read and write the nand chip. This allows you to modify secdata.bin in your nand chip with an older version (the xbox uses a system whereby it’ll write the whole contents of the nand, next time it’ll write in a different location, etc. It does this to increase the nand’s life capacity) and then your xbox will allow you to install games to the hdd etc. After this you can then do the simple write-protect mod (I say simple, it’s very fine soldering!) and I guess follow `Lewk_s post above to get back online (although I haven’t personally tried this one, I think it’s probably bullshit seeing as the hdd is removable and replacable anyway but I will try it and post back my results…).

Oh, P.S.: I bought a new 360 (I have the banned one lying around) and have flashed it with the latest iXtreme LiteTouch firmware, am currently back on Xbox Live and haven’t been banned yet. (Recommended to restore the drive to the original firmware whenever there’s a system update just to be sure [title updates are fine]).
