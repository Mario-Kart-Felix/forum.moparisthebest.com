moparisthebest August 3, 2016, 2:23am #3

What do you mean by ‘safety’? Because, in all honesty, an old very heavy car with an actual frame like the 70 Chevelle is always going to be safer than a newer, lighter car with a ‘uni-body’. As long as you put some decent seatbelts in it that is.

You certainly aren’t going to get any type of fuel efficiency out of a motor like that if you are also after performance. In basically every case there is, you choose either performance or good fuel mileage, think of it as a sliding scale, as you get better gas mileage, your performance goes down, and as you get worse gas mileage, your performance goes up.

Here in the USA, a 70 Chevelle would be very cheap and easy to work on, with just about any aftermarket part you would like available locally at a car show/swap meet or able to be ordered online. Since you live in Australia, chances are most of the parts you need will have to be ordered from the USA, so shipping is bound to be outrageous.

Decide on what you need performance-wise and such, and maybe I can give you some more pointers.
