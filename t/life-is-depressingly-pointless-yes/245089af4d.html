First of all, we live in a society that encourages this kind of living. We are trained since a young age to respect authority unquestioningly, get up in the morning to do work/school, spend your free time doing activities that require minimal use of the brain (Watching TV, playing games, other “passtimes”) at a certain point, our parents kick us out, we get a job, get married, have kids, and then live for another 40/50 years until death.

This is not a comforting thought.

Second, we have a great awareness problem. We are not aware of ourselves, the world around us, or many of the interactions between these. You (Squigglyo) are a great example of this. The world is such an unbelievably large place - it is quite stunning when you begin to realize how large it is - that the very concept of there existing nothing in the world that interests you is just absurd. However, society (And human nature, of which society is a larger manifestation) is at fault again here, too. We tend to look at what we want to see, and anything else falls by the wayside. After awhile, all you can see is a small set of things. If there is something wrong that you are trying to fix, and it is not one of the small set of things you know about, you will die before you ever fix it. (Have you ever had a problem you’ve been wrestling with for weeks, only to have someone come and fix it in 5 minutes by changing something that never came close to entering your mind?) You might think it’s impractical to pay attention to everything around us at once, and that we should focus on only that which is important. However, everything around us interacts in such magnificently complex ways, that if you miss just a single piece of the picture, you will miss the whole thing. Basically, keep an open mind.
This awareness thing extends inward as well. By this, I mean that we do not have an awareness of our own body. Tell me, anyone, do you know what shape your spine is in right now? You should be able to tell without having to look or feel it, via kinesthesia - a sense that tells us the positions of our various parts relative to eachother. If you slouch back into your seat so that your spine forms nearly into the shape of a C, then you are preparing yourself for painful back problems in a few decades or so.
But who, these days, pays attention to the shape of their spine ?! If you do that, you might miss what’s on television, or your MMORPG! Or you could look at your hands, which you no doubt use every day, and depend on more than you can imagine. Straighten out and then curl your fingers repeatedly. Are you aware of the muscles in your forearm, connected by tendons to your fingertips, that are working to move them? Would you notice this if you developed RSI from typing poorly? (Read some accounts from people who’ve gotten RSI from typing, it’s an amazing thought that they have it because they didn’t apply basic knowlege of their own hands/arms)

Life is only meaningless (And thus depressing) if you have nothing to do. So you should do something; you have to set a goal for yourself. There are a few crucial aspects to choosing a goal - for one, a goal has to be very precise - “I want to be the most awesome person alive” is not a precise goal. Depending on how you view “most awesome person alive”, you could work forever without knowing whether you are progressing. “I want to jog 2 miles” is better - either you’ve jogged 2 miles or not, and at any point, you know how many more miles you need to jog after you’ve started.
Secondly, a goal must be within your reach. This is tough, because you never know what is and what is not within your reach. If you are a poor black kid, saying “I want to be the president of the US” may be aiming a tad too high. Then again, you never know, and if you’re feeling lucky, there is nothing you think you can’t do.
Finally (For now), your goal must be something you want. The more you want it, the better. People (Including authorities, loved ones, and yourself) may tell you your goal is crazy, and you have no chance (See “Choosing a goal, above”). If you truly desire your goal, you will have the wisdom to ignore them (Prerequisite to this is knowing whether or not your goal actually IS achievable). However, after all of this, you never achieve your goal, then you’ll probably be more depressed than before. So I will outline the general plan I’ve discovered/read about/concocted for achieving this unspecified goal.

[ul][li]Have a plan of action. A plan is necessary before starting, thought you are free to alter it at any point.[/li]
[li]Use the goal as your center and measuring point: If it is precise, you should be able to easily answer the question “Am I closer to the goal than I was yesterday?”[/li]
[li]Be able to discern whether or not your plan is working - regardless of authority, loved ones, your own intuition, etc. If the whole universe is telling you that you must use plan X, and you are using it but not approaching the goal, you give it up in search for one that works. For example, the fatso who has decided he’s finally going on a diet (+exercise, other healthy stuff, the works). At first it’s hard work, but he’s losing weight. After a few years, however, he is barely losing anything. But since he is being told constantly by commercials, society, his own intuition, that dieting is the answer, he never thinks to try a different course of action. Eventually he gives up, goes back to his old habits, and regains all the lost weight (And then some). [Btw, some fat friends of mine have seen this happen to their fat friends, I’ve not made it up][/li]
[li]Do not underthink things - if you do you’re a fool. Do not overthink to the point you cannot decide what to do anymore either.[/li]
[li]Be willing to try a new plan if you think it will work better. But don’t give up something that’s working consistently just because there is something newer and more modern on the market.[/li]
[li]Be open to incredibly radical stuff; everything is interrelated you know.[/li]
[li]Pie is good[/li]
[li]Keep a log of all your activities and results, all your epiphanies, etc. They might come in handy later (And we tend to forget this stuff)[/li][/ul]

        And finally:

[ul][li]Never give up the curiosity we are born with, when you do, you might as well just return to living pointlessly until you die.[/li][/ul]

Hope this gets you back on the right track.
