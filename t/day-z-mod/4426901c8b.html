Ruby_Forums_Coder August 4, 2016, 9:46am #214

To any of you newbies I strongly advise you watch Frankie’s dayz series, or as much of it as you can. It brought on my playing an absolute shit tonne. I also suggest playing the Arma 2 tutorial missions as well so you have some experience of the game before jumping into a zombie apocalypse. The best advice I can give to you guys is always stay in position and aware of your surroundings, always have a plan and always have some form of cover planned. Especially when playing in a team positioning and awareness is pretty much the most important thing, I can’t stress that enough. We don’t want to be shot by friendly fire when we’re moving round to flank an enemy group or when we’re tracking a chopper down or anything like that, if we’re playing with a team we want to be able to feel safe enough to move without being shot by a squad mate. Which is actually a really good thing that Marc’s server is just starting out, we can have mockup team fights before going to a different server and we start getting in fire fights with others who it isn’t planned with.

We also have a dress code if you will, if you’re playing with us you have to be wearing some kind of glasses on your character. This way we can tell you apart without nameplates. And if we’re playing in a group with a server with nameplates we’ve lightly discussed sticking [MPR] in our names so we can see if its any of us on sight.

Your player type will probably depend what group you’re in. I think myself and Tom are going to “lead” a group each but still be in constant comms with each other so we can still work together. 2 groups of 4 is easier to handle than a group of 8, after all. Basically if you’re with Tom you’ll be a bandit and if you’re with me you’ll be a survivor. For example, Tom shoots on sight regardless and I only shoot on sight if they’re geared up enough to easily kill someone with us (someone running with a DMR in front of me) or bandits.

If anyone has any better ideas on how to run the squad(s) it’d be greatly appreciated since these have only been discussed for a few minutes on Skype and roughly agreed on. We just think that’d be the easiest way.

EDIT: it’d also be pretty fun to try out some mock tactical assaults to work on positioning too. For example, one team defends a fire station and the other team try to infiltrate it.
