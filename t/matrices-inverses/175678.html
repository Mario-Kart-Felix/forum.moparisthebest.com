I’ll do the inverse of the 2x2 matrices simply because the 3x3 is too long winded.

AB=
A B
[ -1 2 ] [ 1 -2 ]
[ -1 1 ] [ 1 -1 ]

So lets find the inverse of A. To prove that its the inverse of B, they must be the same.

So, (this is of matrix A)
a = -1.
b = 2
c = -1
d = 1.

Inverse of A =
1/ad-bc [d -b]
[-c a]

So lets substitute into the formula.
1/( (-1 x 1) - (2 x -1) ) [1 -(2)]
[-(-1) -1]
Tidy up a bit.
1/(-1 - (-2)) [1 -2]
[1 -1]
1/1 [1 -2]
[1 -1]

1/1 is obviously 1. Which gives 1 x [1 -2]
[1 -1]

Thus the inverse is [1 -2] and B is an inverse of A.
[1 -1]

the other way of finding an inverse is to do it via simultaneous equations.
[a b][the matrix] = [1 0] (the identity matrix)
[c d][ ] [0 1]

So inverse of A.
[a b] [ 1 4 ] = [1 0]
[c d] [ -1 -3 ] [0 1]

Multiply em out to give.
[a - b 4a - 3b] = [1 0]
[c - d 4c - 3d] [0 1]

hence
a - b = 1
4a - 3b = 0
Solve these simultaneously.
c - d = 0
4c - 3d = 1.
Solve these simultaneously
get the values of a, b, c,d
and you get your inverse.
