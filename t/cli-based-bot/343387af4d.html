No. RSA encryption relies on the public key being, well, public. This is the key that is in the client. The private key is only known to jagex and is in their servers, because using the private key is the only way to decrypt the data that was encrypted with the public key. If we had both we would be in fat city because we could decrypt/re-encrypt everything on the line and gain access to everything.
[/quote]

If you just want to have the data, you can generate a pair of RSA keys your self. Change the RSA public key in the RS client with your own generated key. Make a ‘proxy’ and let the rs client connect to your proxy and let that your proxy bot decrypt the data with your self generated private key and re-encrypt it with the public key from the rs client and send it to the RS Servers.

That isn’t that hard…
