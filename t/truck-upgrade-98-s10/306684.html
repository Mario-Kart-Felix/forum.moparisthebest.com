You didn’t explain why. Both a supercharger and a turbocharger are forced air induction. This is opposite to a car that is naturally aspirated. A turbocharger operates off of exhaust gases, using the force of the exhaust air to spin a turbine forcing air back into the intake manifold. Waiting for this turbine to ‘spin up’ is the cause of so-called ‘turbo lag’. A supercharger operates via a belt or a chain (usually a cogged belt) connected to the crankshaft, which is what the pistons turn in your engine, therefore whenever your engine is running it is spinning and operating the supercharger, which again forces air into your engine. Superchargers give you more low-end torque compared to turbochargers which give you more high-end torque.

If you are curious why I didn’t mention horse power (HP), it is simply because it is mathematically calculated with this simple formula:
Horse Power = RPM * Torque
