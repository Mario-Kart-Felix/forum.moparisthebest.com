mod_taharok August 1, 2016, 4:26am #21

Dunno, seems like Jagex is starting to diverge from “production” skills. All of the new skills they’ve released since the beginning have been production skills, minus Slayer and Dungeoneering. Other than the new ones, you still have most of the old ones–mining, smithing, woodcutting, thieving, crafting, fletching, etc. Dungeoneering was a really new perspective, seeing as it managed to incorporate every other skill in the game, yet not produce any items. I’m feeling Jagex is making a new movement toward non-production skills.

At the same time, I can’t envision anything they would add on that they couldn’t already incorporate. Jagex does have a lot of creativity, I won’t lie. Slayer is a very excellent skill, and RuneScape is pretty unique in possessing it (as far as I know, anyway). Dungeoneering seems like a copycat of dungeon instances in WoW, but players have been ranting for eons about having dungeons and such like WoW. Jagex did a good job in creating a dynamic world in which these dungeons exist. I think it’s both unique and enjoying.

Most of the other skills people discuss, like breeding or sailing, can be easily tied in with existing skills. Breeding is obviously something Jagex considered, but they didn’t put into the Summoning skill for whatever reason, probably to simplify the game. Sailing is tied closely with construction, but is a lot more complicated (almost unnecessarily). The world doesn’t even seem large enough to accommodate something like sailing. How could everyone own their own boat? Where would the boat travel? Could boats be shared among players? Would they be specific to POHs? How would they have practical use if they are restricted to POHs?

Beyond that, balderdash. Jagex promised us a new skill late 2010, early 2011. I guess we’ll see whatever it is. I’m guessing they are probably going to try pushing the 120 limit again, as well.
