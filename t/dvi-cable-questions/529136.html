  1. http://communities.intel.com/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/2-170979-230419/748c3777_dvi_connector_types.gif
  2. yes and no, what are you using it for
  3. yes, but see 2), you can use a vga > dvi if it’s coming from a digital source (computer), but from an analogue source you may need an analogue to digital converter
  4. see above, dvi from a digital source to a digital vga receiver is fine
  5. yes, if it’s all digital

vga, dvi and hdmi are all just cables at the end of the day, if it’s from a digital source like a computer to a digital receiver like a computer monitor then it doesn’t matter (mostly) what cables you use, the problems come when you try to use things like OLLLLLLLLDDDDDDDDDD TVs which have a vga or a rca cable and you try to plug a hdmi from a digital source into it

of course there are exceptions, you can hook a pc up to a tv with a rca cable, but again, it depends, tell us what you’re trying to do if you want a more specific answer
