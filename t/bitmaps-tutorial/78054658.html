Weibs July 29, 2016, 10:09pm #23

If you take a screenshot and then paste it in paint, you can cut out the piece you want. So then you get a new canvas and paste the part you cut out. You can zoom in, and select a unique part in the bitmap. After you’ve got it down to the part of the image you want, there will be some white space oustide of the image. So you need to drag the canvas in to fit the dimensions of your pic. Like this:

See the bottom right corner of the whitespace(canvas)? There should be a little box that you can drag into fit the size of your image so that it turns out like this:

Then save the pic, and your bitmap code should come out fine.

Weibs July 29, 2016, 10:12pm #29

Well, I’m not totally sure about that whole procedure, but I’ll explain some of it.

Bitmap: Name of the bitmap you’re searching for.

X, Y: Where the coordinates of the upper left corner of the bitmap should be stored if found.

x1, y1, x2, y2: The box to search in, EX: 1, 1, 1000, 1000 would search in a box starting at the coordinates of 1, 1 and ending at coordinates 1000, 1000.

Tolerance: The amount of tolerance to use when searching for the bitmap.

Range: Probably something to do with radius and how far out to search from the center, but not sure.

Accuracy: 1.0 would be 100% accuracy, if you wanted something like 90% accuracy, then use .9 .

AngleIntervals: Not sure, I think it means how much to increase the angle each time.

StartAngle, MaxAngle: Again, not sure, I think StartAngle is what angle to start looking for the bitmap, and MaxAngle could be how far to look for it.

As for how to find the coordinates on the screen, drag the cross hair to the client window (RuneScape) and use the color picker and hover over the spot you want to get coordinates on, click, and the coordinates should show up between ()'s in the debug box.

Hopefully this helps,

-Weibs.
