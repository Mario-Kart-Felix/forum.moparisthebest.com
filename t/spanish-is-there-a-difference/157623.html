xxlegitxx July 30, 2016, 4:47pm #4

Coche nuevo (it’s masculine… why would you say nueva?) means “a brand new car.”
Nuevo coche means “a new (different) car.”

Generally if an adjective follows a noun, it takes it’s normal meaning. If it precedes the noun, it takes on an alternate meaning.

For example. If I switched piano teachers, and I were to tell my friend about it, I’d say something like, “I got a nueva profesora.” However, if I wanted to say that my teacher was inexperienced, I’d say, “I got a profesora nueva.”
