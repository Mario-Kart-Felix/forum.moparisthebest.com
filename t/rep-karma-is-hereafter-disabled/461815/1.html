Rep/Karma is hereafter disabled

Community Suggestions
mod_taharok August 4, 2016, 12:20am #1

I’ve disabled the rep feature from the forum until we replace it with a better system. There has been way too much abuse going on with it and hardly any moderation. Since it does nothing to the website, we’ve discussed disabling it off and on for months. I decided now is as good a time as ever.

The new criteria for MoparScape Vet is simply being nominated. A user no longer needs 50 rep to be nominated.

The criteria for Community Member is approval by an administrator. Previously we used 50 rep as a guide, but we never took the rep itself into consideration, just the contributions the member has made. We will do that without the rep now. If you feel you or someone else deserves community member, feel free to message an administrator. Just try and have a hard skin about it, because we have no problem denying CM to users. It’s an exclusive rank given to the best and most consistent contributors of the community.

Thanks,
Mopar Staff

Edit: To those who’ve already messaged me or another administrator requesting Community Member, this is a blanket denial for now. This has nothing to do with individual requests, but rather that I wasn’t expecting to receive so many requests in such a short time period. I would rather come up with more concrete criteria for the Community Member/MSCP Vet ranks, not to mention these ranks may be changed a bit in the future. Just be patient while we sort this stuff out.

Thanks again.

[quote=“Loveandpower, post:14, topic:461815”]You sir, just earned my respect!

I can’t wait for the system to be redesigned. Are there any ideas yet as to what it will replaced with?[/quote]

There are several, most of which I can’t remember right now. The initial idea is to have two new systems replace the current reputation system. We want one for subjective voting (similar to rep) and one for objective (similar to how stats work). We will use these combined to determine how much a user contributes and what the community thinks of the person. This knowledge can be used to figure out what special ranks or awards the user may deserve.

Awards will have to be reevaluated after implementing that system. We may have additional criteria, such as nominations, to push along more awards. I feel that people with an award should agree when a new user is considered qualified for one. The exact means of achieving this is unknown to me, since adding a nomination system for each award is highly impractical. Either way, we’ll think of something.
