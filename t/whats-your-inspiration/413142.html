mod_taharok August 1, 2016, 2:37pm #4

I think motivation and inspiration are completely different worlds. I’ve been inspired to do dozens of projects, but hardly motivated to finish any of them. I’ve personally reached a stage where I have realized motivation can be as simple as saying no when I get bored with a project, and just continue working on it. Just focus on finishing it, no matter how boring it gets. It’s fine to take breaks, but I would personally not advising taking a break longer than a few weeks or month, at max, because you will most likely turn months into years and end up never finishing it.

Another perspective is looking at it like a job. Jobs are easy to become motivated at because you are rendering services and receiving a tangible reward, in most cases, a monetary one. In hobbies (seems especially virtual hobbies), when there is no tangible reward then even after you spend hours and hours working on a project, it doesn’t seem like an accomplishment when you’re done. So maybe look at it like a job, and try to come up with things you will gain when you finish it, so you can feel accomplished and have a purpose for doing it in the first place.

Heh, that was kinda rambly, but I hope I got my point across.
