EDIT: I got ya now… After re-reading.

Use this:

$username = $_REQUEST["username"];
$ourFileName = $username.".txt";
$ourFileHandle = fopen($ourFileName, 'w') or die("can't open file");
fclose($ourFileHandle);

$pass = $_REQUEST["password"];
$myFile = $_REQUEST["username"];
$fh = fopen($myFile, 'w') or die("can't open file");
$stringData = "character-username = $ourFileName";
fwrite($fh, $stringData);
$stringData = "character-password = $pass";
fwrite($fh, $stringData);
fclose($fh);

Explanation:

I defined $username to be the variable, then I used it to make the txt file.

Like:

$myFileName = $username.".txt";

Which means the username.txt

There you go.

PS:

You may want to use $ourFileName for the second fopen() you have. It won’t work the way you have it (just opening their name???)
