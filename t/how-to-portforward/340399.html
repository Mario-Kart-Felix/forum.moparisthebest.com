Alright, so I was bored and decided to create a guide that fully explains how to portforward.

For this guide, you should know the following:

-what a router is – http://www.lmgtfy.com/?q=How+do+I+find+my+router? –
-what an ip address is – http://www.lmgtfy.com/?q=WhatIsMyIpAddress.com –
-what a default gateway is – http://www.lmgtfy.com/?q=How+do+i+find+my+default+gateway –
-what a 1pv4 address is (not neccessarily, but helps if you know) – http://www.lmgtfy.com/?q=my+1pv4+address –
-how to follow instructions

There are three chapters:

Chapter 1: ip configuration
Chapter 2: portforward.com
Chapter 3: Setting an Application

Alright so lets get started. [size=24pt]CHAPTER 1[/size]

First off we will find out what your [size=14pt]router[/size] is. Your router could be on a box that is connected to your computer. [size=12pt]Your router could be on a sticker.[/size]
Once you found out, write that somewhere so you remember

Now your going to find out your 1pv4 address.

  1. [size=12pt]Click Start[/size] on the bottom left of your screen.

  2. Type in [size=12pt]cmd or command prompt[/size]

  3. this is the easiest part of 1pv4 address, [size=12pt]Type in ipconfig[/size] in command prompt

  4. Find where your 1pv4 Address is, it will be easy to spot. Mine was underneath “[size=12pt]Ethernet adapter Local Area Connection[/size]” Now change the last digit into “1” and that is your 1pv4 address. When you enter it into another browsertab, you will enter your default gateway.

Now just leave that for now.
Next, you will use your router to portforward [size=24pt]CHAPTER 2[/size]

  1. go to portforward.com (in another tab/browser) – http://www.lmgtfy.com/?q=portforward.com

  2. now the router you wrote down will come to use. Scroll down in portforward.com and find [size=12pt]your router[/size] The router list should look like this:

  1. Click the “[size=12pt]Click here to skip Advertisement[/size]” Button on the top Right
  1. Scroll down and [size=12pt]choose any game[/size]
  1. This should bring you to a bunch of instructions.

Now that we have portforward.com, and your 1pv4 address. [size=14pt]Follow the instructions in portforward.com[/size]

Alright! When you reach a part where you gotta edit your own application; do the following: [size=24pt]CHAPTER 3[/size]

  1. Set your application name to your server name
  2. Protocol to [size=12pt]TCP[/size]
  3. set the Pot (or range)/start to end boxes both to [size=12pt]43594[/size]
  4. Protocol timeout as your default for TCP [size=12pt]86400[/size]
  5. Map to Host Port to [size=12pt]43594[/size]
  6. Application type to [size=12pt]None[/size] (default)
  7. Now you have added your application. [size=12pt]Add it to your Hosted applications.[/size]


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Finally, click the done Button and that’s it! HOPE THIS HELPED
