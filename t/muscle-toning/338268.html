lothy July 31, 2016, 4:09pm #5

No such thing as muscle toning. What you’re probably referring to is ‘cutting’, which is basically the phase after bulking up where you aim to cut bodyfat percentage while maintaining your hard-earned muscle mass.
Basically, you need to create a calorie deficit. So whatever your maintenance calorie intake is, minus 5 - 10% from it to begin with.
Make sure that you eat at least 1 gram of protein per pound of bodyweight, and anywhere up to 1.5 grams of protein per pound.

I suggest you look up carb cycling and carb tapering. Tapering is basically the idea that you eat starchy carbs only in the mornings; cycling is basically having a higher calorie intake day, and then a day or two with lower intakes (below maintenance), and then back to a high day (a high day being maintenance or slightly lower).
All the while, you need to be doing regular strength training to promote muscle retention – your body, should it feel deprived, will break down the proteins in your body well before it will break down fats that are stored. This is, for all intents and purposes, a survival mechanism.
The reason you use carb cycling is to trick the body into not entering this survival mode mentality.

EDIT: Also, to be able to eat more calories while cutting, you can add cardio workouts instead to create or increase your calorie deficit.

lothy July 31, 2016, 4:09pm #7

Muscle toning is a term that was used to make the idea of having muscles attractive to women. There’s always tension at ‘rest’ because your muscles are either concentrically or eccentrically contracted.
The difference between visible muscle and invisible muscle (ie, whether you can see somebody’s abs or not) is the amount of bodyfat the person has. So you aren’t ‘toning’ anything – you’re reducing your bodyfat to make the underlying muscle visible. Spot toning is a myth, so please don’t propagate it. =\

And that last bit is wrong too. If you want lean muscle, simply eat either maintenance calories or only ever so slightly above maintenance calories while doing strength building exercises. If you want bulk like a bodybuilder, you eat a reasonable amount above maintenance and train differently (sets of 8 repetitions usually promotes size gains, as does slower lifting).

lothy July 31, 2016, 4:09pm #11

I’ve been cutting for a while now and, contrary to what Original Daniel said, it isn’t easy.
I could probably drop 4.4 - 11 pounds (2 - 5kg) overnight using diuretics (diuretics dehydrate your body, almost completely eliminating water weight) – people in sports (boxing, MMA, other martial arts) with weight classes do it all the time before fights; that’s weight loss right there, and fuck yeah it’s easy.
But fat loss, which is what this topic is concerned with, isn’t easy. You can expect to lose 2 pounds of fat per week at best. If you lose any more than that then you are most certainly losing muscle tissue.
For the record though, most people will only manage to lose 1 pound of fat per week (which is 3500 kCal) without also losing too much muscle mass.
