Normally automatics (especially the older ones that bolt to the huge old V8s used for drag racing) can’t handle the amount of horsepower and torque that drag racers run, at least for anywhere near the same cost as a manual. Obviously those problems don’t come into play with your 80hp english cars. (or any consumer-level cars for that matter)

Now I’m not against manuals, I just don’t think they have many benefits over automatic transmissions in most every day driving situations. Maybe they had a few when 3-speed automatics were the norm, but since almost everything has had 3-speed with over-drive autos (4-speed) since the early to mid 80s, the benefits were mostly negated. And now that, in the last few years most automatics were at least 5-speeds, and Chrysler came out with their new 8-speed automatic transmission this year, it seems that automatics are probably the way to go.

Personally, one thing that turned me off of manual transmissions for gas mileage was a 1989 GMC S15 (5-speed 2.5L 4cyl) I purchased to get better gas mileage driving the 80+ miles round trip to school and back I was driving each day. It ended up getting the exact same gas mileage as my brother’s Chevrolet S10 with the much bigger and faster 4.3L V6 and an automatic transmission (4-speed), so why even bother? At least I sold it for a profit.

And hey, maybe all the automatic transmissions in the kind of cars you drive suck, I don’t know.
