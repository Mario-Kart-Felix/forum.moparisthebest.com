Hmm interesting, maybe our automatics suck? but I can’t see them being too different. It’s just the general consensus here is manual is more fun to drive (anyone who enjoys driving can’t really deny this surely?) as you have a lot more control. Plus they are cheaper, and far cheaper to fix if anything goes wrong.

Unless you have one of those performance based double clutch automatics I can’t see it being terribly faster than a manual switch either, assuming ofcourse you are an experienced driver (avg shift time is very fast for even average drivers, around 500ms-800ms I believe- citation to be found :)) . Professionals will have lightning fast shifts so they almost always use manual, but many these days use flappy paddles (F1 for instance). I’ve also heard people complain about automatics shifts because they don’t always change up/down when you want (but would most peolpe who learned in an automatic even know when this optimum point is? I doubt it…). I’ve never driven an automatic, and whilst it probably makes day to day driving really easy and simple I find it more fun being in control.

I don’t think fuel economy can come into this because any decent manual driver can drive as efficient or more so than an automatic, but an automatics will just be as they are. This is really the reason I will continue to use manual, I can choose to drive and save petrol but if i’m in a rush I can burn it as fast as I want

Intriguing really how two very similar countries opted for different routes. My only guess can be is America is HUGE and thus I bet your average drives are far further and time consuming than ours. It would therefore be logical to have an automatic, and a manual for fun if you were really into your driving.
