I’m not sure which state you’re in, but obtaining a full license in NSW takes four years of driving. One as a learner, one as a P1 provisional driver, and then two as a P2 provisional driver.
You have a test to obtain each license type. The P1 driving test is an actual driving test which lasts for 45 minutes. Until you get a P1 license, you can only legally drive while under the supervision of a fully licensed driver.
To obtain the P1 license, which allows you to drive without supervision, you need to clock up 120 hours in a log book.

In NSW, learner drivers are limited to 80km/h, P1 drivers are restricted to 90km/h and P2 drivers are restricted to 100km/h (which is 62 miles/h). Most other states don’t have these speed limits as international studies have demonstrated that speed restrictions like these don’t generally reduce loss of life.
All learners and P1/2 drivers have to have a Blood Alcohol Concentration of 0 to drive, whereas fully licensed drivers have a limit of 0.05; it used to be 0.08, but it was reduced. I’ve read somewhere that the decrease doesn’t necessarily make a difference, but I doubt they’ll restore it to 0.08 again – we only do evidence based policy when it suits the government.

Anyway, P1 drivers are not allowed to use hands free mobiles, whereas P2 drivers are allowed to use hands free mobiles. P1 drivers aren’t allowed to tow a trailer, whereas P2 drivers can.
P1 drivers also have a night time passenger curfew; they aren’t allowed more than one passenger under the age of 21 between the hours of 11pm and 5am. This restriction doesn’t apply to P2 drivers and above.

I can’t think of anymore.

As for log book integrity enforcement, it’s basically impossible for them. Unless you or someone else gives them reason to suspect that the hours have been fudged, you won’t get caught doing it. Even then I suspect it would be extraordinarily difficult to prove.
