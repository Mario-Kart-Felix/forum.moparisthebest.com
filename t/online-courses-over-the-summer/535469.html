mod_taharok August 4, 2016, 11:18am #4

Plenty depending on the subjects in question. Any decent physics textbook would do you well (which I can suggest at least two, having read through parts of them in my own classes), but you really just need to work through them. Depending on how you want to apply the physics knowledge also changes which books you may study from. For example, if you wish to just study physics for the sake of understanding the theory and problems behind various disciplines of physics, then a simple textbook would suffice (calculus-based, of course). If you’re interested in applying advanced mechanics in physical simulations, then there are other books, articles, and papers that would be useful to you in addition to textbooks (since the theory is always necessary in order to move to application).

That being said, what exactly are you interested in?
