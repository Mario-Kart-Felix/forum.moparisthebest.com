Actually, the Myers-Briggs test was based off of Carl Jung’s research in Analytical Psychology. [Carl Jung was greatly influence by sigmund freud who was a very famous nurologist, who invented phycoanalysis]. The Myers-Briggs test wasn’t developed as a predictor of job success, and is actually discouraged in the Myers-Briggs moral use section of their original publication.

A quote:

“Whether people first hear about the two kinds of perception and two kinds of judgment as children, high school students, parents or grandparents, the richer development of their own type can be a rewarding adventure for the rest of their lives.”

–Isabel Myers

The point of this was to be able to tell people how to improve themselves and lead a more fulfilling life. Not to give companies a ‘possible success meter’ on interviewees.

In-fact the reason its so discouraged as a success meter for jobs is plain and simple: If a job candidate knows what job they are applying for, and is asked to take this test, he/she can [attempt to] answer questions best suited towards that job.

This is honestly only for personal enrichment.

Also, ytuio9, I think you may have a different understanding of Judging vs. Perceiving than the myers-briggs tests means to convey.

Judging means you use your best judgment in a given situation, not necessarily judge others.
