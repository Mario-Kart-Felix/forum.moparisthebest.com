Mopman July 30, 2016, 3:48pm #2

Are you doing the BTec National Diploma for IT? I took a year of that once, and didn’t do any A levels at the same time. In fact, I’ve never heard of anyone taking A Levels at the same time, my timetable was pretty full with just the BTec, it is a full time course after all…

If I was in your position, I would be taking four A Levels. I’m NOT saying this is right for you (and I’m unemployed, so don’t take my fuckin’ advice anyway) but from my experience the BTec/NVQ qualifications hold next to no weight in the IT sector, unless you plan on doing Tech Support, and even then it’s debatable.

If you want to go to uni and do Comp Sci or another programming-related degree, take the maths A level, for sure. It’s a requirement for the best colleges, and highly recommended for the slightly less revered ones. Do it. (It’s really not that bad, I quite enjoyed the AS year at least).

If it seems like you’re gonna be bored shitless, take maths, IT or computing, and then 1 or two fun a levels (just pick them at random from what looks cool, they’re only to make up points). If you get good grades like this, you’re pretty much guaranteed a decent degree course.

You’re making me want to go back to school.

Ahh, the certificate has less units than the diploma I believe? So you would be doing A level alongside it.

Yeah, since the whole points system came in, BTecs are pretty much equal how they should be when apply to unis. The only trouble is if you drop out early and want to get a job (which is what I did) - employers haven’t really switched over to the new ideas for IT yet. If you’re a bricklayer or plumber, a BTec/NVQ is awesome, they consider it valuable and relevant, but for some reason IT employers still like A Levels.

Was just a warning in case you think you might not get to uni. If you’re not a lazy shit like me and actually stick it out though, BTec would be fine. As for if you should do maths or not, hop on UCAS website and check out the courses you’d be interested in, see if they have “A maths related subject at a level” in their entry requirements - I seem to remember most did
