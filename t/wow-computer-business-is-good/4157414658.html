R.I.P_RSC August 1, 2016, 3:14pm #28

I completely agree with you when you say that low-skill jobs are important too. In the western world, more and more don’t want to do low-skill jobs, which is a good thing IMO. The higher skilled a population is, the more they earn and this all stimulates both economical and scientific growth. The focus shifts from basic necessities to knowledge. I also think it’s not that bad that Eastern European workers come to our country to do non-skilled jobs. They earn more than they do over there, and the jobs here get filled too.

But my problem is the rates of the average computer repair shop. I’ve been an intern in one, albeit only a week. But during that week I witnessed the complete randomness on which they base their prices, and the huge margins between hour wages and what is charged. I doubt the average employee earns €140,- an hour. So not only the customer is ripped off, the employees are cash cows themselves.

The course required for an average job in a computer repair shop takes 2 years. I’ve never had any computer related courses or even lessons, except one to teach me to type with 10 fingers and one mandatory Excel module. Yet with the help of the internet and the guts to actually open up a pc I can fix just about any computer problem the average person runs into, without it taking ages.

After reading this ‘rant’ again, I understand it’s stupid. The point is, that even though the average person could do low-skilled jobs, they pay others so they don’t have to it themselves.
