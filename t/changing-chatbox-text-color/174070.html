Purpose: To teach you how to change your chatbox text color

Difficulty: 1/10

Assumed Knowledge: How to follow simple directions

Classes Modified: client.java

Step 1:

Open your client.java find this

                            class30_sub2_sub1_sub4.method385(0, s1 + ":", i1, 822, j1);
                            j1 += class30_sub2_sub1_sub4.method383(anInt1116, s1) + 8;
                            class30_sub2_sub1_sub4.method385(255, aStringArray944[k], i1, 822, j1);

now, where it says “method385(0, s1 + “:”, i1, 822, j1);”
and “method385(225, aStringArray944[k], i1, 822, j1);”

Changing these numbers will change the color of the text after sending the chat. The first one (0) will change the color of your name (example: “xx nub xx: whats up”)

and the second number (225) will change the actual text after sending the chat (example: “xx nub xx: whats up”).

Step 2:

A little ways down from the first bit of code, you will find this

            class30_sub2_sub1_sub4.method385(0, s + ":", 90, 822, 4);
            class30_sub2_sub1_sub4.method385(255, aString887 + "*", 90, 822, 6 + class30_sub2_sub1_sub4.method383(anInt1116, s + ": "));
            Class30_Sub2_Sub1.method339(77, 0, 479, 0, (byte)4);

Just like you did before, this bit of code: “method385(0, s1 + “:”, i1, 822, k1);”

will change the color of your name before sending the chat (example: “xx nub xx: I am about to type this!”).

and this code: “method385(255, aStringArray944[k], i1, 822, k1);”

will change the color of the text before sending the chat (example: “xx nub xx: I am about to type this!”).
