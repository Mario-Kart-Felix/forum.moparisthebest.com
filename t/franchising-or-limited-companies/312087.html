ownagesbot July 31, 2016, 12:17pm #12

No you are wrong akl. You still have full control, you don’t just sell your name with a franchise, you sell your products, your protocol, your everything. For example, have you ever seen a mcdonalds that hasnt got the classic mcdonalds layout inside, or a mcdonalds that has staff that dont have to wear uniform. No. The reason is because when franchising a business, you have a whole contract, in affect you control all the smaller businesses, the owners only really manage these businesses and control the cash flow. Franchisers are also expected to do things such as staff training, this is usually made up by the steep royalty they charge. The key is to remember, with a franchise you buy more than just the name and you have much less control than you may think.

Also as someone previously stated, it depends entirely which market your in, what product it is, who your target audience is, what your aims and objectives are, how much money you have to invest and so much more. I mean, for example say you NEED to have a positive cashflow to stay aboard and your franchise royalties per month cost more than you’ll make in the first 6 months, you can’t possibly become a franchise.

In short, what im saying is: you can’t just say ‘whats best, a limited company or a franchise?’ they both have entirely different attributes for entirely different circumstances and the decision depends entirely on the business and the business plan. You are both nieve and ignorant if you believe otherwise.

From whose perspective is this question? The man at the top, or the one running the shop? My previous answers were all from the man at the top. But after reading some of the other posts I begin to think that you want the answer from the shop manager’s perspective.

It still depends on the product you are selling

If you plan on selling fast food in a small town, or from a small property, you’d join a fast food franchise (not McDonalds). If you have a bigger property and you are in a big town or city, you’d join McDonalds. If you start a big supermarket in a big town/city, you join a franchise. A small supermarket in a small town could be both. A specialized shop (butcher, vegetable shop) usually is a limited company.
