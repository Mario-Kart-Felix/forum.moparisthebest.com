Krichevskoy July 30, 2016, 3:34am #51
semaphore:

Community Member status upgrade vote. (Krich, this is what I should have offered when I was talking about a member sponsor.) In order to become a CM, two or more CM’s must sponsor you and take complete responsibility for your posts until a probationary period is over. Complete responsibility means loss of karma or other punishments for infractions made by a plebe.

This is good. Light mockery is ok (keep it entertaining - simply mean comments aren’t that funny), but we want to avoid being that annoying “forum upper class” that makes the boards so miserable for newbies that they stop coming.

I’ve never really liked that automatic upgrade based on rep - at some point rep becomes devalued, but why keep resetting things when you can just fix them? Even just adding a queue of 50-rep people that would be reviewed would be better. I propose a dual entry system:

  1. On suspicion of extreme competence
    You attain Community Member status by excelling and catching the interest of a patron or two. They propose your name in a voting board. This involves a short probationary period where the patrons are expected both to help you along if you need it (with WHAT, I’m not sure, but it seems like a provision we should have) and to keep you in line, though with a well-chosen member neither should be necessary.

  2. On proof of extreme popularity (among those with rep to give, that is)
    You gain 50 rep. Your name is placed in a queue (or autoposted to a voting board) for review by current Community Members (excluding anyone currently in probation) before granting you access.

Of course, this assumes that the Community Member boards are something worth getting into. We need to work on that - suggestions?
