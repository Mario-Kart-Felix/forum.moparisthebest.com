mod_taharok August 3, 2016, 2:47am #20

I’m really confused right now. Almost anything you’d want to do in DirectX (minus maybe some of the most recent features) are available in OpenGL, too. You would have no problems achieving everything you want in OpenGL, especially since most of the things in your list have nothing to do with the underlying graphics pipeline, anyway.

As far as that video goes, there’s so much to understand about that. You have no idea what the underlying implementations were, what graphics cards, drivers, and driver versions were being used, what versions of DirectX versus OpenGL were being used, whether the shaders were actually identical or slightly different, and so on. It’s also worth noting that on many NVidia implementations of OpenGL, they actually just recompile the GLSL shaders to CG and bind the OpenGL function calls to a DirectX wrapper. You’d be essentially seeing almost the exact same thing programmed using both libraries (unless, again, you are using selective DirectX features).
