Ok, so this is pretty much going to be my first ‘tutorial’ i guess.
without further or do…

some of you who use blender may have a constant problem with lighting.
you either have to much lights and light, or not enough.
in order to get around having to place a lot of lights, there’s ambient occlusion.
now ambient occlusion is a tool that produces ambient light around the scene and determines shadows based
on proximity to other objects.
now in the 'World tab, there’s a tab with a little check box and ‘Ambient occlusion’
check that box. now if you render it out, it’ll look something like this, (except what your scene is. just look at shadows)

you’ll notice that theres A TON of noise in the render.
in order to fix this, go to the ‘gather’ tab (like right under ambient occlusion and EV)
look for the thing that says ‘samples’. mine was defaulted at 5, yours may be different, but yeah
just crank that number up. between 10-100 should do.

the noise in the image is now reduced. :o

(Done on 16 samples.)

well thats it. i know i didnt go in much detail, but this should suffice.
if you want to learn more, i would recommend you experiment.

the body piece used in the so called ‘tutorial’ was just a practice model so i got a feel for armor modeling.)
