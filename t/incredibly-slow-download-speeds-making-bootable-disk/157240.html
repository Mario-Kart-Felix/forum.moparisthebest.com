The_Un-Named July 30, 2016, 4:45pm #8

Since no one has bothered to tell him, the speed provided at speedtest.net is in kilobits, NOT kilobytes. A kB(notice the uppercase b? this means it’s a kilobyte) is a kb times 8. So, say if your ISP offers you 2048kb(2mb) download speed, theoretically you could have 256kB download speed.

Since I assume you have 256kb broadband, your max download speed in kB is 32, and your downloading at 30.
