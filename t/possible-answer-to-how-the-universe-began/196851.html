Think of this. Maybe the big bang did not begin from one “point”. Maybe the universe was “inside out”, so all the matter in the universe was pulled into an area of negative space.

A way to imagine this is with a line. The line has two points, A and B. A is on the left, B is on the right. Imagine A begins moving to the right, and B is moving to the left. The two points are moving together, and the line is getting shorter. Eventually, both points will “overlap” and the line will be a point. However the two points continue moving until A is on the right, and B is on the left.

Now, imagine that in the center of the line, directly between the two points, is a source of gravity. The points will be pulled together, until they pass the center point, at which time they will pass each other and the process will repeat. Now take that one-dimensional example and piece it together with our 3D world. Perhaps once the universe ceases to expand, and all matter returns to the “center point”, it wont simply stop at that one point. Perhaps it will continue “falling” and the momentum will send all the matter into the “negative/inside-out” space. Then the process begins on the other side, and repeats infinitely.

Edit: This would also explain why most scientists would not describe the big bang as an “explosion”, as if the matter was moving outward with the momentum it already had.
