just like a computer, i would rather turn off my computer per day and turn it on the next day, instead of leaving it on 24-7, not only for the power, but for the freshness you get when you first turn your PC on. having a computer left on for a week is slower than from the moment you turn it on, period. (inb4 linux fanboys)

Why are we dissing each others code for who can’t provide weeks of stability running hundreds of players? we are unpaid programmers by hobbie who find spare time to whip this shit up, not exactly working for jagex getting paid full time hiring in some of the worlds best programmers and analysts to make sure a servers going to last weeks.

to me, Stability is making sure you don’t have dupes, making sure you don’t have CPU spikes by having other applications raping your CPU now and then. making sure you don’t have code that’s going to crash the server, and most of all, prevent from DoS in your game servers code, and overall DDoS.

THE PROCEDURE TO MAINTAIN HIGH STABILITY

  • I’d like all of the community to give some input on these steps to help people ensure they have the highest stability they can get. If you skip a step, it could bite you in the ass down the track.
  1. Server hardware (fast dual core, 2gb ram at least, good upstream connection)
  • something dedicated will always be better than a VPS. if your on a VPS and your server is lagging at times, since it’s shared resources it could be that your network or cpu bandwidth are getting raped.
  • since RSCA/RSCD has a single threaded game engine, it will hardly benefit from using more than 1 core. in some server packages you get the choice of a fast dual core of a medium quad, go with the fast dual core. (e.g 3.3ghz dual beats a 2.6 quad)
  1. DDoS protection (optional at first)
  • can be quite expensive and possibly unnecessary until needed, see how you go with none, you may need this down the line though.
  1. Software setup - configure it correctly too
  • XAMPP, WAMP etc can also have SQL security flaws if you don’t set this up properly, i can’t give help on this though.
  • get some resource monitor programs and monitor your system, make sure it’s healthy. the less software you have on your server the less chance things can interfere with your stability.
  1. Auto restarter that can restart the server safely every 12h
  • don’t rely on your self to reboot it when you think it needs one

At this point your all ready, the only thing that’s going to get in your way is DoS. people modifying their client to send invalid data which could possibly crash your server.
This is where RSCA needs to be optimized from crash attacks, as well as packet flooding. some people should work together to purposely attack your own server locally and record where things can go wrong, usually very easy fixes.

if anyone has input on above, feel free to share
