Stop blabbering about what is good and what is evil. Nothing is good or evil. Because it’s socially expected to define something as evil, most people do it. Serial killers, and other ‘evil’ people, have a social defect. It’s not as bad as it sounds, but it pulls them away from social contacts. They don’t follow the social rules. They have their own opinion, and express it.

An example: someone donates all the money he doesn’t need to fulfill his basic needs to charity. He lives in a cheap house, eats cheap food and doesn’t spend his money on stuff he doesn’t need. He’s a volunteer too. Once a year he goes to Africa to help Aids patients, to help dig water pits, to help build houses, etc… It’s expected from you to see this as a good thing, and most of you do. But a coin has two sides.

But what happens if all Aids patients live longer? Is it good? Digging water pits, so that less die of dehydration. Is it good? Again, you are a ‘bad’ person if you say it isn’t. But if Aids patients live longer, they can spread it. Of course they want children, of course they want sex. If less people die of dehydration, they live longer. But it’s a vicious cycle: Less die, more water is needed, more die. Less deaths from Aids, more spreading, more Aids patients.

Everything is both good and evil. Look beyond it.
