Here are a couple of things;

Eating

[ul][li]The biggest thing is don’t overeat. Eat until you feel satisfied, but never completely full. Make sure that you could eat a little bit more when you stop eating without feeling full.[/li]
[li]Avoid fat at all costs. Never consume more than 50 g in one day; the less the better. This includes saturated, unsaturated, and trans fats.[/li]
[li]Eat at least three balanced meals a day, and snack regularly to keep your metabolism going.[/li]
[li]Don’t eat sugar or too much sodium.[/li]
[li]Eat as many fruit and vegetables as possible. Fruits and vegetables are full of simple sugars and complex carbohydrates, which help refuel after a workout and give you lasting energy. Plus, they are hard for your body to digest, which gives you an increase in metabolism; also, they help to prevent colon cancer.[/li]
[li]Don’t eat thick sauces or salad dressings (e.g. alfredo, ranch, caesar). Find lighter alternatives such as Italian and Vinnigrette.[/li]
[li]Eat healthier alternatives to things. For example, drink skim milk v 1/2% and eat whole grains.[/li]
[li]Be careful around dairy. Yogurt, milk, ice cream, and cheese all have an enormous amount of fat.[/li]
[li]Don’t eat processed or packaged food.[/li]
[li]Eat whole or multi-grains.[/li]
[li]Drink at least eight glasses/four quarts of water per day.[/li][/ul]

The best food to eat is carbohydrates. They help to give you energy and are the main thing that is depleted when you exercise. The best carbohydrates are whole or multigrain, and can be found in food such as pasta, bagels, etc. Protein is also important to help muscles, and eating a high(er) protein diet does pretty much the same thing as supplements or protein shakes.

Personally, the best discovery I have found in eating is fruit. If you can overeat on fruit, go for it. Fruit is full of nutrients that help refuel and give energy. It also boosts your metabolism to help burn fat and take up energy, as well as promoting muscle growth, and reducing colon cancer. Just for a comparison, it takes about 2000 calories to sustain a person with a balanced diet for an average day. However, it takes roughly 8000 calories from fruit to sustain the same person.

Workouts:

[ul][li]Always use natural things versus machines. This involves using free weights, running v. going on an elyptical, etc.[/li]
[li]Aerobic exercise is key. Aerobic exercise is any form of exercise that requires an increased intake of breathe. The most common forms are running, swimming, and cardio. Personally, running and swimming are the best.[/li]
[li]ALWAYS stretch before and after exercise. It helps to decrease the risk for injury.[/li]
[li]When you are doing sets (pushups, pullups, abs, etc) limit the amount of rest between exercises. Having about 40-60 seconds is generally the best. Sessions of workouts lasting from 30 minutes to an hour are generally optimal.[/li]
[li]After a workout, eat something with whole grains within 15 minutes of finishing.[/li]
[li]Drink at least 8 oz of water for every pound lost.[/li][/ul]

In summary:

[ul][li]Drink lots of water[/li]
[li]Eat whole grains and carbohydrates[/li]
[li]Eat as many fruits and vegetables as possible.[/li]
[li]Avoid sugar and fat, as well as too much starch, processed and packaged foods, and dairy.[/li]
[li]Don’t eat any blatantly unhealthy stuff (desert, fast food, etc).[/li][/ul]

The above diet promotes muscle growth and recovery. Once you start eating right, the amount of muscle you’ll gain is insane.
