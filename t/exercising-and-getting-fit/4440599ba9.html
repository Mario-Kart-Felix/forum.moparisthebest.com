edit: I got bored so I decided to rip your post apart.

When you lift weights for MUSCLE GROWTH, you are tearing your muscle fibers (whether or not you’re primarily Type I or Type II, depends on your genetic makeup, this goes further in saying that those with primarily Type IIb will gain faster than those with Type IIa). Doing this is utilizing your bodies ATP/CP energy system. In the short, explosive movement (lifting heavy resistance) Adenosine Triphosphate dislocates and releases energy/heat resulting in the force for muscle contraction. The seperate cell than joins to a creatine phosphate cell in your muscle to repair. The rate this repairs at depends on your level of mitochondria and/or your levels of creatine. Lifting weights with a 80%~ of your MR for 8-12 (NEVER MORE THAN ABOUT 15) will yield best results for muscle growth.

Common myth; you cannot “turn fat into muscle”. Whether or not you want to believe it, you can’t. Why do people bulk up to become big and muscly? They do this first to increase their stores of ATP/CP.

Recovery time for the ATP/CP system is about 2 minutes. Best way is to give it 2-3 minutes between sets. Remember, to gain muscle - lift hard, lift heavy.

Also… drinking protein supplements STILL is yet to have it’s effectiveness proven. What IS proven, is that protein synthesis is what causes muscle redevelopment - so idiots believe drinking protein supplements make you gain. No… what makes you gain, is training hard.

You body does not “check if you have enough protein”. You need 1g of protein per 1kg of body weight (I can’t remember the EXACT amount), and that’s it. The rest is wasted and is excreted. You can argue with your bro-science-gym-lingo all you want, that’s fact.

EDIT: A position statement published by the ADA, DOC and ACSM recommends that endurance and strength-trained athletes have between 1.2 and 1.7 g/kg (0.5 - 0.8 grams per pound) of protein for the best performance and health.
