abraham2119 August 3, 2016, 5:21pm #82

I’ve actually had quite a transformation since last year. I started at a really fucking skinny 120 lbs at around 12-14% body fat and decided that it was time to bulk up. I began eating as much as I could without really tracking anything except protein, which I aimed to get 1g/pound of LBM, and packed on muscle like crazy in the beginning (~10 pounds in a month and a half) and even saw some fat loss (newbie gainz). After that, soccer season came along so I kind of laid off the weights but I trimmed down to a shredded ~9% body fat at ~130 lbs with, once again, only tracking my protein count (my soccer practices were two hours a day, four days a week, filled with distance running and HIIT). I’ve since began hitting the weights again and I’ve packed on about 15 lbs and currently hover at 145 lbs at ~10% body fat. I’m now on a quick two week cut for an upcoming trip and thought I’d give LeanGains a shot. It’s been fantastic for cutting, as it’s a great way to cut back on the calories, but I don’t really see myself doing it on a bulk.

My original training regimen was 3 times a week: chest/tri, back/bi, legs/shoulder, but I’ve since changed it to 4 times a week: chest/back, legs, arms/shoulders, , chest/back. My goals have always been aesthetic and I am now finally beginning to understand and put into practice the concept of form over weight and really focus on the mind-muscle connection. I’ve seen gains these past few months that completely overshadow the gains I’ve made throughout the year (chest specifically, I used to be an “arm bencher” and have now focused on pinching my shoulder blades together, sticking out my chest and really focusing on the squeeze and the negatives). In terms of dieting, the one thing I’d recommend is tracking calories and remeasuring your body fat every month (skin calipers are simple to use and closely accurate) and adjust caloric intake to fit goals. It makes a whole world of a difference.

Other than that, it’s all a matter of consistency. Remember, it’s a marathon not a sprint.

Good luck.
