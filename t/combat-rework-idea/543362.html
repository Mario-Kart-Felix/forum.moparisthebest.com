I am sort of “borrowing” this idea from Dragon Age but I think it’s a pretty cool idea for a rsps wanted to get your thoughts.

Melee:
-2hander of course, runecrafting plays a big role here cause you can craft runes. These runes depending on your runecrafting level have different effects such as a fire rune may add a certain % of extra damage of a certain fire element, there may be a body rune that enables body weaknesses/armor protection.
-Duelist/dual wielding, same perception but you would focus the runes to be more so for movement speed and such, and again it totally is determined by your levels.
-shield warrior ( weapon and shield ) same as the above versions.

-depending on the armor the more fatigue or heavy your equipment is the slower you attack, but the more protection you have.

-Spells:
-I haven’t thought of a way yet to add like the entropy spell category but…

-Things such as fire elementalist could have an advantage over an ice elementalist, a combination of logs/and bars make up the type of staff you have and the tier level. You could even craft runes for a melee 2hnader that did fire damage for example

Other thoughts:
-I never liked how runescape you could get 1,000’s of fish and cook it like that. Food should be rare so somehow change the economy but keep it efficient as well. I guess ideally pking would be rather different on a server that had these concepts.
-a rogue class seems interesting to make but I am not sure I would want to use the same concepts as Dragon Age fully for it.

Skills:
-Fishing ( partially discussed above )
-Mining/Woodcutting ( discussed above )
-firemaking would be the only to cook your food on the server I think would be best. Cause you are “travelers” anyway. You don’t have the luxury of going home to a range so to speak.
-Fletching, just like the Dalish elves make great bows on Dragon Age, I think fletching would be a great aset cause it allows you the possibility to craft a better higher tier staff/bow, the more higher tier, the more items required to make it.
-Crafting same as fletching but for armor ( light/medium )
-Smithing ( for making the bars for fletching and such, and also for making Heavy armor, I think Massive armor would be like too hard to stabilize in the economy so I am not sure how that would be added into a server.

Elrick August 4, 2016, 1:11pm #2

Making food hard to obtain while keeping it as the primary source of healing makes things very difficult in terms of training. Unless you planned to hike exp rates when battling monsters to combat the lack of ability to “grind” exp for levels, this would make xp rates worse than regular rs.

Now, if you used a hunger scenario and turned the primary form of healing into potions made by herblore and created ways to buff your character by adding enchants to armours and spell buffs to increase defence ratings, that would be pretty damn cool.

Enchanting armours can be used for a variety of things, not just adding health amounts. Increased damages on specific weapons, damage buffers of different source, and much more could be added. I think Skyrim’s enchanting system, combined with WoW’s idea of breaking down already enchanted equipment into basically “Enchanting essence” would make for a spectacular Runescape system.

Yeah, in Dragon Age ( well at least to my current knowledge ) the way weapons work is that they all have their different stats of course, but speed wise they are different.

I.e. a warhammer is a 2h weapon, just like a 2h sword is a 2h weapon. They re slower to attack with however have increased critical hit possibility and chance to crush frozen enemies.

i.e. a dagger is used more so by a rogue as it has backstab which allows more penalties and critical chances if you are coming from behind the dagger is also a faster weapon to attack with but like the best dagger I have personally seen was like 9.80 and that was uber rare versus some 2h that go intot he early 20’s easily.

there aren’t many scimitars in Dragon Age. I believe there may be a couple and they are from the Dalish area ( ? ) They seem to have the same speed as regular one hand/main hand swords. Most of the one hand/main hand swords average about 8 damage to about 18 damage max.

What really helps is the runecrafting of rune traces on the weapons/armors such as if you gave a weapon that has already been made on fire via a mage spell then add three paragon fire runes you would have like… 30% fire damage at boot to add to it which is pretty awesome.

To even out the combat area, you can also enchant armor to have resistance to specific types of magic, for example you can get some traces that help give like 10-20% fire resistance.
