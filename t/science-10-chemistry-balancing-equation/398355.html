Rixxx August 1, 2016, 12:18pm #9

You’re being careless. It’s Mg[size=6pt]3[/size]N[size=6pt]2[/size] (magnesium nitride) and it reacts with water to produce magnesium hydroxide and ammonia gas.

The left-hand side of the equation must equal the right-hand side of the equation.
Mg[size=6pt]3[/size]N[size=6pt]2[/size] + H[size=6pt]2[/size]O -> Mg(OH)[size=6pt]2[/size] + NH[size=6pt]3[/size]

Since the LHS has 3 Mg but the RHS has 1 Mg, we need to make sure the RHS has 3 Mg as well.
Mg[size=6pt]3[/size]N[size=6pt]2[/size] + H[size=6pt]2[/size]O -> 3Mg(OH)[size=6pt]2[/size] + NH[size=6pt]3[/size]

Now that the Mg count in both sides are the same, we notice that RHS has 6 O but LHS only has 1. Balance it.
Mg[size=6pt]3[/size]N[size=6pt]2[/size] + 6H[size=6pt]2[/size]O -> 3Mg(OH)[size=6pt]2[/size] + NH[size=6pt]3[/size]

Now everything is balanced except N and H, you’ll end up with this:
Mg[size=6pt]3[/size]N[size=6pt]2[/size] + 6H[size=6pt]2[/size]O -> 3Mg(OH)[size=6pt]2[/size] + 2NH[size=6pt]3[/size]
