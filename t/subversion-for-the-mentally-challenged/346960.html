Well, everybody needs version control if they’re going to be developing their own server. It’s important even for someone who isn’t going for the open-source or team approach because it gives an option of a backup just in case you fail completely on accident or something happens to you.

First, go to http://assembla.com. They offer free subversion as well as GIT, Trac, and more. When you get there, click “Create a Space”.

1.jpg1024×768

You will be presented with a variety of options, I have outlined 2 in particular for our purpose in the picture below.

2.jpg1024×768

You will be prompted to register, this is hopefully self-explanatory. If it isn’t, you should get your brain checked. After registration and email confirmation you will be presented with this page. Again, click the “Create a Space” button. Select the same option you did before.

3.jpg1024×768

Now, you will be presented with five paid plans:

4.jpg1024×768

But if you scroll down a little, you’ll see their hidden free plan:

5.jpg1024×768

Fill in your details and everything you want to do and then you’ll be sent to your admin tab. Go to the tab that has your configuration on it.

6.jpg1024×768

You should see a link to your subversion repository, it is important that you copy and write this down.

7.jpg1024×768

Now we will download TortoiseSVN, if you already have it, skip this. Go to http://tortoisesvn.tigris.org and you will be presented with this page:

8.jpg1024×768

Hit the download page link and you’ll be sent to yet another page, click the download that will be used on your system.

9.jpg1024×768

After installing it, you will be prompted to reboot, don’t do so as it’s not required for anything we’re doing. Now, go into any folder (My Documents is a good one) and right click and select “SVN Checkout…”

10.jpg1024×768

Enter your repository address and hit OK.

11.jpg1024×768

If you decided to be an idiot and reboot, you’ll see a green checkmark inside a bubble on the folder that you checked out to, this is good. Now, just to test, let’s create an empty text file in that folder. It should have a question mark on it (if you rebooted). Then, right click and click “SVN Commit…”

12.jpg1024×768

You will be prompted with this interface. Just type in a message or not if you’re an asshole, select all the files that need updating (in our case just the one text file), and hit OK.

13.jpg1024×768

Should all go well, you should end up with this message:

14.jpg1024×768

Now, go into your favorite browser and paste that SVN repo URL into the address bar, should all have gone well, you should see your file on the repo. Give it about 5 minutes max before going back and checking everything, because sometimes it takes a while.

15.jpg1024×768

Now, in your server’s case, I recommend copying and pasting your server files into the SVN folder which you checked out to, and if you use an IDE such as eclipse, to open your project directly from the SVN folder, so that all you have to do is hit “SVN Commit…” every time you update.

I hope this at least helped somebody and I hope that someone uses it.
