Personally I believe there to be myriad approaches to the solvability of such a profound question. Firstly, are humans actually able to solve all NP complete problems? What about NP hard? I sincerely doubt that the human brain is capable of solving an NP hard problem in the general sense, given any reasonable amount of time. But even taking this as a given - is the brain capable of solving problems that cannot be solved on a turing machine? If the answer to that question is no then I seriously suggest a complete re-evaluation of the pumping lemma and it’s tertiary connotations.
