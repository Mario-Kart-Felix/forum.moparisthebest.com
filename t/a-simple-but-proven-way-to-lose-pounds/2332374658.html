I’m not sure about the spicy food.

However drinking a lot of water is really good because it keeps your body hydrated, it flushes your system of excess stuff (thus the potential for weightloss), and it can also fill you up a bit. If you’re working out and actually trying to lose weight then you should be drinking 16+ glasses (at least double the usual recommendation) per day.

Another important thing when trying to lose weight is the way you eat. Many people rush eating, and in doing so they eat excessively. By eating a bit slower you give your body the chance to tell you that you’re full.

Small meals are good for this reason, but it’s often a hard transition to make if you rely on your parents for meals. 6 small meals a day ensure that you have energy but don’t overindulge. You should be having something small every 2 hours or so.
Generally speaking, a piece of fruit every 2 hours will get you by. You can also eat things like nuts, oatmeal (or something else that’s grainy in nature). Avoid overly processed cereals, because they’re crap.

I’m sure there are more approaches, but I can’t be bothered listing them. It’s all about the types of foods you eat though.
For example, you should have things like bananas and apples in the earlier portion of the day because their carbohydrate chains are ‘simpler’ in nature when compared to the carbohydrates in foods like lettuce and broccoli.

EDIT: Oh, and bread is probably best avoided if you want to lose weight quickly. Yeah, it’s a staple meal in the lives of most people, but it’s also not the best (unless full grain, preferably as grainy as possible). White bread is absolutely a no-go zone if you’re serious about getting fit, because it’s just rubbish.
