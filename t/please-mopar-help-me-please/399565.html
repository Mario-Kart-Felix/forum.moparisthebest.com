One way to assert yourself as an adult is to call them by their first name. If you know who you are sending the email too (and usually the staff directory WILL tell you), change the opening to Hello , or just ,

Don’t say ‘my child’, use the childs name.

Make the email more brief, try to get to the point sooner.

If you have first lunch, mention something about how you eat breakfast at ____ and you usually aren’t hungry until later in the day. Or you take class ___ early in the day, but ‘I noticed that he cant focus that early, and he tends to need a more laid back class in the morning’, something like that.

Usually your lunch is based on your class, so they may have to redo your entire schedule just to change one lunch period. Make sure you say you appreciate anything they can do, you don’t want to sound ungrateful.

Add a clause mentioning that if they could get back to you with any other options, you’d like to hear them. You may be able to get more classes with your girlfriend too! (Unless you are taking different subjects?)
