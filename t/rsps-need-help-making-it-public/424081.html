Zentior:

Thanks for telling me nicely.

Let me sum this up for you easily without wasting 2 hours on youtube.

Open your command prompt (cmd.exe) and type in ‘ipconfig’ without the 's.
It should say something like this:

   Connection-specific DNS Suffix  . :
   IPv4 Address. . . . . . . . . . . : 192.168.2.101
   Subnet Mask . . . . . . . . . . . : 255.255.255.0
   Default Gateway . . . . . . . . . : 192.168.2.1

Your IPv4 Address and Default Gateway will be different.
Where it says ‘Default Gateway’, you type that into your browser’s URL bar. See how mine’s 192.168.2.1? I would type 192.168.2.1 into my URL bar. It will bring you to a page that asks about a username and password. It’s usually ‘admin’ for the username and ‘admin’ for the password, without the 's again. Maybe its ‘administrator’ for the username and ‘admin’ for the password. Maybe the username is ‘admin’ and there is no password. Once your in, you can go to the advanced tab/firewall tab or whatever you have and click ‘port forwarding’. Where it asks for the IPv4 address, you put your computer’s IPv4 address which was listed in the ipconfig command. The port is your server’s port (43594 unless you changed it to something else). Save your router’s preferences and turn on your server. Then, go to www.canyouseeme.org and see what it says about port 43594 (whether its open or not).
