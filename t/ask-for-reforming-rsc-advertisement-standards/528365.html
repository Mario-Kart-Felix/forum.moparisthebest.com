Flash Player has malicious features, as do most mobile phones. Digital handcuffs are the most common malicious features. They restrict what you can do with the data in your own computer. Perhaps we ask ourselves, who are we holding accountable?

In light in what has happened regarding NSA violating privacy and general concerns behind the intent of proprietary software, I propose we set new standards to properly regulate private servers.

No more questionable use of software distribution. Stop server owners from violating YOUR freedoms and privacy. Ask for source code before downloading!

In the free/libre software movement, we develop software that respects users' freedom, so we and you can escape from software that doesn't.

Are you ready for change? Here are some proposed standards that ALL server administrators should follow in order to advertise to your fellow moparscapers:

-All software made available to distribute shall be released under the GNU GPL (General Public License).

-There should be public repositories made for each piece of software the end user is required to download in order to use. Absolutely no 3rd party links.

Ex: If one makes a server called FreeScape, the owner shall be responsible for creating a public repository (Github, bitbucket, etc) including licensing made known to the user on the topic, it MUST stay up to date with said updates any developers make throughout the lifetime of it’s distribution.

-Any and all changes made to the software shall be known ON THE ADVERTISEMENT TOPIC for any user to review, including the repository.

If any server owners refuse to comply, you SHOULD have the right to refuse further use of their services and ask for removal from advertisements for no compliance on said standards.

Value your freedom or you will lose it, teaches history. 'Don't bother us with politics', respond those who don't want to learn.
