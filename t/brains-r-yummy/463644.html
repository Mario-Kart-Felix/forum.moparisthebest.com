If the Taliban hasn’t scared you off booking that trip to Pakistan, the latest outbreak of a brain-eating amoeba might. The nightmarish organism with a funky moniker (Naegleria fowleri) enters the body through the nose — hitching a ride on dirty water — then travels up to the brain, where it rapidly destroys tissue, killing its host in about a week. Ten people in Karachi have succumbed to the grey matter-chomping microscopic monster since May. But it can get you at home, too; over the past 10 years, 32 Naegleria fowleri infections have been reported in the U.S. Worried? Look out for fever, nausea, vomiting, a stiff neck and headaches.
