The_Reaper_X July 31, 2016, 3:08am #2

To add an npc, you go into your autospawn.cfg. Look at the format that it is it.

//------NpcID—CoordX–CoordY–Height–RangeX1-RangeY1-RangeX2-RangeY2-WalkType-Descrption

under that you should see numbers. Those numbers follow this format, so the first number should be the NpcID, the second should be the X coordinate you wish the npc to be on, third should be the Y coordinate you wish your npc to be on, etc. I’ll explain range later if you want me to, but for now im just going to tell you that it’s what allows the npc to walk around randomly rather than stay in one place.

Your question as to how to make leveling easier is a very vague description. You can change the amount of exp given if you’re in combat, and out of combat training skills such as fishing, woodcutting, or firemaking.

[center][size=14pt]If you would like to change combat exp, then you would search for[/size][/center]

if (FightType == 1)

under that you should see

TotalExp = (double) (750 * hitDiff); TotalExp = (double) (TotalExp * CombatExpRate); addSkillXP((int) (TotalExp), playerAttack);

the 750* is how much exp you get per hp you hit on your opponent. So if you hit a 5, you would get 750 x 5.

FightType is the type of attack you’re using when in combat. such as accurate, aggressive, defensive, controlled, etc. Next to the if statement of FightType 1 you should see “Accurate”. This means that the skill exp will be applied to you if you are in combat using an accurate attack. Below FightType one you should see FightType 2, which applies to aggressive type combat.

Notice that in the if statement for FightType 1, you’ll see that it shows:

addSkillXP((int) (TotalExp), playerAttack);

This means that it uses the TotalExp, which is that 750, and it applies to Attack. This goes accordingly for the other fight types as well. Under Aggressive you’ll see that it applies to strength, under Defensive it applies to Defense, and so on.

[center][size=14pt]To change bonuses on equipment, you’ll be working in your item.cfg.[/size][/center]

It doesn’t give you a format at the top of the page, it only says item name and shop info. So you’ll have to figure it out sort of on your own. I’ll try to explain it a little more to get you in the right direction though.

Look at this item for example:

item = 636 Robe_top The_ultimate_in_gnome_design. 3000000 3000000 3000000 0 0 0 150 0 0 10 10 10 10 0 0

The number is the item ID. Next it’s the item name, followed by item description. After that is what were going to be editing. The next three numbers as you see are 30000000. That is how much you pay to buy from the shop, how much you get from selling it to the shop, and how much you get from alching it. The next numbers are the bonuses. The only bonus that really matters in most servers is the second to last. that is the strength bonus. The rest of the are the other bonuses, as well as mage, and range bonuses. I don’t have too much time to explain what each number does, because I’m going to school in a bit. So if you need any more help, be sure to repost here and ill help you when i get back.
