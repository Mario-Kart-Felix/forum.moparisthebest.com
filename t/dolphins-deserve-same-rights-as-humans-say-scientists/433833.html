Dolphins deserve same rights as humans, say scientists

General Discussion
R.I.P_RSC August 2, 2016, 4:05pm #1
Dolphins should be treated as non-human "persons", with their rights to life and liberty respected, scientists meeting in Canada have been told. Experts in philosophy, conservation and animal behaviour want support for a Declaration of Rights for Cetaceans. They believe dolphins and whales are sufficiently intelligent to justify the same ethical considerations as humans. Recognising their rights would mean an end to whaling and their captivity, or their use in entertainment. The move was made at the annual meeting of the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS) in Vancouver, Canada, the world's biggest science conference. It is based on years of research that has shown dolphins and whales have large, complex brains and a human-like level of self-awareness. This has led the experts to conclude that although non-human, dolphins and whales are "people" in a philosophical sense, which has far-reaching implications.
Source: [url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-17116882]http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-17116882[/url]

At first, this might seem like a plan made up by a bunch of hippies, but when you think about it, it does raise some interesting questions.

Our society is still based upon the notion that Earth is a planet which is there solely to be used by humans. Everything has to make way for us humans. When we want a road, a building, natural resources, nature has to make way for us. This way of thinking stems from religion, where humans are seen as special creations. We’re not equal to animals, we are their superiors.

But this very different from the consequences of evolution by natural selection. According to this theory, humans are an advanced, but not special species. We’re just the product of millions of years of evolution, and are not vital for this planet. Looking at the last few centuries, the Earth might be a better place without human presence.

But still, even those who accept the theory of evolution don’t always accept that we’re just animals too. They too see the world as a place to exploit. I’m not an exception. I drive a polluting car, I eat non-organic food. Because of our highly stage of adaption to our environment, we can alter that environment without even thinking about it.

Through the years, people have killed masses of other people just because they saw them as inferior. Today, we see killing of other human beings as an unethical act. Even those who support the death penalty don’t support the killing of entire groups. But we’ve drawn a line. We based that line on that same notion that humans are superior, or special beings. We don’t kill the mentally retarded, not because they are intelligent beings, but because they are human. They are one of us. The exact mentality that caused the Holocaust and Apartheid.

Primates and marine mammals (among others) show signs of intelligence too. They live in groups, they form their own cultures, they use tools, they teach their youth. They are sentient beings. What sets them apart from humans is that they are not capable of altering their environment, because they lack the physical or mental capabilities. In a way, that makes them inferior to humans. But does that mean that they too should become objects that we humans can use? Is a whale equal to an insect, just because a whale doesn’t live in a house? That can’t be it, there are still human cultures that have barely changed their environment. But we accept their presence, and leave them alone. We don’t kill them, we don’t eat them. They are human.

So we accept that humans are animals too, we accept that some other species are intelligent, yet we as a society don’t act upon this knowledge. We draw a line, not based on intelligence, but on species. We’ve reached the emancipation of women, we’ve agreed that all humans, black or white, are equal, so the next step should be the acceptance of rights for other intelligent beings.
