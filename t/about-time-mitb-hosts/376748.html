davidi2 August 1, 2016, 4:25am #14

There are quite a few things you can do to reduce the lag you guys are getting, because like people are say, some people have no lag, while others have a lot. First thing you can do is change your render distance. Hit esc, then go to options. Depending on your computer, you may need to set it on ‘Tiny’ to get no lag.

It can also be your connection, which I can’t fix. One thing you can do is go to speedtest.net, and change the server. Find my state (Washington, USA; in the North West of the country) and start the test. My down/up speeds vary by distance etc, but it seems fine for most people. lalagggg

Also, remember: Connection lag is not fps lag. I have experienced connection lag. It goes something like this: You place a block, after about two seconds, the block disappears. Then after a few more seconds (depending on your lag) it will reappear.

If you do get connection lag, like the example above, that’s when there is a problem with our connections.
