[quote=“Source B”]‘You would really wonder to see how these slaves live on board… yet by careful management of our masters of ships they are so regulated that it seems incredible. [quote=“Source C”]‘Two of the officers have the charge of stowing them. At sundown the Second Mate and Boatswain descend, cat [a whip] in hand, and stow the Negroes for the night. Those on the starboard side face forward and in one another’s lap, vulgarly called spoon fashion. On the port side they are stowed with face aft; this position is considered preferable for the free pulsation of the heart. The tallest are selected for the greatest breadth of the vessel, while the short size and youngsters are stowed in the fore part of the ship.’

-A Slaver’s Log Book by Theoplihus Connea, 1854 (slave trade was then illegal)[/quote]

[quote=“Source E”]’…As for provision, they were much better off than in generality of slave-ships… The slaves were supplied for breakfast with a fair ration of ground Indian corn boiled, with a spoonful of molasses to each; they generally had boiled rice for dinner; and supper was the same as breakfast. -Zamba a local ‘king’, son to a king who had acquired considerable wealth as a slave dealer, travelled to the U.S in the early 1800s[/quote]

[quote=“Source F”]‘I always took great pains to promote the health and comfort of all on board, by proper diet, regularity, excercise and cleanliness…’
