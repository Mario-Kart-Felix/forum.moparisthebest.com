tL August 1, 2016, 1:11am #16

Not necessarily. University study isn’t as black and white as education in earlier years, (Primary School, Secondary School and College). Loads of money is pumped into universities, into research, facilities and tuition it isn’t education that is required for someone to take on a successful career, and it isn’t education that everyone enjoys.

It isn’t about setting your career up, it is about furthering your knowledge to hopefully take your eventual career further than it would do. It doesn’t make much economical sense to do such a thing, not everyone will use their university education properly, so all that money is wasted - they’re not earning anymore money, and no more money is being paid in tax than if they didn’t go to university.

The government also pay for a large portion of your tuition fees here, it isn’t them giving you the entire bill to pay, only a part of it. Hence the fairly low fees, just compare Cambridge and Harvard fees, two of the top universities in the world.

I agree, you are pumping money back into the country, so they should help pay for your education too, but I don’t really agree on the government taking the entire bill, because I don’t think it makes much sense.
