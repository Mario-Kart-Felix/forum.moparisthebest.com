PaulT August 4, 2016, 1:59pm #20

That build looks fine!

One thing I noticed was the RAM is A-Data, which I’m sure is a fine brand, but I personally would trade that out for G.Skill, Crucial, Corsair, or Kingston. Just personal preference. G.Skill is really nice if you want to up the clock-speed of your RAM, and I’ve found Crucial to be the most reliable over the long term. Once again, that’s more personal preference. RAM manufacturing is much more consolidated now and it’s not likely that any major brand is going to have a mediocre or substandard part.

Another notation would be the Intel chipset versus a comparable AMD chipset. AMD has a better price to performance ratio; for example, you can get a AMD FX-8350 that clocks in at 4GHz with 8-cores, compared to the 3.5 Ghz Quad Core i5, for $60 cheaper. Combine that with the fact that AMD motherboards are also cheaper, and it’s a pretty big margin. Intel is known for having cooler and more efficient parts, but many benchmarking setups are skewed due to the way instruction sets are set up. For most games a AMD FX-6300 would probably work fine for your processor; it’s a 6-core 3.5GHz part that is $130 cheaper than the Quad-Core i5 at the same clock speed.

PC-Part-Picker is a great site, and I’m sure you can compare different builds there to get a better idea of what kind of parts you really want. Also consider the R9 Graphics card, which comes in many flavors. You can put dual R9’s in Crossfire mode, where you harness the power of two graphics cards put together, as well.

I’m not really an AMD fanboy, but at the $1000 mark you probably will get more bang for your buck with AMD.
