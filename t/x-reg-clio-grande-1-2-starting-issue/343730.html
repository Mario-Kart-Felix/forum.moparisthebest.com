moparisthebest July 31, 2016, 5:07pm #4

You can take the starter to an auto parts store and have it checked for free, around here anyway. If you can’t you can CAREFULLY do it with a set of jumper cables hooked up to the battery, but the ground cable on the starter housing, the positive cable on the solenoid where the positive cable would go on a car, then using a screwdriver or something else jump between the two terminals on the solenoid (the big one where the cable is attached and the much smaller, actuator one.

If it runs, the solenoid and starter are good. If it doesn’t, bypass the solenoid by removing the positive cable from it, and touching it to the wire or strap that goes from the solenoid directly into the starter. If the starter runs then the solenoid is bad, if it doesn’t the starter is bad.

If the starter is good, then you have a cable problem, a switch problem, or if the car is so equipped some fancy computer stopping it from turning over. The first thing you should check regardless is for blown fuses, it’s obvious but easily overlooked, we have all done it.
